Introduction

This document covers the acid clean of the hypochlorite plant of Al-Arish power station.
The hypochlorite plant of Al-Arish power station is manufactured by DENORA, consisting of two units, each unit includes 3 electrolyzers, each electrolyzer, consists of 10 cells of Anodes (titanium ASTM B2 65 Gr.1) and cathodes (titanium ASTM B2 65 Gr.1).

The hypochlorite plant is designed to produce diluted sodium hypochlorite from sea water when D.C current passes through the electrolyzers. The produced hypochlorite solution is used as a disinfectant, when injected into the sea water circuits providing a protection to the equipments against the growth of organic fouling.

                       2NaOH + Cl2  -------->   NaOCl + NaCl + H2

                                                                           Sodium hypochlorite

Side reactions, both chemical and electrochemical, take place simultaneously with the basic reactions, such as the decomposition of hypochlorite to chloride, the anodic oxidation of hypochlorite to chlorate (traces only), the cathodic reduction of hypochlorite to chloride and the anodic evolution of oxygen.
Moreover, some cautions which are present in seawater (e.g. calcium, magnesium and other metals) form hydroxides and carbonates resulting in suspended solids which are carried out of the electrolyzers by the seawater stream.
These side reactions reduce the current efficiency, such that the actual amount of electric power required to produce hypochlorite is higher than the theoretical requirement.                  
In the chemical literature, hypochlorite concentrations are commonly referred to in terms of available or “active’’ chlorine, i.e. the quantity of chlorine having the same oxidizing effect as the hypochlorite when analyzed by standard methods.

Hypochlorite produced from seawater is sufficiently stable to tolerate, without substantial loss of chlorine, the storage conditions required for shock treatment with chlorine injections regularly spaced during the day and corresponding renewal of the hypochlorite solution in the storage tank.

Long storage periods, such as two days or more, should not be considered as a design datum.

Hydrogen gas is produced in the electrolyzer at the rate of about 0.35 m3/kg chlorine.

After long operational periods of hypochlorite plant, some deposits consisting mainly of hydroxides and carbonates which are caused by sea water hardness may be formed inside the electrolyzers; so acid cleaning process must be carried out to remove the formed deposits by dissolving them in diluted solution of hydrochloric acid.

The formed deposits inside the electrolyzers decrease the efficiency of the hypochlorite production process giving low sodium hypochlorite concentration. When acid cleaning is carried out, the sodium hypochlorite concentration becomes 1.5 g/L as active chlorine which is the normal designed value.

Information

 

Plant Description

Seawater Supply System- Seawaters delivered to the chlorination plant by three centrifugal seawater booster pumps and horizontal type (two operating and one is standby).

 

First Acid Tank Filling- Suction of 33% hydrochloric acid to the tank is fulfilled with acid pump. When the demand amount of the acid is sucked (800 liter), the acid tank (5m3) is completed with fresh water to obtain 5% acid concentration.

Note:

The hypochlorite dosing and shocking pumps as also the hydrochloric acid pumps are magnetic couping type. A (dry function can be a cause of trouble)

 

 

Procedure

  1. Prepare the concentrated hydrochloric acid drum at a level higher than acid inlet line.

  2. Connect the drum with the inlet line by means a sealed flexible hose and open the vent at drum top.

  3. Select the acid pump and open its inlet and outlet valve, switch on it and switch off it when the drum is empty.

  4. Replace the empty drum with a new filled drum and repeat the above steps up to the required volume of concentrated HCL.

  5. Complete the acid tank filling with fresh water, opening the valve and checking the level via level indicator.

Directives

Before starting the cleaning, the main power supply to the electrolyzers must be switched off and the following protection equipments should be available:

  • Safety goggles to protect the eyes.

  • Working–dress with buttoned neck and cuffs.

  • Rubber shoes with rubber sole.

  • Emergency eye washer.

  • Emergency shower.

  • First aid box.
    After finishing the cleaning of the electrolyzers the diluted acid must be neutralized before discharging using Weak Alkali (soda ash).

 

 

Electrolyzers Acid Washing

  1. Switch off the power supply to the electrolyzers.

  2. Drain the system.

  3. Open all valves of acid cleaning system between the tank and the electrolyzers and vice versa.

  4. Open inlet and outlet valves of acid circulation pump.

  5. Close any drain valve.

  6. Close valve leads to storage tanks.

  7. Check the acid level on the hydrochloric acid tank. Refill if the level is minor of 50%.

  8. Start the selected acid pump. Let the acid solution (HCl) circulate through the electrolyzer and dissolve the fouling precipitates. Normally 60 minutes circulation should be sufficient to remove the precipitates.

  9. Switch off the selected acid pump and close the suction valve of the pump from the acid tank and open the suction valve from the electrolyzer.

  10. Start the selected pump and recover back the acid from the electrolyzer to the acid tank.

  11. Once the electrolyzer is empty, switch off the acid pump and close its inlet and outlet valves and drain the small amount of diluted acid remaining in the piping by opening the dry valve.

  12. Rinse the electrolyzer with sea water for 2-3 minutes.

  13. After checking the operation valve, the electrolyzer becomes ready for a new start up.  

Spent Hydrochloric Acid Handling

When the concentration of the HCL solution falls to approximately 3% after having been used for several cleanings, it should be either discharged (if the acid solution is turbid) and neutralized with soda ash before discharging, or reconcentrated to 5% (if the acid solution is clear). Assuming that the total volume of the acid solution is 5000 liters and spent HCL concentration is 3%, the value of spent acid to be drained and the volume of conc. HCL to be added comes from the following equation.

                5000 × 5 = (5000 – X) × 3 + (X× 33)       

where:

                X = 167 liters concentrated HCL.                   

Routine Check

Every Two Weeks    Proceed to acid washing of electrolyzers.

Monthly

Accumulation of dust, dirt and corrosion, may cause faulty operation of many devices. Therefore, a periodic routine of cleaning and inspections should be established. Check for cleanliness of the D.C. Power and Control Unit cubicle and surface of components. Check the efficiency of the earthing system.

Yearly    Check for tightness of all bolted connection.

When needed   Reconcentrate spent 3% acid solution, or prepare fresh 5% acid solution.
