Directives

Aeration Problems

Boiling Action, Violent Turbulence Throughout Aertaion Tank Surface, Apparent

 

Possible Cause

Necessary Check & possible Corrective Actions

Over aeration resulting in high DO and/or floc shearing

Check DO, should be in range of 1.0 to 3.0 mg/L throughout tank.

Reduce aeration to maintain DO in proper range by closing one of the air distribution header.

 

Low DO and/or Septic Odors in Mixed Liquor

 

Possible Cause

Necessary Check & possible Corrective Actions

             Under Aeration

 

a. Check DO, should be in range of 1.0 to 3.0 mg/L throughout tank.

b. Check for adequate mixing in Aeration Tank.

            MLSS Too High

a. Check Return Sludge Rate and sludge blanket depth in Settlement Tank

 

Adjust return rate to maintain 80-100 cm depth to sludge blanket in Settlement Tank

 

b. Check sludge wasting

 

Adjust MLSS by increasing the wasting rate to lower MLSS to obtain proper F/M ratio (see Note).

Note: The F/M is called the Food-Mass (or Food Microorganism) ratio and is an expression of the amount of incoming "food" (measured as COD or BOD5 in Influent) compared to amount of "bugs" in the Aeration Tank (measured as MLSS in kg/m3 x volume of tank is m3). The normal F/M range for an Extended Aeration Plant is 0.05 - 0.15

 

FOAMING PROBLEMS

Thick, Scummy Dark Brown Foam on Aeration Tank Surface

 

Possible Cause

Necessary Check & possible Corrective Actions

Aeration Tank is critically under loaded (F/M too low) due to improper wasting program

a. Check and monitor trends for:

-  Increasing MLSS, mg/L

-  Increasing sludge age

-  Decreasing F/M

-  Increasing aeration for the same DO-level

-  Decreasing wasting rate

-  Increasing secondary effluent chlorine demand

-  Decreasing Aeration Tank effluent pH

 

b. Check and monitor influent to Settlement Tank and return rates to each Aeration Tank. Imbalance may be under loading one tank

Equalize influent and return rates to each Aeration Tank

Scum Entering Aeration Tank

Check influent for oil and grease

Actively enforce sewer restrictions for public use of sewers

 

Greasy, Dark Tan Foam that is Strong and Carries over to the Settlement Tank

 

Possible Cause

Necessary Check & possible Corrective Actions

Filamentous organism (Nocardia)

Reduce sludge age

Reduce air flow rate to reduce depth of foam accumulation

Chlorinate return sludge

Spray chlorine solution directly on the foam

Reduce pH in mixed liquor

Inject a mutant bacterial additive

 

 

 

SETTLING TANK

Decreased Settling Rate in Settlement Tank

 

Possible Cause

 Necessary Check & possible Corrective Actions

Changes in Organic Loading and/or MLSS

Maintain constant F/M ratio by adjusting MLSS

Insufficient Nutrients

Add sufficient nutrients to maintain residual. Generally not a problem with Sewage Treatment Plants

Increased Total Dissolved Solids (TDS)

(see Note)

Determine source of increased TDS. Take action to divert. Monitor system to determine if sludge will acclimate to increased TDS

Filamentous Organisms

High organic loadings and/or insufficient nutrients can lead to filamentous organisms, the Dissolved Oxygen should be checked and nutrients ("food") added if deficient

Note: TDS  isn't part of regular tests, but can be carried for the observation

 

Bulking Sludge

Clouds of Billowing Homogeneous Sludge Rising and Extending through the Settlement Tank.

 

Possible Cause

Necessary Check & possible Corrective Actions

Low organic loading (see Note 1)

a. Check and monitor trends for:

-   Changing MLSS

-   Changing sludge age

-   Changing F/M

-   Changing DO-level

-   Changing influent BOD (COD)

b. Adjust wasting rates by not more than 20 % every day until process approaches normal operating parameter's. Temporarily increase return rates to minimize solids carryover from Settlement Tank. Continue until normal control parameter values are approached.

Toxic present causing dispersed growth bulking

Check mixed liquor respiration rate.

Actively enforce restrictions to public use of sewers.

Note 1:Chlorine is ineffective in correcting dispersed growth bulking

            SVI = Sludge Volume Index expressed in mL per gram.

This is the Note 2: Sludge Volume (SV) in [mL/L] after 30 minutes settling time, divided by the MLSS in [mg/L]            SVI = (SV x 1,000)/MLSS in [mL/g]

The index is a measure of sludge settleability and thickening characteristics.

A value less than 100 is good, over 150 is poor

Note 3: SDI = Sludge Density Index = 1/SVI in [g/mL]

 

 

 

 

 

SECONDARY TREATMENT

High Suspended Solids in Secondary Effluent

 

Possible Cause

Necessary Check & possible Corrective Actions

No sludge removal

Initiate and/or increase sludge wastage

High MLSS concentration

Decrease MLSS if possible but not enough to violate discharge permit BOD concentration

Excessive flow

Increase F/M ratio if possible. Low F/M can cause dispersed growth ("Dispersed": Not making proper flocs)

Increased Total Dissolved Solids (TDS)(see Note)

If TDS increased by more than 5,000 mg/L, increase in Suspended Sop lids is likely due to TDS increase. Eliminate source of increased TDS if possible. Evaluate potential for sludge acclimation to increased TDS levels. Add coagulants upstream Settlement Tank

High sludge blanket

Determine whether cause of high sludge blanket is due to inadequate wastage or poor settling. Adjust recycle and wastage rate to decrease sludge blanket and maintain MLSS

Floating sludge

Refer to section on Corrective Actions for Floating Sludge

Note: TDS not part of regular tests, but can be done for observation
