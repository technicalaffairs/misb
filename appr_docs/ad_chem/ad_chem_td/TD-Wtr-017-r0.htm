Introduction  

This document covers the pH value  Control for the feed water and condensate lines of  high or low   pressure boilers.

Ammonia ( NH3 ) or Other volatile alkalis, organic amines, have therefore been used to control feed water pH in particular applications, the most widely used being neutralizing amines (volatile amines) and filming amines are typically used as corrosion inhibitors to prevent the feed water or the steam and condensate lines from the corrosion.

Volatile amines inhibit the corrosion by controlling the condensate pH. Filming amines form the water repellent film on metal surfaces and inhibit the corrosion by preventing the metals from contacting with the corrosive substances, such as oxygen and carbon dioxide.

The pH value

Molecules of water, dissociate or splite into ions

        H2O  -------  [H] + + [ OH] –

At a given temperature the product of the concentrations of these ions , g-ion/L,

          Kw  =  [H] + [OH] –

Where [OH]- concentration of Hydroxyl ion, [H]+ concentration of Hydrogen ion. Kw is constant, this quantity is referred to as the ion product of water.

- For instance, at 298K, Kw = 10-14

- The reaction of a medium is assumed to be neutral if :

[H] + = [OH] -,    g-ion/L

-It is clear that in an acid medium [H] +  must be greater than 10 -7, and in an alkali  medium [H] + < 10 -7 for ions, [OH] - on the contrary is greater than 10 -7 in an  alkali medium and smaller than10 -7 in an acid medium

** The (P) function is used in chemistry to handle very small numbers, it is the negative logarithm of the number being expressed.

          Log 10 1/[H]+ = pH

-Water that has10 -7 gram equivalents per liter of hydrogen ions is said to have a pH of 7.

-The expression p OH = Log 10 1/[OH] -   will be referred to as the hydroxyl value so  

          PKw   = pOH  +  pH  =14     ( t = 298k )

So the neutrality requirement for an aqueous solution water is pH = 7, an acid medium is pH < 7 and pOH > 7, and an alkali medium is pH >7,  pOH < 7.

Directives

 

pH Control

  1. Maintenance of proper pH through out the boiler feed water, boiler, and condensate systems is essential for corrosion control .

  2. Most feed water, condensate, and high pressure boiler water requires direct monitoring of pH.

 

Control of pH is Important for the Following Reasons

  1. Corrosion rates of metals used in boiler systems are sensitive to variations in pH.

  2. Low pH or in sufficient alkalinity can result in corrosive acidic attack.

  3. High pH or excess alkalinity can result in caustic imperilment and foaming, with  resultant  carryover.

  4. Speed of oxygen scavenging reactions is highly dependent on pH levels.

The pH or Alkalinity Level Maintained in a Boiler System Depends on Many Factor, Such as

  1. System pressure,

  2. System metals.

  3. Feed water quality.

  4. Type of chemical treatment applied.

The corrosion rate of carbon steel at feed water temperatures approaches a minimum  value in the pH range of  9.2 – 9.6 as in Figure 1.

 

                Figure 1 iron corrosion product release from carbon steel in boiler feed water

 

The best pH for protection of copper alloys is some what lower than the optimum level for carbon steel. for systems that contain both metals the condensate and feed water pH is often maintained  between 9.0 and 9.6 for corrosion protection of both metals.

Feed Water pH Adjustment

  1. In condensate and feed water system containing both ferrous alloys the most satisfactory pH rang to uses 9. 0. – 9.6.

  2. If In the absence of the copper alloys, corrosion of ferrous components may be further reduced by raising the pH to 9.4 or above.

  3. In drum boiler, any non volatile salts present in the feed water will rapidly increase in concentration in the boiler water to a level determined by the amount of blowdown being employed.

  4. The presence of such salts in feed water could also lead to contamination of steam where  spray desuperheaters are used. Consequently only volatile alkalis are used for feed water pH control .

The approach has several advantages, in particular:

  1. There is no excessive accumulation of alkali in the boiler water, the volatile alkali passing out of boiler with the steam.

  2. As condensates are formed in the lower pressure section of the turbine in the condensers and on the steam side of the feed heaters, the alkali is immediately available in these parts of the steam / water  system, raising pH of the condensates and hence suppressing corrosion.

Ammonia ( NH3) is the most widely used volatile alkali because of :

  1. Its low cost.

  2. Ready availability.

  3. Its stability at high temperature.

Ammonia have some disadvantages in that it is very volatile in steam and it is a relatively weak alkali at high temperatures.

Other volatile alkalis, organic amines, have therefore been used to control feed water pH  in particular applications, the most widely used being

Morpholine, Cyclohexylamine, Diethyl amino ethanol, Amino methyl propanol, Methoxy propylamine
