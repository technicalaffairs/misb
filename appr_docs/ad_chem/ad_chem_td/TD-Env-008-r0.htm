Secondary Treatment    

It is a part of Talkha sewage treatment plant in which the sewage flows into the aeration unit and is mixed with a suspension of living aerobic micro-organisms ( activated sludge ) and aerated in a tank by blowing air through the liquid. The micro-organic use the polluting material in the sewage as food and convert it to carbon dioxide, water and new cell material to maintain efficient mixing and provide the oxygen which the organisms need. The micro-organisms use the polluting matter in the sewage as food and convert it to carbon dioxide, water and new cell material. The aeration of the mixed liquor ( mixture of sewage and activated sludge ) provides the organisms with oxygen and also helps to mix the contents of the tank.

    Bacteria + Organic Matter + O2  ---------->  CO2 + H2O +  New Cell

                                                        6.5 – 8

After several hours aeration, the mixture liquor passes into the settlement tank where the activated sludge settles and is returned to the aeration tank for reuse ( via a return sludge airlift ) to treat more sewage. The clear liquid supernatant at the surface of the settlement tank contains very little residual organic material and can generally be discharged direct to a water course as treated effluent or as in this case given further treatment to improve the quality by,  (via Dual Media Filtration ) which removes the fine suspended solidest, and sterilized ( via hypochlorite ).

Excess activated sludge (cell material ) has to be periodically removed to optimize the plant efficiency. A surplus activated sludge airlift transfers sludge from the aeration tank ( via the settlement tank ) into an aerobic sludge digester, which is aerated by blowing air through the liquor. The sludge (with oxygen but without external food) digests itself to produce a carbon dioxide, water and a much reduced volume of sludge.

In the satec plant, the aeration, settlement and aerobic sludge digestion phases occur continuously.

In addition, as excess sludge builds up in the plant and digester, it is necessary to remove or “ waste“ some sludge from the plant to maintain the correct balance. In this plant this achieved manually by draining off some wastes sludge from the digester to the drying beds to produce a low volume cake.

The activated sludge is a very complex mixture of micro-organisms which can rapidly fluctuate and show very marked variations at different locations.

  1. Bacteria
    The most important group of micro-organisms in the aerobic biological treatment of sewage/effluent. These are small, less than 1µm in diameter and less than 5 µm in length, and occur in many forms in countless numbers. Bacteria utilise substances in true solution and have very fast growth rates and operate most efficiently at pH between (6.5 and 8.5 ).
     

  2. Fungi
    Multi-cellular plant-like organisms which use organic matter as their food supply. These can operate at much lower pH values than bacteria but, since they tend to be difficult to settle because of their filamentous nature, operating conditions are arranged to discourage their formation.

Plant Description

  1. Screening or Comminuter Unit

    Sewage or trade waste flows into the plant through  either a screen or comminutor. The function of comminutor is to chop up solids such as rags without removing them from the sewage flow.

  2. Aeration Compartments

    The annular aeration compartments containing the mixed liquor are fitted with coarse bubble air diffusers supported in clusters on down-pipes connected to the air header. Each individual down-pipe is equipped with a stop-cock, for regulating their supply and allowing the down–pipe with its diffuser cluster to be removed from the aeration tank whilst the plant is in operation.

  3. Blowers and Control Gear

The air for the plant is supplied by Roots type positive displacement  blowers mounted in a separate building. The blowers are equipped with all the necessary safety, stop and non-return valves and are driven by ‘V’ belts and pulleys. Electrical control gear is also installed in the Blower House.

The motor should then be moved on its slide rails until the belts can be replaced and tightened. When correctly tensioned, a pull of 7 kg (15 Ib) at the centre of a belt should produce a deflection of 8 mm.

Check that the pulleys are in line and secure, and that the motor is properly tightened down before restarting the machine. Do not run the blower with the guard removed.

Directives

Routine and Preventive Maintenance:

Routine Check

The following check must be done before each operation: The availability of raw water at inlet of the plant.

  1. The functional status of each equipment.

  2. The functional status of each instrument.

  3. The working condition of sludge particles or other dirt.

  4. Before start up the plant the respective valves opening and closing should be checked up.

Preventive Maintenance

  1. Keep close the sluice gate in case of unavailability of raw water to prevent against any sudden thrust of raw water to the inlet equipments.

  2. Sometimes air diffusers can be blocked up by fine sludge particles or other dirt such as algae etc. which can be removed or washed up by water from external source.

Daily Maintenance on Model R

  1. Inspect liquor in plant and see that:
    * Aeration tank contents are brown with only a faint earthy odor.
    * Effluent leaving the plant's clear.
    * Objects in settlement tank are visible to depth of at least 45 cm (18 inches).  

  2. Remove floating objects from the tank and burn or bury these.

  3. Check that the skimmer is not blocked (e.g. by rags).

  4. Carry out 30 minute settlement Jar Test. If results indicate the need for desluding from aeration tank or to the drying beds, make arrangements to do this.

Weekly Maintenance on Model R

  1. Clean tank at waterline in all areas by brushing and hosing down.

  2. Carry out Ammonia Test

  3. Check Air Supply system for leaks and rectify these as necessary.

  4. Check paintwork for damage and touch up as necessary.

  5. Clean air filters on blowers.

  6. Top up oil in blower gearcases, using the recommended grade. (Do not overfill or allow oil to go in tank).

Operating Directives

The main points requiring attention to ensure correct operation of the plant are dissolved oxygen concentration, mixed liquor suspended solid concentration, mixed liquor pH, and sludge quality.

  1. Dissolved Oxygen Concentration- The concentration of dissolved oxygen not below 0.5 mg/l.

  2. Mixed Liquor Suspended Solid Concentration- The concentration of suspended solid in the mixed liquor in the mixing aeration tank should be maintained in the region of 2000-3000 mg/l with a rapidly settling activated sludge, this will correspond to a volume of approximately 25-40 % after settlement for 30 minutes.

  3. Mixed Liquor pH- For the correct functioning of the biological process the mixed liquor should be in the range pH 6.5 – 8.0.

  4. Foam Control- This may be controlled by the addition of small quantity 90.1 liters-1/4 pt )of the–foam oil.

  5. F/M Ratio = Influent B.O.D in  (kg/day) / MLVSS  (kg) x aeration tank volume.
    MLVSS mean  (mixed liquor volatile suspended solids ). This ratio may be equal 0.15 for a good operation which we can adjust the amount of activated return sludge from the settling tank to the aeration tank.

Operating Tests

  1. Settlement Test
    This simple test provides basic information regarding the operation of the plant and should be carried out daily. A one liter measuring cylinder should be filled with mixed liquor from the mixing aeration compartment and allowed to stand for 30 minutes. The volume of sludge settled out should normally be in the range 25-40 %. If the volume is above 40 % the mixed liquor should be checked.
    The supernatant liquor above the sludge layer should be clear, free from excessive solids layer and should have a faint earthy smell.
    A smell of H2S indicates that the air supply to the plant  is an inadequate and should be increased.
    The sludge should be dark brown in colour, a grey or black colour also indicates inadequate air supply.

  2. Dissolved Oxygen
    A good indication that the dissolved oxygen concentration is sufficient is given by superficial is examination of the mixed liquor. The mixed liquor should have only a faint earthy smell. A black or grey mixed liquor having a foul smell indicates that the air supply is insufficient and this should be increased, we can measure the oxygen by a dissolved oxygen meter, and can be measured chemically using the (copper sulphate-sulphamic acid method ).

  3.  pH
    The determination of mixed liquor pH is best carried out by the use of an electronic pH meter or by the use of pH indicator papers of the appropriate range.

Maintenance of Mechanical Equipment

  1. Aeration Tank
    The walls of the aeration tanks above the water line should be kept clean and free from grease and scum by brushing and/ or hosing as the required air diffuser should be inspected every two months and cleaned.

  2. Air Lifts
    The sludge return air lift should be in operation all the time and only requires attention in the event of a blockage. If should the air lift become blocked it must be freed immediately.

  3. Air Blower
    It should be lubricated and cleaned weekly by brushing and washing    -changing the blower drive motor belts.
    The blowers have been provided with spare drive belts and when necessary these are changed in the following manner:
      *     Turn the power off by closing the locally sited isolator and also the associated isolator on the control plant, for added safety remove the appropriate fuses.
      *     Open the belt guard.
      * Remove the existing V belts, it may be necessary to slacken off the belts first by moving the motor along the side rails.
      * Replace the belts with those provided and re-tighten the motor side rails ensuring that the belts are tensioned correctly and the pulleys are correctly aligned.
      * Replace the belts guard and restart the blower.

  4. Electric Motors
    It must be cleaned, maximum permissible temperature rise of the motor is 65°C. Lubrication should be carried out in accordance with the manufacture's instructions.
