Introduction

This document covers  the sewage water treatment  plant of  Ayoun Moussa PS.

The system collects sewage water from power station in various sumps for treatment. Sewage water is treated, then discharged back to the Red Sea via the seal well in the circulating water discharge.

Sewage treatment plant properties will comply with the requirement of Egyptian Law number (4) for year 1994.

Information

System Overview

The sewage water is drained to the sanitary water lift stations by gravity mains. The water is accumulated in the lift stations; when the water reaches a predetermined level, the lift station pumps start pumping the water out of the lift station and to the sewage treatment plant.

Lift Station       

There are five sanitary water lift stations on site, apart from the colony sewage system. Each lift station contains two pumps; one in operation mode and the other in standby mode.

Lift Station Pumps

The pump system including the pump, motor, and power cable is approved for use in areas classified as hazardous locations, in accordance with the NEC Class1, Division1, Group C and D.  As required by Factory Manual, the motor shall be capable of operating in pumped media up to 400ºC. The motor thermal switch shall allow safe motor operation up to 1270ºC. In addition, an internal flat switch shall be available in the motor chamber.

Equipment is designed to have a life of not less than 30 years at specified conditions excluding expendable items such as packing, seals & gaskets.

Each lift station contains two submersible, non-clog, and wastewater pumps per lift station.  Each pump is equipped with submersible, electric motor, rated for 400 V, 3 phase, 50 HZ operation. The pumps are supplied with a mating cast iron discharge connections and shall be capable of delivering the required flow, at the total dynamic head.

The pumps are firmly connected to the discharge connection, guided by not less than two guide bars extending from the top of the station to discharge connection.

Aeration Zone

The incoming raw sewage is delivered to the plant Aeration Zone via an inlet box and inlet bar screen and passes through communitor. In the Aeration Zone the raw effluent is mixed with returned activated sludge from the Settling Zone and continuously aerated with air from the plant air blowers. This air supplies the oxygen necessary for growth of the micro-organisms and also ensures that the returned activated sludge and incoming raw sewage are thoroughly mixed. After a retention period in the aeration compartment (approx. 48 hours), the treated liquor passes through a transfer pipe into the Settling Zone.

Settling Zone

The Settling Zone allows the sludge and supernatant to separate. The supernatant liquor overflows an annular V notched weir into a collecting channel and hence into the chlorine contact tank. The sludge settles and accumulates in the Settling Zone where it is continuously withdrawn by two ( service/ standby submersible pumps) and returned to the Aeration Zone. Excess sludge that builds up in the system is periodically wasted through a manually operated valve.

Air Blowers

Three air blowers are provided ( two in operation and one standby). Each blower is controlled with an On/Off switch on the control panel. A timer is provided in the control circuit for automatic operation.

Air Diffusers

Air from the air blowers is admitted to the Aeration Zone via a number of air diffusers situated in the bottom of the Aeration Zone.

 Sodium Hypochlorite Dosing Set

Sodium hypochlorite solution is dosed into the treated effluent tank in order to disinfect the treated effluent.

The sodium hypochlorite dosing set is located in the blower house with the dosing pumps ON/OFF switch  located on the control panel.

 Sodium Hypochlorite Dosing

The basic equation for dosing is:

    INITIAL DOSAGE in mg/l  =  DEMAND in mg/l + RESIDUAL in mg/l

The amount of chlorine required to disinfect the treated water will vary according to the composition of the sewage and the degree of disinfection to be achieved, and usually vary between 4 to 12 mg/l in order to maintain a satisfactory chlorine residual of 0.5 to 1.0 mg/l.

Treated Effluent Tank

The treated effluent out from the settling tank flows by gravity into the treated effluent storage tank.

This tank is designed with baffle walls to ensure sufficient time for chlorine dispersal before the treated effluent is withdrawn through pumps.

Treated Effluent Pumps

Two pumps are provided to feed the irrigation network. They are equipped the required suction and delivery isolating valves and a non return valve for each unit.

 

Sludge Drying Beds

Drying beds, are provided to receive and drain water from the excess activated sludge. This group is provided with an integral Supernatant Tank. The drained water is collected in this tank and returned to the aeration tank.  

Sewage Treatment Plant Overview

In the extended aeration treatment plant the incoming raw sewage containing the organic matter, is mixed with previously aerated sewage that is rich in micro-organisms and referred to as activated sludge.

The mixture of activated sludge and raw sewage is aerated to provide the oxygen necessary for the biological degradation of the organic matter, and to assist in the mixing process. The air must be continuously supplied.

As long as the bacterial colony in the treatment plant is well aerated and healthy, there will be no odors from treatment process.

After aeration the mixed liquor is passed to a Settling Zone to allow the activated sludge to settle out prior to recycling, and to allow the supernatant liquor to overflow a weir board to the chlorine contact tank. The excess activated sludge that builds up in the Settling Zone is periodically discharged to sludge drying beds for disposal. After that the treated effluent is disinfected with chlorine to destroy harmful micro-organisms. The chlorine is dosed in the outgoing pipe to the treated effluent tank to provide a 0.5 mg/l residual chlorine in the Treated Effluent Tank.

Directives

Confirm that the plant has been installed according to  instructions manual and it is filled with water or sewage.

Check the sewage pumps and their float switches.

Check that blower gear case oil level is correct.

Check that the chemical dosing pumps are working.

Start the submersible sewage pumps.

Start the blowers and make sure that the blowing of air is sufficient in the aerated section of the grease trap, balancing, selector, aeration tank and sludge holding tank. Allow the blowers to run continuously and check the plant daily. Foaming will almost certainly occur for few days if activated sludge is not seed.

After one week, the mixed liquor in the Aeration Zone should become brown in color and settle rapidly when a sample is examined in a  jar test, using the 30 minutes settlement test explained later. If the mixed liquor has an odor, the Air Supply should be increased.

After three weeks, the plant should be producing a good clear effluent with no smell, apart from a faint earthly odor similar to damp soil.  In cases where the initial load on the plant is much less than that of the design load, the results may take longer to attain.

Start the recycling airlifts at approximately 2/3 of their maximum output by opening the gate valve and closing gate valve.

Do not start the excess sludge removal as long as a sufficient concentration (300 ml) of activated sludge is maintained in the aeration tank.

To maintain the plant at a good operating conditions, a daily routine sludge settling test should take place and desludging is done at 20-40 % ratio.

Foaming may require from time to time and is generally an indication that too much activated sludge has been removed. Adjust the sludge removal routine as necessary.

Don't adjust the stroke length when the pump is stationary; adjustment must only be made during suction stroke.

 

 

 

To disinfect the treated effluent, start the pump and set it at approximately half stroke and after 15 minutes contact time, measure the chlorine content in the contact tank and then adjust the pump output to maintain 0.5 mg/l residual chlorine. Repeat chlorine tests at regular intervals, and adjust the pump delivery accordingly.
