Introduction

This document covers the waste water treatment plant of AL-Arish  power station.

The sanitary plant will be under treatment by an activated sludge plant, aeration basin, clarifier and Aerobic sludge stabilization system, the plant operates continuously at a constant rate therefore due to the high peaks in flow and concentration one equalization basin has been provided with internal aeration in order to avoid bad smell problems the plant has an extended aeration area with sludge recirculation.                             

Time by time the sludge transfers to the storage tank to digested before disposal the separated water from sludge will be disposal directly to the proper disposal area or this separated water will be flowed to the clarified water filter to be treated for reuse.The plant runs on automatic mode and is controlled by PLC on the indoor also manual system is provided to operate different equipments manually.

Information

 

 

General Activities

The system performs the following activities during operation:

Sanitary waste water is collected in a tank after preliminary comminutor process at the inlet of the screen pit.

Later this collected sanitary waste water is lifted up by a pumping station and flowed to the aeration basin where an activated sludge liquor provides an effective degradation of organic matter in order to achieve an elevated oxygen transfer rate.

Fine air bubble diffusers are installed in order to satisfy oxygen demand of activated sludge.

Liquor effluent from the aeration basin will be flowed to the sedimentation tank where biomass will be separated from water as sludge.

A certain amount of recovered sludge is recycled back to the aeration basin in order to maintain an optimum value of sludge concentration in the aeration basin.

A major amount of sludge is collected in the sludge collecting tank where aeration diffusers assure the sludge to be digested before disposal.

Sludge is usually accumulated and barried in segregated sludge pits. To reuse this separated water, filters are provided after filtration.

Treatment Design

Screening Unit-Sewage or trade waste flow into the plant through a screen. The function of the screen is to chop up solids such as rags without removing them from the sewage flow.

Aeration Compartment- The annular aeration compartments containing the mixed liquor is fitted with coarse bubble air diffusers supported in clusters on down pipes connected to the air header. In this compartment the build up of microorganism is carried out and the weight of activated sludge gradually increases. It is mentioned also that the microorganisms use the polluting matter in the sewage as food and convert it to carbon dioxide, water and new all material.   

 

 

Settlement Compartment- The settlement tank is of the upward flow type and is filled with an inlet stilling well, a mechanically driven sludge scraper at the bottom and a mechanical surface skimmer. In this tank the activated sludge will concentrate and settle to the bottom and sucked from the bottom to the sludge storage tank.

Directives

 

Chemical Tests

There are some tests carried out during the process of treatment to check and attain good work, these tests are:

PH

PH test must be carried out for the sanitary water using PH meter in the chemical lab. For the correct functioning of the biological processes, the PH value should be in the range 6.5- 8.5. Low PH values encourage the growth of filamentous micro-organisms which hinder the settlement of the activated sludge. Low PH values can be caused by the discharge of acidic trade waste ( e.g. mineral acids or milk processing waste water ) If the PH value is lower than the demand range, lime or soda ash can be added.

Dissolved Oxygen   

A good indication that the dissolved Oxygen concentration is sufficient is given by superficial examination of the sludge which should have only a faint earthy smell. A black or grey sludge having a foul smell indicates that the air supply is insufficient. The Oxygen value is measured also by Oxygen meter to determine the value.

Settlement Test

This simple test provides basic information regarding the operation of the plant. A one liter measuring cylinder should be filled with mixed liquor from the mixing aeration compartment and allowed to stand for 30 min. The volume of sludge settled out should normally be in the range 25-40 percent. If the volume is above 40 percent the mixed liquor solids concentration should be checked. The supernatant liquor above the sludge layer should be clear, free from excessive solids and should have a faint earthly smell. A smell of hydrogen sulphide indicates that the air supply to the plant is inadequate and should be increased. The sludge should be dark brown in color. A grey or black color also indicates inadequate air supply.

 

 

Turbidity: By Spectrophotometer

The turbidity test measures an optical property of the water sample which results from the scattering and absorbing of light by the particulate matter present. The amount of turbidity registered is dependent on such variables as the size, shape and refractive properties of the particles.

In this method we zeroing by demineralized water and determined the turbidity of the sample at 450 nm by ( NTU ) nephelomteric turbidity unit.  

 

 

Suspended Solids: By Spectrophotometer

This method of determining suspended solids is a simple direct measurement which does not require the filtration or ignition and weighing steps that gravimetric procedures do. The method measure the S.S from ( O – to 750 mg/L ).

We zeroing by deminerlaized water and blend the sample by blender at high speed for exactly two minutes and remove gas bubbles in the sample and measure the S.S of the sample at 810 nm.

Routine and Preventive Maintenance

Routine Check

The following check must be done before each operation: The availability of raw water at inlet of the plant.

  1. The functional status of each equipment.

  2. The functional status of each instrument.

  3. The working condition of sludge particles or other dirt.

  4. Before start up the plant the respective valves opening and closing should be checked up.

Preventive Maintenance

  1. Keep close the sluice gate in case of unavailability of raw water to prevent against any sudden thrust of raw water to the inlet equipments.

  2. Sometimes air diffusers can be blocked up by fine sludge particles or other dirt such as algae etc. which can be removed or washed up by water from external source.

Filling up Procedure for the Sand Filter

 

          The filter should be filled up according to the following procedure

  1. Open the bottom manhole and hand hole.

  2. Insert through the bottom manhole the largest size quartz grit (10/20 mm) careful.

  3. Do the same operation with medium size quartz grit (3/5 mm).

  4. Close the manhole, pay attention to the right position of gasket.

  5. Pour through the top hand hole the smallest size quartz grit (0.4/0.8 mm), then pour anthracite from the top through the same hand hole.

  6. Now close the top hand hole.

  7. After each five years of use sands and anthracites should be substituted by fresh sands as because the filtering capacity become weaken.

  8. Before using sand filter a backwashing should be executed in order to clean up inner dirts of filters.

 

It is designed to remove residual suspended solids to attain suitable water for discharge to the sea. The filter consists of 3 types of sand of different sizes, and anthracite for the removal of odor and color.
