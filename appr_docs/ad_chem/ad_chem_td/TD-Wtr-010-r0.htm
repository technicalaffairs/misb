Introduction

The Consort pH meter P107 measures the pH in a solution. pH is a measure of relative acidity or alkalinity of water; so, it is a most important factor influencing scale–forming and corrosion tendencies.

Information

Each time the instrument is switched on; it will check briefly its internal calibrations while showing all functions of the display. An error message appears when something is wrong.

pH measurement from 0 to 14.

mV measurement from -1000 to +1000 mV.

temperature measurement from 0°C to 100°C.

Error Codes

(Or. pH) = Over range pH ( e.g. interrupted electrode cable ). 

(Or. mV) = Over range mV ( e.g. interrupted electrode cable ). 

(Or.°C)   = Over range°C ( measured degrees out of range ).

(MEM)  = Fatal memory error ( all factory settings are cleared ).

pH measurement

A pH measurement is always relative which makes it necessary to use standards. Each time the pH meter is switched on or electrodes are changed, the instrument should be standardized with 2 buffers differing from each other by at least 2 units, in order to compensate for:

 

 

 

Zero point: Each electrode has its own specific pH value, corresponding to the zero potential point (ISO-pH). This specific pH value can differ from its theoretical zero point (e.g. 7 pH) by a few tenths of a pH.

Slope: A good electrode follows the Nernst Equation within 90 to 100 %. If the slope lies outside these limits, the electrode becomes slow & may not be optimal for most application.

  1. Select the pH  mode.

  2. Adjust the temperature compensation to the temperature of the solution.

  3. After rinsing the electrode with distilled water, immerse them in a buffer solution of pH as close as possible to the ISO-pH of the electrode (e.g. 6.87 pH).

  4. Standardize to the pH of the buffer with the CALIBRATION  button.

  5. After rinsing the electrode with distilled water. Immerse them in a second buffer solution of pH close to the pH to be measured (e.g. 4.01 pH).

  6. Standardize to the pH of the buffer with the SLOPE button.

  7. After rinsing the electrode with distilled water, immerse them in the solution to be measured and read the display.

  8. Rinse the electrode always with distilled water after use and store them in a 3.5 M KCl solution.
    Note: Stirring the solution during the measurements promotes the homogeneity and is therefore always recommended.

mV Measurement

  1. Select the mV mode.

  2. After rinsing the electrode with distilled water, immerse them in the solution to be measured and the display will show the potential between the electrodes immediately.

  3. Rinse the electrode always with distilled water after use and store them in a 3.5 M KCl solution.

°C Measurement

Provided a Pt 1000 thermocompensator is used, read temperature by selecting the °C mode.

Simple Test

Try this procedure if you suspect the electrode performance of the instrument.

  1. Short-circuit the pH/mV input.

  2. Switch the instrument on.

  3. Select the mV range. The display should show [0] ±1 mV.

  4. Run the calibrate procedure if erroneous values are read.

 

 

 

 

 

 

Calibration

This procedure permits to calibrate automatically all amplifiers of the instrument.                                         

  • Short-circuit the pH/mV input.

  • Turn all three potentiometers fully to the right (clockwise) and select the°C mode.

  • Switch the instrument on.

  • The display shows [SELF] for a while and returns to the measurements.

Directives

Maintenance of the CONSORT pH Meter P107

The normal operating temperature should be between 0°Cand 40°C. Never store the instruments in a room with high humidity or at very low temperatures (condensation of water).

Electrode Maintenance 

pH Electrode: A pH electrode is active and stable only after wetting. For this purpose it must be immersed for at least 10 hours in a 3.5 M KCl solution. During short interruptions (e.g. storage) the electrode should be immersed in a 3.5 M KCl solution. In doing this it is always kept ready for use. When the interruption is longer than month, refill the closing cap with 3.5 M KCl and plug it on the electrode tip in order to protect the glass bulb.

 

A polluted electrode may be cleaned with a soft detergent or 0.1 M HCl. Greasy substances may be removed with acetone or alcohol [never do this with plastic electrode]. If the electrode is polluted by proteinaceous materials (such as blood). It should stand in a pepsin solution overnight and then be cleaned before use.

 

The pH electrode wears away by being used. If the electrode tends to respond slower and calibration becomes difficult, even after cleaning, it should be replaced by a new one.

 

Metal electrodes (pt, Ag, Au): Metal electrodes are always ready for use. During short interruptions they are immersed in distilled water. They should be cleaned regularly:

  • Silver electrodes are immersed in a concentrated ammonia solution during one hour.

  • Platinum or gold electrodes are immersed in concentrated nitric acid during one hour.
