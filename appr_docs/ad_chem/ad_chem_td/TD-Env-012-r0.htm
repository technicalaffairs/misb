Introduction

This document covers the waste water treatment plant of AL-Arish  power station.

The waste water treatment system treats the plant liquid wastes and produce an effluent suitable for discharge in Al-Arish Power Station.

The plant serves two (2) nominal 30 MW units fired with natural gas or No6 fuel, and provide oil/water separation, clarification, sludge thickening, dewatering, and filtration of the waste water generated by various plant operation.

Information

Process Description

The plant receives waste water from fuel oil pumping unit, oil tank area, turbine area, fuel storage area and from boiler area to the oily water collecting basin and metal cleaning and boiler blowdown water to the waste water retention basin continuously.

Waste water of oily water collecting tank is transferred to the air flotation basin after oil being separated from waste water by two oil skimmers

The water from the flotation basin will be recirculated through a saturator to increase floating material within the fluid then these floating elements are eliminated to the skimmed oil pit by a scrapper mechanism

The recovered oil by these operations will be taken out to the barrel by screw type skimmed oil pumps From flotation basin separated waste is flowed to the waste water retention basin is flowed to the waste and boiler blow-down waste is added to follow the next stage treatment within the plant.

In the retention basin non neutralized water from clarified water pit, badly filtered water after being utilized will be added to pass through the consequent treatments. Waste water from retention basin will be transferred to the clarifier, where chemicals will be dosed in order to neutralize and for coagulation and flocculation purpose. After being treated chemically the sludge from the bottom of the clarifier basin will be transferred to the sludge thickener

After being dense by thickener the sludge will be transferred to the filter press by mixing with cationic flocculants to increase sludge thickening efficiency. The separated water will be flowed to the retention basin to follow the same treatment. The sludge from filter press will be taken out for disposal. From clarifier separated water will be flowed to the clarifier  water pit. The water from the clarified water pit will be pumped to the automatic pressure sand filter to have the final resultant water.

Main Equipments

The System includes the following Main Equipments:

  • One (1) caustic feed tank and two (2x100%) dosing pumps.

  • One (1) coagulation preparation tank with one electric mixer and transfer pump.

  • One (1) coagulant feed tank with one electric mixer and two (2x100%) metering feed pumps.

  • One (1) flocculants (coagulant aid) dosing packing composed of one dosing hopper, two preparing tanks with two electric mixers one feeding tank and two (2x100%) metering feed pumps.

  • One flocculent preparing and feeding tank with one electric mixer and two metering feed pumps for dewatering in filter press.

  • Retention basin auxiliaries are composed of two (2x100%) waste water supply pumps, retention basin controls and other accessories

  • Oily water collecting basin auxiliaries composed of two oil skimmers, one oil removal system in a flotation basin by means of an air saturating process and two (2x100%) oil transfer pumps.

  • One flocculator with scraper bridge and (2x100%) sludge pumps for discharge to sludge thickener.

  • Two automatic pressure filters.

  • One sludge thickener and two (2x100%) sludge transfer pumps.

  • One sludge dewatering filter press.

  • One sludge cake transfer belt to disposal.

Directives

 

Chemical Treatment

Clarifier

The main aim of waste water plant is the rejection of substances and particles causing turbidity of water to be suitable to discharge it to the sea satisfying the law of environments. Chemical substances were used for this purpose by dosing them in the mentioned water as follow:

Coagulant Feed System

For removing suspend solids, ferric chloride solution ( 40% ) was used where it is injected in the clarifier using ( 2x100% ) dosing pumps and one dosing tank. When dosing occur ferric ions Fe+++ will interact to the small particles scattered in water forming big particles which will settle by gravity

Fe+ +  • -  ------>  Fe •   settling

Coagulation Aid Feed System

For removing the most particles in water anionic polyelectrolyte substance was used which injected in the clarifier by the aid of polyelectrolyte preparing system which consisted of stainless steel tank dosing hopper, service water source and ( 2x100% ) dosing pumps. The poly electrolyte material will form aggregates of big particles previously formed leading to settling down to the bottom of clarifier. The efficiency of removing suspended solids is 90% to a minimum of 25 mg/l.

Sodium Hydroxide Injection

This design is present in the unit which contain soda tank and ( 2x100% ) soda pump for injection in clarifier to adjust the PH water recommended for coagulation process.

Routine Chemical Test

There are different tests carried out on the water produce from the clarifier and from the final treatment of the unit to attain good quality water. These test can be summarized as follow:

PH Value

The PH value is measured in the field and in chemical lab using PH meters designed for this purpose. The suitable PH value for coagulation process was found to be ( 7-8 ) which attained using sodium hydroxide injection.

Turbidity Value

The turbidity of water is attributed to suspended solids of water so this value is measured to keep the suitable turbidity of water using DR 2000 spectrophotometer, the range of turbidity designed for this unit is 25  NUT  after clarifier.

Jar Test

This test is carried out for determination the actual dose of chemicals for obtaining good clarification, the test is occurred using flocculate instrument which include mixers and beakers and dose different values of the chemical in the beakers and start mixers, 20 minutes not good clarification and apply the dose in the water as clarifier.

Final Treatment to Clarifier

Clear water is separated from higher part of clarifier and go to the sand filter to remove any residuals while the settled sludge are sucked from the bottom of the clarifier and discharged to filter press to form cakes. For knowing the quantity of sludge, a settling test is carried out to know the percentage of sludge then sludge pump will operate a definite time to suction the formed sludge: let as the flow rate of sludge pump is 2.2 m3 / h and the quantity of sludge calculated is 1.8 m3 so the time of operation of sludge pum = 1.8 / 2.2 = 0.8 hours = 49 minutes.

Oil System

 

Oil Skimmer

After collecting oily water in oily basin 2 oil skimmer are responsible for the removal of oil on water surface.

After that the unremoved oil will be rejected during another stage which consists of air flotation tank and saturation tank.

 

Air Flotation Tank

Residual oil in water can be removed by pushing air and water during saturation tank then to air flotation tank which has oil scraper that remove the floating oil.

Inlet oil value = 2500 ppm

Outlet oil value = 10 ppm

 

The Oil Test

Determination of oil is carried out in the chemical lab. using separating system and chemical reagent for extraction of oil from water ( 1,1,1 trichloroethane ).

 

 

 

Trouble Shooting

After being checked up thoroughly yet there has some possibility to show malfunctioning in some particular locality of the plant which is appared by the alarming system on the display of PLC. Then the following procedure should be followed up to determine the fault precisely and for the remedy of the fault.

Alarms Receive from PLC

Low Level of PH Value

  1. Verify the exact value of PH on the display screen of PH meter which is the value of PH of flocculation mixing chamber. If the cycle is terminated consider that the lack of injection of caustic soda in the chamber. Verify the quantity of soda in the soda tank.

  2. If the soda injection is regular then verify the PH reading with another portable instrument, if the reading of two meters differs considerably then washout PH probe by taking out from the flocculation chamber.

  3. Before washing probe the water of chamber must be cleaned out. Replace the probe if necessary

High Level of Conductivity

Verify the first surplus valve of high conductivity on the display of conductivity analyzer.
Start up backwashing of sand filters

High Level of Turbidity

Verify the first surplus valve of turbidity on the display of turdbidity analyzer.
Start up backwashing of sand filters

Pump Blocking

  1. Verify the blocking effect of pump.

  2. Verify on the card of sequence that which is the functional phase of the pump and the absence of anything which causes inter blocking.

  3. Verify the correct position selector on the panel.

  4. Verify the occurance of thermic misfire if such a case renews the contact breaker.

  5. If thermic misfire happened before start up the pump by contact breakerdetermine the reasons may be motor blocked or impeller blocked.

  6. Verify the fuse continuation in the panel.

  7. Verify the tension in the power connecting terminal of the pump.

  8. Check up the insertion of cassette of MCC.

 

 

 

 

 

Routine and Preventive Maintenance

Routine Check

The following Check to be done for Better Running of the Plant before each startup:

  1. Check up the availability of water,

  2. Check up the electric tension of each electric equipment separately along with control system,

  3. The availability of chemical reagents,

  4. The availability of service air,

  5. The correct functional status of each instrument and

  6. Check plant pressure in some particular portion where the pressure Is principal issue

Preventive Maintenance

  1. In case of unavailability of waste water keep in idle position all electrical equipment to prevent against any dry running.

  2. Electric tension of each electric equipment should be checked in order to protect against any high or low voltage presence on the terminal point. Also all electrical connections should be verified to avoid any wrong alignment or any short cut of electric cable.

  3. As the plant uses chemical reagents for treatment so it is better to fill up each chemicals tank with respective chemicals in order to prevent against any scarcity of chemicals during operation.

  4. Service air should be available on the terminal point with proper pressure status as to utilize for the pneumatic equipments.

  5. Instruments of the plant should operate perfectly. In case of disorderness of any instrument repairing or replacing should be done.

  6. If it is necessary to stop the plant for any reason then clean up each equipment and tank after being brought up the plant in rest.

Filter Press

The sludge dewatering press is designed to convert the sludge into solid cakes depending on pressuring the sludge inside it with aid of polyelectrolyte dosing ( cationic substance ) and conveying the formed cakes to disposal by belt conveyer.

Note : Filter press sludge from sludge tank, and start filter press according to the quantity of sludge.

Sand Filter

Filling up Procedure for the Sand Filter

The Filter should be filled up according to the following procedure:

  1. Open the bottom manhole and hand hole.

  2. Insert through the bottom manhole the largest size quartz grit (10/20 mm).

  3. Do the same operation with medium size quartz grit (3/5 mm).

  4. Close the manhole, pay attention to the right position of gasket.

  5. Pour through the top hand hole the smallest size quartz grit (0.4/0.8 mm).

  6. Now close that top hand hole.

  7. Before using sand filter a backwashing should be executed in order to clean up inner dirts of filters.
