Introduction

This document covers clarification followed by the filtration of raw water.

Power station needs large quantities of water, raw water supply to power station is a critical factor in the selection of the site.

Raw water is required for three main purposes:

  1. To provide make up water to water/steam cycle.

  2. For cooling of the steam for re-use as a condensate.

  3. General domestic use.

Many natural waters are colored, turbid, and contain suspended solids (silt, clay, etc) and organic matter. The usual form of pre-treatment in power station is the clarification followed by the filtration.

Information

Clarification

Clarification is the term used to encompass coagulation, flocculation and sedimentation. 

Chemical Addition

  1. FeCl3 (Coagulant)

  2. Ca (OH)2

  3. NaOCl
    1, 2, 3 are added to raw water line before mixer and to the clarifier-up flow-type.

 

Reaction

     2 FeCl3 (coagulant) + 3H2 O → 2 Fe (OH)3 + 6 HCl

Ferric hydroxide {Fe(OH)3}are able to coagulate suspended solids present in water and the settling. The quantity of FeCl3 which is necessary.

depends on quantity of suspended solid and jar test determine it.

Ca (OH)2 it is used to neutralize HCl  and reduce temporary hardness

     6 HCl  +  3 Ca(OH)2  → 3 CaCl2 + 6 H2O

    Ca ( HCO3 )2  +  Ca (OH)2 →  Ca CO3 + H2O

       Calcium                Calcium          Calcium

     Bicarbonate            Hydroxide      Carbonate

  

     Mg (HCO3)2  +  Ca(OH)2 → Mg (OH )2 +  CaCO3 + H2O

                 Magnesium         Calcium             Magnesium          Calcium

                Bicarbonate        Hydroxide          Hydroxide           Carbonate

If the proper chemical control is maintained on lime feed, calcium hardness is reduces with benefit for the ion exchange system and increasing the cycle.

  • The addition of Ca (OH)2 and maintain pH = 10, the silica is reduced in filter water.

  • The formation of flocs is better at pH = 10 

  • The use of sodium hypochlorite has the purpose to sterilize the water and to oxidize the organic substances.
