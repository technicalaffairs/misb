Introduction

The thermal and air hazards of Damietta power station resulted from or referred to the harmful effect of exposure of workers in their ( sites of work) to high degree of emitted flue gases more than the permissible value according to the law of environment  (4)  year 1994. Therefore the environmental administration in Damietta power station make a scientific program in order to avoid and protect all the worked persons in the station from the hazards.

Madur GA-40 plus is the microprocessor controlled gas analyser, it is a high quality instrument produced using the latest technical discoveries.

The first draft initiated by chem. Yasser Lotfy Zahra  tested by chem. Mohamed Elsaied Eshra, reviewed by general manager chemist Anis Abou Elez, revised by quality control chemist Wael A. Tantawy at EDEPCo.  And accepted by eng.Monier EL Wakel ,head sector of Damietta P.S.

Information

The GA-40 plus is a multi functional flue gas analyser.

Electrochemical Sensors are used for the measurement of gas concentration.

The instrument can be fitted between 2 and 6 of these sensors.

All analysers are fitted with O2 and CO sensors, further gas cells must be chosen when the instrument is ordered. The GA-40 analyser containing 3 cells, the third one being an NO sensor.

                      Oxygen, O2

 

                      Carbon monoxide Co

 

                      Nitric oxide No

 

                      Carbon dioxide CO2

 

                      Nitrogen oxide NOx

The first three gases (O2,CO,No) are measured directly using the electrochemical cells.The remaining components are calculated.

 

 

 

 

The concentration of oxygen and carbon dioxide are shown in percent. The concentration of the remaining gases is shown as follows:

                     Volume concentration in [ppm],

                     Absolute mass concentration in [mg/m3],

                     Mass concentration relative to the oxygen content in [mg/m3],

                     Mass concentration relative to the energy output in  [mg/MJ].

In addition the air inlet or ambient temperature at a broad range of ambient temperature (0-50oC ) and the flue gas temperature are measured. It is fitted with a rechargeable battery making it independent of the mains power supply.

Element of Gas Circuit

Gas Probe with in–Line Filter- The gas probe is used to extract the gases under investigation and to measure their temperature. The gas probe consists of two parts, the probe pipe with thermocouple (1) and the probe holder (2). As standard a Ni-CrNi thermocouple is used.

 

 

Gas Pump- The internal gas pump draws the gas into the GA-40 plus and passes it through the gas chambers. The gas cells operate best with a flow rate of 90 l/h.

 

 

 

 

 

Gas Chamber- The gas chamber is fitted with between two and six sensors and is characterized by minimal dead capacity.

Basic Principles of Calculating Results

Quantities Obtained from Direct  Measurements(O2,CO, NO, SO2)

The following quantities are obtained by means of direct measurement:

  • flue gas temperature T gas and ambient temperature, expressed in (C).

  • volume concentration of CO (ppm).

  • volume concentration of NO (ppm).

  • volume concentration of SO2 or any ohter optional cell(ppm).

  • volume concentration of O2 (%).

Calculating the Concentration of Carbon Dioxide

 

 

It is calculated on the basis of measured oxygen concentration and the CO2max parameter, characteristic for the given fuel.

Calculating the Concentration of Nitrogen Oxide NOX

In addition to nitric oxide NO, combustion gases contain also higher oxides of nitrogen (mainly NO2), it is possible to calculate  the NO2 contents on the basis of the measured NO. It is generally assumed that nitric oxide NO contained in combustion gases makes up about 95% of the total amount of nitrogen oxides NOX.

                                              0.95

 

 

 

 

 

 

If the optional sensor of the GA-40 plus analyser is the NO2 sensor there is no need to calculate the amount of NO2.Then the NOX concentration is calculated by the analyser as a simple sum of measured NO and NO2 concentrations.

NOX (ppm)  =  NO (ppm) +  NO2 (ppm)

Concentration of " Undiluted " Carbon Monoxide CO undil

 

To make the calculation of the carbon monoxide concentration in combustion gases independent of excess air with which the combustion process is conducted, the idea of  " Undiluted " Carbon Monoxide CO undil was introduced. The value of  CO undil is calculated as follows

CO undil  =  CO . λ

 

Where

 

 CO =  Volume concentration CO (ppm),

 

  λ  =  Excess air number.

Calculating Combustion Parameters

 

Besides calculating gas component concentrations the analyser calculates some parameters describing the combustion process. The most important parameter is the amount of heat convected by combustion gases to the environment- the so-called stack loss SL and calculated on the basic of empirical formula:

SL  = ( Tgas -  Tamb )×( A1/CO2  +  B )

 

Where

 

SL =  stack loss- the percentage  of heat produced in combustion process, which is convected 

         with the combustion gases,

Tgas  =   flue gas temperature,

Tamb  =  the temperature of the boiler inlet air ( it is assumed by the analyser to be the ambient 

             temperature),

CO2  =  the calculated amount of CO2 in combustion gases, expressed in ( % vol ) and

A1, B  =  factors characteristic for a given fuel type.

Beside on the calculated stack loss the analyser estimates the efficiency of the combustion process η ( don't confuse it with boiler efficiency ).

η   =  100%  -  SL

Where:         η = combustion efficiency.

The only quantity decreasing combustion efficiency is stack loss. It is possible to take into account the efficiency reduction caused by incomplete combustion. This loss is represented by a quantity called the loss by incomplete combustion 1L. It determines the percentage of energy loss caused by the presence of flammable gases ( in this case mainly CO ) in the combustion gases. It is calculated on the basis of measured CO concentration in the combustion gases according the following formula:

                           α  × CO %

1L  =  -----------------------

                                  CO %  +  CO2 %

Where

CO, CO2  =  volume concentration of CO and CO2 in the combustion gases,

 

      α  =   the factor specific for a given fuel.

Calculating 1L enables correction of combustion efficiency. Then the corrected efficiency η* is calculated.

η*  =   η  -  1L

The last combustion parameter calculated by GA-40 plus is the excess air factor λ. This factor expresses  how many times the amount of air supplied to the boiler is larger than the minimum amount which is necessary to burn the fuel completely.   Directives

Gas Sensors- The following points are important for maximising the operational life of the cells and achieving measurements without errors.

  • Don't exceed the rated concentration;

  • All the gas cells may react unpredictably to the presence of substances which aren't usually found in flue gases. For this reason, the analyser may not be cleaned with chemical solvents. If organic solvents enter the gas chamber, the cells may become unstable or even suffer permanent damage;

  • Some cells require the presence of a bias voltage when not in use. don't allow the battery to become fully discharged;

  • Don't switch the analyser off before the system is purged of remaining flue gas;

  • Store the analyser in a cool place to reduce the ageing of the cells.

Gas System- The gas system of the analyser is protected by a double in-line filter.

Battery- When the voltage drops below 11 V. an acoustic warning signal is given, the GA-40 plus will switch off automatically if the voltage drops below 10.5 V. The analyser will operate for about 5 hours from the battery. N.B. Some parts of the analyser draw current even when the device is switched off. If the analyser isn't being used then the battery should be charged fully at least once a month.

Service Intervals- The parameters of the electrochemical cells change with time, and therefore service periodically is necessary by re-calibration of the gas sensors using test gases. The recommended service interval is 800 operating hours or 6 months. The operating time and the number of hours until the next service are shown in SERVICE/DEVICE INFO.

Errors during Operation- The analyser checks all circuitry continuously. When the message " ERROR" appear on the screen, the error can be found shown on the screen OPTIONS/SERVICE/CONTROL LIST.

Switching off after Use- The operating life of the electrochemical cells is dependent on the usage of the instrument. The wear and tear on the cells is greater when the cell is exposed to the gas for longer or the concentration is higher. The gas cell is hence "used up" during measurements. For this reason, the analyser shouldn't be switched off until all the gas tubing has been completely cleared of flue gases and the gas cells have been purged with fresh air for several minutes.
