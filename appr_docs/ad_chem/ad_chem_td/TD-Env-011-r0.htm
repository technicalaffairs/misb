Introduction

This document covers  the water treatment  plant of  Ayoun Moussa PS.

The wastewater system collects wastewater from power station in various sumps for treatment. The wastewater is neutralized and treated, then discharged back to the Red Sea via the seal well in the circulating water discharge (DA).

(PLC) is an automatic programmable logic controller system.

The water treatment properties will comply with the requirement of Egyptian Law number (4) for year 1994.

Component Description

The major components of the wastewater system are:

  1. Waste Neutralization Tank
     The neutralization tank accepts chemical cleaning waste and potentially chemical based waste from the condensate polisher area sumps and the demineralizer system waste sump. The contents are neutralized to a pH level of 6-9 with the addition of sulfuric acid (H2SO4) and caustic (sodium hydroxide NaOH). The level switches in the tank, and pH analyzers in the re-circulation line; provide signals to the makeup demineralizer control panel, AN-CP01, which uses the signal in the demineralizer PLC for the waste neutralization transfer  pumps, chemical metering pump controls, and local tank level indication and alarms.

  2. Waste Neutralization Transfer Pump
         Each waste neutralization transfer pump is a 100% capacity pump sized to discharge the contents of a level-full waste neutralizing tank in approximately 5 ¾  hours.

  3. Equalization Basin
    All waste fluids are collected in the equalization basin and mixed thoroughly with two submersible mixers.

  4. Waste Collection Area
    The waste collection area consists of a pre-mix section with floating mixers, two collection basins and a clear well section. Acid and caustic are supplied as required for further neutralization, and coagulant and coagulant aid are added to the premix section.

  5. Vertical Sump Pumps
    Sump pumps in the wastewater system are vertical submerged type pumps. The pumps are manufactured by Ingersoll-Dresser. The pumps are driven by 400 Vac electric motors. All sumps have two 100% pumps (one leading and one lagging). The controls for a sump pump consist of a local hand switch and a ball float type level switch mounted on the base plate (sump cover) for operation. The level control provides a signal to the pump alternator panel, which will start pumps singly in an alternate sequence. If the scheduled pump fails to start, the alternate pump will receive a signal to start. If load demands, both pumps will start. The circulating water pump-house sump pumps are the only exception to this control philosophy. Both pumps feed the oil/water coalescer and will never operate simultaneously.

  6. Coalescer Type Oil/Water Separator
    The coalescer type oil/water separator in the circulating water pump-house area, is sized to process 200 lpm of oily wastes.  The flow capacity of the separator is based on a maximum of one sump pump. The oil/water separator uses coalescing media to separate the oil from the water and then stores the separated oil in an integral oil storage tank.  The oil is then pumped to a truck or drums for disposal off-site.

  7. Air Flotation Type Oil/Water Separator

    Equipment No.                   9-LE-F001

    Operating Capacity            100 M3 /hr

    Operating Pressure            Atmospheric

  8. Parallel Plate Oil/Water Separator

    Equipment No.                   9-LE-F002

    Operating Capacity            100 M3 /hr

    Operating Pressure            Atmospheric

Principles of Operation  

 

Startup

Verify that all sump pumps hand switches are in the AUTO position so that the sump pumps respond to sump level signals.

Normal Operation

Pumps start and stop in response to level signals to maintain tank and sump levels. Where two pumps are installed in one sump, the pumps automatically alternate in operation each time one starts/stops to equalize run time.

During regeneration or a high waste tank level condition, one waste neutralization pump runs continuously, recirculating the water in the waste neutralization tank through the recycle valve.

Shutdown

The sump pumps are not normally shut down (hand switch in the OFF position) except for maintenance. Otherwise, they are allowed to cycle as the sump level requires.

Before shutting down all pumps operating in a sump, ensure that the sumps or equipment discharging to that sump will not require the sump's operation during the shutdown period.

The accumulation of solid waste in the active collection basin will eventually need to be removed. Open the path to the alternate  premix section and close the path leading to the active basin. Allow enough time for the water in the basin to drain into the clear well. Proceed with sludge removal as per the plant procedures.

The sumps receive water from equipment and floor drains. The water is pumped first to the parallel plate oil/water separator (9LE-F002) where the first stage of oil removal takes place. The effluent then flows to the air floatation type oil/water separator  (9LE‑F001) for final  processing.

The following sump pumps deliver wastewater to the air floatation type oil/water separator:

Turbine area for units 1 and 2

Boiler area for units 1 and 2

Fuel oil diked area

Solar oil tank area

Fuel oil pump house

Fire water pump house

Warehouse area

Wastewater is separated from oily wastes and then discharged to the wastewater equalization basin.

The circulating water pump house sumps discharge to the coalescer oil/water separator where the collected oil is stored for off-site disposal and the water is discharged back to the sea via the screen wash system trash basket.

Waste Neutralization

Waste neutralization tank (9-LG-T001) collects wastewater from the water treatment building chemical drain sump and the condensate polisher area sumps.

On a high level signal in the neutralization tank, regeneration of a demineralizer train is interrupted (if in progress), and the contents of the tank are neutralized, then discharged until a low level signal from the tank is achieved.

Waste neutralization is controlled locally at makeup demineralizer control panel (AN‑CP001). Normally, waste neutralization is left in automatic, controlled by the PLC.  The operator can control the system manually, through the PLC, at the local console.

After the regeneration of a demineralizer train, or any time a high level is reached in the neutralization tank, the PLC starts a waste neutralization transfer pump to recirculate tank contents.  The pump takes suction from the tank and discharges the fluid to a common pump discharge header.  An effluent sample line is provided at the pump discharge header to route sample fluid to a pH analyzer and back to the tank.  The pump discharges the fluid through a recycle valve and a recirculating line into the waste neutralization tank recirculation header.  Two educators are used at the recirculation header to promote thorough mixing in the tank.

During recirculation mode, the pH analyzer will continuously check the fluid and send a pH signal to the PLC.  If the pH is within 6.0 to 9.0 (the optimum range for discharge), a dump valve opens and the wastewater is pumped to the equalization basin until a low level is reached in the waste neutralization tank. If the pH is not within the proper range, the neutralization system enters an acid or caustic injection mode and the contents continue to be recirculated.

In the injection mode, an acid or caustic feed pump will be started and the appropriate acid or caustic block valve will open.  This will admit acid or caustic to the appropriate mixing tee as the tank contents are being recirculated.  The acid and caustic block valves are controlled by the PLC.

At the end of the injection mode, the appropriate metering pump is shut down and the block valve is closed.  The neutralization system then re-enters the mixing mode.

After the tank contents have been mixed for the appropriate amount of time, the pH is again checked.  If the pH is acceptable, the pump valve will be opened, the recycle valve closed, and the contents of the tank discharged. If the pH is unacceptable or the pH goes out of range during the pump mode, the injection and mixing process will be repeated until the pH is acceptable and the tank contents discharged.

At the completion of regeneration or whenever the tank is full, and in both cases after an appropriate mixing time has elapsed, the pH will be checked.

If a high level is reached in the tank before a regeneration is completed, the regeneration will be halted and the neutralization cycle performed before the regeneration will be resumed.

 

 

 

 

 

Wastewater Treatment System

The wastewater is collected in the wastewater equalization basin where it is mixed thoroughly by submersible mixers and then pumped to one of two  premix sections in the waste collection area.

In the premix section, waste may be neutralized with sulfuric acid and sodium hydroxide. Sodium  hypochlorite  (NaOCI) is injected in the premix section to control biological growth. The addition of coagulant and a coagulant aid, along with the action of the floating mixers, help suspended solids coalesce into larger particles to allow rapid settling basins. The metering pumps   injecting the coagulant and a coagulant aid are controlled by  the water treatment PLC, which receives a signal from a flow meter downstream of the equalization basin.

The clear well transfer pumps discharge the clear effluent through the pressure sand filters and then to the circulating water seal well and on to the Red Sea.  The pH is continuously monitored and if it exceeds the acceptable limits, it is sent back to the equalization basin.

The solid waste collected must be periodically cleaned out of the collection basin and transported off site for disposal.

Support Systems  

Demineralize System

The demineralize system supplies acid and caustic to the day tanks water premix section for pH control in the waste neutralization tank and the waste.

Circulating Water System

The circulating water discharge seal well accepts the treated waste water and discharges it to the sea.

Sodium Hypochlorite Injection System

The sodium hypochlorite injection system supplies sodium hypochlorite  in the premix section to control biological growth.

Instrument Air System

The compressed air system provides clean, dry air to operate valves and controllers within the wastewater system.

Electrical Power

The low voltage (400 V) distribution system provides power for the pumps, mixers, and other equipments.
