Introduction

The method compares the intensity of light scattered by the sample with the intensity of light scattered by a standard reference sample (formazine polymer suspension) under defined conditions. The higher the intensity of light scattered at 90°C to the path of incident light, the higher the turbidity. Turbidity can be determined for any aqueous sample that is free of debris and rapidly setting materials.

The most common interference are dirty glassware, air bubbles in the sample, vibration that disturb the surface of the sample and color due to dissolved substances.

Instrument Description

  • The model 6035, turbidity measurement is suitable for use in the field or the laboratory, water resistant with rechargeable batteries (6xNi/cad).       

  • The unit operates over the range of 0.00 to 199.9 N.T.U. with resolution of 0.1or 0.01.

  • Read out is via a 3½ digit LCD.

  • Three Glass Filters ( green, blue and red )are available for color correction.

  • Samples and Standards are presented in capped cells to minimize the contamination.

  • Photo detector: Silicon diode with enhanced blue response.

  • Light source: Tungsten filament light source 5V. 230 mA.

 

 

 

 

 

Information      

Displays/Controls

  • Main display: 3½ digit LCD.

  • Range keypad: This switch read with the decimal point indicating the selected range, also set the ca/value.

  • Read keypad: Initiates measurement sequence, the display will clear for 3 seconds and then reading.

  • I/0 keypad: On / Off  for the unit, three short tones will sound before display.

  • Zero keypad: Calibrates to the theoretical value for pure water - 0.02 N.T.U.

  • Cal key pad: CAL is displayed when the key is pressed, the unit will calibrate to 5.00 N.T.U if the pq keypad is used to change the value. When the read keypad is pressed the unit will calibrate to the new value.

  •  pq  keypad: Lower range 0.20 to 19.99 N.T.U. Upper range 2.0 to 199.9 N.T.U.

  • Datalogging keys: These keypads are inoperable if option is not fitted.

Directives

Operation

Prior to sample measurement the following points should be observed:

Standards

The unit provided with 5 N.T.U. standard have an indefinite shelf life. The shelf life is 9 months when opened, it must be kept out of freezing, contamination. It is possible to use Formazine as a calibration standard. Recent studies have found that formazine  carcinogenic. If formazine is used, all appropriate precautions must be taken to prevent exposure to health risks.

 

 

THE USE OF FORMAZINE IS NOT RECOMMENDED

Sample Measurement

To obtain optimum performance the followings should be observed:

  • Calibration standard close to the expected analyte.

  • Use only clean, dust and scratch free sample cells.

  • Ensure the cell is filled to the neck.

  • External surface should be free from fingerprints and moisture.

  • Air bubbles should be removed by gentle inversion.

  • A filter ( blank or colored ) should be fitted into the filter compartment at all times.

  • Pure filtered water should be used. For general measurements the use of a deionised water is satisfactory.

  • During use the lamp is only switched on when the sample is being measured thus helping to conserve battery life.

Measurement Turbidity

  •  Switch the unit on by pressing the I/0 keypad. The display will read and three tones will sound, select the required range by use of range keypad.

  • Prepare a zero solution and place it in a clean turbidity cell, place it in the sample compartment and close the sample area lid. Ensure the etched line on the glass of turbidity cell is in line with the marker (0).

  • Press the Zero keypad, the display will clear for 3 seconds prior to a reading displayed, a single tone will sound at the start.

  • Remove the zero solution from the sample chamber.

  • Prepare a calibration standard and place in a clean turbidity cell, place into the sample compartment.

  • Press the Cal keypad, a single tone will sound and the display will read calibration. If required, the pq keypads should be set to the desired  CAL value.

  • Press the Read  keypad to initiate the measurement sequence, a single tone will sound and the display will clear for 3 second, a tone will sound at the end of the measurement sequence.

Maintenance

The model 6035 has been designed to give optimum performance with minimum maintenance, it is necessary to keep the external surfaces clean and dust free, the sample

area should be kept clean at all times to ensure accurate readings, when not use the unit should be stored in carrying case.

 

Notes

  1. When battery replacement is carried out any previous calibration data is lost.

  2. To recharge the battery, the battery charger should be connected to the unit via the power socket and switched on.

  3. It is recommended that batteries should only charged for maximum of 5 hours.

  4. The replacement of light source is available from the local distributor if the light source has failed.
