Introduction

Oily waste water may contain paraffin, kerosene, petrol, diesel oil, fuel oil, other oils and lubricants, this waste capable of polluting surface or underground water where it has a harmful and toxic effects where it forms a film interferes with the re–oxygenation and natural purification of surface water. So, it is why waste water has to be strictly protected against the risk of contamination by meaning of water separator unit  to treat the waste that contains oils, fuels and solids  in Damietta Power Station which is designed to treat 66 m3/h, with peaks of 168 m3/h, of waste water.

Information

Process Description

API Separator: In this separator there are

  • A mechanical removal process of the oil particles or emulsified oil floating on the surface.

  • A sedimentation process of the solids by coagulation of fine suspended matter with formation of sludge, which is discharged to the clarifier sludge collection sump for water /sludge separation.

Induced Air Floatation

  • It is mainly used to remove the residual oil and solids still present in the waste after the pretreatment process in the API Separator.

  • The separation process takes place in a unit provided with four equal cells in series.

Flow Description

  1. The oil contaminated waste water coming from the drains of Modules 1, 2 and 3 gas and steam turbine areas and fuel oil  treatment skids area is collected  in the three steam  turbine sump basins and from these sumps delivered to the API Separator through the pumps 1,2,3 LG-P-22A/B.

  2. According to the waste water flow measured by the flow transmitter FT-09 the two flow controllers FC–09 A/B will regulate the dosing of Alum and coagulant aid to be delivered into the API Separator by the dedicated pumps of the Chemical  Feed Subsystem.

  3. The required plant supply usually ranges from 23 to 66 m3/h with  peaks of  130 m3/h and eventual maximum peaks of 168 m3/h.

First Stage of the Treatment

The first static stage of oil removal by flotation and solids removal  by  sedimentation is effected in the API Separator by allowing sufficient settling time also for the highest flow rates.

The settled solids (sludge) and the floated oil are scraped by means of the chain rake Z-03, which scrapes both the bottom and the surface of its 12 meter long tank located inside the API Separator.

The sludge collected in the bottom of the separator are delivered through  the sump pump P-24A/B to the Clarifier Sludge Collection  Sump of the Sludge Processing Subsystem.

The skimmed oil collected in the steel basin of chain rake is discharged  through the oil skimmer by gravity into the Waste Oil  Reservoir T-12.

Second Stage of the Treatment

It is effected in the Induced Air Flotation unit Z-04, which has the function to remove the residual oil up to 10 ppm after the first stage.
This unit can handle sudden maximum flows of 168 m3/h for 15 minutes. The pretreated waste water is delivered from the API Separator to the Induced  Air Flotation unit by the water separator transfer pumps p-25 A/B. A/B.
The pretreated waste water with added coagulant aid enters the unit from the inlet compartment and then goes through the four cells in series.
The liquid in each cell is circulated through the agitator impeller Z-04B (C) (D) (E) two or three times before passing to the next cell ( when running at rated flow rate with a four minutes residence time).

The impeller located in the central zone of each cell rotates creating a vortex and a negative pressure which drains air (gas) down  the stand pipe in the top center area entraining it in the liquid and forcing the air rich liquid through out lateral zones of the cell.

The conical shroud of the agitator and vanes redirect the motion of this two phase liquid in a radial pattern. This insures even distribution to all parts of the cell and prevents a circular flow pattern from being established on the lateral  surfaces of the cell which would tend to draw the oil and / or solids to the  center of the cell instead of moving them to the edges of the cell where they  can be skimmed.

Once the two phase liquid enters the lateral zone of the cell the air is released as finely disseminated bubbles.

Under proper conditions, usually imposed by the addition of a polyelectrolyte, the contaminating oils and / or solids adhere to the bubbles.

As the bubbles are entrained they have a potential energy which would  normally be released  by expanding as they rise to the surface.

The theory behind this type of flotation is to contact the bubbles in their high  potential energy stage with the contaminates in order that they can give up  their  potential energy in adhering to the contaminating  particles.

This contact takes place in the mixing and flotation zones. The polyelectrolyte changes the charges of the particles and further enhances and promotes this  adherence.

It is necessary to make sure all the liquid entering the unit has the maximum  exposure to flotation zone, this accomplished by the recirculation pattern and  by the still well which provides a "sweeping" action across the bottom of the cell which is important because the liquid passes under the baffles which divide each of the compartments.

It has been calculated that the liquid in each cell is circulated two to three times through the contact and flotation zones  before passing to the next cell (when running at rated flow rate with four minutes residence time ).
Since the four cells are running in series, the contaminants have 8 to 12  opportunities to be contacted with the air (gas) bubbles.

As previously mentioned, flow through the unit is under flow, each cell being  separated by a baffle which is open at the bottom. The baffle also extends out  of the liquid to the top of the tank interior. This prevents the oil and / or solids  from migrating from one cell to the next.

Each cell removes 75% of  the contaminants which enter that cell and therefore each cell is progressively cleaner than the previous cell. From the outlet compartment of unit Z-04 the clean waste water is discharged to the Lined Pond Basin.

Third Stage of the Treatment

It's water/oil separation consists in separating the residual water from oil.

This stage is manually performed, when the H.L. alarm is operated in the Waste Oil Reservoir, discharging the separated water with the help of the sample valves located along the vertical side of the vessel.

When the vessel is full of oil, this can be manually transferred to disposal or by means of pump P-26. The possibility of recovered oil recirculation to API Separator is provided through pump P-26.

Chemical Treatment

Coagulant Feed System

The purpose of coagulation is to enable the fine colloidal matter present in water to be collected into the form of a bulky precipitate which settles more readily. So, Alum is used by injecting the API Separator by dedicated pumps ( 2x100%) dosing pumps and one dosing tank located on Chemical Feed Subsystem.

Coagulant Aid Feed System

  1. Where a synthetic organic polymers of polyacrylamide type  (polyelectrolyte) are used.

  2.  These polymer molecules are irreversibly adsorbed on the solid particles at several adsorption sites.

  3. Other portion of the molecule extending into the bulk of the solution. These portions are then adsorbed onto other solid particles forming a polymer bridge. This leads to increase the rates of sedimentation.

  4. The expected consumption chemicals, referred to the max. load are the following:

          AL2 (SO4)3 18.H2O   (20% wt)       1584 Kg/day

          Coagulant Aid            (100% wt )    17.6 Kg/day

Protective Maintenance

 

Routine Chemical Test

There are many tests carried out on the water produce from the API Separator and from the final treatment of the unit to obtain high quality water. These tests are

 

pH Value

The pH is measured in the final discharge basin and in chemical lab. using  pH meters designed for this purpose. The suitable pH value for coagulation process was found to be (6-9).

Turbidity Value

The turbidity is measured by using Turb 550 (WTW). Turbidimeters to keep it in permissible limits.

 

 

Total Suspended Solids

The total suspended solid is measured by using an ashless filter paper, previously dried at 105 oC and weighed. Dry at 105oC for about 2 hours until  the weight is constant, then determine ( T.S.S ), the range of T.S.S  is 10 ppm.

The Oil Test

The oil is determined in the chemical lab. using separating funnel and chemical reagents for extraction of oil from water (petroleium ether).

Maintenance Instructions for Quadricell Flotation Separator

 

 

Lubrication

  1. Mechanism bearings: lubricate every 3000 hours with grease N. 2 ( AGIP GRMU-EP2, IP ATESIA CR2, or equivalent). First filling = 0.31 kg. each. Consumption = ~ 4 gm./100h each or the 8 bearings

  2. Skimmer gearmotors: replace oil every 3000 hours, oil viscosity 33.5º E at 50ºC (AGIP BLASIA 450, IP MELLANA 460, or equivalent). First filling = 0.6 I each. Consumption = 0.005 1/100 h each of the 2 gearmotors. Check oil level every 50 days

  3. Skimmer bearings: grease with a high grade silicon grease every 3000 hours. First filling = 50 gm. each.
    Consumption = ~ 1.5 gm./100 h each of the bearings.

  4. Electric motors : lubricate per manufacturer standard.

  5. Skimmer blades: adjust for clearance between blades and level bars from 1mm. to 3 mm. as bearing wear.

  6. Nuts and bolts: check for tightness every 30 days.
