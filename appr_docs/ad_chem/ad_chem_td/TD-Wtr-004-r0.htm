Introduction

Sodium hypochlorite is chlorine derivatives formed by reaction of chlorine with hydroxide. Sodium hypochlorite as chlorine gas is used for:

  • Disinfections,

  • Control of microorganism,

  • Control of taste and odor,

  • Color reduction and

  • Destruction of organic matter.

 Information

Sodium hypochlorite produces hypochlorite ion and hypochlorous acid, as chlorine dose

                                NaOC1     →       OC1-   +      Na +

                               

                                 Sodium            Hypochlorite   Sodium

                             Hypochlorite                 Ion           Ion                                                   

                                 OC1-      +     Na+       H2O →       HOC1      +     NaOH

                          Hypochlorite        Sodium   Water       Hypochlorous        Sodium
                                Ion                    Ion                             acid               Hydroxide

The reaction of hypochlorites and water form both hypochlorous acid and Sodium hydroxide, this causes little change in pH. Hypochlorous acid and hypochlorite ion are oxidizing agents.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Calculation

The dose of hypochlorite to pre-treatment may vary from 1 ppm (1gm/m3) to some. And is determined by organic content, and is regulated in such away that free chlorine content at inter of demi plant not greater than 0.3 ppm. The operation of is automatically control by redox meter after sand filter and tested colorimetric every one hour by operator.

NaOCl is diluted to 2% (8 gm/l) ≈ 60 l to 300l

Flow rate of solution to be dosed

2 gm/m3 - 35 m3.h  (flow rate. raw water )  ≈  8.7 l/h
                     8 gm /l

Directives

  • Sodium Hypochlorite must be stores away form heat and sunlight.

  • Sodium Hypochlorite is diluted by demi-water to avoid precipitation.

  • Sodium Hypochlorite is avoided mixing with acid and FeC13 to prevent rapid generation of chlorine gas.

  • Wearing safety clothes and tools when contact with it.

  • Oxygen cylinder must be near to preparation area.

Steps

Industrial grade of NaOC1 is supplied to station as a liquid with concentration 10-15%

  1. Loading 60l concentrated NaOC1 to be dilated in dilution tank (300l).

  2. Check the concentration by using hydrometer after dilution.

  3. Inject NaOC1 to raw water line by using diaphragm pump (capacity 10l/hr).
