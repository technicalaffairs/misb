Introduction        

This instrument is used for measuring the sound level in different sites in the station, this measurement must be performed every one week.

The first draft initiated by Chemist: Gihan Abd Elsamee Elsaiad; reviewed by department director Chemist Maha.M.S Hamoda; revised by quality control chemist: Wael A. Tantawy at EDEPC, and accepted by eng. Awad  Mohammed Fathy;  Head sector of Talkha power station, and certified by eng. Aly Mostafa Al Lethy EDEPC inspection & quality control head sector.

Information      

Features

  • Large LCD display, easy to read.

  • Frequency weighting networks A&C weighting & Time weighting (Fast/Slow) are designed to meet the IEC651 type 1

  • AC output for system expansion.

  • Build in CALIBRATION VR on front panel.

  • Changeable condenser microphone for high accuracy & long –term stability.

  • Max. Hold function for stored the maximum value on display.

  • Build max. hold reset switch.

  • Warning indicator for over and under load.

  • LCD display for low power consumption & clear read – out even in bright ambient light condition.

  • Used the durable, long – lasting components, including a strong, light weight ABS – plastic housing case.

  • Low battery indicator.

125 Hz - + 1 dB, 250 Hz - + 1 dB,

500 Hz - + 1 dB, 1 kHz - + 1 dB,

8 kHz - + 1.5 dB to – 3 dB

The characteristic is near the "FLAT" Response. Typical, it is suitable for checking the noise of machinery (Q.C.check) & knowing the sound pressure level of the tested equipment.

Measuring  Procedure

  

          1. Slide the "Power off / On selector " (3 –10, Fig 1 ) to the "On " position.

0 = Off,   1 = On

2. For the instant display reading, slide the "Max. hold & Instant display selector" (3 – 4, Fig.1) to the " Instant " position. General  application  always select the "Instant" function. For the "Max. hold" function, please refer to the Max. Hold.

3. Slide the A/C weighting Selector (A/C weighting selector, Fig.1) to" A" or "C" Position for sound level measuring.
 

NOTE 1

  1. The characteristic table of A,C weighting, please ref. NOTE 3.

  2. The characteristic of A weighting is simulated as the "Human Ear Listening " response. Typically, if making the environmental sound level measurement, always select the A weighting typically.

  3. The C weighting characteristic is near the "FLAT " response. Typically, it is suitable for checking the noise of machinery ( Q.C. check) & Knowing the real sound level of the tested equipment.

4. Determine proper measuring range by selecting the "Range selector" in Fig1 to minimize the tolerance of readout. When left corner of LCD  show "5"  indicator, is upper or lower setting in Fig 1.Slide range selector to other range for measuring.

5. According on various measuring sound source, select the" Time weighting selector " in Figure 1  to " F" or "S" position.

Typically select the "Time weighting selector" to " F " position.

6. Hold the instrument in hand and point the microphone at measured noise source, the sound level will be displayed on " dB " ( decibel ) unit.

7. Max. hold

During the sound level measurement, if intend to hold the maximum (peak) value on display, slide "Max. hold & Instant display selector" in Fig.1 to the "Max. hold" position.
 

NOTE 2

  1. During the operation of "Max. hold function" Push" Rest button for Max. hold function " in Fig.1 once a while will rest the max. peak value.
     

  2. IF not use the "Max. hold" function, it should select the "Max.hold & instant display selector " in Fig.1 to the " Instant " position.

Directives

 

Measuring Consideration  

 

  1. Please should select the proper weighting selector (A weighting or C weighting). The A weighting selector will be engaged normally.

  2. Please select proper measurements range to minimize the tolerance of readout.

  3. Please don’t keep or operate the instrument at high temperature & humidity environment for a long period.

  4. Keep microphone dry & avoid serious vibration.

  5. For general application of sound dB measurement, we recommend select the panel switch to the following position:

 Time Weighting ( Fast / Slow ) selector

         Select  to  " F "

 Max . hold & Instant display selector

           Select   to  " Instant "

 

 A/C Weighting selector

         Select  to  " A  weighting "

 

 Range selector

          Select  to  " 30 – 70 dB " or " 60 – 100 dB ".

 

 Power off / On / Internal calibration selector

          Select  to  " 1 " ( On).

Signal Output

The instrument is provided an 3.5 mm Dia. "Phone signal output terminal" ( A/C Weighting selector in Fig.1) for connecting with analyzer, level recorder, tape recorder, controller …etc.    

Replacement of Battery

  1. When the left corner of LCD display show "BAT ", it is necessary to replace the battery. However, in – spec. measurement may still be made for several hours after "Low battery indicator" appears before the instrument become inaccurate.

  2. Slide the "Battery Cover" in Fig.1 & take the battery away from the battery compartment.

  3. Replace with 2 PCs 9V battery (heavy duty type) and reinstate the cover.

Calibration

The sound level meter is built in the internal calibration "Calibration VR" ( Calibration VR (Accuracy adjust VR), Fig.1) on the front panel. Please according the following procedures to calibrate the instrument accurately, if it is necessary.

Calibrated via External SOUND CALIBRATOR

1. Prepare the optional "SOUND CALIBRATOR" such as "SC–940 A" or "SC–941". Power on the Sound Calibrator & plug calibrator output into the" Electric condenser microphone " head in Fig.1 of the Sound Level Meter.

2. Slide the "Range Selector" in Fig.1 to " 60–100 dB " position.

3. Slide "Time Weighting Selector" in Fig.1 to " F " position.

4. Slide the "A/C Weighting Selector " in Fig.1 to "A weighting" position.

5. Carefully adjust the "Calibration VR" ( Calibration VR (Accuracy adjust VR), Fig.1) with"–" screw driver (include accessory), until the display read within " 94.0 + 0.2 " dB.

Calibrated via Internal 94 dB Generator  

Sound level meter is built in the internal standard 94 dB/1 KHz square wave generator for the purpose to calibrate come certain amplifier circuit ( not the complete meter system). Please according the following procedures to calibrate the instrument before making operation, if the instrument not in use for a long time.

The calibration method are similar as above in Calibrated via External SOUND CALIBRATOR, but not connect the external sound calibrator (SC–941A, SC–941...), the other should slide "Power off / On / Internal calibration selector" in Fig.1 to the "CAL. "position". Carefully adjust the "Calibration VR" position "VR" ( Calibration VR (Accuracy adjust VR), Fig.1 ) with " – " screw driver (included accessory), until the display read within " 94.0 + 0.2 " dB.

 NOTE 3

If intend to make calibration accurately of whole meter, it is recommended to use the calibrating procedures of "Calibrated via External SOUND CALIBRATOR.
