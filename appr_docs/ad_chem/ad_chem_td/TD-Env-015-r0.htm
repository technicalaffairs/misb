Description of Equipment

The plate separator consists of

  1. CPS basin; it set into its concrete pit, it should be installed with at least 9'0" of overhead clearance to facilitate the removal of the CPI pack.

  2. Corrugated plate interceptor (CPI) pack.

  3. Bucket oil weir.

  4. The adjustable effluent weir.

  5. Coalescing media.

The oil separator receive the effluents pumped from various points of the power plant.

General Operation

The flow through the system will be by gravity. The influent head is at least 12" above the height of the CPS effluent weir. The influent enters the gravity separation section where heavy solids drop out. The flow then proceeds through polypropylene prefilter media before passing through the corrugated plate interceptor. Separated oil flows into the adjusted oil trough to out, the clean water flows over the effluent weir into a water sump, from which it can flow by gravity to the evaporation pond. If oil level exceed the limits specified, flow will be diverted back to the holding pond.

CPS Operation

The influent overflows the inlet weir into a quiescent zone located ahead of the CPI pack. In this area the liquids velocities are slowed. The lighter gravity liquid rises to the peaks of the corrugations and continues to the rises until reaching the gutters at the inlet of the pack, and the heavier liquid flows out the lower portion of the pack to the effluent trough. If any solids are present, these solids collect in the valleys of the corrugations and are collected in gutters located  at the pack outlet. These gutters direct the solids to the bottom of the separator basin.

Sludge Withdrawal

Sludge buildup should be monitored closely, especially immediately after startup until experience is gained. Solids buildup should not be allowed  to reach bottom of the CPI pack.

Sludge buildup should be monitored  by observing the discharge from the sludge removal pump.

In removing sludge, short intervals of withdrawal are superior to one long withdrawal. The short intervals allow the sludge to resettle  and therefore, help prevent "rat-holing".

Draining

Prior to draining it is necessary to remove the oil layer so as not to contaminate the effluent side of the basin.

Oil removal can be accomplished by lowering the bucket weir or by closing off the effluent so that the water level rises, forcing the oil layer into the oil bucket.

Maintenance Instructions for Corrugated Plate Separator 

 

Periodic

If the sludge level is properly maintained, no routine  maintenance is required on the CPS,  because there are no moving parts.

Supports systems such as oil withdrawal, sludge withdrawal, heaters or feed pump should be maintained in accordance with the instructions for those specific units.

Periodic visual inspection of the unit is recommended. This inspection should check for leaks, and determine whether the unit appears to be functioning properly, i.e. clean water flow over the effluent weir and oil over into the bucket weir.

 

 

 

 

Pack Cleaning

Operating experience has shown that the smoothness of the corrugated plates generally prevents oils and solids from accumulating on their surfaces. If dirt and heavy oil do buildup within the pack or packs, they will require cleaning as this will reduce the efficiency of the unit.

The preferred method is to clean the packs in place. After the unit has been drained, a high pressure hose can be employed to wash down the plates. Prior to draining it is necessary, of course, to remove the oil layer. This is can  accomplished by lowering the bucket weir height.

An alternative method is to remove the packs. Before attempting to lift the pack, the units should be hosed down, the CPI structure won't support the weight of heavy solids during lifting bar through the two lifting lugs.

When putting the unit back in service after draining, always fill with clean water first.

Caution: Do not drain the separator until all of the oil has been removed.
