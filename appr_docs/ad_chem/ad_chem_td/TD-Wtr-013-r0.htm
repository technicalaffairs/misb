Introduction

Sea water is of high salinity caused by dissolved ions in water. The main role of desalination plant is the removal of salts from sea water producing desalinated water which becomes suitable for using or for consecutive treatment.

This document covers the acid clean of the desalination plant of Al-Arish power station.

The cleaning is accomplished by spraying the acid solution over the tubes of one group by using the feed pump as the acid recirculation pump; the pump at the mixing tank is used only for transferring the solution to the process unit.

The acid will not only attack the surface of the scale but also will enter cracks and porous areas of the scale and loosen the bond between the scale and the tube surface. This will cause flaking off of the scale. Both time and acid will be saved if all accumulation of loose scale and sludge are removed from the evaporator not only prior to acid cleaning but also after an initial period of acid circulation.  The scale also should be removed from the feed pump discharge filter. It is essential to trap the loose scale particles to prevent spray nozzles clogging during acid cleaning.

The cleaning process is very important which return the desalination plant to its maximum efficiency giving the design product flow (25 m2/h).

Information

Desalination plant of AL-Arish power station is of type low temperature, horizontal tube, falling film, multi effect unit (with vapor recycle), consisting mainly of 6 heat recovery (evaporator) and 1 heat rejection (condenser).

The desalination unit is based on the multi effect distillation process with a vapor recycle system. The multiple effects are achieved in a series of horizontal tubes, falling film type evaporative condensers. These serve as heat recovery effects, where in through successive steps of evaporation and condensation at equilibrium vacuum conditions, multiple amounts of distillate [product] are derived from given quantity of low temperature steam input.

The train of evaporative condenser units is divided into two groups of 3 effects each, for piping and feed pumping purposes. These groups are here after referred to as the "hot" and "cool" groups, according to their location in the evaporator train, the cool group is adjacent to the main condenser and the hot group is being the opposite end.

Each effect evaporator contains 1734 tubes in each of the four effects at the two sides of the vessel and 1645 tubes in each of the two effects assembled in the middle of the vessel. The tubes outside diameter are 30 mm by 2.48 m long, and made of "titanium" alloy. The tubes are sealed in tube plates at the front [inlet] end, and the rear [outlet] end by rubber grommets.

The condenser contains a single horizontal bank of tubes. Consists of 330 tubes of 30 mm outside diameter, 0.5 mm wall thickness titanium alloy tubes.

This unit produces 600 ton/day and the (T.D.S) of distillate less than 20 ppm and temperature is 35oC and the economy ratio is 7.5/1[ ton product per ton steam ].

Formation of Scale

Since the process unit is designed for low temperature operation. The rate of scale deposition on the heat tubes is inherently low.

This factor plus the proper use of a scale inhibitor makes the need for cleaning usually once a year is sufficient. Any scale deposit that formed usually consists of acid soluble carbonates which can be removed by recirculation of a solution of sulphamic acid, (which is a mild acid compatible with the metal parts of the evaporator). The scale normally forms more slowly on the top rows of tubes than on the lower tubes and also less in the cool group than in the hot group of effects.

Reagents Used in Cleaning Process

  • An adequate quantity of sulphamic acid powder.

  • A supply of readily accessible clean fresh water.

  • pH test paper [litmus] and an acid titration kit.

  • A supply of unslaked line [calcium oxide, CaO] for neutralizing spent acid before discharging it to the waste.

 

Directives

 

 

Preparation of Tubes

Before acid cleaning, the need for cleaning should be verified by actual visual inspection of tubes, this can be done by entering the vessel through the access door; each vessel should be examined starting at the hot end [effect No.1] as the condenser and some of the cooler effects may not require cleaning.

Prior to starting the cleaning procedure, have on hand all the materials and equipment required for the entire acid cleaning operation, which should include the following:

 

  • Safety glasses and rubber gloves should be worn while handling acid.

  • Wearing of a rubber apron and rubber boots is also recommended.

  • Dusting of powder can irritate the nose and eyes.

  • If powder is spilled on the skin or clothing it should immediately be deducted of and rinsed with water.

  • Splashing of acid solution on personnel should like wise be rinsed of immediately with water.

  • Although sulphamic acid in powder form presents little hazard, care should be exercised in its handling and the use of the dry powder in contact with moist skin may cause some irritation for this reason.

An approximation of the total quantity of acid needed can be made from the measured scale thickness on the lower tubes. Pick off pieces of scale from 5 lower tubes and measure their thickness to obtain an average. For each 0.1 mm of average thickness on the lower tubes of an effect; approximately "700" kg of acid per vessel will be required to dissolve the scale completely due to fewer tubes, less acid is required to clean the condenser, which may need cleaning very rarely about "50" kg of acid for each 0.1 mm of average scale thickness at the lower tubes of the condenser.

The above quantity of acid is based on an assumption that the average thickness of the scale for the tubes will be only half the average for the bottom tubes.

The total amount of acid is a very rough approximation; monitoring pH of the solution gives a more accurate acid dosage.

Obviously, manual removal of flaked off loose scale will effectively reduce these quantities of acid considerably. Allowing such loose scale to remain in the system during acid cleaning will consume the acid more rapidly, require larger quantities to be used and take longer time to complete the cleaning. All dirt and loose scale on the bottom of the vessel should be cleaned prior to the introduction to the vessel.

As carbon dioxide [CO2] gas is generated by the scale reaction with the acid. The vessel being cleaned should be adequately vented manholes and viewing port should be opened.

All valves in the non condensable gases "NCG" removal system should be opened. If excessive foaming is encountered during acid recirculation, a small amount of antifoam agent such as silicones emulsion or surfactant like atlas G-3910 can be added to the acid solution.

Prior to charging acid solution into the recirculation circuit. Lower the water level to minimum [about10] above bottom of vessel in the effect to be cleaned. Too high water level will result in excessive dilution of the fresh acid solution.

The spent acid solution after complete the acid cleaning should be neutralized by [calcium oxide, CaO] before discharging it to the waste.

Preparation of Acid Solution

 

 

The acid solution is prepared in the 1m3 mixing tank in a concentration of about 20% by weight in fresh or sea water. Prepare the solution as follows:

  1. Fill the mixing tank about 2/3 full of water and start the mixer.

  2. Add 200 kg of sulphamic acid powder into the mixing tank.

  3. Add water to the mixing tank to bring level up to 1000 liter work.

  4. Continue mixing until all acid is dissolved, then stop the mixer.

  5. If a flexible steam pipe is available, this process can go faster by heating the water up to 50oC maximum.

Acid Circulation

  1. Prior to starting the acid circulation, open all the valves of the vapour to ( NCG ) removal system. Check that the vacuum break valve is open.

  2. Arrange the valve position for the particular group of effects to be cleaned. If number of groups is to be cleaned it is advisable to start with the hottest group of those to be cleaned. Don't clean more than one group of effects at the same time.

  3. Open the valves to and from one of the filters on the feed pump discharge line. Open the valve on the top of the filter, for air and carbon dioxide release.

  4. Start product pump for mechanical seals cooling circulation. Start recirculation the sea water through the group to be cleaned by starting feed pump and setting the flow to 80 m3/hr.
     

  5. Open the valve on suction line from acid tank to acid pump. Start up acid pump and then open discharge valve. Open inlet and outlet valves to strainer.

  6. Transfer the acid solution from the acid mixing tank to the feed pump suction of that specific unit.

  7. When all the acid solution of the mixing tank was transferred, stop acid pump then prepare a new acid solution.

  8. Continue to add acid until the pH is lower than 3 and steady.

  9. As soon as the above occurs stop adding acid and continue the circulation.

  10. Monitor pH every 1/2 hour. When the pH rises above "4" add another batch of a new acid solution. If the height of the acid solution is higher than 1m, stop adding the acid. Recirculate solution until pH rises to 6 and discharge the solution so that its height is 0.3m from the bottom. Do this by either discharging the excess solution to the next group of effects, or by stopping the feed pump, and open the valve to the brine pump suction. Start up the brine pump and open the discharge valve. Since the solution will be discharged within few minutes, be careful to monitor the discharge pressure, flow and liquid level so as not to allow the pump to run dry.  

  11. Start up the feed pump and acid transfer pump and continue as before from step 3.

  12. If two consecutive pH reading are below pH "4" and for at least one hour this signifies that the cleaning process is completed.

  13. As the solution in the clean vessel has some active acid it can be used for cleaning the next group of effects. Before transferring the solution to the new group take a sample after filter and check, if there are no large scale particles in the solution. They may clog the nozzles. If they exists don't use the solution and drain it out. Drain the next group to be cleaned through the brine pump by opening the appropriate valve. Fill the solution to the next group and circulate it until the pH reaches pH "6". Drain the spent solution, fill clean sea water, flush the new group and drain till 10 water remain.

  14. In case the condenser should be acid cleaned, the feed pump will circulate the acid solution through the condenser. Close the feed valves to each of the cold group effects, the hot group isolating valve and sea water in control valve. As there are two outlets from the condenser, one to the feed pumps suction and the second to the brine out pump suction; open the two valves connecting the brine out and the feed pump suction lines to direct the solution drained to the brine pump, back to the feed pump suction. Proceed with the condenser cleaning according to the procedure described above for evaporator vessel cleaning.

Completion of Cleaning Procedure

  1. If the acid concentration levels off and remains constant for at least an hour at some point greater than 2%, it could signify that the cleaning process is complete.

  2. Cleanliness of the evaporator should be confirmed by visual inspection of the lower tubes thought a view port. If in doubt, a more thorough internal inspection should be made through an access opening. But not before the vessel has been flushed with clean sea water and thoroughly ventilated.

  3. If the tubes are clean, the feed and brine valves should be realigned for circulation through the next vessel to be cleaned and the recirculation continued.

  4. Use same procedure for recharging and recirculation as for previous vessel.

  5. When cleaning of the last vessel has been completed the acid should be neutralized before disposal through the brine outfall. This is done by adding unslaked lime [CaO] to the solution using the same procedure as for recharging the acid. The amount of lime to be added will depend on the residual concentration of acid and the number of vessel cleaned; if necessary add sea water to the system through control valve to bring all water levels up to normal, thereby further diluting the acidity.

  6. Recirculate the neutralized solution through all vessels that have undergone acid cleaning. Flush them simultaneously by opening the feed valves as necessary.
     

  7. Check the pH of the recirculation water with calibrated litmus paper or a pH meter. When sufficiently neutralized to meet local regulations, start flushing the vessel by circulation of sea water as for start up.

  8. Increase water circulation rate to the maximum and continue flushing for at least one hour before starting the process. As a further precaution, once production has started by pass the product to the brine discharge for at least one hour after acceptable salinity is indicated.

  9. If the unit is not to be put in operation  immediately after acid cleaning, continue the above flushing procedure for at least two additional hours before shutting down, as a precaution before shutting down, make sure that the pH of the brine discharge is the same as that of the incoming sea water [ at least 8 ].

  10. Check pH of the mechanical seals cooling water in product surge volume due to possible leak in mechanical seals, acid might enter the cooling system. If pH is below 5, replace the water in the product pump circulation.
