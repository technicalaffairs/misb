Introduction

According to the directives of the Central Chemical Laboratories this practice covers the preparation of Metallographic Specimen for failure analysis.

 

Failure investigation and subsequent analysis should determine the primary cause of failure, and, based on the determination, corrective action should be initiated that will prevent similar failures.

Information

 

Stages of an Analysis

Although  the sequence is subject to variation depending on the nature of a specific failure, the principal stages that comprise the investigation and analysis of a failure are as follows:

Collection of Background Data 

Collecting the available information regarding the manufacturing, processing and service histories of the failed component including

  1. Power station ( capacity & name ).

  2. Unit Type ( cooler- heater- condenser- boiler ).

  3. Types of tabs ( ASTM, DIM, AIPI....).

  4. Sample location and sectch of the unit.

  5. Date of beginning the operation.

  6. Number of shut down and operation time before failure.

  7. Number of previous failure and the type of maintenance.

  8. Analysis of inner and outer media up to 3 months ago.

  9. Flow rate and the operating temperature and pressure.

  10. Fuel type ( heavy- light- gas ).

  11. Additive used.

  12. Excess air %.

  13. Plant observation.

It is usually desirable to have photographs taken by professional using zoom camera.

Sample Collection

  1. Selection of samples should be done prior to starting the examination.

  2. The analyst is responsible for ensuring that the samples will be suitable for the intended purpose and that they represent the characteristic of the failure.

  3. It is often necessary to compare failed components with similar components that did not fail to determine whether the failure was brought about  by service conditions or was the result of an error in manufacture.

Preliminary Examination of the Failed Part (Visual Examination and Record Keeping)

  1. The preliminary examination is useful evidence in establishing the cause of failure or in determining sequence of events leading to the failure.

  2. The preliminary examination should begin with unaided visual inspection and before any cleaning.

  3. The examine detect the change of color and texture.

  4. Particular attention should be given to the surfaces of fractures and cracks.

  5. All important features, including dimensions should be recorded either in writing or by sketches or photographs.

Mechanical Testing 

Hardness testing is the simplest of the mechanical tests and is often the most versatile tool available to the failure analyst. Among its many applications hardness testing can be used to:

  1. Assist in evaluating heat treatment.

  2. Provide an approximation of the tensile strength of steel.

  3. Detect softening or hardening  caused by overheating, by decarburization, or by carbon or nitrogen pickup.

Hardness testing measure the resistance to plastic deformation of layers of metal near the surface of the specimen.

Macroscopic Examination

  1. It's detailed examination of fracture surface at magnifications ranging from 1 to 100 diameter.

  2. The amount of information that can be obtained from examination of a fracture surface  at low-power magnification may give an indication of the type of fracture.

  3. The examination may be done with the unaided eye, hand lens or low-power stereoscopic microscope.

Selection and Preparation of Metallographic Sections

Selection and preparation of samples for metallographic or microscopic examination and chemical analysis as in Document Metal-001-r0.

Microscopic ( Metallographic ) Examination

Microscopic examination provide information about:

  1. The failure mechanism.

  2. The method of manufacture of the part under investigation.

  3. The heat treatment to which it has been subjected either intentionally during manufacture or during service.

  4. It provides other service effects such as corrosion, oxidation and severe work hardening of surfaces also are revealed the characteristics of any cracks that may be present.

  5. Metallographic specimen for examination by optical microscope must be prepared as in Document Metal-001-r0.

  6. The optical microscope has been designed by combining various lenses to resolve and reveal the line details of the microstructure of samples.

Chemical Analysis

  1. In failure investigation, routine analysis ( bulk, local, surface, corrosion product .... etc ) to ensure that the material is the one that was specified is recommended.

  2. Chemical analysis of any deposit, scale or corrosion product, or the medium with which the affected material has been in establishing the primary cause of failure.

  3. Electron microscope can be used to determine element concentration in alloys and scales.

  4. X-Ray diffraction to identify crystalline compounds of any corrosion products.

Conclusion

It would be helpful to check the following points in order to formulate the final conclusion.

  1. Specification of material composition.

  2. Mechanical properties : suitable?

  3. Heat treatment ; proper ?

  4. fabrication : proper ?

  5. Repair ( if any ) : proper ?

  6. Operation : proper ?

  7. Maintenance : proper ?

All those points or information are formulated to write the mechanical of the failure and determine the failure mechanism.

Directives

 

Report Writing

A final report on failure analysis should include the following points:

  1. Description of the failed component.

  2. Operating condition at the time of failure.

  3. Service history.

  4. Material specification.

  5. Visual and macroscopic examination.

  6. Microscopic or metallurgical examination.

  7. Mechanical testing.

  8. Chemical analysis.

Recommendation

Recommendation will be reported according to the reasons and the mechanism of failure in order to prevent the repetition of failures.
