Introduction

The CyberScan pH 500 series bench pH meter measure the pH in a solution. pH is a measure of relative acidity or alkalinity of water; so, it is a most important factor influencing scale–forming and corrosion tendencies.

The first draft was initiated by chemist: Mohammed Eshra, Reviewed by chemical general director: Aness Abou Al Ezz, Revised by quality control chemist: Wael A. Tantawy, and accepted By Eng. Mohammed Hamoda Al Sarag, the Head Sector of Damietta Power Station.

Information

General

  1. Solution pH is a measure of the active acid or base in a solution.

  2. pH is defined as the negative logarithm of the hydrogen ion concentration.

  3. Neutral water has (7) values, below (7) approaching (0) are increasingly acid while values above (7-14) are increasingly basic.

  4. Low pH water increases the potential for corrosion of equipment.

  5. High pH water can cause precipitation of calcium carbonate on the surfaces of pipelines, heat exchangers, condensers and other components.

  6. In clarification processes, pH is an important determination, the control of coagulation is optimized through pH control because every coagulant possesses an optimum pH range in which it provides the most efficient performance.

  7. The pH value of boiler water is usually adjusted to control both depositions and corrosion.

Principle

  1. Electrometric determination of the pH of a solution involves the measurement of the electromotive force (EMF) of a cell comprising an indicator electrode. 

  2. An electrode responsive to hydrogen ions such as a glass electrode immersed in the test solution and reference electrode.

  3. These electrode systems are potentiometric devices and when connected with a suitable reference electrode to form an electrochemical cell give voltage out puts related to the activity of the (H+) ion concentration according to Nernist Equation.

  4. The voltage output is proportional logarithm of the ion activity.

Calibration Procedure

pH Calibration Procedure ( with ATC )

  • It is recommended to perform at least 2-point calibration using standard buffers.

  • 1-point calibration can also be used for quick measurements.

  • The instrument is capable of multi–point calibration (up to maximum of 3 points) to ensure enhanced accuracy throughout the pH measurement range (pH 4.01 , 7.00 and 10.0 ).

  • To activate the Automatic Temperature Compensation (ATC), simply plug in the temperature probe into phono jack.

For 1-Point Calibration ( with ATC )

  1. Select pH mode using MODE key.

  2. Rinse the electrode well with de–ionized water.

  3. Immerse the electrode into standard buffer and press CAL key to calibrate the meter. The display will shown the measured reading.

  4. Press CON key when (READY) indicator appears to confirm the calibration. The secondary display automatically scrolls to the next pH buffer calibration option i.e. pH 4.01.
    For 1–point calibration, this can be ignored and the user can exit to (MEAS) mode by selection the CAL / MEAS key.

  5. Press the key (MEAS) to return to the measurement mode.
     

The 1- point calibration procedure is now complete.

For 2 or 3 Point Calibration ( Max 3–Point )

  1. Perform steps (1), (2) and (3) of the 1–point calibration procedure as outlined above.

  2. Select the second desired buffer value using the tor u keys, to calibrate 2 or more points.

  3. Once the calibration is confirmed at the second point you can proceed to calibrate at the third point without the need to return the measurement mode.

Measurement

Note: Remove the protective rubber cap of the electrode before proceeding with measurement. Take care not to exert too much force as this may cause damage to the electrode.

To measure, carry out the procedures listed.

  1. Rinse the Electrode- With water to remove impurities and to activate the electrode if it has been dehydrated.

  2. Switch the Meter on and ensure Meter is in (MEAS) Mode– This is displayed on the top center of the LCD display.

  3. Dip the Electrode into the Sample- This to ensure that the glass bulb of the electrode is completely immersed into the sample. Stir the electrode gently in the sample to create a homogeneous sample.

  4. Take Readings– When the reading are stable, a (READY) annunciator will be displayed.

To hold the value of the measurement reading until it is more convenient to note the reading ( press the Hold key ).

Maintenance of the pH Meter SyberScan 500

Electrode Maintenance

pH electrodes are susceptible to dirt and contamination and need to be cleaned regularly

depending on the extent and condition of use. 

Storage

Always keep the pH bulb wet in pH 4 buffer with (1/100) part of saturated KCl. Other pH buffers or tap water are also acceptable storage media, but avoid storage in de-ionized water.

The protective rubber cap filled with the buffer solution provides storage for long periods.

Electrolyte Replacement (for Refillable Electrodes only)

The reference electrolyte needs to be refilled when the electrode has been used for an extended period, or when the internal electrolyte has dried up.

To accomplish this, follow the procedure detailed below.

 

 

 

 


 

1. Remove the protective rubber cap or sleeve to expose the filling port  of the electrode. Remove the old reference electrolyte with a syringe and a flexible tube.

                                                                 

2.  Fill the new reference electrolyte with (3M KCl) until reaches the level of the refilling port. Replace the Rubber Sleeve.


                                                     
                         

3.  Rinse the electrolyte with de-ionized water and tap dry.

Electrode Cleaning

Salt Deposits

Dissolve the deposit by immersing the electrode in tap water for 10–15 minutes, then thoroughly rinse with de–ionized water.

Oil / Grease Films

Wash electrode pH bulb in a little detergent and water. Rinse electrode tip with de–ionized water.

Clogged Reference Junction

  1. Heat a dilute KCL solution to 60–80oC.

  2. Place the sensing portion of the pH electrode into the heated KCL solution for approximately 10 minutes.

  3. Allow the electrode to cool while immersed in some unheated KCL solution.

Protein Deposits

  1. Prepare a 1% pepsin solution in 0.1M HCl.

  2. Allow the electrode to stand in this solution for 5-10 minutes.

  3. Rinse the electrode with de–ionized water.

Electrode Activation

Generally if the procedure of the storage and maintenance had been closely followed, the electrode can be used immediately.

  1. However, should the electrode response become sluggish, it may be possible that the bulb has dehydrated.

  2. The bulb can be re-hydrated by immersing the electrode in an ideal storage solution (e.g. buffer pH 4 solution) for 1-2 hours.

  3. If this fails the electrode may require re-activation.

  4. At no time should one touch or rub the glass at this cause the build–up of electrostatic charge.

  5. If the above procedure does not re-activate the electrode to acceptable status, try rejuvenating the electrode by following the procedure outlined below.                       

 

 

Rejuvenation Procedure

  1. Dip and stir the electrode in rejuvenation procedure freon or alcohol for 5 minutes.

  2. Leave the electrode in tap water for 15 minutes.

  3. Dip and stir the electrode in concentrated acid (e.g. HCl, H2 SO4) for 5 minutes.

  4. Repeat step 2.

  5. Dip and stir in strong base NaOH for 5 minutes.

  6. Leave for 15 minutes in tap water.

  7. Test with standard calibration buffer solution to see if the electrode yields acceptable results.
