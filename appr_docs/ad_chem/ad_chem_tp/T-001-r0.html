Taking Insulating Oil Samples from Large Equipment for Basic Analysis    (I1-Y1) 		Approved Date:    12 June 1995

Introduction

This document lists the equipment inspection procedures I1, which must be performed on an annually bases Y1, as indicated in the title block and according to the Central Chemical Laboratories Instructions. The first draft initiated by Mr. Helmut, Canadian SISP. The first revision date  4 Feb 2002. 

Safety Precaution 

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area,

  • Wear your safety helmet & safety shoes.

  • There is a danger of falling into the oil containment pit when working near large power transformers.

 

Chemical Precautions

  1. It is recommended to take oil samples for laboratory analysis every one year, frequency may be adjusted by order of Central Chemical Lab. and the approval committee.

  2. Samples must be taken on clear days, precautions being taken to guard against contamination by wind blown dust and sand particles.

  3. It is recommended to take oil samples at normal transformer operating temperature.

  4. Read and record the oil  temperature before taking a sample.

  5. It is recommended to take only thoroughly cleaned small neck, brown glass bottles for sampling, capacity. should be 1000 cc (1 liter). The Central Chemical Lab requests two 1-liter bottles of oil sample.

 

Tools and Equipment

  • Safety helmet, safety belt, and tool set

  • Two cleaned small neck, brown glass bottles for sampling, capacity. should be 1000 cc (1 liter).

  • Cleaning cloth, and labels.

 

Work to be Carried Out

 

  1. Carefully clean the oil sampling valve from dust and dirt. (Impurities which tend to affect the dielectric strength of oil will generally be found at the bottom of the transformer tank, therefore the small sampling valve in located on, or near the main drain valve)

  2. Drain a small amount of insulating oil into a pail to flush out sampling valve and line.

  3. Fill the sample bottle about half full with oil, shake the bottle well and empty the oil 
    into the pail.

  4. Now fill the sample bottle very slowly with oil to avoid air bubbles.

  5. Seal the bottle tight and prepare for shipment to the lab.

  6. The sample must be kept away from exposure to direct sunlight.

  7. Make sure the sample bottle is correctly labled.

  8. Make sure you fill in the attached sample data sheet.

  9. Send the oil sample to the laboratory as soon as possible.
