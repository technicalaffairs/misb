Introduction

The demineralization plant has a chief role in the production of pure water suitable for boiler operation avoiding many problems such as corrosion, scale formation … etc.

Demi.plant include two trains A & B each train consists of 3 filters or vessels, each of them contains a definite type of resin material which is responsible for the demineralization process i.e removing dissolved solids from water and giving demineralized  water depending on the ion exchange phenomenon.

Resin material is divided into two types, cationic removal ( relite CF/CR) which is responsible for removing cations (positive ions) from water and anionic (relite 2 AS/CR) which remove negative ions from water.

The quantity of cationic resin is 700 liter put in the first vessel while that of anionic resin is 750 liter and put in the second vessel. The third vessel has two types of resins, cationic (relite CF/MB) which remove any residual cationic ions after passing through the first vessel (cation) and anionic which remove residual anionic ions after passing through anionic filter (end vessel) and have the type (relite 3 AS/MB). The quantity of resin in third vessel which is called mixed bed is,

Cationic resin: 250 L

Anionic resin: 300 L

Chemical Reactions       

There are chemical reaction occurring between salts dissolved in water and resin material. Assume that the cationic resin has the symbol R.H. where H is the substituting functional group and R-OH is the symbol of anionic resin and OH is the substituting functional group the chemical reactions occurring between cationic resin, anionic resins and dissolved ions, are

Cationic            R – H + Na CL → R – Na + HCL

Anionic             R – OH  + HCL → R – CL + H2O

Supposing that the dissolved salt in water is sodium chloride (Nacl), when passes through cation vessel cation resin absorbs the positive radical of the salt (Na+) and the anion resin which present in the 2nd vessel absorbs the negative radical of the salt (Cl-) giving pure water or demineralized water after passing certain time resin material become exhausted I.E there no ion exchange occur so regeneration process is carried out for activation the resin using acid and alkali.

The sulphuric acid is used for regeneration of the cationic resin

            R – Na + H2SO4 → R – H + NaSO4

While sodium hydroxide is used for regeneration of anionic resins.

            R – CL + NaOH → R – OH + NaCL

Characteristics of Demineralized Water

The plant can run under two different conditions:

  1. Water from wells (raw water).

  2. Water from desalination plant (desalinated)

While fed with well water, all exchangers (cation, anion and mix bed) are used. When the water to be treated comes out of desalination plant (with a conductivity of 10-30 us/cm), then only mixed bed can be used.

When using raw water, the operation time of anion and cation exchanger between two regeneration at 7-5 m2/h capacity is of 12 h and the net treated output is not less than 90m3. but when desalinated water is used the operation cycle between two regeneration becomes not definite, depending on the low and high salinity of desalinated water. Another words, if the conductivity of desalinated water is 10 us/cm the operation cycle between two regeneration is longer than that of 50 us/cm.

Safety Precautions

During taking samples of acid and soda solutions for adjusting the regeneration concentration, the following tools must be satisfied protecting persons against any sudden leakage:

  • Safety goggles.

  • Emergency shower.

  • First aid box.

Environmental Recommendation

  • Acidic and basic regeneration products must be neutralized before discharging using the same chemicals of regeneration ( H2SO4 & NaOH )

Tools and Equipment

  • Acid and soda solutions ( H2SO4 & NaOH ).

Work to be Carried out

Regeneration Process

As mentioned above the regeneration process must be carried out for activation of the resin after certain operation period of the deminrealization plant.

Regeneration is branched into cation regeneration, anion regeneration and mixed bed regeneration.

Cation Filter Regeneration

  1. Backwashing

  2. Inlet acid ( first step )

  3. Inlet acid ( 2nd step )

  4.  Inlet acid ( 3rd step )

  5. Slow rinse

  6. Fast rinse

1 Backwashing

This sequence of regeneration phase will be generated after each 26 successful regeneration cycles .

After backwashing executes the regeneration cycle must be performed two times with the sequence : 1-2-3-4-5-2-3-4-5-6 .

The time of backwashing is 15 minutes

 2-3-4 Acid inlet

In this phase diluted acid of three different concentration shall be injected into the cationic filter at three different steps ( the time of acid inlet is 15 minutes for each step i.e. 45 minutes ).

5- Slow Rinse

This step is important to wash the cation filter after inlet the acid .

6- Fast Rinse

This rinse process is executed to remove the regenerate from the filter .

After the regeneration phase in cationic filter, anionic regeneration is started up with the following phases:

  1. Backwashing

  2. Soda

  3. Slow rinse

  4. Fast rinse

7- Backwashing

This phase is made only 1 time after each 20 regeneration cycles if backwashing is executed the regeneration cycles must be performed for two times with the following sequence:

7-8-9-8-9-10

8- Soda Inlet

In this phase the diluted caustic Soda will be injected into the anionic filter.

9- Slow Rinse

It is a washing process for removing excess Soda from the anion filter.

10- Fast Rinse

This rinse is in which final washing for the resin to remove any regenerate residual from the filter.

 

Mixed bed Regeneration

The mixed bed regeneration phases are

  1. Backwashing.

  2. Stand by.

  3. Acid inlet.

  4. Slow rinse for cationic resin / caustic soda inlet.

  5. Slow rinse.

  6. Drain.

  7.  Air 1st step.

  8. Stand by.

  9. Air 2nd step.

  10. Stand by.

  11. Filling.

  12. Fast rinse.

Backwashing

Backwashing of mixed bed filter differs from those of cation filter and anion filter.

Backwashing of cation and anion filters remove any dirt clay and deposits inside filter while that of mixed bed filter is for separating the cationic resin to the lower part and anionic resin to the upper one, depending on the higher specific gravity of cation resin than that of anion resin to enable entering the acid and soda respectively for regeneration.

Stand by

This step is for making settling of the resin before entering the acid.

Acid Inlet

In this phase, the diluted acid is injected into the mixed bed filter to regenerate the cation resin in the lower part of the filter.

Rinse Cationic Resin + Soda Inlet

In this phase the diluted caustic soda will be injected into the mixed bed filter to regenerate the anion resin in the upper part of the filter. At the same time, the cationic resin will be washed with a stream of demineralized water.

Slow Rinse

This phase is the same as soda inlet but here the closing pump will remain stopped i.e. a stream of demineralized water only enter the filter to wash resin and remove any regenerate residual.

 

Drain

The drain is necessary for the following mixing. In this step, the water of the filter is partially discharged.

Air 1st Step

The air step is carried out using blower when start, the air enter the mixed bed and mix the cationic and the anionic resins after their separation in the backwashing step.

Stand by

The resin will be settled in this step.

Air 2nd Step

This step is the same as the 1st air step and carried out for suring the mixing process of resins.

Filling

The filter is completed with water to the normal operational level.

Fast Rinse

It is a final washing for the removal of regenerate from the filter. The time of this step depends on the specification of product water i.e. when it become within the normal standard values the step will be stopped and the mixed bed will be ready for operation.
