Introduction

This document outlines the testing procedure P1, which must be performed on a monthly bases as indicated by M1 in the above title block

Deposits from plant for generating and using steam may reach the analyst in a variety of forms. They may be still attached to convex or concave surfaces of metal tubes or portions of plant, or they may already have been removed and put into suitable containers; they may be dry, wet, or oily; they may contain inclusions of old scale in a new matrix; they may show strata of deposits built up at different times in the history of the plant. In whatever form a deposit may come it shall be retained in its original form. The remainder shall then be available for analysis.

Summary of Analysis

 This method covers the manner in which the results of examination and analysis for inorganic constituents of deposits formed from waters are to be reported.

Safety Precautions

  • The sample must be taken as the applicable ASTM methods D 3370-76.

  • Acids such as sulfuric acid and others are dangerous, so you have to wear breathing mask.

  • Keep these acids closed. Do not get it in the eyes, on the skin, or on clothing. Do not breathe them, use only in a hood.

Work To be Carried Out

Sampling and Analysis

History of Sample: Information regarding the source and history of the sample shall be included in the report of the analysis.

Name of company supplying sample.

Name and location of plant.

Date and time of sampling.

Number of sample.

Name and other designation of equipment from which sample removed.

Type of deposit, whether scale, sludge, or corrosion product.

Type of treatment applied to the water that formed the deposit.

Physical characteristics:

The physical characteristics of the sample. Such as color, form ( scaly, slimy, drusy ). Hardness, magnetic properties and structure ( amorphous, crystalline ).

Preparation of Sample for Analysis:

Drying- Heat the deposit in an oven for one hour at 105 ± 2°C. The sample may have acquired moisture during grinding. Therefore dry 10-12 g. in a porcelain basin  in an oven for one hour at 105 ± 2°C. Cool in a desiccator and bottle.

Note: Laminated specimens may, be separated into their component sections before drying.

Separation- Separate the dried specimen into its components in one of the following ways:-

a. Deposits attached to curved metal surfaces.

b. Deposits  already detached from their supporting metal surfaces

Breaking- Where necessary, break each sample into pieces of a size suitable for crushing or grinding in mortars.

Crushing- If the sample still contains hard portions which are too large for grinding in an agate mortar, break those down in a percussion mortar.

Grinding- Take the crushed sample, quarter it down, if necessary, to not more than 20 g., and grind in an agate mortar.

Bottling- Bottle each sample separately, labeling the bottle with a description of the contents which gives the history of the sample from the point at which the deposit was received by the analyst.

Preparation of  Oil Sample for Analysis:

  1. Dissolve a small quantity of the sample in 3M  HCl , and filter off any insoluble material on a sintered glass crucible.

  2. Rinse the beaker with light petroleum ( boiling range 40-60°C ) and wash the residue on the filter with this.

  3. Keep this washed residue for the test for other organic matter.

  4. Extract the filtrate and washings by shaking with light petroleum ( boiling range 40-60°C ) in a separating funnel, removing the stopper from time to time to release any pressure.

  5. Run off the aqueous layer, wash the light petroleum layer with distilled water, separate again and pour the light petroleum layer into a small beaker.

  6. Evaporate the organic solvent on a hot-water bath in a fume cupboard. Observe if there is any appreciable residue.

For Organic Matter: Place about 0.5 g. of the sample on a porcelain crucible, heat gently at first and ignite more strongly later. Observe any charring or smoking which indicates the presence of organic matter.

The sample is now ready for analysis, and carry out all analysis on the dried sample

 

Dissolving the Analytical Sample

Solution in Hydrochloric Acid:

This treatment will dissolve water-formed deposits which do not contain a substantial percentage of stubborn components, including calcium sulfate, various silicates, and some of the more refractory spinals. The use of this solvent is advantageous in that it is not oxidizing, and possible interference from sulfate or nitrate is not introduced.

Procedure

  1. Weight 0.5 g of the analytical sample obtained, into 250 mL beaker. Add 50 mL of HCl (1+4) and evaporate to dryness on a hot plate contained in a hood. Add 10 mL of concentrated HCl and again evaporate to dryness. Add 10 mL of concentrated HCl (1+9) bring to a boil, and separate the solution by filtration through ash less filter paper.

  2. Wash the residue and dilute the combined filtrate and washings to a measured volume.

  3. Aliquots of this solution shall be used for the analysis of the constituents to be determined.

  4. The residue may be retained for further examination.

Solution in  Mixed Hydrochloric Acid-Nitric Acid:

This solvent is more effective in eliminating traces of organic material and in dissolving more refractory components which resist HCl alone.

Procedure

  1. Add 5 mL of HNO3 to 0.5 g of the analytical sample that has been weighed  into 250 mL beaker and evaporate to  near dryness on a hot plate contained in a hood. Add 50 mL of  dil.(1+1) HCl   and 5 mL of  HNO3. Evaporate to dryness on a hot plate in a hood. Cool and add 10 mL of  HCl (1+1) acid and 1 mL of HNO3and repeat the evaporation. Allow the beaker to cool and then add 50 mL of dil.(1+1) HCl. Boil until the volume is decreased to approximately 25 mL and  filter through ash less filter paper.

  2. Wash the residue and dilute the combined filtrate and washings to a measured volume.

  3. Aliquots of this solution shall be used for the analysis of the constituents to be determined.

Alkali Fusion:

This method of dissolving the sample is especially useful for the rapid determinations of silica and aluminum.

Procedure

  1. Add 9 sodium hydroxide pellets ( approximately 1.5 g ) to a 75 mL nickel crucible. Slowly heat the crucible over a meeker burner until the pellets are molten. Allow the crucible and its contents to cool to a room temperature.

  2. Weigh 0.05 g of the analytical sample, and transfer to the  crucible containing NaOH. Reheat the crucible to remelt the hydroxide and swirl to mix in the weighed material. Use a nickel wire or rode to complete the mixing. Continue heating for 3 min after mixing, then allow the melt to cool.

  3. Add 50 mL of water to the  crucible and stir the contents of the  crucible occasionally until the melt is disintegrated completely (about 1 h).

  4. Transfer the contents to a 1 liter volumetric flask. Wash the crucible. Dilute to volume with water.

Quantitative Analysis

Drying: Although the sample has been dried during preparation it may have acquired moisture during grinding. Therefore dry 10-12 g. in a porcelain basin in an oven at 105 ± 2°C for 1 hour. Cool in a desiccator and bottle. Carry out all analyses on the dried sample.

 

Oil

  1. Weigh about 2 g. of the dried sample in a 50 ml beaker and transfer to a 500 mL separating funnel by the aid of about 20 mL of light petroleum boiling range 40-60°C. Add 50 mL of water and then gradually add 25 mL of HCl. Cool, if necessary, and shake well. Interrupt the shaking occasionally and release any pressure developed.

  2. Allow to settle, then run off the lower layer into another separating funnel and carry out a second extraction with a further 10 mL light petroleum . Run off the lower layer and reject it.

  3. Add the second light petroleum extract to the first, rinsing the separating funnel with a little light petroleum.

  4. Wash the petroleum extract by shaking with 5 mL portions of water until free from acid.

  5. Filter the extract through a dry ash less  filter paper into a weighed 250 mL conical flask. Wash the paper 3 times with 5 mL portions of light petroleum.

  6. Remove the solvent from the filtrate and washings by evaporation on a water bath.

  7. Add 3 drops of alcohol and dry in an oven controlled at 105 ± 2°C for 2 hours.

Loss on Ignition

Accurately weigh about 3 g. of the dried sample in a previously ignited, cooled and weighed platinum crucible. Heat gently for a few minutes and then strongly for 60 minutes in an electric  furnace at approximately 1200°C with occasional tapping to prevent caking. Cool in a desiccator and weigh. Repeat for a further 30 minutes and until the loss in weight between successive weightings does not exceed 40 mg.

Reporting of Chemical Analysis

All data shall be reported as percentage by weight of the dry sample.

For convenience in calculation, values for oxides, acid anhydrides, and combined water may also be expressed on a basis of chemical equivalents.

Value or relative equivalents. are obtained by dividing each value of percentage by weight by the equivalent weight of the respective oxide, acid radical, or water.

Quantitative determination shall be reported to the nearest 0.1 %.

When a Quantitative determination is made and a negative result is obtained, it shall be reported as "0,0".
