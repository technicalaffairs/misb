Operating Principle

Gas Chromatograph is used for identification of the sample of natural  gas's components and for quantitative measurements. The process depends on the separation of the materials components which occurs at the surface of a liquid phase (the material in the column) as a result of the attractive forces between the adsorbent and the solute (sample) which carried by mobile phase (carrier gas is inert and usually Helium, Nitrogen or Hydrogen.

The differential solubility of a substance in two different phases in the case of Gas–Liquid Chromatography the sample components reach an equilibrium between the gas phase and liquid phase each component has a different partition coefficient thus causing separation in the column after separation the detector which responding to sample components producing an electrical signal that can be measured to quantitative the amount of each components present.
