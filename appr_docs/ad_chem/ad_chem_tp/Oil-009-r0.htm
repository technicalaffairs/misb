Introduction 

This document outlines the testing procedure P1, which must be performed  yearly and may be subject to be changed according to directives of Central Chemical Laboratories.

This test method specifies a procedure for the determination of the kinematic viscosity, ν, of liquid petroleum products, both transparent and opaque, by measuring the time for a volume of liquid to flow under gravity through a calibrated glass capillary viscometer. The dynamic viscosity  can be obtained by multiplying the measured kinematic viscosity, ν, by the density, ρ, of the liquid.

Note 1: For the measurement of the kinematic viscosity and viscosity of bitumens, see also Test Method D 2170, and D2171.

The test method is intended for use with liquids for which the shear stress and shear rates are proportional.

Definitions

  1. Kinematic viscosity, The resistance to flow of a fluid under gravity.

  2. Dynamic viscosity, The ratio between the applied shear stress and rate of shear of a liquid, and it's a measure of the resistance to flow or deformation of a liquid.

Summary of Method

The time is measured in seconds for a fixed volume of  liquid to flow under gravity through the capillary of a calibrated viscometer under a reproducible driving head and at a closely controlled and known temperature. The kinematic viscosity is the product of the measured flow time and the calibration constant of the viscometer.

Tools and Equipment

 

Apparatus

Viscometers, calibrated  viscometers of the glass capillary type, capable of being used to determine kinematic viscosity measuring within the limits of the precision. 

Viscometer Holders, to enable all viscometers which have the upper meniscus directly above the lower meniscus to be suspended vertically within 1º in all directions.

Temperature-Controlled Bath, Use a transparent liquid bath of sufficient depth such, that at no time during the measurement of flow time, any portion of the sample in the viscometer is less than 20 mm below the surface of the bath liquid or less than 20 mm above the bottom of the bath.

Temperature-Control, It's within the range from 15 to 100ºC, the temperature of the bath medium doesn't vary by more than ± 0.02ºC of the selected temperature over the selected temperature over the length of the viscometer, or between the position of each viscometer, or at the location of the thermometer. For temperatures outside this range, the deviation from the desired temperature must not exceed ± 0.05ºC.

Temperature-Measuring Device, It's in the range from 0 to 100ºC- Use either calibrated liquid-in-glass thermometers of an accuracy after correction of ± 0.02ºC or better, or any other thermometric device of equal or better accuracy.

Timing Device-Use any timing device that is capable of taking readings with a discrimination of 0.1 s or better, and has an accuracy within ± 0.07% of the reading when tested over intervals of 200 and 900 s.

Electrical timing devices may be used if the current frequency is controlled to an accuracy of 0.05% or better. Alternting currents as provided by some public power systems, are intermittently rather than continuously controlled. When used to actuate electrical timing devices, such  control can cause large errors in kinematic viscosity flow time measurement.

Calibration and Verification

Viscometers- only calibrated viscometers shall be used, thermometers, and timers.

Certified Viscosity Reference Standards, These are for use as confirmatory checks on the procedure in the laboratory.

If the determined kinematic viscosity doesn't agree within ± 0.35% of the certified value, recheck each step in the procedure, including thermometer and viscometer calibration, to locate the source of errors.

The most common sources of error are caused by particles of dust lodged in the capillary bore and temperature measurement errors.

 

The calibration constant, C, is dependent upon the gravitational acceleration at the place of calibration and this must, therefore be supplied by the standardization laboratory together with the instrument constant. Where the acceleration of gravity, g, differs by more that 0.1 per cent, correct the calibration constant as follows:
                                                     g2   
                                            C2 = --- X C1 
                                                     g1 

Where the subscripts 1 and 2 indicate, respectively, the standardization laboratory and the testing laboratory.

Work to be Carried Out

Procedure for Kinematic Viscosity

  1. Adjust and maintain the viscometer bath at the the required test temperature within the limits taking account of the conditions and of the corrections supplied on the certificates of calibration for the thermometers.

  2. Held the thermometers in an upright position under the same conditions of immersion as when calibrated.

  3. Select a clean, dry, calibrated viscometer having a range covering the estimated kinematic viscosity ( that is, a wide capillary for a very viscous liquid and a narrow capillary for a more fluid liquid). The flow time shall not be less than 200 sec or the longer time noted in Specifications D446.

  4. The specific details of operation vary for the different types of viscometers.

  5. Fill the viscometer in the normal manner when the temperature of the test is below the dew point. To ensure that moisture doesn't condense or freeze on the walls of the capillary, draw the test portion into the working capillary and timing bulb, place rubber stoppers into the tubes to hold the test portion in place, and insert the viscometer into the bath. After insertion allow the charged viscometer to reach bath temperature, and remove the stoppers. When performing manual viscosity determinations, don't use those viscometers which cannot be removed from the constant temperature bath for charging the sample portion.

  6. Use loosely packed drying tubes affixed to the open ends of the viscometer is permitted, but not required. If used, the drying tubes shall fit the design of the viscometer and not restrict the flow of the sample by pressures created in the instrument.

  7. Reverse viscometers used for silicone fluids, flurocarbons, and other liquids which are difficult to remove by the use of a cleaning agent, for the exclusive use of those fluids except during their calibration. Subject such viscometers to calibration checks at frequent intervals. The solvent washing from these viscometers shall not be used for the cleaning of other viscometers.

Cleaning of Viscometer

Between successive determinations of kinematic viscosity, clean the viscometer thoroughly by several rinsings with sample solvent, followed by the drying solvent. Dry the tube by passing a slow stream of filtered dry air through the viscometer for 2 min or until the last trace of solvent is removed.
Periodically clean the viscometer with the cleaning solution, for several hours to remove residual traces of organic deposits, rinse thoroughly with water, and drying solvent, and dry with filtered dry air. Inorganic deposits may be removed by hydrochloric acid treatment before the use of cleaning acid, particularly if the presence of barium salts are suspected. (Warning- It's essential that alkaline cleaning solutions are not used as changes in the viscometer calibration can occur.)

 

 

Calculations

  1. Calculate the kinematic viscosity, ν, from the measured flow time, t, and the viscometer constant, C, as follows:
                        
        kinematic viscosity, ν = Ct 
    where:

ν =  kinematic viscosity, (mm2/s)

 

C = calibration constant of the viscometer, (mm2/s)/ s, and 
t = flow time, s

  1. Calculate the dynamic viscosity, η, from the calculated kinematic viscosity, ν, and the denisty, ρ, as follows:
                          
             η = ρ v 10-3
    where:

 

η = dynamic viscosity, mPa.s,

ρ = density, kg/m3 at the same temperature used for the determination of the kinematic viscosity, and 
v = kinematic viscosity, mm2/s.

  1. The denisty of the sample can be determined at the test temperature of the kinematic viscosity determination by any appropriate method such as Test Methods D 1217, D 1480, or D 1481.

Expression of Results

Report the test results for the kinematic or dynamic viscosity, or both, together with the test temperature.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Precision

Determinability (d)- The difference between succesive determinations obtained by the same operator  in the same laboratory  using the same apparatus for a series of operations leading to a single result, would in the long run, in the normal and correct operation of this test method, exceed the values indicated only in one case in twenty.

Base oils at 40 and 100ºC            0.0020 y            (0.20%)

where y is the average of results being compared.

Repeatability-The difference between succesive results obtained by the same operator  in the same laboratory  using the same apparatus under constant operating conditions on identical test material would, in the long run, in the normal and correct operation of this test method, exceed the values indicated only in one case in twenty.

Base oils at 40 and 100ºC            0.0011 x            (0.11%)

where x is the average of results being compared.

Reproducibility- The difference between two single and independent results obtained by different operators working  in different laboratories  on nominally identical test material would, in the long run, in the normal and correct operation of this test method, exceed the values indicated only in one case in twenty.

Base oils at 40 and 100ºC            0.0065 x            (0.65%)

where x is the average of results being compared.
