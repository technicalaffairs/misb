Introduction

Ultrameter apparatus is a portable, it's used to measure Conductivity, TDS and pH. It is easily used. It is designed to provide quick, easy, accurate measurements by simply pressing one key. There is no "off" key. After 15 seconds of inactivity the instrument turns itself off.

Conductivity, Resistivity, and TDS require mathematical correction to 25ºC values. On the left of the Ultrameter's liquid crystal display is shown an indicator of the salt solution characteristic used to model temperature compensation of conductivity and its TDS conversion. The indicator can be KCl, NaCl, 442 or USER. Selection  affects the temperature correction of conductivity, and the calculation of TDS from compensated conductivity. Generally KCl is used for conductivity, NaCl for resistivity, and 442TM ( Natural Water characteristic ) for TDS will reflect present industry practice for standardization.

Safety Precautions

  • Ensure while filling conductivity cell cup no air bubbles cling on the cell wall.

  • After using the Ultrameter rinse out the conductivity cell cup with clean water. Don't scrub the cell.

  • Calibration is recommended about once per month with Conductivity or TDS solutions. Calibration with pH solutions should be checked twice a month.

Work to be Carried out

Calibration Procedure

  1. Conductivity or TDS Calibration         

    1. Rinse conductivity cell 3 times with a proper standard (KCl or NaCl).

    2. Refill conductivity cell with the same standard.

    3. Press COND or TDS, then press CAL, "CAL" will appear on display

    4. Press   5 or 6until display agrees with the standard.                                             

    5. Press CAL once to confirm new value and end calibration sequence for this particular solution type. If another solution  type is also to be measured change solution type now and repeat this procedure. 

  2. pH Calibration

Important: Always "Zero" the instrument with a pH 7 buffer solution before adjusting the gain with acid or base buffers, i.e., 4 and/or 10, etc.

  1. pH Zero Calibration

  1. Rinse sensor well 3 times with buffer solution (7.0).

  2. Refill sensor well with buffer solution (7.0).

  3. Press pH, then CAL, "CAL", " BUFFER" and "7" will appear.     

  4. Press  5 or 6  until display agrees with buffer value.

  5. Press CAL once to accept the value. The pH zero is now complete. You may continue with pH Gain Calibration or exit by pressing any measurement key. 

b. pH Gain Calibration
 

Important: Always calibrate the instrument with a pH buffer solution before adjusting the gain with acid or base buffers, i.e., 4 and/ or 10. Either acid or base solution can be used for the 2nd point "Gain" calibration and then the opposite for the 3rd point.

  1. Rinse sensor well 3 times with either acid (1-6) or base (8-14) buffer solution.

  2. Refill sensor well again with same buffer solution.

  3. Press  5 or 6  until display agrees with buffer value.

  4. Press CAL once to accept the 2nd point of calibration.

  5. Repeat steps 1 through 4 using opposite buffer solution.

  6. Press CAL to accept the 3rd point of calibration which ends Calibration procedure.

  7. When calibration is complete. Refill sensor well with Myron L Storage Solution and replace protective cap.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Measuring Procedures

  1. Measuring Conductivity / Total Dissolved Solids (TDS)

    1. Rinse cell cup 3 times with sample to be measured.

    2. Refill cell cup with sample.

    3. Press COND or TDS.

    4. Take reading. A display of (------) indicates an overrange condition.

    Important:         After sampling high concentration solutions or temperature extremes, more rinsing may be required. When sampling low conductivity solutions, be sure the pH cap is well seated so no solution washes into the conductivity cell from around the pH cap.
     

  2. Measuring pH

1. Remove protective cap by squeezing its sides and pulling up.

2. Rinse sensor well 3 times with sample to be measured and shake out each sample to remove any residual liquid.

3. Refill sensor well with sample.

4. Press pH.

5. Take reading.


Important: After use, fill pH sensor well with MYRON L Storage Solution, a strong KCl solution or pH 4 buffer, not distilled water, and replace protective cap. Do not allow pH sensor to dry out.
