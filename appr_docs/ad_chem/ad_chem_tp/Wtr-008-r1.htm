Introduction

This document outlines the testing procedures P1, which must be performed on a monthly basis as indicated by  M1 in the above title block, and according to the Directives of the Central Chemical Laboratories.

This test method is intended primarily for  water where the concentration of chloride in the range: 0-50 mg Cl, and 0-500 ppm chloride.

This document was updated at Dec 2008 as follows:

- The document Number changed from Wtr-008-r0 to be Wtr-008-r1.

Summary of Method

Chloride is determined by titrating a neutral or slightly alkaline solution with silver nitrate solution  in the presence of potassium chromate indicator. White silver chloride is precipitated. The end point is indicated by formation  of red silver chromate.

Interferences

 Sulphite interferes and may be removed by the addition of hydrogen peroxide.

Safety Precautions

  • The sample must be taken as the applicable ASTM methods D 3370-76.

  • Make sure the sample bottle is correctly labeled.

  • Make sure the sample bottle is free from dust and dirt.

  • The sample must be kept away from exposure to direct sunlight.

  • Cleaning solvents such as acid dichromate, nitric acid and others are dangerous, so you have to wear breathing mask.

  • Keep this solvents closed. Do not get it in the eyes, on the skin, or on clothing. Do not breathe them, use only in a hood.

 

 

Tools and Equipment

 

Reagents

 

Hydrogen Peroxide 10 volume H2O2.

Calcium Carbonate

Potassium Chromate Indicator Solution

Dissolve 5 gm of K2CrO4 in  water and dilute to 100 ml water.

Sulphuric acid ( 0.02 N)

Pipette 5 ml of the exactly 2 N sulphuric acid into a 500 ml one-mark graduated flask and dilute to the mark. Mix well.

Silver Nitrate ( N/ 35.5 )

Dissolve  4.78 gm  of silver nitrate in water and dilute to 1000 ml in one- mark volumetric flask.

Sodium carbonate( 0.02N )

The water used for this preparation must be free from dissolved carbon dioxide. If in doupt about the water, boil it just before use and cool to room temperature.

- Weigh out exactly 2.65 gm of anhydrous sodium carbonate and dissolve in about 50 ml of water. Transfer the solution to a 500 ml, one-mark volumetric flask and dilute to the mark. Mix well. This solution is exactly 0.1 N. Pipette 20 ml of the 0.1 N sodium carbonate solution into 100 ml one-mark volumetric flask and dilute to the mark. Mix well.

Methyl Orange Indicator Solution

Dissolve 0.1 gm methyl orange in 100 ml hot distilled water, cool, filter if necessary.

Purity of Reagents

All reagents shall conform to the specifications of the committee on Analytical Reagents of the American Chemical Society, where such specifications are available. Other grades may be used, provided that the reagent is of high purity to permit its use without lessening the accuracy of the determination.

Sampling

 

Collect the sample in accordance with the applicable ASTM methods D1066 and D3370.

 

 

 

 

Work to be carried out 

  1. Measure 100 ml of the sample into a porcelain basin. If sulphite is present, add 2 ml of H2O2 10 volume, add 2 drops of methyl orange.
    (i) If the colour is yellow add 0.02 N sulphuric acid until the colour is orange.
    (ii) If the colour is red add 0.02 N sodium carbonate until the colour is orange.
    In either case it is an advantage to add about 0.1 gm of calcium carbonate after neutralization stirring thoroughly.

  2. Then add 0.5 ml of K2CrO4 indicator which will impart a yellow color to the water.

  3. Add N/ 35.5 silver nitrate (AgNO3), stirring well during the addition, until the yellow color just changes to a    reddish brown color.

  4. Record the number of ml N/35.5 silver nitrate used.
