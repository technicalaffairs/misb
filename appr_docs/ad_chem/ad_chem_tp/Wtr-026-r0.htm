Introduction

This document outlines the testing procedure P1, which must be performed monthly  as indicated by M1 in the above title block.

Bacteria may arise from pairs, chains, clusters, or single cells, all of which are include in the term ( colony-forming units ) (CFU).

The heterotrophic plate count ( HPC ), formerly known as the standard plate count, is a procedure for estimating the number of live heterotrophic bacteria in drinking water and measuring changes during water treatment and  distribution or in swimming pools.

Significance of Method

The final count depend on interaction between developing colonies of bacteria. Choose the combination of procedure and medium to produce greatest number of colonies.

Summary of Method

The shape of bacterial colonies are: relatively small-compact-less tendency to encroach to each other. The character of submerged colonies are: slower growing and difficult to transfer. Significant heat make shock for the bacteria but gently exposure to heat 45-46°C for tempering agar.

 

Safety Precautions

  • Collect water as directed in Standard Method Section 9060 A ( sampling ).

  • Initiate analysis as soon as possible after collection to minimize the changes in bacterial population ( elapsed time between processing and collection 8 hours).

  • Maintain the sample at 4°C but don't freeze, this takes place when you can't analysis the sample within 8 h ( the elapsed time 24 hour).

  • Mark each plate with sample number and date and any other information.

  • Prepare at least duplicated plates for each volume of sample.

  • Use a table or bench with non porous surface, clean draft, free, well lighted room and disinfectant before analysis.

Sterilized Petri dish- with 65 Cm2.

Sterilized pipet

Work To be Carried Out

Procedure

  1. Melt sterile solid agar medium in boiling water, provided that the contents remain fully melted, then maintain melted medium in water bath ( 44-46°C ) until used.

  2. Pipet 1 ml of drinking water in sterilized Petri dish.

  3. Use a sterilize pipet for transfer.

  4. Don't prepare sample and pour plates in direct sunlight.

  5. Pour at least 10-12 ml liquefied agar, flame the neck before pouring.

  6. Rotate the dish in one direction and in the opposite direction after adding the liquefied medium.

  7. Let medium solidifies, invert plates and place in incubator.

  8. Avoid contamination, don't touch the pipet tip across lips and necks of bottles.

  9. Don't insert pipets more than 2.5 cm below  the surface of sample when removing sample.

  10. Hold pipet at an angle of about 45°C with tip touch bottom of petri dish when discharging sample portions.

  11. Prepare at least two replicate plates for each sample.

  12. Prepare additional controls ( only liquefied medium without sample) to determine contamination of plate, pipets and room air.

Incubation

Incubates pour plates ( six plates for each samples ) as following:

  1. ( 2 plates + 1 control ) at 37°C for 48 h.   *

  2. ( 2 plates + 1 control ) at 22°C for 48 h.   *

Counting, Recording, Computing and Reporting

 

Counting, Recording

  1. Count all colonies on selected plates after incubation.

  2. Store plates at 5-10°C for no more than 24 h, if counting must be delayed, but avoid this as routine practice.

  3. Record result of sterility controls.

  4. Use an approved counting aid, such as the Quebec colony counter, for manual counting.

  5. Don't pipet more than 2 ml of sample.

  6. Compute bacterial count per milliliter by the following equation:

                                           Colonies Counted

          Cfu / 1ml =  -----------------------------------------

                      Actual Volume of Sample in Dish, ml

  7. Don't report result as " too numerous to count ( TNTC )" if the number of colonies per plate for exceed 300.

  8. Count each of the following types as one ( a chain of colonies-a spreader that develops as a film of growth colony that forms in a film of water at the edge or over the agar surface ).

  9. Count impning colonies that differ in appearance, such as morphology or color, as individual colonies.

  10. Report as " spreader ", if plates have excessive spreader growth.

Computing and Reporting

  1. Report all counts as colony forming units.

  2. Include in the reports the method used, the incubation temperature and time, and the medium, for example, Cfu/ml 37°C /48 h.

  3. Divided total number of colonies or average number, if duplicate plates of the same dilution, per sample volume.
