Introduction

The Stabilized Temperature Platform Furnace (STPF) is a concept that makes proper use of the existing equipment to maximize the signal of the analyte and minimize interferences.and the following conditions:

  • Fast instrument electronics

  • Signal integration

  • Accurate background correction

  • Rapid furnace heating (20000C/s)

  • L, vov platforms and pyrolytically coated graphite tubes  

  • Gas stop during atomization     

  • Use of matrix modifiers (improves the analysis. the modifier salt is added to the sample in high concentration, transforming the analyte element into a more well-defined compound.)

Significance

Atomic Absorption Spectrometry resembles emission Furnace in that  a sample is aspirated into graphite furnace  tube  and atomized. The major difference in the amount of light emitted is measured, whereas in atomic absorption spectrometry a light beam is directed through Furnace tubes, into a monochromator, and into a detector that measures the amount of light absorbed by the atomized element in the Furnace. The amount of energy absorbed in the furnace is proportional to the concentration of the element in the sample .The Furnace Tube Is closer to thermal equilibrium to sample atomization.

 

Summary of Method

This method describes how to prepare and calibrate an atomic absorption spectrophotometer for the quantitative determination of elements in solution by (THGA) Transverse Heated Graphite Atomizer, and preparation of a calibration curve to establish the optimum calibration range for the instrument and analytical method being used.

Purity of Reagents

All reagents shall conform to the specifications of the committee on Analytical Reagents of the American Chemical Society, where such specifications are available. Other grades may be used, provided that the reagent is of high purity to permit its use without lessening the accuracy of the determination.

Sensitivity, Detection Limits and Concentration Ranges

Sensitivity and detection limits vary with instrument, the element determined, the complexity of the matrix, and the technique selected.

Optimum Concentration Range

It is usually starts from the concentration of several times the sensitivity and extends to the concentration at which the calibration curve starts to flatten. To achieve best results, use concentrations of samples and standards within the optimum concentration range of the spectrometer. Safety Precautions

  • Make sure the sample bottle is correctly labeled.

  • Make sure the sample bottle is free from dust and dirt.

  • The sample must be kept away from exposure to direct sunlight.

 

Environmental Recommendation

  • Keep solvents closed. Do not get it in the eyes, on the skin, or on clothing. Do not breathe them, use only in a hood.

  • Place vents in the laboratory to protect personnel from toxic vapors, protects the instruments from corrosive vapors, and prevents furnace stability from being affected by room drafts.

Tools and Equipments

Apparatus

Atomic Absorption Spectrometer

It is consists of a light source emitting the line spectrum of an element (hollow cathode lamp), a device for vaporizing the sample (Furnace), a means of isolating an absorption line (monochromator or filter and adjustable slit) and a photoelectric detector with its associated electronic amplifying and measuring equipment.

Auto Sampler Design

The auto sampler contains all the mechanical and electrical components required to withdraw the correct volume of solution from a selected container inject it into the graphite tube and rinse the pipette tip. (Using the auto sampler to inject solutions improves the precision of the analytical results compared to that for manual pipette.

  

 

 

 

 

 

Cooling System

For rapid and accurate temperature control the furnace must be both heated and cooled. Cooling is achieved using water flowing through tubes that are integrated into the furnace and allow the graphite tube to approach room temperature quickly.

Lamps

Use one hollow-cathode lamp for each element being measured. Multi-element hollow-cathode lamps generally provide lower sensitivity than single-element lamps.

                     

Furnace

The furnace is electrically heated. The voltage is applied across the graphite tube, perpendicular to the radiation beam. The electromagnet is orientated so that the magnetic field is parallel to the radiation beam.

Furnace Program Optimization

A standard furnace program usually includes the following steps:

Drying: The solvent is vaporized during the drying step.

Pyrolysis: The pyrolysis step is used to remove as many matrix components as possible. Matrix modifier can be used to stabilize the analyte or aid in removal of matrix components.

Atomization: The sample is atomized to form ground state atoms in the path of the radiation beam during this step.

Clean-out: High temperature clean-out step after atomization prepares the furnace for subsequent samples.

Each step should be optimized for the particular element and matrix under study. However, prior to running any sample; it is recommended that you run an aqueous standard to check the performance of the instrument.
