Introduction

This document outlines the testing procedures, which must be performed on an annually bases as indicated by Y1 in the above title block, and according to the Directives of the Central Chemical Laboratories.

This test method covers the visual determination of the color of a wide variety of petroleum products such as lubricating oils, heating oils, diesel fuel oils, and petroleum waxes.

This document was updated at 2008 as follows:

- The document Number changed from Oil-004-r0 to be Oil-004-r1.

Significance and Use 

Determination of the color of petroleum products is used mainly for manufacturing control purposes and is an important quality characteristic since color is readily observed by the user of the product.

In some cases the color may serve as an indication of the degree of refinement of the material. When the color range of a particular product is known, a variation outside the established range may indicate possible contamination with another product. However, color is not always a reliable guide to product quality and should not be used  indiscriminantly in product specifications.

Summary of Test Method

Using a standard light source, a liquid sample is placed in the test container and compared with colored glass disks ranging in value from 0.5 to 8.0 when an exact match isn't found and the sample color falls between two standard colors, the higher of the two colors is reported.

Safety Precautions

  • The sample must be taken as MPIS- Document No. TD-SS-003-r0.

  • Make sure the sample bottle is correctly labeled.

  • Make sure the sample bottle is free from dust and dirt.

  • The sample must be kept away from exposure to direct sunlight.

  • As oil sample must be free from water, shall be taken under dry and clear environmental conditions.

 

Environmental Recommendations

  1. Using solvents such as kerosine ( combustible, vapor harmful ) and others for cleaning the valve parts are dangerous, it will expose lung to very hazardous and carcinogenic chemicals, you have to wear breathing mask.

  2. Do not throw the sample oil in the sewage because this oil will prevent the sewage treatment process collect it in tight containers and return it back to the vendor to recycle it. Always remember that "this oil is load for your wastes but it's valuable material for them".

  3. Avoid oil spillage to the ground.

Worked to be carried out

Preparation of Sample

1    Liquid Petroleum Products such as Lubricating Oils

Fill the sample container to a depth of 50 mm or more and observe the color, If the sample is not clear, heat it (6ºC)10ºF  above its cloud point  as determined in accordance with ASTM  D 2500, and observe the color at that temperature. When the sample is darker than 8 color, mix 15 volumes of sample into 85 volumes of the solvent kerosine and observe the color of the mixture.

2   Petroleum Waxes, including Petrolatum

Heat the sample to a temperature 11 to 17ºC (20 to 30ºF)  above its congealing point as determined in accordance with  ASTM D938,  and test at that temperature. When the sample is darker than 8 color, mix 15 volumes of melted sample with 85 volumes of solvent kerosine brought to the same temperature and test the mixture at that temperature.

 

 

Procedure

  1. Clean and dry the accessories before starting the test.

  2. Place a sample container filled to a depth of at least 50 mm with distilled water or deionized water in the      compartment of the colorimeter through which the standard glasses will be observed.

  3. Place the sample in its container in the other compartment. Cover the containers to exclude all exterior light.

  4. Switch on the light source and compare the color of the sample with that of the standard glasses.

  5. Determine for two field comparators which glass matches the color of the sample; or if an exact match isn't possible, then use that  glass which possesses the next darker color.

Report

Report as the color of the sample, the designation of the glass producing a matching color, for example: "7.5 ASTM Color"

If the color of the sample is intermediate between those of two standard glasses, record the designation of the darker glass preceded by the letter "L" for example : "L7.5 ASTM Color".

Never report the color as being darker than a given standard except those darker than 8, for example : "D8 ASTM Color".

If the sample has been diluted with kerosine, report the color of the mixture followed by the abbreviation "Dil,) for example:"L7.5 Dil ASTM Color".

 

Precision and Bias

 

Precision

The precision of this test method as obtained by statistical examination of interlaboratory test results is as follows:

  1. Repeatability- The difference between successive test results obtained by the same operator with the same apparatus under constant operating conditions on identical test material would, in the long run, in the normal and correct operation of the test method, exceed the following value only in one case in twenty: 0.5 color unit.

  2. Reproducibility- The difference between two single and independent test results obtained by different operators working in different laboratories on identical test material would, in the long run, in the normal and correct operation of the test method exceed the following value only in one case in twenty: 1 color unit.

Bias

The procedure in this test method has no bias because the value of ASTM Color is subjective and can only be defined in terms of this test method.
