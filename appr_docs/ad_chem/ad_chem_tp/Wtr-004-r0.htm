Introduction

This document outlines the testing procedures P1, which must be performed on a daily basis as indicated by  D1 in the above title block, and according to the Directives of the Central Chemical Laboratories.

This Practice covers the sampling of water for chemical and physical analysis.

The following is included :       Practice A - Grab Samples

 

Significance

The goal of sampling is to obtain  a truly representative sample.

The most critical factors necessary to achieve this are :

  1. Points of sampling.

  2. Time of  sampling.

  3. Frequency of sampling.

There are general rules are applicable to Grab Samples :

  1. The samples must represent the conditions existing at the point taken.

  2. The samples must be of sufficient volume to permit reproducibility of testing for the desired objective.

  3. The samples must be collected, packed, shipped and manipulated.

 

Summary of Practices

This practice includes Grab Samples procedure which is applicable to sample water at a specific site representing conditions only at the time of sampling from sources such as wells, rivers, streams, pipe lines and steam generators.

 

Definitions   

Sampling- obtaining of a portion representative of the material concerned to be analysed.

 

 

Safety Precautions

  • The sample must be taken as the applicable ASTM methods D 3370-76.

  • Make sure the sample bottle is correctly labeled.

  • Make sure the sample bottle is free from dust and dirt.

  • The sample must be kept away from exposure to direct sunlight.

  • Cleaning solvents such as acid dichromate, nitric acid and others are dangerous, so you have to wear breathing mask.

  • Keep this solvents closed. Do not get it in the eyes, on the skin, or on clothing. Do not breathe them, use only in a hood.

All reagents shall conform to the specifications of the committee on Analytical Reagents of the American Chemical Society, where such specifications are available. Other grades may be used, provided that the reagent is of high purity to permit its use without lessening the accuracy of the determination.

Work to be carried out 

 

Frequency and Duration of Sampling

If samples of water are taken far enough from the shoreline it may be made by taking individual samples at infrequent intervals, such as bi- weekly or monthly, sufficient to cover seasonal changes.

If samples of water are taken near from the shoreline of river, take them at shorterintervals, for instance daily

to provide more exact knowledge of the variation in composition where these are of importance in the use to which the water is to be put.

If greater variation or cycles of pollution occur collect more frequent samples, for example, at hourly intervals.

Water undergoing continuous treatment must be sampled with such frequency that adequate control is assured.

The interval between samples is directly related to the rate at which critical characteristics can reach intolerable limits.

Temperature Adjustment

When samples above ambient temperature are taken, use cooling coils for adjusting it to the ambient temperature. Some test methods require adjustment to other than ambient temperature and can be carried out when indicated.

Particulate Matter

Normally, samples are taken without separation of particulate matter.

Preparation of Sample Containers for Chemical and Physical Analysis

 

The sample containers shall be made of materials that will not contaminate with the sample and before use shall be cleaned thoroughly to remove all extraneous surface dirt. Chemically resistant glass and polyethylene are suitable materials for the containers. The closures for it shall be glass stoppers that have been thoroughly washed, or plastic caps with suitable liners.

 

Point of Sampling

 

Open Bodies of Water

  1. Choose it with extreme care to obtain a representative sample of water, avoid surface scum.

  2. For streams, seas, reservoirs, and other bodies of water, samples are taken at a number of points at the surface and at a number of depths at each points.

  3. The distance between samples is from 1 to 3 miles below the source of pollution.

  4. Collect samples at least one- half mile below water falls to obtain time for escaping entrained air.

Confined Flows

Choose sampling points in pipelines, filters, zeolite and chemical water softeners, deionizing processes, surface condensers, evaporators or condensate return lines with regard to the piping and configuration of the individual piece of equipment containing the water to be tested, the character and changes occurring between the inlet and outlet water, and the rate of passage through the equipment.

Steam Generators

                  The exact point of sampling depends on : steam generator design, location of chemical and

                    water feed, lines, and other local conditions. Samples taken from several locations

                    simultaneously may be desirable. Collect samples through a cooling coil.

Sample Collection for Chemical and Physical Analysis

  1. To sample streams under pressure, regulate the rate of flow in the sample line to not less than 500 ml / min. after first flushing to remove all sediment and gas pockets. Samples at elevated temperature must be collected through a cooling coil.

  2. To sample water from valves or cocks, insert the sample line into the sampling bottle so that it touches the bottom. Allow a volume of water to flow into and overflow from the container before the sample is taken. Fill the container completely and secure the sample without contact with air to prevent change in the concentration of constituent to be determined.

  3. To sample unconfined water at any specific depth flows samples through a tube to the bottom of the container and a volume of sample equal to 4 to 10 times the volume of the container passes through it, or an integrated sample containing water from all points in a vertical section.

  4. To sample water from steam generator, take precautions to avoid dilution by condensed steam or concentration due to flashing.

  5. To ship samples, do not fill the bottle entirely in order to allow some room for expansion when subjected to a change in temperature. An air space of 10 to 25 ml usually sufficient.

  6. Before taking the sample, establish a continuous flow of not less than 500 ml / min., cooled to less than 37C and also rinse the sample container at least 3 times by filling it with water to be sampled, shaking, then emptying.

  7. Collect the water sample by upward displacement through a tube extending to the bottom of container. Allow it to overflow at least 5 times, then stopper it. If analysis can not be made immediately, the containers should be tightly stoppered to prevent atmospheric contamination.

   Preservation of Sample

Add chemical preservations to samples for chemical and physical examinations only as specified in specific test methods. Quick freezing has been found to be beneficial in preserving some organic constituents.

Time Interval Between Collection and Analysis of Samples

In general, allow as short a time as possible to elapse between the collection of a sample and its analysis. Under some conditions, analysis is necessary to secure reliable results.
