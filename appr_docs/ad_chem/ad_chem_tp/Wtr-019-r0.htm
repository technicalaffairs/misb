Introduction

This document outlines the testing procedures P1,which must be performed every  month M1 as indicated in the above title block and according to the Directives of the Central Chemical Laboratories.

This analysis covers the determination of nitrite ion in water.

Significance of Analysis

Nitrites react in warm acid solution (ca. 40°C ) with permanganate solution in accordance with the equation :

   2MnO4- + 5NO2-  + 6H +   →         2Mn++  + 5NO3-  + 3H2O

Summary of Analysis

If a solution of a nitrite is titrated in the ordinary way with potassium permanganate, poor results are obtained, because the nitrite solution has first to be acidified with dilute sulfuric acid. Nitrous acid is liberated, which being volatile and unstable, is partially lost. If, however, a measured volume of standard potassium permanganate solution, acidified with dilute sulfuric acid, is treated with the nitrite solution, added from a burette, until the permanganate is just decolorised, results accurate to 0.5-1 per cent may be obtained. This is due to the fact that nitrous acid does not react instantaneously with  the permanganate.

More accurate results may be secured by adding the nitrite to an acidified solution in which  permanganate is present in excess ( the tip of the pipette containing the nitrite solution should be below the surface of the liquid during the addition ), and back titrating the potassium permanganate with a solution of ferrous ammonium sulphate which has recently been compared with the permanganate solution.

For practice in this determination, it may be determine the purity of commercial potassium nitrite.

Safety Precautions

  • The sample must be taken as the applicable ASTM methods D 3370-76.

  • The sample must be kept away from exposure to direct sunlight.

  • Acids such as sulfuric acid and others are dangerous, so you have to wear breathing mask.

  • Keep these acids closed. Do not get it in the eyes, on the skin, or on clothing. Do not breathe them, use only in a hood.

Tools and Equipment

Apparatus

Pipets- Since this analysis depends on the amount of KMnO4 consumed, it is imperative that pipets for delivering the potassium permanganate  standard solution be calibrated and cleaned for maximum precision.

Volumetric Flasks.

Heater.

Reagents

Potassium Permanganate Standard Solution ( 0.1 N ).

Conc. Sulphuric Acid.

Commercial Potassium  Nitrite.

Work To be Carried Out

Procedure

  1. Weigh out accurately  about 1.1 g. of  commercial potassium  nitrite, dissolve it in cold water, and dilute to 250 ml. in a volumetric flask. Shake well.

  2. Measure out 5 ml. of standard 0.1N-potassium permanganate into a 100-ml. flask, add 2.26 ml. conc. sulfuric acid, and heat to 40°C.

  3. Place the nitrite solution in the burette, and add it slowly and with constant stirring until the permanganate solution is just decolorised, better results are attained by allowing the tip of the burette to dip under the surface of the diluted permanganate solution.

  4. Towards the end of the reaction is sluggish, so that the nitrite solution must be added very slowly.

  5. Repeat the titration with two other 25 ml. portions of the permanganate solution.

Note:   1 ml. N-KMnO4  ≡  0.02300 g. NO2  ≡  0.04256 g. KNO2

 

Calculation

Calculate concentration of nitrite ion in the sample, in milligrams per liter, as follows:

NO2-, mg/L ( ppm ) =  V1 × 2300  / V

Where:

            V1  =  milliliters of KMnO4 solution required for titration of the sample,

            V    =  milliliters of  sample used.
