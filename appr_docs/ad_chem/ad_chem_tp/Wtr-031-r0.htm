 Standard Test Method for Phosphorus in Water

Procedure

Colorimetric Molybdovanadophosphate Method
in Water Using ASTM D 515-79 ( Method C )

 		Approved Date :  02 Oct 2004
Date to be Reviewed: Oct 2009

 

Introduction

This method covers the routine determination of dissolved orthophosphate in water. It is best suited to boiler feedwater and boiler water containing 1 to 10 mg/liter ( ppm ) of phosphorus. The range can be extended upward by diluting the sample.   

The first draft was initiated by chemist: Wafaa Esmail; reviewed by department director chemist: Zakia Soltan; revised by quality control chemist: Mohamed Mosbah Mokpel and accepted by Eng. Awad M. Fatthy; Head Sector of Talkha Power Station.

Significance

Phosphorus is widely distributed in the environment as both inorganic and organically bound phosphates. Sources of phosphates include surface–applied fertilizers, cleaning and laundering products, boiler water conditioners, and drinking water treatment aids. Because phosphorus is a nutrient for photosynthetic organisms, it may be important to monitor and control discharge into the environment.

Summary of  Method

This method is based on the photometric measurement of  yellow color of molybdovanado-phosphoric acid developed in the sample. The color intensity is proportional to the orthophosphate concentration of the sample.  

Interferences

This method is not suitable for highly colored waters, such as tannin-treated boiler water, unless the color is first removed. High concentrations of ferric ions interfere with this test, but generally aren't present in the waters for which the method is intended.

Sampling

When determining only dissolved phosphorus in boiler waters, filter the sample through a 0.45 µm membrane filter to remove particulate matter as soon as possible after sampling. Delay in the filtration  may alter the relationship between the dissolved and particulate forms of phosphorus. Since membrane filters may contain significant amounts of phosphorus, they should be washed prior to use.

 

Analysis is best performed immediately after collecting the sample to minimize possible change. If analysis can't be performed the same day is collected, preserve the sample by adding 40 mg/l HgCL2 to sample and refrigerating it at 4°C. To prevent precipitation of the mercury, add sufficient NaCL to the sample, if necessary, so that it will have a chloride content of at least 50 mg/l.

 

Safety Precautions

  • Collect the sample in accordance with method D 3370-76, Grab Samples.   

  • Store sample in glass bottles.

  • The reagent used must be of sufficiently high purity  to permit its use without lessening the accuracy of determination.

  •  Solvents such as hydrochloric acid and others dangerous, so you have to wear breathing mask.

  • Keep this solvents closed. Do not get it in the eyes, on the skin, or on clothing. Do not breath them, use only in a hood.

 

Tools and Equipment

 

Apparatus

Photometer- The measurements shall be made at a wavelength of 400 to 420 nm. The range of the test depends on the light path length of the solution being measured. The range can be more than doubled by measuring the color at a wavelength from 470 to 490 nm instead of from 400 to 420 nm.

Glasswares, Acid washed- All glassware and sampling bottles shall be soaked in hydrochloric acid (1+3) to remove possible phosphate coating, and rinsed with water prior to use.

      Reagents

  1. Ammonium Molybdate–Vanadate Solution – Dissolve 40 gm of ammonium molybdate [ ( NH4 )6 MO7O24. 4H2O ] in 400 ml of water. Dissolve  1.0 gm of ammonium metavanadate ( NH4VO3 ) in a mixture of 300 ml of water and 200ml of concentrated nitric acid ( HNO3, spgr 1.42 ). Add the first solution to the second solution, mix, and dilute to 1 liter with water.

  2. Phosphorus Standard Solution-( 1 ml  = 0.025 mg of phosphorous ) first prepare a stock solution ( 1.0 ml = 0.05 mg of phosphorus ) by dissolving in water 0.2197g of potassium dihydrogen phosphate ( KH2PO4 ) that has been dried for 1hour in an oven at 105°C, and diluting the solution to exactly 1 liter with water. Prepare the standard solution by diluting 50.0 ml of the stock solution to 100 ml with water in a volumetric flask.

Work to be Carried out

Procedure, Calibration, and Standardization

  1. Transfer  50 ml of a clear sample ( filter if suspended matter is present ) into an Erlenmeyer flask.

  2. If the sample contains more than 10 mg / liter ( ppm ) of phosphorus, use a correspondingly smaller sample diluted to 50 ml with water.

  3. Add 25 ml of ammonium molybdate-vanadate solution to the sample and mix well.

  4. Allow 2 minutes for the color development.

  5. Measure the color absorbance at 400 to 420 nm with a filter photometer or at 400 nm with a spectrophotometer, using water as the reference sample.

  6. Record the phosphorus concentration indicated by the calibration curve prepared which cover the range from 0 to 10 mg/liter ( ppm ) of phosphorus. Note: A separate calibration curve must be made for each photometer. Each curve must be checked periodically to ensure reproducibility.

  7. Prepare the standards by diluting suitable volumes of standard phosphorus solution ( 1 ml = 0.025 mg of phosphorus ) to 50 ml with water.

  8. One milliliter of phosphorus standard solution diluted to 50 ml produces a standard containing 0.5 µg / liter of phosphorus.

  9. Develop the color and measure the absorbance at 400 to 420 nm with a filter photometer or at 400  nm with a spectrophotometer, using water as the reference solution.

  10. Plot the absorbance values obtained as ordinates and the corresponding phosphorus concentrations as abscissas.

N.B. All solution must be at room temperature before they are mixed and must be mixed in the order.

Calculation 

  1. Calculate the concentration of orthophosphate, in milligrams per liter of phosphorus, as follows:

                     Orthophosphate, mg/liter ( ppm ) = A × ( 50 / B )

Where :

A = milligrams per  liter of phosphorus indicated by the calibration curve for the

       determined absorbance, and

B = milliliters of sample used.

   2 .   Convert the concentration of P to PO3-4  as follows:

 

                  PO4 mg/liter = 3.066 x ( mg/liter of phosphorus ).

Precision  

The precision of this method may be expressed as follows:

ST = ( 0.31a  + 3.1 ) / S, and

SO = ( 0.18a + 0.8 ) / S.

Where 

ST = Over–all precision, mg/liter of phosphorus,

SO = Single–operator precision, mg / liter of phosphorus,

 a   = Concentration of phosphate, mg /liter of phosphorus, and

 S  = milliliters of sample used.
