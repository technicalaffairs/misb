Title

Total Copper in Acid ( Iodometric Method )

 

 

Document No:  Wtr-022-r0

Issued to:    Power Stations

Procedure

Using ASTM D 933-31(79)           (P1-M1)

Date Approved:       06 April 2004

Date to be Reviewed:    April 2009

 

Introduction

This document outlines the testing procedure P1, which must be performed on a monthly  basis as indicated by M1 in the above title block

This method can be used for the determination, by titration, of total copper in hydrochloric acid ( HCL) that does not contain a copper complexor. In steel systems, very little copper in solution would be expected.

Summary of Method

The copper in the sample is oxidized. Iodine is liberated by reaction of the copper with potassium iodide ( KI ) in proportion to the amount of copper present, and the free iodine is titrated with standard sodium thiosulfate ( Na2S2O3 ) solution in the presence of a starch indicator.

Safety Precautions

  • The sample must be taken as the applicable ASTM methods D 3370-76.

  • Acids such as hydrochloric acid and others are dangerous, so you have to wear breathing mask.

  • Keep these acids closed. Do not get it in the eyes, on the skin, or on clothing. Do not breathe them, use only in a hood.

Tools and Equipment

Apparatus

Glasses ( Pipettes, Volumetric Flasks, filter paper).

Electronic balance.

Fuming cub ( hood ).

Reagents

Sodium Thiosulfate, Standard Solution ( 0.1N ).

Potassium Iodide ( KI ), granular.

Starch Indicator Solutions.

 

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Pipet an aliquot of the filtered sample into an Erlenmeyer flask containing 25 to 50 mL of water. The aliquot should contain enough copper to give a thiosulfate titration of over 5 mL.

  2. Add 5 g of KI, stopper the flask, and swirl to dissolve, allow to stand for about 5 min. the liberated iodine will turn the solution yellow to brown in color.

  3. Unstopper the flask and titrate with 0.1 N Na2S2O3 solution until most of the reddish-brown color disappear.

  4. Do not titrate to light yellow color.

  5. Add several drops of starch indicator solution and continue the titration until the purple starch-iodine color in the solution disappears.

  6. Record the milliliters of Na2S2O3 solution used.

Calculation

Copper, %  =  ( AN / BS ) × 6.4 

Where:

A   =   milliliters of Na2S2O3 solution required for the titration,

N   =   normality of the Na2S2O3 solution,

B   =   milliliters of sample titrated, and

S   =   specific gravity of the sample.
