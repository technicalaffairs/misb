Introduction

This method covers the determination of the turbidity of drinking water, industrial water and industrial waste water. The nephelometric method for determining the turbidity covers the measurements of relative turbidity and is also applicable to the measurements of low turbidity

Significance

Nephelometric turbidity based on a measurement of the light-scattering characteristics of the particulate matter in the sample.

Summary

Nephelometric turbidity instruments measured scattered light at 90-deg. or 90 and 270-deg. angles to incident beam.

These instruments cannot be calibrated accurately in terms of absolute turbidity, except in the case fluids having uniform-size particles that are less than approximately one fifth of the wavelength of incident light. Nephelos standards have been prepared by thoroughly mixing suitable amounts of finely divided titanium dioxide ( TiO2 ) into partially polymerized polystyrene. Alternatively, a suspension of formazine has been used as a turbidity standard formed by reacting hydrazine sulfate and hexamethylene tetramine under carefully controlled conditions.  

Definitions

Turbidity: Reduction of transparency of a sample due to presence of a particulate matter.

 

 

 

 

 

 

Safety Precautions

  • The reagent used must be of sufficiently high purity to permit its use without lessening the accuracy of determination.

  • The sample must be taken as the applicable ASTM methods D 3370-76.

  • Make sure that the sample bottle is correctly labeled.

  • Make sure that the sample bottle is free from dust and dirt.

Tools and Equipment

Apparatus

Nephelometer or calibrated slit Turbidimeter.

Work to be Carried out

Calibration

Using nephelometric turbidity standards obtain readings with at least three separate standards with in order to calibrate the instrument.

Procedure

 

 

 

Instruments using Nephelometric Standards

  1. Adjust firstly the instrument system physically and electrically in accordance with the manufactures instructions, then replace the standards by the sample in an optically identical tube and record the reading.

  2. The straight line relationship for Tyndall effect for small amounts of scattered 90-deg  or 90 and 270-deg light produces proportional readings for nephelometric turbidity standards up to approximately 100 nephelos units. Therefore, the nephelometric turbidity of the sample can be calculated as the turbidity of the calibrated standard times the ratio of the readings.

Instruments Using Transmitted Light From Calibrated Slit as Reference

  1. Follow the manufacturer's instructions in detail. This type of turbidimeter utilizes a fixed ratio of scattered light to transmitted light intensity.

  2. Adjusts the quantity of light for transmission from the single light source until the observer matches the final intensity of the light for the different paths, by using a calibrated slit. The instrument uses a special cell for the sample.

  3. Estimate the mid-point of the interval of uniform-light intensity for the two fields by rotating the dial in one direction only towards higher values, for all calibrated slit instruments. This eliminates errors caused by lost motion in the mechanism.

 

 

 

 

Calculation 

Calculate the nephelometric turbidity in nephelos units, as follows:

Nephelometric turbidity = A×B

Where:

                A =  instrument reading of scattered light,

                B =  nephelos units per unit of instrument reading.

Report

                 Report results as nephelos units.
