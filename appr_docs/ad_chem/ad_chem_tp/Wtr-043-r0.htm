Introduction

This method is used for controlling sulfide in industrial wastewaters plant effluent.

The maximum limit for sulfide concentration (S--) in industrial wastewaters effluent is (1mg/L) according to Egyptian Law Number (4) for year 1994. The time program for this experiment is once weekly.

Iodometric method can be used for wastewater and partially oxidized water from sulfur springs if interfering substances are removed first.

Sulfide often is present in groundwater, its common presence in wastewaters comes partly from the decomposition of organic matter, sometimes from industrial wastes, but mostly from the bacterial reduction of sulfate. Hydrogen sulfide escaping into the air from sulfide-containing wastewater causes odor nuisances, and it is very toxic.

Iodine reacts with sulfide in acid solution, oxidizing it to sulfur. A titration based on this reaction is an accurate method for determining sulfide at concentrations above (1mg/L) if interferences are absent and if loss of H2S is avoided.

Sampling and Storage

Take samples with minimum aeration. To preserve a sample for a total sulfide determination put zinc acetate solution into bottle before filling it with sample. Use 4 drops of 2 N zinc acetate solutions per 100 ml sample. Fill bottle completely and stopper.

Interferences

The iodometric method suffers interfering from reducing substances that react with iodine, including thiosulfate, sulfite, and various organic compounds, both solid and dissolved. In sample pretreatment to remove interfering substances make the following:

Put 0.15 ml (3 drops) 2 N Zinc Acetate Solution Zn(C2H3O2)2.2H2O in to a 500 ml glass bottle fill with sample, and add 1 ml (2 drops) 6N NaOH Solution (add enough NaOH to produce a pH above 9). Stopper with no air bubbles and mix vigorously. Let the resulting precipitate settle readily for 30 minutes. Filter precipitate through glass fiber filter paper and continue the procedure of the test.

 

Safety Precautions

  • Put the sample in glass bottles with stoppers.

  • Add zinc acetate as a preservative if analysis isn't started at once.

  • Add zinc acetate, NaOH and settle to remove interferences, concentrate samples, and to fresh samples from wells and sulfur springs.

  • Acids such as sulfuric acid, hydrochloric acid and others are dangerous, so you have to wear breathing mask.

  • Keep these acids closed. Do not get it in the eyes, on the skin, or on clothing. Do not breathe them, use only in a hood.

Tools and Equipment

 

Apparatus

Glass bottles with stoppers.

Reagents

  1. Hydrochloric Acid- HCl 6N

  2. Standard Iodine Solution. 0.025 N- Dissolve 20 to 25 gm KI in a little water and add 3.2 gm iodine, after iodine has dissolved dilute to 1000 ml and standardize against 0.025 N Na2S2O3 using starch solution as indicator.

  3. Standard Sodium Thiosulfate Solution 0.025 N- Dissolve 6.205 gm Na2S2O3.5H2O in distilled water. Add 1.5 ml 6N NaOH or 0.4 gm solid NaOH and dilute to 1000 ml, standardize with bi-iodate solution.

  4. Starch Solution- Dissolve 2 gm soluble starch in 100 ml hot distilled water.

  5. Zinc Acetate 2N- Dissolve 220 g Zn(C2H3O2)2.2H2O in 870 ml water; this make 1L solution.

  6. Sodium Hydroxide Solution- NaOH, 6N.

      Work to be Carried out

  1. Measure from a buret into 500 ml flask an amount of iodine solution estimated to be an excess over the amount of sulfide present.

  2. Add 2 ml 6N HCl. Pipet 200 ml sample into flask, discharging sample under solution surface. If iodine color disappears, add more iodine so that color remains.

  3. Back-titrate with Na2S2O3 solution, adding a few drops of starch solution as end point is approached, and continuing until blue color disappears.
