Significance

Nitrogen is a nutrient in the environment and is necessary to sustain growth of most organisms. It exists in several forms such as nitrate, nitrite, organic and ammonia.

Ammonia is a colorless, gaseous compound with a sharp distinctive odor. It is highly soluble in water. The extent of association or ionization in water is dependent on the temperature and PH. It may also be toxic to aquatic life.

Summary of Method

A sample aliquot is Nesslerized directly and the ammonia content determined colorimetrically. Turbid sample may be clarified with ZnSO4 and NaOH solution. The precipitated Zn(OH)2 is filtered off, discarding the first 25 ml of filtrate and ammonia is determined on an aliquot of the remaining clear filtrate by direct Nesslerization.

Sampling

Collect the sample in accordance with Methods D 1066, Specification D 1192, and Practices D 3370 in ASTM. Preserve samples by the addition of 1 ml of concentrated sulfuric acid per litre and store at 4°C. The pH  should be 2 or less. Analyze samples within 24 h of sampling.

Interferences

Glycine, Hydrazine and some amines will react with Nessler’s reagent to give yellow color in time required for the test.

Some organic compound such as  Ketones, aldehydes, alcohols, and some amines may cause an off color on Nesslerization in the distillation method.

Residual chlorine must be removed prior to the ammonia determination by pretreatment of the sample.

Safety Precautions

  • The sample must be taken as the applicable ASTM methods D 3370-76.

  • Make sure the sample bottle is correctly labeled.

  • Make sure the sample bottle is free from dust and dirt.

  • The sample must be preserve by the addition of sulfuric acid, and at pH less than 2 .

Tools and Equipments

 

 Apparatus

Photometer- spectrophotometer suitable for absorbance measurements at 425 nm.

Reagents

Nessler Reagent- dissolve 100 gm of anhydrous mercuric iodide HgI2 and 70 gm of anhydrous-potassium iodide in a small volume of water, add slowly with stirring to a cooled solution of 160 gm of sodium hydroxide in 500 ml of water, dilute to1liter store in the dark for 5 days and filter twice then keep out of sun light.

Sodium Potassium Tartrate Solution (300 gm/L)- Dissolve 300 g of sodium potassium tartrate solution in 1 Liter of water. Boil until ammonia-free and dilute to 1liter.

Ammonia Nitrogen, Standard Solution (1ml = 0.01 mg N)- Dry reagent grade ammonium sulfate{( NH4)2 SO4}for 1 h at 100°C. Accurately weigh 4.718 g and dissolve in water. Dilute to 1 Litre in a volumetric flask. Pipet 10 ml  of this stock solution to a 1 Litre volumetric flask.

Zinc Sulfate Solution, (100 gm/L)- Dissolve 100 gm of zinc sulfate heptahydrate (ZnSO4.7H2O) in water and dilute to 1 Litre.

Purity of Reagents

All reagents shall conform to the specifications of the committee on Analytical Reagents of the American Chemical Society, where such specifications are available.

Other grades may be used, provided that the reagent is of high purity to permit its use without lessening the accuracy of the determination. In addition, this water shall be free of ammonia nitrogen, such water is best prepared by the passage of distilled water through an ion-exchange column containing a strongly acidic cation exchange resin mixed with a strongly basic ion-exchange resin.

These resins should also be selected  so that organic compounds which might interfere with the ammonia determination will be removed.

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Add 1 ml of (ZnSO4 7H2O), to 100 ml aliquot and mix, if the sample contains turbidity.

  2. Add NaOH solution gently until pH is about 10.5, allow to settle and filter with filter paper, discarding the first 25 ml of the filtrate.

  3. Dilute a portion of the filtrate containing not more than 0.1 mg of ammonia nitrogen to 50 ml in a Nesslar tube.

  4. Add 2 drops of sodium potassium tartrate solution to prevent cloudy tubes, and mix.

  5. Add 1 ml of Nessler solution, then wait  20-30 min and measure photometrically at 425  nm.       

Calculation

Calculate the ammonia concentration, in mg/L of nitrogen in original sample, as follows:

Ammonia nitrogen, mg/L = {(A×1000) / S}

Where:

A =  milligrams of ammonia nitrogen observed, and

 

S =  millilitres of sample.

Calculate the ammonia concentration, in mg/L of ammonia in original sample, as follows:

Ammonia, (mg/L)  =  E × 1.22

Where:

E =  ammonia nitrogen , mg/liter.

 

Precision

The precision of the method at ammonia nitrogen levels up to 0.50 mg/L is ± 0.04.
