Introduction

This document outlines the checklists I1, which must be performed on an annually bases as indicated by Y1 in the above title block and according to the directives of the Central Chemical Laboratories.

This method covers the determination of the dielectric breakdown voltage of insulating oils of petroleum origin. The method is applicable to liquid petroleum oils commonly used in cables, transformers, oil circuit breakers, and similar apparatus as an insulating and cooling medium. The suitability of this method for testing oils having viscosities of more than 19 CSt ( mm2 / s )  (100 SUS ) at 40oC ( 104 oF ) has not been determined.

Note 1:

This method is more sensitive to the deleterious effects of moisture in solution than method D 877, especially when cellulose fibers are present in the oil.(D 877 is ASTM standard test method is recommended for acceptance of insulating liquid received from vendors in tank cars, tank trucks, drums, and liquid from power system apparatus rated below 230kv, using Disk Electrodes).

Note 2:

This method is recommended for testing filtered, degassed, dehydrated oil prior to and during the filling of power systems apparatus rated above 230 kv, and for testing samples of oil from the apparatus after filling. It is also finding increased usage for testing oils from transformers in service.

Note 3:

This method is not recommended for, and should not be used for, acceptance tests on oil received from vendors in tank cars, tank trucks or drums.

Significance

The dielectric breakdown voltage of an insulating liquid is of importance as a measure of the liquid’s ability to withstand electric stress without failure. It serves to indicate the presence of contaminating agents such as water dirt, moist cellulose fibers, or conducting particles in the liquid, one or more of which may be present in significant concentrations when low breakdown voltages are obtained. However, a high dielectric breakdown voltage does not necessarily indicate the absence of all contaminants; it may merely indicate that the concentrations of contaminants that are present in the liquid between the electrodes are not large enough to affect deleteriously the average breakdown voltage of the liquid when tested by this method.

 

 

Summary of method

The oil (all cases of oils for transformers, for oil filled cables, for circuit breakers ) whether new or used contained in a specified apparatus, is subjected to an electric field with a continuously increasing rate of voltage rise, till the oil breakdown. This intended to reveal the extent of physical pollution by water and other suspended matter and the advisability of carrying out drying and filtration treatment before the oils are introduced into the apparatus for which they are intended.  

Definition

The dielectric breakdown voltage is the voltage reached during the test at the time the first spark occurs between electrodes. Whether it be transient or established.

Safety Precautions

  • The sample must be taken as MPIS procedure Number TD-SS-003-r0.

  • Make sure the sample bottle is correctly label.

  • Make sure the sample bottle is free from dust and dirt.

  • The sample must be kept away from exposure to direct sunlight.

  • This method is sensitive to the deleterious effects of moisture in solution, so the sample must be taken under clean and dry environmental condition.

Environmental Recommendation

  1. Do not throw the sample oil in the sewage because this oil will prevent the swage treatment process collect in tight containers and return it back to the vendor to recycle it. (always remember that “this oil is load for your wastes but it is valuable material for them”.

  2. Avoid oil spillage to the ground. 

Tools and Equipment

Apparatus

  • Transformer (Step-up transformer).

  • Circuit-Interrupting Equipment.

  • Voltage-Control Equipment – the rate of voltage rise shall be 0.5 kv/s ± 20%.

  • Voltmeter.

  • Accuracy-The combined accuracy of the voltmeter and voltage divider circuit should be such that measurement error does not exceed 5% at the rate -of-voltage rise (0.5 kv/s ± 20%).

Electrodes

 The electrodes shall be polished brass spherically-capped electrodes of the VDE (Verband Deutscher Elektrotechniker, specification 0370) type having the dimensions shown in Fig. 1. They shall be mounted rigidly form opposite sides with the gap approximately centered.

 

 

Test Cell

The test cell shall be approximately cubical. A cell having a capacity of approximately 0.95 liter, has been found to be satisfactory for an electrode spacing of 2 mm or 0.080 in. A cell having a capacity of approximately 0.5 liter has been found to be satisfactory for an electrode spacing of 1 mm or 0.040 in.

The test cell shall be provided with a motor- driven two bladed impeller operating at a speed between 200 and 300rpm. The impeller, located below the lower edge of the electrodes, shall rotate in such a direction that the resulting liquid flow is directed downward against the bottom of the test cell.

The test cell shall be made of a material of high dielectric strength, shall be insoluble in and unattached by any of the cleaning or test liquids used, and shall be non absorbent to moisture and the cleaning and test liquids. So the breakdown may be observed, transparent materials are desirable, but not essential. In order to provided with a cover or baffle that will effectively prevent air from contacting the circulating liquid.

Adjustment and Care of Electrodes and Test Cell

Cleaning :

The electrodes and the cell shall be wiped clean with dry, lint-free tissue paper . It is important to avoid touching the electrodes or the cleaned gage with the fingers..After adjustment of the gap spacing, the cell shall be rinsed with a dry hydrocarbon solvent. A low- boiling solvent should not be used, as its rapid evaporation may cool the cell, causing moisture condensation .If this occurs, the cell should be slightly warmed to evaporate the moisture before using it.Care shall be taken to avoid touching the electrodes or the inside of the cell after cleaning. After thorough cleaning, the cell shall be flushed with new, dry, filtered oil of the type to be tested ( preferably degassed oil ). A voltage breakdown test shall be made on a sample of this oil in the manner specified in this test method.

Daily Use:

At the beginning of each day’s testing, the electrodes shall be examined for pitting and carbon accumulation, and the spacing checked. If the test of any sample is below the breakdown value being used by the operator as a minimum satisfactory value, the cell shall be drained and flushed with good quality oil of the type being tested before testing the next sample. When not in use, the cell shall be kept filled with good quality oil of the type normally tested.

Test Temperature:

The temperature of the sample when tested shall be the same as that of the room but the room temperature shall in no case be less than 20oC ( 68oF ). Testing liquids at temperatures lower than that of the room may give variable and unsatisfactory results.

Work to be Carried Out

Preparation of Sample

A sample of the oil to be tested shall be obtained with appropriate ASTM  sampling apparatus. The sample shall be taken in a dry ,clean bottle which shall be tightly scaled and shielded from light until ready to be tested.The sample container shall be gently inverted and swirled several times before filling the test cell. Repaid agitation is undesirable, since an excessive amount of air may be introduced into the liquid. Immediately after agitation, a small portion of the sample shall be used to rinse the test cell. The cell shall then be filled slowly with the remaining portion of the sample. There shall be an interval of at least 3 min between filling and application of voltage for the first breakdown, and at least 1 min intervals before applications of voltage for successive breakdown. During these intervals and at the time voltage is being applied, the propeller shall be circulating the oil.

Procedure

 
1.

Apply the voltage and increase from zero at the rate of approximately 0.5 kv/s until breakdown occurs, as indicated be operation of the circuit- interrupting equipment record the value. Occasional momentary discharges may occur which do not result in operation of the interrupting equipment : these shall be disregarded.

2.

Determine the value of the dielectric breakdown voltage of an oil by making five breakdowns on one filling of the cup. Examine the five breakdowns for statistical consistency.

  1. If they meet the criterion, use their average to determine the dielectric breakdown voltage of the sample but if they do not meet the criterion, make five additional breakdowns on the sample and use the average of all ten breakdowns as the dielectric  breakdown voltage of the sample.

Report

The report shall include the following:

 



 
 

-The test method used.
-The number of tests, the volts at each breakdown, and the average of all breakdowns reported to the nearest 1kv.If the test made are above or below the specified level, the report shall be made in one of the following ways

  • Passed if all of the five breakdown were above the specified level.

  • Failed if all of the five breakdown were below the specified level

  • Not less than the minimum of the five breakdowns  if all  were above the specified level.

  • Not greater than the maximum of the five breakdowns if all were below  the specified level.

-The approximate temperature of the oil at the time of the test.
-The electrode spacing.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Recommendation

 

 

 

 

 

The dielectric breakdown voltage of a liquid at commercial power frequencies is affected by the degree of uniformity of the electric field, the area of the electrodes or volume of the liquid under maximum stress, the temperature of the liquid (especially as it affects the relative saturation level of moisture in solution), gassing tendencies of the liquid under the influence of electric stress, concentration of dissolved gases (especially if saturation levels are exceeded as result of sudden cooling or decrease in pressure, which may cause the formation of gas bubbles), incompatibility with materials of construction, and velocity of flow. A decrease in dielectric strength of the liquid can have an accentuated effect on the electric creepage strength of solid insulating materials immersed in the liquid.

If the breakdown voltage is not in the proper range for the conditioned oil due to the existence of moisture or impurities it is recommended to send another sample to central chemical laboratory to observe the result or carry out the test in the filed.
