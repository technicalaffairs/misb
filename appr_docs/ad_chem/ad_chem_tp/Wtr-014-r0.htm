Introduction

This document outlines the testing procedure P1, which must be performed every three weeks as indicated by W3 in the above title block.

This method covers the photometric determination of molybdate-reactive silica. It is applicable to most waters but some waters may require filtration and dilution to remove interferences from color and turbidity. It is useful for concentrations as low as 20 µg /L to 1000 µg/L at the higher wavelength (815 nm) and 0.1 to 5 mg/L at the lower wavelength (640 nm).

Significance

Silica concentration is an important consideration in some industrial installation such as steam generation and cooling water systems. Under certain conditions. Silica forms troubles, some silica and silicate scales; particularly on high-pressure steam turbine blades. Silica removal is commonly accomplished by ion exchange, distillation, reverse osmosis, or by precipitation, usually with magnesium compounds in a hot or cold line softening process.

Summary of Method

This method is based on the reaction of the soluble silica with molybdate ion to form a greenish–yellow complex, which in turn is converted to a blue complex by reduction with     1-amino-2-naphthol-4-sulfonic acid.

Interferences

Color and turbidity will interfere if not removed by filtration or dilution. The only specific substance known to interfere in the color reaction is phosphate. Phosphate interference is eliminated by the addition of oxalic acid.

A high dissolved salts concentration, such as in seawater or brine samples, can affect color development. This can be compensated for by preparing standards in a matrix similar to that of samples or by using a standard additions technique.

Strong oxidizing and reducing agents that may be found in some industrial wastewaters may interfere in the reduction step of the reaction. Such wastewaters may also contain organic compounds that may interfere in the color formation.

Tools and Equipment

 

Apparatus

Spectrophotometer- To obtain maximum sensitivity and reproducibility, spectrophotometer readings must be made at 815 nm. However, measurements may be made in the range from 640 to 700 nm with sacrifice in precision.

 

Sample Cells- The cell size to be used depends on the range covered and the particular instrument used.

Reagents:

Note: store all reagents to be used in this method in polyethylene or other suitable plastic bottles.

Amino–Naphtol–Sulfonic Acid Solution.

Dissolve 0.5  gm of 1-amino-2-naphthol-4-sulfonic acid in 50 ml of a solution containing 1 gm of sodium sulfite ( Na2SO3).After dissolving, add the solution to100 ml of a solution containing 30 gm of sodium hydrogen sulfite ( NaHSO3 ). Make up to 200 ml and store in a dark, plastic bottle. Prepare a fresh solution every 2 weeks.

Ammonium Molybdate Solution (75g/L).

Dissolve 7.5 gm of ammonium molybdate {( NH4)6MO7O24.4H2O} in 100 ml of water.

Hydrochloric Acid (1+1)

Mix 1 volume of conc. HCL (sp gr 1.19) with 1 volume of water.

Oxalic Acid Solution(100g/L)

Dissolve 10 gm of oxalic acid H2C2O4.2H2O in 100 ml of water.

Silica, Standard Solution (1ml =0.1 mg SiO2)

Dissolved 0.473 gm of sodium meta silicate ( Na2SiO3.9H2O) in water dilute to 1L.

 

 

Worked to be Carried Out

Calibration and Standardization

Prepare a series of at least four standards covering the desired concentration range by proper dilution of the standard silica solution. Treat 50.0 ml aliquots of the standards in accordance with next procedure. Prepare a blank using a 50.0 ml aliquot of water that has been similarly treated.

For standards in the range from 20 to1000 µg/L, set the spectrophotometer at 815 nm and read the absorbance of each standard against the reagent blank.For standards in the 0.1 to 5 mg/L range, set the spectrophotometer at 640 nm (filter photometer 640 to 700 nm).

Prepare a calibration curve, for measurements at 815 nm by plotting absorbance versus micrograms of SiO2 per liter (ppb) on linear graph paper .For measurements at 640 nm, plot absorbance versus milligrams SiO2 per liter (ppm).

Procedure

  1. Transfer 50 ml or an aliquot diluted to 50 ml of the sample in polyethylene container and add  in quick succession,1 ml HCl (1 + 1) and 2 ml ammonium molybdate solution. Mix well.

  2. After 5 min, add 1.5 ml oxalic acid solution and again mix well.

  3. After 1 min, add 2 ml of amino-naphthol-sulfonic acid solution. Mix well and allow to stand for 10 min.

  4. Prepare a reagent blank by treating 50 ml aliquot of water.

  5. Measure the absorbance of the sample at 815 nm against the reagent blank or at 640 nm for higher concentrations.

Calculations

Calculation is not required. Silica concentration in micrograms SiO2 per liter may be read directly from the calibration curve at 815 nm and in milligrams from the calibration curve at 640 nm.

Precision

The collaborative test of this method was performed in reagent water by six determinations at each level for a total of 72 determinations at each level.

The over-all and single-operator precision of this method for measurements at 815 nm in reagent water are shown as follow.

 

So  =  0.005 X + 0.7

St  =  0.03 X  +  1.3

Where:

So =  single-operator precision, µg/L,

St  =  over-all  precision µg/L,

X  =  concentration of silica determined, µg/L.
