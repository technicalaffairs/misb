Standard Test Methods for Chloride Ion in Water

Test Method B ( Silver Nitrate Titration)

Introduction

This document outlines the testing procedures P1, which must be performed on a monthly basis as indicated by  M1 in the above title block, and according to the Directives of the Central Chemical Laboratories.

This test method is intended primarily for  water where the concentration of chloride in the range 8.0 to 250 mg/L chloride.

 

Summary of Method

Water adjusted to approximately PH 8.3 is titrated with silver nitrate in the presence of potassium chromate indicator. The end point is indicated by persistence of the brick-red silver chromate color.

 

Interferences

Bromide, Iodide and SulPHide are titrated along with the chloride. SulPHite, color and turbidity must be eliminated. Compounds which precipitate at PH=8.3 (certain hydroxides) may cause error by occlusion.

 

Safety Precautions

  • The sample must be taken as the applicable ASTM methods D 3370-76.

  • Make sure the sample bottle is correctly labeled.

  • Make sure the sample bottle is free from dust and dirt.

  • The sample must be kept away from exposure to direct sunlight.

  • Cleaning solvents such as acid dichromate, nitric acid and others are dangerous, so you have to wear breathing mask.

  • Keep this solvents closed. Do not get it in the eyes, on the skin, or on clothing. Do not breathe them, use only in a hood.

 

 

 

Tools and Equipment

 

Reagents

 

Hydrogen Peroxide- ( 30% ) H2O2.

PHenolPHthalein Indicator Solution- ( 10gm/L ).

Potassium Chromate Indicator Solution

  1. Dissolve 50 gm of K2CrO4 in 100 ml water.

  2. Add AgNO3 till slight reddish precipitate is produced. 

  3. Allow the solution to stand, protected from light for 24 hour. 

  4. Filter the solution to remove the precipitate.

  5. Dilute to 1 liter with water.

Standard Solution, Silver Nitrate ( 0.025N )

  1. Dissolve 4.2473 gm of crushed dried crystals of AgNO3 in H2O and dilute to 1 liter.

  2. Standardize against standard NaCl solution.

Standard Solution, Sodium Chloride ( 0.025 N )

  1. Dry several gms of NaCl for 1 hour at 600°C.

  2. Dissolve 1.4613 gm of dry salt in water . 

  3. Dilute to 1 liter in 25°C.

Sodium Hydroxide- ( 10 gm/ L )

 

SulPHuric acid ( 1+19)- add 1 volume of conc. H2SO4 ( sp. gr. 1.84 ) to 19 volume of H2O. Mix well.

 

Purity of Reagents

All reagents shall conform to the specifications of the committee on Analytical Reagents of the American Chemical Society, where such specifications are available. Other grades may be used, provided that the reagent is of high purity to permit its use without lessening the accuracy of the determination.

Sampling

Collect the sample in accordance with the applicable ASTM methods D1066 and D3370.

Work to be carried out 

 

 

 Procedure

  1. Pour 50 ml of the sample containing not more than 20 nor less than 0.25 mg of chloride ion, into a white porcelain container. If sulPHite ions are present, add 0.5 ml of hydrogen peroxide H2O2  to the sample, mix, and let stand for 1 min. Dilute to approximately 50 ml with water, if necessary.

  2.  Adjust the PH to the PHenolPHthalein end point ( PH = 8.3 ) using H2SO4( 1+19 ) or NaOH solution ( 10 g/L ).

  3. Add ≈1.0 ml of K2CrO4 indicator  solution and mix.

  4. Add standard silver nitrate ( AgNO3 ) solution dropwise from a 25 ml buret, until the brick-red (or pink) color persists through the sample when illuminated with a yellow light.

 

 

 

Calculation

Calculate the chloride ion concentration in the original sample, in ppm, as follows:

 

Chloride mg/L (ppm)  = V× N×70906

                                             S

Where

V =  Standard Solution AgNO3 added.

N = Normality of Standard AgNO3 Solution

S = Original Sample.

Precision

The precision of this method may be expressed as follows:

 

ST =  0.013X + 0.70

S0 =  0.007X + 0.53

Where

ST = over-all precision, mg/L ( ppm ) ,

S0 = single-operator precision, mg/L ( ppm ) ,

X = concentration of chloride ion determined.
