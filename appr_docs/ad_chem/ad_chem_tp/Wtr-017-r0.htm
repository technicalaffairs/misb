Introduction

This document outlines the testing procedures P1, which must be performed every six month  as indicated by M6 in the above title block, and according to the Directives of the Central Chemical Laboratories.

The coliform group consists of several genera of bacteria belonging to the family Enterobacteriaceae. Total coliform test is used as an indicator of faecal pollution.

The standard test for the coliform group may be carried out by the Multiple-Tube Fermentation Technique and is applicable within the limitations specified and with due consideration of the purpose of the examination.

When multiple tubes are used  in the fermentation technique, results of the examination of replicate tubes and dilutions are reported in terms of the Most Probable Number ( MPN ) of organisms present. This number, based on certain probability formulas, is an estimate of the mean density of coliforms in the sample. Coliform density, provides the best assessment of water treatment effectiveness and the sanitary quality of untreated water.

Significance of Method

Since coliform bacteria are present in excrement and can be detected in concentrations as low as 1 per 100 ml, they are considered as a sensitive indicator of the  presence of faecal pollution.

For raw water, the detection of  total coliform group,  and then the faecal coliform bacteria may be an adequate guide to the microbial quality of the water. In the control of water-treatment processes, coliform organisms (total coliforms) should not be detectable in finished water. Should any coliform organisms be present in a water supply, it is then important that confirmation be taken in order to determine if the contamination is faecal origin and to aid in tracing the source. This process takes place by the detection of special thermotolerant faecal  coliform organisms

Faecal (thermotolerant ) Coliform Organisms are coliform organisms that are able to ferment lactose at 44°C or 44.5°C, they comprise the genus Escherichia and to lesser extent occasional strains of Enterobacter, Citrobacter, and Klebsiella. Of these organisms, only E.Coli  is specifically of faecal origin. Therefore, detection and identification of E.Coli  is considered to provide sufficient information to assess the faecal nature of pollution.

Definition of the Coliform Organisms

The term coliform organisms (total coliform) refers to  all anaerobic and facultative anaerobic, gram-negative, non spore forming and rod-shaped bacteria capable of growth in the presence of bile-salts or other surface-active agents with similar growth inhibiting properties and able to ferment lactose at 35°C - 37°C with the production of acid, gas, and aldehyde within 24 to 48 hours.

Safety Precautions

  • Satisfactory facilities and equipment for the examination of drinking water are important for ensuring reliable and productive results, even in the presence of few numbers of faecal organisms.

  • Clean, comfortable working conditions are essential, not only to prevent cross contamination of samples, but also for the health and safety of laboratory staff.

  • Attention should be given to good laboratory practice, including the training of staff, the correct operation of incubators and water baths, and careful preparation of media, as well as the use of quality control procedures.

Dilution Water

  1. Buffered Water: To prepare stock phosphate buffer solution, dissolve 34 g potassium dihydrogen phosphate (KH2PO4), in 500 ml reagent grade water, adjust to PH 7.2 ± 0.5 with 1 N sodium hydroxide (NaOH), and dilute to 1 L with reagent grade water.

  2. Add 1.25 ml stock phosphate buffer solution and 5.0 ml magnesium chloride solution (81.1 g MgCl2.6H2O/L reagent grade water) to 1 L reagent grade water. Dispense in amounts that will provide 99 ± 2.0 ml or 9 ± 0.2 ml after autoclaving for 15 min.

Note: Media and diluted water should be sterilized at 120°C and pressure of 1.5 bar for 15 minutes.

 

Worked To be Carried Out

Procedure for Total Coliform Test

For Potable Water

Inoculate 10 fermentation tubes (each containing 10 ml of double strength Mac Conky broth.) with 10 ml of the sample of each.

For Other Water Samples

  1. Arrange fermentation tubes (each containing 10 ml of single strength Mac Conky broth. ) in rows of five tubes in a test tube rack . The number of rows and the sample volumes selected depend upon the quality and character of the water to be examined.

  2. Make sample dilutions (1, 0.1, 0.01 ml etc.). Shake sample and dilutions vigorously about 25 times.

  3. Inoculate each tube in a set of five with replicate sample volumes (in increasing decimal dilutions) .Mix test portions in the medium by gentle agitation.

  4. Incubate inoculated tubes at 35 or 37°C. After 24 ± 2 h swirl each tube gently and examine it for gas and acidic reaction, if no gas or acidic reaction is evident, reincubate and reexamine at the end of 48 ± 3 h.

  5. Production of acid and or gas in the tubes within 48 ± 3 h constitutes a positive presumptive reaction. Submit tubes with a positive presumptive reaction to the confirmed phase.

  6. The absence of acid and or gas formation at the end of 48 ± 3 h of incubation constitutes a negative test.

Procedure for Confirmed Phase Test

For Potable Water and Other Water Samples

  1. Gently shake or rotate presumptive tubes or bottles showing gas or acidic growth to resuspend the organisms. With a sterile loop 3.0 to 3.5 mm in diameter, transfer one or more loopfuls of culture to a fermentation tube containing brilliant green lactose bile broth or repeat for all other positive presumptive tubes.

  2. Incubate the inoculated brilliant green lactose bile broth tube at 35-37°C. Formation of gas in any amount in the inverted vial of the brilliant green lactose bile broth fermentation tube at any time within 48 ± 3 h constitutes a positive confirmed phase.

  3. Calculate the MPN value for the total coliform group from the number of positive brilliant green lactose bile broth tubes.

Procedure for Faecal Coliform Test

  1. Submit all presumptive fermentation tubes showing any amount of gas growth, or acidity within 48 h of incubation to the faecal coliform test.

  2. Gently shake or rotate the positive presumptive fermentation tubes. Using a sterile 3or 3.5 mm diameter loop, transfer growth from each presumptive fermentation tube to EC broth.

  3. Incubate inoculated EC broth tubes within 30 min after inoculation. Maintain a sufficient water depth in water bath incubator to immerse tubes till upper level of the medium.

  4. Gas production with growth in an EC broth culture within 24 ± 2 h or less is considered a positive faecal coliform reaction. Failure to produce gas ( with little or no growth ) constitutes a negative reaction.

  5. Calculate MPN  for the faecal coliform bacteria from the number of positive EC broth tubes.
