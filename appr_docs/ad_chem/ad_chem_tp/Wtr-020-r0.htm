Introduction

This document outlines the testing procedure P1, which must be performed on a weekly basis as indicated by W1 in the above title block .

Oil and Grease is any material recovered as a substance soluble in trichlorotrifluoroethane.   

Dissolved or emulsified oil and grease is extracted from water by intimate contact with trichlorotrifluoroethane.Some extractables, especially unsaturated fatty acids oxidize readily, hence, special precaution regarding temperature and solvent vapor displacement are included to minimize this effect.

Significance

The method is suitable for biological lipids and mineral hydrocarbons, and suitable for most industrial wastewaters or treated effluents containing these materials, when these materials are discharged in wastewater or  treated effluents

Sampling

Collect a representative sample in a glass bottle that has been rinsed with the solvent to remove any detergent film, and acidify in the sample bottle.

Safety Precautions

  • Acids such as hydrochloric acid and others are dangerous, so you have to wear breathing mask.

  • Keep these acids closed. Do not get it in the eyes, on the skin, or on clothing. Don't breathe them, use only in a hood.

  • Don't use any plastic tubing to transfer solvent between containers.

 

 

Tools and Equipment

Apparatus

  • Separatory funnel, 1L with TFE stopcock.   

  • Distilling flask, 125 ml.

  • Water bath.

  • Filter paper, (11 cm in diameter), Whatman No 40 or equivalent.

Reagents:

  • Hydrochloric Acid, HCl (1:1).

  • Trichlorotrifluoroethane (1,1,2-trichlor-1,2,2-trifluoroethane), boiling point 47°C.

  • Sodium  Sulphate, Na2SO4 anhydrous crystal.

Work to be Carried Out 

  1. Collect about 1L sample and mark sample level in bottle for later determination of sample volume.

  2. Acidify to PH 2 or lower, generally, 5 ml HCl is sufficient.

  3. Transfer to separatory funnel.

  4. Carefully rinse sample bottle with 30 ml trichlorotrifluoroethane and add solvent washing to separatory funnel. Preferably shake vigorously for 2 min.

  5. Let layer separate.

  6. Drain solvent layer through a funnel containing solvent-moistened filter paper into a clean tared distilling flask. ( Note ) if a clear solvent layer cannot be obtained, add 1 gm of Na2SO4 to the filter paper cone and slowly drain the emulsified solvent onto the crystals.

  7. Rinse sample container with adequate amount of solvent.

  8. Extract twice more with 30 ml solvent each.

  9. Combine the extracts in tared distilling flask and wash the filter paper with additional 10 to 20 ml of solvent. Distill solvent from distilling flask in water bath at 70°C for 15 minutes and draw air through it with an applied vacuum for the final 1 minute. Cool in desiccator for 30 min. and weigh.

 Calculation

If the organic solvent is free of residue, the gain in weigh of the tared distilling flask is mainly due to oil and grease. Total gain in weigh, A, of tared flask less calculate residue, B, from solvent blank is the amount of oil and grease in the sample:

 

 mg oil and grease / L =   ( A - B ) ×1000
