Significance

The desalination unit is based on the multi effect distillation process with a vapor recycle system. The multiple effect is achieved in a series of horizontal-tube, falling film type evaporative condensers. These serve as heat recovery effects, where in, through successive steps of evaporation and condensation at equilibrium vacuum conditions, a multiple amount of distillate (product) is derived from a given quantity of low-temperature steam input.

The train of evaporative-condenser units is divided into two groups of 3 effects each, for piping and feed pumping purposes. These groups are here after referred to as the hot, and cool, groups according to their location in the evaporator train. The cool group is adjacent to the main condenser, the hot group being at the opposite end.

The input steam is available at a pressure of 3.3 bar while that required by the evaporator first effect is about 0.3 bar, therefore, the thermal efficiency of the process is considerably increased by routing the input steam through a conventional jet ejector (thermal compressor), which compresses and recycles a substantial portion of the vapor generated in the last effect, and reintroduces it into the 1st effect vapor from the last effect not being sucked by the thermo compressor, passes to the heat rejection condenser which also serves to heat and dearate the incoming  sea water feed.

Work to be Carried out

Initially, it is better to refer that there are three types of starting the unit .

First Start

It includes part of the procedures in the commission of the plant and thereafter is to be used only after a major disassembly.

Cold Start

It is defined after a complete plant shut down to a cold condition. The plant may or may not have been opened for inspection or minor repairs.

Warm Start

It is defined as restarting the plant following a temporary stoppage, planned or due to an outage, during which the vacuum in the evaporators has fully or partially been retained.

Generally, in bringing the plant into operation, the various steps in the start-up procedures are grouped in sequence under the following headings:

Preparatory Inspection

Whenever the plant is to be operated following major disassembly or a prolonged shut down, a through inspection should be made of the  components to be operated, to ensure readiness of the installation. particular attention should be given to the following:

  1. All rotating equipment, including motors, are properly lubricated.

  2. Evaporator  feed treatment system is assembled ready for use.

  3. Check that all shut off valves to pressure gauges, level glass and other instruments are open.

  4. Coolant for mechanical seals is piped to the required pumps.

Filling the Evaporators to the Normal Operating Level

If the vessels are dry, the preparatory steps should also include the filling of all process vessels and tanks to normal operating water levels.

There are two methods for doing this, the option selected will depend upon the condition of the plant.

  1. If the unit is being started for the first time, or if work has been done on the internals, for instance in there is a possibility of foreign matter in the system, sea water can be used to fill all vessels and tanks. this will enable flushing out the system with a minimum waste of fresh water.

  2. If the unit has previously been in normal operation and is clean, then it is preferable to use fresh water for charging the condensate and product systems, limiting sea water to the coolant, feed and brine systems only.

Evaporator Evacuation

Production of full vacuum in the process unit may be under taken at some time as the low rate sea water circulation is being established.

  1. Full vacuum condition at the process unit are established when the pressure at the condenser as can observed in the field and on the control panel are about 75mm Hg absolute or 0.1 bar.

  2. Operate the evacuation of the unit by  the vacuum pump.

Final Preparation for Starting

  1. While the NCG (Non Condensable Gases) system is continuing to build the final vacuum, the sea water circulation can gradually be increased and the distribution adjusted as follows:

  2. Start slowly increasing both the rated of sea water supply to 50% and the feed until about 60% is reached.

  3. When water level have stabilized, manually adjust feed valves to give approximately equal of feed flows to effects No.1 in the hot group.

  4. Similarly adjust the feed valves to the cool group for equal distribution of the flow within each group.

  5. Increase both the sea water supply and the feed rate to obtain the full feed flow of about 22.0 m3/h to effect No.1

  6. When vacuum in the condenser has reached about 0.1 bar (75mm Hg Abs) the process may be started.

Starting the Process

  1. Open the steam supply line to the thermo compressor by first opening tie  in valve and then when steam starts to go out from stream trap station, close steam trap valve.

  2. Start the feed treatment dosing.

  3. When the water level in the product surge tank approaches the normal operating level, adjust the automatic set point of controller to match the actual level, and switch from MANUAL to AUTO gradually readjust the set point to 50%

  4. Open product out control valves A,B. these valves will be closed or open according to the product salinity as follows:

  • When conductivity exceeds 50 µs/ cm A will be closed and B open, to deliver the product steam to the brine pump discharge line.

  • When conductivity is below 50 µs/cm B will be closed and A open to discharge the product to the reservoir.

  1. Start to bring the process up to full capacity by increasing both the steam and the feed flow rated in steps.

  2. After the final increase have been made and operating conditions are steady, trim the adjustment of the feed flow manual control valves to each effect to give equal distribution to the effects within each group.
