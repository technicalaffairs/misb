Introduction

This method is used to determine the alkalinity of water imparted by the bicarbonate, carbonate, and hydroxide components of natural or treated water supply.

The first draft was initiated by chemist Amany Mohammed Mohammed EL-Hasanieen; reviewed by department director chemist: Zakia Soltan; revised by quality control chemist: Mohamed Mosbah Mokpel and accepted by Eng. Awad M. Fatthy; Head Sector of  Talkha Power Station.

Significance

Determination of bicarbonate, carbonate, hydroxide and total alkalinity  by titration with standrad solution of a strong mineral acid by means of color.

Residual chlorine affects the indicator color response and can be overcome by the addition of minimum volumes of sodium thiosulfate. Finely divided calcium carbonate and magnesium hydroxide which may cause a fading end point can removed by filtration through fine filter paper.

Summary of  Method

Titration of the sample with 0.02 N HCL using ph.ph. and M.O. as indicators.

 Sampling

For best results, samples should be collected in polyethylene or pyrex bottles, the alkalinity determination should be performed within 1day. Keep samples in a cool place; avoid agitation and contact with air if storage is required.

Safety Precautions

  • The reagent used must be of sufficiently high purity  to permit its use without lessening the accuracy of determination.

  • Water used for this method shall be free from carbon dioxide.

  • Samples containing free available residual chlorine and low alkalinity require pretreatment prior to examination.

  • Select an appropriate buret capacity so that the sample uses at least 20-30% of the total buret capacity, insuring volumetric accuracy.

Tools and Equipment

 

Apparatus

Day light fluorescent lamps.

Reagents

  1. Carbon Dioxide–free distilled water- Remove CO2 by boiling 15 minutes and cooling to room temperature just prior to use.

  2. Ph.Ph Indicator Solution.

  3. M.O. Indicator Solution.

  4. Sodium Carbonate Solution 0.05 N- Dry 3-5 g Na2CO3 at 250°C for 4 h and cool in a desicator. Weigh 2.5 ± 0.2 g, transfer to a 1-L volumetric flask, fill flask to the mark with distilled water, and dissolve and mix reagent. Don't keep longer than 1 week.

  5. Standard Hydrochloric Acid Titrant 0.02 N- Dilute 200 ml of the 0.1 N HCL to 1 litre  with CO2 free distilled water. Standardize by potentiometric titration of 15.00 ml  0.05 N Na2CO3. Calculate normality :

                       A × B

Normality,  N  =   ------------

                                                     53 × C

Where :

 

A  =   g Na2CO3 weighed into 1-L flask,

 

B  =   ml Na2CO3 solution taken for titration, and

 

C  =   ml acid used.

Note : A standard acid  solution exactly 0.02 N is equivalent to 1.00 mg CaCO3 per 1.00 ml.

 

Work to be Carried out

 

Procedure

 

 

Sample size

  • Samples have alkalinity of 500 mg/l  or higher use 50-100 ml of sample and titrate with  0.1 N HCL.

  • Samples have alkalinity of 150 mg/l or below  titrate with  0.02 N HCL.

  • Samples have  low alkalinity of 20 mg/l or below use 100-200 ml of sample and titrate with  0.02 N HCL.

  • If the nature  and concentration of the sample is totally unknown, use a 100 ml sample portion.

 Alkalinity by Ph.Ph. Indicator

  1. Add 0.1 ml (2 drops) ph.ph indicator to 50 ml of sample in erlenmeyer flask.

  2. Titrate over a white surface with 0.02 N HCl to the coloration corresponding to the proper equivalence point of  pH 8.3.

Total Alkalinity by M.O. Indicator

  1. Add 0.1ml (2 drops) M.O. indicator to the solution in which the ph.ph. alkalinity has been determined.

  2. Titrate over a white surface with 0.02N HCl standard acid to proper equivalence point.

  3. The indicator changes to orange at pH 4.6 and to pink at 4.3.

       Precision and Accuracy

A precision of  ± 1mg /L and an accuracy of ± 3 mg /L, expressed as CaCO3 can be achieved with reasonable good technique in the range between 10 and 500 mg / L.
