Introduction

This document outlines the testing procedures P1, which must be performed  every  month M1 as indicated  in the above title block and according to the Directives of the Central Chemical Laboratories.

This Method covers the precise measurement of PH in water utilizing at least two of seven standard reference buffer solutions for equipment standardization.

Significance

The PH determination of water is a relatively reliable indication of its acidic or alkaline tendency. It is not a measure of the quantity of acidity or alkalinity in a water sample (Refer to Methods D 1067).A PH value less than 7.0 shows a tendency toward acidity, while a value greater than 7.0 shows a tendency toward alkalinity.

Most natural waters range between 6.0 and 9.0, but there are notable exceptions such as mine drainage water and unbuffered rain water.

The PH measurement is an important consideration in determining the corrosive action of water and assessing water treatment practices for industrial processes.

Summary of Method

The PH meter and associated electrodes are standardized against two reference buffer solutions which are closest to the anticipated sample PH. The sample measurement is made under strictly controlled conditions and prescribed techniques.

Definitions

PH-  The negative logarithm to the base ten of the conventional hydrogen ion activity.

Purity of Reagents

All reagents shall conform to the specifications of the committee on Analytical Reagents of the American Chemical Society, where such specifications are available. Other grades may be used, provided that the reagent is of high purity to permit its use without lessening the accuracy of the determination.

The water used for the reference buffer solutions shall be prepared as specified for each standard buffer solution. Precautions shall be taken to prevent contamination of the water with traces of the material used for protection against carbon dioxide. The PH of the carbon dioxide - free water shall be between 6.5 and 7.5 at 25°C .

 

Sampling

Collect the sample in accordance with the applicable ASTM method as follows : Method D 1066,  specification D1192 and practices D3370.

Interferences

The true PH is affected by the temperature. The Electromotive Force between the glass electrode and the reference electrode is a function of temperature as well as PH. The temperature effect can be compensated for automatically or manually.

Tools and Equipments

Apparatus

  • Laboratory PH Meter.

  • Glass Electrode.

  • Reference Electrode.

  • OR Combined Electrode.

  • Temperature Compensator.

  • Process PH Measurement Instrumentation.

(Note 1) - Electrodes must be cleaned thoroughly after each measurement, by washing with  water, followed by several rinses with water, after that  immerse the lower third of the electrodes in HCL (1+9 ) to remove any film that may have been formed. Rinse it thoroughly by washing it in several changes of water before returning it to service.

Keep the immersible ends of the electrodes in water between measurements.

(Note 2) - For prolonged storage, glass electrodes may be allowed to become dry, but the junction and filling openings of reference electrodes( combined electrode) should be capped to decrease evaporation, and reference electrodes immersed in saturated potassium chloride solution.

 

Reagents

Reference Buffer Solutions

  1. PHosPHate Reference Buffer Solution (PH = 6.86 at 25°C ).

Dissolve 3.39 g of potassium dihydrogen PHosPHate ( KH2PO4 ) and 3.53 g of anhydrous

disodium hydrogen PHosPHate (Na2HPO4 ) in water and dilute to 1 litre.

PHosPHate salts must be dried for 2 hours in oven at 130° C before weighting.

   2.   PHthalate Reference Buffer Solution ( PH = 4.00 at 25°C ).

Dissolve10.12g of potassium hydrogen PHthalate(KHC8H4O4) in water and dilute to1 liter .

PHthalate salts must be dried for 2 hours in oven at 110°C before weighting.

   3.   Borax Reference Buffer Solutions (PH = 9.18 at 25°C ).

Dissolve 3.80g of sodium tetraborate decahydrate ( Na2B4O7.10 H2O) in water and dilute to1litre.Drying is not necessary.

 

(Note 3)- Keep all the reference buffer solutions will-Stoppard and replace at a shelf of 3 months, or sooner if a visible change is observed.

Work to be carried out 

 

 

Standardization of Assembly

Turn on the analyzer, allow it to warm up thoroughly and bring it to the electrical balance in accordance with the manufacture´s instructions.Wash the electrodes, and the sample cup by means of flowing stream from a wash bottle.

  1. If the anticipated PH of the solution to be tested is less than10.0,select two reference buffer solutions that have a PH value as close as possible to the solution to be tested.

  2. The difference in temperature between the reference solution and the water sample not more than 2°C.

  3. Fill the sample cup with the first reference buffer solution and immerse the electrodes.

  4. If the PH meter is of the zero- null type, set the dial of the meter to equal PH value of the reference buffer solution at the temperature of the buffer.

  5. Engage the operating button, and rotate the asymmetry potential knob until the meter is brought to balance. If the PH meter is of the direct reading type, engage the operating button, turn the range switch to the proper position, and rotate the potential knob until the reading corresponds to the temperature.

  6. Wash the electrodes and sample cup three times with water.

  7. Place the second reference buffer solution in the sample cup, and measure the PH value .Adjust the slope control until the reading corresponds to the temperature corrected value of the second reference buffer solution. Do not change the setting of the asymmetry knob in either case.  

  8. Use additional portions of the second reference buffer solution, as before, until two successive readings  differ by no more than 0.02 PH.

  9. If the anticipated PH of the test solution is greater than 10.0,use the high alkalinity type of electrode and observe the manufacture' s instructions.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Procedure

  1. Standardize the assembly and wash the electrodes by means of a flowing stream from a wash bottle.

  2. Place the water sample in a clean glass beaker provided with a thermometer.

  3. Equip the beaker with a small stirrer, operate the stirrer during the period of PH measurement at a rate to prevent splashing and to avoid loss or gain of acid or basic gases by interchange with the atmosPHere, Stop the stirrer during the peroid of measurement if streaming potentials are noted.

  4. Insert the electrodes and determine a preliminary PH value.

  5. Measure successive portions of the water sample until readings on two successive portions differ by no more than 0.03 units. Two or three portions will usually be sufficient if the water is well- buffered.

  6. Record the PH and temperature of the sample.

Calculation

Most PH meters are calibrated in PH units and the PH of the sample is obtained directly by reading the meter scale.

Report      

The report shall include the following :


 -  Temperature of measurement to the nearest 1°C.

 -  The PH of the test solution to the nearest 0.01 PH unit when the PH measurement lies

     between 1.0 and 12.0.

 -  The PH of the test solution to the nearest 0.1 PH unit when the PH measurement is less

     than1.0 and  greater than 12.0.

Precision    

Under the strict conditions set forth in this method, the precision is 0.05 PH units. In order to attain this precision, the condition of the instrumentation and the technique for standardization and operation is extremely important. When the PH is less than 1.0 and greater than 12.0, the precision is 0.1 PH units.
