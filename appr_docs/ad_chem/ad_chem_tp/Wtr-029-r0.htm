Introduction

This method is used to determine the un-ionized portions of  weakly ionized acids such as carbonic and hydrolyzing salts such as  Fe (II) or aluminum sulfates.

The first draft was initiated by chemist Amany Mohammed Mohammed EL-Hasanieen; reviewed by department director chemist: Zakia Soltan; revised by quality control chemist: Mohamed Mosbah Mokpel and accepted by Eng. Awad M. Fatthy; Head Sector of Talkha Power Station.

Significance

Determination of acid pollutants entering a water  supply by titrating with standard alkali.

Summary of  Method

Titration of the sample with standard 0.02 NaOH using ph.ph. as indicator at pH 8.3 to the faint pink color at room temperature.

Interferences

Dissolved gases contributing to acidity, such as CO2, hydrogen sulfite, or ammonia, may be lost or gain during sampling, storage, or titration. To overcome such effects by titrating to the end point promptly after opening sample container, avoid shaking or mixing, protecting samples from the atmosphere during titration, and letting sample become no warmer than it was at collection.

Residual chlorine may bleach methyl orange indicator in acid medium and can be overcome by dechlorinating the sample with 1 drop of 0.1M sodium thiosulfate.

Sampling and Storage

The sample of water must be collected in polyethylene or pyrex bottles, and stored at low temperature. Fill bottles completely and cap tightly.

Titrate the sample within 1 day, because waste samples may be subject to microbial action and to loss or gain of CO2 or other gases when exposed to air. If biological activity is suspected analyze within 6 hours.

Avoid sample agitation and prolonged exposure to air.

Safety Precautions

  • Store sodium hydroxide in a tightly rubber stoppered pyrex glass bottle.

  • The reagent used must be of sufficiently high purity  to permit its use without lessening the accuracy of determination.

  • Water used for this method shall be free from carbon dioxide.

  • Handle samples from such sources carefully to minimize the loss of dissolved gases.

  • Select an appropriate buret capacity so that the sample uses at least 20-30% of the total buret capacity, insuring volumetric accuracy.

Tools and Equipment

 

Apparatus

Day light fluorescent lamps.

      Reagents

  1. Carbon Dioxide–Free Distilled Water- Remove CO2 by boiling 15 minutes and cooling to room temperature just prior to use.

  2. Ph.Ph. Indicator Solution- Alcoholic, pH 8.3 indicator.

  3. Standard Sodium Hydroxide Titrant 0.02 N- Dilute 20.0 ml 1N NaOH with CO2–free distilled water to 1 litre, and store in a tightly rubber stoppered pyrex glass bottle protected from atmospheric CO2 by a soda lime tube.( For best results, prepare weekly ).Standardize the solution against 0.02 N HCl (which is standaredized against sodium carbonate, Na2CO3 )

Note: A standard NaOH solution exactly 0.02 N is equivalent to 1.00 mg CaCO3 per 1.00 ml.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Work to be Carried out

Procedure

Sample size

Use a sufficiently large volume of titrant (20 ml or more) to obtain good volumetric precision while keeping sample volume sufficiently small to permit sharp end points.

Samples have acidities < 1000 mg/l as CaCO3, use 50 ml or more of sample and titrate with  0.02 N NaOH.

Samples have acidities > 1000 mg/l as CaCO3, use 250 ml or less of sample  and titrate with  0.1 N NaOH.

If the nature  and concentration of the sample is totally unknown, use a 100 ml sample portion.

Ph.Ph. Titration at Room Temperature

  1. Add 0.15 ml (3 drops) ph.ph indicator to 50 ml of sample in erlenmeyer flask, over a white surface.

  2. Titrate with standard 0.02 N NaOH to the appearance of the faint pink color characteristic of pH 8.3.

Ph.Ph. Acidity at Boiling  Temperature

  1. Add 0.15–0.5 m1(3–10 drops) ph. ph. indicator to 50 m1 sample in erlenmyer flask over a white surface.

  2. Heat the sample to boiling and boil for 2 min.

  3. Titrate the hot sample with standard 0.02 N NaOH to a permanent end point.

Note: Phenolphthalein color change is from colorless to the first permanent pink.

Note: Measuring acidity at boiling temperature, to remove the volatile compounds such as CO2,  NH3, N2H4, at high concentrations which may affect the results.
