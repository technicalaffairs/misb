Introduction

This document outlines the testing procedure P1, which must be performed every month as indicated by M1 in the above title block

This method is applicable to the determination of soluble silica ( SiO2 ) in acid and alkaline solvents.

Summary of Method

Silicon compounds in the solvents are concentrated and precipitated as partially dehydrated  SiO2 by evaporation with hydrofluoric acid ( HF ). Dehydration is completed by ignition, and the silica is volatilized as silicon tetrafluoride ( Sif4 ).

Safety Precautions

  • The sample must be taken as the applicable ASTM methods D 3370-76.

  • Acids such as hydrofluoric acid and others are dangerous, so you have to wear breathing mask.

  • Keep these acids closed. Do not get it in the eyes, on the skin, or on clothing. Do not breathe them, use only in a hood.

Tools and Equipment

Apparatus

Filter paper,

Oven,

Crucible,

Electronic balance, and

Fuming cub ( hood ).

Interferences

The anions and cations generally found in cleaning solvents do not interfere with the procedure.

 

 

Reagents

Hydrofluoric Acid ( HF ),

Concentrated Sulfuric Acid ( H2SO4).

Work To be Carried Out

Procedure

  1. Weigh an empty and clean crucible (W1).

  2. Put the insoluble part of the sample after the solubility in the crucible.

  3. Ignite the crucible in the oven at 750oC.

  4. Weigh the ignited crucible after ignition and stay the weight of the ignited residue is stable (W2).

  5. Add hydrofloric acid and 2 drops of conc H2SO4 and evaporate until SiF4 is completely evaporated. Repeat the last step until the weight of crucible is stable (W3).

Calculation

Calculate the weight percent of  ( SiO2 ) in the sample as follows:

 

SiO2, % = (  ( W2- W1 ) - ( W3-W1 ) )  100 / W

 

Where:

 

W  =  Weight of original sample,

W1 =  Weight of the empty crucible,

W2  =  Weight of crucible and sample residue after first ignition,

W3  =  Weight of crucible and sample residue after treatment with hydrofluoric acid ( HF ) and ignition.
