Introduction

This method is used for controlling Aluminum in outlet of filters ( in case of using Alum in clarification) and effluent of  industrial waste water plant.

The maximum limit for Aluminum concentration in industrial wastewaters effluent according to Egyptian Law Number (4) for year 1994 is as follows:

For effluent of industrial wastewaters treatment, max. (3 mg/L) and tested once monthly.

For drinking water, max. (0.2 mg/L) and tested once weekly.

This experiment is tested for total Aluminum ( soluble, colloidal and insoluble ).

Aluminum is the third most abundant element of the earth’s crust, occurring in minerals, rocks, and nearly all natural water as a soluble salt, a colloid or an insoluble compound or wastewater as a residual from alum coagulation. Recent work indicates that filtered water from a modern rapid sand filtration plant should have an aluminum concentration no greater than 50 μg/l.

Significance

With Eriochrome cyanine R dye, dilute aluminum solutions buffered to a pH of 6.0 produce a red to pink complex that exhibits maximum absorption at 535 nm. The intensity of the developed color is influenced by the aluminum concentration, reaction time, temperature, pH, alkalinity and concentration of other ions in the sample. To compensate for color and turbidity, the aluminum in one portion of sample is complexed with EDTA to provide a blank.

The optimum aluminum range lies between 20 and 300 mg/L but can be extended upward by sample dilution.

Sampling Handling

Collect samples in clean, acid-rinsed bottles. Preferably plastic, and examine them as soon as possible after collecting. If only soluble aluminum is to be determined. Filter a portion of sample through a 0.45 μm membrane filter; discard first 50 ml of filtrate and use succeeding filtrate for the determination. Don’t use filter paper, absorbent cotton, or glass wool for filtering any solution that is to be tested for aluminum, because they will remove most of the solution aluminum.

Interference

Negative errors are caused by both fluoride and polyphosphates, when the fluoride concentration is constant, the percentage error decreases with increasing amounts of aluminum. Because the fluoride concentration often is known or can be determined readily, fairly accurate results can be obtained by adding the known amount of fluoride to a set of standards.

A procedure is given for the removal of complex phosphate interference. Orthophosphate in concentration under 10 mg/l doesn't interfere. The interference caused by even small amounts of alkalinity is removed by acidifying the sample just beyond the neutralization point of methyl orange. Sulfate doesn't interfere up to a concentration of 2000 mg/l.

The interference of iron and manganese, two elements often found in water, is eliminated by adding ascorbic acid.

Safety Precautions

  • Make sure the sample bottle is correctly labeled.

  • Put the sample in acid-rinsed bottles, preferably plastic bottle.

  • Acids such as sulfuric acid, nitric acid and others are dangerous, so you have to wear breathing mask.

  • Keep these acids closed. Do not get it in the eyes, on the skin, or on clothing. Do not breathe them, use only in a hood.

Tools and Equipment

Apparatus

  • Spectrophotometer, for use at 535 nm, with a light path of 1 cm or longer.

  • Glassware, treat all glassware with warm 1+1 HCl and rinse with aluminum free distilled water to avoid errors due to materials absorbed on the glass.                                  

Reagents

  1. Sulfuric Acid- H2SO4, 0.02 N.

  2. Ascorbic Acid Solution- Dissolve 0.1 g ascorbic acid in water and make up to 100 ml in a volumetric flask. Prepare fresh daily.

  3. Buffer Reagent- Dissolve 136 gm sodium acetate, NaC2H3O2 .3H2O in water, add 40 ml 1N acetic acid, and dilute to 1L.

  4. Stock Dye Solution, Use Solochrome Cyanine- Dissolve 100 mg in water and dilute to 100 ml in a volumetric flask, this solution should have a pH of about 2.9.

  5. Working Dye Solution- Dilute 10 ml of stock dye solution to 100 ml in a volumetric flask with water. Working solutions are stable for at least 6 months.

  6. Stock Aluminum Solution- Dissolve 8.792 gm aluminum potassium sulfate Al K (SO4)2 .12H2O in water and dilute to1000 ml. ( 1 ml = 500 mg Al ).

  7. Standard Aluminum Solution- Dilute 10 ml stock aluminum solution to 1000 ml with water. ( 1 ml = 5 mg Al ) prepare daily.

Work to be Carried out

 

Preparation of Calibration Curve               

  1. Prepare a series of aluminum standard from 0 to 280 mg/L based on a 25 ml sample.

  2. Add 1 ml 0.02N H2SO4 to each standard and mix. Add 1 ml ascorbic acid solution and mix. Add 10 ml buffer solution and mix with a volumetric pipet, add 5 ml working dye reagent and mix. Immediately make up to 50 ml with distilled water. Mix and let stand for 5 to 10 min.

  3. Read absorbance on a spectrophotometer using wavelength of 535 nm.

  4. Plot concentration of Al against absorbance.

Sample Treatment

  1. Use 25 ml sample and the same procedure and repeat steps 2,3,4.

  2. To one sample add 1ml EDTA solution. This will serve as a blank by complexing any aluminum present and compensating for color and turbidity.
