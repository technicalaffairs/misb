Introduction

This document outlines the testing procedure P1, which must be performed about twice weekly as indicated by W2 in the above title block

 

 

 

 

The BOD test is essentially a bioassay procedure involving the measurement of oxygen consumed by living organisms (mainly bacteria) while utilizing organic matter present in waste, under conditions as similar as possible to those that occur in natural.

Significance

The BOD test is widely used to determine the pollutional strength of domestic and industrial wastes in terms of the oxygen that they will required if discharged into natural watercourses in which aerobic conditions exist. This test is of prime importance in regulatory work and in studies designed to evaluate the purification capacity of receiving bodies of water.

Definition

Biochemical Oxygen Demand is defined as the amount of oxygen required by bacteria while stabilizing decomposable organic matter under aerobic conditions.

The test has been developed on the basis of 5-days incubation period because it has been found by experience that a reasonably large percentage of the total BOD ( 70 to 80 %) is exerted in 5-days and it is also selected to minimized interference from oxidation of ammonia.

 

 

Sampling

  1. Clean the bottles by use of chromic acid or a good grade of detergent.

  2. The bottles must be rinsed with hot water to kill nitrifying organism, then the bottles are rinsed four times with tap water and a final rinse with distilled or demineralized water.

  3. Collect surface water samples in narrow- mouth glass stopper BOD bottles 300 mL.

  4. Sampling from a line under pressure attached a glass or rubber tube  to the tap and extend to bottom of bottle, let bottle overflow two or three times its volume and replace so that no air bubbles are entrained.

  5. Record sample temperature.

 

 

 

 

Safety Precautions

  • The sample must be kept away from direct sunlight.

  • Make sure the sample bottles is correctly labeled.

  • Make sure the sample bottles is free from dust and organic matter.

  • The bottles used for BOD should be equipped with glass stopper that are ground to a point to prevent trapping of air when the stopper is inserted.

  • The bottles must be equipped with some of water seal to prevent air from entering the bottle during the incubation period.

  • Interference of reducing and oxidizing material are masked by low pH value used and addition of NaN3.

Tools and Equipment

Two 300 ml  BOD Bottle, Pipettes (2mL- 3mL- 5mL).

 

Reagents

Manganous Sulfate Solution-Dissolve 364g of manganous sulfate MnSO4.H2O in distilled water, and dilute to1 liter.

Alkali-Iodide, Azide Reagent-Dissolve 500g of sodium hydroxide NaOH  and 135g NaI in distilled water and dilute to 1 liter. Add 10g NaN3 dissolved in 40mL distilled water

Sodium ThioSulfate Standard Solution (0.025M)-Dissolve 6.025g of Na2S2O3.5H2O in distilled water. Standardize with Bi-Iodate solution.

Standard Potassium Bi-Iodate Solution (0.0021M)-Dissolve 812.4mg KH(IO3)2 in distilled water, and dilute to1 liter.  

Starch Solution- Use either an aqueous solution or a soluble starch powder mixtures.

Sulfuric Acid -Concentrated sulfuric acid H2SO4 one milliliter is equivalent to about 3mL Alkali-Iodide, Azide Reagent.

Standardization

  1. Dissolve approximately 2g KI free from iodate in flask with 100mL to 150mL distilled water, add 1mL (6N) H2SO4 or few drops of conc. H2SO4 and 20mL Standard Bi-Iodate solution.

  2. Dilute to 200mL and titrate librated iodine with thiosulfate titrant adding starch toward the end of titration when pale straw color is reached. When the solutions are of equal strength, 20mL (0.025M) Na2S2O3 should be required if not adjust the Na2S2O3 solution to (0.025M).

 

 

 

Work To be Carried Out

Procedure

  1. Filled two or more BOD bottles with sample at least one is analyzed for dissolved oxygen immediately at power station by fixation with manganous sulfate and sodium azide, and the others are incubated for 5-days at 20oC.

  2. Determine the amount of dissolved oxygen remaining in the incubated samples after 5-days, as shown in the dissolved oxygen test. 

Calculation

Calculate the 5-days BOD by subtracting of  5-days results from those obtained on day 0.

                                

BOD in mg/l   =   Doi - Dof

 

 

Where:

 

 

 

 

Doi   =  Dissolved Oxygen initial at zero day,

 

 

Dof   =  Dissolved Oxygen final after 5 days.
