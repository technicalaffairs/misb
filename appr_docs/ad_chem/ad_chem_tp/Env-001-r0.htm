Introduction

Air is the most important requirement for life on our planet. Each person requires an average of 20 Kg of air /day, which is about 15 times more than the same person's daily food requirements. The quality of air is therefore a major health concern. Air pollution can be caused by either gaseous or particulate pollutants or both.

Emitted flue gases more than the permissible value, according to the law of environment 4 year 1994, are not allowed. Therefore the environmental administration in Talkha Power Station makes a scientific program in order to avoid and protect all the worked people in the station from the hazards.

Safety Precautions

Before switching on the apparatus make sure that the power supply is suitable for the instrument.

The mains/power plug shall only be inserted in a socket outlet provided with a protective earth contact. The protective action must not be negated by the use of an extension card without a protective conductor.

Warning

  1. Any interruption of the protective conductor inside or outside the instrument or disconnection of the protective earth terminal is likely to make the apparatus dangerous. Intentional interruption is prohibited.

  2. When the apparatus is connected its supply, terminals may be live, and the opening of covers of removal of parts ( except those to which access can be gained by hand ) is likely to expose live parts.

  3. Any adjustment, maintenance and repair of the opened apparatus under voltage shall be avoided as far as possible and, if inevitable, shall be carried out only by a shelled person who is aware of the hazard involved.

  4. Make sure that only fuses with the required rated current and of the specified type are used for replacement.

  5. The use of makeshift fused and the short – circuiting of fuse holders are prohibited.

  6. Whenever it is likely that the protection has been impaired, the apparatus shall be made inoperative and be secured against any an intended operation.

  7. The protection is likely to be impaired if, for example, the apparatus:

  • Shows visible damage.

  • Fails to perform the intended measurements.

  • Has been subjected to prolonged storage under unfavorable conditions .

  • Has been subjected to severe transport stresses.

Safety Aspects Continued

Caution

Due caution should be exercised when installing, operation and servicing this equipment. Attention is drawn to the following safety points:

Power

Ensure that main supplies are isolated, when making access to the equipment for maintenance or servicing purposes. Read the instructions carefully to ensure correction of all mains, signal leads and probe connections.

Eye Protection

Eyes and face should be suitable protected by goggles when looking into hot furnaces. Precaution should also be taken when removing instruments from pressurized ducts.

 

 

 

          Protective Clothing

When working in the vicinity of hot boilers and furnaces, protective clothing should be worn at all times, particularly protection of the hands.

Fault Finding

The instrument must be fully isolated from the power supply before internal, electrical maintenance or servicing work, and should only be undertaken by a suitable authorized/ qualified engineer.

Static Discharge

Under certain boiler conditions a high static charge may build up. With non conductive stack entry points the normal procedure to avoid damage to the instrument or person, is to make a connection from the probe extension to ground /earth before inserting into stack. With conductive (metallic) entry points the metal surround should be grounded.

Situations may occur, for instance where the sampled hot gases have just passed through electrostatic precipitators, where a high static charge would build up on the instrument probe if ungrounded. Further contact by any grounded object, i.e. a person touching the keyboard and forming a discharge path, may then result in damage to the instrument.

Hazardous Gases

All recognized safety procedures should be used when operating with hazardous gases.

Land Combustion accepts no responsibility for damage to the instrument, or injury to the person while following procedures, nor will the company accepts liability for failure to comply with safety precautions.

Smoke Measurements                   

Note: A special heated smoke probe must be used to perform any smoke measurements.

 

 

 

 

  1. Select the SMOKE option from the main menu. Follow on screen instructions.

  2. The heated smoke probe is fitted in place of the gas probe. The smoke probe connects using the same input ports as the standard gas probe. Allow approximately 1 minute for the probe to heat up.

  3. Insert filter paper in the holder inside the probe handle mechanism. Once ready insert the probe into the flue gas stream.

  4. Press GO (F4) on the instrument.

  5. The elapse time necessary for the probe to make an accurate measurement can be set in the SETUP – SYSTEM menu. It is possible to adjust both the elapse time (default time 30 sec. and the pump speed default 7500 to either increase or decrease the quantity of smoke deposited on the filter. A flow meter (not supplied) is necessary to determine flow rate of the flue gas, to calculate the elapse time to make an accurate reading.
    Note: Regulations govern the exact conditions of acceptable smoke measurement. This is dependent upon the country and the regulatory body. Check for local regulations to ensure the measurement meets the accepted standard.

  6. The elapse time will count down until sufficient smoke as passed the filter to make an acceptable measurement.
     

  7. Remove the smoke filter paper from pistol grip handle.

  8. The grayness of the paper has to be compared against a standard Bacharach chart to give an indication of density.
