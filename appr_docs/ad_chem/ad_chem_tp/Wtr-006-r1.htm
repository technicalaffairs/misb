Introduction

This document outlines the testing procedures P1, which must be performed on a monthly basis as indicated by  M1 in the above title block, and according to the Directives of the Central Chemical Laboratories.

This method covers the determination of nitrate ion in water. Samples containing 1 to 50 mg/Liter of nitrate, as NO3, may be analyzed by this method. The concentration range may be extended by dilution of an appropriate aliquot.

Summary of Method

Nitrate ion reacts with brucine in strong sulfuric acid solution to develop a yellow color. The color does not follow the Beer-Lambert relationship. However, a plot of absorbance versus concentration produces a smooth curve. It is necessary to develop a series of standards simultaneously with the samples. The color is measured at 410 nm.

The rate of color development varies directly with the temperature and the intensity varies inversely with the temperature.

Interferences

Strong oxidizing or reducing agents interfere. Oxidizing agents may be determined with orthotolidine. Chlorine interference up to 5 mg/liter may be eliminated by the addition of sodium arsenite.

High content of organic matter may interfere by increasing the absorbance readings at 410 nm and also because of the possible charring effects from the concentrated sulfuric acid (H2SO4).This can be minimized by  pretreatment with aluminum hydroxide and specially treated activated carbon.

Nitrite interference up to 7 mg/liter is eliminated by the sulfanilic acid in the brucine reagent solution.

Safety Precautions

  • The sample must be taken as the applicable ASTM methods D 3370-76.

  • Make sure the sample bottle is correctly labeled.

  • Make sure the sample bottle is free from dust and dirt.

  • The sample must be kept away from exposure to direct sunlight.

  • Cleaning solvents such as acid dichromate, nitric acid and others are dangerous, so you have to wear breathing mask.

  • Keep this solvents closed. Do not get it in the eyes, on the skin, or on clothing. Do not breathe them, use only in a hood.

Tools and Equipment

 

Apparatus

Photometer

A spectrophotometer or filter photometer suitable for measurements at 410 nm with a light path of 1 to 5 cm.

Reagents and Materials

Purity of Reagents

All reagents shall conform to the specifications of the committee on Analytical Reagents of the American Chemical Society, where such specifications are available. Other grades may be used, provided that the reagent is of high purity to permit its use without lessening the accuracy of the determination.

Purity of Water

Unless otherwise indicated, references to water shall be understood to mean reagent water conforming to Type 1 of ASTM Specification D 1193, for reagent water.

Activated Carbon

Heat fine grain (0.2 to 0.5-mm diameter ) activated carbon to red heat in an atmosphere of carbon dioxide , cool, extract with n-hexane, and wash with water at 80°C.

Aluminum Hydroxide Cream

Dissolve 124 g of potassium or ammonium alum in 1 liter of water. Heat to 50°C and slowly add with stirring 55 ml of ammonium hydroxide ( NH4OH, sp gr 0.90 ). Let stand 1h, and then wash the precipitate with water by mixing and decanting until free from ammonia chloride, nitrate, and nitrite ions.

Brucine - Sulfanilic Acid Solution

Dissolve 1 g of brucine sulfate ( Caution: Note1 ) and 0.1 g of sulfanilic acid in approximately 70 ml of hot water. Add 3 ml of hydrochloric acid ( HCl, sp gr 1.19 ), cool, and dilute to 100 ml with water. This solution is stable for several months. The pink color that develops slowly does not affect its usefulness.

Note 1: Caution- Brucine sulfate is very toxic; take care to avoid ingestion.

 Potassium Nitrate, Standard Solution

(1 ml = 1 mg NO3)-Dry potassium nitrate (KNO3) in an oven at105°C for 24 h. Dissolve 1.6306 gm in 20 ml of water and dilute to 1 liter.

 

 

 

Sodium Arsenite Solution ( 1.83 g / liter )

Dissolve 1.83 g of sodium arsenite ( NaAsO2 ) ( Caution: Note 2 ) in 1 liter of water.

Note 2: Caution- Toxic: take care to avoid ingestion.

 Sulfuric Acid ( 500+ 75 )  

Carefully add 500 ml of sulfuric acid ( H2SO4, sp gr 1.84 ) to 75 ml of water. Cool and keep in a tightly stoppered container to prevent absorption of moisture.

Sampling

Collect the sample in accordance with the applicable ASTM method, as follows: Sampling Water and Steam, and D3370- Practices for Sampling Water.

 

Work to be carried out 

 

Calibration and Standardization

  1. Prepare a blank and a series of standards using the standards  nitrate solution. The standards should cover  the range from 0 to 50 mg/liter of nitrate as No3.

  2. Treat in accordance with procedure from 3 to 9.

  3. Plot the absorbance versus the nitrate concentration in mg / liter.

  4. Prepare a calibration curve with each batch of samples analyzed.

 Procedure

  1. If residual chlorine is present, add 0.1 ml of arsenite solution to a 50-ml portion of sample for each 0.05 mg/liter of chlorine. Add 1 drop in excess and mix.

  2. If the water is turbid or has high organic matter ( color ), remove by adding 0.5 g of the specially treated activated carbon and 3 ml of aluminum hydroxide cream to 150 ml of the sample. Mix well, allow to stand for a few minutes and filter, discarding the first portion of the filtrate.

  3. Into a clean dry 50-ml beaker, pipet a 5.0-ml portion of the filtrate from the previous step or of the clear, colorless water containing not more than 50 mg/ liter of nitrate ion.

  4. Add 1.0 ml of brucine-sulfanilic acid reagent.

  5. Into a second 50-ml beaker measure 10 ml of H2SO4.

  6. Mix the contents of the two beakers by carefully adding the sample with the brucine-sulfanilic acid reagent to the beaker containing the acid. Pour from one beaker to the other 6 times to ensure mixing.

  7. Set aside for 10 ±1 min in a dark place.

  8. While the color is developing add 10 ml of water to the empty 50-ml beaker . After 10 min interval, add the 10 ml of water to the sample and mix as before. Allow to cool 20 to 30 min in the dark.

  9. Measure the absorbance of the treated sample at 410 nm against a blank treated similarly to the sample except for omitting the addition of the brucine-sulfanilic acid reagent.

  10. Determine the concentration of NO3 in the sample from a newly prepared calibration curve, correcting the observed value for any sample dilution.

Calculation

Read the nitrate ion concentration directly from the calibration curve in mg/liter.
