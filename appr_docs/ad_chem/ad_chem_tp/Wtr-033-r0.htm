Introduction

Total nonfilterable residue is the retained material on standard glass fiber filter after filtration of a well mixed sample. The residue is dried at 103 to 105oC to a constant weight. If the suspended material clogs the filter and prolongs filtration, the difference between the total residue and the total filtrable residue provides an estimate of the total non-filtrable residue.

Volatile non filtrable residue and fixed non filtrable residue can be determined on the material retained on the glass- fiber filters in the gooch crucibles on completion of the drying at 103 to 105oC.

Significance

Determination of total suspended solids in water by filtration of a well mixed sample. The residue is dried at 103 to 105oC to a constant weight. Results are reported as milligrams of  total nonfilterable residue per liter of sample.

Safety Precautions

  • The sample must be taken as the applicable ASTM methods D 3370-76.

  • Acids such as sulfuric acid and others are dangerous, so you have to wear breathing mask.         

  • Keep these acids closed. Do not get it in the eyes, on the skin, or on clothing. Don't breathe them, use only in a hood.

  • The reagent used must be of sufficiently high purity  to permit its use without lessening the accuracy of determination.

  • Store sample in glass or plastic bottles.

  • Some evaporation residues readily absorb moisture; rapid weighing is essential. Some residues contain  materials, such as ammonium carbonate, that decompose below 103oC; others contain liquids, such as glycerol and sulfuric acid, that will remain as a liquid residue at 103oC with or without solution of salts that might also be present.

Reagents

      Work to be Carried out

 

 

Preparation of Glass Fiber Filter

  1. Place filter either on membrane filter apparatus or the bottom of a suitable gooch crucible.

  2. Apply vacuum and wash filter with three successive 20-ml portion of distilled water. Continue suction to remove all traces of water, and discard washings.

  3. Remove filter from membrane filter apparatus and transfer to an aluminum or stainless steel planchet as a support.

  4. Remove crucible and filter combination if a  gooch crucible is used.

  5. Dry in an oven at 103 to 105oC for 1 hr. Store in desiccator until needed. Weigh immediately before use.

Sample Treatment

  1. Because excessive residue on the filter may entrap water and extend drying time take for analysis a sample volume that will yield between 2.5 mg and 200 mg total non filtrable residue as a practical limit, filter 100 ml of well mixed sample under vacuum.

  2. Wash filter with three successive 10 portions of distilled water. Remove filter from membrane filter funnel assembly  carefully and transfer to an aluminum or stainless steel planchet as a support.

  3. Remove crucible and filter combination from crucible adapter  if a  gooch crucible is used.

  4. Dry for at least 1 hr. at 103 to 105oC, cool in  desiccator to balance temperature and weigh.

  5. Repeat drying cycle until a constant weight is attained, or until weight loss is less than 4% of previous weight or 0.5 mg, whichever is less.
