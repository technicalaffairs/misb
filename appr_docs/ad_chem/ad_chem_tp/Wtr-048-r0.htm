Introduction

The Hydrogen electrolyzing plant generates Hydrogen and Oxygen by the electrolysis of water. The installation will be constructed in order to fulfill the following functions:

  1. Delivery of Hydrogen of 0.7 MPa constant pressure for cooling the 200 MW generator.

  2. Filling of Bottles with High-Pressure Hydrogen.

  3. Filling of Bottles with High-Pressure Oxygen.

  4. Safe Discharge of Oxygen into the Atmosphere, if it should not be filled into Oxygen Bottles.

Circuit of Electrolyte

For the electrolytic process an 28% solution of potassium hydroxide (KOH) will be used as an electrolyte. The electrolyte is prepared in a dissolving vessel of carbon steel, into which it is pumped from the electrolyzer by a pump. This pump is of cast iron. The vessel has a withdrawable basket for dissolving the KOH.

Circuit of Feedwater

The demineralized water needed for the electrolytic process is supplied by the piping into the equalizing and storing vessel. From here, the water is lead into the electrolyzer and/or into the dissolving vessel of the electrolyte. This vessel is made of PVC.

The conductivity of the demi-water will be continuously measured at the intake. Conductance = (2µs/cm).

Circuit of Hydrogen

The produced hydrogen is delivered into the gas reservoir. The reservoir will have a gas inlet and outlet and a water inlet and overflow. The level regulator controls the volume of hydrogen production by adjusting the electric current of the rectifier. The hydrogen from the reservoir is pressed by a four-stage compressor cooled by air to 20 MPa outlet pressure. Into the suction part of the compressor, a filter for trapping eventual traces of KOH is inserted. The filter body is of carbon steel, the candle filter of polypropylene. The filling station will be at the same time for filling four high-pressure steel bottles with Hydrogen to 20 MPa final pressure.

Oxygen Circuit

The produced Oxygen is accumulated in the gas reservoir of similar structure than the hydrogen reservoir. The Oxygen is further pressed by a double stage membrane compressor, where its parts touched by the Oxygen are made of stainless steel. Into the suction part, a filter for trapping eventual traces of KOH to minimum  is inserted and is of similar structure as that in the hydrogen circuit. Also the station for oxygen filling will be of similar structure as that for hydrogen filling. It is proposed for alternating filling of 2 bottles.

Procedure

The water electrolysis is the most simple procedure in order to produce Hydrogen and Oxygen of high purity. Water electrolysis needs little space and can be easily integrated into each industrial plant.

You only need water and electrical energy therefore largely independent of raw material and position. No waste and side products result, so no environmental problems present. The gases are produced in one process, therefore high operational safety, automatic operation, simple way of handling and maintaining. We can regulate the gas production between 25 and 110 % of the nominal capacity therefore automatic adjustment to consumption and economical use of night tariff. Pure gases are always available because only pure water is decomposed and the water electrolysis can be started and stopped within a few minutes.

The electrolytical decomposition of water into its elements; Hydrogen H2 and Oxygen O2 follows the equation

2 H2O + 2 e-  -------->  2 H+ + 2 OH - (cathode)

2 OH -        -------->     O + H2O + 2 e - (anode)

 H2O   -------->     H2 +1/2 O2

The production is affected by a flow of direct current in the cells of the electrolyzer. Hydrogen is generated at the cathode and Oxygen at the anode.

Pure potassium hydroxide (KOH) is only used as a conductor to increase the conductivity of the feedwater. Because only pure water is decomposed the concentration of the electrolyte remains constant and the decomposed water has to be supplied.

The gas production, according to the law of Faraday, is in direct proportion to the electric energy used, 96500 Coulomb (ampere seconds) produce 1.008g H2 and 8.000g O2. The current exploitation is roughly 99% so that the specific energy used is only determined by the cell voltage.

Operation Regimes, Starting and Stopping

The electrolyzer itself can work within an operation extent from 30 to 100%. The volume of production is controlled by the gas level regulator in the hydrogen reservoir. The production of hydrogen has to be so secured that the pressure in the central distribution system for the generator does not drop below the absolute value of 0.7 MPa. Hydrogen and Oxygen for filling the bottles at the filling stations will be taken irregularily according to the needs of the power plant operation. If the filling of the bottles with oxygen is not required at the same time as the hydrogen is being produced, the oxygen will be discharged as by-product into the atmosphere.

During the first 1000 hours of the electrolyzer operation, it will be operated with an electrolyte concentration of 8% KOH with slightly reduced output.

The starting and stopping lasts only a few minutes and the installation gets to its full power with the required quality very quickly.

If the installation gets full of air, when the operation is stopped or at its first start, the hydrogen circuit is to be rinsed by nitrogen from the supplied bottles.
