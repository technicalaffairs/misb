Introduction

This document outlines the testing procedures P1, which must be performed on a monthly basis as indicated by  M1 in the above title block, and according to the Directives of the Central Chemical Laboratories.

This test method covers the determination of hardness  in water by titration. This test method is applicable to waters that are clear in appearance and free of chemicals that will complex calcium or magnesium. The lower detection limit of this test method is approximately 2 to 5 mg/l as CaCO3; the upper limit can be extended to all concentrations by sample dilution. It is possible to differentiate between hardness due to calcium ions and that due to magnesium ions by this test method.

This test method was tested on reagent water only. It's the user's responsibility to ensure the validity of the test method for waters of untested matrices.

Summary of Test Method

Calcium  and magnesium ions in water are sequestered by the addition of disodium ethylenediamine tetraacetate. The end point of the reaction is detected by means of Chrome Black T, which has a red color in the presence of calcium and magnesium and a blue color when they are sequestered.

Significance and Use

Hardness salts in water, notably calcium and magnesium, are the primary cause of tube and pipe scaling, which frequently causes failures and loss of process efficiency due to clogging or loss of heat transfer, or both.

Hardness is caused by any polyvalent cations, but those other than Ca and Mg are seldom present in more than trace amounts. The term hardness was originally applied to water in which it was hard to wash; it referred to the soap-wasting properties of water. With most normal alkaline water, these soap-wasting properties are directly related to the calcium and magnesium content.

Tools and Equipment

Reagents

Ammonium Buffer-  Ammonium Chloride-Ammonium hydroxide.NH4OH, Dissolve 67.6 g of Ammonium Chloride (NH4CL) in 200 ml of water. Add 570 ml of concentrated ammonium hydroxide (NH4OH, sp gr 0.9000).

Add 5.00 g of magnesium salt of EDTA and dilute to 1000 ml. Store in a tightly stoppered plastic bottle to prevent the loss of ammonia.

 

Calcium Indicator- Mix 0.20 g of Meroxide and 100 g of NaCL (A.R.) by grinding them together to 40 to 50 mesh size.

 

Hardness Indicator Solution Add 1ml of Na2CO3 solution (30g/l) to 30 ml of water. Add 1.0 g of Eriochrome Black T, and mix. Adjust the pH of the solution to 10.5 by addition of Na2CO3 solution (30g/l). Dilute the solution to 100 ml with isopropanol (99 percent), ethanol (95 percent) or methanol are also satisfactory. Store the solution in a tightly Stoppard, dark-colored bottle to reduce deterioration due to air and light. This solution has a storage life of about 1 month.

Note 1: The indicator prepared, stored, and used in powder form. The powder has a storage life of at least 1 year. It is prepared by grinding 0.2 g of Eriochrome Black T with 80 g of powdered sodium chloride (NaCL). A dark-colored bottle should be used for storage.

 

Sodium Hydroxide Solution (50g /L)- Dissolve 50 g of sodium hydroxide ( NaOH ) in water and dilute to 1 Liter.

 

Sodium Ethylenediamine Tetraacetate Standard Solution (0.01M 1ml = 0.400 mg Ca or 0.243 mg Mg)-. Dissolve 3.72 g of Na2EDTA dihydrate which has been dried overnight over H2SO4 in a desicator, in water and dilute to 1000 ml in volumetric flask. The reagent is stable for several weeks. Adjust the strength of  this solution against 0.01 M calcium chloride solution (CaCL2).

Work to be carried out 

 

Procedure

Total Hardness

  1. Measure 100 ml of the sample into a glass conical flask 250 ml (Note2).

  2. Adjust the pH of the sample, to10 by adding ammonia buffer solution

  3. Add 2 or 3 drops of hardness indicator and stir (Note3).

  4. Add standard sodium ethylenediamine tetraacetate solution (0.01M)  slowly from the burette with continuous stirring until the color changes from red to blue. The titration  should be completed within 5 min. After the buffer addition.
    (Note2): Volume of sample depends on the concentration of total hardness.

(Note3): If powdered indicator is used, add a sufficient quantity of it to produce the required depth of color (0.2 g)

 

Calcium Hardness

  1. Measure 100 ml of the sample into a glass conical flask 250 ml (Note4).

  2. Add 2 ml of sodium hydroxide solution and stir.

  3. Add 0.2 g of calcium indicator and stir.

  4. Add standard sodium ethylenediamine tetraacetate solution (0.01M)  slowly from the burette with continuous stirring until the color changes from salmon pink to orchid purple. the titration should be Completed within 5 min. after the buffer addition.

    (Note4): Volume of sample depends on the concentration of calcium hardness.

Calculation

  1. Calculate the hardness, epm, of the sample as follows:

                 Hardness,  epm   =   10 C/S

             Where :              

                  epm = equivalent parts per million; milliequivalents per liter,

                C = standard (Na2H2EDTA) solution added in titrating hardness, mL,

                 S = sample taken, ml.

  1. Calculate the calcium hardness, epm, of the sample as follows:

             Calcium Hardness,  epm  =   10 D/S

  Where :

                 epm = equivalent parts per million; milliequivalents per liter,

                D = standard (Na2H2EDTA) solution added in titrating calcium hardness, mL,

                S = sample taken for test, ml.

  1. Calculate the magnesium hardness, epm, of the sample as follows:

             Magnesium Hardness,  epm  =   E - F

  Where :

                 epm = equivalent parts per million; milliequivalents per liter,

                 E =  Hardness,  epm and

                 F =  Calcium Hardness,  epm

  1.   Calculate the hardness as CaCO3 of the sample as follows:

                 Hardness,  mg/l as CaCO3   =   1000 C1/S1

             Where :              

               

                C1 = standard (Na2H2EDTA) solution added in titrating hardness, mL, and

                S 1= sample taken, ml.

  1. Calculate the calcium hardness, as CaCO3 of the sample as follows:

             Calcium Hardness,  mg/l as CaCO3   =   1000 D1/S1

  Where :

                D1 = standard (Na2H2EDTA) solution added in titrating calcium hardness, mL, and

                S1 = sample taken for test, ml.

  1. Calculate the magnesium hardness, as CaCO3 of the sample as follows:

             Magnesium Hardness,  mg/l as CaCO3  =   G - H

  Where :

                 G=  Hardness,  mg/l as CaCO3 and

                 H =  Calcium Hardness,  mg/l as CaCO3

  1. Calculate the total hardness of the sample as follows:

                 Hardness,  ppm   =   V x10 as CaCO3

             Where :              

                 

               V = milliliters of standard (Na2H2EDTA) solution (0.01 M )added in titrating total hardness.

  1. Calculate the calcium hardness of the sample as follows:

             Calcium Hardness,  ppm  =   V x10 as CaCO3

  Where :

                

              V = milliliters of standard (Na2H2EDTA) solution (0.01 M )added in titrating calcium hardness.

  1. Calculate the magnesium hardness of the sample as follows:

             Magnesium Hardness,  ppm  =   E - F

  Where :

                 E =   total hardness,  ppm as CaCO3

                 F =  calcium Hardness,  ppm as CaCO3

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Precision

  1. The precision of the total hardness test method  may be expressed as follows:

            ST = (1.15X + 1.1) / V

            SO = (0.25 X + 0.3) / V

           

     Where : 

            SO = single-operator precision

            ST  = over all precision

            X   = concentration of total hardness  in ppm

            V  =  milliliters of sample taken for titration

     

  1. The precision of the calcium hardness test method  may be expressed as follows:

            ST = (1.15X + 0.7) / V

            SO = (0.05 X + 0.04) / V

           

     Where : 

            SO = single-operator precision

            ST  = over all precision

            X   = concentration of calcium hardness  in ppm

            V  =  milliliters of sample taken for titration
