Introduction

According to the directives of the Central Chemical Laboratories this practice covers the preparation of Metallographic Specimen for failure analysis.

This branch of  metallography is variously called light metallography, visible-light metallography, or optical metallography, to distinguish it from electron metallography.

Significance

Examination of defects that appear in finished or partly finished product and studies of parts that have failed in service.

Summary of Practice

Preparation of Metallographic Specimen generally requires five major operations:

  1. Sectioning,

  2. Mounting,

  3. Grinding,

  4. Polishing and

  5. Etching.

Sampling

A well-prepared Metallographic Specimen is:

  1. A representative sample,

  2. Sectioned, ground and polished so as to minimize disturbed or flowed surince metal caused by mechanical deformation, and thus to allow the true microstructure to be revealed by etching,

  3. Free from polishing scratches and pits and liquid staining,

  4. Polished so that inclusions are preserved intact,

  5. Flat enough to permit examination at high magnification.

Safety Precautions

  • Solvents such as HF, HCL, HNO3, and  H2SO4 acids are dangerous, they will expose lung to very hazardous so you have to wear breathing mask.

  • Keep these solvents closed and in a hood. Do not get them in the eyes, on the skin, or on clothing. 

  • Using of bacalite is very dangerous and carcinogenic chemical, so you must not breath the vapor of it, and keep it away from skin by using gloves.

 

 

 

Tools and Equipment

Cut off Machine- Provides safety, convenience and routinely produces high quality surface finishes with minimal deformation. The cut off machine operates in either manual or automatic mode and provided with coolant pumb.

 

Mounting Press Equipment- The mold produced is cylindrical in shape with 1.25 in from bacalite material under heat and pressure.

 

Grind Equipment- Using abrasive grit and grit size from 60 mesh through 600 mesh.

 

Polisher Equipment- By using of cloth-covered laps (velvet) and suitable polishing abrasives as ( Alumina ).

Work to be Carried out

Specimen Preparation

  1. Cut the metal specimen by a high-speed cutter into a small piece. Then, in order to prevent specimen from change of structure because of frictional temperature, cool down the specimen by soapy water or water itself as well as light oil.

  2. By using a file, flat the specimen.

  3. Make the rough polishing as follows.

  • Polish the specimen by using the emery paper No. 60.

  • Move the specimen on the emery papers in one-way direction. Polish the specimen until the scratch made by emery paper disappear from the specimen surface.

  • When the emery paper is changed to another type, wash the specimen by water.

  • Nextly, use emery papers 120, 240, 320, 400, 600 in order, without omitting any of them.

    4.   Make the final polishing as follows.

  • Permeate the polishing cloth by dripped water. Gradually drop a suitable volume of polishing fluid from the plastic bottle while the specimen is equally pressed.

  • Slowly run the specimen between from edge to center of the disc while the disc rotates. Slowly move the specimen in reversed direction against rotation of disc in order to prevent from COMET-TAIL. Final polishing is allowed with low speed by placing the specimen at 25-50 mm distant from the center of the disc for 3-5 minutes.

    5.   Make washing the specimen as follows.

  • Wash the polished specimen with water. Wipe the specimen with absorbent cotton. In addition, wipe the specimen with another cotton immersed in Alcohol.

    6.   Make etching as follows.

  • Wash the specimen with water. Soak the specimen in alcohol. Make etching procedure by using suitable fluid according to the material of specimen.

    7.   Make storing the specimen

  • Take care not to put fingerprints or oil dirts on the specimen surface. Keep the specimen in discator or seal a thin film on the specimen surface by using anti-corrosive aerosol spray.
