Introduction

Dissolved gases can cause corrosion in the water circle of the steam production devices. The main gases which must be removed from the water are: O2 and CO2. One of the methods used is the use of  deaerators.

Information

 

The two major types of pressure dearators, or deaerating  heaters are the tray-type and spray-type.

 

Tray-Type Deaerating Heater

It consists of a shell. Perforated inlet water spray distribution pips, direct contact vent condenser, tray stacks and protective interchamber walls temperature of the steam. Most of non-condensable gases principally O2 and free CO2 are released to the steam as the water is sprayed into the unit. Seals are provided as barriers to prevent recontamination of tray stack water by gases from the spray section in the tray section. Water cascades from tray to tray, breaking into fine droplets or films which intimately contact the incoming steam. The steam heats the water to the saturation temperature of the steam and remove all but the very lost traces of oxygen. Deaerated water falls to the storage space below where a stream blanket protects it from recontamination. From the water is vented to the atmosphere it is essential that sufficient venting is provided at all times or deaeration will be incomplete.

There are several common variation to the basic tray deaerator design. Spring-loaded spray valves can distribute the inlet water steam flow through the tray stack may be cross flow or counter-current to the water. The deaerated water is frequently stored in a separated shell either vertical or horizontal.

Spray-Type Deaerating Heater

It consists of a shell, spring-loaded inlet spray valve, direct contact vent condenser and a steam scrubber for final deaeration. The incoming water is sprayed into a steam atmosphere and heated to withen a few degrees of the saturation temperature of the steam most of the non condensable gases are released to the steam and the heated water falls to water seal and drains to the lowest section of the steam scrubber. The water is scrubbed by a large volume of steam and heated to the saturation temperature prevailing at this point. The intimate steam water contact achieved in the scrubber very efficiently strips the water of dissolved gases.

As the steam-water mixture rises in the scrubber a slight pressure loss the deaerated water temperature to remain a few degrees below the inlet steam saturation temperature. The deaerated water overflows from the steam scrubber to the storage section below steam enters the deaeator through a chest on the side, and flows to the steam scrubber, passes up into the spray heater section to heat the incoming water. Most of the steam condenses in the spray section to become a part of the deaerated water output. A small portion of the steam vented to the atmosphere removes non condensable gases.

One other pressure deaerating heater is the jet atomizing type in which the incoming water sprays into a steam atmosphere. Here the water is heated sufficiently to release the majority of the non condensable gases. The water then delivered into a high velocity. The high velocity steam heats the water to the its saturation temperature  and scrubs all but the last traces of oxygen from the fine water droplets.

Thermal deaeration in talkha steam electricity 2×2/0 M.W station is happened by using “Deaerator” thus by inverse motion of the incoming water from down to fighter through small opens called nozzles. The steam passing through water by this method result to breaking the dynamic balance for the rate of solubility of gases in water and exiting the gases from water. This balance is breaking by rising the temperature of water which result to separating the gases particles from the water.

The increasing of water surface resulting from cascading it through nozzles on the woody or plastic barriers result in increasing the rate of separating gases from water.

In most cases, the capacity of deaerator to remove the soluble oxygen is 99% to 99.94% from the total quantity of oxygen in the make-up water and the residual oxygen is removed by chemical deaeration by using Hydrazine N2H4. 

The concentration of the residual oxygen after deaerator should not to be exceeding 10 ppb after deaeration treatment.

Deaerating Condensers

 In central station main turbine condensers have air ejectors to remove dissolved gases. Sometimes because enough deaeration is obtained from these condensers the pressure deaerator is omitted from the feedwater cycle. But since there is a danger of air leaking into the system, both during start up and while the condensers are operating at low loads, pressure deaerotors are included in many central station designs.

Vacuum Deaeration

Vacuum deaeration is used for deaeration water treatment at temperatures below the atmospheric boiling point to reduce the corrosion rate in water to its saturation temperature by applying a vacuum to the systems rather than heating the water divide the water into small particles to facilitate gas removal and vent the exhaust gases. Incoming water enters through the spray pipe and falls through a column packed with arching rings or other the vacuum maintained by steam jet educators or vacuum pumps depending on the size the system.

Applications and Advantages

Pressure deaertors used to prepare boiler feedwater produce deaerated water which is very low in dissolved oxygen and free carbon dioxide. Venderors usually guarantee less than 0.005 cubic centimer (cc) per liter (6.5 ppb of oxygen and zero free carbon dioxide).

Vacuum deaerators used to protect water distribution lines which are not designed to provide a complete deaeration as the pressure deaerators. They will usually reduce the oxygen content to about 0.25 to 0.50 cc/liter (0.33 to 0.65 ppm).

Limitations

Inlet water to the deaerators should be largely free from suspended solids which could clog spray valves and ports of the inlet distribution and deaerator trays. In addition spray valves ports and deaerator trays may plug with scale which forms when the water being deaerated has high hardness and alkali levels. While pressure deaertors to reduce oxygen to very low levels even trace amounts of oxygen may cause corrosion damage to system consequently good operating practice requires removal of the last traces of oxygen with a chemical oxygen scavenger such as sodium sulfite and hydrazine.
