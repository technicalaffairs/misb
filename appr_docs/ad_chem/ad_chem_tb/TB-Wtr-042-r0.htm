Information

Operating Principle

The FormacsLT analyzer uses the following method to determine the amount of organic carbon.

The sample is transferred to the chemistry unit by means of the integrated auto sampler.

The FormacsLT analyzer uses a peristaltic pump to continually aspirate samples and mix them with reagents throughout a series of reaction steps.

The sample is first acidified with sulfuric acid and purged with Nitrogen gas. This liberates and disperses any inorganic or volatile organic carbon. After purging the sample is debubbed and resampled. The resample is then mixed with a persulphate / tetraborate reagent and air (free of carbon dioxide) and then passed through the UV digestion coil. The process oxidizes the organic carbon to CO2.The Carbon dioxide is expelled from the solution by acidifying and purging by means of the stripper unit. The flow of nitrogen transports the CO2 to the infrared detection unit. The absorbance of the carbon dioxide is measured at 4.2µm. The analogue signal from detector is transferred to the PC for further calculation by windows software. The signal output is quantified by reference to calibration standards.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Procedure

One of the important indices of water quality is the amount of organic substances. This amount can be defined with aid of analytical methods and by expressing it either in the Chemical Oxygen Demand (COD) or in Total Organic Carbon (TOC). Due to the environment protection legislation the analytical method for Chemical Oxygen Demand is not in favor. The automated facilities of the FormacsLT analyzer enables precise result for the determination of organically bound carbon in all kinds of industrial wastes, drinking water, surface water, sea water. The concentration that can be measured will generally fall within the range of 0.05 to 1000 mg/l organic carbon.

Total Organic Carbon (TOC)

TOC is the organic carbon that is converted into carbon dioxide after oxidation. Direct TOC measurement is done after acidification of the sample.TOC in water samples should ideally include carbon in volatile materials; although most laboratories report TOC samples where the volatile have already been removed before analysis. The result is still generally accepted as TOC. To include volatiles measure TC and IC separately, TOC is the difference between them, TOC = TC-IC.   

Requirements

 

 

To operate the TOC analyzer some requirements have to be supplied:

Laboratory Requirements

  • Nitrogen gas, free of CO2 and organic contaminants.

  • Gas reducing valve for 300 kpa.

  • Waste facility.

  • Glass reagent containers and glass measuring flasks for standard preparation.

Required Chemicals

  • Anhydrous Potassium Biphthalate, C8H5KO4.

  • Sulfuric acid H2SO4

  • Potassium persulfate, K2S2O8

  • Sodium tetra borate, Na2B4O7.10H2O

  • Antifoam B

  • Distilled Water

Instrument Requirements

  • Nitrogen supply: make sure the nitrogen supply has pressure of 2-3 bar. High purity nitrogen is recommended 99.999%

  • Power supply: Check the power supply is (220V/50Hz or 110V /60Hz or 240V/50Hz). The instrument must only be connected to line supply with ground detector (earth).

  • Power extension bar for at least 5 power connections.

  • FormacsLT analyzer

  • Printer

  • Computer

  • Computer Screen

Preparation of Standards

The preparation of the standard solutions for TOC is based on the description in “the standard methods for the examination of water”, 17th  edition.

 

 

Preparation of the TC Stock Solution: 1000 mg C/l

Dissolve 2.1254g anhydrous Potassium Biphthalate in carbon-free water and dilute to 800 ml. Preserve by acidifying with H2SO4 to pH 2.0 ± 0.1 and fill up to 1000 ml; 1.00 ml = 1.00 mg carbon/l. Mix well. The standard will be stable for about 4 weeks at 4°C. In a well-closed glass bottle.

Preparation of Reagents

  • Sulfuric Acid 0.03 N:Dilute carefully 0.84 ml 96%sulfuric acid in ± 800 ml distilled water. Fill up to 1 liter and mix well.

  • Digestion Reagent:Dissolve 12 g potassium per sulfate and 34 g sodium tetra-borate in ± 800 ml distilled water. Fill up to 1 liter and mix well. Store in a glass bottle.

  • Sulfuric Acid 0.5N:Dilute carefully 14 ml 96% sulfuric acid in ± 800 ml distilled water. Fill up to 1 liter and mix well. Store in a glass bottle.

Start Up Procedure

  1. Close pump deck.

  2. Place the tubing in the reagent containers.

  3. Switch on the computer and the printer

  4. Start the software and switch on the pumps, UV digestion and sparging, by means of the software.

  5. Set the software in Analysis position.

  6. Run the base line on the computer screen.

  7. When the baseline is steady, reset the IR detector.

  8. Fill the sampler tray with samples.

  9. Auto zero the baseline if necessary.

  10. Now the system is ready to run an analysis.

Shut Down Procedure

  1. Switch off the sparging and set the analyzer in the standby mode, by means of the software.

  2. Close the software and switch off the computer and the printing.

  3. Switch off the FormacsLT TOC mains switch, open the pump decks and close the gas supply on the bottle
