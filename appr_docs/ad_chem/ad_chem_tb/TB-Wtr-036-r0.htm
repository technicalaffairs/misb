Introduction

 

This document covers  Heat Recovery from Blowdown Water Methods for saving energy. As the result of employing the measures for saving energy and for diversifying energy resources. Therefore, it is necessary to encourage the energy conservation in order to keep the economical growth.

This document was initiated by EDEPC inspection & quality control chemist: Saad Sadeek; and revised by EEHC, quality control head sector chemists: Mohamed Tawfik and Safaa Mamdouh.

This document is accepted by eng. Samir Abd El-Galil: EDEPC inspection and quality control general director & MPIS coordinator, and certified by engineer; Mervat Ezz Eldeen Mahmoud; EDEPC inspection and Quality Control Head Sector.

Information

The heat exchange between feedwater and blowdown water using a continuous blowdown equipment recovers the heat energy in the blowdown water and saves the fuel cost of boiler.

Unit Price of Heat Recovery from Blowdown Water

The heat of blowdown water is recovered by using a continuous blowdown equipment shown in Figure 1.

Figure 1  Flow diagram of a continuous blowdown equipment

 

 

 

The theoretical maximum heat recovery from blowdown water is calculated by the equation (1).

Qmax = B X 1,000 X (IB1 - IF1) ................. (1)

where

Qmax = theoretical maximum heat recovery (kcal/h)

B = blowdown (t/h)

IB1 = enthalpy of blowdown water at heat exchanger inlet (kcal/kg)

IF1 = enthalpy of feedwater at heat exchanger inlet (kcal/kg)

The practical heat recovery is calculated by equation (2) or (3).

Q = B X 1000 X (IB1 - IB2) ...................... (2)

Q = F X 1000 X (IF2 - IF1) ....................... (3)

where

Q = practical heat recovery (kcal/h)

IB2 = enthalpy of blowdown water at heat exchanger outlet (kcal/kg)

F = feedwater (t/h)

IF2 = enthalpy of feedwater at heat exchanger outlet (kcal/kg)
