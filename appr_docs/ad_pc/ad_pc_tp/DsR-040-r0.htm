Introduction

    The P10 phase comparison relay is used to detect and isolate phase to phase, phase to ground and three phase faults on extra high voltage network lines operating at 500KV or 220KV.  It is a static relay is made by GEC. 

    This procedure details the steps required to completely check the proper operation and calibration of the P10 distance relay. It is recommended this procedure be carried out on a yearly bases. This work be carried out section of this document is divided into 3 sections.

    The first preliminary steps to prepare for relay testing describes the activities required prior to testing. Some of these steps could be performed days before the actual test. The second section Preparation for testing describes the steps to set up the equipment for performing the actual tests. The third section is the actual test. 

    The document is formatted the way to minimize the possibility of error causing personal injury or damage to equipment.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, qualified maintenance staff carry the suitable safety ID category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required,

  • Personal protective equipment must be used as safety shoes, and safety Helmet .

  • Ensure that Line isolation is done.

  • Make sure CT secondary circuits are shorted and VT secondary circuits are opened before injection.

Work to be carried out

Preliminary Steps to Prepare for Relay Testing

  1. Review technical references material Manufacturer's manual.

  2. Obtain copy of relay setting from field maintenance file and compare it with the setting on the relay.

  3. Obtain outage for line protected by this relay.

  4. Check that the bus and line disconnect switches are open and tagged.

  5. Inspect the CT secondary circuits, the VT Miniature circuit breakers (MCB'S), and all cables supplying the relay.

  6. Isolate the following circuits

  • Breaker trip coils. y.

  • Breaker failure scheme associated with this rela

  • Disturbance alarms and fault recorder associated with this relay.
     

Prior to Starting Testing Procedure (s) Ensure the Following Steps

  1. Carefully follow all test procedure steps from page 2 to page 7.

  2. Verify the test results match the relay setting, if not consult technical services.

  3. Test trip the breaker associated with the relay.

Testing Procedure (s)

Sequence of the Test

  1. Switch the equipment out of service.

  2. Transfer the receiver and transmitter channel from line position to dummy load position it will break the H.F line at the terminating unit.

Transmitter Output Level Test

  1. Connect on pin 8, 9 or 10 and 6.16 (Module no.5) an oscilloscope.

  2. Transmit continuously and measure the dummy load level measured by oscilloscope and the built in voltmeter (AC/60v).

  3. Check that the oscilloscope measurement is between 74 and 82 Vp.p.(24V and

  4. 29V r.m.s value) at the same time measure the frequency . check for correct frequency with tolerance ±5 HZ.

Receiver Test

  1. Set the line dummy load link to Dummy load - test level, connect the oscilloscope between pins 9 or 10 and 6 -16 and connect the built-in voltmeter set to 6v DC to the control module monitor point (on Module no.6).

  2. Transmit continuously and adjust the level by means of test level control until the M.P is just low.

  3. Measure the receiver voltage (between pins 4 - 14 and 6 - 16) on both oscilloscope and the built in voltmeter (AC) and record as alarm sensitivity.

  4. Set the modulation angle to minimum and transmit modulated transmission. Slowly reduce the level until the equipment trips. Measure the values with the oscilloscope and the built in voltmeter and record as functional sensitivity.

High Frequency Gap Performance

  1. Transmit modulated transmission and increase the test level control to give the alarm sensitivity level slowly increase the modulation angle until the equipment operates and record the setting which should normally be 30°.

  2. Measure the H.F gap (at 70% of full level). Set the line Dummy load links to dummy load full level to receiver and increase the modulation angle until the equipment operates and increase modulation angle until the relay operates. Record the modulation angle reading and the corresponding H.F gapit should not exceed 55°.

Sequence Network and Starting Units

  1. Using the current injection equipment to inject a current to the relay through the test plug but, before inserting the test plug short links are positioned on the right hand side of plug to connect terminals 5 to 6, 7 to 8 and 9 to 10 this to short the current transformer secondery circuit.

  2. Connect the output from the test equipment to the left hand holes 5 and 6 of test plug

  3. Replace the heavy-duty connector by the test plug.

  4. Insert the P10 monitor box into the test socket on Module 16 .

Tap Check for Negative Sequence Starter (I2)

  1. Inject into phase (A) current equal to 3 times the I2 % tap setting ( If the starters are impulse starters they will not operate if you slowly increase the current ).

  2. Measure and note the D.C voltage across the monitor box at terminal 9 and 19 (approximately 1.2 volts).

  3. Record the value of the current which gives exactly the 1.2VD.C.

Tap Check for Positive Phase Sequence Starter (I1)

  1. Apply a current to phase (A) current equal to 3 times I1% Tap setting.

  2. Measure and note the DC voltage across the monitor box terminal 18 and 9 (approximately 1.2V)

  3. Record the value of the current which gives exactly 1.2 VDC.

Tap Check for Modulation Threshold Setting (Ic1)

  1. Put the switch on the monitor box into the MOD position.

  2. Connect the test current out put to the left hand holes 7and 8 of the test plug.

  3. Increase the current slowly at phase until the box lamp lights, this should occur at 0.25* Ic setting * 1 amp for equipment rated 1 amp.

The High Set and the Low Set Setting of the Starter

The High Set Setting for Negative Phase Sequence Starter (I2)

  1. Plug the single phase output from the test circuit into the left hand holes 5 and 6 of the test plug.

  2. put the switch on the monitor box into the H.S position.

  3. Set the current to three times the plug setting current.

  4. Switch off the current for at least 15 seconds and then check whether the indicator lamp on the monitor box lights for approximately 0.5 seconds when the current switched on .

  5. Adjust the current level until the exact setting current is found by repeated tests always leaving at least 15 second after switching off.

  6. Repeat the test for phases B (terminal 7 & 8) and phase C (terminal 9& 10)

The High Set Setting for Positive Phase Sequence Starter (I1)

Repeat the above tests but press the Inhibit NPS button the monitor box to raise the setting of the negative sequence high set well above the positive sequence setting.

The Low Set Setting for Negative Phase Sequence Starter (I2)

Repeat the test 1 above but with the switch on monitor box in position LS and the current set two times the plug setting current. The nominal low set setting is 0.67 times the high set setting.

The Low Set Setting for Positive Phase Sequence Starter (I2)

Repeat the test 1 but with the switch on the monitor box in position LS and the current set 1.5 time the plug setting current. the nominal low set setting 0.5 times high set setting. Also press the In Inhibit NPS button.

The Low Set Setting for Negative Phase Sequence Non mpuls Starter (xI2)

  1. Put the selector switch on the monitor box is L.S position.

  2. Increase the current slowly until the monitor box lamp lights this should occur at 3* 0.67* (I2 ) * ( xI2 ).

Checking the Operating Time

  1. Link the left hand hole to the right hand hole on position 1 of the heavy duty test plug.

  2. The timing will be checked for a phase A to neutral for positive sequence and negative sequence starter also for a phase B to C current

  3. Connect the injection current set to the start terminal of the timer and connect the terminal YH1, YH3 from P10 relay to stop the timer.

  4. Set the current nine time I2 tap setting.

  5. Switch off the current for at least 5 seconds and reset the timer.

  6. Switch on the current and check the operating time. Repeat step 4& 5 several times to obtain average operating time.

  7. Change the current level to nine times I1 tap setting.

  8. Repeat test 4 &5 with the Inhibit NPS push button depressed. this will give the operating time using positive sequence starters.

  9. Connect the current input into the left hand holes 7 and 9 and link holes 8 and 10 on the heavy duty test plug . this to check the effect of a phase to phase current and to check the Band C phases.

  10. Set the current to 3é3 times the I2 setting and repeat test 4&5.

Channel Voltage

  1. Be sure that the two p10 relays from the two end are switched to out of service and set the line / dummy load links to line position for the two relay from both end of the Line

  2. Monitor the line with the scope (pins 1-11 to 6 - 16 ).

Transmit Level

Set end A to reflex channel test -Ext. TX, and voltmeter to TX/60v. Set end B to reflex channel test- normal. Operate the test start button at end A and record the voltmeter reading and the scope level. While the level is constant for over a second following the reflex transmission -and check for no clipping of the sine wave.

Receive Level

  1. Set end A to reflex channel test-Normal and voltmeter to RX/20V.

  2. Set end B to reflex channel 1 test - EXT TX operate the test start button at end A. Note the voltmeter reading and record the scope level.

  3. If the voltmeter rang can be reduced to get an indication near to full scale repeat the test then record the voltmeter reading.

Final Check

  1. Ensure that the links, Modules and heavy current connectors are fitted correctly for normal operation at both end.

  2. The reflex channel test set to normal from both end then switch the two relay from both end to In service.

Return the Relay to Service

  1. Switch of the test equipment and remove the test leads which are connected between the relay and the test equipment.

  2. Restore the CT circuit to the relay.

  3. Depending on the field site the following restoration steps be required:

  • Disturbance alarms and fault recorder associated with this relay.

  • Breaker failure scheme associated with this relay
  • Breaker trip coils.

  1. The transmitter and receiver channels U-Links on module 5 should be transferred to the normal position which connects the equipment to the high frequency power line carrier and restore the H.F channel.

  2. The test selector switch on module (3) should be at normal position.

  3. Switch the equipment to be in service.

  4. The built in voltmeter should be at AC/60V scal and at TX position.
