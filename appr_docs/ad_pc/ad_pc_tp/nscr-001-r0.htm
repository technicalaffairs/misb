Introduction

This document outlines the testing procedure I1, which must be performed on an annually bases as indicated by Y1 in the above title block

The type SGC relays are intended to protect generator, against possible damage from unbalanced phase currents resulting from prolonged faults or unbalanced load conditions. In this case the stator current will include a negative sequence component ( I2 ) of the generator current that sets up a counter rotating flux field in the machine. This causes double - frequency currents to flow in the rotor which result in local heating. This relay has two ways to trip. One by the constant  K, the other by the time trip scale.

The first draft  initiated by eng. Fayza El-Sharkawy, tested by eng. Shirin Hamdy & eng. Azza Salama at 29 April 2003, revised by EDEPCo. Quality Control eng. Adel El-Nagar, accepted by General Director eng. Mahmoud Saker and approved by eng. Moustfa Alam Head Sector of Talkha Power Station.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued & the staff  of test must be qualified and carry the suitable safety precautions.

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • The generator must be out of service.

Tools and Equipment

  • Test Leads, Test Plugs .

  • Manta test Set OR Double - F2200 test set

Work to be Carried Out

Preparation

  1. Make Sure CT secondary circuit are shorted at the incoming terminal blocks before isolating the relay current circuit for test

  2. Look at field maintenance file, compare with the settings value on relay, and  record  it on the test sheet.

Testing the Relay

  1. Connect the relay studs (10,19) to the DC source where stud 10 is the- ve and stud 19 is the + ve. fig 1 .

  2. Connect the test leads to the relay contact ( 1 – 11 ) fig 1 .

Phase ( R ) Test

  1. Connect test leads to the  current terminals ( 3 – 4 )  fig 1.

  2. Increase the current slowly until the relay picks up and then the red light appears and wait until the relay trips .

  3. Record the pick up current  and time values in the test sheet .

  4. Adjust the value of the constant K and the time trip scale to the setting values .

  5. Increase the current to 10 A and wait until the relay trips .

  6. Record the current and time values in the test sheet .

Phase ( S ) Test

  1. Connect test leads to  the current terminals ( 5 – 6 ) fig 1.

  2. Repeat the steps from 4 to 8 .

Phase ( T ) Test

  1. Connect test leads to the  current terminals ( 7 – 8 ) fig 1 .

  2. Repeat the steps from ( 4 to 8 ).

Return Relay and Associated Equipment to Service

  1. Return relay to service by removing the test leads from the relay terminal block and the test set i.e. :
     -     Disconnect the test leads from the terminals ( 3 - 4 ), ( 5 - 6 ), ( 7 - 8 )
     - Disconnect the test leads between the trip contact ( 1- 11 )
     - Disconnect the leads from the DC. terminals  ( 10 - 19 ).

  2. Make sure CT and PT secondary circuits are in normal condition.

  3. If any equipment associated with the relay has been isolated, it should be returned to service by the following restoration steps:
           - Breaker trip coils.
           - Breaker failure scheme associated this relay.
           - Disturbance alarm and fault recorder associated with this relay.
