Introduction

This document outlines the testing procedure P1, which must be performed every 6 months as indicated by M6 in the above title block.

This patent concerns the test of protection relays of the exciter manufactured by “ SKODA PLZEN”.  The exciter is of type HE500630/2, rated active power 1210KW, rated apparent  power 2200KVA, stator nominal voltage 525V, and rated field current 190A .

The first draft initiated by eng .Maysa Ahmady, tested at 27 Jan. 2003 by eng. Iman Rizk El–Ashry, reviewed by quality control engineer eng. Adel  Abd El Kader EL Nagar of EDEPCo, accepted by general director eng . Mahmoud El Saied Sakr, and approved by eng. Mohamed Reda Abdel Azez Soliman Pre. Sector Head of Talkha PS 2x210 MW.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safely category

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks and No Smoking sign surrounding the work area must be used.

  • Wearing safety helmet, shoes, masks are necessary.

    •

  • Ascertain the disconnection of the exciter, the  generator, main  transform, and home transformer .

  • Be aware of parts in service.

  • Follow the work permission instructions. 

Tools and Equipment

  • An A.C. voltage source of type Z11.

  • An  A.C. current source of   type Z11.

  • Varying - Resistance Box .

  • An ammeter & A voltmeter.

  • A timer unit TM-2 ( Program )

  • Some leads & screws.

 

 

 

 

 

Work to be Carried Out

 

Preparation of Relays

  1. Review the manual.

  2. Prepare the previous results of the relay testing.

  3. Check the wires of the relay beforehand.

  4. Be sure that the unit and the transformer are disconnected and the DC source is available.

  5. Check the existence of the d.c. sources in panels.

  6. Disconnect the trip leads of the relay and connect the timer to them.

  7. Check all the leads used in tests

Preparation of Testing Tools

  1. Connect the testing units of Z11 to a power source.

  2. Connect the timer to a power source and its terminals to the trip terminals of the relay under test.

  3. Place an ammeter on current leads of Z11 to measure the current.

  4. Connect the voltmeter in parallel with the voltage leads of Z11 ( the other unit ) to measure the voltage.

 

The test of some exciter protection devices is achieved by isolating either CT or PT circuits or both.

This is done by using a special socket for testing.

This makes the test results depend only on the action of relays

 

 

Differential of exciter type R32X1

 

It is a three point differential protection with the measuring member in each phase, locked switching – on surge.

C.T.    TA12 & TA14   2500/5A

Test steps

  1. Place a socket ZZ10 in Z13 (TA12), and another one in Z14 (TA14).

  2. Fix the timer leads in (172,188) at the rear of panel HH03.

  3. Inject the current in points ( 11,12,13,14N ) of Z13 either ph-ph or ph-N

    Or inject the current in points ( 11,12,13 ) of Z14 ph-ph.

    Or inject the current in terminal (14(N)) of Z13, and (11,12,13)(phase) of   Z14.

  4. Increase the injected current gradually till the red flag of the device displays. Let the timer start acting till it stops (almost instantaneously)

  5. Record the results in each case of injection.

 


 

Over Current type A33TX1

It is a three phase over- current protection with a distinguishing of the effected phases, and a time member.

C.T.    TA13   2500/5A

Test Steps

  1. Place the socket ZZ10 in Z11 (HH01).

  2. Fix the timer leads in (172,199) at the rear of panel HH02.

  3. Inject the current in points (11,14),(12,14),(13,14(N))of Z11 respectively phase-N .

  4. Increase the injected current gradually till the red flag of the device displays.

  5. Let the timer start acting till it stops (its red flag displays) indicating the tripping time of the device.

  6. Repeat the steps for each phase.

  7. Record the results.

 

Over Load type A3TX1

 

It is a three phase over- current protection without distinguishing of the effected phases with a time member.

C.T.    TA13   2500/5A

Test Steps

  1. Place the socket ZZ10 in Z11 (HH01).

  2. Fix the timer leads in (172,197) at the rear of panel HH04.

  3. Inject the current to points (11,14) then (12,14), and finally (13,14(N)) of Z11 ph-N.

  4. Increase the injected current gradually till the red flag of the device displays.

  5. Let the timer start acting till it stops (its red flag displays) indicating the signaling time of the device.

  6. Repeat the steps for each phase.

  7. Record the results.

  

Rotor Earth fault protection (  1st  unit  210MW ) type GRX1

 

It is an earth – fault protection and measuring of insulation state of generator stator. It is the protection with D.C. superposition.

 

G2 Rotor insulating condition checking

  1. Supply 220 Vdc between 15 (-) & 16 (+). Supply an a.c. voltage of 100V to terminals 12,13 .

  2. A 750 V will be measured on C4 then with " IZOL. G2 " pressed down.

  3. Connect resistance R = 68 kW ( by a resistance box) to terminal 1{ ground } and 4. With " Isolation G2" push  button pressed down the 1A, 2A relays will start operating and the respective millimeter on the panel must indicate 20 mA  in stabilized state / within about 3 sec .

  4. Repeat the test with 220 Va.c to terminals 12,14 .

 

G2 Generator earth connection test

  1. Supply 220 Vdc between 15 (-) & 16(+) . Supply an AC voltage of 100 V to terminals 12 , 13.

  2. Terminals 1,5 are disconnected. With the press of " TestG2" push button, the B, 1B relays, J reed relay, C,D relays will close.

  3. The alarm relay will fall into the red flag and the 1B relay has about 2 sec slow release.

  4. Connect terminal 1{ground}& 5 through resistance about 220 ohm  The D relay closes with voltage lower than 50V. Check that contact on terminals 6 - 7 & 8 - 9 closes.

  5. Press down the Reset push button then the white flag displays again. + 220 V on terminal 11, the B, 1B,J,C, and D relays will start operating. The NG2 magnetic indicator will start operating. Circuits of contacts on terminals 6-7 , 8-9 are opened . With + 220V connection to terminal 10, the magnetic indicator will release.

Testing of exciting of terminals 12-14

Supply an a.c. voltage of 100V  to terminals (12,13) and measure by a voltmeter the existence of 220 V on terminals (12,14)

 

Rotor Earth fault protection (  2nd unit  210MW ) type ALOX GR100

It is an earth fault protection and measuring of insulation state of generator stator. It’s the protection with DC superposition.

 

Test steps

  1. Check the existence of the dc voltage ( 220 Vdc ).

  2. Fulfill the self – test of the device.

  3. Place the leads of the varying-resistance box on terminals (3,5) at the rear of panel hh06

  4. Decrease the value of resistance gradually till the red lamp indicating both the first stage (R <) and the relay signal - switches on.

  5. Keep on decreasing the resistance till the red lamp indicating both the second stage (R<</t) and the relay tripping value - switches on.

  6. Record the value of the resistance for the two stages & compare them with the previous ones. 

 

Pro – Maintenance Procedures

  1. Loosen the leads of the testing instruments.

  2. Check the return of relay- wires and trip-leads to their original places and tighten them carefully.

  3. Press the main “RESET” button in HH01.

  4. Be sure that the relay is completely ready for service any time.

  5. Alienate the testing materials and equipments in addition to the caution signs and leave the place clean.

  6. Return the work permission.
