Introduction

    This procedure details the steps required to completely check the proper operation and calibration of the RAZOA relay. It is recommended this procedure be carried out on a yearly bases. This work be carried out section of this document is divided into 3 sections.

    The first preliminary steps to prepare for relay testing describes the activities required prior to testing. Some of these steps could be performed days before the actual test. The second section preparation for testing describes the steps to set up the equipment for performing the actual tests. The third section is the actual test.

    The document is formatted the way to minimize the possibility of error causing personal injury or damage to equipment.

Safety Precautions

  • Work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category, 

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required,

  • Wear your safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes .... etc

  • Line isolation must be done

  • Make sure CT secondary circuits are shorted before isolating the current circuits for test current injection.

  • Switches off DC supply before inserting or withdrawing any card from the relay.

Tools and Equipment

  • Test equipment type TURH, Test plug type RTUH.

  • Auxiliary transformer 380/110 V.

  • Timer, Voltmeter, Clip-on ammeter.

  • Current Source.

Work to be Carried Out

Preliminary Steps to Prepare for Relay Testing

  1. Review technical reference material manufacturer’s manual.

  2. Obtain outage for line protected by this relay.

  3. Check that the bus and line disconnect switches are opened and tagged

  4. Inspect the secondary circuits of CT, VT and all cabling supplying the relay.

  5. Before inserting the test plug into the relay, check from the station drawing that the test plug will isolate

  • Breaker trip coils and transfer trip scheme.

  • Breaker failure scheme associated with this relay.

  • Fault recorder, event recorder and disturbance alarm associated with this relay.

  • Acceleration scheme.

  1. Auto-recloser associated with this relay.
  2. If the test does not isolate one of the pervious steps you have to isolate it.

Testing the Relay and the Relay Outputs

  1. Carefully follow the test procedures

  2. Verify the test result match the relay setting .If not consult the technical services.

  3. Test trip all breakers associated with the relay.

  4. Return relay and the associated equipment to service.

Preparations

  1. This procedure is used to perform off load test.

  2. Look at the EEA panel nameplate and check that EEA system designation nameplate agrees with the line that has been taken out of service.

  3. Using a clip-on ammeter check there is no current flowing in the terminal no. 3, 5, 7 and 9 on the rear side of the test switch.

  4. Using voltmeter check there is no volt on the terminals no. 11, 12, 13, and 14 on the rear side of the test switch.

  5. The P- switch on the relay should always be kept in 01 position when testing RASOA.

Testing Procedure (s)

Checking the Starting Element

Over Current Starting Unit

Zero Sequence Current Relay INS

  1. Insert the test plug to the RASOA test switch, and then bridge the terminal No. 4, 6, 8 and 10 on the test plug.

  2. To perform this test you have to obtain a continues varying current source Inject slowly AC current to the neutral with any phase (terminals 3 and 9 on the test plug) until the indication N appears on the relay. Read off the current value then switch of the current source.

Over Current Relays IR, IS and IT

  1. The over current relay is tested in the same way as the zero sequence relay INS but you increase the current to the terminal 3 and 9 on the test plug until the indication R appears

  2. For over current relay IS inject the current to the terminals 5 and 9 on the test plug until the indication S appears.

  3. For over current relay IT inject the current to the terminals 7 and 9 on the test plug until the indication T appears.

Under Impedance relays ZR, ZS and ZT

  1. Connect the two cases together by using the RTXG connector. Connect terminals RSTN of the test set to the 110 Va.c. output of the Aux. transformer. Connect the test set with the test plug and with the timer as shown in figure (1).

  2. Insert the test plug to the RASOA test switch , then connect the 380Va.c three phase voltage input to the Aux. transformer.

  3. Set the switches:  Q in position I.,  M in position 0, until further notice, P in position I.

  4. Set the product of the S1 and S2 setting equal to the rated current of the RASOA relay.

  5. Turn the main switch H to position I, with switch AF position I, the light emitting diode above AF is lit if all terminals R, S, T and N are supplied with voltage. The light emitting diode remains dark if one or several phases are missing.

  6. The phase sequence in the a.c supply is checked with the switch AF in position 2 the light emitting diode is lit if the phase sequence is RST the diode is dark if the phase sequence is reversed.

  7. Set the switch H to position O. Set the switch F to position RN.

  8. Set the switch Gx and potentiometer Fx at their highest value and GR potentiometer FR in position 0, i.e. the voltage supplied to the relay will lead the current with approx. 90°.

  9. Turn switch M to position 1 then, turn switch H to 1 position.

  10. Reduced the setting of GX and FX one step above the operating value of the impedance relay, which is checked by observing the indication R in RAZOA. Reduce the setting of FX until the relay operates by time delay T4.

  11. Read off the value of GX and FX then turn of the switch H to position 0.

  12. Tests could be repeated in other phase angles with other combinations of GX, FX, GR, and FR.

  13. Repeat steps 7 until 12 for the other type of faults, switch F at different positions SN, TN, RS, ST, and RT.

Checking of the Measuring Elements

The Reach of the First Zone for Phase to Earth Faults

  1. Block the time steps, 3, and 4 by temporarily putting the switches D: T2, D:T3, and D:T4 in off position.

  2. Set the selector switch F in position RN and Q in position 1.

  3. Set the product of S1 and S2 equal to the rated current of the relay.

  4. Set the switches G, and Fx to the highest value and switches GR and FR to position 0, the voltage supplied to the relay will lead the current with approx. 90° .

  5. Turn switch m to position 1.

  6. Turn switch H to position 1 and reduce the GX until the measuring unit operates which checked by observing the indication TRIP. Then increase Gx to one step above the operating value. Reduce the setting of FX until the measuring unit operates.

    7. Note. The value of the setting (GX + GR/1000) or (GR + FR/1000) must always be above 0.100, otherwise the voltage TURH will be too small giving a big error in the test result.

The Reach of first Zone for Phase to Phase

  1. Set the switch F in position RS, ST, and TR depending on the phase combination to be tested

The Reach of the Second Zone

  1. De-block the second time step, by putting switch D: T2 of RGTA unit back in on position.

  2. Repeat the test steps under 1, 4 and 3 for all type of faults.

The Reach of the Third Zone

  1. De- block the third time step by putting the switch D: T3 of RGTA unit back in on position.

  2. Repeat the same test as for second zone.

Return Relay to Service and Associated Equipment

  1. Switch of the Test equipment.

  2. Withdraw the test plug from the relay.

  3. If any equipment associated with the relay has been isolated without the test plug it should be return to service by the following restoration steps:

  • Fault recorder, event recorder and disturbance alarm.

  • Acceleration scheme.

  • Breaker failure scheme.

Request Power Line be returned to Service

  1. Take crossed reading of all AC current and voltage supplied to the relay, and compare it with the load conditions. That reading should agrees the station meters reading: (Mw/MVAR/Power factor)

  2. Place a copy of the test result in the field maintenance.
