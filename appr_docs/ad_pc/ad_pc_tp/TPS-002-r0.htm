Introduction

This document outlines the testing procedure I1, which must be performed every 6 months as indicated by M6 in the above title block.

This patent concerns the test of Protection Relays of home transformer, manufactured by “ SKODA ”and “ABB ARAB” .  The transformer is of type KTRT 18X20, rated power 20MVA, vector group Dd0, and voltages 15.75 ±8x2%/6.3KV.

The first draft initiated by eng. Maysa Ahmady, tested at 27 Jan. 2003 by eng. Iman Rizk El–Ashry, revised by quality control engineer eng. Adel  Abd El Kader EL Nagar of EDEPCo, accepted by general director eng . Mahmoud Sakr, and approved by Eng .Mustafa Alaam Sector  Head.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks and “no smoking” signs surrounding the work area must be used.

    •

  • Wearing safety helmet, shoes are necessary

  • Ascertain the disconnection of the exciter, the generator, main transform and home transformer .

  • Be sure that the team members wear the necessary manufacturing safety equipments

  • Be aware of parts in service.

  • Follow the work permission instructions. 

 

Tools and Equipment

  • An A.C. voltage source of type Z11

  • An  A.C. current source of   type Z11

  • Varying - Resistance Box

  • An ammeter & A voltmeter

  • A timer unit TM-2 ( Program )

  • Some leads & screws.

 

 

 

 

 

Work to be Carried Out

 

Relays Preparation

  1. Review the manual.

  2. Prepare the previous results of the relay testing.

  3. Check the wires of the relay beforehand.

  4. Be sure that the unit and the transformer are disconnected and the d.c. source is available.

  5. Check the existence of the d.c. sources in panels.

  6. Disconnect the trip leads of the relay and connect the timer to them.

  7. Check all the leads used in tests.

 

Tools Preparation

  1. Connect the testing units of Z11 to a power source.

  2. Connect the timer to a power source and its terminals to the trip terminals of the relay under test.

  3. Place an ammeter on current leads of Z11 to measure the current.

  4. Connect the voltmeter in parallel with the voltage leads of Z11 ( the other unit ) to measure the voltage.

 

The test of some home transformer protection devices is achieved by isolating either C.T. circuits  or P.T. circuits or both .This is done by using a special socket for testing. This makes the test results depend only on the action of relays .

Test steps                               

  1. Connect the current leads of a tester unit Z11 to points (261,262) & (261,263) & (261,264) on low tension side of TA 10/1,TA11/1 respectively (on rosette) then terminals (268,265 ) & ( 268 ,266 ) & ( 268 , 267 ) on high tension side of TA8/1 respectively ( on rosette ) .

  2. Inject current to each couple of points till the red flag of the device displays.

  3. Repeat the steps for each pair of points either on L .T .S or H .T . S .

  4. Record the results.

Over Current , Type A3TX1

It’s a 3 phase over current protection without distinguishing of the effected phases with a time member.

C.T  : TA 8/2                    1000/5A

Test steps                            

  1. Place the socket ZZ10 in Z12.

  2. Fix the timer leads in ( 172, 185 ) at the rear of panel HH02 .

  3. Inject the current to terminals ( 11,14 ) &  ( 12,14 ) &  ( 13,14 ) of Z12 respectively ( ph – N ).

  4. Increase the current till the red flag indicating phase displays.  Let the timer start acting till it stops indicating the tripping time of the device (its red flay displays).

  5. Repeat the steps for each phase.

  6. Record the results.

Over Load , Type A3TX1

It’s a 3 phase over current protection without distinguishing of the effected phases with a time member.

C.T  :    TA 8/1                1000/5A

Test steps                            

  1. Place the socket ZZ10 in Z12.

  2. Fix the timer leads in ( 172, 188 ) at the rear of panel HH05 .

  3. Inject the current to terminals ( 11, 14 ) &  ( 12,14 ) &  ( 13,14 ) of Z12 respectively ( ph – N ).

  4. Increase the current till red flag indicating phase displays. Let the timer start acting till it stops indicating the signaling time of the device (its red flay displays).

  5. Repeat the steps for each phase.

  6. Record the results.

Over Current & Short Circuit of 1BA ,Type IKT943
        C.T. :                 TA10/2           2500/5A

Over Current & Short Circuit of 1BA  ,Type IKT943    
      C.T. :                 TA11/2           2500/5A

The previous 2 devices have the same sequence of testing and give only signals

 

 

Over Current Element

  1. Connect the tester ( Z11 ) leads to ( A2,A3 )  of the device.

  2. Loosen ( A4 , A5 )leads and connect the timer leads to them.

  3. Connect the main D.C. of the cubicle.

  4. Increase the current till the yellow lamp of the device ignites ( signal value ) . let the timer start acting till the red lamp ignites and the timer stops .

  5. Repeat the test by connecting the tester to ( A6 , A7 ) then ( A10 ,        A11 ) instead of ( A2,A3 )  .

 

Short Circuit Element

  1. Connect the tester ( Z11 ) leads to ( A2,A3 )  of the device .

  2. Loosen ( A8 , A9 )leads and connect the timer leads to them .

  3. Connect the main D.C. of the cubicle.

  4. Increase the current till the red lamp of the device ignites and the timer closes instantaneously.

  5. Repeat the test by connecting the tester to (A6  , A7 ) then ( A10 ,     A11 ) instead of ( A2,A3 )  

  6. Rerecord the results.
     

Both the over current and short circuit elements gives signals only with no tripping.

 

Short Circuit  ,Type D15X1

It’s a double - stage, 3 phase impedance protection with a circular characteristic and distinguishing of the effected phase. The second stage has a time member

C.T. :                      TA 8/2                1000/5A

       P.T. :          TV 2/2    15/√3 : 0.1/√3 : 0.1 / 3 KV .

Test Steps 

  1. Place the socket ZZ10 in Z2, and another one in Z9 (HH01)

  2. Fix the timer (element ) leads in ( 172,193 ) at the rear of panel HH02 .

  3. Inject the current to points (11,12) then  ( 12, 13 ) and finally  ( 11,13 ) of Z9 phase to  phase.

  4. Inject the voltage by the other tester to points (11, 12) then (12, 13) and finally ( 11,13 ) of Z2 phase to  phase.

  5. Fixing a value  of 4.3A, increase the injected voltage till the red flags indicating the injected phases disappear (reset value ) then decrease the voltage gradually  till the disappearing flags reappear( set value  of 1st stage ) . Let the timer act indicating the tripping time of the  1st stage.

  6. The tripping voltage (1st stage) represents the S.C. impedance of 1SP.

 

To test the 2nd stage Test

  1. Drive the 1st stage device out of place.

  2. Leave the timer leads in ( 172 , 193 ) at the rear of panel HH02 .

  3. Repeat the previous steps .

  4. When the flag of 2nd stage appears make the timer start acting till it stops recording the tripping time of 2nd stage.

  5. Repeat the previous steps for each pair of phases.

  6. Repeat the results of each phase – phase.

 

Returning the Relays to Service

  1. Release the leads of the testing instruments.

  2. Check the return of relay- wires and trip-leads to their original places and tighten them carefully .

  3. Press the main “RESET” button in HH01 for those devices in the control room .

  4. Press the “RESET” button for those devices in the 6.3 KV hall and check the relay setting .

  5. Be sure that the relay is completely ready for service any time.

  6. Alienate the testing materials and equipments in addition to the caution signs and leave the place clean.

  7. Return the work permission.
