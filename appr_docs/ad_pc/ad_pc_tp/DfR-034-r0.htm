  Introduction

This document outlines the testing procedure P1, which must be performed yearly as indicated by Y1 in the above title block.

The 7UT23 is a high Speed biased differential relay suitable for protecting three winding transformers it is extremely Stable during through external faults and will not operate during inrush currents on energizing. It is manufactured by Siemens.

This procedure details the steps required to completely check the proper operation and calibration of the 7UT23 relay ( figure 1 ).

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding work area is required.

  • Wear your safety equipment as helmet, shoes, breathing mask, safety glass...etc.

  • Power transformer isolation

  • Make sure CT secondary circuits are shorted before isolating the relay current circuits for current injection

  • Switch off DC power Supply before inserting or withdrawing any card from the relay module.

Work to be Carried Out

The work to be carried out section of this document is divided into 3 sections :

  1. The first is (preliminary steps to prepare for relay testing) describes the activities required prior to test .

  2. The second is (preparation for test) describes the steps to prepare the equipment for performing the actual test.

  3. The third is the actual test.

Preliminary Steps to Prepare for Relay Testing

  1. Obtain outage for Transformer Protected by this relay.

  2. Check the two bus disconnect Switches are opened and tagged.

  3. Inspect the CT secondary circuits and all cables supplying the relay including matching CTs .

  4. Use the station drawings to isolate the following:

  • Breaker trip coils.

  • Breaker failure scheme associated with the relay.

  • Disturbance alarms and fault recorder associated with the relay.

  1. Carefully follow these test procedure steps:

Preparation

  1. This procedure is used to perform off load relay testing.

  2. Look at the REA panel nameplate and check that this EEA system designation agrees with the

  3. Power transformer that has been taken out of service for maintenance

  4. Check there is no AC current form CT's by using a clip on ammeter.

  5. Check there is no AC voltage form VT's by using a voltmeter.

Relay Current Setting Test

  1. Connect the secondary injection test terminal to phase (R) terminal on UHV side terminal block.

  2. Slowly increase the current until the relay operates, as indicated by the Light Emitting Diode ( LED ) on the relay front panel.

  3. Record the operating (differential) current and check that this is within: ( ± 10% ) of the expected current as stated in the maintenance file.

  4. Check the relay trip contacts are closed when the relay picks up and that these contacts open as the current is reduced.

  5. Repeat the same for the alarm contact.

  6. Switch off the secondary injection test set.

  7. Press the reset button on the relay front panel and check that the led indicator resets and the alarm contact open.

  8. Repeat the steps from 1 to 7 for phases S , T on UHV side.

  9. Repeat the steps from 1 to 7 for phases R , S and T on HV side.

  10. Repeat the steps from 1 to 7 for phases R , S and T on LV side.

NOTE: Before removing the AC operating (injection) current Check there is no DC on the relay.

 Operating Time Test

  1. Inject current until the relay operates and switch off the test set.

  2. Connect the timed contact of the relay to the" stop timer" terminals of the secondary injection set.

  3. Switch on the current suddenly and note the operate time.

  4. Record the relay operate time, this should be < 30  mSec.

Return The Relay and Associated Equipments to Service

  1. Verify the test result match the relay setting, If not consult technical services.

  2. Trip the circuit breaker associated with the relay.

  3. Return relay to service by reconnecting the relay terminals which had been isolated before, and make sure that  CTs and VTs secondary circuits are in normal conditions.

  4. Any equipment associated with the relay which had been isolated before must be returned toservice by the following restoration steps.

  5. Breaker trip coils.

  6. Breaker failure scheme associated with this relay.

  7. Disturbance alarm and fault recorder associated with this relay.

 Request Power Transformer to be Returned in Service

  1. Take crossed readings of all AC currents and voltages from both HV and LV sides and compare them to those for normal load condition.

  2. Check that these readings agree with station meters: MW, MVAR, Ammeters and power factor

  3. Place a copy of the test result in the field maintenance file.
