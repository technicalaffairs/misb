Introduction

Breaker failure relay is a local back up relay scheme to protect the electrical System in case a circuit breaker fails to interrupt a fault. The Breaker failure relay has a time delay to ensure the clearing of fault by primary protection. This procedure, details the steps required to completely check the proper operation and calibration of the Breaker failure relay. It is recommended this procedure be carried out on a yearly bases. This work be carried out section of this document is divided into 3 sections.

The first preliminary steps to prepare for relay testing describes the activities required prior to testing. Some of these steps could be performed days before the actual test. The second section Preparation for testing describes the steps to set up the equipment for performing the actual tests. The third section is the actual test (Test Procedure).

The document is formatted the way to minimize the possibility of error causing personal injury or damage to equipment.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Maintenance staff should be will trained and qualified or directly supervised by some one who is.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear your safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes... etc.

  • Line or transformer isolation is required.

  • Make sure CT secondary circuits are shorted before isolating the relay current circuit.

  • Switch off DC power supply before inserting or withdrawing any card from the relay

 Tools and Equipment
  • Secondary injection test set
 

Work to be carried out

Preliminary Steps to Prepare for Relay Testing

  1. Review technical reference material: Manufacturer's manual.

  2. Obtain copy of relay setting from field maintenance file and compare it with the setting on the relay.

Preparations

  1. Obtain outage for line protected by this relay.

  2. Check that the bus and line disconnect switches are opened and tagged.

  3. Inspect the CT secondary circuits, and all cabling supplying the relay.

  4. Use the station drawing to isolate the following
  • Breaker trip coils and transfer trip scheme.
  • Breaker failure scheme associated with this relay.
  • Disturbance alarms and fault recorder associated with this relay.

Test Procedure

  1. From DC drawing, remove test switches (output circuits) which make trip for all circuits breakers of lines and initiate the bus bar lockout relay (86B).

  2. From AC drawing, open the test switch which fed the SX91 relay by AC current and insure that the relay is completed isolate from secondary circuits of current transformers.

  3. Connect between current terminals on test set to Ts/IA (E, D) for phase (R) on the relay.

  4. Inject and increase the current from zero to the setting value in HM91 unit in the relay.

      Note: During the injection current, we must simulate an operation of any protection to initiate the breaker failure relay.

  5. The relay and the lockout will operate and the red trip led will illuminate.

  6. Decrease the injected current.

  7. Reset the relay by the white reset button in the unit HT92 and the lockout will reset

  8. Repeat  privies step but change the injection terminal on the relay to TS/IA to (g, f) for phase (S) and (I, H) for phase (T).

  9. Record the results in the test sheet.

  10. For measuring time T1 and T2 in unit HT92, set T2 to zero value.

  11. Take free contact from the lockout relay to stop the timer.

  12. Record the time , it will be T1.

  13. Return T2 to the setting value.

  14. Repeat the step 11 to 13 and the time reading on timer =T1+T2

  15. Record the time reading on test sheet.

  16. Switch off the secondary injection test set.

  17. Remove the test lead, which is between the test set and the relay.

Return Relay and Associated Equipment to Service

  1. Return the AC test switches, which fed the SX91 by current from current Transformer (TS/IA) to its normal position.

  2. Return the output test switches (out put circuit) to its normal position and any equipment associated with the relay by the following restoration steps

  • Breaker trip coils and transfer trip scheme.

  • Breaker failure
