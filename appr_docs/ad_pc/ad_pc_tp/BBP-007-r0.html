Introduction

The INX-5 bus-bar protection is intended for very rapid and selective protection of high and extra-high voltage switchgear in 50 and 60 HZ Networks.

    This Bus-bar protection scheme detects all types of faults in systems with solidly earthen or insulated neutral. It can be used for the protection of outdoor systems as well as indoor metal-clad and gas isolated switchgear.

    The INX-5 is particularly suitable for application in systems with high short-circuit capacities and critical stability conditions, because of its extremely short tripping time and high reliability. The modules are then strictly assigned to the different parts of the system such as busbar, sectionalizers, feeders and couplers.

    Bus-bar protection systems can be provided for substations having maximum of quadruple Bus-bar system and a total of 63 feeders. (1 coupler = 2 feeders).

    This procedure details the steps required to completely check the proper operation and calibration of the INX-5 distance relay. It is recommended this procedure be carried out on a yearly bases. This work be carried out section of this document is divided into 3 sections.

    The first preliminary steps to prepare for relay testing describes the activities required prior to testing. Some of these steps could be performed days before the actual test. The second section Preparation for testing describes the steps to set up the equipment for performing the actual tests. The third section is the actual test.

    The document is formatted the way to minimize the possibility of error causing personal injury or damage to equipment.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, qualified maintenance staff carry the suitable safety ID category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required,

  • Personal protective equipment must be used as safety shoes, and safety Helmet.

  • Ensure that the Line isolation is done.

  • Make sure CT secondary circuits are shorted before isolating the relay's current circuit and injection the test current.

  • Don’t open the secondary circuit of CT since the high voltage produced may be dangerous to people and could damage equipment.

Tools and Equipment

  • Two secondary injection test sets.

  • Clip ON ammeter.

Work to be Carried Out

Preliminary Steps to prepare for relay testing

  1. Review technical reference material: Manufacturer’s Manual.

  2. Obtain a copy of relay setting from field maintenance file and compare it with the setting on the relay.

  3. Inspect all CT secondary circuits and all cabling supplying the relay.

  4. Use the station drawing to isolate the following

  • Breaker trip coils.

  • Breaker failure scheme associated with this relay.

  • Disturbance alarms and fault recorder associated with this relay.

Testing the Relay, and Protection Output

  1. Carefully follow these test procedure steps

  2. Verify the test results match the relay setting - If not consult technical services.

  3. Test trip all breakers associated with the relay.

Testing Procedure (s)

Tripping Test

  1. Put the switch "ON/OFF" on module EV92 in position "0" to interrupt all tripping circuits. This will result in the following

  • The Bus-bar block signal is annunciated in control room.

  • The yellow LED No. 2 and the red LED No. 3 on relay are lit up in EV92 module.

  1. Turn the key of the key-switch on module EV92 to the vertical position and leave it in the relay.

  2. Put the switch " ON/OFF " on module EV92 in position "1".

  3. To trip one or more circuit breakers, press on the green push-button on the relevant module

  • AB96 module : Trip all circuit breakers.

  • HI92 module : Trip the associated line breaker only.

  • HI95 module : Trip the associated bus coupler only.

  1. When one or more circuit breakers are tripped the red LED located above the green button on the relevant module will be illuminated and also the red LED No. "1" on module EV92.

  2. Record the results in Test Sheet No. 1, a copy is attached to this procedure.

  3. After every circuit breaker trip test, the circuit must be " RESET " by the white push-button "1" on module EV92.

  4. Put the switch " ON/OFF " on module EV92 in position "0" to interrupt all tripping circuits.

  5. Turn the key of the key-switch on module EV92 to the horizontal position and remove it.

  6. Put the switch " ON/OFF " on module EV92 in position "1".

Current Injection Test

  1. Turn off the manual block switch (ON\OFF) on module EV92 by putting it to position "O" to interrupt all tripping circuits and then

  • The Bus-bar block signal is annunciated in the control room.

  • The yellow LED No. 2 and the red LED No. 3 on relay are lit up in EV92 module.

  1. For every feeder and transformer, put a short on the terminal blocks connected to the CT’s and then open the link between the CT’s and the matching transformers on the INX5 module.

  2. Connect test leads between the two terminals on the secondary injection test set and the two terminal blocks for phase R as shown in fig. 1.

Supervision Test

  1. Slowly raise the injection test current until the yellow (LED) marked "ID " of the corresponding moduleIR91lightsup and also yellow LED "ID " for the same phase in module EV92 lights up.

  2. Record the "D I" values in Test Sheet No. 2. (Normally " D I " = 10 % of the Ikmim which is the differential current alarm level).

  3. After every injection current test on the relay , "RESET" the push-button No. 1 on the module EV92.

Operating Value Test

  1. Increase the injected current until the tripping value is reached and then

  • The Red LED’s which correspond to the appropriate phase at the IR91 module being tested will light up

  • The Red LED No. 1 in EV92 will light up.

  • For all HI92 modules for the appropriate Bus-bar, the Red LED’s located above the green push-button will light up.

  1. RESET the relay with push-button No. 1 on the module, EV92, after every injection current test.

  2. Record the pickup values in the Test Sheet No. 2.

  3. Repeat the above steps from step 1 to step 9 for the other phases.

  4. Repeat the above steps from step 1 to step 10 for the other feeders for this busbar protection.

Automatic Testing

    The testing checks all current circuits and the capability of the bus bar protection for tripping (internal short circuit) and for blocking by the saturation signals (external short circuit with saturation).

    The test program is divided into cycles 1 to 8 which are indicated by the numerical displays on module XL91. Each cycle consists of definite test steps.

  1. A test sequence can be started automatically by a pressing the start button on XL91 module.

  2. While a test sequence is in progress the external signal "protection in test" appears.

  3. The external signals "protection in test", "protection blocked" and "electronic defect" appear in the event of a test stop.

  4. Test Starting Is Impossible

  • When a trip command is present.

  • When an electronics monitor has operated.

  • When a monitor in the test unit XL91 has picked up (the green stand-by lamp of which is then out).

  • When the over all measurement IM91 is picked up or has not been rest.

  1. If the protection has been manually blocked an automatic start is prevented.

  2. If a defect is found, the test cycle is stopped with the fault code shown on the digital display on the XL 91 module. This and the additional signals supplied by other modules should make it is possible to locate the fault.

  3. In the event of a test stop the following procedure is recommended:

    4. Note: All signals present, pay particular attention to defect indications and which availability lamps are out on individual modules.

  4. Manually block the protection.

  5. Press the " END" button on XL 91 module to reset the test unit but it can not be done if a defect found in cycle "7 " or " 8 ". In this case it is only possibly to reset system by switching off the supply voltage to the protection in the INX5 system.

Return Relay and Associated Equipment to Service

  1. Return relay to service by removing the test lead connections from the relay.

  2. Any equipment associated with the relay has been isolated should be returned to service by the following steps

  • Breaker trip coils.

  • Breaker failure scheme associated with this relay.

  • Disturbance alarms and fault recorder associated with this relay.

  1. Check that CT and VT secondary circuits are in normal condition.

Request Power Line be returned to Service

  1. Take crossed readings of all AC currents and voltages supplied to the relay and compare them to those for normal load conditions.

  2. Check the readings agree with station meters: (MW/ MVAR/Power Factor/Amperes/Voltages)

  3. Place a copy of the test result in the field maintenance file.
