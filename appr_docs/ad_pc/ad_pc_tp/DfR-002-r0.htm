Introduction

This procedure details the steps required to completely check the proper operation and calibration of the transformer protection, Differential Relay types D202 & D203 relay. It is recommended this procedure be carried out on a yearly bases. This work be carried out section of this document is divided into 3 sections.

The first preliminary steps to prepare for relay testing describes the activities required prior to testing. Some of these steps could be performed days before the actual test. The second section Preparation for testing describes the steps to set up the equipment for performing the actual tests. The third section is the actual test.

The document is formatted the way to minimize the possibility of error causing personal injury or damage to equipment.

D202

Designed for two-winding transformer and unit connection transformers and generators. Inter-turn faults in the transformer winding are also detected to avoid unselective tripping when the transformer is switched on, the relay is made inrush-proof.

D202 + D203

For use with three winding power transformers, ancillary D203 contains the parts required for the third winding in addition to the differential relay.

It has three CT's the necessary rectifier and a tripping contactor.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area,

  • Wear safety and healthy equipments as safety helmet, safety shoes ..etc

  • Power transformer must be isolated.

  • Make sure that CT Secondary circuits are shorted before isolating the current circuit for test current injection.

Tools and Equipment

  • Two current source, 2 Multi-meter

  • 2 Variable resistor (0-100 ohm) 200 W

  • 2 switch S1, S2, Diode (Rectifier)

Work to be Carried Out

PICK UP TEST

  1. Connect the Secondary injection test set to the relay as shown in figure (1)

  2. Increase the injected current slowly until the relay operates.

  3. Record the operating (differential) current.

  4. Check the relay trips contacts are closed when the relay picks up, and these contacts open as the current is reduced.

  5. Switch off the secondary injection test set.

  6. Press the reset button on the relay in front panel and check that the flag reset.

  7. Inject the current until the relay operates and switch of the test set.

  8. Connect the timed contact of the relay to the “stop time” terminals of the secondary injection test set as shown in figure (2)

  9. Switch on the current suddenly and record the operate time.
