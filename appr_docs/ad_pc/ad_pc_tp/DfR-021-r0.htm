Introduction

Transverse differential protection is considered a main port. for lines-132k.v parallel lines.
 This protection operates instantaneously to protect transmission lines against phase to ground or double phase short-circuits, this port. must been serviceably when the two circuits are in parallel the protections disconnected automatically in the following two cases:

  • When the circuit breaker of any of the parallel circuits is disconnected manually or automatically

  • When the BUS - coupler circuit breaker is disconnect in this case, a special link (13H) must be connected in order to but again the transverse diff. port. into operation when the two cir .s, are connected to the same BUS-BAR the principle of operation of transfers diff. port. depends on measuring the magnitude and direction of the difference between the two currents of the parallel lines, in normal condition this different is equal zero, in short-circuit condition the value of this difference is sufficient for operation of the starting current relay (CR)

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, qualified maintenance staff carry the suitable safety ID category,

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area is required,

  • Personal protective equipment must be used as safety shoes, and safety Helmet.

  • Ensure that the Line isolation is done.

  • Make sure CT secondary circuits are shorted and PT secondary circuits are opened.

Work to be Carried Out

Preliminary steps to prepare for relay testing

  1. Review technical reference material: Manufacturers manual

  2. Obtain copy of relay setting from field maintenance file and compare it with the setting on the relay

  3. Obtain outage for line protected by this relay

  4. Check that the bus and line disconnect switches are opened and tagged

  5. Inspect the CT see. circuits, the V.T. miniature cir. B. R. (MCB) and all cabling supplying the relay

  6. Use the station drawing to isolated the following:

  • Breaker trip coils

  • Breaker failure scheme associated with this relay

  • Disturbance alarms and fault recorder associated with this relay

Testing the relay and protection output

  1. Carefully follow these test procedure steps from page 4 to page 7

  2. Verify the results match the relay setting if not consult technical services

  3. Test trip the breaker associated with the relay

Prior to starting testing procedure ensure the foll. steps

  1. This procedure is used to perform off load relay testing

  2. Check that the panel nameplate agrees with the EEA designation of the line that has been taken out of service for maintenance

  3. Check there is no AC current from CT by using clip - on ammeter

  4. Check there is no AC voltage from VT by using voltmeter

  5. Check all external connections to the relay are in good conditions and check that the terminal block wires are tight
