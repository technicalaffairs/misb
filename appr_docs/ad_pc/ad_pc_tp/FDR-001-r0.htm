Introduction

This document outlines the testing procedure P1, which must be performed on an annually bases as indicated by Y1 in the above title block

The CHC11A relay is a complete three phase and ground multi contact high-speed non-directional over current relay. It has two targets, it may be applied wherever a high speed fault detector is required and it is used to detect the failed circuit breaker and to select the back up breakers to  be tripped in order to isolate the fault. The relay has also a phase fault detector ( PFD ) used to detect three phase, phase to phase double phase to ground faults, and a ( GFD ) unit ( ground fault detector ), which would detect single – phase to ground faults.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued & the staff  of test must be qualified and carry the suitable safety category.

    •

  • The transformer must be out of service.

Tools and Equipment

  • Test leads.  
  • Test plug handle.
  • Secondary injection test set.

Work to be Carried Out

Preparation

  1. Make Sure C.T secondary circuit are shorted at the incoming terminal blocks before isolating the relay current circuit for test

  2. Look at field maintenance fill and compare the position of the setting switches on relay with those shown on the setting sheet .

Testing the relay

  1. Connect D.C voltage  between terminals ( 9  – 10 ) where 9 is the – ve and 10 is

    the  + ve .

  2. Connect test leads to the relay contact ( 1 – 12 ) .

  3. Make short contact to the terminals ( 4 – 6 – 8  ) .

Phase to ground test

  1. Connect test leads to  current terminals ( 3 – 4 ) .

  2. Increase the current until the relay picks up and closes its contacts .

  3. Record the current value and the time value in the test sheet .

  4. Connect test leads to  current terminals ( 5 – 6 ) & ( 7 – 8 ) respectively .

  5. Repeat the steps 5 and 6 .

Phase to phase test

  1. Connect test leads to  current terminals ( 3 – 5 ) , ( 5 – 7 ) , ( 7 – 3 ) .

  2. Repeat the steps 5 , 6 each time of the terminals .

GFD unit test

  1. Connect test leads to  current terminals ( 14 – 15 )  .

  2. Increase the current slowly until the GFD  unit picks up and the target appears instantaneously

  3. record the current value in the test  sheet .

The steps for putting the relay into service        

  1. Check that the CT and PT secondary connections return to its normal position as before the test.

  2. Disconnect the test leads from the terminals ( 14 - 15 ).

  3. Disconnect the test leads from the trip contact ( 1 - 12 ).

  4. Disconnect the short connection between terminals ( 4 - 6 - 8 ).

  5. Disconnect the leads from the D.C. terminals  ( 9 - 10 ).
