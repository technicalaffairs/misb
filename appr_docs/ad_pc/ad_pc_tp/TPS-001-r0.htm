Introduction

This document outlines the testing procedure I1, which must performed every six months as indicated by M6 in the title block.

This patent concerns the test of Protection Relays of home transformer, manufactured by “ SKODA ”and “ABB ARAB”.  The transformer is of type KTRT 18X20, rated power 20MVA, vector group Dd0, and voltages 15.75 ±8x2%/6.3KV.

The first draft initiated by eng. Maysa Ahmady, tested at 27 Jan.2003 by eng. Iman Rizk El-Ashry, revised by EDEPCo. quality control engineer eng. Adel Abdel Kader EL Nagar, accepted by general director eng. Mahmoud Sakr, and approved by eng. Mustafa Allam Talkha Power Station Sector Head

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety class,

  • Safety fence with caution marks & “no smoking” signs surrounding the work area is required,

  • Wear safety helmet, safety shoes

  • Make sure that CT secondary circuits are shorted before isolating the current circuits for test transformer protection scheme.

  • Ascertain the disconnection of the exciter, generator, main transform and home transformer

  • Be aware of parts in service.

  • Follow the work permission instructions. 

Tools and Equipment

  • An AC voltage source of type Z11.

  • An AC current source of type Z11

  • An ammeter & A voltmeter.

  • A timer unit TM-2 ( Program )

  • Some leads & screws.

 

 

Work to be Carried Out

Relay Preparation

  1. Review the manual.

  2. Prepare the previous results of the relay testing.

  3. Check the wires of the relay beforehand.

  4. Be sure that the unit and the transformer are disconnected and the d.c. source is available.

  5. Check the existence of the d.c. sources in panels.

  6. Disconnect the trip leads of the relay and connect the timer to them.

  7. Check all the leads used in tests.

Test Tools Preparation

  1. Connect the testing units of Z11 to a power source

  2. Connect the timer to a power source and its terminals to the trip terminals of the relay under test

  3. Place an ammeter on current leads of Z11 to measure the current.

  4. Connect the voltmeter in parallel with the voltage leads of Z11 ( the other unit ) to measure the voltage.

The test of all main transformer protection devices is achieved by isolating either CT circuits or PT circuits or both.

This is done by using a special socket for testing. This makes the test results depend only on the action of relays

Testing Procedures

 

Differential of  Unit  1BT10, R43X1,SKODA  for ( Generator + Transformer )

 

It is a 3-point differential protection with a measuring member in each phase locked switching on surge


Test Steps 

 

C.T. TA 9/1,TA1/2  10.000/5A

TA5/3        1000/5A

  1. Place the socket ZZ10 in Z10 (TA 9/1)(HH01)

  2. Inject the current on points (11,12,13,14(N)) of Z10 either phase - phase or phase - neutral

  3. Fix the timer leads to (172,197) at the rear of panel HH02

  4. Increase the current till the red flag of the device displays and the timer closes (almost instantaneously)

  5. Repeat the test for each case and record both the current and tripping time

  6. Place the socket ZZ10 in Z17 (TA1/2 & TA5/3)(HH01)

  7. Inject the current phase - phase on Z17 for each current transformer

  8. Repeat the steps 3 to 5

  9. Place a socket ZZ10 in Z10 and another one the Z17

  10. Inject the current on the neutral of Z10 and a phase of Z17 (TA1/2) then (TA5/3)

  11. Repeat the steps 3 to 5

  12. Record the results

 

Short Circuit In Neutral, A22X1

 

It is a one - phase over current protection suitable e.g. for locking of earth fault protection GF1XIby the earth current of very high voltage

 

 

 

 CT  TA6/1  750/5A (1st unit)

   TA6/1  1000/5A    (2nd unit)

 

Test Steps

  1. Place the socket ZZ10 in Z5 (TA6/1) (HH01)

  2. Inject the current in points (11,12(N)) of Z5

  3. Fix the timer leads to (172,195) at the rear of panel HH05

  4. Increase the current gradually till the red flag of the device displays. At this moment, make the timer start acting till it stops indicating the signaling time

  5. Record the current and time

 

Tank Protection type A33TX1,  F17

It is a three phase / RST / over current protection with distinguishing of the effected phase with a time member

It is a protection device for short circuit protection of the body of tank this operate if any earth fault happened on the body itself .

 

C.T.     TA7     200/5A

Test Steps

  1. Place the socket ZZ10 in Z6 (HH01)

  2. Inject the current on points (11,12(N)) of Z6

  3. Fix the timer leads to (172,196) at the rear of panel HH02

  4. Increase the current gradually till the red flag of the device displays. At this moment, make the timer start acting till it stops indicating the tripping time

  5. Record the current and time

 

Comparison Protection (Pilot )  F52  , TYPE S103, S105

This protection is a differential protection on the Transmission Line between transformer 1ATO, (15.75/220kv) and B.B. GIS, restricted by operation of over current F51 on that TL .

 

C.T.      TA5/1       1000/5A

Test Steps

  1. Place the socket ZZ10 in XJ2 in HS01

  2. Inject the current on points (11,12,13,14(N)) of XJ2 either phase - phase or phase - neutral

  3. Increase the current gradually till the red flag of the device displays

  4. Repeat the test for the different cases of injection

  5. Record the current value in each case

 

Quick Acting Relay (Pilot) type A15

 

C.T.       TA5/1       1000/5A

Test Steps

  1. Place the socket ZZ10 in XJ1 in HS01

  2. Inject the current on points (11,12) then (13,14) and finally (15,16) of XJ1

  3. Increase the current gradually till the red flag of the device displays

  4. Repeat the test for each cases of injection

  5. Record the results

 

Earth Fault Protection type GF1TX1

 

The protection measures voltage of the generator neutral against the earth. At earth connection Ug=0 takes place and the protection acts

 

 

 

P.T.  TV3/1    15:Ö3/0.1 : Ö3/ 0.1 : 3 KV

 

Test Steps

  1. Place the socket ZZ10 in Z1 (HH01)

  2. Inject the voltage in phase R & the neutral of TV 3/1

  3. Fix the timer leads to (172,195) at the rear of panel HH05

  4. Increase the voltage gradually till the red flag of the device displays. At this moment, make the timer start acting till it stops indicating the signaling time.

  5. Record the voltage and time

 

 

 

Restricted Ground Fault type RADHD,

 

C.T.   TA25(1st unit)   1000/5A
         TA(2nd unit)      1000/5A
           TA5/2              1000/5A

Test Steps

  1. Place the special testing socket of the device in its place in HS01

  2. Fasten the tester leads in (A3,A4) of the socket

  3. Increase the injected voltage gradually till the red flag of the device displays

  4. Record the value of the voltage

 

Differential of AT10 type RAX1

 

 

C.T.   TA4/2     10.000/5A
          TA 5/4       1000/5A

Test Steps

  1. Place the socket ZZ10 in X01 (TA5/4) in HS07

  2. Inject the current to points (3,4,5) of Z01 phase to phase

  3. Increase the injected current gradually till the red flags of the injected phases display. The timer closes instantaneously

  4. Record the values of current and time values in the test sheet

  5. Repeat the previous steps to points (3,4,5) of Z02 phase to phase in HS07

  6. Record the values of current and time values in the test sheet.

  7. Repeat the previous steps to points (1,2,3) of Z04 - operating coil - phase to phase in HS07

  8. Record the values of current and time values in the test sheet.

 

Returning the Relays to Service

  1. Release the leads of the testing instruments.

  2. Check the return of relay- wires and trip-leads to their original places and tighten them carefully.

  3. Press the main “RESET” button in HH01 for those devices in the control room.

  4. Be sure that the relay is completely ready for service any time.

  5. Alienate (push out) the testing materials and equipments in addition to the caution signs and leave the place clean.

  6. Return the work permission.
