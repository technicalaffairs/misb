Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Outage arrangement is required.

  • Check from the station drawing that the test plug will isolate breaker trip coils, breaker failure scheme, and any alarm associated with the relay.

  • If the test plug does not isolate the previous steps you have to isolate it.

Tools and Equipment

  • Test Plug handle, Test Leads

  • Clamp ammeter, Secondary injection test set OR Double-F2200 test set

Work to be Carried Out

Preparation

  1. Make Sure CT secondary circuit are shorted at the incoming terminal blocks before isolating the relay current circuit for test.

  2. Look at field maintenance file and compare the setting on relay with those shown on the test sheet

Testing the Relay       

  1. Check the pickup of the main unit it should pickup 30 % of tap rating +10% or the pickup should be between 0.78 and 0.96 Amp. the main unit trips at (1 – 1.5 A).

  2. Connect test leads between the current terminals (3 - 5) terminal 5 is the common, terminal 3 is the service transformer. Figure 1.

  3. Increase the current until the relay trips instantaneously.

  4. Record the current value in the test sheet .

  5. Connect test leads between the current terminals ( 4 -5 ) terminal 4 is the generator (1) 11.5 KV terminal 5 is the common.

  6. Increase the current until the relay trips instantaneously the trans. Lock out the two generators lockout .

  7. Record the current value in the test sheet .

  8. Repeat the steps 1 , 2 and 3 to terminals ( 6 - 5 ) which terminals is the generator (2) 11.5KV and to terminal ( 5 -7 ), which terminal 7 is the transformer 220KV.

  9. The instantaneous over current unit located at the upper right hand side of the relay by passing a high current through the terminal ( 5 – 6 ).

Electrical Test

  1. Minimum pick up of main operating unit .

  2. Minimum pick up of the instantaneous over current unit

  3. The instantaneous over current unit located at the upper right hand side of the relay should be checked by passing a high current through the (5 – 6) terminal.

Return Relay and Associated Equipment to Service

  1. Return relay to service by removing the test leads from the relay terminal block and the test set i.e.
    -     Disconnect the test leads from the terminals ( 3 - 4 ), ( 4 - 5 ), ( 5 - 6 )

  2. Make sure CT and PT secondary circuits are in normal condition.

  3. If any equipment associated with the relay has been isolated, it should be returned to service by the following restoration steps:
           - Breaker trip coils.
           - Breaker failure scheme associated this relay.
           - Disturbance alarm and fault recorder associated with this relay.
