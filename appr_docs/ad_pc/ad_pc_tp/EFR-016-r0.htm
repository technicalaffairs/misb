Introduction

This document outlines the testing procedure P1, which must be performed every 6 months as indicated by M6 in the above title block.

The IAV relays are single phase, voltage operated induction – disk relays with time delay, The type IAV51A ( 64 B ) is an over voltage relay used to protect the generator bus bar from ground faults. The type IAV51K ( 64 G ) is a low pick up voltage relay used for ground fault protection It is of the same construction except that it has a capacitor and tapped resistor giving a low pick – up voltage.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued & the staff  of test must be qualified and carry the suitable safety precautions.

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area

  • The generator must be out of service.

Tools and Equipment

  • Test Plug handle and test Leads.

  • Secondary injection test  set

Work to be Carried Out

Preparation

  1. Make Sure P.T secondary circuit are opened at the incoming terminal blocks before isolating the relay voltage circuit for test.

  2. Obtain copy of relay setting from field maintenance file and compare it with the setting on the relay.

Testing the Relay

For Bus Bar Ground Relay (51A) 

  1. Connect the test leads between  the voltage terminals ( 5 - 6 ) .                 figure (1)

  2. Connect the test leads between the relay contact ( relay trip ) ( 1 , 2 )        figure (1)

  3. Increase the voltage until the relay picks up  .

  4. Record this voltage  value in the test sheet as the relay start

  5. Increase the voltage to the values ( 100 % , 120 % , 140 % , … to 220 % ) of rated value .

  6. Record the time values each time in the test sheet  .

For Generator Ground Relay (51K)     

  1. Connect  the test leads between the voltage terminals ( 3 – 6 ) .                  figure (2)

  2. Repeat the steps from 2 to 4 .

  3. Increase the voltage values ( 30 , 40 , 50 , 60 . 65 ) and record the trip time at each voltage value in the test sheet.

The steps for putting the relay into service         

          Check that the CT and PT secondary connections return to its normal position as before the test.

 

For Bus Bar Ground Relay

  1. Disconnect the test leads from the terminals ( 5 - 6 ).

  2. Disconnect the test leads from the trip contact ( 1 - 2 ).

For Bus Bar Ground Relay

  1. Disconnect the test leads from the terminals ( 3 - 6 ).

  2. Disconnect the test leads from the trip contact ( 1 - 2 ).
