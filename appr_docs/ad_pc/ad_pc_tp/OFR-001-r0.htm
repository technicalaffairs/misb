Introduction

This document outlines the testing procedure P1, which must be performed on an annually bases as indicated by Y1 in the above title block

These relays are of induction disk type, for protection of apparatus against the effect of over frequency or under frequency. These relays are used to make on alarm and raise a target, They have two  contacts. The left contact closes on under frequency and the right contact closes on over frequency.

The first draft  initiated by eng. Fayza El-Sharkawy, tested by eng. Shirin Hamdy, eng. Azza Salama at 29 April 2003, revised by EDEPC quality control Eng. Adel El-Nagar, accepted by General Director eng. Mahmoud Saker and approved by eng. Moustfa Alam Head Sector of Talkha Power Station.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the maintenance staff should carry the suitable ID safety category,

  • Maintenance staff should be trained well, qualified or directly supervised by some one who is.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Personal safety and healthy equipment must be used as safety shoes, safety helmet ... etc

    •

  • The generator must be out of service.

Tools and Equipment

  • Test Plug handle and Test Leads.

  •  PCR 500 L Test set .

Work to be Carried Out

Preparation

  1. Make Sure P.T secondary circuit are opened at the incoming terminal blocks before isolating the relay voltage circuit for test

  2. Look at field maintenance file and compare the position of the setting switches on relay with those shown on the test sheet .

Testing the Relay

  1. Connect the test leads between the relay contact ( trip contact ) (1 - 2 ).

Low ( Under ) Frequency Test

  1. Connect the relay studs ( 5 – 6 ) to voltage terminal by the test leads.

  2. Increase the voltage to 115 v.

  3. Adjust the frequency to the normal value.

  4. Decrease the frequency slowly until the left target ( flag ) appears .

  5.  Record the frequency value and the time value at which the flag appears in the test sheet .

High ( Over ) Frequency Test

  1.  Connect the relay studs ( 7 , 8 ) to the voltage terminal by the test leads .

  2.  Repeat the steps ( 3 , 4 ).

  3.  Increase the frequency slowly until the right target appears.

  4.  Record the frequency  value and the time value at which the flag a pears  in  the test sheet.

Return Relay and Associated Equipment to Service

  1. Return relay to service by removing the test leads from the relay terminal block and the test set i.e.
    -     Disconnect the test leads from the terminals ( 7-8 )
    - Disconnect the test leads between the trip contact ( 1 - 2 )

  2. Make sure CT and PT secondary circuits are in normal condition.

  3. If any equipment associated with the relay has been isolated, it should be returned to service by the following restoration steps:
           - Breaker trip coils.
           - Breaker failure scheme associated this relay.
           - Disturbance alarm and fault recorder associated with this relay.
