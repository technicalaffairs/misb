Introduction 

The PYTS is a fast and accurate switched distance relay scheme, which employs the mho principle of measurement. It provides phase and earth fault protection and can be applied economically to short or medium length over head transmission and distribution lines. Complete three phases, three- zone distance protection is provided using a mho characteristic for power system.

This procedure details the steps required to completely check the proper operation and calibration of the PYTS relay. It is recommended this procedure be carried out on a yearly bases. This work be carried out section of this document is divided into 3 sections

The first preliminary steps to prepare for relay testing describes the activities required prior to testing. Some of these steps could be performed days before the actual test. The second section Preparation for testing describes the steps to set up the equipment for performing the actual tests. The third section is the actual test. The document is formatted the way to minimize the possibility of error causing personal injury or damage to equipment.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Maintenance staff should be well trained, qualified or directly supervised by some one who is.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Safety and healthy equipment must be used (safety helmet , safety shoes ... etc).

  •  Line isolation and work permit required.

  •  Make sure CT secondary circuits are shorted for isolating the current circuits for test current injection

  •  Set the earthling link on the relay to line side position

Tools and Equipment

  • ZFB test set

  • Monitor point box

  • Time inter voltmeter with pulse start and stop facilities

  • Two heavy current test plugs for the modular case.

  • AC voltmeter, 0-250 volts

  • DC voltmeter, 0-250 volts.

  • Multi-range AC ammeter O-IA, 0-5A, 0-IOA.

Work to be carried out

Purpose of the Tests

  1. The correctness of all wiring

  2. Correct calibration of the starting, measuring, timing and auxiliary relays

  3. Correct polarity of the main VT and CT circuits so that the scheme operates for faults in the required direction

  4. Look at field maintenance file and compare the position of the setting switches on relay with those shown on the setting sheet

Testing the Relay, and Protection output

  1. Carefully follow these test procedure steps

  2. Verify the test result match the relay setting if not consult technical services

  3. Test trip the circuit breaker associated with the relay

Zone Timer Test

  1. Select the switch of "auto-reclosure " on "out" position.
  2. Connect a dc voltmeter (0:100v) to the monitor point box-terminals 23 (+) & 24 the voltmeter should indicate30 vd.c.
  3. Make short between 23 (+) and 22 for phase A at this moment tripping flag indication A then Z2 thenZ3.
  4. Remove the short between 23 (+) and 22 and make reset to the protection relay on the module 7
  5. Repeat the above step 3 and 4 for phase B and phase C according the table below.

Function Test "Current Setting At Zero Voltage"

  1. Ensure that the switch of "auto reclosure" on"out" position

  2. Select(R-N) on the supply unit selector ewitch.

  3. Select switch S2 on the control unit on "ON" position.

  4. Select the "%potential" switches S5 and S6 on the control unit to zero.

  5. Select Switch  S1 on the supply unit to the "ON" position. The A.C. voltmeter on the control unit should indicate zero volts.

  6. Select switch S3 on the control unit on normal position.

  7. Press the "Fault push button" on the control unit. Tripping flag indication ( A, Z2, Z3) should appear and make reset.

  8. Repeat step 7 for (Y-N, B-N, R-Y, Y-B and B-R).

  9. Select switch S3 on the control unit on "REVERSE" position

  10. Repeat step 2, 7 and 8 but the protection relay should make block.

Tests On Measuring Unit

Zone One Reach

  1. Ensure that the switch of "auto reclosure" on "out" position.

  2. Select (R-Y) on the supply unit selector switch.

  3. Select switch S3 on the control unit on "Normal" position.

  4. Select the "%potential" switches S5 and S6 on the control unit with a value around to the value of Pi.

  5. Press the "Fault" push button on the control unit with changing the value of PI by using S5 and S6 until get the last value to get zone I Reach.

  6. Tripping Flag indication (A, B) should appear.

  7. Record this actual value of P% through the reading of switches S5 and S6 on the 'test sheet at the column of " P% ACTUAL and FLAGS".

  8. Make reset for the protection relay.

  9. Repeat step 2 to 7 with changing (R-Y) to (YB) and (RB)

  10. Repeat 9 with changing PI to P' for (R-N), (Y-N) and (B-N)

Zone 2 Reach
  • Repeat step zone 1 reach with using P1 and P'2.

Zone 3 Reach

  • Repeat step zone 1 reach with using P3 and P'3.

Zone EX Reach

  1. Put the switch of "auto reclosure" on "IN" position.
  2. Repeat step 2 to 10 for PEx and P'EX.

Trip Test

  1. Request the operator to switch on the main circuit breaker with bus bar and line isolators in off position.

  2. On test plug A make short between left, right sides of terminals no. 1, 2

  3. Press the "fault" push bottom on the control unit with any % potential value of Pi or P2 or P3.

  4. Check that the circuit breaker tripped with alarm.

  5. Finally switch off the Z.F.B test set and take out the test plugs A then B.

  6. Return back the heavy current connectors I Y then I Z.

On Load Tests

  1. With the line energized check the voltage input to the relays across each pair of phases and each phase to neutral.

  2. Check for correct phase rotation with a phase rotation meter and should beABC.

  3. The directional test should be carried out with the relay energized from the line main VT's and CT's with a load current 'in the tripping direction and preferably at a lagging power factor

  4. Remove the relay cover and connect the monitor point box to module 1 of the scheme Monitor points 2 and 24 must be joined as shown in fig. (1).

  5. Remove heavy current connector "I Z" from the right side of the relay case and replace it with test plug A wired as shown in fig. (5)

  6. Omitt'mg connections across terminals 1,2,3 and 4 of test plug A will automatically isolate the out going trip circuit to the circuit breaker.

  7. Select switch KD on module 6 to the position marked "oo".

  8. Link together terminals 22 to 23 on the monitor point box , this selects the mho unit to the (A-N) phase and the scheme should trip instantly and indicate a (A-N) fault.

  9. Remove the link from the monitor point box.

  10. Reset the flag indicator with switch "R" on module 7.

  11. Link terminals 21 and 23 on the monitor point box . This selects the mho unit to the (B-N) phase and the scheme should trip instantly and 'indicate a (B-N) fault.

  12. Repeat paragraph 8 and 9.

  13. Link terminals 20 and 23 on the monitor point box this selects the mho unit to the (C-N) phase and the scheme should trip instantly and indicate a (C-N) fault.

  14. Repeat paragraph 8 and 9.

  15. The current supplied to the relay should now be reversed by the use of test plug "B" as shown in fig (6) with the cross over connection made on phases A,B and C of the current circuits.

  16. Repeat paragraph 7 to 13. In this case however, 'instantaneous tripping should not take place since the load current is in the non- tripping direction.

  17. Finally remove test plug "A" from the modular case then "B" and replace it by heavy current connector "IY" then (C I Z15).

  18. Put the setting KD on the original position given in the maintenance file.
