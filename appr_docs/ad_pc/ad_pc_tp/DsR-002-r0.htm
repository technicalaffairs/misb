Introduction

Main Features

  • Short-circuit protection for all types of faults in effectively earthed power transmission systems.

  • Completely static design of all measuring and logic circuits with contact outputs for tripping and remote signaling.

  • Duplicate-channel construction of measuring and logic circuits and duplicate-channel control of the trip relays.

  • On-line measuring systems arranged to continuously monitor the power system quantities.

  • Of them have an offset quadrilateral impedance characteristic; the remaining 6 have a quadrilateral impedance characteristic and use cross-polarization techniques for directional measurement.

  • Optimum matching to the fault parameters; the polygon-shaped tripping areas provide high fault sensitivity combined with exceptional stability against load encroachment and power swings.

  • Shortest tripping time typically 12 ms.

  • The measurement is based upon a duplex block-block coincidence measuring principle. This technique enables half-cycle minimum operating time combined with accuracy and inherent stability against interference and adverse transients.

  • Separate and independent setting of reach along the R-and X-axis for each measuring element allows:

  • Compensation of unequal phase-impedances on non-transposed lines.

  • Setting of high R-to X ratios on phase-to-ground units where high fault resistance compensation may be necessary.

  • Setting of small R-to-X ratios on phase-to-phase units where load encroachment may be a problem but high fault resistances are not to be expected.

  • Healthy phase or memory voltage polarized directional measurement provides unlimited directional sensitivity for terminal faults.

  • Impedance measuring characteristics and tripping logic are designed to provide phase-selective starting and tripping commands for single-pole auto reclosing schemes.

  • Single-pole or three-pole tripping of single-phase-to-ground faults can be plug-selected.

  • Time-delayed back-up distance zones three of which are directional forward zones;

  • The remaining one being a zone with an offset characteristic and with separate settings for forward and reverse reach.

  • The hardware is of plug-in modular construction. It forms part of a series of highly compatible, standardized distance protection schemes (7SL13, 7SL24, 7SL40).

  • Relay inputs are provided for blocking or zone-extension from external sources

  • A built-in directional test feature allows a directional check of the measuring systems this feature is particularly helpful to verify the correct polarity of C.T. and VT connections.

  • Dead fault protection is incorporated and enables instantaneous tripping of the offset distance zone when switching onto a dead fault.

  • DC/DC converter for supply of static circuits, the operating principle of which of which permits a wide station battery voltage variation.

  • The distance protection is rewired for a range of frequently ruined supplementary functions:

  • Measured voltage monitor 7TU32; (Fuse failure detector) monitors continuously the V.T secondary voltages for short-circuits and open-circuits.

  • Instantaneous short-circuit trip 7TJ15; practically independent of d.c. component (transient free) it enables a current grading in the case of long lines both with and without series capacitors.

  • Power swing auxiliary 7TL16 + 7TW42; prevents distance protection tripping due to power swing and can also be used for intentional selective de-coupling of network sections undergoing power swings.

  • Phase memory 7TF2; enables a correct directional measurement even for faults behind series capacitors.

  • A comprehensive range of supplementary functions is available.

  • Tele-protection interfaces 7SW11.

  • For all transferred tripping, and unblocking schemes

  • Auto re-closing schemes 7vk3.

  • Single-pole or three-pole, one-shot or multi-shot.

  • Parallel line auxiliary 7SM11.

  • Compensates the influence of inductive coupling of the zero-sequence systems in the case of parallel lines.

  • Distance-to-fault locator 7SE10,

  • Operating with a digital calculator

  • Test-combination 7XV66,

  • Which isolates the VT and CT inputs in the test position and enables secondary injection testing via a standard test plug.

Safety Precautions

To avoid any feedback from associated equipment (Auto re-closing unit or signal transmission option) the following precautions must be observed:

  • Line isolation and work permit required.

  • The mode of operation of the input relay K2 on 7TS48 (DE149 b8-d8) must be set so that the relay is not blocked when the voltage transformer protection device is off.

  • The power supply to the AR unit must be switched off. (Alternatively the AR Power supply should be withdrawn from the rack in the volt-free condition). Switching off the AR unit alone is not sufficient, because when the AR unit is switched off the distance relay is coupled in the three phase mode (phase selective trip is not then possible).

  • The carrier interface option must be taken out of operation. (put the miniature slide-switch on module 7TW65/66 in the BLOCK position). In the SV position (signal comparison) the trip command will be blocked if the receive signal is missing.

  • Isolate DC supply to relay before any adjustments or maintenance on the relay.

Work to be Carried Out

Preliminary Steps to Prepare for Relay Testing

  1. Review technical reference material manufactures / manual.

  2. Obtain copy of relay setting from field maintenance file.

  3. Obtain outage for line protected by this relay.

  4. Check that the bus and line disconnect switches are opened and tagged.

  5. Inspect the secondary circuits of CT, VT miniature circuit breakers.

  6. (MCB) and all cabling supplying the relay.

  7. Isolate the breaker failure scheme associated with this protection.

  8. Block any disturbance alarms or fault recorders associated with this protection.

  9. Turn off the associated auto re-closure scheme.

Preparation of Test

  1. Place the two cases one on the top of other (the voltage unit on the top of current unit).

  2. Connect the two units together by the cable connector 7XV6201 between test pulg “X2” in current unit and test plug “X1” in voltage unit.

  3. Connect the power supply cable 7XV6200 (3*380 V) between the plug “X1” in the current unit and the source (careful the positive sequence).

  4. Connect the cable 7XV6200-1 between the plug “X3” in current unit and the test plug 7XV61 on the relay.

  5. Connect an ammeter with the socket “17,18” in current unit and voltmeter with the socket “5,6” in voltage unit.

Directional Check

  1. Simulate a 3-pole fault L1-L2-L3 (without E).

  2. Adjust the voltages at the rated voltage.

  3. Adjust the currents at approximately 0.5 *rated current (symmetrical for 3-phases)

  4. In the forward direction, the green LED’S on the six modules 7TL52 come on.

  5. By changing over the directional plugs on module 7TL51 or reversal of the voltages from the test set, the opposite check can be made.

Checking the Current Detectors

(A) Minimum Earth Current Pick up 7TJ5250

  1. Simulate a fault L-E.

  2. Set voltage at rated value, current at zero.

  3. Slowly raise the current.

  4. When the set value is exceeded, the indication “pick-up E” comes on.

(B) Minimum Phase Current Pick up 7TJ1500

  1. Simulate a fault L-E.

  2. Set a voltage and current at zero.

  3. Slowly raise the current.

  4. When the set value is exceeded, a phase selective trip results.

  5. Repeat the test for the other phases.

Start Operation Test

 Off Set Quadrilateral Polyon Characteristics

  1. Preparation of test sheet with the critical values.

  2. Turn D.C switch to position “O” on DC/DC converter module 7TN36 on the relay.

  3. Remove under voltage module “u9” 7TV32 on the relay.

  4. Set timer plug T3, T4 at ∞ on module 7TT19.

  5. Turn the test switch (Test/service) on the relay to position “TEST”.

  6. Insert the test plug in the test socket module on the relay.

  7. Return the DC switch to position “1” on DC/DC converter module 7TN36 on the relay.

  8. Switch on the main switch (-S1) on the current unit for a.c supply of the current and voltage units, and also switch (-S13) for voltage unit.

  9. Check phase sequence, It must be positive sequence:

            If signaling lamps (-H13) is green then the phase sequence is positive.

            If signaling lamps (-H14) is red then the phase sequence is negative.

  1. Adjust the selector switch (-S5) at 0º angle.

  2. Adjust the switch (-S15, -1X) of current phase selection at L, on current unit and adjust the selector switch (-S6,Ux5-6) of voltage phase selection at (L1-N) on voltage unit.

  3. Select The fault type (IL1, IN), (UL1, UN) by pressing the pushbutton switches  (-S2), (-S5) on the current unit and (-S1), (-S4) on the voltage unit.

  4. Increase the voltage from the variable transformer knob (Curse and Fine) to be more than the calculation value of voltage.

  5. Press on the pushbutton (-S8) to “START” the injection, and adjust the current at 1A from the single phase variable transformer (-T1) for current I L1.

  6. Adjust the knob”N1” at angle 0º

  7. Decrease the voltage slowly until reach to the starting value and then the signaling lamps marked on these modules:
       - Indicator JE >> on module 7TJ5210

       - Indicator JE >> on module 7TJ5250

       - Indicator (L1) on module 7TW5000 “Starting for phase R”

       - Indicator ( I ) on module 7TS40 “Forward direction at 0º, 90º only”

  1. After the trip command come on, press on the pushbutton “STOP” “-S9” to stop the injection current to the relay.

  2. Read and record the noted pickup value in test sheet No. (1) and compare the measuring value with the calculation value.

  3. Repeat the above steps from steps (12) to steps (17) for the other angles 90º, 180º, 270º

  4. Repeat the above steps from step (10) to step (18) to measure the tripping values for other phases L2-N, L3-N, L1-12, L2-L3, L3-L1.

    3) U test         = JP (1+ZE/ZL) *r                                             4) U test = JP *2 r

CB Tripping Test

  1. Select a type of fault say LI-E on the test set.

  2. Adjust the test to make a trip at certain zone say (zone 1) and certain angle say (90º)

  3. Switch off the pushbutton (-S1) on the current unit

  4. Connect CB for the line (without disconnected switches)

  5. Put the test switch (TEST/SERVICE) on the relay to position “SERVICE”

  6. Switch on the main switch (-S1) on the current unit to a trip CB for the line.

Return Relay to Service

  1. Turn DC switch to position “O” on DC/DC converter module 7TN36 on the relay.

  2. Remove the test socket from the relay.

  3. Return back the under voltage module “UO” 7TS48

  4. Return back the timer plugs t2, t3, t4, to their normal positions.

  5. Return back the following equipment to service:

  6. Disturbance alarm and fault recorder.

  7. The associated auto-reclosure scheme.

  8. The associated breaker failure scheme.

  9. Return the test switch (TEST/SERVICE) on the relay to position “SERVICE”

  10. Return the DC switch to position “1”

Request Power LINE be Returned to Service

  1. Take crossed readings of all AC currents and voltages supplied to the relay and compare them to those for normal load conditions.

  2. Check that these reading agree with station meters: (MW, MVAR, Power factor, Amperes, Voltages)

  3. Place a copy of the test results in the field maintenance file.
