Introduction

    This procedure details the steps required to completely check the proper operation and calibration of the R-23 bus bar differential relay. It is recommended this procedure be carried out on a yearly bases. This work be carried out section of this document is divided into 3 sections

    The first preliminary steps to prepare for relay testing describes the activities required prior to testing. Some of these steps could be performed days before the actual test. The second section Preparation for testing describes the steps to set up the equipment for performing the actual tests. The third section is the actual test.

    The document is formatted the way to minimize the possibility of error causing personal injury or damage to equipment.

    The R23-V relay includes the summation transformer T1 and two tracing relays A and B of type RP 46, which are energized across the bus bar isolator auxiliary contacts.

Description of the Relay Type R23 M

    The relay R23 M is a measuring relay for two bus bar systems. The differential current of the system A flows through the transformers TA and TA and through the transformers TB and TB of system B .The rectifier bridges and sensitive measuring relays FA/FB of type UCR2 are connected to the secondary sides of these transformers.

    The relays DA and DB are connected into the stabilized circuit, the stabilizing currents of the other relays flow to the potentiometers KA and KB.

    These potentiometers serve to set the stabilizing stage .The relay DA, DB throws over their contacts da, db in case of a bus bar short- circuits and the flag type relay KA, KB of type RP 91 operates.

    The tripping pulse runs to the particular relays admit that the tripping pulse passes through to the circuit breakers.

    The relays FA, FB lie in the non-stabilized circuit. Their sensitivity is higher than that of relays DA, DB however it is above the magnitude of the equalizing currents with normal operation

    The protection is blocked by relay BA (blocking of system A) and BB (blocking of system B)

Description of the Relay Type R 23-S2

    The relay serves the combined bus bar coupler and is composed of two summation transformers T1, T2 and the tracing relays A, B, 1p of the type RP47. The tracing relays are energized by the bus bar isolator auxiliary contacts .The contacts a 11/12, b 11/12 interrupt the secondary circuit of the transformer T1, and the contacts 1b 11/12 the secondary circuit of the transformer T2. The secondary current of the transformer T1 is led to the differential circuits of the R23 protection across contacts a 31/32, d31/32 and via stabilization transformers T3, T4, contact 1b 31/32 has a similar function.

    The tripping pulse of the measuring relay R23-MI is 1ed to relay Ma via contacts a 21/22 and MB via contacts 1b 21/22, b21/22. Description of the Auxiliary Time Relay R23-P

    After the time set, the auxiliary time relay R23-P is to signal the operation of the FA, FB relay and take out of operation the bus bar protection on the respective bus bars.

    It includes two time relays TKA, TKB of the type TK11 and two relays PA, PB. The time relay TKA/TKB measures the time. If the measuring relays FA/FB remain in operating position for a period longer than adjusted.

    At the same time, the relay HA/HB in the measuring cubicle starts to operate.

Description of the Testing Device R23-Z

    The testing device R23-Z suitably amends the relay R23-M. It makes measurement of the differential current in circuits A and B easy during operation and is well-proven for periodical tests of the R23 protection.

Safety Precautions

  • Line isolation and work permit required.
  • Make sure C.T secondary circuits are shorted before isolating the current circuits for test current injection.
  • Check there is no current from CT’s by using clip on ammeter.
  • Isolate the breaker failure scheme associated with this protection.

Tools and Equipment

  • Secondary injection test set.
  • Clip-on ammeter.
  • Voltmeter.
  • Test leads.

Work to be Carried Out

  • Don’t open circuit the secondary circuit of CT since the high voltage produced may be dangerous to people and could damage equipment.
