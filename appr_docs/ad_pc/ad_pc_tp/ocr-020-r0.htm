Introduction

This procedure details the steps required to complete and check the proper operation and calibration of the over current relay type 7TG14 using secondary injection test set.

The document is formatted the way to minimize the possibility of error causing personal injury or damage to equipment.

The first draft initiated by eng. Hiat Farouq Mohamed, tested by eng. Bahaa Eldeen Ramdan & Abdel Fatah Mohamed at 28 Dec 2003 Transformer no.(3) 220/66kv Sabtia (1) substation, accepted by Protection General Director eng. Fikry Ibrahim Salam, and approved by eng. Samir Saleh Ismaiel Extra High Voltage Head Sector of Cairo zone

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Equipment isolation, (disconnect switches are opened and tagged).

  • Measure the current circuit of the relay by clip ammeter to be sure that is no current.

  • Make sure CT secondary are shorted before isolation relay current circuits for test current injection.

Tools and Equipment

  • Current injection equipped with timer

  • Multi meter, Clip meter

  • Test leads, Tool kit

Works to be Carried Out

 Preparation

  1. Review technical reference material (Manufacturer's manual)

  2. Obtain copy of relay setting from field maintenance file

  3. Obtain outage of the equipment (Line or transformer) protected by relay

  4. Review station drawing

  5. Check that the bus and equipment disconnect switches are opened and tagged

  6. Prepare the test sheet for the tested relay

  7. Short the current circuit of the relay at the C.T side on the relay terminals

  8. Isolate the current circuit of the relay for current injection, be sure the injection current will flow on the relay under test only

  9. Isolate the trip, B.F., fault recorder and any associated equipment

  10. Obtain the input terminals of the relay (current terminals) and also the output terminals (trip terminals) from the station from the station drawing

Testing the Over Current Element

  1. Inject current phase [terminal (S1-S2)]

  2. Slowly increase the current until the relay pick up and record it as a pick up value

  3. Slowly decrease the current until the relay drop off and record it as a drop off value

  4. To measure the time connect the out trip contact to stop terminals of the tester then inject current equal twice the pick up value and record it as an operating time

  5. Repeat the above steps (from 1 to 4) on the others phases

Testing the Earth Fault Element

  1. Inject any phase to neutral (for example R-N)

  2. Slowly increase the current until the relay pick up and record it is a pick up value (for earth fault)

  3. Measure the time by injection current equal twice the pick up value and record it as an operating time.

Note: Compare the recorded values with the setting values of the relay and give your comment

Return Relay and Associated Equipment to Service

  1. Disconnect the connection between the relay and the test equipment

  2. Connect the current circuit of the relay then remove the short circuit at the C.T. side

  3. Connect the trip B.F., fault recorder and associated equipment

Request Power Equipment be Return to Service

  1. Take crossed reading of all AC current 

  2. Compare this to the normal loading

  3. Place a copy of the test results in the field maintenance file
