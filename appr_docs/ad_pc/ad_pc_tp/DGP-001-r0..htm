Introduction

This document outlines the protection relays test for the diesel generator.

The first draft initiated by eng. Abdel Raheem Salem Mohamed Ez, tested by eng. Khaled El-Etreby & eng. Atef Farh, revised by quality control eng. Tawfik Elabasery at EDEPCo., and approved by eng. Monear Mahmoud El-Wakeel: Head Sector of Damietta Power Station.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area,

  • Steam turbine out of service and work permit required.

  • Make sure CT secondary circuit are shorted

  • Voltage transformer miniature circuit breakers (m.c.b) is opened.

Tools and Equipment

  • Siemens test set which consists of two units

  • Current unit type 7VP48, and Voltage unit type 7VP49.

  • Voltmeter, Ammeter, Test leads, screw drive.    

Work to be Carried Out

Differential Protection

  1. Stop the D.G. i.e. out of service, open its CB 6.3kv and in test position,

  2. Pass current through the operating coil to permit the relay operates,

  3. Inject the current to the relay for each phase of the two sides the current leads put as the follow:
        Phase                   Neutral side                       Output side
        PH-R                        3 - 2                                 1 - 2
        PH-S                         6.5                                   4 - 5
        PH-T                       10 - 11                              9 - 10

  4. Determine the pick up valve and drop out value and time of pick up value from timer point (32,38) L.O.R.

Reverse Power

  1. Stop the D.G. i.e. out of service, open its C.B. 6.3kv and in test position.

  2. Disconnect the voltage terminal (5,6,7,16) on the relay

  3. Connect the voltage leads UR US UT UN to terminal (5,6,7,16) respect on the relay.

  4. Connect the current leads (IR IN) to terminal (9,8) respect on the relay.

  5. Inject 3-Æ  voltage UL = 100v

  6. Inject small current to adjust the angle at 180º then increase it until lamp light (Ipu =    mA) then determine the time and angle zone, at 180º again decrease the current until lamp distinguish (Id.o) and measure the time from point (32,38) L.O.R.

  7. Determine the angle zone for different values of current.

  8. Repeat the previous steps for     PH-S (IS, IN) to terminal (15,14)
                                                     PH-T (IT, IN) to terminal (13,12)

  9. Reconnect the voltage terminal (5,6,7,16) on the relay.

Loss of Field

  1. Stop the D.G. i.e. out of service, open its C.B. 6.3kv and in test position.

  2. Disconnect the voltage terminal (5,6) on the relay

  3. Connect the voltage leads (tester) (UR US) to terminal (5,6) on the relay

  4. Connect the current leads (Ig In) to (1,2) on the relay

  5. Inject voltage UL = 100v and inject small current to adjust the angle at 270º

  6. Inject the current value =        A

  7. Decrease the voltage until the flag appear, record Up.u (diameter + offset)

  8. Decrease the voltage until the flag disappear then increase the voltage until the flag appear again, record Up.u (offset) and measure the time from timer point (32,38) on L.O.R.

  9. Repeat the previous steps for PH-S
                                                         (Us, Ur) to terminal (5,6)
                                                          (IS, IN)  to terminal (3,4)

  10. Reconnect the voltage terminal (5,6) on the relay.

 

Stator Earth Fault

  1. Disconnect the m.c.b (fuse) F56 in cabinet 6.3kv CB.

  2. Disconnect the link of terminal "56" for protective relay F38.

  3. Connect the voltage leads (tester) to terminals (53,56) on the relay F38 (E,F)

  4. Inject the voltage and increase it until the flag on CB panel appear, then record Upu and measure the time from free contact on K21.

  5. Decrease the voltage until the flag disappear, then record Udo.

 

 

 

Over Current Relay 75J500 Siemens

 

Over Current Test

  1. Inject current for phase R

  2. Connect the current leads on test device L1, N to the points (1A2,1A1) on the relay.

  3. Increase the current to pick up value (I>lamp light), record this pu value

  4. Decrease the current to reset value, record this d.o value

  5. Measure the time from free contact (2B1, 2B4)

  6. Repeat the previous steps for ph. S & ph. T, but the current leads connect the points (2A1,2A2) ph.S & (3A1,3A2) ph. T

Short Circuit Test

  • Repeat the previous steps of over current test but record the S.C value (I>>lamp light)

Tip Circuit Test

  1. Adjust the S.C. value for any phase

  2. Close the CB on the test position (Rack out position)

  3. Inject the current, the C.B is tripped.
