Introduction

The relay type PH 54/160T under voltage relay 54-160V, provided to circuits as an under voltage protection relay in russian`s protection system the over-current protection with under voltage blocking to ensure that the fault is over-current where any over-current attended with under voltage the PH 54/160T relay provided to this system.

The first draft initialed & tested by eng. Tarik Mohamed Ahmed at Maghagha SS, accepted by general director eng. Aemad Makalaa, and approved by eng. Yahya Mohamed Mahmoud head sector.

Safety Precautions

  • Work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff must carry the suitable ID safety category

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area isrequired.

  • Wear appropriate personal protective equipment must be used, safety shoes, helmet breathing mask, safety glasses

  • Line isolation is required

  • Make sure VT secondary circuits are opened before testing the relay

  • Make sure relay contacts are isolated from circuits

Tools and Equipment

  • Secondary injection tester

  • Voltmeter, test leads

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Make sure vt secondary circuits are opened and isolated from the relay

  2. Isolate relay coil from the voltage circuits

  3. Look at field maintenance file and compare the position of the setting on the relay with those shown on the setting sheet

  4. Make sure relay contact was untied before testing

 

Relay Test

  1. Switch of the dc mcd

  2. Untie terminals to relay contacts from terminal blocks and connect them to stop contact sockets of the tester

  3. Untie terminals to relay coil from terminal blocks and connect them to voltage sockets of the tester

  4. Clean the relay contact and inspect tying

  5. Switch on the tester and show if the stop contact led of the tester light indicate that the relay contact is closed

  6. Increse voltage to open the relay contact and record Vr reset value, so decrease voltage to close contact and record Vop operation value

  7. Record results in this table.
