Introduction

The REL 511 is a universal line protection, control monitoring terminal that can be applied to the distribution and sub-transmission overhead lin and cables in solidly earthed, as well as in high impedance earthed and isolated networks. The basic protection function in REL 511 is a distance protection with individual measuring elements for the different types of faults that belong to the  various impedance zones. The terminal includes five impedance measuring zones with programmable directionality.

The independent measuring elements provide general fault criterion 

This procedure details the steps required to completely check the proper operation and calibration of the relay. It is recommended this procedure be carried out on an annually bases. The document is formatted the way to minimize the possibility of error causing personal injury or damage to equipment.

The first draft initiated by eng. Samah Mokhtar Mostafa, tested at 15 Oct 2002 by eng. Yousef Melad Shahat at El Eslah Portable station, accepted by General director eng. Abdel Menem Rabie Motalaqeh, and approved by eng. Fawzy Mourse Mohamed: Head Sector of Aswan Network.

 Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued

  • The qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable ID safety category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required,

  • Line isolation and work permit required

  • Make sure that CT secondary circuits are shorted before isolating the current circuits for test distance protection

Tools and Equipment

  • XS92a test set

  • Voltmeter, Ammeter, and Test leads 

Work to be Carried Out

Steps to Prepare for Relay Testing

  1. Review technical reference material and manufacturer's manual

  2. Obtain copy of relay setting from field maintenance file and compare it with the setting on the relay

  3. Obtain outage for line protected by this relay

  4. Check that the circuit breaker is opened and tagged

  5. Inspect the CT secondary circuits, the VT miniature circuit breakers ( mcbS) and all cabling supplying the relaying

  6. Use the station drawings to isolate the following:
         * Breaker trip coils
         * Breaker failure scheme associated with the relay and disturbance alarms

Testing the relay

  1. Carefully follow this test procedure

  2. Verify the test results match the relay setting

  3. Trip the circuit breaker associated with the relay

Preparation

  1. This procedure should be used for performing off load line

  2. Look the EEC panel name plate and check this EEC system designation name plate agrees with the line that has been taken out of service for maintenance 

  3. Make sure CT secondary circuits are shorted at the incoming terminal blocks before isolate the relay current circuits for test

Reading the setting from the relay using the PC

  1. By using the six keys on the MMI (Man Machine Interface) of the relay read the setting

Distance protection test

  1. Be sure that the DC power of the relay is correct by applying a DC voltmeter across points X16 (17) and X16 (18) on the rear of the relay

  2. Connect the current test leads on the current terminals of the relay X11(1,2,3,8) and the voltage test leads on the voltage terminals of the relay X12 (1,6,8,10)

  3. Connect AC power to the test set

  4. Inject current, voltage and phase angle by selecting the value of impedance (X)

  5. To test zone (1) phase R to Neutral:
    a) Take the value of (X) greater than the maximum value of working for zone 2 and decrease it slowly until the  relay operates and the Red LED (right) on the MMI be steady light
    b) Display the last disturbance on the LCD of the MMI and check the displayed disturbance with the actual fault disturbance

  6. Repeat step 5 to test zone (1) phase S to Neutral

  7. Repeat step 5 to test zone (1) phase T to Neutral

  8. Repeat step 5 to test zone (1) phase R to S

  9. Repeat step 5 to test zone (1) phase S to T

  10. Repeat step 5 to test zone (1) phase T to R

  11. Repeat step 5 to test zone (1) phase R to S To T

  12. To test zone (2) phase R to Neutral :
    a) Take the value of (X) greater than the maximum value of working for zone 2 and decrease it slowly until the relay operates and the Red LED (right) on the MMI be steady light
    b) Display the last disturbance on the LCD of the MMI and check the displayed disturbance with the actual fault disturbance   

  13. Repeat step 5 to test zone (2) phase S to Neutral

  14. Repeat step 5 to test zone (2) phase T to Neutral

  15. Repeat step 5 to test zone (2) phase R to S

  16. Repeat step 5 to test zone (2) phase S to T

  17. Repeat step 5 to test zone (2) phase T to R

  18. Repeat step 5 to test zone (2) phase R to S to T

  19. To test zone (3) phase R to Neutral:
    a) Take the value of (X) greater than the maximum value of working for zone 2 and decrease it slowly until the relay operates and the Red LED (right) on the MMI be steady light
    b) Display the last disturbance on the LCD of the MMI and check the displayed disturbance with the actual fault disturbance

  20. Repeat step 5 to zone (3) phase S to Neutral

  21. Repeat step 5 to zone (3) phase T to Neutral

  22. Repeat step 5 to zone (3) phase R to S

  23. Repeat step 5 to zone (3) phase S to T

  24. Repeat step 5 to zone (3) phase T to R

  25. Repeat step 5 to zone (3) phase R to S to T

Return relay and associated equipment to service

  1. Return relay to service by removing the test leads from the relay terminals and the test set

  2. Make sure CT and VT secondary circuits are in normal condition

  3. Remove the disturbance alarm LED's on the relay which is caused by the test

  4. Request feeder to be returned in service

  5. Take phasor readings of all AC currents and voltages and compare them to those for normal load condition

  6. Check that these reading agree with station meters (MW, Mvar, Amperes and power factor)

  7. Place a copy of the test result in the field maintenance file
