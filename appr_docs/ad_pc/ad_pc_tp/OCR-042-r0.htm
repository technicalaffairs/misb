Introduction

The type ISM21 relay is an electromechanical over current relay with a variable time - lag independent of the current. It is connected to the current transformers also it can be used to detect short circuit in transformers. This procedure details the steps required to completely check the proper operation and calibration of the ISM21 relay. It is recommended this procedure be carried out on yearly bases

The first draft initiated and tested by eng Nadia Mohamed Abd El Ghany , accepted by general director eng. Fakry Ibraheem Slaam

Main Features

  • Separate setting for pick-up current and time-lag.

  • High switching capacity change over contact.

  • Independed setting of instantaneous tripping at max. current.

  • One unit for each phase

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category

  • Safety fence with caution mark surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, gloves ... etc

  • Line isolation required

  • Make sure that the line is isolated by using clamp meter you must measure zero current in current circuit and by using voltmeter to measure zero voltage in voltage circuit.

Tools and Equipment

  • Secondary injection test set type Sverker

  • Clamp meter

  • Voltmeter, Test leads

Work to be Carried Out

 

Preparation

  1. Obtain a copy of relay setting from field maintenance file and compare it with the setting on the relay

  2. Review technical reference material and manufacture's manual

  3. Check that the circuit breaker is opened and tagged

  4. Inspect CT secondary circuit, TV miniature circuit breaker

There are two types of Testing the Relay

  1. The first test by isolating the relay from its casing and test it

  2. The second test by injection of the current on the terminals of relay casing which the relay in it's casing

Note   If you used the first test make sure that CT secondary circuit are shorter at the in coming terminal blocks before isolating the relay from it's casing

Current Test

  1. Look at field maintenance file and compare the setting on the relay with those shown on the setting sheet

  2. Label the wires before removing it from terminal block of the relay

  3. Remove the input current terminals number 1,2 from the relay

  4. Connect the test leads between the current terminals number 1 and 2 on the relay to the current terminals on the tester type (Severker) as shown in the following figure(1)

  5. Switch on the tester press the push bottom (ON)(▼), the red led (ON) light and slowly in crease the injected current to the relay by the current wheel of the tester until the wheel of the current coil of the relay starts to move and the knob indicator of starting current appear

  6. The relay operateswith a pick-up current at the setting value with ± 5% increasing or decreasing

  7. Record the value of the pick-up current which appears on the display of the tester in test sheet

  8. Calculate Reset factor: Kres =  I res  /  Iop should be ≥ 90%  

  9. Slowly decrease the wheel of current injection on tester until the current coil of the relay resets

  10. Record the value of the reset current appear on the display of the tester in tester in test sheet

  11. Slowly decrease the wheel of current injection to zero current then press the push bottom (off)(▼) which the red led (off) light

Time Test

  1. Inject the current from the tester higher than the setting value by + 10% over the setting so the relay picks up firmly

  2. Keep the injected current and switch off the test set

  3. The number of terminals block of output contact on the relay to trip the circuit breaker are (3),(6) which terminal block (3) take appositive of D.C and the other terminal block (6) go to trip C.B

  4. Measure by a voltmeter the terminal block (3) to ensure that the positive D.C on the relay on this terminal and remove it from its terminal block

  5. Connect the time delayed contact number (3),(6) on the relay to two terminals of the timer on the relay (connect to stop contact unit on the tester)

 The stop contact on the tester is a free contact i.e.

  1. Be sure that the red led of normally open contact of initiation lighted by pressing the push bottom (▲) and be sure that the red led of normally open contact (free contact) lighted by pressing the push bottom (►) in stop unit

  2. Switch on the tester to inject the current by pressing the push bottom (on + time) to the relay, we observe that the relay picks up and the timer operates and stops after a delayed time setting

  3. The value measured of time delay appears on the display of tester and record it in test sheet as shown as in figure 2

  4. Press the push bottom (off + time) on the tester

  5. Remove the terminals which connected to the timer of the relay and connect again the positive terminal to terminal block (3)

Trip Test

  1. Request the operator to switch on the main circuit breaker

  2. Switch on the tester and inject the current so the relay picks up and after a certain time delay, the out put contact on the relay closes makes the circuit breaker trip

  3. Be sure that another out put contact on the relay (9),(10) terminal blocks is closed makes a signal alarm

  4. Slowly decreases the injected current and press the push bottom (off) and switch off the tester

  5. Compare the measured values of current and time recorded in test sheet by the setting values recorded also in test sheet to ensure that the response of the relay is correct after we obtain the reset factor calculated
