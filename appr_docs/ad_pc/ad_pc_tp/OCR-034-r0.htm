Introduction

    The METI 31+ MCGG 63 relay is designed specially for directional control of the time delayed element of the over Current relay type MCGG, and Manufactured by GEC. This procedure details the steps required to completely check the proper operation and calibration of the METI 31+MCGG 63 relay. It is recommended this procedure be carried out on a yearly bases. This work be carried out section of this document is divided into 3 sections.

    The first preliminary steps to prepare for relay testing describes the activities required prior to testing. Some of these steps could be performed days before the actual test. The second section Preparation for testing describes the steps to set up the equipment for performing the actual tests. The third section is the actual test. 

    The document is formatted the way to minimize the possibility of error causing personal injury or damage to equipment.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the maintenance staff should carry the suitable ID safety category,

  • Maintenance staff should be trained well, qualified or directly supervised by some one who is.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Personal safety and healthy equipment must be used as safety shoes, safety helmet ... etc

  • Line isolation is required.

  • Make sure CT Secondary circuits are shorted before isolating the relay current circuits for current injection.

Tools and Equipment

  • Siemens test set type 7VP 48 & 7VP 49

  • Voltmeter, Ammeter, Test leads

 

 

Work to be Carried out

Testing the Relay and Protection Outputs

  1. Carefully follow these test procedure steps

  2. Verify the test result match the relay setting. If not consult technical services.

  3. Test trip the circuit breaker associated with the relay.

Testing Procedure(s)

Preparations

  1. Make sure CT secondary circuits are shorted at the incoming blocks before isolating the relay current circuits for test.

  2. Look at field maintenance file and compare the position of the setting switches on relay with those shown on the setting sheet.

  3. Set the earthing link on the relay to line side position.

Over-Current Test

  1. Switch off the DC power of the relay.

  2. Take out the Relay from its casing.

  3. Connect 220V d.c to the Relay terminal No.'s (13, 14) where 13 is +ve and 14 is -ve.

  4. Connect test leads between the current terminals No 21 and 22 on the relay. to the current terminals on the test set for phase "R".

  5. Slowly increase the injection current until reach to the operating value and then the red "LED" labled "t" light. The relay must operate with a current between +10% of the setting value.

  6. Record this value (Ioper) on the test sheet (1- a).

  7. Increase the injected current with a value 25 % larger than "Ioper" operating value.

  8. Slowly decrease the current until the relay resets.

  9. Record this value "Ireset" on the test sheet (1-a)

  10. Switch off the test set.

  11. Connect leads between time delayed trip output contact terminal No.'s (29, 30) on the Relay to the timer (Normally open) on the tester .

  12. Inject current with a value 25 % larger than the operating value "Ioper".

  13. Ensure that the Relay operates.

  14. Switch off the tester set Switch on and make Reset for the timer on the tester.

  15. Switch on the tester unit. Take the time value and must agrees with the setting value on the Relay.

  16. Repeat step No "15" two or three times and take average value.

  17. Record this value on the test sheet "1.a"

  18. Repeat the above test from step No 4 to 17 but inject current between terminals as follow. For phase S terminals No. (23, 24) for phase T terminals No. (25, 26) on the relay.

Directional Test

  1. This relay set has a selection of relay characteristic angles (-60°, -45°,0°,+30°,+45°)

  2. Ensure that the polarities of all connection are correct according to station drawing.

  3. Switch off the DC power of the relay and take out the directional element relay and DC power supply (converter) to the relay from its casing.

  4. Make connection as shown in figure no "3".

  5. Connect 220 v d.c supply to terminal no.s (27, 28) on the d.c power supply unit where terminal no-27 is +ve and 28 is -ve.

  6. Connect the out put of the d.c power supply terminal no.(13 ,14 ) where (d.c converter) connected to 13 (directional element relay). 14 (d.c converter) connected to 14 (directional element relay).

  7. Connect 220 v d.c to terminal no. (13, 14 ) on the over current element relay where 13 is + ve , 14 is -ve.

  8. Set the angle on the relay to " + 30 ° "

  9. Connect test leads between the voltage terminals no. (17, 18) on the directional element relay for phase R and tester unit phase R also.

  10. Connect leads between the current terminal (no. 23 on the directional element relay and no. 22 on the over current element relay) and tester unit phase R also.

    Note The direction of current must enter terminal no. 23 on the directional element relay

  11. Connect a DC voltmeter (30 v range or greater) to the relay monitor sockets mounted on the front plate with polarity as shown on the relay.

  12. Switch on the test set.

  13. Inject 100 a-c volt from the test set.

  14. Use phase rotation indicator to be sure phase rotation R, S and T on the relay.

  15. Inject current into the relay until it operates.

  16. Adjust the phase shift angle between the injected current and voltage to a value equal 30 degrees.

  17. Observe the output element (red "LED" labeled t light) on the over current element relay at this moment, it must be picking up.

  18. Increase the angle on the test set until the relay output element of over current resets.
    Note: Operation of output contacts on the directional element relay can be monitored by applying a voltmeter (d.c voltmeter) across the test points on the relay front plate.

  • A negative reading indicates output contacts open.

  • A positive reading indicates output contacts closed.
     That is mean, negative reading is operating area, positive reading is non operating area.

  1. Record this angle value on the test sheet "1-b" at this moment, the angle should be approximately 120 °.

  2. Continuous to increase the angle until the relay picks up again record this angle value on the test sheet "1-b". At this moment , the angle should be approximately 300°.

  3. Repeat the above test from step "9" to step "20" for phase S, and T with taking into consideration the connection must be according to drawing shown in figure no. 1 Repeat the above test from step "9" to step "20" but change the angle on the relay to (-60°, -45°, 0°, +45°).

  4. The angles obtained during the above tests should be within +6° of the boundary angles quoted in the table 1.

    Front Panel SwitchPosition Relay Characteristic

    Phase Shifter Indication
    - 60° I lags V by 60° 30° lag through 0° to 150° lead
    - 45° I lags V by 45° 45° lag through 0° to 135° lead
    0° in phase 90° lag through 0° to 90° lead
    + 30° I leads V by 30° 120° lag through 0° to 60° lead
    +45° I leads V by 45° 135° lag through 0° to 45° lead

  5. Finally put the setting angle for the directional element relay on the original position given in the maintenance file.

  6. Return the over current element relay, directional element relay and d.c power supply unit (DC converter) to their cases.

  7. Switch on the DC power of the relay.

Minimum Operating Voltage

  1. Repeat the above test from step no. 8 to step no. 17 but at step no.13 must start the applied voltage from zero and then slowly increase its value until the relay picks up at this moment record this value (voltage) in the test sheet "1-b" for phase R.

  2. Repeat step no. 1 for the other phases (S & T).

Trip Test

  1. Request the operator to switch on the main circuit breaker.

  2. Operate the output auxiliary contact by injection to trip the circuit breaker.

  3. Check that the circuit breaker tripped.
