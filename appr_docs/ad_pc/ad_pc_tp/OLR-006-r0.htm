Introduction

 This document outlines the testing procedures P1, to be performed every six months as indicated by M6 in the above title block.

This procedure, details the steps required to completely check the proper operation and calibration of the relay ( STKK ). 

The first draft initiated & tested by eng. Mahmoud El-Attar, accepted by eng. Saad El-Attar: protection general manager, revised and certified by eng. Mohamed Omran: development & quality surveillance general manager and MPIS Canal zone coordinator, and approved by eng. Al-Saied Abd El-Azez Alnagy: head sector of protection, measuring & testes.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, qualified maintenance staff carry the suitable safety ID category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required,

  • Personal protective equipment must be used as safety shoes, and safety Helmet.

  • This procedure should be performed for off load lines so line isolation and work permit are required.

  • Isolate the relay DC supply before drawing out any relay card.

  • Isolate the relay associated breaker trip Coils, breaker failure system, disturbance alarm and fault recorder systems

Tools and Equipment

  • Secondary Injection test set ( Sverker ).

  • Test leads,

  • Avometer

  • AC supply

Work to be carried out

 

Relays Preparation

  1. Review technical reference material: Manufacturer's manual.

  2. Obtain copy of relay settings from field maintenance file and compare it with the relay settings .

  3. Obtain outage for line protected by this relay .( work permit must be issued )

  4. Check that the bus and line disconnect switches are opened and tagged .

  5. Make sure CTs Secondary circuits are shorted before isolating the relay current circuits for current injection.

  6. Isolate the relay DC supply .

  7. Check that all cables supplying the relay and all wire connections are in good conditions.

Testing the Relay and Protection Outputs

Relay with Make contact or Break contact

Relay with Make Contact

  1. Under operating conditions, in figure 1, the screw ( O ) is screwed into the upper hole.

  2. Upon removing it, the tripping circuit is interrupted. See corresponding diagram.

  3. When screwed into the lower hole, the current transformer is short circuited.

  4. By opening the links ( P ) and connecting the test set terminals to the middle screws, the relay can be tested in site.

Relay with Break contact

This relay possess two screws which in normal operation conditions are screwed into the blind holes ( fig. 4 ).

  1. Screw one from the two screws into the upper hole between the upper contacts to short out the tripping contact which is normally closed.

  2. The second screw is screwed into the lower hole to short out the current transformer ( fig. 5 ) by opening the links ( P ) and connecting the test set terminals to the middle screws, the relay can be tested in site .

Tester Preparation

  1. Connect the power supply to the tester unit (Sverker ).

  2. Connect the connecting terminals between the relay and Sverker.

Parameters Preparation

  1. Review the test certificate for required settings.

  2. Pickup value = Iop = 0.4  IN

Relay Testing  The test is accomplished in two stages:

  1. Finding relay operating current value.

  2. Finding relay operating time.

Finding relay operating current

  1. Connect the relay to tester phase ( S ).

  2. Slowly increase the injected current until the relay operate ( indicated by the flag ).

  3. Record the operating current ( Iop ) in test sheet.

  4. Check that the relay trip contact is closed. ( for Relay with Make contact ).

  5. Check that the relay Alarm contact and breaker failure contact.

Finding relay operating time

  1. The operating time should be measured using the trip contact ( free contact ).

  2. Inject testing current until the relay operate, then switch off the test set ( Sverker ).

  3. Connect the timed contact of the relay to the timer stop terminals of the secondary injection test set ( Sverker ).

  4. Switch on the tester with timer ( ON ) and measure the operating time .

  5. Record the operating time in test sheet .

NOTE : How to find " free contact" on relay ( STKK ):

  1. Select any free contact and measure it`s DC voltage ( must have not DC voltage ).

  2. check that it is normal open contact using  avometer buzzer.

  3. check that it is close with trip , using tester ( Sverker ) and avometer buzzer .

Return Protection Scheme and Associated Equipment to service

  1. Return relay ( STKK ) to service by removing the test leads from the relay terminals.

  2. Make sure CT Secondaries are not short circuited.

  3. Place the associated breaker failure ( BF ) protection scheme back in service.

  4. Restore the previously blocked disturbance alarms and fault recorders associated with this protection.

  5. Turn ON the power supply DC to relay ( STKK ).

  6. Take crossed readings of all AC currents and voltages supplied to the relay ( STKK ) and compare them to those for normal load conditions.

  7. Check that these readings agree with station meters MW, MVAR, Power Factor, Amperes, and Voltages.

  8. Place a copy of the test results in the field maintenance file.
