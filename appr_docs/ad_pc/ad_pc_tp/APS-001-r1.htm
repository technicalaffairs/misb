Introduction

This document outlines the testing procedure P1, which must be performed every six months as indicated by M6 in the title block.

This patent concerns the test of protection relays for the indoor 6.3KV circuit breakers, manufactured by ABB ARAB. The CB's are of type either HB 07. 12.40V or HC 07. 25.40V, rating currents 1250 A, 2500A respectively, and short circuit capacity 43.5KA, 3 sec.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified test staff should carry the suitable safely category

  • Safety fence with caution marks and no smoking signs surrounding the work area must be used.

  • Wearing safety helmet, shoes, cloths, and masks are necessary

  • Be aware of parts under voltage

  • Disconnect the cell of work

  • Draw the CB of the concerned cell in the 6.3kv hall

  • Put the CB at test position

  • Check the indicating lamps of the cell

  • Check the existence of  DC of the cell

 

Tools and Equipment

  • A bag of screws and nails.

  • Two testers units  of type Z11.

  • Some wires.

  • A timer TM –2 ( Program ).

  • A voltmeter and  an ammeter.

 

The Protection Devices for Motors

  • Under voltage device.

  • Multi – function motor protection device  ( S.C. – O.C. current – negative phase sequence earth fault ).

 

The Protection Devices for Transformer

  • Under voltage.

  • Earth fault.

  • Short circuit + Over current.

  • Differential.

  • Zero sequence relays.

 

Work to be Carried Out

 

Under Voltage Device ( UKT 911 )  (Figure 1)

 

Type: UKT 911                                                              Vp = 2KVEFF

Function: F27                                                                   fn = 50/60 HZ

Manufacturer: ABB (Switzerland)                                    (Un)  Rated voltage = 100V

Setting: 0.7 Un ( Un =100V ),  T motor = 5 sec               Auxiliary voltage: 220V dc 

  1. Measure the 220 V DC control voltage .

  2. Disconnect the D.C.. see  figure (1)  

  3. Loosen ( A5, A8 ) of the device and put Z11 leads .

  4. Put timer leads on ( A1, A2 ) .

  5. Reconnect the D.C.

  6. Inject a voltage through Z11 till the reset value to remove the fault sign of the device ( yellow one )

  7. Decrease the voltage again to record the pick up ( operating ) value .

  8. The timer starts counting at this moment and closes after the rated time. The  trip sign (red one) shows up.

  9. Record the results and compare them with the previous ones at table 1. 

 

 

 

Motor Protection (MCX912)  (Figure 2)

Multi – Function Motor Protection

Type: MCX 912                                                        Vp = 2KVEFF

Auxiliary voltage: 220V dc                                       fn = 50/60 HZ

Manufacturer: ABB (Switzerland)                               Rated current = 5A 

                  

Included Function :                         Over current protection

                                                      Short circuit  protection

                                                      Earth fault protection                                        

                                                      Negative Phase Sequence

Over Current

  1. Measure the 220 Vdc. control voltage.

  2. Disconnect D.C. switch. see  figure (2) 

  3. Put the leads of Z11 on (A2, A3) of the device.

  4. Disconnect the trip leads either on (A8,A9) or (A4,A5), and connect the timer leads on them.

  5. Connect the D.C. again.

  6. Inject the current by Z11.

  7. Observe the device screen till it flashes indicating a certain mode.

  8. At a certain time the timer stops and the screen displays the operating mode (03) .

  9. Connect (A10, A11) instead of (A2, A3).

  10. Record the results for phase R and phase T at table 2. 

 

Short Circuit

  1. Do the same procedures of testing over current element.

  2. Mode (01) will appear on the device screen.

  3. Record the results for phase R and phase T at table 2. 

 

Earth Fault       

The test is only performed at  (A6, A7) on the device. These two terminals are the only connected terminals to a ring core.  

The same procedures mentioned before. Mode (07) will appear after its rated time.

Record the results for phase R and phase T at table 2.  

 

Negative Phase Sequence

The test is only performed at  (A6, A7) on the device. These two terminals are the only connected terminals to a ring core.

  1. Do the same procedures of testing over current element.

  2. Mode (05) will appear on the device screen.

Record the results for phase R and phase T at table 2.  

 

Earth Fault  Protection of Transformers (IKT 941)  (Figure 7)

Earth Fault  Protection

Type: IKT941                                Auxiliary voltage : 220V

Function: F51N                               Setting  Earth Fault : 0.2In, T=0.1 sec

(In) rated current : 1A                      Manufacturer : ABB ( Switzerland )     

Insulation test voltage for 1 min: Vp:2KVEFF

  1. Measure the 220 Vdc. control voltage.

  2. Disconnect  the D.C. switch .(Figure 7)

  3. Put the leads of Z11 on (A2, A3) of the device. (F51N  Figure 7)

  4. Disconnect the trip leads either on (A8, A9) or (A4, A5), and connect the timer leads on them.

  5. Connect the D.C. again.

  6. Inject the current by Z11.

  7. Increase the injected current till the yellow lamp of the device switches on (signal).

  8. At a certain time the timer stops counting, and the red lamp switches on (trip).

  9. Record the results and compare them with the previous ones at table 4.  

 

Short Circuit & Over Current Protection of Transformers  (IKT 943)   (Figure 7)

Manufacturer : ABB ( Switzerland )              Vp : 2KVEFF            

Type: IKT943                                               fn : 50/60 HZ

 Function : F50/51                            Auxiliary voltage : 220V d.c

(In) rated current : 5A                        Short circuit setting : 15 In

Over current setting : Ir = In ,T= 6 sec

Over Current

  1. Measure the 220 Vdc; control voltage.

  2. Disconnect the D.C. switch. (Figure 7)

  3. Put the leads of Z11 on (A2, A3) of the device.  (F50/51  Figure 7)

  4. Disconnect the trip leads of (A4, A5), and connect the timer leads on them.

  5. Connect the D.C. again.

  6. Inject the current by Z11.

  7. Increase the injected current till the yellow lamp of the device switches on (signal).

  8. At a certain time the timer stops counting, and the red lamp switches on (trip).

  9. Record the results and compare them with the previous ones & settings.

  10. Repeat the same sequence for leads (A6, A7) then (A10, A11) instead of (A2, A3).

 

 

 

Short Circuit

  1. Measure the 220 Vdc. control voltage.

  2. Disconnect the D.C. switch .(Figure 7)

  3. Put the leads of Z11 on (A2, A3) of the device. (F50/51  Figure 7)

  4. Disconnect the trip leads either of (A8, A9), and connect the timer leads on them.

  5. Connect the D.C. again.

  6. Inject the current by Z11.

  7. Increase the injected current till the red lamp of the device switches on (trip). At this moment the timer closes instantaneously.

  8. Record the results and compare them with the previous ones & settings.

  9. Repeat the same sequence for leads (A6, A7) then (A10, A11) instead of (A2, A3).

 

Zero Sequence Relay (E.F.R)    (UKT 911)     (Figure 3)

Type: UKT 911                                              Vp : 2KVEFF                                            

Function: F27G                                                fn : 50/60 HZ                                           

Manufacturer: ABB (Switzerland)             (Un) Rated voltage = 100V            T =1. 5 sec    Setting: 0.2 Un ( Un =100V )                      Auxiliary voltage:220V dc

It is available only in the measuring cubicles.

  1. Measure the 220 Vdc. control voltage.

  2. Disconnect D.C. switch. (Figure 3)

  3. Loosen (A5, A8) of the device. (F27G  Figure 3)

  4. Put the leads of Z11 on (A5, A8).

  5. Connect the timer leads on (A1, A2) of the device.

  6. Connect the D.C. again.

  7. Inject the voltage by Z11.

  8. Increase the injected voltage till the yellow lamp of the device switches off (reset value).

  9. Decrease the injected voltage till the yellow lamp of the device reappears (set value).

  10. After a certain time the timer stops counting, and the red lamp switches on (trip).

  11. Record the results. at table 8

Differential Protection (DTN 920)  (Figure 4)

Manufacturer : ABB (Switzerland)

Type : DTN 920                         Function:F87

(In) rated current : 5A                  fn : 50/60 HZ  

Setting: 0.2 Un                             Insulation test voltage for 1 min: Vp:2KVEFF

For Incoming 1BA, 2BA and OBL

  1. Measure the 220 Vdc. control voltage. see figure 4

  2. Inject current by Z11 in(268,265)&(268,266) & (268, 267) on the rosette for the incoming cubicles [high tension side ].

  3. Inject current by Z11 in  (264,261) & (264,262) & (264, 263) on the rosette for the transformers    [low tension side ].

  4. Increase the injected current till the red lamp of the device switches on. The relay trips instantaneously in each case.

  5. Record the results for both sides at table 6. 

 

For Transformer Outgoing

  1. Measure the 220 Vdc. control voltage. see  figure 6   page no. 18.

  2. Inject current by Z11 in  (250,231) & (250,235) & (250, 239) on the rosette for the transformers [high tension side ].

  3. Inject current by Z11 in(261,262)& (261,263) & (261, 264) on the rosette for the incoming cubicles [high tension side].

  4. Inject current by Z11 in  (250,231) & (250,235) & (250, 239) on the rosette for the transformers [high tension side ].

  5. Record the results for both sides .

 

Finally –maintenance procedures

  1. Disconnect all the testing leads and estrange the testing tools.

  2. Put the  C.B. in its original state to be on service any time.

  3. Press the “RESET” button for the devices and check the relay setting .

  4. Connect the D.C. after the limit switch connection.

  5. Make the place clean of work signs.
