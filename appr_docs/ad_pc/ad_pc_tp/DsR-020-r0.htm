Introduction

L Z92 is high-speed distance relay (static relay) but switched distance relay with three time stepped zones. The relay is used as backup to a backup function of the distance relay L Z96. The relay provides three pole tripping output for all faults, the first zone can either be disable or time delayed so that three pole tripping is not interfering with L Z96 single pole tripping (Device No. 21B / 21GB).

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category

    •

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

    •

  • Line isolation is required.

  • Make sure CT secondary circuits are shorted and VT secondary circuits are open.

Tools and Equipment

  • Secondary injection unit [XS92a with YX91].

  • Voltammeter.

  • Clip on ammeter.

  • Test loads.

Work to be Carried Out

Preliminary steps to prepare for relay testing

  1. Review technical reference material: Manufacturer's manual.

  2. Obtain copy of relay setting from field maintenance file and compare it wit the setting on the relay

  3. Obtain outage for line protected by this relay.

  4. Check that the bus and line disconnect switches are opened and tagged.

  5. Inspect the CT secondary circuits, the VT miniature circuit breakers (MCB's) and all cabling supplying the relay.

  6. Use the station drawing to isolate the following:
     - Breaker trip coils.
    - Breaker failure scheme associated with this relay.
    - Disturbance alarms and fault recorder associated with this relay.

Testing the Relay and Protection output

  1. Carefully follow these test procedure steps .

  2. Verify the test results match the relays if not consult technical services.

  3. Test trip the breaker associated with there.

Prior to starting testing procedures ensure the following steps

  1. This procedure is used to perform off load relay testing.

  2. Check that the panel nameplate agrees with the EEC designation of the line that has been taken out of service for maintenance.

  3. Check there is no AC current from CT by using clip-on  ammeter.

  4. Check there is no AC voltage from VT by using voltmeter.

  5. Check all external connections to the relay are in good condition and check that the terminal block wires are tight.

Preparing the tester unit (XS92a) as a figure (2-1)'(2-2) as the following:

  1. It should be earthling the tester unit 9XS92a).

  2. D/C of the relay is off [NF93 is off] DC-DC converter.

  3. Put the test connector YX91 in the test unit in YX91 for the relay.

  4. Put the jumper between the two units [3 Jumpers]

  5. Put the jumper between + I & + I in the lower unit.

  6. Put the unit (YB92) in the hand terminal in the control unit (XC92) in the upper unit.

  7. Put the jumper between the red point and No.(11)& yellow point and No (12).

  8. Put the key in the connector unit (YX91) on (ON position).

  9. Connect the DC-DC converter (NF93) on the relay.

  10. Connect the supply of the test unit (XS92a)[220VA].

  11. Connect the switch on the lower unit and on the upper u for test unit XS92a

  12. Use the (YB92) hand terminal according to the test step.

Checking of Line Trip

  1. Disconnect the under voltage protection for [M1  , M2] - (K40) and connect the (C. B) only.

  2. Making jumpers in (XS 92a) between (1, 2) , (3, 4) , (5, 6) , (7, 8).

  3. Write the program:
                   F F -         FR = 50                     DFR = 0 
                                    SQ  = 1F1                       FC    = 111
                                    A    = 0                     TF  = 400
                     Z Z - Z L =  Z1/2              PZL  ~  85ْ                    K0 = K0  of  relay

  4. Press on (Start).

  5. Trip the line (C.B) and auto - Reclosing (3 - ph)

  6. Press (0 , enter)

  7. Disconnect the (SX 92a).

  8. Disconnect D.C - D.C. converter (N F93)

  9. Remove the test plug (YX 91).

  10. Fixed the under voltage protection (K 40).

  11. Review the setting of the relay protection.

  12. Connect the D.C - D.C. converter (NF F93)

  13. Finish the permit.

  14. Connect the line. 

  15. We measure the current of the line by clip-on ammeter and measuring the voltage by voltmeter and record in test sheet.

  16. Place a copy of the test result in the field maintenance file.
