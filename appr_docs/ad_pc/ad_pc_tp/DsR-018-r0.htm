Introduction 

Characteristic of the impedance measuring function in the line protection REL100 

This procedure details the steps required to completely check the proper operation and calibration of the REL 100 relay. It is recommended this procedures be carried out on an annually bases.

The first preliminary steps to prepare for relay testing describes the activities required prior to testing. Some of these steps could be performed days before the actual test. The second section Preparation for testing describes the steps to set up the equipment for performing the actual tests. The third section is the actual test.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, qualified maintenance staff carry the suitable safety ID category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required

  • Personal protective equipment must be used as safety Helmet, and safety shoes... etc.

  • Check that the Bus and line disconnected switches are opened and Tagged.

  • Make sure CT secondary are shorted.

  • VT miniature circuit Breaker (MCB's) is isolated (opened) .

  • Isolate DC supplies to Relay. When doing any adjustments or Maintenance on The REL100 relay.

  • Isolate breaker failure (B.F) scheme associated with relay

  • Disturbance alarm or fault recorder associated with relay

  • Breaker trip coil and transfer trip scheme

  • Auto- recluse associated with the relay REL 100 (test plug isolate the above all points)

Tools And Equipment

  • Test set type XS92a.

  • Ammeter, Test leads

  • Timer

Work to be Carried Out

Step to Prepare for Relay Testing

  1. Review technical reference material manufacture's manual

  2. Obtain copy of relay REL 100 setting from field maintenance file

  3. Obtain outage for line protected by this relay REL100.

  4. Check that the bus and line disconnect switches are opened and tagged.

  5. Block protection signal to: breaker trip coil and transfer trip schemes.

  6. Go to the switchgear and inspect the CT's and VT miniature circuit breaker (MCB's) and all cabling supplying the relay.

  7. Check that the cables are in good condition and the all wire connections are tight and clean.

Testing Procedures 

Zone Reaches

Zone I        Phase To Earth

  1. Restore DC supply to REL100 relay scheme.

  2. Prepare the set test XS92a as following:

MOD                                  ZZ  ( distance)

ZL                                      up to setting X1  PZL=90°  OR  up to setting  R1 at PZL =0°

PZL                                    angle of setting line protected (0…..360˚)

K0                                                    0.7

FC                                      fault code forward or reverse (111) phase to earth

SQ                                     1F1

A                                        2

TF                                       time fault zone (250) m.sec

DZL                                   step parameters and range limits (0.1)

Start(-)                                injection of testing signals.

 

NOTE

  1. We Entered The all above Parameters according to the flow chart of set Test XS92a 

  2. When the relay REL 100 gives tripping signal press ◙ to stop injection.

  3. Read The values in hand terminal of test set X292a and compare this value with the value of setting in test sheet of relay.

  4. Press the reset button on the relay front panel and check that the LED indicator resets and the alarm contacts open.

  5. Repeat the above test with the phase S by using FC "fault code" (112) &T by using FC 113 for X1 at angle----& R1 at angle PZL 0˚ , Make sure Relay REL100 gives Trip signaling with all phases R, S, T.

  6. Repeat the above test , but in the reverse direction by using FC " fault code" 011,012, 013, and make sure that the relay REL100 is blocked in reverse direction.

Phase to Phase
  1. Enter the all above parameters, using FC " fault code"

Fault Code (FC)            Phase R-S              121                           forward direction (Trip)

                                      Phase S-T              122                           forward direction (Trip)

                                      Phase T-R              123                           forward direction (Trip)
In The reverse direction (021) & (022) & (023) block

Zone II

  1. Repeat the above test for zone I, but change the value of X2 at PZL =90˚ , R2 at PZL = 0,Tf up to time of Z2 .

  2. Make sure that the Relay " REL100" in forward direction (Trip) and reverse direction (Block).

Zone III

  1. Repeat the above test for zone II , but: X3 at PZL =90˚ and R3 at PZL =0˚, TF up to Time of Z3.

  2. Make sure that the Relay REL100 in forward direction (Trip) and reverse direction ( Block)

Zone IV

  1. Repeat the above test for zone III, but X4 PZL =90˚ and R4 at PZL =0˚,TF up to Time of Z4.

  2. Make sure that the relay REL100 in forward direction ( Trip ) and reverse direction ( Block ) with all phases R, S, T.

Measuring the Operating Times

  1. The operating time of a distance Relay should measured using the tripping contact ( free contact) connected the free contact to (+) Red "__\__ " "yellow " in the front panel of set test XS92a.

  2. Entered the all parameters of Z1 by ZL = 0.5 ZL (setting)

  3. When the trip occurs the injection automatically stopped by free contact. Then read the value of time zone I in hand terminal.

  4. Repeat the above step to measuring the time of Zone II, III, IV

                        Time of Z1     at  ZL = 0.5 Z1

                        Time of Z2     at  ZL = 0.5 ( Z1+ Z2 )

                        Time of Z3     at  ZL = 0.5 ( Z2+ Z3 )

                        Time of Z4     at  ZL = 0.5 ( Z3+ Z4)

NOTE 

How to Find  "Free Contact" For Relay REL 100

  1. Select any contact (free contact ) and measured DC (Not Found)

  2. Measure ( Buzzer ) by using Avometer in this contact (Not Found).

  3. Make trip by using set tester XS92a for Relay REL 100,then this free contact measured Buzzer by Avometer.

  4. Then connect this free contact to set test XS92a in to point Red (+) and yellow " __\__ " in the front.

Communication Scheme

  • According to setting adjust the scheme on No "2"

Send Signal

  1. Repeat the test of zone I and make sure that the trip occur at time of zone I (t1) = 200 m.s

  2. Measure by using Avometer on d.c voltage on send contact on the relay (REL 100)
    * Avometer reading (0 V) contact close at trip (Z1)
    * Avometer reading (220 V) contact open at trip (Z1)

  3. Measure the d.c voltage by using Avometer on  send contact on the communication panel on the other side or exist counter on communication panel  to send signal other side

End to End Test " Receive Signal "For Relay REL100

  1. Repeat the test of Zone II and Make sure that the trip occur at time of Zone II (T2) (500 m.sec)

  2. Make Bridge on receives Signal.

  3. Make sure that the relay REL100 trip Zone II at Time of Zone I (100 m.sec)

Switch ON to Fault (SOTF) Test

  1. Repeat the test of Zone (4) but ( SQ = 0F1 ) in set test XS92a

  2. Make sure that Zone IV trip at time of Zone 1

Return Protection Scheme and Associated Equipment to Service

  1. Return the rely REL100 to service by with drawing the test plug handle from the relay.

  2. Make sure C.T secondary are opened.

  3. Return the relay to service by restoring supply from voltage transformer (V.T) (M.C.B) is closed.

  4. Place the associated Barker Failure ( B.F) protection scheme back in service.

  5. Restore the previously blocked disturbance alarms and fault recorder associated with this protection.

  6. Return the relay Power Line Carries (PLC) links to their normal position.

  7. Turn ON the associated auto - reclos scheme.

  8. Turn ON the power supply DC to relay REL100.

  9. Take crossed reading of all AC currents and voltages supplied to the relay REL100 and compare them to those for normal load conditions.

  10. Check that these readings agree with station Meters , MVAR , Power Factor ,Amperes , voltages.

  11. Place a copy of the test results in the field maintenance file and Test sheet
