Introduction

The scheme protection system for steam turbine is manufactured by CEE France and this Scheme is a solid state type. The scheme protection will protect generator, step up transformer, There is table for protective relays.

 

The first draft initiated by eng. Abdel Raheem Salem Mohamed Ez, tested by eng. Khaled El-Etreby & eng. Atef Farh, revised by quality control eng. Tawfik Elabasery at EDEPCo., and approved by eng. Monear Mahmoud El-Wakeel: Head Sector of Damietta Power Station.

 Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Steam turbine out of service and work permit required.

  • Make sure CT secondary circuit are shorted.

  • Voltage transformer miniature circuit breakers (m.c.b) is opened

Tools and Equipment

  • Siemens test set which consists of two units

  • Current unit type 7VP48.

  • Voltage unit type 7VP49.

  • Voltmeter, Ammeter.

  • Test leads, screw driver.    

Work to be Carried Out

Preliminary Steps to Prepare for Relay Testing

  1. Review technical reference material manufactures / manual.

  2. Obtain copy of relay setting from field maintenance file.

  3. Obtain outage for unit protected by this relay

  4. Check that the bus and unit disconnect switches are opened and tagged.

  5. Inspect the secondary circuits of CT .

  6. Inspect  VT miniature circuit breakers (m.c.b.) and all cabling supplying the relay.

  7. Block any disturbance alarms or fault recorders associated with this protection.

Preparation of Test

  1. Place the two cases one on the top of other (the voltage unit on the top of current unit)

  2. Connect the two units together by the cable connector 7 XV6201 between test plug "X2" in current unit and test plug "X1" in voltage unit.

  3. Connect the power supply cable 7XV6200 (3*380 V) between the plug "X1" in the current unit and the source (careful the positive sequence).

  4. Connect an ammeter with the socket "17 ,18" in current unit and voltmeter with the socket "5,6" in voltage unit.

Generator Differential  

  1. To permit the relay operate the current must be pass through the operating coil.

  2. Inject the current to the relay for each phase of the two sides the current leads put as the follow:  
          Phases                  neutral side                     Output side  
          PH-R                    AR3, AN3                    AR4,AN4   
          PH-S                    AS3,AN3                      AS4,AN4       
              PH-T                    AT3,AN3                      AT4,AN4

  3. Increase the current until the green lamp distinguished read and record the pick up value then decrease the current the green lamp light, read and record the drop out value.

    4.

  4. Increase the current the to pick up value then measure the time T1 (timer point 149,150) free contact.

  5. Repeat this for each phases of the two sides.

Transformer Differential

  1. To permit the relay operate the current must be pass through the operating coil.

  2. Inject the current to the relay for each phase of the two sides the current leads put as the follow:                                                  
                              phases                    L.V. side                          H.V. side  
                              PH-R                    AR7, AN7                    14(relay), AN7
                              PH-S                    AS7, AN7                     18(relay), AN7
                              PH-T                    AT7, AN7                     22(relay), AN7

  3. Increase the current until the green lamp distinguished read and record the pick up value then decrease the current untill the green lamp light, read and record the drop out value.

  4. Increase the current to the pick up value then measure the time T1 (timer point 149,150) free contact.

  5. Repeat this for each phases of the two sides.

Over All Diff

  1. To permit the relay operate the current must be pass through the operating coil.

  2. Inject the current to the relay for each phase of the two sides the current leads put as the follow:                    
                               Phases                   neutral side             220kv side
                               PH-R                    AR1, AN1           14(relay), AN1      
                               PH-S                    AS1,AN1             18(relay), AN1       
                               PH-T                    AT1,AN1             22(relay),AN1

  3. Increase the current until the green lamp distinguished read and record the pick up value then,

  4. Decrease the current until the green lamp light, read and record the drop out value.

  5. Increase the current the to pick up value then measure the time T1 (timer point 149,150) free contact.

  6. Repeat this for each phases of the two sides. 

Reverse Power

  1. Disconnect the two fuse switches for sec. P.T.

  2. Disconnect the links of terminals VR2,VS2,VT2

  3. Connect the voltage leads UR, US, UT  . from the tester to terminals VR2,VS2,VT2 (relay side)

  4.   Connect the current leads IR, IN  from the tester  to terminal (2,3) on the relay

  5. Inject 3-ط voltage  UL = 100v 

  6. Inject small current (mA) to adjust the angle at 180 on the digital where the selector current L1 and Voltage (U1-U2) then increase it until green lamp distinguished record the p.u. value (Ipu= mA) then determine the operating angle zone, at 180 again decrease the current until lamp light record the d.o. value(Id.o)

  7. Connect the current leads IT, IN  from the tester  to terminal (10,11) on the relay .   

  8. Inject 3-ط voltage  UL = 100v 

  9. Inject small current (mA) to adjust the angle at 180 on the digital where the selector current L3 and Voltage (U1-U2) then increase it until green lamp distinguished  record the p.u. value (Ipu= mA) then determine the operating angle zone, at 180 again decrease the current until lamp light record the d.o. value (Id.o)

  10. Determine the long time at the p.u value  T (timer point 149,150) free contact.

  11. determine the short time at the p.u value   T (timer point 13,17).   

Loss of Field

  1. Disconnect the two fuse switches for sec. P.T.

  2. Disconnect the links of terminals VR2,VS2,VT2

  3. Connect the voltage leads UR, US, UT  . from the tester to terminals VR2,VS2,VT2 (relay side) 

  4. Connect the current leads IR, IN  from the tester  to terminal (2,3) on the relay 

  5. Inject 3-ط voltage  UL = 100v 

  6. Inject small current (mA) to adjust the angle at 270 on the digital where the selector current L1 and Voltage (UR-UT) which not exist this require reversing the current then increase it until green lamp distinguished record this value (Ipu= mA) then determine time T (timer point 149,150) the operating angle zone, at 270 again decrease the current until lamp light  record this value (Id.o)

  7. Connect the current leads IT, IN  from the tester  to terminal (7,6) on the relay .  

  8. Inject 3-ط voltage  UL = 100v 

  9. Inject small current (mA) to adjust the angle at 270 on the digital where the selector current L3 and Voltage (UT-UR)  then increase it until green lamp distinguished record this value (Ipu= mA) then determine time T (timer point 149 , 150) the operating angle zone , at 270 again decrease the current until lamp light record this value (Id.o)

Voltage Restraint Over Current

  1. Disconnect the two fuse switches for sec. P.T.

  2. Disconnect the links of terminals VR2,VS2,VT2

  3. Connect the voltage leads UR, US, UT  . from the tester to terminals VR2,VS2,VT2 (relay side) 

  4. Put the current leads as follow: 
                    PH-R        2,3     on the relay
                    PH-S        6.7     on the relay

  1. Apply 100 v

  2. Increase the current until the green lamp distinguished read and record the pick up value then decrease the current the green lamp light, read and record  the drop out value.

  3. Increase the current to pick up value  then measure the time T1 (timer point 149,150) free contact 

  4. Repeat the previous step for each phases at different values of the voltage 50 v , 0.0v

  • Short Circuit test   

  1. As the o/c test but   its value known from the flag (b) not from lamp.

  2. Repeat this for all phases.

Stator Earth Fault

  1. Inject the current to the relay by putting current leads to terminals A01,A02

  2. Increase the current until the green lamp distinguished read and record  the pick up value then decrease the current the green lamp light , read and record the drop out value

  3. Increase the current to pick up value  then measure the time T1 (timer point 149,150) free contact 

Transformer Earth Fault

  1. Inject the current to the relay by putting current leads to terminals A03,A04

  2. Increase the current until the green lamp distinguished read and record  the pick up value then decrease the current untill the green lamp light, read and record the drop out value

  3. Increase the current to pick up then measure the time T (timer point 149 , 150) free contact .

Over Fluxing

  1. Disconnect the two fuse switches for sec PT.

  2. Disconnect the links of terminals VR2,VS2,VT2

  3. Connect the voltage leads UR, US  from the tester to terminals VR2,VS2 (relay side)  

  4. Increase the voltage until the green lamp distinguished, read and record the pick up value then decrease the voltage untill the green lamp light, read and record the drop out value.

  5. Increase the voltage to pick up then measure the time T1 (timer point 149,150) free contact.

Voltage Balance

  1. Disconnect the two fuse switches for sec. PT.

  2. Disconnect the links of terminals VR1,VS1,VT1 & VR2,VS2,VT2 and VR3,VS3,VT3and VR4,VS4,VT4

  3. Put the voltage leads to prot. side : 
    ph-R-S points 22,23,  phR-T 22,24 and ph S-T 23,24 on relay AVR ph-R-S points 6,7,  phR-T 6,8 and ph S-T 7,8 on relay

  4. Increase the voltage (p.u) until the flag appear.

Rotor Earth Fault

  1. Put the variable resistance between the slip ring of the rotor or terminal A0 in the protection cabinet and earth then decrease the resistance value gradually until the trip commend appear at L.O.R.  

  2. Measure this resistance value (Rpu).

Dead Machine Relay

Relay Type : RAGUA,                                    Manufacture:   ABB

  1. Increase the voltage (p.u) until the flag appear.

  2. Disconnect the m.c.b for sec. P.T.

    3.

  3. Disconnect the link for terminal  VR1,VS1,VT1 & VR2,VS2,VT2 & VR3,VS3,VT3 and VR4,VS4,VT4

  • Under voltage Element Test

  1. Connect the voltage leads to points  VR2,VS2

  2. Increase the voltage to 100 v to release the under voltage element

  3. Wait 4 sec. the fuse failure flag is appear

  4. Decrease the voltage until fuse failure is release, record this value as P.U value

  5. Increase the voltage until the under voltage element is released record the d.o. value .

  6. Repeat the steps from 3-5  but the voltage leads connected to terminals VR3,VS3

  • Over Current Element

  1. Connect the current leads to the terminals PH-R (AR5,point 4 on relay) 

  2. Increase the current until the trip flag is appeared, record this pu value

  3. Decrease the current until the flag red release, record this d.o value

  4. Repeat, the stops from to 3 for ph.s & ph.T but with connecting the current leads to terminals  PH-S (AS5,point 6 on relay) PH-T (AT5,point 8 on relay) 

  • Functions of Dead Machine Test

  1. Connect the voltage leads to the terminal (VR2,VS2)

  2. Connect the current leads to the terminal PH-R (AR5,point 4 on relay) 

  3. Connect the timer to free contact on this relays

  4. Inject the pu. value of current at voltage element is released and not released

  5. Measure the time of trip, record this voltage

  6. Repeat the steps from 2-5 to the other phases

    7.

  • Back up function

  1. Inject the voltage 100 v as pervious

  2. Inject the pu. current for ph. R as pervious

  3. Decrease the voltage to p.u. value, trip command appear

  4. Measure the time as pervious

  • Trip C ct test

  1. Adjust the current to p.u. value, while the voltage equal zero

  2. Close the unit CB's without isolators

  3. Inject the current, the unit CB's will tripped

Frequency Protection

Over and Under Frequency

  1. Disconnect the m.b.c. of the secondary circuit of the potential transformer.

  2. Disconnect the links of  ......  MAC 124 (VR2, VS2, VT2).

  3. Connect the voltage leads to points terminals   ......  MAC 124 (VR2, VS2, VT2).

  4. Inject the voltage, increase the voltage to Un. (normal)

  5. Increase the frequency until the relay picks up, record this value F > pu, then decrease the frequency until the relay drop off, record this value F > do.   (do: drop out)

  6. Measure this time from free contact where the relay picks up.

  7. Again adjust the frequency to Fn. (n: nominal)

  8. Decrease the frequency until the relay picks up, record this value F < pu, then increase the frequency until the relay drop off, record this value F < do.

  9. Measure this time from free contact where the relay picks up.

  10. When the relay operate, decrease the voltage until the relay block, record this value U block, then increase the voltage until the relay ready, record this value U ready.
