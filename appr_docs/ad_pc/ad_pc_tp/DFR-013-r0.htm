Introduction

The digital transformer protection scheme RET 316*4 is one of the new generation of fully digital protection systems. It's designed for fast selective protection of two and three winding power transformers.

The unit detects the following faults on power transformers:
     - All phase faults.
     - Earth faults where the power transformer star-point is solidly or low-impedance grounded.
     - Inter - turn faults

This procedure details the steps required to completely check the proper operation and calibration of the relay. It is recommended this procedure be carried out on an annually bases. The document is formatted the way to minimize the possibility of error causing personal injury or damage to equipment.

The first draft initiated by eng. Samah Mokhtar Mostafa, tested at 4 Dec. 2002 by eng. Yousef Melad Shahat, and approved by eng. Fawzy Moursy Mohamed: Head Sector of Aswan Network, Upper Egypt zone.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area,

  • Line isolation and work permit required

  • Make sure that CT secondary circuits are shorted before isolating the current circuits for test distance protection

Tools and Equipment

  • XS92a test set

  • Voltmeter, Ammeter

  • Test leads 

 

 

Work to be Carried Out

Steps to Prepare for Relay Testing

  1. Review technical reference material and manufacturer's manual

  2. Obtain copy of relay setting from field maintenance file and compare it with the setting on the relay

  3. Obtain outage for line protected by this relay

  4. Check that the circuit breaker is opened and tagged

  5. Inspect the CT secondary circuits, the V T miniature circuit breakers ( mcb.s) and all cabling supplying the relaying

  6. Use the station drawings to isolate the following:
         * Breaker trip coils
         * Breaker failure scheme associated with the relay and disturbance alarms

Testing the relay

  1. Carefully follow this test procedure

  2. Verify the test results match the relay setting

  3. Trip the circuit breaker associated with the relay

Preparation

  1. This procedure should be used for performing off load line

  2. Look the EEC panel name plate and check this EEC system designation name plate agrees with the line that has been taken out of service for maintenance 

  3. Make sure CT secondary circuits are shorted at the incoming terminal blocks before isolate the relay current circuits for test

Reading the Setting from the Relay by using the PC

  1. Connecting the personal computer to the relay

  2. Run the REL 316*4 program

  3. Read the setting

Differential Protection test

  1. Be sure that the DC power of the relay is correct by applying a DC voltmeter across points H2 and H3 on the rear of the relay

  2. Connect AC power to the test set

  3. Connect the current by selecting the operation of current (I) on the test set

  4. To test winding (1):

  5. Connect the current test lead on the current terminals of the relay T1 and T2 (phase R to Neutral)

  6. Increase the value of current slowly until the relay operates and the LED of (diff. protection trip) lights

  7. Connect the current test lead on the current terminals of the relay T3 and T4 (phase S to Neutral)

  8. Repeat step 6

  9. Connect the current test lead on the current terminals of the relay T5 and T6 (phase T to Neutral)

  10. Repeat step 6

  11. To test winding (2):

  12. Connect the current test lead on the current terminals of the relay T7 and T8 (phase R to Neutral)

  13. Increase the value of current slowly until the relay operates and the LED of (diff. protection trip) lights

  14. Connect the current test lead on the current terminals of the relay T9 and T10 (phase S to Neutral)

  15. Repeat step6

  16. Connect the current test lead on the current terminals of the relay T11 and T12 (phase T to Neutral)

  17. Repeat step 6

  18. To test winding (3)

  19. Connect the current test lead on the current terminals of the relay T13 and T14 (phase R to Neutral)

  20. Increase the value of current slowly until the relay operates and the LED of (diff. protection trip) lights

  21. Connect the current test lead on the current terminals of the relay T15 and T16 (phase S to Neutral)

  22. Repeat step 6

  23. Connect the current test lead on the current terminals of the relay T17 and T18 (Phase T to Neutral

  24. Repeat step 6  

 

Test of external circuits

Test the external circuits using the test function provided by the MMC as attached

Return relay and associated equipment to service

  1. Return relay to service by removing the test leads from the relay terminals and the test set

  2. Make sure CT and V T secondary circuits are in normal condition

  3. Remove the disturbance alarm LED's on the relay which is caused by the test

  4. Request feeder to be returned in service

  5. Measures the currents and voltages in the secondaries of the main CT's and VT's and compare the results with the values displayed by the MMC

  6. Take phasor readings of all A C currents and voltages and compare them to those for normal load condition

  7. Check that these reading agree with station meters (MW, Mvar, Amperes and power factor)

  8. Place a copy of the test result in the field maintenance file

Test functions

Since some of them can disrupt the normal operation of the device, test functions can only be accessed after entering the valid password. They are used mainly during commissioning and may only be activated when the plant is out of service, or with the tripping and signaling circuits externally disconnected if in service.
 

The protection is pre-initiated upon closir, the "test functions" menu and the set of parameters previously used in operation reactivated

The procedure for using the test functions can be seen from the following flow chart

The next window requires the input of one or several test values. The simulation of the input signals check the operation of the function and its tripping and signaling channels

Testing the tripping relays, signaling relays or LED's:

After entering the slot number, one or several tripping channels may be selected for testing (by pressing < ins > ). Upon executing the command, the corresponding tripping relays, signaling relay and LED's of the channels concerned are energized.

It is only possible to test tripping commands and signals of one input/output unit at a time and the signals must be of the same type, e.g. either signaling relays or LED`s 

Parset switching

To test a protection function belonging to another set of parameters, the respective set of parameters has to be activated first. Menu item "Parset switching" enables the parameter set to be selected and activated after entering the valid password
