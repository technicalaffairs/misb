Introduction

This document outlines the testing procedure P1, which must be performed on an annually bases as indicated by Y1 in the above title block

The type SFF31 relays are static under frequency relays that provide a single set point for under frequency tripping if the system disturbance such that the load excceds the generation the system is in danger of collapse, then this relay will trip the system. (Generator Protection)

The first draft  initiated by eng. Fayza El-Sharkawy, tested by eng. Shirin Hamdy & eng. Azza Salama at 29 April 2003, revised by EDEPC quality control eng. Adel El-Nagar, accepted by general director eng. Mahmoud Saker and approved by eng. Moustfa Allam: Head Sector of Talkha Power Station.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued & the staff  of test must be qualified and carry the suitable safety precautions

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • The generator must be out of service.

Tools and Equipment

  • Test Plug Handle .

  • Test Leads .

  • PCR 500L Test Set .

Work to be Carried Out


Preparation

  1. Make Sure PT secondary circuit are opened at the incoming terminal blocks before isolating the relay voltage circuit for test ,

  2. Obtain copy of relay setting from field maintenance file and compare it with the setting on the relay.

Testing the Relay

  1. Connect the relay studs ( 7 , 10 ) to the DC source 125 V DC where stud 7 is the + ve and stud 10 is the – ve.

  2. Connect the test leads between the trip contact ( 1 – 2 ).

  3. Connect test leads to line to line terminals ( 4 – 5 ).

  4. Increase the voltage at 115 v.

  5. Adjust the frequency to a normal value.

  6. Decrease the frequency slowly until the relay trips instantaneously.

  7. Record the frequency value and the time trip in the test sheet.

Return Relay and Associated Equipment to Service

  1. Return relay to service by removing the test leads from the relay terminal block and the test set i.e.
         -     Disconnect the test leads from the terminals (  4 - 5  )
         - Disconnect the test leads between the trip contact ( 1 - 2 )
         - Disconnect the DC terminals from ( 7-10 )

  2. Make sure CT and PT secondary circuits are in normal condition.

  3. If any equipment associated with the relay has been isolated, it should be returned to service by the following restoration steps:
         - Breaker trip coils.
         - Breaker failure scheme associated this relay.
         - Disturbance alarm and fault recorder associated with this relay.
