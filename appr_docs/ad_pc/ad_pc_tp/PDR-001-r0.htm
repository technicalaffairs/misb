Introduction

This document lists the testing procedure P1, which must be performed on an annually bases as indicated by Y1 in the above title block

The type GGP53C relay is an extremely sensitive three phase, time delay, power directional relay designed to provide anti-motoring protection for steam turbine generators upon the loss of its prime mover power. It has an instantaneous  power directional element.

The first draft  initiated by eng. Fayza El-Sharkawy, tested by eng. Shirin Hamdy & eng. Azza Salama at 29 April 2003, revised by EDEPCo. quality control eng. Adel El-Nagar, accepted by General Director eng. Mahmoud Saker and approved by eng. Moustfa Alam Head Sector of Talkha Power Station.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the maintenance staff should carry the suitable ID safety category,

  • Maintenance staff should be trained well, qualified or directly supervised by some one who is.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Personal safety and healthy equipment must be used as safety shoes, safety helmet ... etc

    •

  • The generator must be out of service.

Tools and Equipment

  • Test Plug handle,  Test leads

  • Manta injection test set

Work to be Carried Out

Preparation

  1. Make Sure C.T secondary circuit are shorted at the incoming terminal blocks before isolating the relay current circuit for test.

  2.  Look at field maintenance file and compare the position of the setting switches on relay with those shown on the test sheet.

Testing the Relay

  1. Connect test leads between  the  relay contact ( relay trip ) (1 – 11 ) .

  2. Make short connections between terminals ( 12 – 15 ) , ( 13 – 16 ) , ( 9 – 14 )  , ( 2 – 10 ) .

  3. Connect the leads to the voltage terminals ( R : 14 , S:13 , T:15  ).

Phase ( R ) Test

  1.  Connect test leads between the current terminals ( 3 – 4 ).

  2. Increase the voltage to the value of 115 V.

  3. Adjust the current to 1 A.

  4. Increase the angle between the current & voltage until the directional unit picks up and continue in creasing the angle until the directional unit releases.

  5. Record the angle interval in the test sheet.

  6. Increase the current and the angle slowly until the induction disk picks up and  wait for the relay to trip.

  7. Record the current, angle, time values at which the relay trips in the test sheet.

    8.

Phase ( S ) Test

  1. Connect the test leads between the current terminals ( 5 – 6 ) .

  2. Repeat the steps from ( 5 to 10 ) .

Phase ( T ) Test

  1. Connect test leads between the current terminals ( 7 – 8 ) .

  2. Repeat the steps from ( 5 to 10 ) .

Return Relay and Associated Equipment to Service

  1. Return relay to service by removing the test leads from the relay terminal block and the test set i.e.
    -     Disconnect the test leads from the terminals ( 7 - 8 ).
    - Disconnect the test leads between the trip contact (1 - 11 )
    - Disconnect the short connection between terminals ( 12 - 15 ) , ( 13 - 16 ) , ( 9 - 14 ) , ( 2 - 10 )
    - Disconnect the leads from the voltage terminals ( R :14, S:13, T:15  ).

  2. Make sure CT and PT secondary circuits are in normal condition.

  3. If any equipment associated with the relay has been isolated, it should be returned to service by the following restoration steps:
    - Breaker trip coils.
    - Breaker failure scheme associated this relay.
    - Disturbance alarm and fault recorder associated with this relay.
