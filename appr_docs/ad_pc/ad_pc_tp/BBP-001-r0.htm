Introduction

    The MBCZ10 bus bar protection is Modular, solid state, low impedance biased differential based on the circulating current principle and dual cratered of operation, provides continuous supervision of CT secondary circuits and constant monitoring of vital circuits extremely stable up to the Max switch gear.

    This procedure details the steps required to completely check the proper operation and calibration of the MBCZ10 relay. It is recommended this procedure be carried out on a yearly bases. This work be carried out section of this document is divided into 3 sections.

    The first preliminary steps to prepare for relay testing describes the activities required prior to testing. Some of these steps could be performed days before the actual test. The second section Preparation for testing describes the steps to set up the equipment for performing the actual tests. The third section is the actual test.

    The document is formatted the way to minimize the possibility of error causing personal injury or damage to equipment.

Safety Precautions

  • Work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category, 

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required,

  • Wear your safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes .... etc

  • Outage arrangement must be done.

  • Before removing a module, ensure that you are at the same electro static potential as the equipment by touching the case.

  • Handle the module by its front-plate, frame or edges of the printed circuit board.

  • Switches off DC supply before inserting or with drawing any card from the relay.

  • Before withdrawing MBCZ II, MBCZ12, MBCZ13 modules from its case fit CT shorting to test bulge before inserted into test block.

Tools and Equipment

  • ZFB test set: This test set consists of 4 units: supply unit, control unit, fault impedance unit and CT unit.

  • Timer, Test Plugs 1V and 2V

  • Multimeter, Test leads, Screwdriver

Work to be Carried Out

Preliminary Steps to Prepare for Relay Testing

  1. Review technical reference material, manufacturer manual.

  2. Obtain copy of relay setting from field maintenance file and compare it with the setting on the relay.

  3. Inspect the CT secondary circuits and all cabling supplying the relay.

  4. Use the station drawing to isolate the disturbance alarm and fault, recorder associated with this relay. Breaker trip coils and also breaker failure scheme.

Testing the Relay and Protection O/P

  1. Carefully follow this test procedure from page 2 to page 4.

  2. Verify the test results match the relay setting, if not consult technical services.

  3. Test trip all breakers associated with the relay.

Testing the Relay

N.B: The MBCZ10 have a test module, plug test lead connect between test module and measuring zone to be tested.

Check of the MBCZ 10 Operating Values Differential Supervision Relays

Injection Tests via the test unit

  1. Turn the key switch on the alarm unit to the protection out position.

  2. Remove the covers from the test unit and the zone unit to be checked.

  3. Plug test lead into the test unit using the end of the lead with the 3-5 m jack plug, the 2 m plug on the other end of the test lead is then plugged into the test I/P on the phase of the zone to be tested (1).

  4. Block the trip O/P relay for a short period during the test.

  5. Set the selection switch on the front of the test unit to inject X I and set the digital Current switch to 00.

Test Biased Differential Measuring Units

  1. Set the MAN/AUTO switch to MAN.

  2. Set the current to 80% of the marked value of the selected setting and the relay should not indicate a trip.

  3. Increase the current to the marked setting value and the protection should operate.

  4. There should be a trip indication on the element begin tested and 2 protection operated.

  5. Indication on the alarm unit, the latter has a /5 seconds delay.

  6. Release the injection bottom and rest the indication.

  7. Check that when the inject button is now depressed the trip is instantaneously, and that when the current is injected for five seconds no pratic fail – indication comes up.

  8. Repeat steps from 1 to 6 on the other two phases and then on each other zone in turn.

Test CT Supervision Circuit

  1. Increase the current until the meter reads current just below the setting of the Supervision relay.

  2. Then increase slowly until the supervision operates to indicate a CT fault.

  3. Repeat steps from (1 to 2 ) on the other phases and then on each other zone in Turn.

Alarm Circuit Check

  1. Switch off each of the protection power supplies in turn.

  2. Check that alarm in given each time, also the protection should continue to function on the supply from the remaining power supply unites.

  3. When the power supply for the isolation relay is turned off check that all isolation

  4. Supervision alarms operate.

TEST Supervision for Isolator Repeat Relays

  1. Switches off the power supply to the isolator repeat relay circuit.

  2. The indicators for the isolator repeat relay supervision should light up, will be followed after a delay by an alarm indication on the modules containing the repeat relays.

  3. The power fail alarm should also be initiated by the action of switching off the power Supply.

BZT and Breaker Fail Circuit

Units for External Breaker Fail BZT Check

  1. To test BZT relay first ensure that the key switch is a protection out, then bridge the BZT Contacts.

  2. Check that the feeder under test and all the circuit breaker modules connected to the same Main zone and the alarm unite show a trip indication.

  3. If the feeder is not connected to main zone (because all its isolators are open) check that Protection operated appears on the alarm unit followed by common alarm.

 

 

Feeder Units for Internal Breaker Fail / Feeder unit manufactured before Aug. 1989

  1. For this test it is necessary for the feeder to be connected the bus bar or appear to the Scheme to be connected.

  2. Note to which zone the connection or simulated connection is made.

  3. Set the switch on phase c of the appropriate zone measuring unit to 0.5.

  4. Inject current equal to 0.2 x-rated current / matching ratio in to terminals 21 and 22 of the feeder unit.

  5. This should not cause any trip or protection operated indicators to light.

  6. Bridge the breaker fail contact on the feeder protection.

  7. Check that all the circuit breaker units on the relevant zone give a trip indication and the feeder unit under test shows a BACK trip as well.

Feeder Units Manufactured After August 1989

  1. Leave the breaker fail contact open.

  2. Connect current injection to terminal 21 and 27 and link 22 and 28.

  3. Set the check zone measuring unit phase C is to (0.5 In E) the same as the main zone.

  4. Put the current setting of the breaker failure to 200mA.

  5. Inject a current equal 0.2 rated current.

  6. Check that a trip indication appears on the main and the circuit breaker units in the main zone followed by a back trip on the feeder unit under test.

Return Relay, and Associated Equipment to Service

  1. Remove the plug test lead from the test module also from the measuring zone to be tested.

  2. If any equipment with the relay has been isolate without the test plug, it should be returned to service.

Request to Return every thing to Service

  1. Take crossed reading of all AC currents supplied to the relay and compare them to those for normal load condition.

  2. Check that reading agree with station meters (MW/MVAR/ power factor amperes/ Voltages).
