Introduction

This document lists the testing procedure I1, which must be performed on an annually bases as indicated by Y1 in the above title block

The PJG12B relay is designed primarily for the detection of grounds in normally ungrounded machine field circuits and cause an alarm.

 Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued & the test staff must be qualified and carry the suitable safety category..

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • The generator must be out of service.

Tools and Equipment

  • Secondary injection test set.

  • Test plug handle.

  • Test leads.

  • Voltmeter.

Work to be Carried Out

Preparation

  1. Make sure PT secondary circuit are opened at the incoming terminal blocks before isolating the relay voltage circuit for test

  2. Obtain copy of relay setting from field maintenance file and compare it with the setting on the relay.

 

 

Testing the Relay 

  1. Connect the test leads between the voltage terminals ( 9 – 10 ). see figure 1

  2. Connect the test leads between the trip contact ( 1 – 2 )

  3. Make a short connection between terminals ( 3 – 4 ) .

  4. Increase the volt slowly until the relay picks up and the target appears .

  5. Keep the pick up voltage value until the relay trips .

  6. Record the voltage value & the time value in the test sheet .


 Return Relay and associated Equipment into Service

  1. Check that the CT and PT secondary connections return to its normal position as before the test.

  2. Disconnect the test leads from the terminals ( 9 - 10 ).

  3. Disconnect the test leads from the trip contact ( 1 - 2 ).

  4. Take off the short connection from terminals ( 3 - 4 ).

  5. If any equipment associated with the relay has been isolated, it should be returned to service by the following restoration steps:
        - Breaker trip coils.
        - Breaker failure scheme associated this relay.
        - Disturbance alarm and fault recorder associated with this relay.
