Introduction

This document lists the testing procedure P1, which must be performed on an annually bases as indicated by Y1 in the above title block

The IAV69A relay is a time delay under voltage and over voltage relay designed to be used whenever protection against an  abnormal voltage condition is required. This relay has two targets and closes its left hand contacts when the voltage increases the predetermined value and its right contacts when the voltage decreases another predetermined value, in both cases one target of the two appears.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Maintenance staff should be will trained and qualified or directly supervised by some one who is.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear your safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes ..etc.

  • The generator must be out of service.

Tools and Equipment

  • Secondary injection Test set.

  • Test Plug handle, Test Leads.

Work to be Carried Out

Preparation

  1. Make Sure PT secondary circuit are opened at the incoming terminal blocks before isolating the relay voltage circuit for test.

  2. Look at field maintenance fill and compare the position of the setting switches on relay with those shown on the setting sheet.

Testing the Relay

  1. Connect test leads between the relay contact ( relay trip ) (1 – 11 ).

Under Voltage Test

  1. Connect test leads between the low voltage terminal ( 5 – 6 ).

  2. Adjust the voltage to 120V on the secondary injection test set.

  3. Decrease the voltage slowly until the right contact closes and the right flag appears.

  4. Record the voltage value and the time value at which the contact closes in the test sheet.

Over Voltage Test

  1. Connect the test leads between the high voltage terminals ( 15 – 16 ) .

  2. Adjust the voltage to 120V on the secondary injection test set.

  3. Increase the voltage slowly until the left contact closes and the left flag appears and  trips.

  4. Record the voltage and the time values in the test sheet.

Return Relay and associated Equipment to Service

  1. Return relay to service by removing the test leads from the relay terminal block and the test set i.e.
    -     Disconnect the test leads from the terminals ( 15 - 16 )
    - Disconnect the test leads from the terminals ( 5 - 6 )
    - Disconnect the test leads from the trip contact ( 1 - 11 )

  2. Check that the CT and PT secondary connections return to its normal position as before the test.

  3. If any equipment associated with the relay has been isolated, it should be returned to service by the following restoration steps:
           - Breaker trip coils.
           - Breaker failure scheme associated this relay.
           - Disturbance alarm and fault recorder associated with this relay.
