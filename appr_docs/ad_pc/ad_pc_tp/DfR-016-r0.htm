Introduction

This procedure details the testing procedures and calibration P1, which recommended to be carried out every six months as indicated by M6 in the title block..

The type CFD relays are used for differential protection of alternating current machines against phase to phase and phase to ground faults. For an internal fault the relay will close its contacts. If on external  fault therefore the relay will not operate.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • The generator must be out of service.

Tools and Equipment

  • Test Plug handle .

  • Clamp ammeter, Test Leads .

  • Secondary injection test set .

Work to be Carried Out

Preparation

  1. Make Sure C.T secondary circuit are shorted at the incoming terminal blocks before isolating the relay current circuit for test

  2. Look at field maintenance file and compare the setting on relay with those shown on the test sheet.

 

Testing the Relay

  1. Connect test leads between  the current terminals ( 5 – 6 ). Figure 1

  2. Connect test leads between the timer leads ( 1 – 2 ) ( the relay contact ).

  3. Increase the current slowly until the relay operates.

  4. Record this value in the test sheet.

  5. Connect the test leads between the current terminals ( 6 – 7 ).

  6. Repeat steps 3 & 4 for terminals ( 6 – 7 ).

  7. Connect the test leads between the current terminals ( 5 – 7 ).

  8. Repeat steps 3 & 4 for terminals ( 5 – 7 ).

Return Relay and Associated Equipment to Service

  1. Return relay to service by removing the test leads from the relay terminal block and the test set i.e.
    -     Disconnect the test leads from the terminals ( 5 - 7 )
    - Disconnect the test leads between the trip contact ( 1 - 2 ).

  2. Make sure CT and PT secondary circuits are in normal condition.

  3. If any equipment associated with the relay has been isolated, it should be returned to service by the following restoration steps:
           - Breaker trip coils.
           - Breaker failure scheme associated this relay.
           - Disturbance alarm and fault recorder associated with this relay.
