Introduction

This document outlines the testing procedure I1, which must be performed every six months as indicated by M6 in the above title block.

This patent concerns the test of protection relays of the generator manufactured by “ SKODA PLZEN”.  The generator is of type 3 H6688/2VH, rated active power 222MW, rated apparent  power 277.5MVA, power factor 0.8, and voltages 15.75  ± 5%KV.

Safety Precautions

  • Ascertain the disconnection of the exciter, generator, main  transform and home transformer .

  • Set up a work permission including maintenance members, safety class, etc.

  • Put caution signs at the place of work, make a fence ( rope), if possible, around the work area and put “no smoking” signs.

  • Be sure that the team members wear the necessary manufacturing safety equipments.

  • Be aware of parts in service.

  • Follow the work permission instructions. 

Tools and Instruments

  • An AC voltage source of type Z11

  • An  AC current source of   type Z11

  • Frequency Converter Set

  • Varying - Resistance Box

  • An ammeter & A voltmeter.

  • A timer unit TM-2 ( Program )

  • Some leads & screws.

Work to be Carried Out

 

Preparation of relays

  1. Review the manual.

  2. Prepare the previous results of the relay testing.

  3. Check the wires of the relay beforehand.

  4. Be sure that the unit and the transformer are disconnected and the d.c. source is available.

  5. Check the existence of the d.c. sources in panels.

  6. Disconnect the trip leads of the relay and connect the timer to them.

  7. Check all the leads used in tests.

 

Preparation of testing tools

  1. Connect the testing units of Z11 to a power source.

  2. Connect the timer to a power source and its terminals to the trip terminals of the relay under test.

  3. Place an ammeter on current leads of Z11 to measure the current.

  4. Connect the voltmeter in parallel with the voltage leads of Z11 ( the other unit ) to measure the voltage.

 

The test of almost all generator protection devices is achieved by isolating either CT circuits or PT circuits or both.

 This is done by using a special socket for testing .This makes the test results depend only on the action of relays.

Test steps

  1. Place the socket (ZZ10) in XJ1 in panel HS01.

  2. Inject the current to the operating coil ( inner points of XJ1 )to terminals ( 11,12,13,14 ) of the socket  phase to phase (ph-ph) .

  5. Record the results.

  6. Repeat the steps for the 2- transformers sides :

Impedance S.C. 1st stage + 2nd stage, D15X1

It’s a double stage,3 phase impedance protection with a circular characteristic and distinguishing of the effected phase. The second stage has a time member .

P.T.                TV 2/2    15/ Ö3 :0.1/Ö3 :01/3 KV

C.T.                                     TA 2/1     10,000/5

Test Steps

  1. Place a socket (ZZ10) in Z2 ( TV 2/2 ) .

  2. Place a socket (ZZ10) in Z9 ( TA 2/1) .

  3. Fix the timer leads in (172,198) at the rear of HH05.

  4.  Inject current to the outer points of Z9 ph-ph (11, 12) & (11,13) & (12,13) respectively.

  5. Fix the value of the injected current at 4.3A

  6. Increase the injected voltage on the outer points of Z2 (the same phases of current injection) till the red flag vanishes.

  7. Keep on decreasing the voltage till the red flag of the device – indicating the injected ph-ph- displays and the timer closes almost instantaneously. The tripping voltage (1.st stage) represents the S.C. impedance of SP.
         - To test the second stage for the same ( ph-ph ).

  8. Fix the timer leads in (172,200) at the rear of HH05.

  9. Repeat the same steps of the first stage.

  10. When the flag of 2nd stage appears, make the timer start its action till it stops recording the trip time.

  11. Repeat the previous steps for the each pair of phases.

  12. Record the voltage and time for 1st &2nd stage for each phase – phase.

 

Short circuit , A33TX1

 

It’s a 3 phase over current protection with a distinguishing of the effected phase with a time member The device is blocked by the under voltage device (F376)

C.T.                           TA2/1                           10,000A

Test Steps

  1. Drive the under voltage device out (F376)

  2. Place the socket (ZZ10) in Z9.

  3. Inject current via Z11 in terminals (11,14) then (12,14) and finally (13,14) of Z9  phase – neutral injection.

  4. Connect the timer leads to (172,191) at the rear of HH04.

  5. Increase the current till the red flag of the injected phase displays .At this moment make the timer start its action till it stops indicating the tripping time in the case of over current only.

  6. Repeat the steps for each phase.

  7. Record the results.

  8. Drive the under voltage device (F376) in .

  9. Repeat the same steps .The tripping time in this case for each phase is instantaneous. (over current+ under voltage ) .

  10. Record the results in the absence and presence of F376.

 

Over load , A3PX1

 

It’s a single phase protection with one dependent member and two independent members. One independent measuring member conditions action of the dependent member

C.T.                      TA2/1                 10,000/5A

Test Steps

  1. Place the socket (ZZ10) in Z9 .

  2. Inject current via Z11 on terminals (11,14) of Z9  (R-neutral) .

  3. Increase current gradually till the flag of ( the dependent then the conditional and finally the effectual independent member ) displays i.e.  the flag of both first and second stage displays .

  4. Record the results.

 

Field loss (1st & 2nd stage) + Asynchronous operation , D20X1

 

Field failure protection with automatics for control of output . It has a circle shifted distance characteristic to the negative impedance semi –Axis.

P.T.        TV 2/2      15/ Ö3 :0.1/Ö3 :01/3 KV

C.T.                                TA 2/1     10,000/5

 Test Steps

  1. Place a socket (ZZ10) in Z2 and another one in Z9

  2. Fix the timer leads in (172,190) at the rear of panel HH03.

  3. Inject a current of 2.3A to points (11,14) (R-N) of Z9

  4. Inject the voltage on terminals ( 11, 12 )& (12,13) & ( 11,13 ) of Z2 (ph-ph) respectively .

  5. Keeping a value of 2.3A injection, increase the voltage till the red flag of the injected 2 - phases disappears (reset value) .

  6. Decrease the injected voltage gradually till the vanished   flag reappear. Make the timer start acting till it stops. (almost instantaneously) .

  7. Repeat the steps for the other 2 pair of phases.

 

To test F441 – Asynchronous operation      

  1. Follow the previous sequence of steps for testing the field loss device except :

  2. Fix the timer leads in (172,192) at the rear of panel HH03.

  3. Inject the current 2.3 A to points (11,14) of Z9 (R-N).

  4. Inject the voltage on terminals (11,13) only of Z2 (R-T)  .

  5. As soon as the flag of (R-T) displays in F45 ( field loss device ) , make the timer start its action till it stops  (10 seconds ) . All the red flags of F441 display simultaneously, the moment the timer closes indicating the first and second stages of F441 .

  6. Record the time and voltage.

 

Unbalanced load 1st, 2nd stage , N10X1

 

It’s a dependent protection at phase unbalance .  The protection  measures magnitude of the negative phase sequence  of the stator current I2 and in dependence on it, it determinates delay time according to “ Reverse current  – time“ characteristic.  

C.T.             TA 2/1                 10,000/5A.

 

 

Test Steps

  1. Place the socket (ZZ10) in Z9 (TA2/1) .

  2. Inject the current to the inner pts. of Z9 (phase R or T-N) .

  3. Fix the timer leads in terminals (172,188) at the rear of panel   HH02.

  4. Increase the current gradually till the red flag of the device (1 st stage) displays.

  5. Repeat the steps for the other phase. (T or R).

  6. Change the timer leads to terminals ( 210, 222) .

  7. Repeat the steps of the first stage for the second stage.

  8. To check the time element, inject a current of 5 A, and record the trip time.

  9. Record the results of the first and second stages of currents.

P.T.                TV 2/2    15/ Ö3 :0.1/Ö3 :01/3 KV

C.T.                                       TA 2/1     10,000/5

 

Test Steps

  2. Put a socket (ZZ10) in Z9.

  3. Fix the timer leads in ( 172,189) at the rear of panel (HH02) .

  6. Fix the injected voltage at 57.7V then raise the injected current till the red flag of the device displays. At this moment make the timer start its action till it stops.

  7. Repeat the steps for each   phase.

  8. Record the results.

Over voltage (1), V2TX1

It's a double stage over voltage protection with delay of the first stage 

P.T. TV 1/2    15/ Ö3 :0.1/Ö3 :0.1/3 KV for 1 st unit 210MW

 P.T. TV 3/2    15/ Ö3 :0.1/Ö3 :0.1/3 KV for 2 nd unit 210MW

Test Steps

  1. Place the socket ZZ10 in Z3 ( 1 st unit 210MW ) .

  2. Place the socket ZZ10 in Z1 ( 2 nd unit 210MW ) .

  3. Fix the timer terminals in (172, 187 ) at the rear of panel HH03 .

  4. Inject the voltage to points ( 11 , 12 ) of Z3 (R-S) ( 1 st unit 210MW ) .

  5. Inject the voltage to points ( 11 , 12 ) of Z1 (R-S)  ( 2 nd unit 210MW ) .

  6. Increase the voltage gradually till the red flag displays. At this moment let the timer start its action till it stops.

  7. Record the voltage and time of the first stage.

  8. Keep on increasing the voltage till the red flag of the 2 nd stage displays. Make the timer start its action till it stops. Timer terminals are ( 210 , 220 ) for 2nd stage .

  9. Record the results of the 1st and 2nd stage.

Under frequency + under voltage + Over frequency, F11X1

P.T.             TV 2/2                15/ Ö3 :0.1/Ö3 :0.1/3 KV

Test Steps

  1. Place the socket ZZ10 in Z2.

  2. Fix the timer terminals in ( 172 , 189 ) at the rear of panel HH04 .

  3. Connect the leads of the Frequency Converter set to points (11,12) (R-S) of Z2.

  4. Inject a voltage of 100V on (11,12)

  5. Change the frequency (raise) gradually till the red flag of the over frequency displays. ( set value) .

  6. Decrease the frequency till the red flag of the over frequency vanishes (reset value)

  7. Keep on decreasing the frequency till the red flag of the under frequency displays ( set value )

  8. Record the results.

  9. Note: the device gives almost the same results at any voltage lower than 100V.

Rotor Earth Fault Protection type F431, SKODA

It is an earth fault protection and measuring of insulation state of generator stator. It’s the protection with DC superposition.

It is of type GRX1 for unit 1

G1 Rotor Insulating Condition Checking

  1. Supply 220 Vdc between 15 ( -) & 16 (+) . Supply an a.c. voltage of 100V to terminals 12,13 .

  2. A 750 V will be measured on C4 then with " IZOL . G2 " pressed down.

  3. Connect resistance R = 68 kW ( by a resistance box) to terminal 1{ ground } and 4 . With " Isolation G2" push  button pressed down the 1A, 2A relays will start operating and the respective millimeter on the panel must indicate  20 mA in stabilized state / within about 3 sec.

  4. Repeat the test with 220 Va.c to terminals 12,14.

 

G1 Generator Earth Connection Test

  1. Supply 220 Vdc between 15 (-) & 16(+). Supply an a.c. voltage of 100 V to terminals 12 , 13.

  2. Terminals 1,5 are disconnected. With the press of " Test G2" push button, the B, 1B relays, J reed relay, C,D relays will close.

  3. The alarm relay will fall into the red flag and the 1B relay has about 2 sec slow release.

  4. Connect terminal 1 {ground}&5 through resistance about 220 ohm. The D relay closes with voltage lower than 50V. Check that contact on terminals 6 - 7 & 8 - 9 closes.

  5. Press down the Reset push button then the white flag displays again + 220 V on terminal 11, the B, 1B,J,C, and D relays will start operating. The NG2 magnetic indicator will start operating. Circuits of contacts on terminals 6-7 , 8-9 are opened . With + 220V connection to terminal 10, the magnetic indicator will release

It is an earth fault protection and measuring of insulation state of generator stator. It’s the protection with DC superposition.

It is of type GR100 for unit 2

 

Test steps

  1. Check the existence of the dc voltage ( 220 VDC ) .

  2. Fulfill the self – test of the device.

  3. Place the leads of the varying-resistance box on terminals (3,5) at the rear of panel hh06

  4. Decrease the value of resistance gradually till the red lamp –indicating both the first stage (R <) and the relay signal – switches on.

  5. Keep on decreasing the resistance till the red lamp –indicating both the second stage (R<</t) and the relay tripping value – switches on.

  6. Record the value of the resistance for the two stages & compare them with the previous ones.

Pro – Maintenance Procedures

  1. Loosen the leads of the testing instruments.

  2. Check the return of relay- wires and trip-leads to their original places and tighten them carefully.

  3. Press the main “RESET” button in HH01.

  4. Be sure that the relay is completely ready for service any time.

  5. Alienate the testing materials and equipments in addition to the caution signs and leave the place clean.

  6. Return the work permission.
