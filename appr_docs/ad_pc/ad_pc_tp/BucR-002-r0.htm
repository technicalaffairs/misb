Introduction

This document outlines the testing procedures P1, to be performed every six months as indicated by M6 in the above title block.

This procedure, details the steps required to check the proper operation of the Buchholz relay (DIN42566 ). 

The first draft initiated & tested by eng. Mahmoud El-Attar, accepted by eng. Saad El-Attar: protection general manager, revised and certified by eng. Mohamed Omran: development & quality surveillance general manager and MPIS Canal zone coordinator, and approved by eng. Al-Saied Abd El-Azez Alnagy: head sector of protection, measuring & testes.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued.

  • The qualified maintenance staff carry the suitable safety ID category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required,

  • Personal protective equipment must be used as safety shoes, and safety Helmet.

  • This procedure should be performed for off load lines, so line isolation is required.

  • Isolate the relay associated breaker failure system, disturbance alarm and fault recorder systems.

  • This procedure should be performed for off load lines so line isolation and work permit is required.

  • Isolate the relay associated breaker failure system, disturbance alarm and fault recorder systems.

Tools and Equipment

  • Tool kit

  • Avometer   

Work to be carried out

 

Relays Preparation

  1. Review technical reference material: Manufacturer's manual.

  2. Obtain outage for line protected by this relay. ( work permit must be issued ).

  3. Check that the bus and line disconnect switches are opened and tagged.

  4. Check that all cables supplying the relay and all wire connections are in good conditions.

relay Testing

The test is accomplished in two stages

  1. Buchholz relay Alarm operation.

  2. Buchholz relay Trip operation.

Buchholz relay Alarm operation

Slowly press down Buchholz Relay Test knob until the relay Alarm operate ( indicated by the flag and horn sound ).

Buchholz relay Trip operation

  1. Turn ON the transformer circuit breaker ( without voltage ).

  2. Turn the Buchholz Relay Test knob 60o while pressing down rapidly until the relay Trip operate ( indicated by the flag, horn sound and circuit breaker tripping ).

Return Protection Scheme and associated Equipment to service

  1. Restore the barker failure and fault recorders associated with this protection.

  2. Place a copy of the test results in the field maintenance file.
