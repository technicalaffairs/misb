Introduction

Contaminants in SF6 such as moisture and sulphur dioxide, the dielectric strength of the gas arc quenching abilities are affected.

This instrument is used for determining the value of sulphur dioxide and moisture content in SF6 value.

The system uses a flow of gas from the equipment through a chamber, pulled through a drager tube by a hand pump. The drager tube then registers the reading on the tube.

Information

Parts of the System
  1. Flow meter with magnetic holder and valve for adjusting gas flow
  2. Drager Accuro hand pump
  3. Break off facility for tubes
  4. Drager tubes for moisture and SO2
  5. Suitable adopter for specified champer

The Dragar Tube

  • Drager tubes are used with the Aerotester for measurement of sulphur dioxide and moisture content.
  • The tube has an indicating scale for mg/liter on the water vapour tube and parts per million on the SO2 tube.

 Dew point Tube

  1. Use the water vapour tubes .05 to 1 mg/L rating.
  2. Check expiry date on the tube to ensure they are within the required date.
  3. The tube contains a yellow indicating layer when the air or gas is pulled through the tube the indicating layer changes colour to blue in the presence of water vapour.
  4. Read the scale on the tube where the colour stops.
  5. The scale for dewpoint in mg/liter must be converted to degrees celsius by using the conversion chart.

Sulphur Dioxide Tubes

  1. The SO2 tubes scales 1 to 25 ppm or .5 to 5 ppm .
  2. Check expiry date on the tube to ensure they are within the required date
  3. Unlike the water vapour tube the SO2 tube tips can be broken without being in the plastic tube since there is no danger of contamination from SO2 in the atmosphere.
  4. The scale for ppm can be read directly from the tube without converting the reading.
  5. The tube contains a greyblue indicating layer
  6. When the air or gas is pulled through the tube the indicating layer changes colour to white in the presence of sulphur dioxide.
  7. Read the scale on the tube where the colour stops.
  8. This is the reading of SO2 in ppm.

Taking Dew Point Readings

  1. Dew point in the SF 6 GAS is the temperature at which water vapour starts to condense.
  2. The dewpoint temperature determines the maximum water content possible in the SF6 at that temperature.

Directives

Use of Tester

Dew Point Testing

 1.

Check the gas detection hand pump for leaks.

 2.

Check valve on the flow meter is closed.

 3.

Connect the flow meter to the equipment.

 4.

Select the proper tubes for the sampling.

 5.

Set SF6 gas flow to 2L/minute and let flow for 3 minutes.

 6.

The drager tubes are extremely sensitive to water vapour and may become contaminated. if subjected to atmospheric air when they are opened before placing into the tube.

Because of this the tubes must be first inserted into the plastic hose and then the tip must be broken while in the tube.

 7.

To accomplish this. The tube tip must first be scratched to ensure proper breakage. The tube break off facility may be used for this or the lower plate on the pump.

 8.

Insert the tube in either hole and rotate against the ceramic cutter until the tube is sufficiently scratched.

 9.

Insert the scratched tube into the hose on the flow meter closest to the flow meter.

 10.

Ensure the direction arrow is directed toward the pump.

 11.

Bend the tube until the tip breaks inside the hose.

12.

After this is accomplished break the other end of the tip and immediately insert it into the pump connection. Arrow side direction.

 13.

Make sure the pump counter is set to zero before test and pump three times until the counter registers3.

 14.

The measurement should take approximately 1.5 minutes at which the mg/L at the blue color can be read on the tube.

 15.

After the reading has been determined use the chart provided to compare the mg/L on the drager tube to the dewpoint given on the chart.

 16.

Record the readings for future reference

S02 Testing

 1.

Check the gas detection hand pump for leaks

 2.

Check valve on the flow meter is closed

 3.

Connect the flow meter to the equipment. Use the suitable adapter.

 4.

Select the proper tubes for the sampling

 5.

Set SF6 gas flow to 2L/ minute and let flow for 3 minutes

 6.

When testing for sulphur dioxide the breaking of the tips can be accomplished before putting the tube into the plastic hose because as mentioned before there is no risk of contamination.         (Caution after breakage tube tips are very sharp)

to accomplish this the tube tip must first be scratched to ensure proper breakage. 
The tube break off facility may be used for this or the lower plate on the pump.

 7.

Insert the tube in either hole and rotate against the ceramic cutter until the tube is sufficiently scratched.

 8.

Break off the tip by inserting in the hole in the bottom of the pump next to the strap.

 9.

Insert the tube with the broken tip into the hose on the flow meter closest to the flow meter.

 10.

Ensure the direction arrow is directed toward the pump.

 11.

After this is accomplished break the other end of the tip and immediately insert it into the pump connection.

 12.

Make sure the pump counter is set to zero before test and pump the pump until the counter registers 10 for a reading of 1 to 25 ppm or pump 20 times for a measurement of. 5 ppm.

 13.

Test time is approximately 3 minutes for 10 sucks, for 1 to 25 ppm scale, and 6 minutes for 20 sucks for the 0.5 to 5 ppm scale . The white portion of the tube is the ppm on the tube.

 14.

After the readings with SO2 are finished pump the hand pump several times to clear the SO2 from the pump.

 15.

Record readings for future reference
