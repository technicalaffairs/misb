Guideline for Total Dissolved Gas

The total ppm of all gases dissolved in oil should not exceed 700.  Higher totals indicate incipient problems that require specialized diagnosis.

Rate of Accumulation

In addition to guide lines listed above, the rate of gas generation also provides critical information about changes occurring in the transformer. Rapid build up indicates internal problems.

Monitoring of Dissolved Gas Levels

It is necessary to establish a computerized data base as a means of monitoring the absolute levels and rate of increase of dissolved gases. Rigorous monitoring will provide sample warning of incipient faults.

Analysis of combustible gas provides a breakdown of the composition of the gas. These guides indicate probable location and type of fault.

Note: 

In every case there may be other factors or variables that may alter the interpretation.

In the interpretation of gas analysis information, three considerations are crucial:

  1. The total amount of combustible gas

  2. The amount of each individual gas

  3. The rate of increase of combustible gases.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Acceptable Results for Basic Oil Tests

  1. Neutralization number is a measure of acids present in insulating oil usually as a result of oxidation.

    1. Acceptable level is equal to or less than 0.4 mg (KOH) per gram of insulating oil.

    2. If neutralization number is between 0.4 and 0.7 then reclaim using filler earth and vacuum treatment or completely replace oil, whichever is more economical.

    3. If level is more than 0.7 then replace oil.

  2. Dielectric strength determines the insulating ability of oil. This test is very sensitive to the presence, of impurities such as carbon, lint, fibre-and metal chips.

    1. Oil dielectric strength is good, if it is equal to or greater than 40 kv (by IEC and VDE standard mm gap).

    2. Minimum level for treatment is 25 kv by (IEC and VDE standards).

    3. Replace oil if dielectric strength is less than 25 kv

  3. Interfacial tension measures presence of soluble contaminants in oil.

    1. Minimum acceptable for new oil is 40 dynes/cm

    2. Minimum level of IFT for treatment is 17 dynes/cm

    3. Replace oil if  IFT < 15 dynes/cm or less.

  4. Moisture content of oil in ppm indicates the quantity of water in insulating oil measured in parts per million (ppm).

    1. A moisture level of 10 ppm or less is acceptable.

    2. If moisture level is more than 15 ppm then treat oil.
