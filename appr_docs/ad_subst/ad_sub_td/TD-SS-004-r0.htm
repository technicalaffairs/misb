Introduction

Sulphur Hexa Fluoride SF6 is a gas used in electrical apparatus as electrical insulation and arc quenching medium. It is important that tests are performed on the gas so that any change in its insulating properties can be corrected and rectified.

Contaminations

Common contaminants of SF6 gas are moisture and air. These contaminants can be present even in new gas purchased from the suppliers in standard steel cylinders.

Testing for Contaminations

Testing for moisture content is well established and various instruments such as moisture monitors or dew point testers are available for field-testing.

The presence of air in SF6 gas is detectable by using a trace oxygen analyzer. The concentration of oxygen will indicate the concentration of air, considering that oxygen is 21% of the air content.

Under electrical fault or arcing conditions some of the SF6 gas will decompose into other gases. When these more reactive gases combine with components of the internal parts of the switchgear in the presence of moisture, solid , highly corrosive by - products may by formed.

It is possible to introduce oil into SF6 gas when compressors are used to transfer gas from GIS equipment to gas carts and vice versa

Directives

Guidelines For Result Evaluation

Modern high voltage equipment is independent on high quality SF6 gas as a dielectric medium. Generally, arc by - product in a gaseous form may exist in small amounts in a disconnected compartment or circuit breaker, but are not expected in bus sections. Moisture content may vary in different gas sections of bus and other sections of higher pressure.

Moisture is dangerous when temperatures may decrease to a value where condensation on the internal support insulators could occur.

There is little reason for SF6 gas to degrade in service unless some fault is causing a discharge inside the bus, thus creating by - products. An alternately, insufficient vacuum treatment may leave moisture in the compartments which will later absorbed by the gas. There are also recorded cases of large amounts of air being introduced into SF6 equipment during filling because of leaks in the filling equipment.

SF6 Gas Specifications

Specifications for new SF6 gas should be used as a guide. They are as follows:

Moisture                                 15 PPM

Air                                         0.05 % by mass

Oil Content                             10 PPM

Hydrolysable Flurides (HF)     1 PPM

CF4                                         0.05 % by mass
