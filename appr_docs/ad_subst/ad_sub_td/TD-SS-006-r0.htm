Introduction

Sulphur Hexafluoride SF6 is a gas used in electrical apparatus as electrical insulation and arc quenching medium. It is important that tests are performed on the gas so that any change in its insulating properties can be corrected and rectified. The most common contaminant is moisture ingress, thereby decreasing the insulation properties of the gas.

The first draft initiated by the Canadian, Mr. Helmut and approved at 10 June 96 and revised at 4 Feb 2002.

Information

Test Instrument

The following directive/instructions are developed for MBW Dew point Measuring Instrument Dp9

Important

The most accurate test results will always be obtained at close to atmospheric pressure, a pressure reducing device should be used when testing SF6 gas under system pressure, Sampling lines must be clean an dry, the lines and instrument must be purged for several minutes with the gas to be tested before the actual test is carried out.

Directives

Testing Method

  1. Adjust the pressure reducing valve at the sampling point to approximately 1 bar above atmosphericpressure. Install the flowmeter on top of the instrument , connect the sample gas line starting from thesampling point to the Gas In connection on the instrument. Make sure that all connections are tight and that all valves are closed .

  2. Open the main valve of cylinder or gas compartment slightly Open flowmeter valve (8) completely, open control valve (5) slightly until the flowmeter indicatesa gas flow through the instrument of 30 L/H.

  3. Press Mains switch, the button should be illuminated . Set Mode switch to Measurement, the button is in the released position. The indicator needle will first remain in the No Dew position ( red field ). After a short time it will move into the green field (Correct position) and remain there. This indicates that the cooling capacity is sufficient and the instrument will correctly display the dew point. If the indicator needle remains inside the red field, the effective dew point lies below the cooling capacity of the instrument.

Note

Observe the digital read-out until it has stabilized, this can sometimes take a considerable amount of time, make sure the gas flow through the instrument constant throughout the test.

Once you have obtained a stable dew point reading , use the chart on the next page to convert your dew point reading to moisture content in ppm by volume or ppm by weight . With a ruler draw a straight line from 1 bar on the right column through the point in the center column of your obtained reading. Extend the line to the left column and read off moisture content of the gas under test in ppm by volume.

If there is no means of reducing the breaker or GIS compartment pressure , use the same test hook- up but ignore the pressure reducing valve. However you will now use the flowmeter valve to control the gas flow throug the instrument. First open valve (5) all the way , then open the valve (8) on the flowmeter slightly until you have the desired gas flow of 30 L/H .To obtain a ppm reading use the chart again but this time start with the exact pressure you are applying to the ( Gas In ) point of the instrument e.g .3.5 bars or 6.2 bars..

For further information on instrument calibration and cleaning of the internal dew point mirror consult the manufacturers instruction manual .

Interpetation Of Test Results

New SF6 gas should have a moisture content of approximately 125 ppmv equivalent to 15 ppmg. A reading of 500 ppmv (62ppmg) or higher indicates problems. The gas should be exchanged with new gas or in case of GIS equipment (a very large quantity of gas ) it is more economical to use the gas truck to re-condition the gas.
