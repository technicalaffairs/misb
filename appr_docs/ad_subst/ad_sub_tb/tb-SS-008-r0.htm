Purpose of this Paper

This paper gives information, recommendations and ideas about how to recycle gas and minimize release of SF6 gas today, creating gas inventories of filled-in and remaining gas quantities, about various gas processing units and test equipment for professional gas handling.

Introduction

The rules for handling and disposal of SF6 gas and Its mixtures with other gases are getting more ecologically conscious and thus more stringent. Personnel must be aware of the international policies regarding gas-handling etc., in order to perform  installation and maintenance without letting gas out into the atmosphere. It is therefore necessary to have professional staff and equipment available.

SF6 in electrical power equipment is used in an enclosed manner end must not be released into the atmosphere. While SF6 itself is not consumed in significant quantities. Its performance can degrade due to contamination by air, moisture, decomposition products etc. It is desirable both from an ecologic and economic point of view to keep SF6 at a low contamination level by careful handling, so that it can be reclaimed and reused many times.

SF6 can be continually reused during maintenance, repair and decommissioning as long as the use criteria are fulfilled. SF6 gas can also be transferred from equipment, being phased out into newly installed equipment. It thus goes through a continuous cycle of reuse. Such a systematic reuse of SF6 requires that the gas be kept at a quality level at which it can perform its functions. This can be achieved by proper and professional handling and reclaiming, which will be d6scribed in following chapters.

As a general rule one shall always refer to the manuals for more arid detailed informations.

    Installation/Gas Filling

The first gas handling procedure normally is evacuating and gasfilling, except for some apparatus, which are delivered with full, operating pressure (MV) see Equipment item/types of apparatus/medium voltage  (MV) paragraph 

To reach the quality of SF6 gas and the rated pressure, The gas fillings must be done with gas processing equipment having a sealing system designed to withstand both pressure and vacuum to avoid contamination of SF6 by air. Enclosures must be evacuated before SF6 is filled in.

 

    Repair and Maintenance

Severe years experience of maintenance work on SF8 apparatus tells us that we seldom have to open SF6 enclosures. It is more probable that we have to work with the external parts of the operating device. But sometimes we have to open a SF6 enclosures for maintenance or, repair. In that case one has to be prepared and have competent personnel with a good knowledge of gas handling, good equipment and perfect personal protection. 

Although the details of gas handling on site or in factories may vary depending on the apparatus volume, gas pressure an opening the apparatus, the basic handling procedure will be more or less the same, 

Detailed Information about gas reclaiming connections can be found in Service cart's manuals,

       Categories of Gas

Four SF6 gas categories will be distinguished In this chapter: CIGRE [51 

New gas:            

Gas supplied in, cylinders by the original SF6 gas producers and complying with a standard for new SF6 such as the IEC Standard 376 or a national standard e,g, ASTMD 2742-92.Once the gas has left the cylinders in which it, was supplied by the producer, it has to be considered as USED GAS and has to be  assigned one of the three f0owing categories.

Non-arced gas:

Gas that has been handled in any way but has not, experienced arcing. The major contaminants in non-arced gas may be air (mainly introduced  by handling) and humidity  (mainly described from surfaces) Small quantities of SF6  decomposition products (typically by the producer, in the 100 ppmv range)  may also be  present when partial Discharges have occurred in the gas.

Normally arced gas: 

Gas recovered from switchgear compartments after normal switching operation. Normally arced gas may contain in addition to air and humidity:
- the Inert gas CF, generated by arc erosion of polymers
- corrosive gaseous SF6 decomposition products less than about 200 ppmv as residues which have not been removed by absorbers.
- Solid decomposition products, mainly metal fluorides and tungsten oxifluorides, usually referred switching dust".        

Heavily arced gas:
Gas recovered from equipment In which failure arcing has occurred in this case, high levels of solid and gaseous contaminants have to be expected, the gaseous contaminants may reach levels of several % vol.

SF6 specific gas connections and self-sealing valves, also prevent accidental gas loss or contamination with air by false handling.

SF6 gas is delivered In storage tanks or cylinders (bottles) depending on the volume of the equipment. The gas filling procedure can be:

From container as gas        =>   as gas            => Gas Cart          =>     Equipment

From container as gas        =>   as gas                 directly             =>     Equipment

Warning:

"Never heat a gas cylinder with an open flame, Energize evaporators/heaters only when transferring the gas, Do not invert the gas cylinders to speed up the transfer of SF6"

 

Gas filling from tank bottle to equipment, will leave a rest of gas inside the bottle, which was not reported in the past The reasons for the remaining SF6 in the bottle could be:

   - Gas tanks-/bottle can not be emptied completely because overpressure) in the tank 'is needed to transfer the gas.
   - The quantity of delivered gas to site exceeds the needed quantity.

The most important thing is therefore to weight and report the quantity of gas remaining after completed work and to keep it under control until has boon sent back to the supplier.

Suggestions for gasfilling:
    - Use only sealing systems with approved specific Connections, (valves adapters)
    - Weigh gas bottles and after filling and report remaining gas quantity.

Processing Solid SF6 Decomposition Products

Equipment

The better the design and quality of the various components of an SF6 processing equipment, the lower the probability  of introducing undesired contamination's into the SF6 gas, An investment in good quality equipment may thus be justified by the 
saving of cost for special procedures which may otherwise be required to recycle SF6.

    Gas Handling

The basic scheme of SF6 reclaiming equipment is shown in Fig. 3-1, which also illustrates the used terms, It consists of a compressor which recovers gas from the equipment, passes it through a humidity and a particle filter to remove dust and corrosive decomposition products and compresses It Into a storage container or  cylinder. In the case of heavily contaminated gas, an additional preflilter (humidity and dust filter) Is needed, An additional component of the gas handling unit is a vacuum pump which serves to 6ir while evacuating the equipment before filling it with SF6.

      The major performance characteristics of a gas-reclaiming device are:
      
      - Easy transportability
      - Storage capacity
      - High storage pre5sure (Liquefaction, 50 bar)
      - Very low recovery pressure (maximum 50 mbar residual SF6 pressure)
      - High recovery speed
      - High evacuation speed (down to 1 mbar residual air pressure)
      - Fail-safe operation control (preferably automatic)
      - Easy and safe filter etxchange.

Prefilter for Arced and Heavily Arced SF6 Gas (Humidity and Dust Filter)

Prefilter must be connected betw8en the SF6 insulated equipment and the SF6 declaimer to keep heavily arced gas from damaging reclaimed and from contaminating reclaimed SF6 already stared in the gas cart's storage vessel.

The characteristics of prefilter units should be in accordance with table 1 of the appendix and should not contain components that form stable gases. They should have self sealing pressure and vacuum tight connections at Input and output and should be designed for easy exchange of filter material (i.e. cartridges), preferably without the need to remove any connections, thus reducing leakage. They should be portable for ease of use.

Hoses used to recover arced gas are not allowed to be used with non arced or new gas If possible they should have different Color (e.g. orange marking) to avoid mistakes.

Filter elements 8hould be adequately sized to remove moisture, gas by-products and particles larger then 10 micron in size (as a second barrier for trapping particles which may have beer) transmitted by a non-efficient particle filter).

Recommendation:            Always use Prefilter for safety.       

   Filter Unit

A particle filter should be placed directly behind the input of a gas declaimer. It should be capable of holding back particles larger than I micron In size, to prevent th8m entering mechanical parts of the gas declaimer and/or being discharged into the air via the vacuum pump, It should facilitate easy exchange of filter material (i.e. cartridges), preferably without the need to n5move any connections thus reducing gas losses.

These filter elements should be adequately sized to remove as well moisture and reactive decomposition products,

    Oil Trap (Filter)

Only in case gas reclaimers do not have oil free pumps and compr6ssors, they require oil traps at the input of the storage container,

    Compressors

For reclaimers used by ABB, there are two different systems. One with vacuum and piston compressors and another system combining the compressor with the vacuum pump (see enclosed diagram of different designs).

A piston compressor alone can only achieve a relatively low differential pressure, which would limit the residual recovery pressure and the recovery speed. Optimum performance is achieved with a combination of a piston compressor and a vacuum compressor in series,

    Vacuum Pumps

Most vacuum pumps are oil lubricated and produce some oil mist at their exhaust. The oil mist should be collected in standard oil trap. If a vacuum pump is used in the SF6 circuit of a reclaimer air tightness between vacuum- and compression section mu5t be ensured.

     Evaporator / Heater

An evaporator is useful to accelerate gas filling. It increases the gas pressure in the storage container. The evaporator should be controlled by a thermostat. The outlet of any storage container should be placed on the top of the container so that no liquid SF6 can flow out of the container when filling the gas into equipment.

    Gas and Hose Connections

Gas and hose connections should be self sealing to prevent air, moisture and dust from entering the gas processing equipment, Those gas and hose connections need to be both pressure- and vacuum tight,

 

    Gas Piping and Pipe Junctions

Gas piping and pipe junctions must be of high quality and should preferably use a metal reusable sealing system, proven in its performance with SF6 and its decomposition by-products. The following characteristics are essential:

  • Pressure and vacuum tight

  • Vibration proof

  • Reusable (indefinite refitting possible)

  • Temperature change resistant

All components (gauges, valves, filters, etc.) should be securely mounted to the frame of the gas cart, such that pipe work does not have to support them. This prevents stress cracks causing either gas losses or inadvertent gas contamination.

Heavy components (i.e. compressors and vacuum pumps) must be "shock-proof"  mounted and be connected to the fixed pipe work via flexible connections.

An important performance feature of the declaimer is its size and transportability. For small gas volumes equipment may be transported in a oar and for larger volumes a trailer mounted self contained assembly may be required,

    Control Instruments

Control Instruments such as manometers, vacuum meter, pressure reduction valves, non-return valves must be installed on the reclaimer, Manometers are required to control the compressors, the filling pressure of the equipment and in the bottle/tank

Types of Apparatus

    Medium Voltage (MV)

Medium voltage SF6 apparatus normally have a small SF4 volume. SF6 handling is rarely required, Normally a now pole or chamber Is simply exchanged on site against the used one which is brought to the factory for further action. Only in few 3-phase enclosed apparatus gas may need handling on site.

For this purpose, small and handy transportable reclaimers are available at, for example, Dilo, Transorfilter and Cryoquip.

SF6 Switchgear (in air insulated substations / AIS)

AIS apparatus with SFG or gag mixtures normally have a gas content volume of > 4k9 and require portable, easy to handle and rather light reclaimers. It facilities to heat and cool are available, this will be a advantage.

Reclaimers for AIS are available from Dilo and Transorfilter.

    Gas Insulated Switchgear (GIS)

For gas handling on GIs with largo quantities of SF6, reclaimers with high recovery and evacuation speed and high storage capacity are required.

Gas handling in GIs normally is performed by competent specialists from service departments of GIs producers or utilities.

Adequate reclaimers are available from DILO and Transorfilter,

    Gas Quality Control

To asses its reusability, the reclaimed SF6 gas has to be checked for Its compliance with the purity requirements of Table 2, Appendix by measuring its residual impurity levels. 

 

 

The measurement devices applied for this purpose are.

  • Hygrometer to determine the dewpoint / humidity

  • SF6 percentage meter to determine the content of non-SF6 gases such as air N2 and CF4

  • Reactive decomposition product meter to determine the content of toxic/corrosive

  • decomposition products, mainly HF, SO2 and SOF2 

All above-mentioned measurement devices are available on the market as portable equipment. We recommend the following features for this equipment,

  • Portable and easy to operate and handle

  • Designed for measurements in the range around the maximally admissible Impurity levels

  • Negligible SF6 release

  • Minimum measurement time.

We specifically recommend:
- Hygrometer:                         MBW, Panametrics DILO, COSA
- SF6 percentage meter:         DILO
- Decomposition products:      DILO, Draeger, Auer

        => All the above test equipment need a flush of SF6 for testing! This causes SF6 gas release. 
             The released quantity per measurement should be minimal.
