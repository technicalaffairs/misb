Bulletin:
 Checking of a Hot Spot in Winding of Russian Power Transformers 132/33/11KV and Refilling Oil

Document No.:  TB-SS-009-r0

Issued to:    Cairo 500 ME zone

Status:              APPROVED

 

Approved Date:        09 Sep 2003

Date to be Reviewed:   Sep 2008

Introduction

This Bulletin initiated by eng. Khairy Isehaq; General director of Cairo 500kv substation, Middle Egypt Zone EETC.

The purpose of this bulletin is to provide field staff with a general information regarding winding checking the presence of a hot spot caused wherever and refilling with oil.

Information

Most of the information in this document pertains specifically to Russian Transformers, however can be used as a guideline for other manufacture types. However if available, it is advisable to use the actual information specified by the manufacture of the transformer under test.

Recommendation

Following are general guidelines for performing a check for winding about hot spot and refilling oil as the follows procedures:

Safety Precaution 

  • A work order must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable ID safety category,

  • Transformer isolation and proper grounding on all its sides is required.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area,

  • Wear your safety helmet, safety shoes, and safety belt when cleaning suspension Insulator.

  • There is a danger falling into the containment pit when working near large transformer.

  • It is recommended to use only fiberglass (PVC) ladders when working in the switchyard.

Environmental Recommendation

  • Before starting the cleaning of all porcelain surfaces of all transformer wear your breathing mask to protect lung tissues from the dust particulate.

  • Don't burn the cloth used in cleaning. Collect it and send it to the dump.

  • Fix any oil leaks to prevent pollution and save money of wasted oil.

  • Oil leaks to the ground have very bad impact on the environment, mainly on soil and ground water and eventually on the ecosystem. "It takes only I liter of Hydrocarbon spilled in an aquifer to render 1,000,000 liters of potable water useless for human consumption

Tools and Equipment

  • Safety helmet, safety belt, safety shoes, and tool set

  • Fiberglass PVC ladder.

  • Voltage indicator, rubber gaskets

  • Portable earth for discharging, new transformer oil

  • Fine cloth for cleaning purposes, Silicagel,

Work to be carried out

In Case of Presence of a Hot Spot Caused Wherever Carry out the Following Steps:

1.             Get a tank whose volume exceeds the volume of oil in the transformer.

2.             Flower the oil level below the radiators upper flanges to avoid leakage.

3.             Remove the safety pipe and block its hole on the transformers cover by a flange.

4.             Remove the explosion membrane of top changer and block its place.

5.             Remove the gas piper on the transformer cover as well as motors of cooling fans.

6.             Remove the 132, 33, 11kv through bushings as well as the neutral bushings of 132 and 33kv.

7.            Disconnect the radiators and both of the slicagil filters and block all opening on the cover using flanges also disconnect the vertical and horizontal connections of the top    hanger motor drive .

8.             Disconnect the gas protection device the flow meter and all secondary terminals so as not to hinder removing the casing.

9.            Make a closed oil cycle using an oil treatment plant to raise the oil temperature than that of the ordinary atmospheric temperature by twenty degrees so as to minimize the winding absorption to humidity.

10.         Connect the valve on the transformer top to a silica gel filter to dry air replacing the oil drawn using pump of a capacity 100 ton/hour.

11.         Release the bolts connecting the casing and the base .

12.        Release bolts connecting the casing of the lop changer and that of the transformer freeing the top changer to be able to remove the transformer casing

13.         Use a 16 ton crane and two examined steel wire to lift the 7 ton casing up carefully.

14.        Check the transformer internal winding and tighten the iron core then treat all the hot spots that caused the operation of the gas protection.

15.         Replace all the transformers rubber gaskets by new ones.

16.         After work is completed restore the casing and the tap changer to their initial positions.

Preparation for Refilling the Transformer with Oil

1.            To achieve pressure equilibrium between the tap changer isolating cylinder and the transformer tank, use two nibble one on the top of the tap changer insulating cylinder and the other on the transformer tank , and connect the two with a reinforced  hose capable of supporting vacuum.

2.            Use an oil plant to evacuate the transformer gradually till the vacuum eventually reaches (-o.98) bar is at least for one hour to protect the transformer casing from damage.

3.             Continue evacuating maintaining the same vacuum pressure for at least 8 hours.

4.            Pump the oil through the bottom valve upwards till the oil level reaches a distance of 30 cm below the transformers top cover so that the oil would not be withdrawn outwards by the vacuum.

5.             Retain the vacuum for at least another two hours in order to get rid of all air bubbles in the oil.

6.             Stop the evacuating machine and connect the valve to an auxiliary silica gel filter in order to let in dry to replace the vacuum.

7.             Complete refilling the transformer with oil .

8.             After maintenance is completed  perform, the megger tests , tan d ,  R.D.C and turns ratio.
    - In case of unsatisfied results repeat again the previous steps for good drying
    - Reconnect all previous disconnected parts.

9.             Then prepare the transformer to be energized.
