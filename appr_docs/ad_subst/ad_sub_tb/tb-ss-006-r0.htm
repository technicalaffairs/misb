 Changing cracked flanges for hollow
support and arcing chamber insulators		 Approved Date:     29 Dec 1997
Date to be Reviewed:    Dec 2002


Introduction

    The purpose of this bulletin is to alert substation maintenance staff to defective flanges on hollow support and arcing chamber insulators of air blast circuit breakers 66kv type 3P72- Rade Concar.

    In Delta Zone after some years of putting this type of circuit breakers in operation, cracks were discovered on the flanges connecting the hollow support insulators to the flanges of the air tanks, and the hollow support insulators to the arcing chamber insulators. These cracks appeared between the stud holes connecting the flanges together.

Information

    The flanges of (the hollow insulators) the arcing chamber insulators and there stud bolts are manufactured from aluminum alloys which tented to corrode after several years exposure to weather conditions. Cracks are formed at points, on weak points of the flanges between the holes of the stud bolts. The cracks (if small) allow air to leak, however if left not repaired may cause the insulator to explode.

Recommendations

    It is recommended defective flanges be replaced with new one as soon as possible after they have been discovered.

    Middle Delta Zone has been successful in manufacturing new flanges and their stud bolts from copper and have changed most of the aluminum flanges. These repairs have resulted in good reliable services for several years. They don’t expect further problems in this area.

Procedure for Replacing Cracked Flanges       Drawings refer to mpis doc no. CB-007-r0

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued.

  • CB must be isolated and proper earthling at the two sides of the CB is required.

  • Safety fence with caution marks around the work area is required.

  • Cut off the DC voltage.

  • Wearing safety helmet is required.

  • Use only fiberglass ladders.

  • Close the through valve (1 fig 1).
  • Open the drainage valve (24 fig 1) to drain the air out of the tank and be sure that the breaker is free of pressure. The pressure can be read at the control pressure gauge placed in the control cubicle.
  • Personnel carrying out this work should be trained and qualified or directly supervised by some one who is.

Tools and Equipment
  • Mobile crane-Triple sheaved-Sufficient and suitable polypropylene rope to lower arcing chambers and hollow support insulators to ground - suitable steel shackles.
  • One set of metric open end box wrenches size range 13 mm to 24 mm, Adjustable spanner, One set of screwdrivers and Torque wrench.
  • Fine file, Fine emery paper, Wipers.
  • Hammer ½ kg, Hammer 2 kg, Rubber hammer, suitable steel punch to drive out studs.
  • Sufficient numbers of stud bolts used in assembling the flanges.
  • Tin cans to hold bolts, nuts, studs, washers…etc.
  • Complete set of gaskets (8 gaskets for every pole).
  • Complete set of flanges (Lower and upper flanges of arcing chambers insulators and hollow support insulators.
  • Electrical disk saw, hand saw.
  • Petroleum jelly (Vaseline).

Special Safety Precautions

  • Before working ensure the crane operator is fully aware of neighbouring apparatus and the safe limits of approach to over head lines or bus-bars.
  • The mobile crane must be grounded (earthed).
  • Don't use the mobile crane for the actual lift but attach a rope block and use the crane hook as a gin pole

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Remove the protective cap at the end of arcing chamber that covers the valves (30, 31, 32 fig 2). Use spanner 19

  2. Disconnect the copper tubes connecting the insulating tube ( 20 , 26 fig 2 ) and each of the valves (30 , 22 , 32 fig 2) and the injector (17 fig 2) Use spanner 24

  3. Disconnect the insulating tube (20, 26 fig 2).

  4. With the polypropylene rope blocks hang the arcing chamber with the crane hook.

  5. Release the nuts of the 12 stud bolts connecting the lower flange of the hollow support insulator to the flange of air tank. Use spanner 17

  6. Using the crane hook as a gin pole lower the complete pole (arcing chamber and hollow support insulator) till the lower flange of the hollow support insulator becomes in your reach to easily handled, then dismantle it. Close the tank flange with any provisional cover to prevent ingress of dirt or any thing in the tank. Support insulator and arc chamber should be lowered slowly as to prevent porcelain from shocks.

  7. To get the flange out of the porcelain of the hollow support insulator, hammer at the 3 piece split ring using a rubber hammer to get them out, then the flange will be free.

  8. Move the complete pole (arcing chamber and hollow support insulator) aside and put it on a wooden base.

  9. Release the 12 stud bolts connecting the upper flange of the hollow support insulator and the lower flange of fixed contact base. Use spanner 17

  10. Move the complete arcing chamber with the fixed contact base aside and lower it on the wooden base.

  11. Release the 12 stud bolts connecting the lower flange of the arcing chamber to the upper flange of fixed contact base. Use spanner 17 and tube spanner 17

  12. Hammer at the 3-piece split ring using a rubber hammer to get the flange out. If the stud bolts are difficult to release because of the weather conditions, saw the 2 studs in the direction of the fixed contact outlet valve using a hand saw, then saw the other 10 studs using an electrical disc saw.

  13. Hammer at the remaining of the stud bolts using suitable steel punch to drive them out from upper flange of fixed contact base.

  14. Exchange the flange with another new one and erect it in its place after cleaning the 3 split ring surfaces with fine emery paper.

  15. Erect the lower flange of arc camber to the upper flange of fixed contact base with their 12 stud bolts. Use spanner 17

  16. Release the nuts of the 6 stud bolts connecting the base of entire moving contact to the upper flange of arcing chamber insulator gradually by half turn all around                            Use spanner 19

  17. Pull out this base with entire moving contact exactly parallel to the chamber centerline, taking care that the porcelain screen head would not be broken.                  Use spanner 17 and tube spanner 17

  18. To seperate the upper flange of arcing chamber from the porcelain, hammer at the 3-piece split ring using a rubber hammer to get them out, then the flange will be free.

  19. Replace this flange with a new one, after cleaning the 3 split ring surfaces with fine emery paper

  20. Replace the entire moving contact in its place into the arcing chamber and tighten the 6 studs. Use spanner 19

  21. Exchange the lower flange of arcing chamber with another new one and erect it with the fixed upper flange of the fixed contact base after cleaning the 3 split ring surfaces with fine emery paper and tighten their 12 studs. Use spanner 17

  22. Raise the arcing chamber with complete fixed contact up and erect it to the upper flange of the hollow support insulator with their 12 stud .

  23. Raise the complete pole (arcing chamber and hollow support insulator) up over the flange of the air tank, then place the new flange with its 3 split ring after cleaning them with fine emery paper and erect it to the flange of air tank with their 12 stud bolts.

  24. Erect the control insulator on its place and connect all control tubes to their previous places. Use spanners 24 and 19

  25. Measure the support column insulation by megger.

Notes: Tighten all nuts with torque wrench to their correct value.
