Preventive maintenance is a cycle of planned inspections, tests, overhauls and servicing activities designed to keep the Network equipment in good operating condition and avoid breakdowns. Our goal is to achieve satisfactory service from our electrical equipment, as economically as possible. We intend to accomplish this through the development and implementation of a series of procedures and inspections.

The following designation and definitions are used in Ontario Hydro to provide a coding system for maintenance activities. The coding system will be very important when EEHC and it's 13 companies moves to apply a computerized maintenance work management system.

Information

The procedures fall into four different categories. These are defined and described in the list of definitions in the following section. The procedures are given the following designations:
        - Equipment inspections (I1, I2, I3)
        - Minor Maintenance (P1, P2, P3)
        - Major Maintenance (P4)
        - Capital Maintenance (C1)
        - Equipment Modification Procedures (MOD)

The intention is to have up to P-4 Technical procedure written for each major class of equipment. Although not always specifically stated in the Station Inspections and Maintenance Procedures, the requirement to make necessary repairs or at least to report deficiencies is always implied and expected.

While it is recognized that maintenance frequencies must be adjusted, at times, to coordinate equipment outages, efforts should be made to avoid exceeding the recommended time between overhauls.

Following are definitions of the various maintenance activities and inspections performed on Network Equipment:

 

Equipment Inspections (I1, I2, I3)

The Inspections detailed in MPIS documents comprise checks and tests on station equipment. They may require readings to be taken or equipment condition reports to be made. Usually the work can be completed without an outage. The designations " I1, I2 and I3" are used to identify Inspections of increasing complexity on any one piece of equipment. Where only one Inspection is required the designation II is always used.

An I2 Inspection incorporates the steps from the I1, along with additional checks or tests. Similarly the I3 Inspection includes the II and I2 checks plus additional items. The monthly station inspection should include all the items designated (M1), while the quarterly station inspection will include the (M1) items plus those designated (M3). There are also a few inspections designated as daily (D1) and weekly (W1).

Minor Maintenance Procedures (P1, P2, P3)

The Minor Maintenance Procedures detailed comprise visual checks, tests and equipment maintenance and servicing work of a relatively minor nature. Usually an outage is required to complete the work. The designations "P1, P2 and P3" are used to identify the different Minor Maintenance Procedures required on any one piece of equipment.

The " 1, 2 or 3 " do not represent time intervals but rather increasing degrees of complexity. As with the Inspections, the P2 incorporates the P1 and the P3 incorporates the P1 and P2.

Major Maintenance Procedures (P4)

The Major Maintenance Procedures "P4", outline the steps required for dismantling and overhauling station equipment. An outage is always required to complete the work.

Capital Maintenance Procedures (C1)

This class of a Routine Maintenance Procedure is used to extend the life span of aging equipment. Usually the "C1" Equipment Rebuild Procedure comprises a P-4 overhaul plus extra work to replace worn out components such as the bearings in Oil Circuit Breakers (OCB'S), the "O" rings in Air Blast Breakers (ABB'S), the contacts in Under Load tapchangers (ULTC'S), etc. Like the P4 overhauls. The content of the "C1's" is defined by the head office functional (operations) department in consultation with Zone staff.

The extra C1 work should be done along with the P4, during the same outage. Of course the timing of this work must be adjusted by the severity of the service duty, and actual condition of the equipment.

Equipment Modification Procedures (MOD's)

"MOD's" are procedures, which outline special modification work required to overcome some known weakness in the equipment. A Zone in conjunction with Head Office Functional Department or committee usually initiates them. This nonstandard work is detailed in various Technical Directives and or Bulletins each of which require a modification to a specific type of equipment.

Scheduling Maintenance Activities

The above definitions describe the degree of inspection/maintenance to be performed on Network equipment. However to establish a consistent process for maintaining equipment it will be necessary to properly schedule these events.

Inspection and Maintenance Schedules will be developed to show the frequency requirements for each listed inspection and maintenance procedure. These will represent the current "standards of maintenance" which is required on the electrical power system. These frequencies are cross-referenced in the introduction and/or title of each procedure, e.g. (P1-Y1), (P4-Y6), etc.

The Inspection and Maintenance Schedule will be issued in a separate directive at a later date.

While it is recognized that this standard may not always be met to the letter, any temporary reduction in maintenance must be corrected as soon as possible. Should it become necessary for anything more than a minor, short term deferral of routine maintenance, the responsible Head Office Functional Department should be consulted and a plan worked out to resolve the problem.
