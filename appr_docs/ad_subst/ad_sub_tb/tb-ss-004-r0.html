Introduction

This bulletin outlines the method for testing the power transformer after operation of flow meter protection

Information

Flow meter protection: 
It operates when the oil velocity exceeds 0.9 m/sec. This occurs due to the presence of a hot spot inside the tape changes insulator cylinder causing expansion of oil and its flow through the flow meter protection device leading to tripping the transformer.

Tripping might be caused by overloading which indicates the presence of a large quantity of carbon inside the tape. Changer cylinder causing bad contact.                    
                                                                    OR: 
If the flow meter operates due to short circuit on the lines 132kv the short circuit current would depend on the distance between point of shortage and the transformers. In such case the valve of the short circuit current is too high and increases as that distance decreases.

Recommendations

After operation of the flow meter protection, you should carry out the following procedure:

Safety Precautions
  • A work permit must be issued.
  • In both cases the transformer must not be switched on again unless the following precautions tests are taken
  • The transformer is entirely disconnected and the earthing took place in the three directions.
  • Worker must put on protective helmet .
  • A safety belt must be also put on while disconnecting the 500kv terminals.
  • Worker must have a comprehensive knowledge of the required job.
  • A safety fence with caution marks surrounded the work area is required.

 

Tools and Equipment
  • Complete tool kit/set, including a torque wrench,
  • Lubricating grease (light grade),
  • Fine cloth for cleaning purposes,
  • Micro Ohm tester,
  • Megger tester.

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Disconnect the terminals of the 500kv 132kv ,11k.v or primary and secondary terminals and neutral 
    consecutively.

  2. Perform a megger test for R15 and R60 consecutively given that the ratio R60 / R15 must not be less than 1.3

  3. Check the R.D.C at the primary winding especially at the point where the transformer operated immediately 
    before being switched off and compare it with the manufacturer certificate and repeat for all tap - changer steps.

  4. Replace the tap changer oil by a pre-amine quantity of the same of oil previously used as following :

    • Get a press filter and close the valve between the conservator tank and the tape changer.
    • Remove the tap changer membrane at the top of the tap changer cover.
    • Drain the oil through the suction pipe using a transparent hose in order to observe the oil colour 
      of the oil withdrawn eventually you ‘ll find that the oil turns black due to presence of carbon.
    • To clean all contacts from carbon press oil hot at 80oC down words using a filter press , 
      while tap changer is operated up and down.
    • Withdraw the oil once again observing its colour till it retrains to its original colour.
    • Restore the membrane once more to its initial position and tighten it carefully. Then the valve between the conservator tank and tape changer is reopened.
    • Re-detect the R.D.C at every step of the top changer and compare with the manufacturer certificate
       

    5. N.B : You’ll find at that the value is now the same as that in the certificate if not you have to make capital maintenance 
    on the tap changer and change the burned contact.
     

  5. Examine an oil sample chemically and electrically
  6. Prepare the transformer to be put in service once more.
