Introduction

This document outlines the inspection checklist I2, which must be carried out every 3 months as indicated by M3 in the above title block. The first draft has been initiated by eng. El-Sayed Abdel Moneam El-Geddawy and tested at 19 May 2002 for charger no.1 at Sheraton El Matar 66kv Substation, Cairo zone.

 

The Charger technical data:

Input voltage 3*380 v AC,    Output voltage 220 v DC,     Output current 50A DC

Input voltage tolerance + 15%,    Input  frequency tolerance + 5%

Safety Precautions

  • Work order must be issuedand the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Switch off the power supply,

  • No smoking is permitted,

  • Wearing goggles, safety shoes, helmet, and rubber gloves is necessary

Tools and Equipments

  • Digital voltmeter

  • Suitable tool set

  • Vacuum Cleaner or blower

  • Brush

Work to be carried out 

  1. Normal operation of the charger is putting it on AUTOMATIC POSITION. In this case it must charge the battery with floating (normal) charging voltage

  2. For a power failure of a period < 5 minutes, the charger will remain in FLOAT mode on return of power

  3. For a power failure of a period > 5 minutes, the charger will automatically switches to the HI-RATE mode, when power returns and continue for a period before returns to FLOAT (according to the adjustable period)

  4. To check the previous characteristics, switch of the power supply of the charger for a period < 5 minutes (e.g. 4 minutes), and observe (after switching on power supply) that the charger continued working in FLOAT mode

  5. Check the operation  FLOAT voltage using digital DC voltmeter (must be 245 v ± 1 %)

  6. Then switch of the power supply of the charger for a period > 5 minutes (e.g. 6 minutes) and observe (after switching on power supply) that the charger transferred to HI-RATE mode

  7. Check the operation HI-RATE voltage using digital DC voltmeter (must be 253.75 v ± 1 %)

     Turn off Charger and Then:

  8. Repeat maintenance steps that performed monthly according MPIS doc no. Ch-010-r0

  9. Check condition of internal components

  10. Check wire connections and be sure that there is no loosing

  11. Check general conditions of the charger such as gaskets, vents, etc

  12. Clean the charger (from inside and outside) using blower
