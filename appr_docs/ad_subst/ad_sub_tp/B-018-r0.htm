Introduction

This document outlines the inspection checklist I3, which must be carried out on an annually bases as indicated in the above title block by Y1. The first draft has been initiated by eng. El-Sayed Abdel Moneam El-Geddawy and tested at 10 June 2002 for battery no.1 at Sheraton El Matar 66kv Substation, Cairo zone.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • No smoking in battery room or any things leads to fire is permitted
  • Using safety equipments, and isolated tools, safety goggles, rubber gloves, rubber apron and safety boots is necessary
  • Don't put any tools or metal things on the top of battery (to avoid short circuit danger)
  • Don't use the same tools for maintenance in alkaline cells and acidic cells  

Environmental Precautions

  • Wear your breathing mask if you have to use any chemical materials or solvent because it is dangerous, it will expose lungs to very dangerous and carcinogenic chemicals

  • Don't burn the cloth used in cleaning, collect it and send it to the dump.

Tools and Equipment

  • Personal standard safety and isolated tool set, goggles, gloves, and apron
  • Pieces of clothes, Brushes, Hydrometer
  • Voltmeter (Voltmeter with parallel resistance is preferred)
  • Maintenance Record Data sheets (MRDS) ref. step 8

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Repeat the monthly maintenence according to MPIS document no. B-017-r0

  2. Record the density of electrolyte in each cell (the standard values according to manufacturer are between 1.19 & 1.12 gm / cm3), write the values in the maintenance card.

  3. Record the temperature of electrolyte in each cell.

  4. Disconnect AC supply to battery charger.

  5. Immediately after disconnecting AC power supply, Record overall battery voltage every 15 minutes for one hour (i.e 4 times), after that Record overall battery voltage every one hour for 7 hours (i.e 7 times)

  6. Measure the voltage in the end of the previous step (i.e 8 hours) < if the voltage is below 215 volts, put the battery under High Charging for 6 hours.

  7. After finishing the step 5, and battery voltage is still above 215V put the battery under normal charging then put it in service.

  8. Record all the readings you have read, Click here to view and print the MRDS
