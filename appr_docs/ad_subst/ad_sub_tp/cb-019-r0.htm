Introduction

This procedure details the steps required to check the value of dew point of the Sulphur Hexa fluoride (SF6) gas. It is important that test is performed on the gas so that any change in its properties can be noticed by measuring the moisture value in it.

The most accurate test results will always be obtained at close to atmospheric pressure, when testing (SF6) gas under system pressure you have to use a pressure reducing valve and adjust it at the sampling point to approximately 1 bar above atmospheric pressure to obtain actual and accurate results.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required 

  • Wear your safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes ... etc

  • The equipment must be isolated and proper earthing is required.

  • Make sure that the measuring gas outlet (No 2 figure 2) is open.

  • Make sure that the power supply is 220 volt, and the voltage selector is the same 220v.

  • Gas pressure not exceed more than 10 bar.

Tools and Equipment

  • The moisture-measuring device complete as in figure (3),

  • The suitable wrenches for the hose connection you will use,

  • Clean cloth.

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Check the condition of the instrument before use and if the instrument has not been in operation for some time all tubes and couplings, which were not stored closed and filled with dry gas must be flushed for 10 minutes with dry SF6 gas (maximum pressure 10 bar/1Mpa).

  2. To do this open the flow-meter valve (No. 5 fig 1) completely and control valve (No. 5 fig. 3) slightly to attain the necessary gas flow of approximately 40 1/h. Damp couplings can be dried with a normal hot air fan.

Checking the Dew Point Mirror Condition

  1. Press the MAIN switch (No.1 figure 1)

  2. Close control valve completely and open the flow meter valve.

  3. Press the MODE switch (no.2 fig 1), (MIRROR CHECK), and the indicator needle must be in the middle of the red field, if the indicator needle is in the green field or at the right edge of the red field it means that the mirror is contaminate and it must be cleaned as following:

  4. Wait until temperature indication shows approximately the ambient temperature. (If mirror temperature is below ambient temperature when opening the measuring head, dew will form on them mirror.

  5. Clean the mirror with neutral tissue or with cotton

  6. Re-install front part of measuring head.

  7. If the indicator needle is in the middle of the red field it means that the cleaning process is finished. If the indicator needle with the green field or at the right edge of the red field. Set indicator needle with LIGHT INTENSITY (9 fig. 1) to the middle of the red field. If this is not possible (range of LIGHT INTENSITY) potentiometer is not sufficient), lamp and photocell in front part of measuring head must be cleaned.

Measurement

Make the necessary connections to sampling point and instrument plug in the MAINS lead as in figure 3.

At System Over pressure

  1. Press the MAIN switch (No. 1 figure 1)

  2. Press the MODE switch (No. 2 figure 1)

  3. Close flow-meter valve (No. 5 figure 1) at the front panel completely.

  4. Open control valve completely. figure (5)

  5. Open flow meter valve slowly until the required gas flow of 30 to 40 l/h is indicated

  6. Check dew point, mirror condition. The indicator needle must be in the middle of the red field.

  7. Release mode switch (No.2 fig 1) (light is off), the instrument starts cooling until dew point is reached and needle of mirror check indicator moves in to the green field.

  8. Wait until the indication of the digital display stabilizes.

  9. Read dew point an dew point indicator (No. 7 fig 1)

At Atmospheric Pressure

  1. Press the MAIN switch (No 1 fig 1)

  2. Press the MODE switch (No. 2 fig 1).

  3. Close control valve completely.

  4. Open flow-meter valve (No.5 figure 1) completely.

  5. Open control valve slowly until the required gas flow of 30 to 40 l/h is indicated.

  6. Check dew point mirror (No.3 fig 1). The indicator needle must be in the middle of red field.

  7. Release mode switch (No. 2 fig 1) (light is off), the instrument starts cooling until dew point is reached and needle of mirror check indicator moves in to the green field.

  8. Wait until the indication of the digital display stabilizes.

  9. Read dew point an dew point indicator (No. 7 fig 1)

  10. Read and record the pressure of gas vessel (Power switch, gas bottle, etc…).

  11. Use the chart on fig 4 to convert your dew point reading to moisture content in ppmv (by volume). Draw straight line between your obtained readings of pressure on right column and dew point on center column, then extend the line to the left column and read the moisture content of the gas under test in ppmv.

Note:   New SF6 gas should have a moisture content less than 125ppmv equivalent to 15 ppmg.

  Used gas should have a moisture content less than 500ppmv (62ppmg), and if it is more, the gas should be exchanged with new gas or in the case of GIS equipment (a very large quantity of gas) it is more economical to use the gas truck to re-condition the gas.
