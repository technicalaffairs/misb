Introduction

This document out lines the battery inspection I1 to be performed an a monthly bases M1 as indicated in the above title block

The first draft initiated by eng Mohammed Aly Aly Hassan at El_Shabab Gas power station and tested at 21 May 2002, East Delta Company for Electricity.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area,

  • No smoking is perminted in battery room

  • Use standard personal safety equipment, goggles, rubber gloves, rubber apron.

  • Do not place any tools on top of battery cells (danger of short circuit)

  • Never use the same tools on lead cells you may have used before on alkaline cells

Tools and Equipments

  • Complete toolset, Digital voltmeter, hydrometer, and glove.

Work to be carried out 

  1. Read overall voltage of bank and record on battery maintenance card,

  2. Disconnect AC supply to battery charger,

  3. Inspect the outside of cells for cracks and leakage of electrolyte

  4. If you discover defective cell use the recommended procedure to replace with a new cell

  5. Inspect electrolyte level and record density for each cell, add distilled water if level is below normal

  6. Inspect inter - cell connection and terminals of cells and clean any salt deposits with distilled water

  7. Check terminal connection for tightness

  8. Lubricate terminals of cells with a thin film of vaseline

  9. Clean top of cells

  10. Read voltage per cell and record on battery maintenance card

  11. Check ventilation system

  12. Again read overall voltage of bank and record on battery maintenance card and compare the reading from step No1

  13. If overall voltage is below 125V place battery bank on (high) charge for 4:5 hours after which the charger back on normal charge

  14. Compare the present readings with the last readings

  15. Inspect visually for SCR, diod and capacitors
