Introduction

This procedure details the minor maintenance procedure P1, which are performed on a 3-month basis as indicated by the M3. This procedure document is the first level of maintenance performed on this type of transformer. 

Safety Precaution 

  • A work permit must be issued and qualified maintenance staff should be trained and qualified or directly supervised by some one who is, and should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounded the work area is required.

  • CB must be isolated and proper earthing at the two sides.

  • Local grounded must be used.  

  • DC control voltage must be isolated,

  • Wear appropriate personal safety equipment, i.e. safety helmet and safety shoes.

  • Use only fiberglass ladder.

Environmental Recommendation

  • Before starting the cleaning of all porcelain surfaces of all transformer wear your breathing mask to protect lung tissues from the dust particulate.

  • Don't burn the cloth used in cleaning. Collect it and send it to the dump.

  • Fix any oil leaks to prevent pollution and save money of wasted oil.

  • Oil leaks to the ground have very bad impact on the environment, mainly on soil and ground water and eventually on the eco-system. "It takes only I liter of Hydrocarbon spilled in an aquifer to render 1,000,000 liters of potable water useless for human consumption"

Tools and Equipment

  • Safety helmet, safety shoes, and tool set

  • Complete tool kit/set, including a torque wrench,

  • Air blower, Lubricating grease (light grade),

  • Fine cloth for cleaning purposes,

Work to be carried out

  1. Check entire transformer for oil leaks, special attention should be made to piping connections.
    Note:      Oil leaks must be repaired as soon as possible, serious leaks immediately others on the next scheduled outage. Leaked oil is not only costly to replace, it is also harmful to the environment. Leaked oil must be cleaned up and properly disposed of, this process can also be costly
     

  2. Check oil level gauge on the main tank to ensure level is in a safe operating range       (above minimum). Take into account the ambient temperature when making this inspection.  

  3. Check the oil level gauge on the tapchanger compartment; ensure it is also in the safe operating range (above minimum). Take into account the ambient temperature when making this inspection.  

  4. Check bushing oil levels via the sight glass on top of the bushings, it must be visible

  5. Check the oil temperature gauge and record the maximum temperature degree rise as indicated by the drag hand reading on a Maintenance Record Data Sheet MRDS

  6. Check the winding temperature gauge and record the maximum temperature degree rise as indicated by the drag hand reading) on a Maintenance Record Data Sheet MRDS

  7. Check cooling fans to ensure they all operate properly and free of excess noise. Lubricate fans if necessary, i.e. on a yearly basis or if they are making excess noise.  

  8. Check the control wiring of the tapchanger, clean the tapchanger mechanism cabinet with an air blower, and then clean the controller contacts.  

  9. Check the control wiring of the transformer cooling, clean the transformer mechanism cabinet with an air blower, then clean/inspect the controller contacts, ensure breakers/fuses are in operating condition. Also check terminal strips for loose connections and check for signs of overheating

  10. Check the condition of the air breather silica gel, it should be pink colour. If it has changed to a blue colour, it requires changing/drying. Check the oil level of the breather sump.  

  11. Clean the earthing resistor isolator, ensure the movable contacts are clean and apply a thin layer of grease. Also check the hinge joint of the isolator and apply a small amount of lubrication to ensure it moves freely.  

  12. Check the condition of the neutral point current transformer. Ensure it is not cracked or showing signs of over heating, also check the connections for tightness.  

  13. Using a torque wrench check that all electrical connections are properly tightened, take special care that bushing terminal clamps are torqued properly.  

Note: Ensure oil samples for gas and chemical analyses are taken on a yearly basis. See T-001-r0 How to take Oil Samples and TD-SS-003-r0
