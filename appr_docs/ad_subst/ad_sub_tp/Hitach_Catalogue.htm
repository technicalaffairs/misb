Insertion into isolated position in cubicle

  1. Remove the interlock lever (15) at the front plate of mechanism housing from the disinterlock position to the interlocked position as shown in figure 13

  2. Push the breaker by hands, and stop the breaker where indication on front cover of the breaker (↓) coninsides with the mark plate on cubicle floor. This is isolated position

  3. Reset the interlock lever (15) to the disinter locked position.

Moving to the service position

  1. Operate the interlock lever (15) as same as A-1

  2. Push the breaker by hands, then breaker will go forward and stop

  3. Attach the draw-out handle (62) to both hooks, one on the cubicle and another in the breaker through the intel of draw-out handle (5) as shown in fig 11 and 13

  4. Push up the draw-out handle (62) and move the breaker to the service position as shown in fig 12

  5. Remove the draw-out handle (62) from the breaker and reser the interlock lever (15) to the disinter locked position

Draw-out operation can be done by the same method as inserting operation described in above

Same inter locks as mentioned in above step I are secured the safe operation in the intermediate position between isolated and service position.

Same inter locks as mentioned in above step II are incorporated

SF6 Gas System

Figure 14 shows the diagram of sf6 gas piping system SF6 gas pressure is monitored by the pressure gauge (18) When the gas pressure in the breaker drops below 3 kg/cm2, the pressure switch (25) operates, causing the auxiliary relay to lock the control circuit to prevent closing or opening. The time alarm circuit is operated at the same time

To prevent leakage of sf6 gas gas tightness tests on each component are made before and after assembly using a highly sensitive sf6 gas leak detector. This results in complete gas tightness.

Trial operation before service

Manual trial operation

  1. Charge the closing spring
    Insert the manual closing 36 spring charging handle (71) to its inlet (7) and move it up and down about 8 times up to charged-discharged indicator (6) indicating charged

  2. Close the breaker
    Pull the closing button (17), the breaker is closed, and open-closed indicator (8) indicates "closed" and charged-discharged indicator (6) changes to "discharged"

  3. Open the breaker
    Pull the trip button (16), the breaker is open, and open-closed indicator (8) changes to "open". Repeat these operation for several times and confirm the breaker to be operated normally.

Electrical trial operation

  1. Connect the control circuit
    Connect the test terminal (72) or secondary plug to the breaker, then the motor (43) starts to charge the closing spring (36), when the closing spring (36) has been charged, charged-discharged indicator (6) indicates "charged"

  2. Close the breaker
    Operate the control switch for closing, the breaker is closed, open-closed indicator (8) indicates "closed" and charged-discharged indicator (6) changes to "discharged" and then the motor (43) starts to charge the closing spring (36) immediately. Therefore the closing spring (36) is recharged, and charged-discharged indicator (6) indicates "charged"

  3. Open the Breaker
    Operate the control switch for open, the breaker is open, open-closed indicator (8) changes to "open". If you operate the control switch for closing immediately after operation for open, the breaker may be closed because of the closing spring (36) having been charged
    Repeat above operation 2 and 3 for several times and confirm the breaker to be operated normally.

Properties of sF6 gas

SF6 gas weights approximately 5.5 times as much as air.

Being colorless, odorless, and inert in its in its ordinary conditions, this gas is highly stabilized. This gas is liquefied at -62C under atmospheric pressure and at 0C under pressure of 12 kg/cm2 respectively. Since fptm type gcb uses gas at a pressure of 4 kg/cm2, liquefaction temperature of the gas is as low as about -37C, requiring no heater. Further, since the coefficient of heat transfer of SF6 gas exceeds that of air under the same condition approximately by a factor of 2.5 any temperature rise in case of energizing is low compared with the case of using air. Also, although it varies depending upon the shape of electrode, the dielectric strength of SF6 gas in a uniform electric field is 2.5 to 3 times as great as great as that of the atmosphere, becoming equal to that of insulating oil at an approximate pressure of 2 kg/cm2 (at 20C). Being considerably great in electron bending capability, sf6 gas displays splendid arc-extinguishing performance

Precaution of sF6 gas handling and safety

  1. Regarding the poisonous characteristic of sf6, the result of various experiments conducted on which rabbits and mice revealed that sf6 gas itself is inert and produces no poisonous effect on the human body. Concerning tests on its poisonous character, it is stipulated that mice shall be able to withstand without hindrance the atmosphere of gaseous mixture consisting of 75% sf6 and 25% oxygen for a period of 14 to 16 hours

  2. Decomposition products produced by arc discharge (HF,SF4,SF2,S2F2, etc) are harmful. Therefore, necessary steps have been taken by Hitachi to minimize these products, such as controlling of moisture in the interrupter to 150 ppm by volume or less (moisture in the atmosphere is approximately 20.000ppm by volume) and the absorption of decomposition products by absorbent (synthetic zeolite). Although a small amount of powdery sulfide and fluoride is found in the container inside the tank after breaking a large current, the above power has produced to harmful affect on the human body so far. It is advisable, however, always to wear a mask and gloves for a detailed inspection or contact replacement

SF6 gas is approximately 5.5 times as heavy as air

It is a colorless, odorless, inert gas. Consequently, care should be exercised with ventilation when handling this gas, as fer as possible avoid handling the gas in an enclosed room or pit. Should a large amount of sf6 gas escape, it floats on the lower layer, so that there is a possibility that only a large amount of SF6 gas is released in a narrow or improperly ventilated space during an inspection, there is possibility of oxygen shortage. Exercise the utmost care
