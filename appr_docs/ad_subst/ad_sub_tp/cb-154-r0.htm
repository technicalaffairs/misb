Introduction

This documents out lines the check list I1, which must be performed every four months as indicated by M4 at the titled block.

The first draft was initiated by eng. Al-Sayed Abdel Monem Al-Geddawy, and tested on  El harbea feeder no. 4 at Sheraton Almatar Substation at 06 March 2002 Cairo Zone.

Safety Precautions

  • A work order must be issued, the maintenance staff should be qualified and carry the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, ... etc

  • Ensure that the CB is out of service and out of its place.

  • Switch off DC source

Environmental Precautions

  • Before start cleaning, wear breathing mask to protect lung tissues from dust particles and chemical solvent which, if exist, expose lungs to danger

  • Don't throw grease on ground, collect it and send to central labs to recycle it.

  • Don't burn clothes used in cleaning, collect it and send to dumb  

Tools and Equipment

  • Suitable tool set

  • Pieces of clothes and rags

  • Grease and cleaning liquid (like gasoline)

  • Safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, ... etc

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Clean porcelain insulators of the circuit breaker from dust, dirties, deposits ....etc then check these insulators to be free of cracking and scratching

  2. Check contacts to be in good condition, also check the spring pressing the contact to get good contact

  3. Clean the contacts with gas to remove the old grease and then put new grease

  4. Inspect contact arms and check it to be in good condition and attached well to breaker body and to be exactly horizontal

  5. Check the guide of the contactor

  6. Inspect the vacuum capsules as explained in the Arabic document

  7. Clean the socket by video cleaner

  8. Check the mechanism of the circuit breaker, clean it with gas and grease it, check its parts for easy move

  9. Retighten bolts and screws

  10. Switch on the circuit breaker and off in test position mechanically and electrically

  11. Oiling the wheels of breaker tracks to make it easily move in and out of cell
