Introduction

This documents out lines the major  maintenance procedure P2, which  must be performed every fifteen years Y15 or every10,000 operations depending on conditions of use, as indicated at the above titled block.

The first draft initiated by eng Khaled Ibrahem Aly El Sherkawy, at new Sharkia 220kv substation and approved by Sector chairman, CB put in service at April 1994, Sina Sector, Canal zone.

Experience with this type of circuit breaker today allows, by overhauling a few sub-assemblies, to guarantee operators reliable and economical operation equivalent to new circuit breakers. In addition, significant savings can be made by retaining existing installations and equipment peripheral to the circuit breakers: e.g. civil engineering, overhead line, bus bars, measurement equipment, etc.

Safety Precautions

  • Work order must be issued and the qualified maintenace staff should the suitable safety category
  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area
  • Wear safety helmet, shoes

Tools and Equipment

  • Persenal protective equipment,
  • SF6 gas bottle, Densistats and Densistat seals kit,
  • Sealing kit, Arc contact kit, Threaded fasteners kit,
  • Oil, 25 liters, Oil filter kit, Accumulators Control,
  • Hydraulic actuator, Position feedback cylinder,
  • Hydraulic pump, Stainless steel pipe kit and couplings,
  • Low pressure pipe kit.

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Check SF6 gas pressure 7 bar at 20oC, to ensure satisfactory pressure level in the compartments. using SF6 bottle,

  2. Analyze SF6 gas and replace if necessary, to ensure correct pressure level in the compartments.  See figure 1

  3. Check and overhaul Densistats. If necessary change SF6 gas controllers with a new electronic densistat to give better stability with time,

  4. Replace all seals, replace all arc contacts, and replace all threaded items to ensure correct sealing. using sealing kit, arc contact kit, and threaded fasteners kit,

  5. Change oil and oil filter, to maintain normal pump starting cycle using oil, 25 liters, oil filter kit. for more details see figure 2 and paragraph of starting up the hydraulic control mechanism,

  6. Check response times and synchronization to check the correct operation of circuit breaker, see paragraph of checking CB operating times

  7. Measure wear of arc contacts, to maintain performance and to ensure fault current switch off

  8. Perform dielectric test on the auxiliary circuits with reduced voltage applied during 1 second to avoid disconnection of parts of the circuits,

  9. Measure the resistance of the main contacts,

  10. Measure the nitrogen pressure, must be included between 180+20 bars at 15 o C and replace the accumulator of tolerance.

Hydraulic Controls

  1. Using accumulators control and hydraulic actuator, Replace or overhaul: accumulators (replace the elastomers in the accumulators", control unit, pole actuator, position feedback cylinder, hydraulic pump and Densistats to guarantee operation,

  2. Replace pipe work, couplings, and change low pressure pipes. using stainless steel pipe kit, couplings, and low pressure pipe kit,

  3. Use controller for hydraulic maintenance, reduce pressure to zero bar, bleed the drive, and ensure there is no air in the drive system,

  4. Measure the hydraulic pressure of the thresholds and the re inflation pressure of the accumulator

  5. Don't leave the controller permanently calibrate the pressure gauge,

  6. The controller can be connected on the high pressure coupler, and ensure taps K2 and K3 are closed.

  7. Connect orifice K7 to the reservoir plug  see figure 2

Mechanical Controls

  1. Read and record the counter readings,

  2. Visually inspect mechanism,

  3. Visually inspect electrical equipment and safety devices,

  4. Clean and grease,

  5. Check for absence of corrosion to ensure fault current switch off,

  6. Check the general condensate inside the cabinet through ventilation and heating. In case of oxidation inside the cabinets:

    1. Check the proper operation of heating,

    2. Check the proper orientation of the ventilation inlets which should neither be closed up, nor receive direct water ejection.

    In case of continuous oxidation after check and repair, please contact Merlin Gerin

Starting Up the Hydraulic Control Mechanism

Filling the Hydraulic Control Mechanism

Preparation

  1. Fit the draining equipment on bleeding plugs pu6 and pu12,

  2. Connect the pressure controller to pressure tap PAE,

  3. Open valve K3 of the pressure regulating valve,

  4. Remove the control unit cover,

  5. Fill the tank with oil filtered at 20 microns,

  6. Fill the oil to rest 15 min before continuing filling.

Drain by Pu6

  1. Maintain the opening and closing pilot valves in the open position position by pressing on the electromagnets using a wedge

  2. Run the hydraulic pump

  3. Remove the draining equipment from bleeding plug Pu6 when the oil runs out clear and bubbles free. If necessary, accelerate the oil return by the bleeding plug by slightly closing valve K3.

  4. Release the closing and opening electromagnets

Drain by Pu12

  1. Set the pressure to 50 bar by modulating the opening of valve K3

  2. Send pulses to the closing electromagnet

  3. Remove the draining equipment from PU12 when the oil runs out clear and bubble free

  4. Close valve K3

  5. Let the hydraulic pressure build up until the pump stops

Overhauling

  1. Check there is no air in the control circuit

  2. Close the cover

  3. Replace the bleeding plug caps

  4. Disconnect the control unit

  5. Adjust the oil level in the tank according to ambient temperature (the amount of oil contained in the accumulators inflated to rated control pressure varies according to the nitrogen temperature)

Slow operations

The slow operations enable displacement of the mobile assembly to perform inspections during assembly and overhauls


Closing

  1. The circuit breaker is in the open position, the hydraulic control mechanism is drained

  2. Close valve K3

  3. Run the pump. As soon as pressure reaches 40 to 80 bar, press the electromagnet of the closing valve : the jack slowly rise on the pump discharge rate. At the end of travel, the pressure quickly rises.

  4. Stop the pump

Opening

  1. Open valve K3 to bring the pressure to zero

  2. Close valve K3

  3. Run the pump : the jack slowly moves under the pump discharge rate. At the end of travel, the pressure quickly rises

Checking Circuit Breaker Operating Times

  1. Check that operating times remain identical to the initial values, for initial values, refer to the individual tests performed in the works:

  • A rated control voltage and rated hydraulic pressure

  • Opening time tolerance ± 3 msec

  • Closing time tolerance ± 5 msec

  • Time spread of the poles

  • Opening time: 5 mesc

  • Closing time: 8 msec.

If operating times are out of tolerance, detect the cause of the disturbance:

  1. Check the rated supply voltage of closing and opening device,

  2. Check the rated hydraulic pressure for operation,

  3. Check the voltage drops in electric circuit of operating device,

  4. Contact Merlin Gerin in case of abnormal mechanical operation.
