Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection I1, to be performed every six months as indicated by M6 in the title block

The first draft initiated by eng  Jourget Ramsis Haroon, tested at 25 April 2004, by eng. Ahmed Abd Allah Anwar at Cairo East substation 66/11kv, accepted by eng. Ahmed Shelabi Faisal General Director, and approved by eng.Samir Saleh Ismael Extra high voltage Sector; Cairo Zone.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear the safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, ...etc.

  • Isolate the circuit breaker and ground it.

  • Isolate the DC voltage.

Tools and Equipment

  • Set of common tools, keys, screw drivers,

  • Cleaning cloths, SF6 gas and machine.

  • SF6 gas detector, and vacuum cleaner

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Clean the interior of the circuit breaker, control panel to remove all accumulated dust and dirt using vacuum cleaner

  2. Clean panel exterior, bushings busbars, and connector from dust and dirt.

  3. Check control wiring for loose connections and any signs of overheating and tighten all connections as required.

  4. Clean all bolts of the main connectors at the circuit breaker terminals, tighten all loosen bolts of the connectors as required. make notes of any signs of over heating, smooth it if necessary.

  5. Check and tighten all ground connections if necessary repair any damaged ground connections.

  6. Check control devices such as relays, limit switches, ...  in control panel for proper operation, repair  or replace faulty devices.

  7. Check the operating mechanism of the circuit breaker, inspect for loosen bolts, nuts or linkage.

  8. Check for gas leaks of the circuit breaker, check the gas gauge.

  9. Check the cabinet heaters.

  10. Check the circuit breaker ON/OFF indicators and adjust its position as required.

  11. Check the close spring charged at indicator.

  12. Check the trip and close springs indication, visually make note any remarks for repairing.

  13. Check the circuit breaker functions from remote and local.

  14. Check the door gasket.

  15. Check the SF6 gas quality (according to technical bulletin in document no: TB-SS-008-r0)

  16. Close the work order and inform shift engineer that the circuit breaker is ready to be in service.
