Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection I1, to be carried out on an annually bases as indicated by Y1 in the title block

The frequency and sort of inspection and maintenance basically depend on the service conditions e.g. frequency of operation interrupted current, values and relative power factor as well as the installation ambient temp

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the voltage area.

  • Wear safety helmet, shoes, breathing mask, safety glass ... etc

  • Equip the CB with an operation counter.

  • Any CB which will only operate a few times or which will remain (ON) or (OFF) for long periods, should be operated from time to time.

  • The CB in service should be visually inspected from outside to detect dust, dirt, damage, indication from manometer.

Tools and Equipment

  • Safety helmet, shoes, breathing mask, safety glass ... etc

  • Safety fence with caution marks is required

  • Tools set as drivers, screws, nuts

  •  Blower, brusher, rag

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Clean with dry brusher or rag the dust on the device.

  2. Replace the damaged spring if it is damaged or oxidized.

  3. Refit the locking rings in their housing and tighten the nuts or screws suitably.

  4. Replace the wire fasteners and reconnect the detached wires.

  5. Replace the damage insulating parts.

  6. Clean with a rough rag soaked in a suitable solvent and lubricate moderately with neutral grease the oxidized contacts.

  7. Clean the connection and breaker terminals with a rough rag soaked in suitable solvent and grease them.

  8. Tighten the earthing connection fully and cover it with neutral grease.

  9. Check the supply voltage of the operating mechanism and electrical accessories, which must be between 85-110%

  10. Check the gas pressure using its device its value 5009 kpa, in temp, 20°C otherwise according to the curve

  11. Carry out gas filling according the procedure shown in the catalog.

Checking the gas pressure

Note:

The following procedure is valid for HA1-HA2 circuit breakers only. For HA3 circuit breakers operations of gas filling pressure checking and its eventual reset are not required

  1. Check that the circuit breaker is out of service (both primary and secondary circuits isolated).

  2. Make sure that all the circuit breaker parts are at ambient temperature.

  3. Remove the protection cover (2) and the plug (1) from the gas filling valve (see fig. 1).

  4. Apply the gas pressure checking device (see fig 2) to the gas filling valve and measure the pressure.

The correct pressure value is 500kpa (absolute value at an ambient temperature of 20 °C.

For different ambient temperature values. use the corrector curve shown in figure 20.

Pressure values between 400 and 500 kpa (absolute value at an ambient temperature of 20 C°) do not jeopardize the correct operation of the breaker. Pressure reset must only be carried out if the pressure value drops below 400 kpa (absolute value). The limit also corresponds to the operating threshold of the pressure switch (if fitted).

In case of a completely discharged circuit breaker (relative pressure zero) it must be sent back to ABB SACE for repair.

Filling with gas

To carry out gas filling it is necessary to have a SF6 gas cylinder complete with pressure reducer as well as a pressure checking and adjustment device which must be adjusted to a value of 500 kpa (absolute value see curve in figure 3)

A Teflon or stainless steel pipe of adequate length and complete with the necessary screw type and fast-on coupers must be connected to the outlet of the checking and adjustment device (see figure 4)
The SF6 gas must comply with the requirements indicated at par 8.3.5

Let gas out of the cylinder into the air for at least 20 seconds in order to clear the connection pipe of air pockets insert the fast-on coupler in the gas filling valve of the circuit breaker and proceed with filling by supplying the gas slowly.

At the end of this operation remount the plug (1) and the sealable protection cover (2) (see figure 1)

Requirements for the SF6 gas

The gas must comply with IEC standards. Publication No. 376 (Specification and acceptance of new SF6 gas) and any further revision as well as with the corresponding Italian CEI standards Publication No. 410

It must also be remembered that impair the gas characteristics and compromise the functionality of the circuit breaker.
