Introduction

This document lists the equipment inspection steps (visual inspection) I1 for GIS Back Portion type 8DP3 Siemens, which must be performed every six months bases as indicated by M6 in the title block.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued.

  • The CB and the back portion must be fully isolated and earthed.

  • Control Power disconnected.

  • Personal protective equipment must be used (safety shoes, Helmet, breathing mask, safety glasses)

  • Maintenance staff should be well trained, qualified or directly supervised by some one who is.

  • Caution marks must be hanged on the work place, safety rope around the work place.

Tools and Equipment

  • Vacuum cleaner up to 2000watt,

  • Torque Wrench, Compete Set of Tools, Pressure Gauge,

  • Hydraulic oil, Gas detector, Emergency Light,

  • Dew point, Acidity and Air Content Equipment, 

  • Paint and paint brushes.

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Carry out visual check of the bays, where applicable also check parts outside the building, e.g. overhead-line termination modules.

  2. Record any special events (as requested by system management).

  3. Check gas filling using a precision pressure gauge. (All gas compartments with the exception of the circuit breaker module; 
    refer to MPIS document nos. CB-061, 062, 063-r0

  4. Top with gas, if necessary.

  5. Trace and eliminate leak, if necessary.

  6. Check lock nuts of operating linkages for disconnectors, earthing switches for tightness.

  7. Motors assembly type 3DX601: Check control section and test heater output (without taking readings).

  8. Motors assembly type 3AC. 16: Grease and Oil. Test heater output (without taking readings).

  9. Check operating linkages for disconnectors, earthing switches for easy movement. 
    To do this watch the On-Off  indicators: Their movement must be uniform and smooth.

  10. Test-operate, i.e. open and close, each disconnector and earthing switch five times by its motor assembly (not by hand).

  11. After test-operating disconnectors, earthing switches and high-speed earthing switches check their mechanical indicators for the 
    On and Off positions and note any discrepancies.

The following steps depending on the condition:

  1. Check all remade joints (covers and flanges) for any signs of leakage (final check). 

  2. Measure moisture content of gas in all gas compartments (final check). 

  3. Measure air content of gas in all gas compartments (final check). 

  4. Paint finish and anti-corrosion protection to be touched up by customer's personnel.

  5. Bushings of overhead-line termination modules to be cleaned by customer's personnel.
