Introduction

This document lists the Minor Maintenance P1, which must be performed every three years as indicated by Y3 in the title block or 500 switching operation which come first. The first draft initiated by eng Gamal Soliman, Assuit 500kv substation ME.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category, 

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area,

  • CB must be fully isolated.

  • Throughly exhaust comprised air from the CB air receiver.

  • Earthing switch must be closed on both sides of CB to ensure it is properly grounded.

  • Personal protective equipment must be used (safety shoes, Helmet, breathing mask, safety glasses)

Tools and Equipment
  • Complete tool set including screw driver set torque wrench. and Blower.
  • SF6 gas leak detector.
  • 220Vac source, Megger 1000 V
  • Portable pressure gauge standard.

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Inspect visually all parts of circuit breaker.

  2. Using SF6 gas leak detector to detect any leakage from piping joints and sealed parts .

  3. Electro-magnetic valve See Fig.1, clean the hook, apply a turine oil to it.  When the lever is depressed with fingers, the link is disengaged.

  4. Check the Airing valve No. 407 Fig 2  to measured the discharge (normal discharge 300-700 cc/min at free air .To do that connect a hose to the outlet and use a measuring cylinder to measure the discharge under water as shown in fig.3.

  5. Check each part of the auxiliary switch. for normal tightening and check the marks of open and closed state.

  6. Compressed air controller Fig.4

  • Clean and apply a thin coat of HITALUBE 280 to the piston rod of operating cylinder.
  • Retighten bolts of main valve.
  • Retighten union nuts and bolts for air piping .
Control Cabinet

  1. Calibrate the air pressure gauge by using a standard pressure gauge or calibrating apparatus.

  2. Check the lock pressure and release pressure on calibrated gauge for the pressures switch. 
    Setting values as follows:
    - Alarm and closing lock out ON 13.0 ± 0.2 ,OFF 14.0 ± 0.2  
    - Alarm and opening lock out ON 12.5± 0.2 ,OFF 13.5 ± 0.2
    - Reclosing condition ON 15.0 ± 0.2 ,OFF 14.7 ± 0.2

  3. Correct the contacts of the Trip-free contactor with smooth cut file or very fine sandpaper, when contacts are solid or roughened considerably.

Operating Rod System
  1. Remove the plug of the oil dashpot and check the oil level (oil level should be as high as the plug level oil) JIS-C2320-2.
  2. Check nuts and lock plates for looseness, Check split pins and joint pins of the rod system.
  3. Confirm that the stroke of operating rod must be 200mm ± 2mm.To do that Prepare the spindle and ratchet spanner delivered with GIS CB proper. Attach a spindle to the operating cylinder and use a ratchet spanner to measure the stroke of operating mechanism.
  4. Check Terminals for looseness and cubicle interior for rusty parts.
  5. After filling the system with control air (15 kg /m2) confirm that all stop valves in the air system are kept open in the normal position.
  6. Checking after restoration. Check nuts and lock plates for looseness, check split pins and joint pins.
  7. Record the maintenance results which has be done and all parts which have been changed in maintenance card.
  8. Measure the insulation resistance of the control circuit by Megger 1000 V.
