Introduction         

This document outlines the inspection and minor maintenance procedures P1, to be performed every six-months as indicated by M6 in the title block.

These activities are performed by station maintenance staff and would also be performed at this time.  

Safety Precaution

  • A work permit must be issued and the maintenance staff should carry the suitable ID safety category

  • Personnel carrying out the maintenance should be trained and qualified or directly supervised by some one who is.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear your safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, ... etc.

  • The CB must be isolated and properly earthed at both sides, it should be in ON position.

  • Disconnect DC voltage.

  • Use only fiberglass ladder.

Environmental Recommendations

  •  Do not throw any oil into the sewage because the oil prevents the sewage treatment  process.

  • Collect the oil that drained out of the poles into a barrel and return it back to the vendor to recycle it to save money.

  • Collect the oil used in washing the poles in another barrel and do not re-use it for filling the poles unless filtered, dried and its dielectric strength measured with an oil tester (not less than 40 kV / 2.5 mm).

  • Don't burn cloth used in cleaning but collect it and send it to the dump.

Tools and Equipment

  • One set of open-end wrenches.

  • One set of screwdrivers, Torque wrench.

  • Fine emery cloth, Some of clean and dry cloth.

  • Benzene and Grease, One barrel of new oil.

  • The special tools of CB.

  • Tin cans to hold bolts, nuts, studs, washers, …etc.

Work to be Carried Out

Inspection of Fixed and Moving Contacts

  1. Open the drainage oil- plug to drain the oil out of the CB in a suitable container.

  2. Unscrew the 11 screws of the upper cover (1 fig.1) of the arcing chamber and pull it up.

  3. Unscrew the 4 screws that fix the fixed contact to the arcing chamber (1 fig.2) using socket wrench 14 with long arm.

  4. Pull the fixed contact up.

  5. Inspect the fixed contact segments, if it is in a good condition it means that the moving contact is probably also in a good condition.

  6. If its segments are slightly burnt it should be cleaned by a fine emery cloth.

  7. If it is burnt with punctures it means that both of the moving contact and fixed contact are in a bad condition their contact surface should be changed and overhaul maintenance may be made to the arcing chamber. (Refer to MPIS overhaul maintenance document for this CB).

  8. Clean the fixed contact with benzene and new oil.

Inspection of the Came Oil Seal

Check for any oil leakage around the came and if there is leakage of oil, the came oil seal has to be changed as follows:

  1. Remove the cotter pin of the pin which fixing the came to the connecting rode (2fig.1) and pull the pin out.

  2. Release the connecting rode (3 fig.1) down to the ground, and take care not to damage the porcelain of connecting rode.

  3. Unscrew the screw that fixing the came to the came shaft (4 fig.1).          Use spanner 14

  4. Unscrew the 4 screws that fixing the flange of the came to the CB (2fig.3).Use spanner 19

  5. Pullout the came with its flange using special clamp (fig. 4), (3 fig. 2).

  6. Pull out the old oil seal and clean its seat with benzene and fine emery cloth and lubricate it, and replace the old oil seal with new one.

  7. Clean the came shaft using fine emery cloth and lubricate it.

  8. Reassemble the came with its flange to its previous position using special tool             (fig.5), (4 fig.2).   

  9. Screw in the oil drainage plug again and refill the arcing chamber with new oil and drain it for cleaning.

  10. Reassemble the upper cover of the arcing chamber to its original position and open the refilling oil plug to refill the CB with new oil.

  11. Close and open the Breaker two times and drain the oil out again from oil drainage plug to ensure complete cleaning of the arcing chamber.

  12. Refill the CB with new oil to a proper level.

  13. Perform timing test before putting the CB in service and record all that have been done in the maintenance card.
