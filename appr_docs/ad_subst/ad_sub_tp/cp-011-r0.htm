Introduction

    This document outlines the inspection activities to be performed on a daily basis by substation maintenance staff. These activities is the first level of inspection. The compressor must be taken out of service to perform this inspection.

Safety Precautions

  • A work order must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area,

  • Before doing inspection be sure that the starting switch of the unit is in Off position and the main electrical power supply has been secured to prevent the unit from any accidentally starting.

  • Wear standard personal safety and healthy equipment as helmet, safety shoes, rubber gloves, ... etc.

Environmental Recommendation

  • Fix any oil leaks to prevent pollution and save money of wasted oil. Oil leaks to the ground have very bad impacts on environment, mainly on soil and ground water, and eventually on the ecosystem. "It takes only I liter of Hydrocarbon spilled in an aquifer to render 1,000,000 liters of potable water useless for human consumption!

Tools and Equipment

  • Cleaning material, rags, air blower, grease, Oil, solvent.

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Manually drain the condensed water from high-pressure air tanks by opening the drainage valve for few seconds to let out all the condensed water. Drainage of condensed water should be done in early morning before the temperature has started to rise.

  2. Manually drain condensed water traps from the air network.

  3. Check compressor belts tension if exist (incase of belt drive)

  4. Check for any oil leakage.

  5. Check grease in lubrication points in motor and fill if needed.

  6. Check the oil level in all units using deep stick (the level must be between Max and Min marking.

  7. Check the inlet cleaner, clean and refill with new oil if needed.

  8. Operate the compressor and Check and record air pressure values of all stages:

      First stage 2 atm.
      Second stage 11 atm
      Third stage 40 atm.
      Fourth stage 160 atm (if exist).

  1. Check oil pressure 2 – 4 atm.

  2. Check oil temperature and if it excess 80 0 C switch off the unit.

  3. Be sure that the condense valve operate in good condition after any stop for compressor.

  4. Check for any leakage of air and oil during the operation of the unit.

  5. Stop the compressor and be sure that the automatic condensate drain valves are operate.

  6. Put the starting switch in automatic position from the operating log.

  7. Record the operation hours number.

  8. Put the unit in service.

Notes:

  • Changing oil every fifty hours of operation or every 3 months, whichever occurs first.

  • Inspection and cleaning the inlet air filter with new oil every 100 hours of operation.

  • Replacing oil filter element of oil filter every three hundred hours.

  • Replacing the wool-packed elements in first and second stage traps, flushing and cleaning the third stage and fourth stage traps every thousand hours.
