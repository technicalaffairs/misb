Introduction

This documents out lines the minor maintenance procedure P1, which must be performed every five years Y5 as indicated at the above titled block.

The first draft initiated by eng Khaled Ibrahem Aly El Sherkawy, at new Sharkia 220kv sabstation, Sina network Sector, Canal zone and tested at 16 June 2002

Safety Precautions

  • Work order must be issued and the qualified maintenace staff should the suitable safety category
  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area
  • Wear safety helmet, shoes

Tools and Equipment

  • Persenal protective equipment
  • SF6 gas bottle, Oil filter kit, oil 25 liter, Grease,
  • Densistat seals kit

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Check SF6 gas pressure 7 bar at 20oC, to ensure satisfactory pressure level in the compartments. using SF6 bottle,

  2. Check operation of densistat, to guarantee operation, using Densistat seals kit,

  3. Visually inspect poles (condition of insulators, rust, sealing), to ensure good insulation to avoid risks of flashover using Densistat seals kit

  4. Clean insulators

  5. Visually check electromechanical equipment, to ensure correct operation of components

  6. Check safety systems

  7. Check response times and synchronization (depending on model) to check correct operation of circuit breaker

  8. Measure wear of arc contacts, to maintain performance and to ensure fault current switch off

Hydraulic Controls

  1. Read and record meter readings,

  2. Check oil level and check for leaks,

  3. Using Oil filter kit, Densistat threshold and signaling, to cure any leaks,

  4. Pre-pressurize accumulators,

  5. Clean tank and system,

  6. Refill and bleed system,  for detail see the paragraph ** at the end of the document,

  7. Check tightness of pipes and unions, leakage may appear on hydraulic connections when leakage remains after retightening operation, it is necessary to replace copper gaskets, connections and pipes
    -Leakage one drop per day (12 cm3/year) is acceptable, one drop per hour not acceptable means 300 cm3/year

  8. Check tightness of hydraulic components,
    note: The actuator is equipped with recuperation drain for used oil. Oil presence is not abnormal, mainly after thousands of operations

  9. Check control tightness of the discharged auxiliary tank.

  10. Check internal tightness of hydraulic components, deduct the average number of daily startings of the hydraulic pump, when the circuit breaker is not in operation, if exceeds 10, internal tightness if the drive is not safe enough, detect the cause of the fault.

Mechanical Controls

  1. Read and record meter readings,

  2. Visually check mechanism,

  3. Visually check electromechanical equipment and safety devices,

  4. Clean and grease, to ensure fault current switch off,

  5. Check for absence of corrosion.

** Filling the Hydraulic Control Mechanism

Preparation

  1. Fit the draining equipment on bleeding plugs pu6 and pu12,

  2. Connect the pressure controller to pressure tap PAE,

  3. Open valve K3 of the pressure regulating valve,

  4. Remove the control unit cover,

  5. Fill the tank with oil filtered at 20 microns,

  6. Fill the oil to rest 15 min before continuing filling.

Drain by Pu6

  1. Maintain the opening and closing pilot valves in the open position position by pressing on the electromagnets using a wedge

  2. Run the hydraulic pump

  3. Remove the draining equipment from bleeding plug Pu6 when the oil runs out clear and bubbles free. If necessary, accelerate the oil return by the bleeding plug by slightly closing valve K3.

  4. Release the closing and opening electromagnets

Drain by Pu12

  1. Set the pressure to 50 bar by modulating the opening of valve K3

  2. Send pulses to the closing electromagnet

  3. Remove the draining equipment from PU12 when the oil runs out clear and bubble free

  4. Close valve K3

  5. Let the hydraulic pressure build up until the pump stops

Overhauling

  1. Check there is no air in the control circuit

  2. Close the cover

  3. Replace the bleeding plug caps

  4. Disconnect the control unit

  5. Adjust the oil level in the tank according to ambient temperature (the amount of oil contained in the accumulators inflated to rated control pressure varies according to the nitrogen temperature)

Slow operations

The slow operations enable displacement of the mobile assembly to perform inspections during assembly and overhauls


Closing

  1. The circuit breaker is in the open position, the hydraulic control mechanism is drained

  2. Close valve K3

  3. Run the pump. As soon as pressure reaches 40 to 80 bar, press the electromagnet of the closing valve : the jack slowly rise on the pump discharge rate. At the end of travel, the pressure quickly rises.

  4. Stop the pump

Opening

  1. Open valve K3 to bring the pressure to zero

  2. Close valve K3

  3. Run the pump : the jack slowly moves under the pump discharge rate. At the end of travel, the pressure quickly rises
