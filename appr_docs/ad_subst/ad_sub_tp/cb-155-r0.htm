Introduction

This documents out lines the check list I2, which must be performed on an annually bases as indicated by Y1 at the titled block.

The first draft was initiated by eng. Alsayed Abdel Monem Al-Geddawy, and tested on the feeder no. 40 at Fifth Urban Community 66kv Substation at 21 Agust.2002 Cairo Zone. 

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area must be used.

    •

  • Wearing safety helmet, safety shoes, masks are necessary.

    •

  • Switch off DC source

Environmental Precautions

  • Before start cleaning, wear breathing mask to protect lung tissues from dust particles and chemical solvent which, if exist, expose lungs to danger

  • Don't throw any cleaning liquid on ground, collect it and send to dumb.

  • Don't burn clothes used in cleaning, collect it and send to dumb  

Tools and Equipment

  • Suitable tool set

  • Pieces of clothes and rags

  • Grease and cleaning liquid (like gasoline)

  • Helmet, safe shoes and masks

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Clean porcelain insulators of the circuit breaker from dust, dirties, deposits ....etc.

  2. Check porcelain insulators to be free of cracking and scratching

  3. Inspect well the flower contacts and check it to be in good condition, check its component (fingers) to be correct order and the spring compressed it is in good condition .

  4. Clean flower contacts, remove the old grease from the contacts and then put new thin film of grease on it .

  5. Check the contact arms to be well fixed and in horizontal position to avoid bad contact between breaker contact and bas bar contact .

  6. Inspect the pressure of SF6 gas and it to be in the safe area (green area)

  7. Clean the socket by video cleaner

  8. Check the mechanism of the circuit breaker, clean it with gas and grease it, check its parts for easy move

  9. Switch on the circuit breaker and off in test position mechanically and electrically

  10. Oiling the wheels of breaker tracks to make it easily move in and out of cell

  11. Measure the contact resistance

  12. Measure switching time (switching off time and switching on time)
