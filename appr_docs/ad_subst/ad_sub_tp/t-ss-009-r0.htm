Introduction  

This document lists the equipment Inspection checklist I1, which must be performed on a daily bases as indicated by D1 in the title block for all HETACHI energized transformers by operation staff.

Safety Precautions

  • Don't go too close to energized areas on the transformer while it is operating too close, means as a general understanding within a spherical area whose center is the top of the bushing and whose radius is the full length from the top of the bushing flange (ground potential) of the bushing.

    •

Work to be carried out

 

 

Transformer Temperatures, Load And Voltages

  1. Read and record oil and winding temperature, check that the maximum temperatures are not being exceeded, also check that the temperature is acceptable by comparing it with past entries in the inspection log.

  2. Voltages and load must be kept within the specified limits, which are in the instruction manual.

Oil Level

  1. Read and record the values on the oil level gauges, check that the oil level readings are correct for the oil temperature, in compliance with the graph on the nameplate located near them, (in nameplate) and this graph also appears in the transformers instruction manual.

Audible Noise And Loosely Bolted Parts           

  1. Listen and check for any abnormal sounds from the main tank and accessories as a rattling sound indicates loose bolts and a repetitive scraping sound indicates worn fan or oil pump bearings.

Loosely Bolted Parts

  • Visually inspect bolted connections in the transformer's main and auxiliary circuits, foundation bolts and all other bolted connections to ensure that all parts are secure.

Valve Condition

  1. Check that none of the valves is loose, such as the oil inlet and out let valves. For valve condition, refer to oil piping diagram in the instruction manual.

 

Oil Leakage

  • Check for leakage from the transformer tank, valves, pipes, gauges, gasket joints welds etc.

Dehydrating Breather

  1. The silica-gel absorbent inside the breather bottle is normally magenta blue, but turns pink if excessive moisture is present, dry or replace the silica gel if more than 70% is pink.

  2. Check that the sight-glass level of the sealing oil in the oil cup is between the HIGH and LOW marks, it must be correct at all times because excessive sealing oil in the cup may contaminate the silica-gel, while a shortage of oil may not seal the conservator properly.

Pressure Relief Device

  1. Check for cracks, oil or gas leakage or any sign of damage to the pressure plate.

Cooling Equipment

  1. On forced oil cooled and forced oil/forced air-cooled transformers, check for abnormal rotation related sounds and vibrations.

  2. Check for an excessive buildup of grime on the radiator surface and remove it.

  3. Check that any oil flow indicator installed in the system shows that oil is circulating.

  4. Check that radiator valves are in the open position.

On Load Tap Changer (OLTC)

  1. Make sure no oil is leaking from the main tank into the OLTC by checking that the OLTC conservator oil level is not too high. If the oil level is too high, oil may be seeping through the insulating cylinder of the diverted switch compartment, if this compartment is leaking the escaping contaminated oil may seriously affect the transformer's insulation specifications, leading to internal arcing.

  2. Check the level of oil in the gearbox , it should be up to the red mark, usually oil needs to be supplied every 3 to 6 months, use turbine oil SAE 20 W120.

  3. Check for any abnormal sounds or vibrations during operation.

  4. Check that the stop position indicator is positioned within the safety zone (usually marked green).

  5. Record the number of tap changing operations and check that the operations counter functions correctly.

Bushings

  1. If the bushings are equipped with an oil gauge, use binoculars to visually check the oil level and for signs of leakage.

  2. Check the porcelain for cracking and other damage.

  3. Check that there is no overheating of the terminals, inspect the terminals for heat discoloration using binoculars or infra red scope.

  4. Check the pressure of N2 gas, the gauge pointer should not be in red range and if the gauge pointer is in red range, gas shall be refilled up to 0.2 kg/cm2.

Protective Relays

  1. Check the transformer's protective relays at the site control room and check that they have not actuated.

Conservator  

  1. Open the conservator drain valve about once every 3 months to drain off any water condensation.
