Introduction

This document lists the equipment inspection I1, which must be performed  on a monthly bases, M1 as indicated in the above title block.

Safety Precautions 

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required,

  • Transformer isolation and earthing is required.

  • Wear safety helmet, shoes, harness.

  • A proper well fixed ladder should be used for climbing on top of the Transformer.

  • Wear appropriate breathing apparatus and maintain good ventilation during cleaning operations.

  • While working at night ensure adquate lighting is provided for the entire entire work area.

Tools and Equipment

  • Portable earthing extension ladder, screw drivers, set of common keys and blower.

  • Safety helmet, shoes, harness,breathing apparatus ,adquate lighting .

  • Recording sheet.

  • Oil sampling equipment and report forms.

Work to be Carried out


Main Transformer Checks

  1. Dry cleaning of all bushings and star point earthing insulators.

  2. Check oil leakage of all things. (bushing, cooling radiators, gauges and main tank), Repair oil leaks to prevent ground pollution and save money of wasted oil.

  3. Manually operate the cooling fans (I st and 2nd stage) and check for the proper operation and listen for unusual noises. Measure the noise level and vibration of cooling fans (not to exceed 40-60 decibels).

  4. Clean all external cubicles and check for anything unusual. (loose wiring, heating terminals, lightning), Check also the rubber gasket and clean with blower.

  5. Check all oil level gauges for proper level. (for Bushings, conservator and tapchanger) investigate any levels not correct.

  6. Check the color of the silica-gel breathers, it must be changed if the color has changed from blue to pink.

  7. Check the oil in the breather for level and color. Change if necessary. Visually check grounding points for signs of heating, tightness and general condition.

  8. Take two oil samples from the transformer, one sample for Gas-in-oil analysis annually and the other for electrical tests every 6 months.

Tapchanger Checks

 

Every 3 months the tapchanger must be operated through its range to check for proper operation and to internally self-clean the selector contacts. To do so complete the following steps:

  1. Record the original tapchanger position before starting.

  2. Electrically lower to tap #1 from the control room.

  3. Attempt to the lower further, the tapchanger should not operate if the electrical limits are set properly.

  4. Electrically Raise from tap # I up to tap # 21.

  5. Attempt to raise further, the tapchanger should not operate if the electrical limits are set properly.

  6. Lower tapchanger to the original tap position at start.

  7. Check that all three tapchangers are on the same tap position when checks are completed.

Fire System Checks

 

Every 3 months (January, April, July, October) the fire system must be checked for proper operation. If a MPIS document exists for these checks, follow the proper procedure. If there is no MPIS document, use the following steps as a guide

  1. Check Water Pressures gauges (The correct readings should be 9 to 10 bar for'all gauges at transformer fire room).

  2. Test the Jockey pump operation (It should start at 6 bar and stop at I0 bar to maintain proper system pressure).

  3. Check the start up pressure of the main pump (should start at 5 bar) to provide pressure for a fire.

  4. Operate system to test water spray on the transformer and all system functions.

  5. Check for correct water level in the resevior tank.

  6. Check the operation of one heat sensor by applying heat.
