Introduction

This document lists minor maintenance procedures P1, which must be performed every ten years or according to contact life (No. of short circuit operations) as indicated by Y10 in the title block.

The first draft document initiated & tested by eng. Mohamed Ali Ibraheem at Beer El-Abd substation 220kv, accepted by eng. Barakat Saif Aamr: general manager, revised by quality control manager: eng. Mohamed Emran, and certified by eng. Ali Atiia Eldeeb: head sector of canal western networks and MPIS coordinator.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • CB must be isolated completely and the earthing switch must be connected on both sides of the CB.

  • Isolate the DC voltage. (switch off the control circuit and insert closing and tripping preventive pins)

  • Ensure that the CB grounded on both sides

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area

  • Wear safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, shoes, safety glasses ... etc

  • Wear breathing mask to protect your lung tissues from the dust particles (before using a blower to clean the control cabinet and insulators) 

Tools and Equipment

  • Suitable tool set

  • Micro-ohmmeter, blower

  • Gas detector, acidity equipment, air content equipment

  • Pieces of clothes and rags

  • Grease and cleaning liquid (like gasoline)

  • Hydraulic oil and SF6 gas if needed

Work to be Carried Out

Visual inspection of circuit breaker components

  1. Check any abnormal noise

  2. Check any rust at metalic frame and remedy it

  3. Check the cabinet heaters

  4. Check the door packing, replace it if necessary

  5. Retighten the earth terminal

  6. Clean the porcelain from dirties and check for cracks

  7. Inspect the accumulator and interruption chamber for any cracks

  8. Check registered number of operation counters and annotate it

  9. Check pump running timer settings : 3 min

  10. Check oil leak from hydraulic pump and also oil level in reservoir

  11. Check normal working pressure for hydraulic : 335 bar
    Warning : don't operate the C.B. unless it is filled with SF6 gas and the pressure at 7 bar

  12. Check  the setting of gas pressure gauge, refer to  gas reference values :
    Normal working pressure            : 7 bar at 20°C
    Gas trip lock out pressure            : 5.9 bar at 20 °C (Reset :6.2 bar)
    Gas alarm, gas lockout pressure   : 6.3 bar at 20 °C (Reset : 6.6 bar)

  13. Test the time for closing and opening operation :
    Closing time must be about            : 65 m sec.
    Opening time must be about           : 27.5 m sec.

  14. Test the maximum timing spread of main contacts :
    Closing                                        : 10 ms over 3 phases
    Opening                                       : 3 ms over 3 phases

  15. Retighten of hydraulic parts

  16. Read and annotate the number operation of accumulator and annotate it.

  17. Check the nitrogen pressure (about : 200 bar)

  18. Check the safety valve for SF6 gas :
    Must open when the pressure gas reach : 7.7 bar according to doc. no. CB-019-r0
    Note : new SF6 gas should have a moisture content less than 125 ppmv equivalent to 15 ppmg.
    Used gas should have a moisture content less than 500 ppmv (62ppmg), and if it more, the gas should be exchanged with new gas or in the case of GIS equipment (a very large quantity of gas) it is more economical to use the gas truck to re-condition the gas

  19. Test SF6 moisture content  < 62 PPMw, must change the gas if more than that

  20. Test SF6 oil content           < 10 PPMw.

  21. Test SF6 Hydrogen Fluoride (HF) content < 1 PPMw.

  22. Test SF6 sulpher dioxid ( SO2 ) content     < 0.05 %

  23. Measure SF6 percentage volume               ≥ 99 %

  24. Measure the contact resistance                  ≤ 100 µΩ (according to doc. no. CB-004-r0)

Checking the number of pump restarts

  1. The number of pump restarts reflects the effectiveness of the seals on the unit. It depends upon the amount of leakage, the accumulator volume and the working tolerance of the pressure switch.

  2. The volume injected at each pump restart is calculated by multiplying running time in minutes by 2 (1/m).
    Then from the number of restarts the rate of leakage is deduced. A leakage of 4 liters per day should be investigated.

Checking the oil level in the reservoir

A visual check of the oil is required. If the level has dropped the oil must be topped up and the reason for the fall investigated.

Normal : This mark corresponds to the oil level when the circuit breaker is in the open position, with the maximum pressure in the accumulators and a temperature of + 15c

Warm : This mark corresponds to the oil level for the above conditions but at a temperature of + 40°C
At high temperatures the pre-inflation pressure rises, the oil volume is reduced in the accumulators and the oil level rises in the tanks.

Cold : This mark corresponds to the oil level for the above conditions, but at a temperature of - 25°C
At low temperatures the pre-inflation pressure is reduced, the oil volume is increased in the accumulators and the oil level lowered in the tanks.

For intermediate temperatures, the oil level should be adjusted proportionally to the temperature, between warm and normal, or between normal and cod.
When the oil level is check (every 12 months) the temperatures should be taken into account, in order to assess whether there has been any loss of oil.

Note : When topping up the oil, use only an oil complying with the required specifications.

Checking the pre-pressurisation of the accumulators

With the circuit breaker open

  1. Reduce the pressure in the unit to zero, using pressure regulator type 5472.

  2. Open cock k3 on the pressure regulator in order to use the pressure gauge.

  3. Using the hand pump, or the motor pump set, pressurise the unit. At first the pressure will rise quickly and than stabilize when the accumulator pistons begin to move.

At this point the pressure gauge should be read.

  1. In the event of any pre-pressurisation greater than 10 % of the nominal value, the defective accumulator should be identified and replaced

Note : It is necessary to take account of variation in the nitrogen due to temperature changes. for the accumulators pre-pressurised too 200 bar at 15°C the variation in pressure is 0.87 bar/degree °C
This factor is valid for the temperature range - 25 °C + 40 °C

Bleeding the circuits

  1. With draw detents to service position do not unscrew the detent more than 3 # turns (see fig 1) using the pressure regulator case type 6554,

  2. Fit the following equipment

    1. Pressure regulator 5472 on to valve PAE (K2 and K3 open)

    2. Draining equipment 5451 onto valves PU6, PU8, PU12 & PU18

  3. Remove the cover from the control unit, and replace with the cut away protective cover

  4. Using a wedge on solenoids AF1 and AF2, keep valves AH1 and AH2 open.

  5. Remove the cover from reservoir D and fill the reservoir through orifice D1 or cock R

  6. Start the motor pump unit and as soon as the oil flows clear (for a minimum of 10 sec)

    1. Re-close cock K3 of the pressure regulator

    2. Remove the equipment from PU6 and then from PU18
      Caution: do not leave the pressure regulator permanently connected to the unit

  7. Stop the motor pump unit and remove the wedges from solenoids AF1 then AF2

  8. Restart the motor pump unit and maintain the pressure at 70 bar. Actuate the closing solenoid AF1 several times until the oil flows out clear from PU8. Stop the motor and remove the equipment from PU8

  9. Restart the motor pump unit and when the pressure reaches 70 bar, actuate the closing solenoid AF1 several times until the oil flows out clear from PU12,  
    Remove the equipment from PU12
    Note    : during the above operations, the pump may be cut off if the pressure rises above 70 bar.

  10. Leave the pump running and check the overall pre-pressurisation of the accumulators.
    The pressure will rise quickly then, at the point where the accumulator piston starts to move very slowly.

  11. Leave the pump running unit automatic cut-off pressure is reached.

  12. Top the oil up to the correct level and replace the cover on the reservoir.

  13. Once the mechanism has been purged a visual inspection of it should be mad to ensure on hydraulic fluid has been spilt. Particular care should be taken to ensure both opening and closing coils are not contaminated. A suitable cover should be used to achieve this during purging. Any affect areas should be cleaned; in the case of coil it must be removed from the mechanism and dismantled prior to cleaning. A suitable solvent and lint free cloth should be used to achieve this.
    Failure to fully clean the mechanism could result in the circuit breaker failing to operate.

  14. Change oil.

  15. Replace filter.

  16. Retest accumulator (Differ from country to another).
