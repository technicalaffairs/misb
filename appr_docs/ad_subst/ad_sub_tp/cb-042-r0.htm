Introduction

This procedure details the outlines of the minor maintenance steps required for the SF6 Outdoor Circuit Breaker 132kv FX11, GEC Alsthom P1, which are performed from 4 : 6 year as indicated by the Y4 : 6 as indicated in the above titled block.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued

  • CB must be isolated and proper earthing at the both sides of the CB

  • Interrupt the control circuit (DC volt).

  • Discharges closing spring 70.02 and tripping spring 40.04 by operating manual lever trip and manual lever close.

  • Wearing safety helmet is required.

Tools and Equipment 

  • Densimeter test device w4

  • Thermometer w52

  • One set of metric open-end box wrenches.

  • Cleaning cloths, Lubricating grease

Work to be carried out

  1. Make the annual inspection according to MPIS document no. CB-041- -r0

  2. Inspection of Densimeter switching points to determine the pressure values S1 and S2 at which contacts of Densimeter act where:
    S1= signal 1 Alarm pressure
    S2= signal 2 " trip and lock"

a)  Determine approximate gas temperature with thermometer closely fitted to Densimeter.

b)  Determine correction value ?P from the diagram fig (1).

c)  Determine pressure values for S1 and S2

S1 theoretical = S10? (T=20°C, h = 0-1000 m)+DP
S2 theoretical = S20 (T=20° C, h =0 -1000 m)+DP
Where S10 & S20 can be determined from the manual.
To determine the actual values for S1 & S2.

  1. Open the two isolating terminals k for signals S1 and S2 in motor wound spring mechanism.

  2. Connect terminal k for signal S1 to line tester (L) fig (2).

  3. Make sure that nozzle D of Densimeter test device is closed, connect Densimeter test device W41 to Densimeter support (80.02 fig.3) and tighten with nuts (M fig. 3).

  4. Open nozzle D of Densimeter test device W41 and note that pressure in gauge A decreased.

  5. Record instantaneous pressure value at gauge (A) at the same moment as the indicator L work.

  6. Reclose nozzle D at Densimeter test device.

  7. Compare instantaneous pressure value with theoretically determined pressure value.

  8. Connect line tester L to terminal k for S2 and repeat the above steps.

  9. Pressure difference (S1-S2) should be (0.3± 0.1 bar), if not follow steps in MPIS doc no. CB-044-r0

  10. Close isolating terminal k for signals S1, S2 in motor wound spring mechanism

  11. Check the Densimeter function in the control room.

  12. Check gas pressure and top up with SF6 gas to reach the normal operating pressure.

  13. Clean sealing surfaces of seal cover 80 .09 and of Densimeter support 80.02 with clean cloth

  14. Remove valve plate 80 .08 with the nut M and apply thin coating of lubricant of SF6 L44 to O. rings 80-25 of seal set and then connect it again.

  15. Grease the total perimeter of seal cover 80.09 with grease L42 fig (4).

  16. Check all connections for leaks with leak detector w51.

  17. Carryout all gas tests.
