Introduction 

This document lists the equipment Inspection checklist I1, which must be performed on an annually bases as indicated by Y1 in the title block.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should be carry the Suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear your safety and healthy equipments safety helmet, safety shoes …. etc

  • Transformer outage is required.

  • Never perform any work on the transformer before isolating the fire protection system.

  • You must carry portable fire extinguisher when entering the transformer room after the fire.

  • Vent the flooded transformer room before entering it after the fire.

  • Any person inside the transformer room must immediately leave it after hearing the fire alarm. 

  • Any doors leading to the room must be kept closed.

  • To prevent the accidental discharge of the system, do not touch the pilot cylinder weighing device inside the transformer room.

Tools and Equipment

  • Safety Helmet, Safety Shoes.

Work to be carried out

  1. Perform 6 Month Inspection as described in MPIS document Number FS-SS-003-r0

  2. Check the water storage tank and all piping for any signs of rust or damage to the painted

  3. The inside of the water storage tank must be inspected every 2 Years for rust deterioration

  4. At the CO2 cylinder installation, secure the CO2 cylinder valve head with a locking pin

  5. Disconnect the tie-rods from the saw- toothed slide by removing the connecting pins

  6. Drain all the water from the storage tank

  7. Open the man-hole and perform an internal inspection of the tank

  8. Any rusted or corroded surfaces must be cleaned and painted. Use appropriate breathing apparatus and adequate ventilation while performing this step. Also following are suggestions to reduce the environmental effects of spray painting

    • Maintain 6"- 8" distance between spray gun and the work-piece

    • Maintain gun speed of about 250 feet/ minute

    • Hold gun perpendicular to the surface (If gun is arched 45°, the over spray can be as high as 65%)

    • Avoid excessive air pressure for coating atomization (by air pressure adjustment over-spray can be reduced to 40%)

    • Recycle over spray.

  9. When completed, secure the manhole in place

  10. Refill the tank to the correct level with water

  11. Reset the CO2 valve trigger mechanism and remove the valve locking pins

Compressor Maintenance

In addition to monthly compressor maintenance perform the following:

  1. Clean carefully all outside parts of the compressor and the motor

  2. All compressor valves and valve seats need to be inspected after 2000 hours of compressor running time. Damaged valves and valve seats need to be replaced
