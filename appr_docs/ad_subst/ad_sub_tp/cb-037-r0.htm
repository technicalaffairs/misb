Safety Precaution

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff must carry the suitable ID safety category,

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area is required,

  • Wear the appropriate safety equipment as safety shoes, helmet, breathing mask, safety glasses,

  • The circuit breaker should be switched off, fully isolated, and grounded on both sides,

  • Earthing switch must be closed on both sides of the breaker,

  • Switch off control current for CB (d.c).

    •

Environmental Precautions

  • Before using a blower to clean the outside compartment of the circuit breaker from dust, wear your breathing mask to protect your lung tissues from the dust particulate.

  • Don't burn the cloth used in cleaning, collect it and send it to the dump.

  • Fix any oil leaks from connections, safety valve, bleeding washers, and motion transmission shaft to prevent pollution and save money of wasted oil.

  • Oil leaks to be ground have very bad impact on the environment, mainly on the soil and ground water and eventually on the ecosystem. “ It takes only 1 liter of Hydrocarbon spilled in an aquifer to render 1,000,000 liters of potable water useless for human consumption “.    

 

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete set of substation maintenance hand tools including screw driver set, torque wrench,
  • Emergency light, air blower,
  • 220 Vac supply, emergency light,
  • SF6 gas leakage detector,
  • Portable pressure gauge standard.

Work to be Carried Out

Note: Although not always specified, all checks are performed on all three-breaker phases.     

  1. Inspect visually all components of the circuit breaker for signs of overheating in the mechanism boxes, signs of electro static discharge, small oil leaks, weld cracks, foundation, fixation of circuit breaker.… etc.

  2. Check for AC and DC supply source.

  3. Check all bolts and fasteners to ensure they are properly tightened, including mechanism bolts.

  4. Check that all cable plugs are secure in their receptacles so proper contact will be made.

  5. Check the earthing points of the circuit breaker to ensure they are secure and have not been damaged.

  6. Ensure the pressure should be 330bar, check the oil pressure should be 330 bar at normal condition.

  7. Check the operation of the hydraulic oil pump by reducing the hydraulic oil pressure (bleed a quantity of oil pack into the reservoir until the pump starts) and check the function of the pressure switch operation and record the stage of pump operation

  8. Check and record the number of hydraulic pump operations as indicated on the operation counter and compare with the previous month.

  9. Check and record the operation of the heater circuit of mechanism.

  10. Check oil level of main tanks for three phases.

  11. Check oil level of mechanism of parallel resistance

  12. Check oil leaks for all connections.

  13. Check oil leaks for safety valve.

  14. Check oil leaks for bleeding washers.

  15. Check oil leaks for motion transmission shaft (Ram).

  16. Check all terminals connections also the pump terminals.

  17. Oil bleeding must be done.

  18. Check auxiliary switch terminal and all plugs regard connections cables

  19. Check on / off coils.

  20. Check the oil level of the paralleled resistor through the oil level indicators on the hydraulic mechanism of the resistor. Also check the level of hydraulic oil in the breaker operating mechanism.

  21. Check and record the pressure in the accumulator, ensure that the initial pressure ~ 230 bar.

  22. Check high-pressure pipes to ensure they are in their proper location and sealing properly.

  23. Check the hydraulic oil reservoir tank to ensure the cap is sealing properly.

  24. Retighten all bolts and earth connection of foundation and fixation of circuit breaker.

  25. Clean all mechanism parts.

  26. Check and record the operation pressure of the pressure switch on all 3 phases, this is done through the amount of pressure gauges and observing the operation of oil switch contacts as the pressure is lowered. The shut off pressure for the pump must be checked during pump up. Also during pump up the time taken for the pump to completely charge their respective systems should be recorded this value is typically around 400 seconds.

  27. Check door gaskets for all panels.

  28. Check SF6 humidity content air content < 2 %,  acid content < 10 PPM. 

  29. Check gas dew point reference to MPIS approved document no. TD-SS-004- r0.

  30. Check the contact resistance for circuit breaker & all compartment

  31. Check on / off timing.

  32. Measure SF6 pressure of circuit breaker.

  33. Check gas pressure switch (for all stages)

  34. Clean all outside compartment of circuit breaker from dust, oil and other things using blower and rags.
