Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection  I1, which are performed on an annual basis Y1 as indicated in the above title block,  The first draft initiated by eng.Hanafi Kamel Mohamed Saleh, tested by Mohamed Sayed Esmaiel Taha, at 22 Sep 2001, El_Tibeen 500kv substation, Cairo zone

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area,

  • Appropriate personal protective equipment must be used as safety shoes, Helmet, breathing mask, and safety glasses.

  • Circuit breaker must be fully isolated.

  • Earthing switch must be closed on both sides of the breaker to ensure it is properly grounded.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete set of substation maintenance hand tools including screwdriver set, torque wench.

  • Emergency light, 220 Vac supply.

  • Portable pressure gauge standard, and Endscope.

Work to be Carried Out
  1. Check visually all components of the circuit breaker.

  2. Check the oil levels in tanks.

  3. Check the tightness of hydraulic circuits. (pipes and components)

  4. Check  the wiring of the hydraulic panel ref. electrical diagram. 

  5. Check  the condition of the electrical connections. 

  6. Check the position of mechanical optical indicators.

  7. Measure the time necessary for the pump motor of  the hydraulic operating mechanism to reach level VE after a cycle OCO performed starting Kiosk >3min.

  8. Measure the SF6 filling pressure of the compartments. (6 bar)

  9. Measure stages of  SF6 pressure of the compartments. (alarm 5.2bar, auto trip 5 bar)

  10. Test the operation of the internal arc detector using endscope.

  11. Record the response times on opening:
    For line circuit breakers starting from the moment when the voltage is applied to the tripping coil until the opening of contacts. The time should be inferior to 27 ms.

  12. Record the synchronism on opening: It should be inferior or equal to 5 ms. (the delay time between phases not greater than 5 ms).

  13. Record the response times on closing:    
    For line, circuit breakers starting from the moment when the voltage is applied to the closing coil until the closing of the contacts. The time should be including between 54 to 70 ms.

  14. Record the synchronism on closing: It should be inferior or equal to 5 ms. (the delay time between phases not greater than 5 ms).
