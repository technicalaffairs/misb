Introduction

This procedure details the minor maintenance procedures P2, which must be performed every five years as indicated by Y5 in the title block. In addition to the procedures listed, it is expected the checks, which are done more frequently, would also be completed at this time.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence (rope) with cautions marks surrounding the work area.

  • Circuit breaker isolation and earthing is required.

  • Wear your safety helmet, shoes .... etc.

  • Change remote to local position.

Tools and Equipment

  • Voltmeter, Complete tool set,

  • Dew point, Acidity and Air content equipment,

  • Contact Resistance equipment,

  • Timing test equipment.

Work to be carried out

  1. Check AC and DC supply source.

  2. Check SF6 pressure and air pressure manometers.

  3. Check air and gas leaks.

  4. Clean mechanism aux. switch. by dry air (use blower).

  5. Check the aux. switch functioning.

  6. Check mech. position indicators and observe the three poles.

    7.            (Visual check the two signal pole discrepancy and pole failure)

  7. Clean the local cabinet by dry air (use blower).

  8. Check the heater of local cabinet also mechanism panel heater.

  9. Retighten all connections terminals of remote and local cabinet.

  10. Drain compressed air conservator.

  11. Check the earthed connections.

  12. Check gas insulation resistance.

  13. Check gas content and dew point.

  14. Check tightness of bolts and all connections and retighten it if it is necessary.

  15. Check mechanical operation.

  16. Measure contact resistance according to MPIS document no. CB-SS-004- -r0.

  17. Measure break and make times.

  18. Check refilling and reduction valve and tightness.

  19. Check refilling time in comparison of reference value.

  20. Check tightnes of air and gas pipes.

  21. Check the noise of GIS compartments.

  22. Measure the resistance of earth.

  23. Make trip operation by protective relays.

  24. Check setting and functioning of SF6 manometer contacts.

  25. Measure Dew point test

  26. Check functioning of compressed air pressure gauge.

  27. Check pressure drop during switching.

  28. Check door gaskets for all panels
