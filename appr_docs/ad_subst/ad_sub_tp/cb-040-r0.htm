Introduction 

The Major Maintenance Procedures "P4" outlines the steps required for dismantling and overhauling station equipment. An outage is always required to complete the work. This procedure details the major maintenance activities, which are performed on an annual basis as indicated by the Y1.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued.

  • Circuit breaker must be out of service.

  • Work must be done in a clean area or in workshop.

  • Make sure that the breaker is open.

  • Close and open the breaker to make sure that the opening and closing spring are released before starting work.

Tools and Equipment

  • One set of metric open end box wrenches size range 13 mm to 24 mm.

  • Adjustable spanner- One set of screwdrivers -Torque wrench - Fine file-Fine emery cloth -Wipers

  • Complete set of gaskets for the circuit breaker.

MATERIAL REQUIRED

  • One barrel of new oil (its dielectric strength not less than 40 kV/2.5).

  • Dielectric strength oil tester.

  • Some of gasoline, lubricating oil and grease.

  • Clean pails for changing oil.

  • Tin cans to hold bolts, nuts, studs, washers… etc.

Environmental Recommendations

  • Do not throw any oil into the sewage because the oil prevents the sewage treatment process.

  • Collect the oil that drained out of the poles into a barrel and return it back to the vendor to recycle it to save money.

  • Collect the oil used in washing the poles in another barrel and do not re-use it for filling the poles unless filtered, dried and its dielectric strength measured with an oil tester (not less than 40kV/2.5 mm).

  • Do not burn cloth used in cleaning but collect it and send it to the dump.

Work to be carried out

Inspection of Fixed Contact

  • After the breaker is put out of service, without draining the oil out, the following operations are to be done on each pole:

For Circuit Breaker type M12-31-16-1600A (Fig. 1& 2)

  1. Unscrew the four screws, which kept the metal cap (No. 7.1) to the insulating cylinder (No.5) and raise it up with the upper connection. Use spanner 32 (using Allen Key 10).

  2. Lift the fixed contact (N0. 8) up.

  3. Unscrew the screw (20 fig. 2) of the insulating cover of the fixed contact. Use screwdriver

  4. Take off the insulating cylinder (21 fig. 2).

  5. Unscrew the 4 screws that fix the copper cylinder, which cover the contact blades using screwdriver.

  6. Inspect the fixed contact elements and the contact springs:

  7. If the contact elements and contact springs are in good condition and not deteriorated, clean them with new oil and clean cloth, then reassemble the fixed contact as follow:

  8. Place the insulating cylinder (21 fig. 2) to the porter of contact.

  9. Fit the screw (20 fig. 2) to fix the insulating cylinder (21 fig. 2).

  10. Place the fixed contact into the pole in its previous place.

Replacement of fixed Contact  (FIG. 3)

  • If any element of the fixed contact is deteriorated, the replacement can be performed as follow:

  1. Unscrew the screw (51fig. 2) and pull out the tress (52 fig. 2) from the porter of the fixed contact (53 fig. 2).

  2. If there are only some pitting on the contacts fingers, it is necessary to file them by fine file without modifying the profile of elements and then clean out fillings and clean the fixed contact with new oil.

  3. If there are more than one of contact fingers is deteriorated, remove the two springs 54 of fixed contact to disassemble the fingers and change it. Use screwdriver

Reassembling Fixed Contact

  1. Place the contact fingers together to their original position to the porter allowing the two springs be around them.

  2. Reassemble the copper cylinder to its position to cover the fixed contact blades.

Inspection of   the Moving Contact

  1. Inspection of moving contact is not necessary unless a remarkable deterioration is observed on fixed contact. If necessary the inspection of moving contact is to be made as follow:

  2. Make sure that the breaker is open.

  3. Drain the oil out by removing the oil drain plug (14 fig 1) and allow the oil to drain out of pole into a container placed under each pole.

  4. Remove the 4 screws (Allen Key 10) that fixing the insulating cylinder to the lower terminal part and unscrew the screw fixing the insulating cylinder to the circuit breaker back cover (sp.24).

  5. Remove the 4 screws fixing the insulating cylinder to oil sump. Spanner 22

  6. Lift the insulating cylinder and lay it gently on a clean board, take care not to damage the lower face.

  7. Unscrew the screw that fixing the arc chamber to the Aluminum cylinder base and pull out the arc chamber.

  8. If the arc chambers are not deteriorated clean them with new oil and clean wipers, change it if needed by unscrewing the screw 72 and pull out the sliding contact, then locate the new one with the sliding contact in the insulating cylinder.

  9. Unscrew the two screws that fixing the Aluminum cylinder to the lower terminal and pull out the Aluminum cylinder.

  10. Pull out the lower terminal with the sliding blades for the inspection of the moving contact rod.

  11. If the tip of the contact rod is badly deteriorated it must be replaced.


Replacing the Moving Contact (FIG 3 & 4)

After operation described in previous item are carried out, the following additional operations are to be carried out:

  1. Unscrew the nuts (25 fig 3) from the opening spring (one nut is left screwing).

  2. The opening spring rotate manually in the direction to the poles until lost the force.

  3. Remove the ring (27 fig 3) then the pin (26 fig 3) with a needle nose pliers.

  4. With a spanner 27mm mouth gently rotate the main shaft (28 fig3) in the direction of the closing of the circuit breaker (in anti-clockwise). When the contact rod are lifted and the upper side of the lever (63 fig 4) is out of the oil sump the rotation should be stopped, then the guide and the conical shock absorber (61 fig 4) should be lifted, take care that the angle on the shaft of the oil sump is maximum 45o (see fig 4).

  5. With a needle-nose plier remove one ring (64 fig4) and pull out the pin (65 fig4).

  6. Replace the moving contact complete (12 fig4) and the gasket for oil sump (66 fig4) if needed.

  7. On the new moving contact put the guide and shock absorber (61 fig 4).

  8. Connect the lever (62 fig 4) and the moving contact with the pin (65 fig 4 ) and fit the ring (64 fig 4) with the special pincer rotate the main shaft in clock-wise up to the open position of the moving contact

  9. Fit the pin (26 fig 3) in the holes on the frame and on the opening spring and place the ring (27 fig 3) with special pincer, the opening spring (24 fig 3) rotate in the direction to the operating mechanism until the dimension 207 mm (see fig 3).

  10. Tighten the nuts (25 fig 3) of the opening spring and then reassemble the poles.

Mounting of Fixed Contact (FIG. 1)

  1. Reassemble the Aluminum cylinder and arc chamber together to their original place

  2. Reassemble the upper insulating cylinder to its position.

  3. Locate the fixed contact in the insulating cylinder and screw in the 4 screws (7.1 fig.1).

  4. Refill the poles with fresh oil to wash the three poles.

  5. Fit the insulating cover (10) with a gasket.

  6. Close and open the breaker two times then drain the oil out.

  7. Refill the three poles with new oil for operation.

Oil Refilling

As the dealing experience with this Type of circuit breakers we find that it can undergo four opening operations at short circuit without replacing oil , but you have to replace oil after five short circuit as follows:

  1. Before starting drainage the oil out of the poles be sure that the breaker is in open position, then remove the outlet plug (14 fig 1) and allow all oil to drain out of poles into a container replaced under each pole.

  2. Close the outlet plugs, open the filling plugs at the top of each pole and pour oil into each pole.

  3. After oil has reached the oil level indicators (9 fig 1) close and trip the breaker three times to wash the arc chambers, and contacts.

  4. Remove the outlet plugs and allow all oil to drain out of the poles.

  5. Repeat the previous steps until you find the drainage oil be cleaned.

  6. Refill the poles with oil of dielectric strength not less than 30 kV/mm , during the filling the breaker must be in open position and oil must be poured slowly into each pole till the right mark on the oil level indicators.

  7. Wait a few minutes and if the oil level has lowered, fill up to the right mark.


Mechanical Checking

Note:
All mechanical test operations must be carried only when the 3 poles are filled with oil

  1. Before putting the CB into service, it is necessary to carry out some hand closing and opening operations to make sure that the operating mechanism works satisfactorily and carry out some electrical closing and opening operations to make sure that connection are right and that the closing mechanism operates satisfactorily.

  2. For hand closing operation, insert the detachable crank (15 fig.1) into its place and manually rotate the main shaft in clock-wise until the free rotation of the shaft, the state of springs is indicated by a colored indicator on the cover of the operating mechanism (Red: spring loaded-white: spring released), then turn the (16 fig 1) in clock-wise (to direction of the mark "1". The state of the breaker is indicated by a colored indicator on the cover of the operating mechanism (red: breaker closed - white: breaker released).

  3. To trip the breaker turn the crank (16 fig 1) in anti -clockwise (to the direction of the mark "O"

  4. Check that the releases trip the breaker satisfactorily.

  5. Check that the auxiliary switch works satisfactorily.

  6. If closing springs are released, the motor when energized loads the sprigs and stops automatically as soon as springs are fully loaded.

  7. If the breaker works with series releases, adjust the trip setting of releases.

  8. Clean the mechanism parts by means of a paint brush drenched with diesel oil and petrol, be sure that the breaker is open and springs are released before starting cleaning

  9. Clean the metal parts by means of a clean dry duster and clean the insulating parts by means of another dry duster.

  10. After making sure that the operating mechanism is in good working condition and that the pole are filled with oil at the right level, the breaker can be put into service.

  11. Finally record all that has been done on the maintenance card of the circuit breaker.
