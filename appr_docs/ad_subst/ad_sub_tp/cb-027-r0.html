Introduction     

This document lists the equipment inspection checklist I2, which must be performed on an annually bases as indicated by Y1 in the title block.

Safety Precaution

  • A work permit must be issued.

  • Wear your safety helmet & safety harnesses.

  • Safety fence with caution marks around the work area is required.

  • CB most be isolated and proper earthing at the two sides.

  • Isolate control circuit (DC voltage).

  •  Inter lock mechanically the electro-valves by means of levers provided for this purpose.

  • Use only fiberglass ladders.

  • Personnel carrying out the maintenance should be trained and qualified or directly supervised by some one who is.

Environmental Recommendation

  • Using solvents such as gasoline and others for cleaning the oil filter is dangerous, it will expose lungs to very hazardous and carcinogenic chemicals. You have to wear your breathing mask.

  • Don't burn the cloth used in cleaning; collect it and send it to the dump.

Tools and Equipment

  • Safety helmet, safety belt, and tool set, fiberglass ladders,

  • Complete tool kit/set, including a torque wrench.

  • Some of clean and dry cloth,

  • Special grease, (Some of Benzol).

Work to be carried out

  1. Lubricate the working cylinder using oil (13 fig. 3): unscrew the plug of the relief opening on its cover.

  2. lubricate the main valve (9 fig 2): unscrew the lubricating plug at the valve cover to it. 

  3. Isolate the horizontal shaft of the isolator (83 fig 5):
    -     Remove it's cover (85 Figure 
    -     Lubricate its bearings through the lubrication openings.

  4. Lubricate through the openings above the piston. for Air impactors, for all electro valves (11 fig 7),

  5. Drop 2-3 drops of Benzol on the Electro-valve shaft.

  6. Grease with special grease the following parts after cleaning with gasoline
    - Slide contact in the chamber (67 fig 4) after cleaning by gasoline.
    - Isolator driving shaft (37 fig 3) through the two lubricators fitted to the drive support, 
       cylindrical gear wheel and the gear rack in the working cylinder (13 fig 3)

  7. Geard segment (88 fig 5), and the bevel gear wheel (89 fig 5), First remove the cover of the isolator (85 fig 5).

  8. Check the tightness of all nuts and screws. Keep in mind that the joint and connections are likely to  be loosened due to vibrations.

  9. Check the work of the injector. Put the wet hand close to the nozzles and find out whether the air is streaming.

  10. Check the oil level in shock absorbers (20 fig 4) which must reach the screw hole .

  11. Check the liquid level in water resistance it must be between the minimum and maximum marks on the glass of the indicator and add liquid if needed.

  12. Upon these inspections carry out some closing and openings operation control the position of the isolator in vertical position.

  13. Perform timing test and micro ohm on CB and compare them with values in the test certificate.

  14. Record all results on maintenance certificate

Notes:      Closing time      0.25 sec,                   Opening time    0.07 sec
