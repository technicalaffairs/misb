Introduction

    This procedure is considered as a minor maintenance P1. It should be done every year Y1 in addition to activities mentioned in the daily/weekly Inspection documents.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, qualified maintenance staff carry the suitable safety ID category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required,

  • Wear standard personal safety and healthy equipment as helmet, safety shoes, rubber gloves, ... etc.

  • Before doing maintenance, inspection, or service work on the unit be sure that the starting switch is in OFF position to prevent the unit from accidentally starting.

Environmental Recommendation

  • Do not throw the sump oil to the sewage because this oil will prevent the sewage treatment process. Collect it in tight containers and return it back to the vendor to recycle it. Always remember that "This oil is load for your wastes but it's valuable material for them."

  • Avoid oil spillage to the ground because this oil spillage has very bad impact on the environment.

  • Using solvents such as gasoline and others for cleaning the 1st, 2d, and 3rd stage of air Head, and Valve, Inlet Air Filter will expose lungs to very hazardous and carcinogenic chemicals. You have to wear your breathing mask.

  • Don't burn the cloth used in cleaning, collect it and send it to the dump.

  • Fix any oil leaks to prevent pollution and save money of wasted oil. Oil leaks to the ground have very bad impacts on environment, mainly on soil and ground water and eventually on the ecosystem. "It takes only 1 liter of Hydrocarbon spilled in an aquifer to render 1,000,000 liters of potable water useless for human consumption".

Tools and Equipment
  • Tool set, Cloth,
  • Sodium Hydroxide Solution, Benzene,
  • New air filter cartridge,
  • Grinding Past, gasket set,

Work to be Carried Out
  1. Open the oil drain plug to drain the oil out of the sump (figure 2).
  2. Disassemble inlet air filter (figure 1).
  3. Disassemble 1st stage inlet manifold (figures 1, 2) one bolt Use spanner 19.
  4. Disassemble 1st stage discharge manifold (figure 2).
  5. Disassemble 2nd stage inlet manifold.
  6. Disassemble 2nd stage discharge manifold.
  7. Disassemble 3rd stage discharge manifold.

First Stage Air Head and Valve (fig 3)
  1. Unscrew the six cap screws no.s 4 & 5 of the first stage air head. Use spanner 19
  2. Pullout the air head and the entire valve.
  3. Unscrew the two nuts no. 6 of studs no.14 to disassemble the valve from the air head.
  4. Disassemble the entire valve by unscrewing the two machine screws no. 11.
  5. Clean the parts of the valve from part no. 7 to part no.14 in figure 3 using benzene and dry it.
  6. Check all parts of the valve and change any part if needed.
  7. Change the two gaskets no. 2 & 3.
  8. Reassemble the entire valve to the air head.
  9. Repeat all the previous to make maintenance to the other unit of the first stage air head and valve.

Second Stage Air Head and Valve (Figure 5)

  • Repeat all the previous steps1: 8 (First stage air head and valve) to make maintenance to the second stage air head and valve (the second stage as the same of the first stage) except that there is a metal ring with calibrated thickness between the valve and cylinder incase of canceling the forth stage clean it and reassemble it again.     Use spanner 14

Third Stage Air Head and Valve (Figure 7)

  1. Unscrew the six cap screws no. 3 of the air head and pull out the air head. Use spanner 19

  2. Pull out the entire valve.

  3. Unscrew the two screws socket head of the third stage no. 7 figure 7.

  4. Check and clean all valve’s parts from no.4 to no.11 fig.7 with benzine

  5. Make smoothing to the valve seat no.5 inlet valve plate no.10 and discharge valve plate no.8 using fine grinding past and clean all parts with benzine and dry it.

  6. Change O-ring no. 2.

  7. Reassemble the entire valve of the third stage

Fourth Stage Air Head and Valve (figure 9) If exist

  1. Unscrew the four cap screws no. 4.

  2. Pull out the entire valve.

  3. Check and clean all parts from part no. 2 to 13.

  4. Change air head gaskets no. 2, O-rings no. 3 and copper washer no. 11.

  5. Change any part (if needed).

  6. Reassemble the entire valve.

  7. Reassemble all stages in their original position on the cylinder.

Inlet Air Filter (figure 13)
  1. Unscrew the nut of its cover.
  2. Clean the filter element no. 3 with flow of air and benzine and dry it with flow of air or replace it if needed.
  3. Clean the oil bowl no. 4 with benzine and refill with new oil to its correct level.
  4. Reassemble the inlet air filter in its place.

Oil Filter (Figure 14)

  • Disassemble the oil filter set (figure 14) and clean its parts and change oil filter element no.5 figure 14.

Radiators (Figure 16)
  1. Unscrew the separator for the four stages no. 1,7,13,15 clean with benzine , dry with flow of air and replace it if needed.
  2. Clean and inspect for damaged fins for radiator.
  3. Clean the radiator tubes with flow of Sodium Hydroxide Solution,water and dry it with flow of air.
  4. Reassemble the separator.

Reassembling the Whole Unit
  1. Reassemble all manifolds (inlet, outlet) for all stage.
  2. Refill the oil again in oil sump to a proper level between Max. and Min. marks of the lube oil dip stick.
  3. Refill motor ball bearing with grease.
  4. Run the compressor manually and listen to any abnormal sound.
  5. Put the starting switch in “ON” position and operate the compressor and listen to any abnormal sound.
  6. Check every stage pressure on pressure gauges (figure 9)
        • first Stage 2 atm.
        • second Stage 11 atm.
        • third Stage 40 atm.
        • fourth Stage 160 atm. ( if exist)
  7. Check oil pressure (1:4 atm) on oil pressure gauge.
  8. Check for any air or oil leakage.
  9. Record the maintenance which has been done and all parts which have been changed in maintenance cared.

Before Putting the Unit in Operation You Have To Do All the Following Electrical Tests:

  1. Trip the main supply of 0.4 KV

  2. Trip the control switches.

  3. Check all termination.

  4. Check the terminals of cables 0.4 KV

  5. Check and clean the contacts of auxiliary relays in the control cabinets of the compressor.

  6. Check and clean the contacts of air pressure and oil pressure manometers.

  7. Check and clean the contacts of starting contactors.

  8. Check the signal relays.

  9. Check the starting time relay.

  10. Check the control circuit.

  11. Check the electrical valves and testing them.

  12. Adjust the air pressure stages as follows.

    • First stage 2 atm.

    • Third stage 11 atm.

    • Fourth stage 40 atm .

    • Oil pressure 1:4 atm and oil temp. 80° C

  13. Adjust the delay time between starting and loading the stages (about 30 sec).

  14. Test the operating of compressors automatically from control board.

  15. Put the compressor in service
