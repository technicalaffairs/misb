Introduction

This document outlines the major maintenance procedure P2, to be carried out every twenty years as indicated by Y20 in the above titled block. The first draft initiated by Mahmoud Awad Salama at Sandob 220kv substation, Delta zone.

The major maintenance will be done after 3000 operations or 20 years, service in the case of breakers operated more than 1000 times a year, after a maximum of 6000 operations.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued,

  • The qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category, 

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area,

    •
  • Wear your safety helmet, shoes .... etc
  • Isolate the breaker.
  • Discharge the capacitors by reclosing the breaker.
  • Earth and short-circuit the breaker in accordance with VDE 105, part 1.
  • Disconnect the auxiliary supply by tripping the line-side MCB
  • Reduce the oil pressure of the electro hydraulic operating system to zero by operating pressure release valve.
  • Transfer the SF6 to a service unit and vent the pole columns. If no such unit is available let the SF6 escape into the open
  • Comply with all the local safety regulations

Points to be noted

  1. The spring washers, split pins and tab washers of any screwed joint opened for maintenance pur-poses must be replaced.

  2. The same applies to all seals and gaskets exposed in the process. All open breaker parts should be covered to prevent the ingress of dirt while assembly is in progress.

  3. During normal operation no dust should result from switching. If dust is found after repeated interruption of high short-circuit currents, proceed as follows:
     - Any switching dust should be removed immediately the breaker is opened and any subassemblies have been removed.
     - When this dust is exposed to the air for some time it will absorb moisture and can then only be removed with difficulty.
     - Gloves should be worn.
     - Remove the dust with a piece of cloth or use a vacuum cleaner. Don't stir up unnecessarily.
     - Throw the dusty cloth into a refuse bin immediately afterwards.

Tools and Equipment

  • Safety helmet, shoes .... etc, complete tool set.
  • SF6 gas detector, timing test equipment, dew point, decomposition, contact DC resistance

Work to be Carried Out

  1. General checks for the circuit breaker. Figure 1

  2. Read and record the number of make-and-break operations and any particular occurrences            

  3. Check SF6 filling.   SF6 gas pressure = 6.5bar

    4.

  4. Draw off the SF6 gas

  5. Open all interrupter units (fig 2) and make a visual check of the contact system and the blast cylinder fig 3.

  6. Check the sliding faces of the crosshead and the coupling pins and bearings for signs of wear

  7. Replace filters 22.31 Figure 2

  8. Examine and clean the operating rod.

  9. Auxiliary switch: Sparingly oil the bearings, examine and grease the coupling linkage.

  10. Reassemble all interrupter units, and fill in the SF6.    

  11. Make a visual leakage check of the hydraulic system.

  12. Drain the oil and clean both the oil tank and the intake filter.       

  13. Examine the hydraulic storage cylinder, the main valves and the operating cylinders.

  14. Fill in new oil.

  15. Check the provisional Nitrogen pressure.   Alarm of Loss of N2 at 345bar

  16. Raise the oil pressure in the storage cylinder to the nominal level.  

  17. Measure the current input of the motor at this pressure.

  18. Carry out test operations for electro hydraulic operating mechanism. 
    for hydraulic oil alarm at 273bar, lockout at 253bar

    19.

  19. Operate the breaker at reduced operating voltage control.

  20. Check the operating pressure of the safety valve.

  21. Check the inner sealing of the hydraulic system.

  22. Check the operating values of the SF6 density monitors and oil pressure monitors. 
    for SF6: alarm at 5.7 bar, blocking 5.5bar
    for hydraulic oil alarm at 273bar, lockout at 253bar

  23. Check the breaker pressure gauge for SF6 gas pressurized oil.

  24. Check the current input of the anti-condensation heaters: check the heating effect.

  25. Check the enforced triple-pole operation feature and the anti-pumping feature.

  26. Check all fully assembled breaker for SF6 gas leaks.

  27. Check the seal assembly of driving rod for SF6 gas leaks.

  28. Check that the external connecting leads are seated firmly.

  29. Tighten the internal terminals in the control unit.

  30. Check the paint finish and touch up where necessary.

  31. Carryout all the timing test: 90ms for CB closing, 50ms for opening, dew point, decomposition, contact dc resistance,  and air content test.
