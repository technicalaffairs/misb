Introduction

This document lists the equipment inspection steps I1 for GIS Circuit Breaker 66 Kv type 8DN8 EGK and GIS Circuit Breaker 220kv type 8DP3 Siemens, which must be performed every six months bases as indicated by M6 in the title block.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued.

  • CB must be fully isolated.

  • Control Power disconnected.

  • Grounding switch must be closed on both sides of CB to ensure it is properly grounded.

  • Personal protective equipment must be used (safety shoes, Helmet, breathing mask, safety glasses)

  • Maintenance staff should be well trained and qualified or directly supervised by some one who is.

  • Caution marks must be hanged on the work place, safety rope around the work place.

Tools and Equipment

  • Vaccum cleaner up to 2000watt,

  • Tourqe Wrench,

  • Pressure Gauge, Hydralic oil,

  • Compete Set of Tools,

  • Gas detector, Emergency Light,

  • Dew point, Acidity and Air Content Equipment, 

  • Paint nd paint bruhes.

Work to be Carried Out

  1.  Record number of make-break operation (as per operation counter or requested by system management).

  2. Carry out visual check.

  3. Record any special events (as requested by system management).

  4. Check hydraulic operating system for external leakage (pipes, valves, etc.).

  5. Check hydraulic operating system for internal leakage in on and off positions (pressure loss per hour at nominal pressure and with oil pump set switched off).

  6. Check gas filling of main module using a precision pressure gauge.

  7. Top with gas, if necessary.

  8. Trace and eliminate leak, if necessary.

  9. Gradually reduce oil pressure in hydraulic system to zero. Checking the operation of pressure monitors for lockouts and signals: 
    - No.5 "General lockout (oil)"
    - No.6"Lockout (oil)"
    - No7"Oil delivery faulted".

  1. Check oil level in oil tank 5.27 (1-0410) (hydraulic operating system pressureless. (Off state).

  2. In this state check pipe couplings of hydraulic operating system retighten as necessary using a torque wrench (see "General installation instructions".

  3. Vent hydraulic operating system including oil pump.

  4. Start oil pump set, 5.137.321 , 5.137.339, (1-1210) and check priming pressure of nitrogen filling in hydraulic storage cylinder 5.33 (1-0410) using a precision gauge.

  5. If necessary replenish nitrogen filling in hydraulic storage cylinder 5.33 (1-0410).

  6. Check safety valve 45.249.11 (1-1510) (opening and closing pressure).

  7. Replace position switch S2, part. No5.137 .303.9 (1-1210), of volume switch 5.137.303 on electric motor 5.137.321 (1-1210)of the oil pump set.

  8. Carry out test operations.

  9. Measure pressure of gas filling (final check).

  10. Check all remade joints (covers and flanges) for any signs of leakage. (final check).

  11. Check hydraulic operating system for external leakage (final check).

  12. Secure pressure release valve 45.249.17 (1-1510) (final check).

  13. Measure moisture content of the gas (final check).

  14. Measure air content of the gas (final check).

  15. Paint finish and anti-corrosion protection to touched up by customers personnel.

The following alarms occurs at 

- Laking oil pressure alarm at 303 bar.

- Blocking of  ON and OFF alarm at 273 bar. 

- Nitrogen (N2) leakage alarm at 345 bar.

- Critical oil pressure alarm 253 bar.
