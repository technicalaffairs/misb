Introduction

This document lists the equipment Inspection checklist I1, which must be performed from one month to 3 months as indicated by M1:3 in the title block. The first revised date 13 May 2002

Safety Precaution 

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area,

  • Transformer isolation and proper earthing is required.

  • Wear safety helmet, shoes, breathing mask, and harness when cleaning suspension insulators.

  • There is a danger of falling into the oil containment pit when working near large power transformers.

  • There is danger of falling off the top of the transformer when cleaning insulators, wear your safety belt.

  • It is recommended to use only fiberglass (PVC) ladders when working in the switchyard.

Tools and Equipment

  • Safety helmet, safety belt, and tool set

  • PVC ladders, breathing mask, rags

Work to be carried out

  1. Check for signs of oil leaks.

  2. Check oil levels in tank, conservator and oil filled bushings if possible.

  3. Check oil temperature and winding temperature indicators, record maximum temperature indication from drag hand before resetting drag hand.

  4. Make a visual inspection of all cooling fans.

  5. Remove any debris from fan screens.

  6. Switch on cooling fans manually and listen for abnormal noise.

  7. Inspect the transformer control cabinet, check the condition of door seal blows out the inside of the control cabinet with compressed air using moderate pressure.

  8. Perform the same inspection as in step 7 on the control cabinet of the tap changer.

  9. Check the condition of the silica gel breather, replace silica-gel if one or more sections are discolored (from white to pink).

  10. Check for correct level of sealing oil in lower glass cup.

  11. Check for correct level in all high voltage bushings.

  12. Thoroughly clean all porcelain surfaces of all bushings.

  13. Return transformer bank to normal service.
