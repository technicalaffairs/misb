Introduction

This procedure details the minor maintenance procedure P1, which are performed on a 4-month basis as indicated by the M4.

This procedure documents is the first level of maintenance performed on this type of transformer. The first revision for this document was 19 May 2002      

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounded the work area is required.
  • Transformer isolation and proper earthing is required from both sides.
  • Wear your safety helmet and safety belt when cleaning suspension insulators.
  • Using fiberglass PVC Ladders when working in the switchyard.

Tools and Equipment

  • Voltage indicator.

  • Portable earths for the 3 phases.

  • Extension Ladders (made of non-conductive material).

  • Complete tool/set, including a torque wrench and screwdrivers.

  • Fine cloth for cleaning purposes.

Environmental Recommendation

  • Before starting the cleaning of all porcelain surfaces of all transformer, wall bushings, insulators, neutral CT, earthing switches and contacts, wear your breathing mask to protect lung tissues from the dust particulate. Don't bum the cloth used in cleaning. Collect it and send it to the dump.

  • Fix any oil leaks to prevent pollution and save money of wasted oil. Oil leaks to the ground have very bad impact on the environment, mainly on soil and ground water and eventually on the ecosystem. "It takes only I liter of Hydrocarbon spilled in an aquifer to render 1,000,000 liters of potable water use less for human consumption".

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Clean all porcelain surfaces of all transformer, wall bushings, and insulators.

  2. Clean neutral CT earthing switches check that all bolted connections are tight.

  3. Inspect the transformer control cabinet, blow out the inside of the control cabinet using compressed air.

  4. Clean contacts of all neutral disconnect switches lubricate all bearing points.

  5. Check for signs of oil leaks.

  6. Check oil level in tank, conservator and all filled bushings.

  7. Check oil temperature and blinding temperature indicators. Record maximum temperature indicators.

  8. Make visual inspection of all cooling fans, remove any debris from fan screen. Switch on cooling fans manually and listen for abnormal noise.

  9. Check the condition of the silica gel if one or more sections are discolored. Check for the correct oil level in bottom of oil cup.

  10. Check breakdown voltage of insulating oil (every one year).

  11. Inspect and clean tap changer control cabinet. Check all electrical connections for tightness. Check motor operating mechanism.

  12. Take oil samples for laboratory analysis and gas analysis for main tank and tap changer.

  13. Perform a complete tap changer from highest and lowest and back to the position it was found in, this action will clean all internal contacts of the tap changer.

  14. Checking the earthing connections and making sure that the cross-sections area of the earthing copper conductor is not less than 95 mm2.
