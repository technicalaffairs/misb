Introduction 

The purpose of this document is to describe the various inspection/maintenance procedures (General Inspections), which must be performed on this fire protection equipment. The maintenance/inspection requirements for daily D1, monthly M1, 3month M3 and annual Y1 intervals are included in this document

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should be carry the Suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear your safety and healthy equipments safety helmet, safety shoes …. etc

  • Transformer outage is required.

  • Never perform any work on the transformer before isolating the fire protection system.

  • You must carry portable fire extinguisher when entering the transformer room after the fire.

  • Vent the flooded transformer room before entering it after the fire.

  • Any person inside the transformer room must immediately leave it after hearing the fire alarm. 

  • Any doors leading to the room must be kept closed.

  • To prevent the accidental discharge of the system, do not touch the pilot cylinder weighing device inside the transformer room.

Tools and Equipment

  • Safety Helmet, Safety Shoes.

Work to be carried out

Daily inspection    (I1-D1)

Check for the correct weight of the CO2 cylinders, the cylinder must be taken and recharged, if  a loss of CO2 discharge is detected.

 

 

Monthly inspection   (I2-M1)

Check the weight of the pilot cylinders.

3 Month Inspection  (I3-M3)

Check freedom of movement of the fusible link wire rope.

Annual Inspection    (I4-Y1)

  1. Perform a functional test of the system manually by discharging one or two cylinders. You must follow these steps:

  2. Make sure that the stand- by CO2 cylinders are fully charged.

  3. Unscrew the pneumatic release device of the second or third cylinder only and carefully hang it down by its connecting hose.

  4. Turn slowly the valve of the required pilot cylinder which is responsible for operating the system on the transformer, by its hand-wheel counter-clockwis e fully open.

  5. Observe the operating of the drop curtain during the test.

Installing new or Recharged C02 cylinders

  1. Remove the transport caps from the cylinder valves.

  2. Install the weighing bracket and secure the cylinders into the bracket.

  3. Screw on the connection hose between the cylinder valves and the CO2 manifold.

  4. Install the cylinder valve opening device. Make sure the lever is aligned with the slot in the valve head so it can move to the horizontal position when required.

  5. Insert the connecting pin of the tie-rods and the saw-toothed slide.

  6. Ensure that the whole assembly is free to move.

  7. Remove the safety pins to release the manual valve heads. Be sure that all systems are in working order and the detection system is switched back to the ON position (to prevent accidental gas discharge ).

  8. Reset the electrical discharge trigger switch manually by pressing down the button provided at  the base of this contact switch.
