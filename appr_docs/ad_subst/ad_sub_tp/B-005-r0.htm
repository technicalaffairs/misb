Introduction

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, qualified maintenance staff carry the suitable safety ID category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required,

  • Use standard personal safety and healthy equipment as helmet, safety shoes, goggles, protective clothing, rubber gloves

    •

  • Don't smoke in the battery room.

  • Don't place any tools on top of battery cells (Danger of short circuit).

  • Never use the same tools on alkaline cells, you may have used on acid cells.

  • All tools will be used must be insulated.

Tools and Equipment

  • Standard personal safety and healthy equipment

  • Battery Tool set, soft brush, Vaseline

Work to be Carried out

  1. Read total voltage of bank and record it on battery maintenance card.

  2. Disconnect AC supply to battery charger.

  3. Inspect the container of cells for cracks and leakage of electrolyte.

  4. If you discover a defective cell use the recommended procedure to replace with new cell.

  5. Inspect electrolyte level and record density for each cell, add distilled water if level is below normal.

  6. Inspect inter-cell connectors and terminals of cells and clean any salt deposits with distilled water.

  7. Check terminal connections for tightness.

  8. Clean terminal connection with soft brush.

  9. Check and add a thin film of Vaseline to the terminal connections.

  10. Check for vent cap damage.

  11. Read voltage per cell and record it on battery maintenance card.

  12. Again read total voltage of bank and record on battery maintenance card and compare the reading from step 1.

  13. In case of 48 volt battery if voltage is below 48 volts, place battery bank on float (fast) charge for 4-5 hours after which you switch the charger back on trickle (normal charge).

  14. In case of 220 volt battery if voltage is below 220 volts, place battery bank on float (fast) charge for 4-5 hours after which you switch the charger back on trickle (normal charge).

    15.

  15. Compare the present readings with the last readings.

  16. Return battery bank to regular service.
