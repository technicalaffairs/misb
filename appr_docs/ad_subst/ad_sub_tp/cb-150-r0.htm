Introduction

This document outlines the  major maintenance Procedures P2, which must be performed every 12 year or after operations at rated 2000 as indicated by Y12 in the above title block

The first draft initiated by eng. Ibrahem Ibrahem Shehata, tested  at Erouq El-Solb substation 220kv West Canal.
 and revised as kataloge recommended by quality control eng. Mohamed Omran, accepted by general director eng. Sohair Nageb Zaki and approved by eng. Farg Aly El-Azab Head Sector of West Canal Zone Network, ETEC.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable ID safety category, 

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area,

  • Wear your safety helmet, rubber gloves, protective clothing, gas mask and breathing equipment shoes .... etc

  • Isolate the breaker

  • The position indicator of the closing spring shows (discharged)

  • Earth both sides of the breaker

  • Disconnect supply and motor voltage

  • When open the pole column, pump the SF6 gas in service unit and evacuate the column, then ventilate the pole columns

  • Remove decomposition product by apiece of cloth or use a vacume cleaner replacement of the arcing contacts in closed, dry and dust free rooms only don't replace it in the substation in inclement weather condition 

Tools and Equipment

  • Safety helmet, shoes,

  • Gas detector

  • Torque wrench 30 - 150 Nm, Torque wrench 150 - 650

  • Wrench for fixed arcing contact

  • Revision set, Adjusting device closing spring

  • Revision trestle, Cleaning alcohol

  • CRC anti corrosion spray, Loctite green and blue

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Open the pole column in closed, dry and dust - free rooms only. The arcing contacts can easily be replaced by sing the revision trestle (fig B3)

  2. Breaker in end - position off

  3. Closing spring "discharged"

  4. Store the SF6 gas in a service unit and evacuate the columns. then  ventilate the pole columns

  5. Fix a rope to the upper HV - terminals of the pole columns (fig B1)

  6. Undo the four fastening bolts of the support / crank case connection

  7. Lift the pole column, transport to the workshop and mount them on the revision trestle in upright position.

Dismounting the complete breaking unit from the support unit

  1. Drive rod and lever (3) uncoupled and linkage cover sheet dismounted    

  2. Mount the revision set (2.07) at the support and couple it with the lever (3) and measure the length L1 to an accuracy of ± 2 mm.

  3. Dismount the filter housing fig (B1 item A-A)

  4. Loosen the bolt (1.08.34) by approx. 1.5 turns pull the securing pin (1.08.33)up wards and fasten the bolt again. the heads of the pins are now uncovered

  5. Pull the pin (1.12.06) out of the opening spring connection (1.11.06) see fig (B1)

  6. Fix a rope to the upper HV - terminals of the pole columns

  7. Undo bolts M 16 x 50 (fig B1, item A,1.15)take care the support porcelain may be damaged by falling bolts

  8. Move the interrupter unit manually into dismounting position by using the revision set. This position is reached when L2= L1 + 78mm fig (B16) pull the pin out of the opening spring connection beforehand

  9. Lift the interrupter unit by using a crane until a split of 60 ... 90 mm is reached between of the support  porcelain

  10. Move the interrupter unit in direction end - position off by turning the streching bolt (2.07.03) until the locking pin (1.12.02)is uncovered the locking pin (1.12.02) is uncovered fig (B1) locking position of the interrupter unit is the limit of the movement. The internal lever follows the movement, insert the opening spring connection (1.11.06) into the internal lever (1.08.10)

  11. Pull out the locking pin (1.12.02)

  12. Lift the breaking unit until the connecting piece (1.12.01) and the cap nut (1.12.04) are threaded out of the connecting tube (1.03.07) and place it on a suitable base

  13. Protect the support unit and mount the cover to provide water penetration

Change of the arcing contacts

  1. Loosen the expansion alts at the fixed contact (1.10)

  2. Lift the fixed contact upwards out of chamber porcelain, and clean the surfaces and remove any decomposition products

  3. Replace the fixed contact (1.1.02) Renew bolt (1.01.07) thrust piece (1.01.18) and PTFE - shield (1.01.05)

  4. Undo the connection chamber porcelain (1.02) support for contact (1.05) by loosening the bolts(1.26)fig (B2 item B)

  5. Lift the chamber porcelain and clean it surface from any decomposition products

  6. Pull the complete interrupter unit (1.03) out of the support for contact

  7. Mount a new interrupter unit in the support for contact (1.05)and change locking pin (1.12.02) and gaskets (104.1.07) and guide stripe PTFE (1.05.03,1.05.04) mount the chamber porcelain, fasten the bolts (1.26) and tighten with 210 Nm. and mount the fixed contact in the chamber porcelain

  8. Place the chamber porcelain and support for contact an a suitable trestle

  9. Couple the closing buffer (10.03) and the connecting tube with the locking pin (1.12.02) and pull them down until they lock the position of the locking pin is about 15 mm below the fasten fad of the support porcelain in fig(b3)

  10. Undo the six expansion bolts of the fixed contact. The locking bolts remain in the interrupter unit, screw three centering pins (10.02) into fixed contact fig (B3)

  11. Put three spacers (10.01) evenly spread on the upper flange of the chamber porcelain

  12. Guide the fixed contact (1.01) into the chamber porcelain and put it on the spacers. the centering pins will center the fixed contact

  13. Move the interrupter unit (1.03) by pushing the closing buffer (10.03) up to the lower support into centered position and secure with two bolts M16

  14. Remove the spacers (10.01) and drop the interrupter unit

  15. Mount expansion and locking bolts

  16. Pull the closing buffer (10.03) with the connecting tube and the locking pin (1.12.02) down until they lock. The position of the axle of the locking pin is now about 15 mm below the fastening pad of the support celain (fig B3)

Mounting of the breaking unit on the support unit

  1. Remove the uncover gasket (1.07) of the support porcelain

  2. Left the breaking unit and guide the connecting tube (1.03.07)onto the connecting piece (1.12.01), the drillings of the pins of the connecting tube and of the connecting piece must be in the line fig (B1, item C)

  3. Mount the locking pin (1.12.02)

  4. Drop the breaking unit and fix it with bolts (1.15) and washers (1.14) on the support unit. Fasten the bolts with 210 Nm

  5. Move the interrupter unit in end-position off by using the revision set. When drillings of the internal lever (1.8.10) and the opening spring connection (1.11.06) are in line

  6. Mount the pin (1.12.06) of the opening spring connection (1.11.06). (fig. B1 item B)

  7. Lock the securing pin (1.08.33) by loosening the bolt (1.08.34) by approx. 1.5 turns, push the securing pin down and fix the bolt (1.08.34) with 13 Nm. The heads of the pin is covered partially by securing pin

  8. Dismount revision set and mount linkage cover sheet                               

Replace the adsorption filter (fig. B5)

  1. Undo the filter housing (1.08.21), remove securing ring (1.08.20) and perforated sheet(1.08.05)

  2. Pull out the used filter cartridge (1.08.04), insert a new cartridge and replace securing ring and perforated sheet.

  3. Replace the 0-ring (1.08.36)

  4. Mount the cover and fasten the bolts with 10 Nm

  5. Evacuate the breaker, top up with SF6 to the rated pressure and put it into operation

  6. With a SF6 leak detector, check all flange-connections opened during the revision
