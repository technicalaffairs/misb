Introduction

This document lists the major maintenance procedures P1 for GIS Back Portion type 8DP3 Siemens, which must be performed every 10 years as indicated by Y10 in the title block.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued.

  • The CB and the back portion must be fully isolated and earthed.

  • Control Power disconnected.

  • Personal protective equipment must be used (safety shoes, Helmet, breathing mask, safety glasses)

  • Maintenance staff should be well trained, qualified or directly supervised by some one who is.

  • Caution marks must be hanged on the work place, safety rope around the work place.

Tools and Equipment

  • Vacuum cleaner up to 2000 watt,

  • Torque Wrench, Compete Set of Tools,

  • Pressure Gauge, Hydraulic oil, Gas detector, Emergency Light,

  • Dew point, Acidity and Air Content Equipment, 

  • Paint and paint brushes.

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Carry out visual check of the bays, where applicable also check parts outside the building, e.g. overhead-line termination modules.

  2. Record any special events (as requested by system management).

  3. Check gas filling using a precision pressure gauge. (All gas compartments with the exception of the circuit breaker module; 
    refer to MPIS document nos. CB-061, 062, 063-r0

  4. Top with gas, if necessary.

  5. Trace and eliminate leak, if necessary.

  6. Check reading of pressure gauge in normal pressure range against reading of precision pressure gauge.

  7. Check lock nuts of operating linkages for disconnectors, earthing switches for tightness.

  8. Motors assembly type 3DX601: Check control section and test heater output (without taking readings).

  9. Motors assembly type 3AC. 16: Grease and Oil. Test heater output (without taking readings).

  10. Check operating linkages for disconnectors, earthing switches for easy movement. 
    To do this watch the On-Off  indicators: Their movement must be uniform and smooth.

  11. Test-operate, i.e. open and close, each disconnector and earthing switch five times by its motor assembly (not by hand).

  12. Measure the maximum current input of the motor assemblies at rated voltage:
    Type 3XD601 during opening and closing.
    Type 3AC.16 during opening.

  13. Measure the opening and closing times of the disconnector motor assemblies at rated voltage:
    Type 3XD601 during opening and closing.
    Type 3AC.16 during opening.

  14. After test-operating disconnectors, earthing switches and high-speed earthing switches check their mechanical indicators for the 
    On and Off positions and note any discrepancies.

  15. Replace filter material of the voltage transformer module.

  16. Connect hose of service unit to maintenance point on the gas monitoring block.

  17. Pump out gas filling {All compartments except that of circuit breaker module and voltage transformer module.)

  18. Remove covers fitted with filter holders.

  19. Inspect and clean all parts inside the chambers (ribbed insulators, conductors, shielding covers etc.), and also the inside walls.

  20. Check moving and fixed contacts of disconnectors and earthing switches and high-speed earthing switches for signs of wear and replace
    as necessary. This also applies to any other moving parts. Parts that can still be used should be cleaned and greased sparingly.

  21. Place new filter bags in boxes and fit the covers.

  22. Evacuate gas compartments and fill with gas to about rated filling pressure.

  23. Check pressure gauges
    The following signals must be initiated:
    No.9 "SF6 pressure rising"
    No. 10 "Loss of SF6"
    No. 11 "Minimum SF6 density.

  24. Check to see that all connections at terminals and contactors etc. are tight.

  25. Check heater output in all cabinets (without taking readings).

  26. Check pressure gas filling (final check).

  27. Check all remade joints (covers and flanges) for any signs of leakage (final check).

  28. Measure moisture content of gas in all gas compartments (final check).

  29. Measure air content of gas in all gas compartments (final check).

  30. Paint finish and anti-corrosion protection to be touched up by customer's personnel.

  31. Bushings of overhead-line termination modules to be cleaned by customer's personnel.
