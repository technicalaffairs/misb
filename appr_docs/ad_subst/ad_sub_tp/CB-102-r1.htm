Introduction

    This document outlines the inspection activities as indicated by I1 in the above title block to be performed on annual base Y1. The first draft initiated at Demo substation, eng Abdel Tawab Ramadan. The first review at Nov 2007.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence with cautions marks surrounding the work area

  • Circuit breaker must be isolated and proper earthing at two sides of CB is required.

  • Interrupt remote control and motor circuits (this achieved by receiving the multi-pin plug at the left hand side at the operating mechanism)

  • Switch breaker ON and OFF manually in order to discharge the closing and springs. 

Tools and Equipments

  • Safety equipment "belt, shoes,...,etc".

Work to be carried out 

  1. Determine the SF6 gas pressure with the plug-in manometer and compare with value from the gas pressure curve. Top-up with SF6 gas if the pressure had dropped to about the level of the topping-up (refilling) pressure.

  2. Check the oil level, of the trip dash-pot (damper) 5-4 figure1 in the "ON" position, must lie above the center marking of the gauge glass. If the oil level is too low or if traces of oil are visible, the dash-pot must be replaced as following:

    1. Measure dimension "E" with depth gauge from the stud (5-5) to the rim of the dash pot housing figure 1

    2. Loosen locking-nut (5-6) figure 4 and relieve dash pot of the load by rotating the spindle (5-7) in an anti-clockwise direction see figure 2

    3. Dismantle stud (5-5) and remove the dash pot (5-4)

    4. Replace with new dash pot (5-4) and fil stud (5-5)

    5. Load dash pot by rotating spindle (5-7) in a clockwise direction until dimension "E" figure 1 is obtained secure with lock-nut (5-6) tightening torque 77 Nm

  3. Verify the function of operating mechanism heating element.

  4. Read and record the operating counter.
