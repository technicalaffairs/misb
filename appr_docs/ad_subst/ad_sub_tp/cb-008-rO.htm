Introduction

This procedure details the Equipment Inspection activities, which are performed on an annual bases as indicated by Y1 in the title block.

Safety Precautions

  • Work permit must be issued.

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounded the work area is required.

  • CB must be isolated and proper earthing at the two sides of the CB is required.

  • Isolate the DC control voltage.

  • Wearing safety helmet is required.

  • Use only fiber glass ladders.

Tools and Equipment

  • One set of metric open end box wrenches.

  • Torque wrench, Petroleum jelly (Vaseline).

  • Some clean and dry cloth.

  • New 3 injectors.

 Work to be carried out

  1. Visually check the compressed air feeding all the system (air tanks, tubes, connections, through valves, condensate pots, reduction valves, etc.) for leaks.

  2. Check the air flow through the injector. To do this checking close the CB again then separate the outlet tube behind the injector and put your finger into the fitting. If the injector is correct, a slight flow of air should be felt at the finger. If this is not the case you have to change the injector. Use Spanner 24.

  3. Drain out the air of the system after completing step No. 2.

  4. Check the accuracy of pressure gauge using a control pressure gauge.

  5. Check the accuracy of pressure relays using a control pressure gauge. The pressures at which the relay should respond are indicated on the label, or on basic wiring diagram (pressure relay closes the contact at 14 atm pressure drop, and opens the contact at 14.5 atm at pressure rise).

  6. Clean the breaker filter (27 fig 1) and dry it's filtering element (33 fig 18 no. 4 of 4) at a temperature less than 80 degrees C until humidity has evaporated.

  7. Lubricate slightly the shafts of the auxiliary switch and electro-valves.

  8. Visually check the auxiliary switch contract pressure in both positions.

  9. Tighten the nuts of the arcing chamber by dynamometric spanner with a torque of 2 kpm. and tightening should be carried out gradually by going around twice until all nuts are tightened.

  10. Perform micro ohm and timing test oc CB record the results, compare with previous test results and report/investigate significant changes.

  11. Record all results on maintenance card.

Note: Pressure relay closes the contact at 14 atm pressure drop and opens the contact at 14.5 atm at pressure rise.
