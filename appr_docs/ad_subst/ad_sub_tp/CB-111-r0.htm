Introduction

This documents out lines the check list I1, which must be performed on an annually bases Y1 as indicated at the above titled block.

The first draft initiated by eng Khaled Ibrahem Aly El Sherkawy, at new Sharkia 220kv sabstation, Sina network Sector, Canal zone and tested at 16 June 2002

Safety Precautions

  • Work order must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should the suitable safety category

  • Safety fence with cautions marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear safety helmet, shoes

Tools and Equipment

  • Persenal protective equpnet

  • SF6 gas bottle

  • Densistat seals kit

  • Oil filter kit, Grease

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Check SF6 gas pressure 7 bar at 20oC, to ensure satisfactory pressure level in the compartments. using SF6 bottle,

  2. Inspect poles visually, to ensure good insulation and to avoid risks of flashover on insulators. using seals kit.

  3. Clean insulators.

  4. Visually check electromechanical equipment to ensure correct operation of components.

  5. Check safety systems.

Hydraulic Controls

  1. Read and record counter readings

  2. Check oil level corresponding to the level mark on the tank and check for leaks,   using oil filter kit,

  3. Check tightness of all hydraulic components:

    Note: The actuator is equipped with recuperation drain for used oil. Oil presence is not abnormal, mainly after thousands of operations, leakage may appear on hydraulic connections when leakage remains after retightening operation, it is necessary to replace copper gaskets, connections and pipes.

    -Leakage one drop per day (12 cm3/year) is acceptable, one drop per hour not acceptable means 300 cm3/year

  4. Check control tightness of the discharged auxiliary tank.

  5. Check internal tightness of hydraulic components, deduct the average number of daily startings of the hydraulic pump, when the circuit breaker is not in operation, if exceeds 10, internal tightness if the drive is not safe enough, detect the cause of the fault.

Mechanical Controls

  1. Read and record the counter readings,

  2. Visually inspect mechanism,

  3. Visually inspect electrical equipment and safety devices,

  4. Clean and grease,

  5. Check for absence of corrosion to ensure fault current switch off,

  6. Check the general condensate inside the cabinet through ventilation and heating. In case of oxidation inside the cabinets:

    1. Check the proper operation of heating,

    2. Check the proper orientation of the ventilation inlets which should neither be closed up, nor receive direct water ejection.

    In case of continuous oxidation after check and repair, please contact Merlin Gerin
