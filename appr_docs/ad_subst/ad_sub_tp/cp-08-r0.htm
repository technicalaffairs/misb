Introduction

    This procedure details the items, which are checked every two years. They include testing and major maintenance. In addition to the procedures listed, it is expected the checks, which are done more frequently, would also be completed at this time.

Safety Precautions

  • Work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff must carry the suitable ID safety category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required,

  • Personnel carrying out the maintenance should be trained and qualified or directly supervised by some one who is.

  • Wear appropriate personal protective equipment must be used: safety shoes, helmet breathing mask, safety glasses

    •
  • Before doing maintenance, inspection or service work on the unit be sure that the starting is in OFF position or the wiring disconnect from the line to prevent the unit from accidentally starting.
Tools and Equipment
  • One set of open end spanners - One set of socket wrenches - One set of screwdrivers.
  • Fine grinding paste - Fine emery cloth - wipers.
  • Tin cans to hold bolts, nuts, studs, washers, ...etc
  • Some benzene for cleaning - Machine oil - grease.
  • Complete set of the compressor gaskets.

ENVIRONMENTAL RECOMMENDATION

  • Fix any oil leaks to prevent pollution and save money of wasted oil. Oil leaks to the ground have very bad impacts on the environment, mainly on soil and ground water and eventually on the eco-system. "It takes only I liter of Hydrocarbon spilled in an aquifer to render 1,000,000 liters of potable - water use less for human consumption".
  • Don't throw any oil into the sewage because the oil prevents the sewage treatment process.
  • Collect the oil that drained out of the poles into a barrel and return it back to the vendor to recycle it to save money.
  • Collect the oil used in washing the poles in another barrel and do not re-use it for filling the poles unless filtered, dried and its dielectric strength measured with an oil tester (not less than 40 kV / 2.5 mm).
  • Don't burn cloth used in cleaning but collect it and send it to the dump.
Work to be carried out

Air Intake Filter (Fig 6)

  1. Unscrew the nut of air cleaner cover and remove the cover (No 1).
  2. Remove the filtering element (No 2), clean it with benzene, and dry it with flow of air.
  3. Raise the air intake circular channel (No 3), drain the oil out and clean with benzene.
  4. Check the air cleaner gasket (No 5) and change it if needed.
  5. Unscrew the oil drain plug (34 fig. 1) to drain all oil out

The Cooler Unit (Fig 5)
  1. Disconnect the two blowing out let pipe connection (No. 14) and the two bosses (No.10) and all other connection related to the cooler.
  2. Unscrew the eight nuts connecting the base of cooler unite (1 fig 7).
  3. Withdraw the cooler unit out and clean its tubes with flow of sodium hydroxide solution using closed circuit and pump and dry it with flow of air.
  4. Disconnect all air pipes connections between stages and the air cooler

Tension Adjusting Roller (No. 59) of the Belt (Fig 2)
  1. Release the grease cup (65).
  2. Release the tension adjusting roller screw and remove the two belts.
  3. Remove the axial shaft of the tension-adjusting roller.
  4. Remove the two ball bearings from the shaft.
  5. Clean and check them and change if needed.
  6. Collect the tension-adjusting roller and grease the ball bearings.

Note: In some cases the fan don’t use the belt for working but using special motor (3 phase – 2.2KW - 220/380V – 1450 rpm) and its maintenance as follows:

  1. Check the tightness of the motor base bolts and the fan .
  2. Check the fan motor-ball bearings.
  3. Check the tightness of the cover of the motor.

 

The Fan (Fig 2)

  1. Release the grease cup (65).
  2. Unscrew the adjusting screw (63).
  3. Withdraw the fan shaft (62fig 2) with the fan impeller (60 fig 2).
  4. Remove the two ball bearings of the shaft.
  5. Clean check the bearings and change if needed.
  6. Collect the fan shaft with bearings and grease them.

First Stage Cylinder Head and Valve (Fig 1& 3)
  1. Unscrew the six nuts of the cylinder cover studs and raise the cover (4 fig 1) up and the central nut of the cover (socket wrench 17), which connecting between cylinder head and valve.
  2. Remove the valve boards (3 fig1) and remove the two gaskets (No 8 fig 1).
  3. Unscrew the two fitted bolts (No3 fig 3) and raise the upper valve board (2 fig 3) up.
  4. Remove the suction plates (No. 6 fig 3) then remove the delivery plates ( No.8 fig 3).
  5. Clean all valve parts with benzene.
  6. Make smoothing to the upper and lower board surfaces of the valve using very fine grinding past.
  7. Clean the upper and lower boards surfaces of the valve.
  8. Put the suction and delivery plates in their places and check its clearances (must be not more than 0.2 mm) OR replace if needed.

First Stage Cylinder and Piston (Fig 1)
  1. Remove the cotter pin that fix the two nuts of the end of the connecting rod to the crankshaft (50 fig 2) and unscrew the two nuts.
  2. Remove the half bearing of the connecting rod, pull out the first stage piston, and the connecting rod.
  3. Unscrew the four nuts of the studs that fixed the first stage cylinder (1) to the crank case (N0. 32) if needed and clean the inner surface with benzene and dry it
  4. Pull out the piston pen to separate the piston (No 2) from its connecting rod.
  5. Change the two copper bearings of the piston, the copper bearings of small end of the connecting rod, and check the connecting rod alloy.
  6. Remove the four rings (No 9) of the piston: (3) compression and (1) oil wiper.
  7. Clean the piston with benzene and change the four rings.
  8. Remove the two crank case hatch covers (no.35).

Second Stage Cylinder Head and Valve (Fig 3, 6)

    Second stage is similar to the first stage

Second Stage Cylinder and Piston (Fig 3)

    Second stage is similar to the first stage

 

Third Stage Cylinder Head and Valve (Fig 3 & 7)

  1. Unscrew the eight nuts of the cylinder cover studs and raise the third stage cylinder cover 10 fig 3

  2. Remove the copper sealing ring (No11fig3).

  3. Raise the third combined valve (fig 7).

  4. Unscrew the nut (8fig7) and remove the body, upper portion (1fig 7) carefully because of compressed spring.

  5. Remove the circular suction plate (3fig 7) and delivery circular plate (5fig7).

  6. Remove the suction valve spring (2fig 7) and delivery valve spring (6fig 7)

  7. Clean all parts of the valve and clean all suction and delivery plates holes.

  8. Make smoothing to the suction and delivery circular plates with grinding paste.

  9. Clean all parts with benzene and collect the valve.

Third Stage Cylinder and Piston (Fig 3)
  1. Unscrew the four nuts of the studs that fixed the third stage cylinder (No 16) to the cross head cylinder (No 17) and raise the cylinder (No 16) up, and clean it with benzene.
  2. Remove the cotter pin that fixed the two nuts of the end of the connecting rod to the crankshaft (No. 50 fig 4) and unscrew the two nuts.
  3. Remove the half bearing of the connecting rod and pull out the third stage piston with its connecting rod.
  4. Disconnect the two end plugs of the piston pin and pull out the piston pin to separate the cross head (No. 18) from its connecting rod.
  5. Change the copper bearing of the cross head, the copper bearing of the small end of the connecting rod and check the connecting rod alloy.
  6. Remove the 5 rings of the piston and clean it with benzene, and change the 5 piston rings.
  7. Unscrew the four screws that fixed the third stagpiston (No 15) to cross head (N0. 18), and pull it out.
  8. Unscrew the four nuts of the studs that fixed the cross head cylinder (N0. 17) to the crank case (No. 32) and clean the inner surface with benzene and dry it.

Crank Shaft (50 Fig 4)
  1. Unscrew the 4 screws that fix the flywheel cover to the compressor base and pullout the cover of the flywheel.
  2. Unscrew the three screws (sp24) that fixing the flywheel to the crankshaft sleeve.
  3. Pull out the flywheel (No 67) away from the crankshaft.
  4. Pull out the sleeve with its key.
  5. Disconnect the crankcase rear cover (79) and pull out the set of shims (80).
  6. Disconnect the drive pulley (46) from the front end of the crankshaft.
  7. Disconnect the crankcase front cover (44) by unscrewing 6 screws (sp17) and pull it out with its gasket (43).
  8. Disconnect the rubber tube joint between the crankshaft and oil pump and disconnect the copper tube between the gauge filter (38) and the oil pump (40).
  9. Disconnect the oil pump housing no. (No 41) with the oil pump and the vertical gear and the oil filter.
  10. Disconnect the oil pump driving gear (No 54).
  11. Pull out the oil distributing ring (49).
  12. Pull out the set of shims (55).
  13. Pull out the moving shell (57).
  14. Pullout the crankshaft (50 fig 2, clean it, check any corrosions for its surface and its bearing alloys.
  15. Disconnect the two counter weights (No 82) if needed and keep them away.
  16. Change the two ball bearings from the crankshaft (50).

Motor Drive (Fig 2)
  1. Disconnect the electrical connections of the motor and isolate it properly.
  2. Disconnect the 10 coupling pins (No.69 ) with its rubber sleeves(No.68) using special tool to separate the engine half coupling (No.70) from the fly wheel (No 67)
  3. Disconnect the four bolts of the motor (2 fig.7) to release the motor from the welded bed plate and keep it away.
  4. Disconnect the back cover of the fan by unscrewing the 4 screws sp.17 and remove the fan key from the motor shaft then pull out the motor fan.
  5. Unscrew the 8 screws that fix the cover of the back ball bearing and check the bearing then clean it with benzene and put new grease if it is in good condition.
  6. Pull out the engine half coupling (No. 70) by pull out its feather key (No. 75) and disconnect the front cover of the front rolling bearing and check the bearings and change it if needed or clean it with benzene and put new grease if there are in good condition.
  7. Reassemble the two bearings cover of the motor.
  8. Reassemble the fan on the motor shaft and fix its cover.
  9. Rotate the motor manually and check for any abnormal sounds.

Reassembling and Finishing
  1. Reassemble all parts of the compressor after changing all gaskets.
  2. Crankshaft and its accessory oil pump, oil pump housing, crank case front cover, and drive pulley (46 fig.2).
  3. Adjust the horizontal movement of the crank shaft by using the set of shims (80 fig.2)
  4. Reassemble the flywheel to the crankshaft.
  5. Reassemble the motor drive to the flywheel.
  6. Check the rubber sleeves around the bolts of flywheel.
  7. Check the 12 coupling pins and change any of them if needed.
  8. Put the flywheel on the crank end and check the clearance between the flywheel and the engine half coupling (it must be 2 mm).
  9. Reassemble the cylinders of the 3 stages to its places on the crankcase (32fig.1).
  10. Reassemble completely the connecting rods to their pistons then reassemble them to the crank shaft starting with the third stage
  11. Reassemble the valves and airheads of all stages.
  12. Reassemble the fan and the cooler unit (radiator).
  13. Disconnect the breather cover (31 fig.1) and refill the compressor with oil from the breather tube, refill the oil filter with oil and check manually the pump working.
  14. Reassemble the air intake filter (fig.6) to the inlet of the 1st.stage.
  15. Reassemble all air pipe connections (out let and inlet ) for all stages, Oil pump and radiators.
  16. Connecting rods, pistons, cylinders, valves, air head of all stages.
  17. All air pipe connection (out let and inlet) for all stages
  18. Run the compressor manually and listen to any abnormal sound.
  19. Run the compressor by the motor and listen to any abnormal sound.
  20. Put the starting switch in “ON” position and operate the compressor.

  21. Check every stage pressure on pressure gauges (fig. 5)
    First Stage 3 atm.
    Second Stage 12 atm.
    Third Stage 40 atm.

  22. Check oil pressure (2:4 atm) on oil pressure gauge.

  23. Check for any air or oil leakage.

  24. Record the maintenance, which has-been done and all parts, which have been changed in maintenance card.

  25. Record the number of operating hours

Before Putting the Unit in Operation You Have To Do All Following Electrical Test

  1. Switch off the main supply of 0.4 kV.
  2. Switch off the control switches.
  3. Check all termination.
  4. Check the terminals of cables 0.4 KV.
  5. Check and cleaning the contacts of auxiliary relays in the control cabinets of the compressor.
  6. Check and clean the contacts of air pressure and oil pressure manometers.
  7. Checking and cleaning the contacts of starting contactors
  8. Check the signal relays.
  9. Check the starting time relay.
  10. Check the control circuit.
  11. Check the electrical valves and testing them

  12. Adjust the air pressure stages as follows:

    13. First stage 3 atm.            Second stage 12 atm.

    Third stage 40 atm.         Oil pressure 2:4 atm.

    Oil temp. max 80oC

  13. Adjust the delay time between starting and loading about 20 second.
  14. Test the operating of compressors manually, automatically.
  15. Put the compressor in service.
