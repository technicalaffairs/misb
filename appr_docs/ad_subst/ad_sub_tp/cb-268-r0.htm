Introduction

This document lists the major maintenance procedures P2, which must be performed every twenty years as indicated by Y20 in the title block

The first draft initiated by eng. Mohamed Abdel-Azez, accepted by general director eng. Ahmed Ebrahem Soliman, and certified by eng. Esam Sadeq: Alx Network general director and MPIS coordinator.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required

  • Wear your safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, ... etc

  • CB must be fully isolated

  • Control power disconnected

  • Grounding switch must be closed on both sides of CB to ensure it is properly grounded

  • Personal protective equipment must be used ( safety shoes, helmet, breathing mask, safety glasses)

  • Maintenance staff should be well trained and qualified or directly supervised by some one who is

  • Caution marks must be hanged on the work place, safety rope around the work place

Tools and Equipment

  • Vacuum cleaner up to 2000 watt

  • Torque wrench compete set of tools

  • Pressure gauge, hydraulic oil

  • Gas detector emergency light

  • Dew point, acidity and air content equipment

  • Paint and paint brushes

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Record number of make-break operation (as per operation counter or requested by system management)

  2. Carry out visual check

  3. Record any special events (as requested by system management)

  4. Check hydraulic operating system for external leakage (pipes, valves, etc)

  5. Check hydraulic operating system for internal leakage in on and off positions
    (pressure loss per hour at nominal pressure and with oil pump set switched off)

  6. Check gas filling of main module using a precision pressure gauge

  7. Check reading of pressure gauge 45.129(1-1510) for gas and 45.209 (1-1510) for pressurized oil at nominal pressure against readings of precision pressure gauge

  8. Check gas density monitor 45.135(1-1510) using the service unit of the SF6 filling device A2 ("loss of SF6 "and" general lockout (SF6)")

  9. Pump gas out of main module

  10. Gradually reduce oil pressure in hydraulic system to zero. Checking the operation of pressure monitors for lockouts and signals
    No.5 "General lockout )oil)"
    No.6 "Lockout (oil)"
    No 7 "oil delivery faulted"

  11. Remove cover of man way opening 5.115 (1-0410) in partition plate 5.105 (1-0410) and prepare circuit - breaker module for withdrawal

  12. Mount W345 truck assembly on circuit - breaker module

  13. Undo partition plates 5.105 (1 - 0410) and remove circuit - breaker module

  14. Clean all the poles including their operating rods (switching dust)

  15. From an interrupter unit 5.261(1-0610) (remove fixed contact tube support 5.261.11 together with fixed contact tube 5.261.25 (1-0810) and check. In the On start also inspect guide tube 5.261.33 and the front end of blast cylinder 5.261.41 (10-810)

  16. If any damage is found dismantle, inspect and if necessary repair the other two interrupter units as well

  17. Replace all three coupling contacts 5.269 (1-0510).Clean coupling contacts and contact pieces 437.7 (1-0388) and grease sparingly

  18. Reinsert circuit - breaker module and bolt partition plate 5.105 (1-0410) to the chamber of the main module

  19. Remove W 345 truck assembly

  20. Reach through the man way opening 5.111 (1-0410). Connect up tile flexible copper links 5.249.11 and 5.253.11(1-0510) and refit shielding plate 36.75 of earthling switch 35.11 (1-0388)

  21. Place new filter bags in box 5.119(1-0410) and fit the covers 5.115 (1-0410)

  22. Connect hose of service unit to maintenance point 45.107 (1-1510) in automatic control unit of the circuit breaker, evacuate main module and fill with gas

  23. Check oil level in oil tank 5.27 (1-0410) (hydraulic operating system pressure less off state)

  24. In this state check pipe couplings of hydraulic operating system retighten as necessary using a torque wrench see "General installation instructions"                                                                                                              

  25. Take oil sample frame bottom of tank 5.27 (1-0410) at oil drain plug 5.137.141.15 (1-1210) and examine (red and clear = good condition, opaque and miky state = poor condition, record result)

  26. Drain oil from hydraulic operating system, clean oil tank and wash out filter 5.27.49 (1-1210)

  27. Refill with new hydraulic oil

  28. Vent hydraulic operating system including oil pump

  29. Start oil pump set, 5.137,+5.137.339 (1-1210) and check priming pressure of nitrogen filling in hydraulic storage cylinder 5.33(1-0410) using a precision gauge

  30. If necessary replenish nitrogen filling in hydraulic storage cylinder 5.33 (1-0410)

  31. With rising and felling oil pressure check pressure monitors for correct operation (lockouts and signals):
    No .4 "General lockout (N2)"
    No .5 "General lockout (oil)"
    No .6 "Lockout (oil)"
    No .7 Oil delivery faulted"

  32. Check safety valve 45.24911(1-1510) (opening and closing pressure)

  33. Replace position switch S2, part No 5.137 .303.9(1-1210), of volume switch 5.137.303 on electric motor 5.137.321 (1-1210) of the oil pump set

  34. Lightly oil sliding surfaces of mechanism parts between main valve 137.141 (1-1210) and auxiliary switch 137.369(1-1210), and also on /off indicator 137.429 (1-1210). Check all mechanism parts. (Do not oil auxiliary switch)

  35. Check the closing magnets 137.51and tripping magnets 137.69. (1-1210) of the control valve blocks : terminals, condition aluminum ring (setscrew), magnets firmly tightened

  36. Check effect of electric heaters, heaters, e.g. 45.199 (1-1510), without carrying out measurement

  37. Check to see that all connections to terminals and contractors, etc are tightened

  38. Carry out test operations

  39. Test for correct functioning:
    Operate breaker, check signals and lockouts, anti-pumping feature, on-off indicators, ... etc
    (This test normally extends to the control room console)

  40. measure pressure of gas filling (final check)

  41. Check all remade joints (covers and flanges) for any signs of leakage. (Final check)

  42. Check oil level in tank (final check)

  43. Check hydraulic operating system for external leakage (final check)

  44. Secure pressure release valve 45.149.17(1-1510)(final check)

  45. Measure moisture air contact of the gas (final check)

  46. Measure air contact of the gas (final check)

  47. Paint finish and anti-corrosion protection to be touched up by customer's personnel

The Following Alarms Occurs at:

  1. Leaking oil pressure alarm at 303 bar

  2. Blocking of on and off alarm at 273 bar

  3. Nitrogen (N2) leakage alarm at 345 bar

  4. Critical oil pressure alarm 253 bar

Gas Alarms

  1. Gas leakage alarm at 5.7 bar (normal at 6.5 bar)

  2. Blocking the on and off alarm at 5.5 bar (normal at 5.5 bar)

  3. Chamber gas leakage alarm at 2.5 bar (normal at 4 bar)

  4. Critical gas leaking alarm at 2 bar (normal at 4 bar)
