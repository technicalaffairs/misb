Introduction          

This procedure details the outlines steps required for the major maintenance activities P4, which are performed every ten years as indicated by the Y10. An outage is always required to complete the work.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category, 

  • Safety rope with caution marks surrounding the work area,

    •

  • Wear your safety helmet, shoes .... etc

  • Circuit breaker must be isolated and properly earthed.

  • Isolate the DC control voltage.

Environmental Recommendation

  • Before using a blower to clean all compartments of the circuit breaker from dust, wear your breathing mask to protect your lung tissues from the dust particulate.

  • Don't bum the cloth used in cleaning, collect it and send it to the dump.

  • Fix any oil leaks from connections, safety valve, bleeding washers, and motion transmission shaft to prevent pollution and save money of wasted oil.

  • Oil leaks to the ground have very bad impacts on the environment, mainly on soil and ground water and eventually on the ecosystem, "It takes only I liter of Hydrocarbon spilled in an aquifer to render 1,000,000 liters of potable water useless for human consumption".

  • SF6 leaks also have bad impacts on the environment.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete tool bag,

  • Grease, grease gun,

  • Pressure checking device,

  • Bleeding device,

  • Wrench for coil, Lockotite.

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Make sure that the CB is isolated from all the AC and DC sources.

  2. Open the oil plug at the lower part of the oil tank to drain the oil out and the oil pressure becomes zero in the mechanism (Figure 1)

  3. Unscrew the screws that fix the upper cover of the mechanism from the both front and backsides. (Figure 2).

Disconnecting the Accumulators of the Hydraulic Operating Mechanism

  1. Unscrew the 4 bolts, which fix the hose to the base of the accumulator (Figure 3).

  2. Disconnect the hose and keep it away (Figure 4).

  3. Unscrew the 8 screws that connect the accumulator to the CB (Figure 5)

  4. Disconnect the two accumulators and pull them away (Figure 6).

  5. Disconnect the mechanism door‑using screwdriver (Figure 7).

  6. Unscrew the 18 screws (Figure 8) to disconnect the upper cover of the mechanism.

  7. Raise the cover on 4 pins (Figure 9) facilitate the disassembling operation.

Disconnecting the Auxiliaries Contacts

  1. Unscrew the 4 screws of the Auxiliary contacts ballet (Figure 10).

  2. Disconnect the open and close ‑indicator Figure11.

  3. Fix the auxiliary contacts plate and the indicator in a place away from the front of the CB Figure 12.

  4. Disconnect all oil pipes as in (Figures 13, 14, 15, 16, 17).

Releasing the Ram from the Coupling Rod

  1. Unscrew the 6'screws (using Allen Key 10) that Fix the coupling between the mechanism and the pole (Figures 18, 19, 20, 21, 22 ).

  2. Pull Lip the coupling sleeve to release it (Figure 23).

  3. Unscrew the two screws in the upper part of the mechanism from the backside using Allen key 14 (Figure 24 a).

  4. Insert the special tool No.31 (Figure 24 b ) at the back side of the mechanism and tighten all its screws to fix it very well (Figure 24 c ).

  5. Pull up the coupling rod carefully using the socket wrench 24 of the special tool No. 31 till the end of its stroke (as in Figure 25).

  6. Unscrew the two screws in the upper part of the mechanism from the front side (using Allen Key 14) Figure 26 a.

  7. Insert the special tool (No.3 2 a) and tighten all its screws to fix it very well (Figure 26 b )

  8. Operate the special tool using the socket wrench until its arm touchier load the corn of coupling rod Figure 26c then 
    pull out the special tool no 3 1 from the backside (Figure 27).

  9. Using the special, tool (no.312a) continue pulling up the connecting rod until the end of the special tool stroke.

  10. Insert the metal bar (No.32 b) and Fixit death its Screws (Figure 28).

  11. Pull out the Specie tool (No.3 )2 a) (Figure 29).

  12. Disconnect all the Screws, which Fix the mechanism to the breaker chamber (Figure 3 0).

  13. Install the hydraulic removal parts (special cart) No. (33a Figure 31 a) and No. (33b Figure 31 b) 
    and (No 33 c Figure  3 2) to use the cart in taking the mechanism of the breaker.

  14. Fix the cart to the mechanism very well as shown in Figure (33, 34, and 35).

  15. Get the mechanism out using the cart (Figure 36) taking into consideration not to damage the hoses connected to the mechanism.

  16. Connect the mechanism very well to the winch hook (Figure 37), release it from the cart (Figure 38) and raise it to keep it away in a safety, 
    clean and dry place (    Figure 39).

Mechanism Maintenance

  1. Release the accumulator hoses from the mechanism.

  2. Disconnect the tank from the mechanism.

  3. Disconnect the hoses flanges.

  4. Raising the raw up.

  5. Disconnect the ram flange using heat and plastic hammer.

  6. Disconnect the mechanism base.

Reassembling the Hydraulic Operating Mechanism

  1. Install the ram rod inside the piston and install them in the mechanism base.

  2. Reassembling the 'Mechanism base and tighten it using Lockotite for all the screws.

  3. Reassembling the tank to the mechanism.

  4. Install the hoses flanges.

  5. Reassembling the mechanism holding cage.

  6. Raise the mechanism using the winch hook (Figure 40) and down it at the back of the breaker.

  7. Put the mechanism the special cart (Figure 41).

  8. Return the mechanism to its original place (Figure 42) and fix it very well.

  9. Release the cart and take it out of the mechanism (Figures 44, 45, 46).

  10. Retighten all nuts, screws, hoses and pipes (Figures 47, 48, 49, 50, 51).
