Introduction

This procedure details the major maintenance activities (2nd level of maintenance as indicated by the P2), which are performed for all Hetachi Transformers from 2:3 years as indicated by Y2:3, and MPIS documents # T-009-r0, T-10-r0

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • The high voltage CB and low voltage CB of the transformer must be isolated and proper earthing at the two sides is required.

  • Wearing safety helmet is required, and use only fiberglass ladders.

  • Be sure to ground the transformer bushing terminals with on-site grounding equipment and with ancillary grounding cable of a thickness specified by on-site regulations.

  • Personnel carrying out the inspection should be trained and qualified or directly supervised by someone who is.

  • If it becomes necessary to enter the main tank, be sure that the oxygen level is sufficient to provide safe This should be 12 point font size and should not proceed past the edge of the header, there will have to be some adjustments.

Tools and Equipment

  • Megger 2000 volt.

  • Binoculars or infrared scope.

  • Safety helmet, Fiberglass ladders.

Work to be carried out

Insulating Oil (Dielectric Strength)

  1. Using an oil tester, make a dielectric strength measurement of the insulating oil in the main tank (re. MPIS Document No. TB-SS-002-r0).

On Load Tap Changer (Diverter Switch)

  1. Check the dielectric breakdown voltage and neutralization number of the diverter switch oil, as described in MPIS Document No. TB-SS-002-r0.

On Load Tap Changer (Motor  and Shaft)

  1. Check for any worn, loose, or damage parts, especially nuts and bolts.

  2. Check that the motor-insulation resistance to ground measures at least 2 meg-ohms, using a 500V megger.

  3. Check all switch contacts. Refer to the instruction manual.

Bushings (Porcelain)

  1. Check that the porcelain elements on the bushings are clean and free from damage.

  2. There should no be oil leakage. If a gauge is provided, check and record the reading.

  3. Check SF6 pressure it should be in correct pressure as in manual.

  4. Check that there is no local overheating of any part of the bushings by using binoculars or an infrared scope.

  5. Inspect the terminals and top shields of the bushings, check that there are no loose connections and that the bushing terminals have not changed color.

  6. Check the pressure of N2 gas, the gauge pointer not be in red range and if the gauge pointer is in red range gas shall be refilled up to 0.2 kg/cm 2.

Bushings (Condenser)

  1. Check all the items listed in previous item

  2. Since the bushing is a condenser type, measure the insulation resistance with a 1000V to 2000V megger and the insulation power factor (tan delta) between the bushing terminal and the potential or test tap, this is to detect any deterioration of the bushing insulation.

Resistance Temperature Detector "RTD" ( Resistance Bulb/search Coil )

  1. Check that the temperature given by the RTD on the control room panel matches the indicated temperatures of the oil or winding indicators.

  2. Check that the RTD resistance value is correct for its temperature coefficient.

  3. Check for oil leakage and loose bolts and nuts on terminals

Winding Temperature Indicator Includes Relay

  1. Check the physical appearance of the relay, there should be no broken or loose connections nor any sign of internal condensation or rust.

  2. Check for breakage or damage to the capillary piping and rubber mounts.

  3. Make sure the relay operates at the preset values required for the transformer being used.

Oil Temperature Indicator  ( Includes Relay )

  1. Check the physical appearance of the relay, there should be neither broken or loose connections nor any sign of internal condensation or corrosion.

  2. Open the cover of the terminal box, and check the terminals and other connections for loose bolts. Also check for signs of condensation and rust.

  3. Check for breakage or damage to the capillary piping and rubber mounts.

  4. Make sure the relay operates and at the preset values required for the transformer being used.

Bucholz Relay

  1. Check for oil leakage and presence of gas, draw off a sample of gas from the relay and analyze it as described in MPIS Document no. TD-SS-003-r0, Gas analyze of insulating oil.

  2. Note that if the relay has already activated, a problem may exist inside the tank (e.g., with the core and coil) and a more precise inspection should be made.

  3. Make a function test to the relay to be sure that it will activate the alarm as first stage and trip as second stage.

Pressure Relief Device

  1. Make a visual inspection. There should be no signs of valve damage or corrosion.

  2. Check that there is no oil leakage.

  3. Manually check the operation of the auxiliary contacts to make sure they will activate the alarm and trip

Conservator Oil Level Indicator

  1. Check that the oil gauge indicates the correct level of the oil temperature by referring to the curve on the oil-level temperature graph on the transformer nameplate.

  2. Visually check the oil leakage.

Conservator Oil OR Water Volume

  1. If the conservator is a diaphragm type, open the drain valve and check to determine that no oil is found and the accumulated amount of water is no more than usually expected, if any oil is found and the water level appears excessive, inspect the conservator diaphragm and gasket, if there is evidence of component damage or a rupture in the diaphragm, replace the faulty parts

Tank

  1. Check for oil leakage and rust.

  2. Inspect the painted exterior to ensure that there is no rusting of the edges or seams

High Voltage Testing each 2 Years

  1. DC Resistance for windings.

  2. Turns Ratio for windings.

  3. Insulation for windings by megger (R60/R15).

  4. Tan delta for windings and bushings
