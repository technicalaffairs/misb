Introduction

This document details the major maintenance procedures P2, which must be performed every ten year as indicated by Y10 in the title block.    

The first draft initiated by eng Samir Ayad from Sabtia 1, 220kv Substation

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area,

  • CB must be isolated completely, grounded on both sides of the CB.

  • Ensure that the earthing switch connected on both sides

  • Wear safety helmet, shoes, breathing mask, safety glasses …

  • Change Remote position to Local,

  • Shut off air valves,

  • Remove DC plugs for each pole,

  • Discharge air from tank,

    • Silicon grease, combination pliers, telephone pliers, mechanics pliers, adjusting pliers, machinist’s pliers, 

    • Side-cutting pliers, special tin snip, lacquer scraping tweezers/32 ribs, tweezers, cross leg soldering tweezers,

    Work to be Carried Out

    1. Check SF6 pressure on manometer.    (7 bar)

    2. Check air pressure on manometer.      (10 bar)

    3. Check compressed air manometer with control instrument.

    4. Check SF6 manometer with control instrument.

    5. Check adjustment and function of manometer contacts.     375F3 for air,    F1 for A, B, C for gas
      SF6 contact manometer, temperature-compensated (F1) fig 1
      SF6-warning, 1st stage              pe    =    6.0 bar    (p1)
      SF6-operation, blocking            pe    =    5.5 bar    (p2)

    6. Check SF6 moisture content (desired level <-20oC).

    7. Check SF6 air content (desired level <2%).

    8. Check SF6 acid content (desired level <5-10 PP mg ).

    9. Check SF6 tightness at drive shifting drill–hole.

    10. Check for air and gas leaks.

    11. Drain compressed air receiver.

    12. Check high pressure flexible tube, if necessary replace,

    13. Check adjustment and function of compressed air pressure switch.   376F4   figure 3

      Note:    Adjustment work on the pressure switches must be carried out only by AEG-TFK staff

      Pressure switch of pneumatic system  (F4):         figure 3
      Contact 1:        air refilling            start            pe =     9.3 bar     (p6)
                                                         stop            pe =   10.0 bar     (p7)
      Contact 2:        auto-reclosing      blocking      pe =     8.5 bar     (p8)
      Contact 3:        "on" blocking                          pe =     6.9 bar     (p3)
      Contact 4:        "off" (operation)   blocking      pe =     6.0 bar     (p8)
       

    14. Check tightness of screw and pipe coupling.
      External diameter of pipe            Permissible moment
              10 mm                                        7 Nm
              12 mm                                      10 Nm
              15 mm                                      14 Nm
              18 mm                                      17 Nm
       

    15. Check refilling valve function: opened and closed and tightness. (Refilling valve 373 (Y0) figure1)

    16. Check refilling time as on the time relay setting (K2) fig. 2, "on" delay for refilling time monitoring.  ( t set approx. 2 times the refilling time after O-C-O )

    17. Check auxiliary switch for function and burning of contacts.

    18. Check screw coupling of electrical connections if necessary re-tighten.

    19. Check fixed main contact and if necessary replace. (1, 2),  figure 1

    20. Check movable contact and if necessary replace. (1, 2),

    21. Check insulated nozzle and if necessary replace. (1, 2),

    22. Replace molecular sieve filter (1),

    23. Carryout test switching operations.

    24. Check operating times.
      Make time                  120 ms +   10%
      Operating time                    40 ms +   10%
      Break time                          6      0 ms +   10%

    25. Checking the pressure drop when switching as the following steps.
      -       Produce operating pressure.   10 bar
      -       Separate refilling valve from voltage or close shutoff valves (371/372).     Figure 3
      - Operate the circuit breaker electrically.
      -       Read off values on manometer (375) five minutes later.    Figure 3
         At 20 OC the difference is about 0.6 bar for opening, and about 0.3 bar for closing the breaker.
      - Markedly increased pressure drops point to a fault in the pneumatic system.

    26. Check the mechanical ON, OFF position indicators.

    Remarks

    1.      Require opening of gas compartment.

    2.      Random check on circuit breaker with max operating duty further check only on negative result.
