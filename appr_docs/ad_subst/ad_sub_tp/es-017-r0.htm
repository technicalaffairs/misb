Introduction

This document details the equipment inspection I1, which should be carried out on an annually bases as indicated by Y1 in the title block.

The first draft initiated by eng. Nashwa Hussein Mohamed, tested by eng. Ahmed Abd Alla Fatihy, accepted by eng. Samir Mohamed El Azab: general director, and approved by EETC Cairo Zone coordinator eng. Ahmed Shelaby Faisal Head Sector of EHV networks.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear appropriate personal protective safety equipment as safety shoes and helmet

    • ... etc

  • Ensure that the earthing switch must be disconnected and properly grounded on both sides using portable grounds.

    •

  • Circuit breaker must be isolated completely and grounded on both sides.

  • Isolate the DC voltage

  • Change remote/local control switch to local position.

 Tools and Equipment
  • Complete substation maintenance tool set,
  • Multimeter, air blower, gas detector, 220 volt AC supply
  • End scope equipment, and moisture tester.
Work to be carried out

  1. Complete a visual inspection of all external components of the earthing switch, during this inspection check for any abnormal conditions, paint condition etc.

  2. Using the multimeter check for correct control voltage and operating voltage, the control voltage should be 220Vac, and the operating voltage 220Vdc.

  3. Clean the mechanism cabinet using the air blower, then auxiliary contacts inspected and cleaned.

  4. Check the control circuits through examination of all circuit wiring and terminals connection for any signs of overheating

  5. Check the operating mechanism (auxiliary switches, interlocks, ... etc) and check the operating rod (motion transmission shaft) looking for signs of distortion.

  6. Retighten all coupling bolts and joints on the operating rods.

  7. Check the SF6 gas pressure this value should be 4 bar at 20oC.

  8. Using the leak detector check the entire disconnect switch for SF6 gas leak.

  9. Complete a second function operation of the switch via Local and Manual control, checking that the position indicator is operating correctly, also observe any unusual operating noises.

  10. Using moisture tester, measure the dew point of the insulating gas it should be no greater than 15ppm as indicated in MPIS document TD-SS-004-r0.

  11. Measure and record the operating pressures of the SF6 pressure switch. the 2 settings, P1 should operate at 3.2 bar and P2 at 3 bar at 20oC

  12. Using end scope equipment, check the position of the main contacts after both open and close operations.
