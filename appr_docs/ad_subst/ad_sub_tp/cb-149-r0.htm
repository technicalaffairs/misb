Introduction

This document outlines the maintenance procedures P1, to be performed after short circuit.

The first draft initiated by eng. Ibrahem Ibrahem Shehata, tested by A forighn expeart at 07 Sep.1995  on the circuit no.1 of Ataka Cement 220kv substation, and revised by quality control eng. Mohamed Omran, accepted by general director eng. Sohair Nageb Zaki and and approved by eng. Farg Aly El-Azab Head Sector of West Canal Network, Canal Zone, ETEC.

 

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Safety rope with caution marks surrounding the work area,

  • Wear your safety helmet, safety shoes safety harness

  • Circuit breaker isolation and earthing is required.

    •

  • Before starting maintenance or inspection jobs requiring the enclosure to be opened remember to isolate and earth the switchgear assembly on which work is to be carried out, even prior to drawing off the SF6 and opening the enclosure. Release the hydraulic pressure and disconnect the auxiliary supply. Adhere to the safety regulations.

Environmental Precautions

  • Before using a blower for cleaning from dust, wear your breathing mask to protect lung tissues from the dust particulate.

  • Don't burn the cloth used in cleaning, collect it and send to the dump.

 

Tools and Equipment

  • Safety helmet, shoes.

  • Gas detector and vacuum cleaner

 

Work to be Carried Out

 

General Precautions

  1. The spring washers, split pins and tap washers of any screwed joint opened for maintenance purposes must be replaced.

  2. The same applies to all seals and gaskets exposed during external metal parts should be lubricated with Tecty1 506 at intervals of 2 to 3 years.

  3. All open breaker parts should be covered to protect them against the ingress of dirt while assembly is in progress. Any switching dust which has settled in the breaker and on the built-in parts during operation should be removed immediately the breaker is opened and any subassemblies have been removed. When this dust is exposed to the air for some time it will absorb moisture and can then only be removed with difficulty.

  4. Gloves should be worn.

  5. Wipe off the dust with a rag or use a vacuum cleaner. Don't disturb the dust unnecessarily. Throw the dust and the rag into a refuse bin as soon as the job is finished.

Visual Check

  1. Check SF6 pressure and, if the values do not tally, test the pressure gauges and check the breaker for leaks.

  2. Read and record the counter reading (where applicable).

  3. Draw off the SF6.

Interrupter unit

  1. Open one interrupter unit of each breaker pole, check the contacts and replace them if they show signs of severe are erosion, if contact replacement proves to be necessary, open the interrupter 6 units and inspect contacts.

  2. Replace filters.

  3. Refill the SF6
