Introduction

This document outlines the inspection checklist I2, which must be carried out on a monthly bases as indicated in the above title block by M1. The first draft has been initiated by eng. El-Sayed Abdel Moneam El-Geddawy and tested at 06 May 2002 for battery no.1 at Sheraton El Matar 66kv Substation, Cairo zone.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • No smoking in battery room or any things leads to fire
  • Using safety equipments, and isolated tools, safety goggles, rubber gloves, rubber apron and safety boots is necessary
  • Do not put any tools or metal things on the top of battery (to avoid short circuit danger)
  • Do not use the same tools for maintenance in alkaline cells and acidic ells  

Environmental Precautions

  • Wear your breathing mask if you have to use any chemical materials because it is dangerous, it will expose lungs to very dangerous and carcinogenic chemicals

  • Don't burn the cloth used in cleaning, collect it and send it to the dump.

Tools and Equipment

  • Personal standard safety equipments, goggles, gloves, safety boots, apron
  • Pieces of clothes and rags
  • Brushes
  • Lay - less or glass vessel and funnel Voltmeter (Voltmeter with parallel resistance is preferred)
  • Recording sheets as MRDS of MPIS doc # B-002-r0
  • Hydrometer for measuring electrolyte density

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Repeat the inspection that must be made according to MPIS document no. B-016-r0

  2. Disconnect the AC supply of the battery charger

  3. Record the whole voltage of the battery

  4. Inspect all cells from outside to discover if there is leakage of electrolyte, cracks in electrolyte container *body of cell)

  5. Record the density of each cell and check if the density is in the save values (the save range is between 1.16, 1.12 gm / cm3), write the values in the maintenance card

  6. If you find any cell has low or over density values (under 1.19 or above 1.12 gm / cm3), use electrolyte or distilled water to get the save value and record what you did
           - Cell no :                    - The density :                         - Treated or not :

  7. If you discover defective cells you must replace it with a new one (according recommended procedures to save people and equipments)

  8. Inspect the electrolyte level (10 - 15 mm above the highest of electrodes according to manufacturer), use distilled water to get the correct level and record that in battery battery sheets as:
            - Cell no. (that has low level):                              - Treated or not

  9. Inspect the connectors and terminals of cells and remove any deposits of salt (if exists)

  10. Check and tighten well the connectors between cells

  11. Clean tops of cells

  12. Lubricate cells terminals with a thin film of Vaseline

  13. Avoid coating the rubber parts with Vaseline, because Vaseline damages it.

  14. Read the voltage of every cell and record it in maintenance card (standard value is 1.3 v / cell

  15. Record the whole voltage of the battery, and compare it with the reading taken in step 3, write the readings in the maintenance card

  16. If the new reading of battery voltage is below 215V, you must put the battery under high charging position for about 6 hours, and then put the battery under normal charge.

  17. Record (again) the battery voltage after the high charging and compare it with the previous readings
