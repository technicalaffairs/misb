Introduction

    This document outlines the inspection and minor maintenance procedures P I, to be performed on a six-month basis M6. These activities in addition to the weekly and monthly inspection would also be performed at this time.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, qualified maintenance staff carry the suitable safety ID category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required,

  • Wear standard personal safety and healthy equipment as helmet, safety shoes, rubber gloves, ... etc.

  • Ensure compressor is properly isolated,
  • Do not wear loose clothing near the compressor while it is running,

Tools and Equipment
  • Cleaning material, rags, air blower, solvent.
  • New compressor oil, 12 litres.
  • Complete set of substation maintenance hand tools.

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Complete all the steps outlined in MPIS document CP-SS-006-rO, Monthly Inspection (12-Ml)

  2. With compressor completely isolated, change the crankcase oil by removing the drain plug and attached oil strainer. Also clean the strainer with solvent.

    3. Note: Drain the oil into a container ensuring proper disposal of the scrap oil and the solvent used to clean the strainer.

  3. Fill the compressor with new approved oil,

  4. After the oil change operation is complete, the compressor must be started several times in quick succession to prime the oil pump. This is required as the oil lines were drained with the crankcase.

  5. Inspect the air filter cartridge, if it is dirty, blow it out with compressed air from the inside outwards. If the paper elements in the cartridge are damaged, install a new filter cartridge. Ensure the filter cartage is properly seated after the cartridge isre-assembled.

  6. With the compressor at rest, check the oil level in the crankcase. The oil level should never fall below the sight glass.

  7. With the compressor running, check for any unusual noises or vibrations.

  8. Check for air leaks in piping and pipe connections.

  9. Read and record air pressure gauges and compressor running time as indicated on the hour meter.

  10. Clean the compressor room floor.

  11. Put the unit in service.
