 SF6 Gas Filling (Compensation) 
Using Standard Gas Cylinders 		Approved Date:     23 Dec 1996
Date to be Reviewed:   Dec 2001

Introduction

Any SF6 gas filled switching equipment may experience a gradual loss of gas pressure due to normal leakage. During routine inspections make sure that the gas pressure is:

  • 6.5 bars in conventional breakers 3AR1/EG or 8D4 breaker compartments in GIS equipment

  • 3.2 bars in other bus compartments of GIS equipment.

In case the above pressures values are lower or a "loss of SF6 gas" alarm has been indicated it is possible to refill (compensate) the leaked gas by simply using a standard SF6 gas cylinder. There is no need to use the large gas maintenance truck available in GIS stations. Use the following procedure below, the diagram will help you to make the right connections 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Safety Precautions

  • Work permit must be issued.

  • Use, only commercial g-d, new SF6 gas, moisture content will be less than15PPM and air content less than 0.05%

  • The filling of gas compartments should be done very slowly since the process will create a large temperature drop in the cylinder and supply hose.

  • For safe SF6 gas handling practice refer to Document No. TD-SS-005-r0

Work to be carried out 

  1. Clean the filling device (gas regulator and connecting hose)

  2. Check that the Cylinder valve No. 1 and The valve No.2 are closed

  3. Open the cylinder valve no. 1 for a very short moment to blow out the dust and close the valve again

  4. Firmly connect the regulator to the cylinder valve

  5. Open cylinder valve No. 1 and note the internal cylinder pressure on gauge (A) What should it be (260-320) Normal lower means 15 mpt 1

  6. Turn the regulator adjustable gradually and adjust for the required filling pressure indicated on pressure gauge (B).

  7. Connect the filling hose to the gas regulator

  8. Open valve (No.3) on the regulator gradually to fill the hose with SF6 gas, close valve (No.3) again

  9. Attach the other end of the filling hose to the measuring point on the side of the valve cubicle

  10. Again open valve No.3 on the regulator to fill the hose and all pipe connections up to the valves inside the valve cubicle

  11. Now open the valve inside the valve cubicle corresponding to the gas compartment which requires filling

  12. The corresponding pressure gauge will indicate which compartment requires additional SF6 gas if large amount of gas are added allow for temperature to stabilize

  13. When the correct compartment pressure is reached, make certain that the valve inside the valve cubicle is tightly closed

  14. If other compartments need topping-up in the same GIS breaker bay, repeat steps 11 to 13

  15. Close valve No.1 on top of the SF6 gas cylinder

  16. Disconnect the hose from the measuring point and screw on the flange cap

  17. Close valve No. 3 and disconnect the hose from the regulator

  18. Unscrew the regulator from the SF6 gas cylinder

  19. Return the cylinder, the regulator and the filling hose to their proper storage space

  20. Check any leakage of the gas from valve cubical or measuring point
