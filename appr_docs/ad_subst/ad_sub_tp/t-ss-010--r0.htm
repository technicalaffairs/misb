Introduction

This document lists the procedure details the minor maintenance activities (1st level of  maintenance as indicated by the P1), which must be performed from 1:2 years as indicated by the Y1:2. The first check is performed by MPIS Document # T-009-r0, the daily check.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Personnel carrying out the inspection should be trained and qualified or directly supervised by someone who is.

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area,

  • Wear your safety helmet, safety shoes, safety harness and use only fiberglass ladders.

  • Proper well fixed ladder should be used for climbing on top of the Transformer tank.

  • Wear your breathing mask before dry cleaning of bushings and earth point insulators.

  • The high voltage CB and low voltage CB of the transformer must be isolated and proper earthing at both sides is required.

  • While working at night ensure adquate lighting is provided for the entire work area.

  • Be sure to ground the transformer bushing terminals with on-site grounding equipment and with ancillary grounding cable of a thickness specified by on-site regulations.

  • If it becomes necessary to enter the main tank, be sure that the oxygen level is sufficient to provide safe working conditions.

Tools and Equipment

  • Dielectric strength oil tester.

  • Oil sampling bottles and all equipment mentioned in MPIS document no. TD-SS-003-r0 for insulating oil sampling.

  • Reference thermometer and bath of water.

  • Spare parts for cooling equipment (oil pump, cooling fans, etc).

Work to be carried out 

          

Insulating Oil   

  • Sample and analyze gas taken from the insulating oil for main tank and tap-changer (see MPIS Document no. TB-SS-002-r0 insulating oil deterioration).

On load Tap Changer (OLTC)

  1. An initial inspection of the OLTC is required after the first 10,000 tap changes or one year from start of operation, whichever occurs first. After that, the interval between inspections should be increased to 30,000 operation, or approximately 2 years.

  2. Check the log for the average number of tap changes per day. The recommended maximum is 20.If the frequency of tap changes exceeds 30 per day, readjustment of the voltage- regulating relay may be necessary.

  3. Check the breakdown voltage of the diverter switch insulating oil and the neutralization value of the oil. If in either case the insulating oil is found to have a breakdown voltage of < 40 KV/2.5mm, or a neutralization value > 0.3 mg KOH/g, replace the oil (see MPIS Document TB-SS-002-r0 for details).

Temperature Indicator

  1. Test the operation and accuracy of the indicator by inserting its sensing element into a bath of hot water and comparing operating temperatures with a reference thermometer Adjuster or replace the unit if any error is observed.

  2. Check for moisture inside the back of the gauge, there should be no condensation or rust.

  3. Check that all contacts, rubber fittings and the capillary tube on the indicator are in good condition.

  4. Make sure that the alarm and trip contacts (if fitted) operate properly, clean or replace them if they do not.

  5. When cleaning the dial cover do not use solvents such as ketones, thinner, and benzene, which may damage the cover.

Indicator Level

  1. Check that the gauge indicator shows the normal oil level, prescribed in the oil temperature graph on the nameplate near the main nameplate.

  2. Clean the dial glass, or replace it if damaged.

  3. Check that the alarm contacts are not closed.

  4. Check that there is no oil leakage or rust.

Oil IL Flow Low Indicator (If Installed) 

  1. Check that there is no oil leakage or parts damage. The indicator needle must point to FLOW, when the transformer pumps are operating.

General Cooling Equipment
( Oil Pumps, Cooling Fans, Radiators, for example if installed )

  1. Check that the oil pumps are operating and have no oil leakage or abnormal noise. If any abnormal noise is heard, most likely the oil pump bearings need replacing.

  2. Check that the cooling fans operate smoothly and create no abnormal noise or vibration.

  3. If any abnormal noise is heard, it is very likely that the fan bearings need replacing.

  4. Check for excessive buildup of grime on the radiator surfaces and clean it.

Water Cooled ( Cooling Equipment, if installed )

  1. Check that there is no water leakage or rust on the exterior, if necessary, disassemble the cooler as instructed in the instruction manual supplied with the transformer and inspect the interior for rust, corrosion, oil and water leakage and other problems.
