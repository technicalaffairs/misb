Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection steps I3, which must be performed on an annually bases as indicated by Y1 in the title block.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff carry the suitable safety ID category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required,

  • Use standard personal safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, goggles, protective clothing, rubber gloves ... etc

    •

  • Don't smoke in the battery room.

  • Don't place any tools on top of battery cells (Danger of short circuit)

    •

  • Never use the same tools on alkaline cells you may have used before on lead acid cells

    •

  • All tools will be used must be insulated.

    •

  • Never use a hydrometer used before on lead acid cells

Tools and Equipment

  • Personal safety and healthy equipment as safety boots, safety goggles, rubber gloves, rubber apron ... etc

  • Hydrometer, Tool set.

Work to be Carried out

  1. Perform the monthly maintenance procedure from step 2 to step 9

    2. In addition perform the following steps:

  2. Measure and record the specific gravity of the electrolyte in each cell (The standard for above Varta cell type is 1,19 with a tolerance of + - 0,02).

  3. Check and record the temperature of the electrolyte in each cell.

  4. Disconnect AC supply to battery charger.

  5. Read and record overall battery voltage immediately after AC power is switched off and then after fifteen (15) minutes for the duration of one hour. Continue recording the voltage every hour for eight (8) hours while observing at the same time that the voltage does not fall below 215 volts.

  6. If voltage should fall below 215 volts, terminate test, place battery bank on float (high) charge for eight (8) hours.

  7. When step 5 is completed, place selector switch to trickle (normal) charge and return battery bank to regular service.

  8. Compare the readings you have taken to the readings recorded in previous years.
