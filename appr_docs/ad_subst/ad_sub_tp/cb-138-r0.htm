Introduction  

This document lists minor maintenance procedure P1, which must be performed after five years as indicated by Y5 in the above title block.

The first draft initiated & tested at 22 Jan 2004 by eng Abdel Rahman Hassan Abdel Rahman Seiody at Cairo North 220kv substation, accepted by eng Ahmed Shelaby Faisal General Director, and approved by eng Samir Saleh; EHV Network Head Sector Cairo zone EETCo.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category, 

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear your safety helmet, safety shoes .... etc

    •

  • Switch off the circuit breaker.

  • Ensure that the operating mechanism has been secured against unintentional movement

  • Ensure that the control voltage supply lines have been disconnected and correctly earthed.

  • If the operating mechanism has been removed from the circuit breaker, do not perform any manipulations on the operating mechanism if the spring assembly is completely or only partially charged.

Tools and Equipment

  • Safety helmet & safety shoes

  • Portable air blower, torque wrenches with extension spec.

  • Torque screw driver with extension spec.

  • Filter's Hammer, Cotter pin driver 

  • Benzing assembly tool.

  • SF6 gas detector.

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Carry out the annual procedures stated in the MPIS document no. CB-110-r0

  2. Thoroughly inspect the part and, if necessary, replace it, it is not necessary to replace the part if it shows no wear or only slight wear on the endangered areas or the silver coating is not damaged.

Operating Mechanism

Disassembling         Figure 7/3

Important Preparations

Maintenance works must not be performed unless:

  1. The operating mechanism has been secured against unintentional movement.

  2. The control voltage supply lines have been disconnected and correctly earthed.

  3. If the operating mechanism has been removed from the circuit breaker, do not perform any manipulations on the operating mechanism if the spring assembly is completely or only partially charged.

Relieving the Spring Assembly       Figure 7/3 a, b

  1. By turning the selector knob 13205 (Figure 3/1 a), relieve the spring assembly such that the last switching operation to be carried out is an opening one.

  2. Initial position: operating mechanism in "OPEN" position.

  3. Using the selector knob 13205 switch the breaker to "CLOSE" position.

  4. Fully charge the spring assembly manually and then switch again to "OPEN" and "CLOSE" by means of the selector knob. The springs are then positioned as shown in Figure 7/3 a.

  5. Now place a block of wood measuring 20x50x150 mm on the spring plate 13302 and then switch to "OPEN" for the last time. The spring plate 13302 must rest on the block of wood. The spring assembly is then not fully relieved but is blocked (Figure 7/3 b)

  6. Initial position: operating mechanism in "CLOSED" position

  7. Using the selector knob 13205 switch to  "OPEN" and "CLOSE". The springs are then positioned as shown in Figure 7/3 a. Proceed as above described.

Disassembling the Operating Mechanism       Figure 7/4 b

  1. Detach pull rod 10731 from lever 13120 by with drawing the spiral pin 10739 from bolt 10735. Remove the bolt and replace the spiral pin.

  2. Remove the operating mechanism by detaching the screw connections 10007/10008/10009. Replace lock washer 10007.

  3. Lift off the operating mechanism.

Reassembling the Operating Mechanism       Figure 7/4 b

  1.  Reassembly in reverse order.

 

Checks       Figure 7/4 a, b, c

After completion of  the necessary preparations, checks are carried out according to the above procedures as follows:

Screw Connections

  1. Check screw connections for tightness (mind MD in illustrations). When tightening loose connections replace washers and tension elements.

    1. Cheese-head screw 13069 (Figure 7/4b) for securing the cam disc 13060 to actuating shaft 13600.

    2. Hexagon screw 13059 (Figure 7/4a) or securing the cover disc 13051 and bearing cover 13605 to casing of operating mechanism 13010.

    3. Cheese-head screw 10009 Figure 7/4b for securing the casing of operating mechanism 13010 to casing 10700 of the extinction unit.

    4. Locknut 13706 (Figure 7/4c) for securing the lock 13700 to the casing of the operating mechanism 13010.

Cable Connections

  1. Check cable connections for contact.

  2. Tighten all loose connections.

Lubrication     Figure 7/2

In the lubrication chart (Figure 7/2 a, b ,c) the lubricating points are  marked and the lubricants to be used are identified by symbols.

Follow the relevant treatment and storage instructions for lubricant specifications.

Maintenance Works      Figure 7/5

In the event of a complete overhaul of the operating mechanism the manufacturer recommends replacing the relevant circuit breaker with a reserve breaker.

Replacing the Wear parts

Replacing the Clutch        Figure 7/5 a, b

  1. Disconnect the power leads to the rewind motor 13401 from the limit switches 13431/13432.

  2. Disconnect screw connections 13407 / 13408 / 13409, replace spring rings 13407, and washers 13408.

  3. Pull remind motor 13401 with pinned freewheel clutch 13410 from eccentric shaft 13470.

  4. Remove and replace spiral brace 13419, then pull clutch coupling 13411 off transmission shaft and replace.

  5. Fit freewheel clutch 13410 on to transmission shaft and pin.

  6. Fit rewind motor 13401 on to eccentric shaft 13470

  7. Secure rewind motor with screw connection 13407/13408/13409.

  8. Check that the freewheel clutch 13410 function correctly.

Replacing the Eccentric Shaft        Figure 7/5 a, b

  1. Disassemble remind motor as above described.

  2. Disconnect screw connections 13487/13489 and remove, and replace spring washers 13487.

  3. Pull bearing bracket 13481 off eccentric shaft 13470.

  4. Detach the roller chain 13464 from sprocket wheel lift 13475.

  5. Lift out eccentric shaft 13470 and replace.

  6. Replace the roller chain 13464 onto sprocket wheel lift 13475 and ensure that the parts are fitted in the correct position. Figure 7/5c.

  7. Maneuvers the eccentric shaft into the correct bearing position in the housing.

  8. Fit bearing bracket 13481 onto eccentric shaft and secure with screw connection 13487/13489.

  9. Tighten screw connection only slightly, as the bearing bracket must be able to move.

  10. Push eccentric shaft in the direction of the arrow.  Figure 7/5c

  11. Adjust the necessary play by shifting the bearing bracket & then tighten.

Replacement of Ball Sockets          Figure 7/5 d, f, h, i

  1. Open locking device of ball socket 13286.

  2. Dismantle circlip 13284, remove plain washer 13287, replace both washers.

  3. Pull ball socket off spherical gudgeon (Figure 3/1a) respect. 13941 (Figure 7/5i) and remove rod 13280 respect. 13281

  4. Measure length L1 respect L2 of rod 13281 respect 13280.

  5. Disassemble ball socket and replace.

  6. Readjust to previously measured lengths L2 respect. L1  by turning adjusting nuts 13285 (Figure 7/5h), Lock ball socket 13286 and adjusting nuts 13285 with hexagon nuts 13288/13286.

  7. Refit rod 13281 respect.13280.

  8. Re-close locking device of ball socket 13286.

  9. Fit pin washer and refit circlip.

Note:  It is important to ensure that the dimensions L1 respect. L2 are adhered to exactly, otherwise the values of dimensions S12, S13 and play S3 set on the operating mechanism can not reproduced.

Replacing the Driver and Tripping Rod        Figure 7/5 i

  1. Open locking device of ball socket 13286 on spherical gudgeon 13943.

  2. Pull ball socket off spherical gudgeon.

  3. Detach spherical gudgeon 139.47/13948/13949   2*).

  4. Detach screw connections 13407/13408/13409,
    2*) Replace spring washers 13947 and plan washer 13948.

  5. Replace driver 13940 and tripping rod 13950.

  6. Secure driver 13940 with screw connection 13947/13948/13949  2*).

  7. Refit spherical gudgeon 13941 and spring ring 13943  2*).

  8. Insert tripping rod and check for free free movement.

  9. Check play S1 according to Figure 7/5 e and if necessary, readjust by turning the guide bolt 13961, then lock with counter nuts.

Replacing the Cylindrical Traction Springs         Figure 7/5 g

  1. Detach both cylindrical traction springs 13725 from carrier 13701 of lock 13700 and replace.

  2. Fit traction springs first to both 13729 and then hook into carrier. For mounting position refer to Figure 7/5 g

Replacing the Roller Chains           Figure 7/6 b

  1. Relieve spring assembly as already described in Relieving the Spring Assembly

  2. Detach end link with spring catch "B" of roller chain 13301 from chain cam plate 13610.

  3. Lift chain holder 13303 out of spring plate 13302.

  4. Detach end link with spring catch "B" from chain holder. Replace roller chain 13301.

  5. Reinstall  in reverse order.

Replacing the Lever                      Figure 7/6 c

  1. Relieve spring assembly as already described in Relieving the Spring Assembly

  2. Drive out spiral brace 13129 and replace.

  3. Withdraw bolt 13125 from housing cpl.13010 .This releases lever cpl. 13120 and washers 13128. replace lever cpl., bolt and washers.

  4. Reinstall in reverse order.

  5. Adjust cam roller 13123 to position of cam disc 13060 with washers 13128.

Replacing the Reserve Parts         Figure 7/6 b

  1. Replace the cam bevel gear, the chain cam disc and the bevel gear. (Figure 7/6 a, b), follow the treatment and storage instructions as mentioned at the manual.

  2. Relieve spring assembly as already described in Relieving the Spring Assembly

  3. Dismantle the shaft of the operating system as the following steps:

    1. Open locking devices of ball socket 13286 of the tripping rod 13950 on spherical gudgeon 13941. Pull ball socket off spherical gudgeon.

    2. Open spring catch "B" on roller chain 13301 on the side of the chain cam plate 13610.

    3. Unscrew screw connection 13057/13059. Replace spring ring 13057.

    4. Shift actuating shaft 13600 some mm (a fraction of an inch) to the right and lift the shaft out of the casing of the operating mechanism 13010.

  4. Unscrewing screw connection 13067/13068/13069 provides access to cam disc 13060 and cam bevel gear cpl.13630.

  5. Redial grooved ball bearing 13603 and bearing cover 13605 are disassembled by detaching circlip 13606. Replace circlip 13606.

Note:  Take care  when reassembling to ensure that the marks on the actuating shaft 13601 on the one hand and on the cam bevel gear 13602 and cam disc 13660 on the other hand are aligned with each other. Only in this way can the correct function of the operating mechanism be assured.

  1. Reassemble in reverse order.

  2. Lubricate according to Figure 7/2a, b, c.

 

 

Replacing the Cam Disc               Figure 7/4 a, b

  1. Relieve spring assembly as already described in Relieving the Spring Assembly

  2. Unscrew screw connection 13067/13068/13069. Replace spring ring 13067.

  3. Pull of cam disc 13060 and replace.

  4. Reassemble in reverse order.

  5. Check play; adjust with spacer (strip disc) 13065 to 0.3 mm (=0.012 in )( fig 7/6c).

Note:  Ensure when reassembling that the marks on  the marks on the actuating shaft 13600 and cam disc 13060 are in alignment with each other.

   6.    Lubricate according to Figure 7/2c.

Replacing the Pull Rod            Figure 7/4 b

  1. Drive out spiral brace 10739 at either end. Replace washers 10738.

  2. Remove bolts10735 at either end.

  3. Remove and replace pull rod 10731.

  4. Reassemble in reverse order.

Replacing the Locking Device      Figure 7/4 c

  1. Disassemble shaft of the operating mechanism as already described in Replacing the Reserve Parts 

  2. Unscrew locknut 13706. Replace this and spring washers 13707.

  3. Disassemble and replace locking device 13700 cpl.

  4. Replug buffer 13705 into new locking device.

  5. Reassemble in reverse order.

Replacing the Magnet Coil         Figure 7/7 a, b

  1. Disconnect plug contacts "P" of electrical connections.

  2. Unscrew screw connections 14016/14017/14019.

  3. Pull off and replace coil 14011/14012.

  4. Reassemble screw connection.

  5. Check dimension "s"; values in table of legend to Figure 7/7a, b, c. System de-energized, coil and armature separated to maximum spacing.

  6. Plug in electrical connections again.

Replacement of the Auxiliary Switch       Figure 7/7a, c

  1. Disconnect plug contacts "P" of electrical connections.

  2. Unscrew screw connections 13506/13508/13509 and disassemble guard plate 13522.

  3. Remove bolt 13533.

  4. Withdraw lever 13512.

  5. Disassemble auxiliary switch 13500 and replace. (for mounting position see Figure 7/7c)

  6. Remount auxiliary switch; install lever, bolt, and guard plate.

  7. Plug in electrical connections again.

Testing of Circuit Breaker

  1. Measure the timing test (open - close) cycle

  2. Measure the contact resistance.
