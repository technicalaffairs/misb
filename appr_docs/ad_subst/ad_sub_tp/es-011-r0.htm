Introduction

This document outlines the inspection checklist I2, to be performed every five years Y5 as indicated in the above title block. The first draft initiated by eng Samer Ayad at Sabtia 220/66/11kv substation
The earthing switch has the following specifications:
   - Rated current                                                            1600 A
   - Rated short - time current                                           40 KA
   - Rated power - frequency with stand voltage             460 KV
   - Rated lightning impulse with stand voltage               1050 KV
   - Rated peak with stand current                                  100 KA    

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area,

  • Earthing switch compartment must be isolated and grounded.

  • Wear appropriate work safety equipment, helmet shoes .............. etc.

Tools and Equipment

  • Multi-meter - buzzer, gas detector, stopwatch, screw drivers, wrenches 13,15,17 mm, dc clamp ampere

Description

 

Figs. 3a and 3b show the motor drive with its cover 35 removed.

The drive with terminal section is dust tight and water proof, the model for plug connection is dust tight. The heating resistors 67.prevent condensation. They must always be in circuit.
The terminal section of units likely  to be exposed to temperatures below-25oC (down to -50oC) is provided with two heating resistors ,which must be switched on during the cold season but may be switched off when it gets warmer.
The gearing is pre lubricated. The gearing compartment is connected with the control and terminal compartment by vents and must never be filled with grease.

Laying the pipes and cables                Laying the SF6 pipes

  1. The gas-filled compartments are connected with each other with the control unit and with the gas-monitoring panel as shown in the special pipe diagrams and layout plans furnished for each installation. Use the pipe fittings etc. supplied with the switchgear as separate items.

  2. Some of the pipe sections are pre-assembled at the factory but long pipe connections have to be made at the site

  3. Care should be taken to avoid formation of oxides (sealing) caused by heat during brazing. For this purpose pass a protective gas through the pipe while brazing.

  4. Don't use any flux Use a solder containing phosphorus (e.g.L-Ag 15 P DIN 8513 and ISO 3677)

The following gases can be used:
            Nitrogen
            Forming gas (nitrogen - hydrogen)
            Argon and all inert gasses
            Mixed gas (inert gas - nitrogen)
            The pipe joints are sealed with U sit rings.

When making the pipe joints. take good care to see that all parts that are to come into contact with SF6 are kept meticulously clean.

Laying the control and signaling cables

The control and signaling cables should be laid and connected up the accordance with the special diagrams supplied for the switchgear. The cable ducts. clip fittings and clips etc. are supplied as separate items.

Charging the filters

The filters must not be exposed to a humid atmosphere. Their filling must therefore not be in contact with the air for more than 15 minutes.
The filter material is supplied in small bags of 175g and in large bags of 260g in sealed tins.

When the tins are properly sealed. a vacuum forms which is recognizable by a clearly audible hissing sound as the tins are opened. If a tin is no longer properly sealed. the vacuum collapses and the filter material will absorb moisture. Filter material from improperly sealed tins should therefore not be employed because the filter capacity will no longer be adequate for the maintenance interval specified.

When installation has been completed and immediately prior to evacuating the breaker and filling in the SF6. position the filter bags at the points indicated in the basic circuit diagram and the master drawing. These points are also marked by adhesive labels ... filter

Proceed as follows:

Unscrew the covers concerned. place the page in the filter and immediately refit the cover (fig. 3/24 and fig. 3/25)

The quantity of filter material stated in the drawings normally suffices for an operating period of 10 years. If the voltage transformer is directly mounted on a busbar chamber (measuring bay. ct. fig. 2/8), a SF6 filter coupling is to be used instead of the coupling shown in fig. 3/26. This additionally contains a filter to prevent contamination of the relatively large busbar chamber in case of a fault in the voltage transformer. The connection is to be made in the same way as described above.

Filling in SF6 and checking the settings of the contact-making manometers

The SF6 used for insulating and arc-quenching purposes can be purposes in from a service unit which is fully equipped for this purpose.
If no service unit is available the sf6 can be taken from gas cylinders. (SF6 filling device W 294 : see operating instructions for the circuit-breaker) A vacuum pump must then be made available so that the gas-filled sections can first be evacuated.

Contact-making manometers have adjustable pointers fitted with electrical contacts. When the pressure drops to the preset value, the contacts close and initiate a signal

Important

The SF6 gas should not be blown into covers with filter (e.g. fig. 3/25) as this is likely to damage the filter bag.

The voltage transformer is supplied with a pressurized gas filling and should be installed in this state.

Sequence of operations for fitting the SF6 gas coupling 65.7 (figure 3/26) :

  1. Fit the coupling assembly to the particular compartment (e.g. cable termination compartment in fig. 3/27 a) and tighten union nut 65.7.3

  2. Evacuate the switchgear compartments and fill with SF6 gas up to approximately 0.5 bar

  3. Fix the pipe to the voltage transformer. Tightening nut 65.10 causes two valves to open and to connect the cable termination compartment with the voltage transformer compartment .

  4. Continue to fill the switchgear with SF6 gas up to the normal operating pressure.

To Remove the coupling proceed in the reverse order.

 

Important !

The connection between the union nut 65.7.5 and the coupling spring part 65.8.6 should not be loosened (danger of leakage)

Installation

  1. The drive is shipped together with the isolator/earthing switch, ready for 

  2. The drive must not be stored outdoors for more than three months without the anti-condensation heater being switched on.

  3. If this period has been exceeded. the drive must be inspected before being installed. The supplier cannot be held responsible for any damage caused by corrosion.

  4. The drive, which is specially equipped for use with the switch, must be secured in its final mounting position so that the openings of the venting stubs and compression glands are facing downwards.
    Drill fixing holes into the supporting plate or other support and firmly bolt on the drive using the fixing bolts (complete with flat washers and spring washers) supplied

  5. Tighten the bolts uniformly in diagonal sequence.

  6. Enter the control and auxiliary cables in the motor drive with terminal section through the compression glands and connect them to the terminal blocks in accordance with the table supplied. when the connections have been made check to see that the sealing faces of the adapter section and the gasket 3.1 of cowl 3. are clean.

  7. Replace the cowl and tighten the screws uniformly. (Apply a small amount of talcum to gasket 3.1 to prevent an adhesive effect) If the motor drive is equipped for plug connection, fit the plug with the control and auxiliary cables

  8. The installation of the cowl : Set up the cowl in the correct position (Fig. 3a,3b) on the stay-bolts which are guiding the cowl. Before press the gasket on the housing, guide the centering-pins in the belonging center bore. Now screw on the grip nut symmetrical.

  9. Do not cant the cowl when it setting up, it is possible to damage the housing respectively the gasket is out of duty.

  10. When work on the motor drive has been completed and immediately prior to putting it into operation remove the adhesive tape from the six vents and the plastic plugs from the venting stubs.

  11. Before carring out any other work on the drive, i.e. work where the risk of foreign matter entering cannot be ruled out, seal the particular apertures with adhesive tape.

  12. Remember to remove the adhesive tape, so that the gear compartment is adequately vented.

Operation

  1. The drive can also be actuated by hand in case the supply should fail. Use a hand-crank with 13 mm width across flats and a (slipper) clutch (Fig.5), or a hand drill (Fig.6). These tools are only supplied to special order.

  2. To actuate the drive by hand, remove the two wing screws 43. (Fig.4a) and withdraw cover 45. Following this. place the hand-crank or the hand drill on hexagon shank 39. Operate the drive by turning the hand-crank or hand drill, paying due attention to the symbols "O" for off and "l" for On on the drive cowl.

  3. Slipping of the (slipper) clutch indicates either that friction in the mechanism is excessive or that the particular position has been reached.

Maintenance, Spare parts

The drive requires no maintenance. The gearing is pre-lubricated at the factory.

It is advisable to lift off cowl 35. (Fig. 3) after six years to check the control section

Furthermore, the seal 35.5 and the sealing ring 45.5 and 59.1 (Fig. 4) should be checked and replaced if necessary.
