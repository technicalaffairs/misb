Introduction

This document lists the equipment inspection, which must be performed on an annually bases as indicated by Y1 in the title block for Step down Power Transformer: 

Class: OA/FARating: 5600/7000 KVA

Voltage : 11500 V: 3300 V-Y

Vector Group : DynI
BIL HV : 110 KV

Safety Precautions

  • Transformer isolation and proper grounding is required

  • A work permit must be issued.

  • Wear your safety helmet.

  • There is a danger of falling into the oil containments pit when working near large power transformers.

  • There is danger of falling off the top of the transformer when cleaning insulator, wear safety belt.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete tool set.

  • Digital DC voltmeter.

  • Megger device.

  • Blower or vacuum cleaner.

  • PVC Ladder - Brushes.

 

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Determine that the oil level in the transformer tank and all liquid filled
    compartments, such as junction boxes or switches, is satisfactory. Test
    the dielectric strength of the liquid. Oil from the tank bottom that tests
    24KV or less should be filtered. Check often based upon need.

  2. Clean all bushings if dirty, and inspect the porcelain for cracks.

  3. Check the pressure relief device, if furnished.

  4. Check temperature gauge, liquid level gauges, pressure gauges, and other
    indicators. Record results.

  5. Check temperature gauge drag pointer to see if there is evidence of excessive
    loading at some time in the past.

  6. Carryout the Megger check or power factor check of insulation for comparison with
    previous observations.

  7. Clean fan blades and check fan operation by turning control switch to "Manual".

  8. Check paint on thank and accessories and repaint when required.

  9. Check Tan Delta ( Capacitance and Dissipation Factor ).

  10. Check of Tap Changer.

  11. Excitation current Test.

  12. Make certain that no tools or other objects have been left in or on ,the
    transformer.
