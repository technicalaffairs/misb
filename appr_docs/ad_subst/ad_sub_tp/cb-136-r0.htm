Introduction

This document outlines the major maintenance procedure P2, which must be performed every ten years as indicated by Y10 in the title block.

The first draft initiated by eng. Anter Aly Bakr, accepted by general director: eng. Ibrahem M. Sayed El Ahl at Awlad Hmam 220kv substation, and approved by eng. Abdel Rahem Abdel Rahem Abdel Rahem; EHV Network Head Sector, Delta zone.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued,

  • The qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category, 

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area,

    •
  • Wear your safety helmet, shoes .... etc

  • Circuit breaker isolation and earthing is required

  • Change remet local control switch to local position

  • Separote rebilling volve from volt a geand close shut off valve (371 / 372) fig 3

  • Vent the air from compressed air receiver via drain screw (314) or valve (319) fig 3

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Check SF6 pressure on manometer.    (8 bar at 20 oC, rated pressure=7bar))

  2. Check compressed air manometer with control instrument.

  3. Check air pressure on manometer.      (20 bar)

  4. Check SF6 manometer with control instrument.

  5. Check setting and function of manometer contacts.    
    SF6 contact manometer, temperature-compensated    F1 density controllere
    K1:  SF6-warning, 1st stage              pe    =    7.0 bar    (p1)
     K2:  SF6-operation, blocking 1         pe    =    6.5 bar    (p2)
    K3:  SF6-operation, blocking 2         pe    =    6.5 bar    (p2)

  6. Check SF6 moisture content (desired level <20oC).

  7. Check SF6 air content (desired level <2%).

  8. Check SF6 acid content (desired level <5-10 ppmg ).

  9. Check SF6 tightness at drive shafts, (sniffing drill-hole)

  10. Check for air and gas leaks.  (audible noise or frequent refilling)

  11. Before starting any work on the pneumatic system:
    - Close shutoff valves 371, 372 from the supply line and secure against unintentional opening, by removing the handle.
    - Switch off mini-circuit-breakers of the auxiliary voltage
    - Vent receiver 309 be means of valve 313. The flexible tube 317 on the monitoring block 374 may be released only after the receiver has been vented.

    12.

  12. Drain compressed air receiver.

  13. Check high pressure flexible tubes if necessary replace.

    14.

  14. Check adjustment and function of compressed air pressure switch 376F4 Fig. 2, Adjustment work on the pressure switches must be carried out only by AEG-TFK staff 

  15. Check tightness of screw and pipe coupling.
     External diameter of pipe            Permissible moment
            10 mm                                        7 Nm
            12 mm                                      10 Nm
             15 mm                                      14 Nm
             18 mm                                      17 Nm

  16. Check refilling valve for function and tightness. Refilling valve 373 (Y0) fig. 2

  17. Check auxiliary switch S1 for function and burning of contacts.    Figure  4

  18. Check screw coupling of electrical connections if necessary re-tighten.

  19. Check SF6 pressure in the pole column and check the pressure of air in the compressed air receiver before switching.

  20. Check fixed main contact and if necessary replace.       1)    2)

  21. Check movable main contact and necessary replace..   1)    2)

  22. Check insulating nozzle and if necessary replace.          1)    2)

  23. Replace molecular sieve filter.                                      1)

  24. Carry out test switching operations, (before switching check SF6 pressure in the pole columns, and the pressure of air in the compressed air receiver of the breaker):
    - 10 C-O-switching at one minute intervals.
    - Including 2 O-    switching with the manual emergency valve.

  25. Check times of switching operations. Opening time 40 ms +10%, break time 60 ms +10%

  26. Checking the pressure drop when switching as the following steps:
    -     Produce operating pressure.   10 bar
    -     Separate refilling valve from voltage or close shutoff valves (371/372).  Figure 2
    - Operate the circuit breaker electrically.
    -     Read off values on manometer (375) five minutes later.    Figure 2
       At 20 OC the difference is about 0.6 bar for opening, and about 0.3 bar for closing the breaker.
    - Markedly increased pressure drops point to a fault in the pneumatic system.

  27. Check the mechanical On, OFF position indicators.

Remarks

1) Requiring opening of gas compartment

2) Random checks on circuit breaker with max operating duty, further checks only on negative result.
