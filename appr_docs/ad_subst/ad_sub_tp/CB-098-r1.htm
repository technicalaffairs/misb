Introduction

This procedure outlines the major maintenance procedure P2, which must be performed every ten years Y10 as indicated in the above titled block. The first draft initiated by Demo substation, Eng. Abd Eltawab Ramadan. The first review at Nov 2007.

Safety Precautions

  • Work permit must be issued.

  • Safety fence with cautions marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • CB must be isolated and proper earthingat on the two sides of CB is required.

  • Interrupt remote control and motor circuits (this achieved by receiving the multi-pin plug at the left hand side at the operating mechanism)

  • Switch breaker ON and OFF manually in order to discharge the closing and trip springs. 

Tools and Equipments

  • Safety equipment "belt, shoes,...,etc".

Work to be carried out 

 

The following inspections are to be carried-out in addition to the visual inspections (annual inspection) and inspection of state of CB (every 5 years).CB-097-r0

Note : Measurements were under taken on each pole; the dimension Z imprinted on the cover flange is the over-shoot of the stroke in the off position.

  1. Check -up on alignment of the operating rod, the breaker has been de-energized and the closing spring is fully discharged. remove the front cover of the operating mechanism; operating rod cover (6-2) and the lever cover (6-3).

  2. Attach measuring gauge.

  3. Measure and record in "open" position of the circuit breaker the length "A"

  4. The circuit breaker should be switched "ON" to closed position. After that measure and record length "E" the measured dimensions in the different position to the position of the CB are :
                              AU:  "off" - stop
                              Z   :   over travel in "off"
                              A  :   position "off"
                              H  :   nominal travel
                              E  :   position "on"

  5. Check the length "L" between the pins of the spring shack which is stamped on the spring shackle 
    (CB in "closed" position)

  6. After that CB should be switched off.

  7. Check subsequently if CB has reached position "off" and if closing spring is discharged.

  8. Disconnect main drive rod (6.1) from lever (2.5)

  9. Move the switching element of the CB with lever (2.5) to the "off" -stop after that measure and 
    recorded the length "AU"













      

"0": play 0 at "OFF" position of the circuit breaker
5.5: Dash-pot rod link
5.6: Coupling Stud







 

 

  1. The adjustment of the mechanical linkage is correct if:
                    * The over travel in position "off" (AU-A> 6.5 mm) or stamped Z-measure
                    * Travel H=A-E=170.0±1.0 mm

  2. If one of the measure is not correct adjustment must be made at the drive rod.

  3. No play O must now exist between the dash-pot rod link (5.5) and the coupling stud (5.6) if the play is not "O" adjustments at the dash-pot rod link must be made.

  4. If all the measurements are correct reconnect the main drive rod (6-1) at the lever (2-5)

  5. Torque check:- control the torque at all lock nuts with a torque wrench.
                  * Main operating rod (6-1) 170 Nm
                  * Dash-pot rod link (5-5) 100 Nm

  6. Check if all split pins have been installed and spread a part

  7. Replace all protective covers; reconnect the control cable to the operating mechanism

  8. The CB is now ready for operation

  9. Repeat the above steps for the tow remaining poles.

  10. Measure the operating time of CB and record.

The BHG 114 is a single pressure puffer type SF6 gas circuit breaker with one break per phase. It is designed for incorporation in the B212 metal clad switchgear concent and meets the requirements of appropriate standard.

Each of the three horizontal lying circuit breaker poles constitutes together with the motor wound spring operating mechanism FK 2-2 a single phase operating unit.

The three poles are fixed to the switchgear supporting structure.

The break employed for the BHG 114 is identical to that of the HGF 100 single column outdoor puffer type circuit breaker. Owing to the simple design, little requisite for maintenance and a high reliability have been achieved.
