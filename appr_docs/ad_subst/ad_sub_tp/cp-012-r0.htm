Introduction

    This document outlines the inspection activities I1, to be performed on a weekly basis by substation maintenance staff.

    These activities are in addition to the daily inspection, which would also be performed at this time. The compressor must be taken out of service to perform this inspection.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, qualified maintenance staff carry the suitable safety ID category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear standard personal safety and healthy equipment as helmet, safety shoes, rubber gloves, ... etc.

  • Ensure compressor is properly isolated.

  • Do not wear loose clothing near the compressor while it is running.

Environmental Recommendation

  • Before using a bower to clean the air filter, wear your breathing mask to protect lung tissues from the dust particulate.

  • Using solvents such as gasoline and others for cleaning the oil filter is dangerous, it will expose lungs to very hazardous and carcinogenic chemicals. You have to wear your breathing mask.

  • Don't bum the cloth used in cleaning, collect it and send it to the dump.

Tools and Equipment
  • Cleaning material, rags, air blower, solvent.

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Perform a general inspection of the compressor room, check that the room is clean and well lit, clean room if necessary and replace defective lighting units. Ensure the room is well ventilated and the room temperature must be less than 40° C.

  2. Operate the compressor control switch to check that supply voltage is present, this also ensures the switch is in good mechanical operating condition.

  3. Check the compressor crank case oil level through the level indicator, ensure the level is between the minimum and maximum indication lines.

  4. Drain the condensate water from the main air recevior tank.

  5. Operate the compressor, Check and Record air pressure values for all stages:

    6.    - First stage 2 atm.
       - Second stage 11 atm.
       - Third stage 40 atm.
       - Fourth stage 160 atm (if exist    ).

  6. Check oil pressure 2 – 4 atm.

  7. Check oil temperature and if it excess 80 0 C switch off the unit.

  8. With the compressor running check that all pressure relief valves are properly seated by listening for air leaking at the outlets of the valves.

  9. With the compressor isolated visually, check coupling lubrication, there should be a noticeable amount of grease between the shaft and coupling housing.

  10. All air filter must be cleaned by blowing high pressure air through them.

  11. Clean the oil filter using solvent and blowing them dry with compressed air.

  12. With the compressor running, check for any air & oil leaks, and fix any leaks.

  13. Check the operation of both electrical and mechanical pressure reducing valves.

  14. Ensure that the condensed valves operate properly after any stop for the compressor.

  15. Record the operation hours number.

  16. Put the unit in service.
