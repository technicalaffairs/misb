Introduction

This procedure details the items, which are checked every 5 years Y5. They include testing and major maintenance P2. In addition to the procedures listed, it is expected the checks, which are done more frequently, would also be completed at this time.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area.

  • Wear standard personal safety and healthy equipment as helmet, safety shoes, rubber gloves, ... etc.

  • Before doing maintenance, inspection, or service work on the unit be sure that the starting switch is in OFF position or the wiring disconnected from the line to prevent the unit from accidentally starting.

Environmental Recommendation

  • Do not throw the sump oil to the sewage because this oil will prevent the sewage treatment process. Collect it in tight containers and return it back to the vendor to recycle it. Always remember that "This oil is load for your wastes but it's valuable material for them. "Avoid oil spillage to the ground because this oil spillage has very bad impact on the environment.

  • Using solvents such as gasoline and others for cleaning the 1st, 2nd, 3 rd and 4th stage of Air Head and Valve, Cylinder and Piston is dangerous, it will expose lungs to very hazardous and carcinogenic chemicals. You have to wear your breathing mask.

  • Don't burn the cloth used in cleaning, collect it and send it to the dump.

  • Fix any oil leaks to prevent pollution and save money of wasted oil. Oil leaks to the ground have very bad impact on the environment, mainly on soil and ground water and eventually on the ecosystem. "It takes only 1 liter of Hydrocarbon spilled in an aquifer to render 1,000,000 liters of potable water useless for human consumption.

Tools and Equipment

  • Tool set, Cloth,

  • Sodium Hydroxide Solution, Benzene,

  • New air filter cartridge,

  • Grinding Past, gasket set,

  • O-ring set, Oil seal set.

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Open the oil drain plug to drain the oil out of the sump (figure 2).

  2. Disassemble inlet air filter figure 1.

  3. Disassemble inlet manifold figures 1, 2 one bolt.

  4. Disassemble 1 st stage discharge manifold figure 2.

  5. Disassemble 2-nd stage inlet manifold.

  6. Disassemble 2-nd stage discharge manifold.

  7. Disassemble 3-rd stage discharge manifold.

First Stage Air head and Valve (Figure 3)

  1. Unscrew the six cap screws no. 4 & 5 of the first stage air head. Use spanner 19

  2. Pullout the air head and the entire valve.

  3. Unscrew the two nuts no. 6 of studs no.14 to disassemble the valve from the air head.

  4. Disassemble the entire valve by unscrewing the two machine screws no.11.

  5. Clean the parts of the valve from part no. 7 to part no.14 in figure 3 using benzene and dry it.

  6. Check all parts of the valve and change any part if needed.

  7. Change the two gaskets no. 2 & 3.

  8. Reassemble the entire valve to the airhead.

  9. Repeat all the previous steps from item1 to item 8 to make maintenance to the other unit of the first stage airhead and valve.

First Stage Cylinder and Piston (Figure 4)

  1. Unscrew the four cap screws no. 2 and the washer no.3. Use spanner 19

  2. Pull the cylinder no. 1 out.

  3. Disconnect the two lock rings of piston pin, no. 8 and withdrew the piston pin no.

  4. Pull out the piston no. 7 and clean all parts (piston pin, piston …) with benzene then change the gasket of cylinder flange no. 4, the two piston compression rings no.5 piston ring, oil wiper no. 6 And piston pin no. 9 if needed

  5. Repeat all the steps from 1 to 4 to make maintenance to the other unit of the first stage cylinder and piston.

Second stage Air head and Valve (Figure 5)

  • Repeat all the steps from step 1 to 8 to make maintenance to the second stage air head and valve (the second stage as the same of the first stage) except that there is a metal ring with calibrated thickness between the valve and cylinder incase of cancelling the forth stage clean it and reassemble it again.

Second Stage Cylinder and Piston (Figure 6)

  • Repeat all the steps from item1 to item 4 to make maintenance to the second stage cylinder and piston (second stage as the same of first stage) Use spanner 14

Third Stage Air head and Valve (Figure 7)

  1. Unscrew the six cap screws no. 3 of the airhead and pull out the airhead no.1. Use spanner 17.

  2. Pull out the entire valve.

  3. Unscrew the two screws socket head of the third stage no. (7 figure 7). Use Allen 5

  4. Check and clean all valve parts from no .4 to11 with benzene, dry and change any improper parts (if needed).

  5. Make smoothing to the valve seat no. 5, inlet valve plate no.10 and discharge valve plate no. 8 using fine grinding paste and reclean all parts with benzene and dry it.

  6. Change O ring no 2.

  7. Reassemble the entire valve of the third stage.

Third Stage Cylinder (figure 8)

  1. Unscrew the four cap screws no.3 and their washers no. 4 and remove them with their washers. Use spanner 19.

  2. Pull out the air cylinder no.1 and cylinder cross head no. 5.

Fourth Stage Air head and Valve (Figure 9)

  1. Unscrew the four cap screws no. 4.

  2. Pull out the entire valve.

  3. Check and clean all parts from part no. 2 to 13.

  4. Change the air head gaskets no. 2, O-rings no. 3 and copper washer no. 11

  5. Change any part (if needed).

  6. Reassemble the entire valve.

Forth Stage Cylinder (Figure 10)

  1. Unscrew the four cap screws no. 3 and their washer no. 4.

  2. Pull out the air cylinder no.1 and pull out cylinder cross head no. 5.

  • * In some cases there is no need for fourth stage so the fourth stage consist of a flat plate for closing the cylinder, cross head no.5, and accouter weight fixed on cross head no10 and it will be maintained as follows: -

  1. Unscrew the four screws no.3.Fig 10 modified

  2. Pull out the flat plate from the cylinder and cross head no5 fig.10 modified.

  3. Pull out the cylinder cross head no.5.

Connecting Rod (Figure 11)

  1. Withdrew the cotter pin no.7 then unscrew the four nuts no.8 and their bolts no. 9.

  2. Pullout the upper part of connecting rod master no. 6.1 and the one half bearings no.11.1 (fig. 17)

  3. The lower part of connecting rod master no 6.2 will drop down allowing to disassemble the third and forth stage pistons as follows:-

Third Stage Piston

  1. Unscrew the two cap screws no.11 fig (8) and pull up the piston no. 9.

  2. Disconnect the end plug piston pin no. 10, pull out the piston pin no. 12 and pull up the piston no. 9.

  3. Clean all parts using benzene and change the O-ring no. 2, gasket no. 6, three piston compression rings no. 7, piston ring oil wiper no. 8 and change piston pin no. 12 if needed.

Forth Stage Piston (Figure 10)

  1. Unscrew the four cap screws no. 3 and their washers no. 9.

  2. Pull out the plate no. 7 and piston complete no.13.

  3. Disconnect the two end plugs no.11, cross head No.12 cross head no.10.

  4. Clean all parts with benzene and change O -ring no. 2, gasket no. 6, piston complete 13 and cross head pin no.12 if needed.

For modified Forth Stage (Figure 10 modified)

  1. Unscrew the two cap screws no. 8 and their washers no. 9.

  2. Pull out the counter balance weight (fitted instead of plate no.7)

  3. Disconnect the two enplugs no.11, cross head No.12 cross head no.10.

  4. Clean all parts with benzene and change O -ring no. 2, gasket no. 6, piston complete 13 and cross head pin no.12 if needed.

  2. Unscrew the nut no.1 and remove it with its lockwasher no.2 (fig.17).

  3. Pull out the fan and hub assembly no.3.

  4. Unscrew the 6 cap screws no.6.

  5. Pull out the cover fan end no.4.

  6. Pull out the oil seal no.5

  7. Unscrew the 4 screws no. 17 and their washers no.18 use Allen 5.

  8. Pull out the complete oil pump from no.8 to no.16 (fig.17).

  9. Remove the retaining ring no.19 and the washerno.20.

  10. Remove the oil seal no.21and no.28.

  11. Unscrew the two Allen screws from the coupling sleeve (that connect the two parts of coupling) and disconnect the two retaining ring then move the sleeve to any side. (fig. 18).

  12. Disconnect the electrical connection of the motor and move its two cable out.

  13. Unscrew the two cap screws (no.62 fig.18) and four cap screws (58 fig.18) connecting the motor to compressor.

  14. Pull out the motor from the base.

  15. Disconnect the lock-nut no65 fig18 (use span tube55) then pull out the have part of the coupling outside.

  16. Disconnect the two crank covers no. 1 figure 15 by unscrewing the eight cap screws no. 3.

  17. Pull the crankshaft no. 24 figure 11with all parts.

  18. Clean all parts with benzene and change ball bearing no. 15, 16 in case of motor driving and ball bearing no. 17 in case of belt driving.

  19. Clean all parts of the oil pump figure 17, Change oil seal no.5, the fan gasket end cover no. 8, O-rings no.10, 15 oil seals no. 21, 28, gasket no. 30 or change the oil pump complete if needed.

  20. Reassemble the crankshaft with its cover, connecting rod master no. 6 with its bearings no. 11fig 11.

  21. Reassemble all cylinders and pistons for all stages in there previous positions.

  22. Reassemble all the entire valve to the airhead for all stages in their original position on their cylinders.

Inlet Air Filter (Figure 13)

  1. Unscrew the nut of its cover.

  2. Clean the filter element no. 3 with flow of air and benzene and dry it with flow of air or replace it if needed.

  3. Clean the oil bowl no. 4 with benzene and refill with new oil to its correct level.

  4. Reassemble the inlet air filter in its place.

Oil Filter (Figure 14)

  • Disassemble the oil filter set (figure 14) and clean its parts and change oil filter element no. 5 figure 14.

Radiators (Figure 16)

  1. Unscrew the separator for the four stages no. 1, 7, 13, 15, 18, clean with benzene, and dry with flow of air or replace if needed.

  2. Clean and inspect for damaged fins of radiator.

  3. By using close circuit with pump, clean the radiators tubes with flow of Sodium hydroxide solution, then water and dry it with flow of air.

  4. Reassemble the separator to radiators block and reassemble the radiators block to its sub base.

Reassembling the Whole Unit
  1. Reassemble all manifolds (inlet, outlet) for all stages.
  2. Refill the oil again in oil sump to a proper level between Max. and Min. marks of the lube oil dipstick and refill the oil pump and its tubes with oil. .
  3. Refill motor ball bearing with grease
  4. Run the compressor manually and listen to any abnormal sound.
  5. Run the motor manually and listen to any abnormal sound.
  6. Reassemble the motor no. (57 fig.18) to compressor with the screws no.62, 58 fig.18.
  7. Connect the coupling between the motor and the compressor 64 fig. 18.
  8. Put the manual condensed drain valve on NO LOAD position 14 fig. 18.
  9. Put the starting switch in “ON” position and operate the compressor and listening to any abnormal sound and observe the oil pressure gauge to be sure that the oil pump is operate in good condition
  10. Put the manual condensed drain valve on FULL LOAD position.
  11. Check every stage air pressure on pressure gauges (figure 9) First Stage 2 atm.

    12. Second Stage 11 atm.

    Third Stage 40 atm.

    Fourth Stage 160 atm. (If exist).

  12. Check oil pressure (2:4 atm) on oil pressure gauge.
  13. Check for any air and oil leakage
  14. Record the maintenance, which has-been done and all parts, which have been changed in maintenance cared.
  15. Before putting the unite in service you have to do all following electrical test:
  16. Switch off the main supply of 0.4 kV.
  17. Switch off the control switches in cabinet.
  18. Check oil pressure device termination.
  19. Check the terminals of cables 0.4 kV at the two sides.
  20. Check and cleaning the contacts of auxiliary relays in the control cabinets of the compressor.
  21. Check and cleaning the contacts of air pressure and oil pressure manometers.
  22. Check and cleaning the contacts of starting contactors.
  23. Check the signal relays.
  24. Check the starting time relay.
  25. Check the control circuit
  26. Check the electrical condensed valves and test them.
  27. Check the air pressure stages as follows. First stage 2 atm
    Second stage 11 atm
    Third stage 40 atm
  28. Oil pressure 2 - 4 atm and oil temperature 80 ° C.
  29. Adjust the delay time between starting and loading the compressor stages about 30 sec.
  30. Test the operating of compressors manually and automatically.
  31. Put the compressor in service.
  32. Record the measuring results on protection card.
