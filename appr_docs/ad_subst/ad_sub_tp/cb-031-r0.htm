Introduction 

This document outlines equipment inspection I2, to be carried out on an annually bases as indicated by Y1 in the title block.

In addition to the procedures listed, it is expected the checks, which are done more frequently, would also be completed at this time.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued.

  • Safety fence with caution marks around the work area is required.

  • Wear the safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet ... etc.

  • CB must be isolated and proper earthling at the two sides of the CB is required.

  • Isolated control circuit (DC voltage).

  • Use only fiberglass ladder.

Tools and Equipment
  • Torque wrench and one set of metric open end box wrenches.
  • One set of screwdrivers.
  • Air blower.
  • Fiberglass ladder.
  • Clean wipers.
  • Heavy machine oil for the operating pump.
  • Dielectric strength oil tester.
Work to be carried out
  • Perform all steps in MPIS Document No. CB-030-r0 (Daily inspection)

Breaker Closing Mechanism

  1. Check the links move freely.

  2. Manually close and trip the breaker and ensure that there is no bending then lubricate with light machine oil.

Control Relay (1 fig. 1)

  1. Withdraw the main fusses and check mechanical parts for free movement with control switch in off position.

  2. Clean arc chutes using fine emery cloth and examine contacts and retighten all screws.

Connoting Rod and Clevises (2 fig. 2)

  • Remove covers and check that all pins are secure examine springs, check that all adjusting screws and nuts are tighten, then lubricate with heavy machine oil after cleaning with benzene

Auxiliary Switches

  • Clean the contacts with emery cloth or file with a fine file if it is copper, brass, etc. but if it is silver plated do not file but clean only with dry cloth and lightly smear with vaseline and make sure that operating rods are well adjusted.

Bushings

  • Check for any oil leakage, check for cracks or other damage and clean the porcelain.

Oil in Circuit Breaker

  1. Clean and open the plug of outlet valve at the bottom of the oil tank.

  2. If the dielectric strength is less than 30kv/2.5 mm, dry and recondition until it with stands at least 40KV/2.5 mm.

  3. Record all has been done in maintenance card.
