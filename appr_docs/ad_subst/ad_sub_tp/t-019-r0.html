Introduction

This document lists the equipment Inspection checklist I2, which must be performed on an annual bases as indicated by Y1 in the title block.

Safety Precaution 

  • A work permit must be issued and qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Wear your safety helmet, shoes and safety belt when cleaning suspension insulators.
  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounded the work area is required.
  • Transformer isolation and proper earthling is required.
  • There is a danger of falling into the containment pit when working near large power transformer.
  • It is recommended to use only fiberglass (PVC) ladders when working in the switchyard.
  • Local grounded must be used.

Environmental Recommendation

  • Before starting the cleaning of all porcelain surfaces of all transformer wear your breathing mask to protect lung tissues from the dust particulate.

  • Don't burn the cloth used in cleaning. Collect it and send it to the dump.

  • Fix any oil leaks to prevent pollution and save money of wasted oil.

  • Oil leaks to the ground have very bad impact on the environment, mainly on soil and 
    ground water and eventually on the eco-system. "It takes only I liter of Hydrocarbon spilled 
    in an aquifer to render 1,000,000 liters of potable water useless for human consumption

Tools and Equipment

  • Safety helmet, safety belt, safety shoes, and tool set

  • Blower, hays voltmeter, screwdriver set, and Fiberglass PVC ladder

  • Voltage indicator, Portable earth for discharging,

  • Fine cloth for cleaning purposes, Silicagel,

  • Blower, rags, voltmeter, screwdriver set, PVC Ladder, gas oil analyzer, 

  • Oil break down device tan equipment, 

  • Megger at least 5000V and Turns Ratio test equipment. 

 

Work to be Carried out

  1. Repeat the previous monthly inspection procedures in document no.  T-018-r0

  2. Check all painted surfaces of the transformer, remove signs of rust and repair use rust prevention.

  3. Check conditions of earthling bus (system) all bolted connection for tightness.

  4. Check pressure relief on main tank and OLTC tank

  5. Open bleed valve on bukhelez relay until a small a mount of oil escapes test alarm and 
    trip circuit using push button next to vent valve, Check all electrical connections.

  6. Check cooling fan and screen for dust accumulation, blade cracks, motor bearing noise and 
    heating (motor or ball bearing). It is recommended to change motor bearing after 3000 running hours or 5 years).

  7. Check main transformer and OLTC control cabinet connections tightness, space heater operation fan control operation,
    and temp. Setting adjustments, motor operating mechanism.

  8. Use a water pressure to clean radiators and transformer tank, walls, unsealed wiring must be covered plastic to 
    avoid short circuit. It is recommended to use the transformer deluge system to perform cleaning operation, it will 
    provide a functioning test for the system at the same time.

  9. Take and record insulation resistance readings and compare to the previous test and historical data.

  10. Take and record cap. Dissipation factor, tans delta and compare to historical data.

  11. Perform ratio test {lowest & highest and neutral tap position and compare to historical data.

  12. Perform a complete tap changer OP. From highest to lowest step and back to the position it was found 
    in to clear all internal contacts of tap changer. (This step should be done every 3 Months)

  13. Inspect condition of external tap changer drive shaft, tighten all coup lings and bolted connections.

  14. Take and record final reading on tap changer operation counter.

  15. Take oil sample for laboratory analysis and gas analysis each for both main and OLTC tanks.
