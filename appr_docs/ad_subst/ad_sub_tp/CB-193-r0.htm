Safety Precautions

  • Work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff must carry the suitable ID safety category,

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks must be surrounding the work area,

  • Wear appropriate personal protective equipment as safety shoes, safety helmet, breathing mask, safety glasses

  • Circuit breaker must be fully isolated

  • Earthing switch must be closed on both sides of the breaker to ensure it is properly grounded

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete set of substation maintenance hand tools including screw driver set torque wrench

  • Emergency light, 220 Vac supply

  • SF6 gas leak detector

  • Portable pressure gauge standard

    •

Work to be Carried Out

Note : Although not always specified, all checks are performed on all three-breaker phases.

  1. Complete a visual inspection of all components of the circuit breaker, look for signs of overheating in the mechanism boxes, signs of electro-static discharge, small oil leaks, weld cracks ... etc.

  2. Check the hydraulic oil pressure, the pressure should be 330 bar at normal condition.

  3. Check the operation of the hydraulic oil pump by reducing the hydraulic oil pressure (bleed a quantity of oil back into the reservoir until the pump starts).

  4. Check and record the number of hydraulic pump operations as indicated on the operation counter. Station operators do this daily

  5. Check the operation of the heater circuit of mechanism

  6. Check high-pressure pipes to ensure they are in their proper locations and sealing properly

  7. Check the hydraulic oil reservoir tank to ensure the cap is sealing properly

  8. Check the sf6 gas pressure this value should be 6 bar at 20 oC.

  9. Using the leak detector check the entire CB switch for SF6 gas leak
