Introduction

This document lists the equipment inspection I2, which must be performed on an annually bases Y1 as indicated in the above title block

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area,

  • Transformer isolation and earthing is required.

  • Wear your safety helmet, safety shoes, safety harness.

  • Proper well fixed ladder should be used for climbing on top of the Transformer tank.

  • Wear your breathing mask before dry cleaning of bushings and earth point insulators.

  • While working at night ensure adquate lighting is provided for the entire work area.

Tools and Equipment

  • Tools: Portable earting extension ladder, screw drivers, set of common keys and blower.

  • Test equipment: Ratio meter, Megger, cap, bridge/tan delta, winding resistance.

  • Spare parts and materials: Oil sampling equipment and report forms.

Work to be carried out  

  1. Carry out all the monthly checks listed on MPIS Document no. T-007-r0.

  2. Check & record the oil and winding temperature gauges for the correct calibration and setting:

  • Stage # 1 Fans should start at 65° C winding temp. and stop at 60 °C.

  • Stage # 2 Fans should start at 80° C winding temp. and stop at 75 ° C.

  • High winding temperature alarm at 95°C. 

  • High winding temperature trip at 110°C.

  • High oil temperature alarm at 85 °C.

  • High oil temperature trip at 95 °C.
     

  1. Transformer Test Group Shall Perform the following tests:
  • Capacitance Bridge/ Tan Delta tests on Transformer & Bushings.
  • Insulation of 500, 220, and 11kv windings using a 500 volt megger.
  • Turns ratio test on all tap positions.
  • DC winding resistance tests.
  • Function test protective trip relay (Buchholz) for the tapchanger.
  • Function test protective trip & gas accumulation alarm (Buchholz) for the main transformer.

  1. Check the core ground resistor to be 20k ohms using a 500 volt megger maximum.

  2. Test the correct function of the oil level alarms 

  3. Operate pressure relief (control) switches by hand of the main tank to check trip signal.
