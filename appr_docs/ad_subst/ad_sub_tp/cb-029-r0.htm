Introduction

This procedure details the items, which are checked every 5 years Y5. They include testing and major maintenance P2. In addition to the procedures listed, it is expected the checks, which are done more frequently, would also be completed at this time.

 Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued.

  • CB must be isolated and proper earthling at the two sides of the CB is required.

  • Isolate control circuit (DC voltage).

  • Safety fence with caution marks around the work area is required.

  • Wearing safety helmet is required.

  • Use only fiberglass ladders.

  • Open the drainage valve to drain the air out of the tank and be sure that the breaker is free of pressure. The pressure can be read at the control pressure gauge placed on the middle air tank of the circuit breaker.

  • Personnel carrying out the maintenance should be trained and qualified or directly supervised by some one who is.

Tools and Equipment

  • Mobile crane, Triple sheaved, Sufficient and suitable polypropylene rope to lower arcing chambers and hollow support insulators to ground.

  • Torque wrench with its socket wrenches, One set of metric open end box wrenches, one set of Allen keys and one set of screwdrivers.

  • Fine file, fine emery paper and wipers.

  • Air blower or vacuum cleaner.

  • Hammer 1/2 kg, hammer 2 kg and rubber hammer.

  • Fiber glass ladder.

  • All special tools of the CB

  • Petroleum jelly (Vaseline).

  • Tin cans to hold bolts, nuts, studs, washers………etc

  • Complete set of gaskets.

  • Metal brush for cleaning the internal and outside surfaces of the CB air tank rusty places.

  • Bronze aluminum metallic paint for painting the outside surfaces of the CB tank.

  • Bituminous paint and suitable paint brushes for repainting the internal surface of the tank

Special Safety Precautions

  • Before working ensure the crane operator is fully aware of neighbouring apparatus and the safe limits of approach to over head lines or bus-bars.

  • The mobile crane must be grounded.

  • Do not use the mobile crane for the actual lift, but attach a rope block and use the crane hook as a gin pole.

Environmental Recommendation

  • Do not throw any remains of the paint into the sewage because it prevents the sewage treatment process.

  • Do not burn cloth and wipers used in cleaning, but collect and send it to the dump.

  • Before starting the cleaning of the air tank, wear your breathing mask to protect lung tissues from dust particulate.

  • If you are going to repaint the outside surface of the tank by using spray gun for painting process. Try to follow the following instructions to reduce wastes and protect your health:

  1. Maintain 6": 8" distance between spray gun and work piece
  2. Maintain gun speed of about 250feet/minute.
  3. Hold gun perpendicular to the surface (If gun is arched 45o , the over-spray can be as high as 65%)
  4. Avoid excessive air pressure for coating atomization (by air pressure adjustment over-spray can be reduced to 40%)
  5. Recycle over spray.

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Look at the Previous overhaul report including all tests and manufacture

  2. Remove the high voltage connections at the two sides of the arcing chamber.

  3. Disconnect the water resistance (19 Fig 4) and lower it down aside on a wooden base, taking care not to break it. Use spanner 19

  4. With the polypropylene rope blocks hang the arcing chamber with the crane hook.

  5. Release the nuts of the 8 stud bolts which connecting the lower flange of the arcing chamber with the upper flange of the support insulator. Use spanner 19

  6. Using the crane hook as a gin pole lower the arcing chamber.

  7. Take it to the workshop for checking maintenance.

  8. Unscrew the nuts at the connection spindle (55 Fig 4) and remove the relief cover (56 Fig 4). Use spanner 42

  9. Unscrew the 3 nuts on the plate that covers the deionizer and remove the plate with the spindle (55 Fig 4), and the de-ionizer (45 Fig 4). Use spanner 19

  10. Unscrew the 6 screws that fixed the nearer of the movable contact (57 Fig 4), pull the moving contact out and take care not to break the screen (49 Fig 4) Use spanner 17

  11. Inspect the moving contact (61 Fig 4):

  • If its surface is in a good condition it means that the fixed contact is probably also in good condition and there is no need to disassemble it.
  • If its surface is slightly burnt it should be cleaned by a fine file and a fine emery paper, then placed again in its previous position into the arcing chamber.
  • If more than 1/3 of the contact surface is damaged it should be changed.

Changing the Moving Contact

  1. Put the bearer of the movable contact to vertical position and by means of a special 100mm dia hook spanner unscrew the counter nut (59 Fig 4) and unscrew the screen (49 Fig 4) to remove it.

  2. Unscrew the counter nut 50 Fig 4) by means of a special 50 mm dia hook spanner, and by means of the 60 mm dia. hook spanner unscrew the movable contact (61 fig 4).

  3. Change the movable contact and screw the counter nut (50 Fig 4) and by pressing the contact head by hand, make sure that the contact moves smoothly and that the spring returns it to the initial position.

  4. Carefully screw the screen (49 fig 4) to fix it to the moving contact.

  5. Inspect the segment contact (51 Fig 4) and its springs for this inspection it is necessary to remove the cap of the bearer (60 Fig 4) by means of a special pipe wrench.

Changing the Fixed Contact

  1. Pull out the cap (47 Fig 4) and the spring (63 Fig 4).

  2. By a special pipe wrench only loosen the turbine (46 Fig 4). Do not remove it, Since its position is exactly determined.

  3. Unscrew the bearer of the fixed contact (48 Fig 4) by hand and take it out.

  4. Fit the new contact.

  5. Fit the bearer in its previous position.

  6. Before replacing the parts of the arcing chamber clean all parts and internal insulator surface of the arcing chamber.

  7. Replace the moving contact in its place into the arcing chamber.

  8. Change the cover gasket of the relief chamber (56 Fig 4).

  9. Check the oil level in the shock absorbers (20 Fig 4), which must reach the screw hole.

  10. Check the liquid level in water resistance, if it is decreases you must add distilled water and glycol in such a ratio that total resistance amounts to 200 K ohm +3%

  11. Disassemble the main valve for maintenance (fig 6)

  12. Release the four bolts, which connect it with the chamber casing.

  13. Release the four screws which connecting the valve with the bent. use spanner 24

  14. Disconnect the copper tubes connecting the valve with the electro-valve and de-compressor (144 fig 1).

  15. Release the four stud nuts of the main valve cover. Use spanner 14

  16. Check the piston (94 fig 6) and piston rings (101 fig 6), clean them with benzene and change the gaskets (99 Fig 6) and the rings if needed.

  17. Assemble the main valve once again.

  18. Examine the electrovalves and change the gaskets (fig 7) if necessary and check its function.

  19. Remove the cover of the isolator and check the horizontal shaft, also examine, clean and grease the ice breaking mechanism (Fig 5).

  20. Maintenance of the air tank and return pressure valve

  21. Remove the two covers of the inspection holes which one of them is provided with return pressure valve

  22. Clean the tank from dirtiness and possible condensate by a flow of compressed air using a vacuum cleaner and a brush with a long handle.

  23. If the internal surfaces are rusty it should be cleaned by metal brush then twice coated with bituminous coating.

  24. Change the two gaskets of the inspection holes covers.

  25. Repaint the out side surface of the air tank.

  26. Clean the return pressure valve and check its gasket and spring change if it need.

  27. After finishing the maintenance of all the circuit breaker parts re assemble it once again as in its previous position and recheck the tightening all nuts and bolts by torque wrench.

  28. After finishing the maintenance of all the CB parts reassemble it once again as in its previous position and recheck the tightening of all nuts and bolts by torque wrench.

  29. Recharge the CB with air and visually check all feeding pipes for leaks.

  30. Before putting the CB in service perform micro ohm and timing tests.

    Note:

    Closing time: 0. 25 sec,             Opening time: 0.07 sec

  31. Measure the insulation resistance of support column by megger.

  32. Record all results on maintenance card.
