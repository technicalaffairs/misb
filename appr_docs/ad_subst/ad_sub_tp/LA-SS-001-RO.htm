Introduction

This document lists the equipment inspection I1, which must be performed every two months as indicated by M2 in the title block

The first draft initiated by Mr. Helmut Canadian SISP, S/S specialist, and first approval at 08 July 1996, first review date 03 March 2002, and second review date 15 Jan. 2006. The document was updated by Ataka PS.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued,

  • The qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category, 

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Appropriate personal protective equipment must be used, i.e. safety shoes, helmet, breathing mask, safety glasses.

  • Isolation and proper earthing is required.

  • There is danger of falling off the top of the arrester when cleaning insulators or checking connections, wear your safety belt.

Tools and Equipment

  • Safety belt, and safety Helmet

  • Rags

Work to be carried out

  1. Check porcelain for cracks, and chipped skirts.

  2. Check for loose caps and tighten if necessary.

  3. Thoroughly clean all porcelain surfaces

  4. If fitted, read and record discharge counter reading.
