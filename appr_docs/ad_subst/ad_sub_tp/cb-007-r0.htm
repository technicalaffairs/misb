Introduction

This procedure details the steps required to completely check for the moving contact and should be performed after 10 breaking of full sc or 3000 effected operation cycles

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • CB must be isolated and proper earthing at the two sides of the CB is required.

  • Isolate the DC control voltage.

  • Wearing safety helmet is required.

  • Use only fiberglass ladders.

  • Close the valve (1 fig 2).

  • Open drainage valve (24 fig 1) to drain the air out of the tank and be sure that breaker is free of pressure, the pressure can be read at the control pressure gauge placed in the control cubicle.

Tools and Equipment

  • One set of metric open end box wrenches size range 13 mm to 24 mm.

  • One set of screwdrivers.

  • Fine file and fine emery paper.

  • Some clean and dry cloth.

  • Petroleum jelly (Vaseline) to lubricate gaskets.

  • New gasket (25 fig 4 ) with its plate.

  • Torque wrench

Work to be Carried Out

Disassembly of the moving contact complete

  1. Remove the protective cap at the end of arcing chamber, which covers the valves (30-31-32 fig2). Use spanner 19

  2. Disconnect the copper tubes connecting the insulating tube (20-26 fig 2) and each of the valves (30-32-22 fig 2) and the injector (17 fig 2). Use spanner 24

  3. Disconnect the insulating tube (20-26 fig 2).

  4. Release the nuts of the 6 stud bolts (32 fig 3) gradually by half turn all around.  Use spanner 19

  5. Pull out the entire moving contact exactly parallel to the chamber center line, taking care that porcelain screen head (19 fig 4) would not be broken.   Use spanner 19 and tube spanner 19

  6. If the contact surface is slightly burnt take it to the workshop to be cleaned by a fine file and a fine emery paper.

  7. Visually Check fixed Contact for condition.

  8. Clean the internal surfaces of the arcing chamber with dry cloth.

  9. Inspect the gasket and replace if it is necessary.

  10. Slightly lubricate gasket

  11. Replace moving contact assembly and the connections tubes.

  12. Repeat the above steps for the remaining 2 poles.

  13. Before putting the C.B in service open, close and open it again and recheck for air leaks.

  14. If the contact surface is burnt with punctures of a depth of 1-2 mm it should be changed and overhaul maintenance should be made to all other parts of arcing chamber according to MPIS Procedure Document No. CB-009-r0.

  15. Perform micro ohm and timing tests on CB

  16. Record all that has been done on the maintenance card.

Environmental Recommendation

  • Don’t burn cloth used in cleaning but collect and send it to the dump for proper disposal
