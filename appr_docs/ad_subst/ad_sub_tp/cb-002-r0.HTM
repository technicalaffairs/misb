Hydraulic Oil Filling Procedure (I1-Y2)		 Approved Date:   23 Dec 1996
Date to be Reviewed:  Dec 2001

Introduction

This document outlines the hydraulic oil filling procedures I1, which must be performed every two years as indicated by Y2 in the title block, This procedure should be carried out when:
    - Cleaning or changing the oil filter.
    - Changing the pump hose.
    - Repairing or changing the pressure relief valve (14.37).

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, qualified maintenance staff carry the suitable safety ID category

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area,

    •

  • Wear your safety helmet, shoes .... etc.

  • Breaker must be isolated.

  • Control power disconnected.

Tools and Equipment

  • Tools:        Normal Tools

  • Material:    Oil Type Aeroshell Fluid # 4, Rags

Work to be carried out

Note: Refer to the attached drawings. Figures no. 1 & 2 . 

  1. Unscrew cap (14.3. 1) from the filler of the oil reservoir

  2. Take out the venting screws (14.12)

  3. Open the pressure equalizing valve (14.37) in the control unit

  4. Pour new hydraulic oil into the oil reservoir. Use only Aero Shell Fluid (4), do not substitute any other oil.

  5. Take extreme care to see that no foreign matter (dust, sand) enters the hydraulic system. Fill the oil in slowly to avoid air bubbles entering the system.

  6. To fill the high pressure side of the hydraulic system, start the oil pump and stop it soon as the oil comes out of the vents on the operating mechanism

  7. Refit the venting screws (14.12) and screw them tight

  8. Again add oil to the reservoir up to the level in step 5

  9. Refit cap (14.3)

  10. Close the pressure equalizing valve (14.37)

  11. Start the oil pump and raise the pressure in the hydraulic system to the normal value

  12. After a settling period of about 10 minutes, carry out 5 test operations (open -close)

Caution:  Do not pressurize the operating system until the breaker has been filled with SF6 gas. Test operations must not be carried out without the correct SF6 gas pressure to avoid the risk of mechanical damage. Minimum SF6 pressure for test operations is 4.5 bar
