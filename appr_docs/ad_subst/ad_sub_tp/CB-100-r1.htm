Introduction

    This document outlines the minor maintenance procedure as indicated by P1 in the above title block to be performed every 5 years Y5. The first draft initiated at Demo substation, eng Abdel Tawab Ramadan. The first review at Nov 2007.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence with cautions marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • CB must be isolated and proper earthingat the two sides of CB is required.

  • Interrupt remote control and motor circuits (thisachieved by receiving the multi-pin plug at the left hand side at the operating mechanism)

  • Switch breaker ON and OFF manually in order to discharge the closing and springs. 

Tools and Equipments

  • Safety equipment "belt, shoes,...,etc".

Work to be carried out 

 

Checking the stroke as follow and if necessary make correction:

  1. Remove pin (80) figure 1 and open breaker to stop with the switching lever,

  2. Measure over - travel for ( open ) stroke nominal value of U open > 6 mm

  3. Insert pin (80) and close breaker with slow closing device

  4. Remove pin (80) and close breaker with switching lever up to "stop"

  5. Measure over -  travel for "close" stroke. Minimum value U close > 8 mm

  6. Measure stroke. Nominal value 170 + 2 mm
       
    Notes : to make correction as follow:
    Over - travel close ("ON" stroke) and over - travel -open ("OFF" stroke) adjustment of operating linkage (91)

 

Checking the Densimeter

  1. By reducing pressure, determine point of switching (signal 1 and signal 2), then compare with gas characteristic curve readjust the densimeter if necessary using microswitched see submitted curve.

Oil Dash Pot 

  1. Inspect top end of oil dash pot through inspection window. If traces of oil are present on oil dash pot remove and recondition as following:
     
       1- Remove side cover of operating mechanism.
       2- Remove oil dash pot and strip down
       3- Repeat the above steps for remaining two poles.

Caution: The operating linkage (91) figure 2, has been aligned at the factory and hence must not be misaligned.
