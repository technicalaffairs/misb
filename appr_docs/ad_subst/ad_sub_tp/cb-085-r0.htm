Introduction

This document details the major maintenance procedure activities P2, which must be performed every 12-16 years basis or every 2000 times operation Y12:16 as indicated at the above title block.

The first draft initiated by eng. Mohamed Khames Taha Sues 500, Canal west zone.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Isolate the CB and earth both sides

  • Discharge the closing spring

  • Disconnect supply and motor voltage

  • Discharge the SF6 GAS to atmospheric pressure

  • Comply with the relevant safety regulations

  • Personal protective equipment must be used ( breathing - mask - helmet - safety shoes .. etc)

 Tools and Equipments

  • Mounting device (special tool) with spanner

  • Torque wrenches,

  • Complete set of tools

  • Gas detector,

  • Vacuum cleaner

  • Gas density switch,

  • Paintbrushes

Spare Parts

  • Set of gaskets for 6 breaking units.

  • Set of contacts for 6 breaking units

  • ABB Grease (type G & K)

Instructions for Maintenance

Life of the Breaker    


Some points that secure a long life can be named:
        a) The burning off of the arcing contacts is small,
        b) Every breaking units is supplied with drying agent that at least meets the life of the breaker,
        c) The bearings in the gas filled space are maintenance free,
        d) The sealing of the flanges are achieved by double O rings.

 

Note: The first cause to open a breaking element is with one of:
          a) Heavy burnt arcing contacts,
          b) Ascertained leakage of SF6 gas,
          c) Ascertained mechanical failure.

 

Life of contacts

        The diagram below shows the number of interruptions as function of the breaking current that the Teflon parts of a HPL breakers can withstand before they have to be changed.


Number of Interruptions

Number of interruptions between two revisions at different breaking current levels.

 

ex : The breaker manages 20 interruptions at 40ka breaking current.

For a by bass switch a normal life of contacts and Teflon parts is 2000 operations.

 

External Cleaning

        All the HPL breakers are (wash proof) and can be cleaned by means of rising with water without damaging vital parts.

 

Lubrication

  1. The bearing of the breaker not need 1ubrication before the general overhaul.

  2. Plain bearing in mechanism detail as (arms, links and link gears) and the teeth of gears will be painted on in a thin layer of (grease G)

  3. Check of dashpots oil is necessary.

Treatment of Contact Surfaces

        When breaking elements are dismantled, the contact shall be treated as follows.
 

Silvered Surfaces

        Shall be cleaned with soft cloth and solvent (trichloroethane)

 

Copper Surfaces

        Shall cleaned with soft cloth and solvent (trichloroethane).

 

Aluminum Surfaces

        Cleaned with emery cloth or steel brush and coated with thin layer of Vaseline

 

Treatment of gaskets and seating surfaces

  1. With reassemble of the breaker all gaskets shall be replaced by new one.

  2. The sealing surfaces of the flanges are coated with corrosion preventing agent.

  3. Material for degreasing and cleaning trichloroethane (art. no 11771016-611)

  4. Material for greasing of O-rings and nitrite rubber in static sealing (ABB grease G or Aero shall grease 22, Mobil grease 28)

  5. Material for rust protection of phosphatized steel Valvoline tedyle 506(art no. 1241 Doll-108)

  6. Material for the solution of contact glue (Acetone)

Instruction for gas filling

After overhaul or repair of the breaker, it shall be filled with SF6 gas as follows:

  1. Evacuate till a pressure of (1-m bar) and continue with pumping at last (1 hour) from the moment the pressure of (1-m bar) is reached.

  2. Stop the evacuation and fill the breaker with dry and clean nitrogen up till atmospheric pressure

  3. Evacuate again to a pressure (1-m bar) and fill the breaker with clean SF6 gas to the normal working pressure. (Or to storing pressure 0.25 bar) 

Work to be carried out 

  1. Prior to dismounting the function values of the CB should be checked and recorded

  2. Measure the resistance of the main contact

  3. Measure the operating times (C - O - CO)

  4. Check of the SF6 GAS quality

Dismounting of the breaking units

  1. Empty the SF6 gas of the CB to the atmosphere pressure,

  2. Dismount the breaking unit and place it on appropriate transport base

  3. Transport the breaking unit to the work shop for the overhaul

  4. Place the breaking unit on the mounting device ( special tool ) and connect their mechanism together,

  5. Dismount the (capacitors - top covers - desicants container - upper current collector with upper terminal flange), 

  6. Dismount breaking units isolator carefully,

  7. Dismount the lower current collector & take out the puffer,

  8. The main components of the breaker pole will now be accessible for cleaning, inspection and replace the burnt or damaged parts.

  9. Replace the contacts set and nozzles when the fixed breaking contacts ( arc contact ) has been burned down max 
    (3 MM.) or hard burned on the outer diameter. as shown below:

 

 

Upper current collector

  1. Normally only replace meant of the fixed arc contact is required (using special tool) if more, carefully cleaning or replacement of the contacts fingers (main contacts) should be required

  2. Grease the are and main contacts with (Grease K) and remount in reveres order

Lower current collector

Contact finger should be dismount when necessary and replaced or treated as above in the upper current collector 

Puffer

  1. Replace the following parts nozzle, sleeve, contacts, springs, and breaking contact. These parts and contact sets are delivered in composed parts mounted together.

  2. Treat and contact surface, puffer contact and puffer cylinder and lubricate with thin layer of (grease K) and assemble all part and remount in reverse order.

Upper Mechanism

  1. No lubricantion of the mechanism IS required AS all the bearings have teflon-coated bushes. The upper mechanism
    with the piston for the puffer does not need to be dismounted if it is not severely polluted by waste products 

  2. If the mechanism is dismounted the following parts should replaced

  3. All O-rings replaced and lubricated

  4. Teflon bands for piston and checking of piston valve is level and easily movable

    Note: The guide and bush do not need to be changed

Mounting of the breaking unit

  1. Mount all parts IN reverse order of dismounting.

  2. As follows (using A new O-rings set & New desiccants) { Puffer - lower current collector - berating unit isolator - upper current collector}

  3. Check alignment of the fixed & moving contact by operate the puffer slowly towards the closed position.
    After place the breaking unit In closed position by mounting device making a vacuum & refill with SF6 gas

Filling the Gas

  1. Check with A snuffer or soapy water or { using GAS detector ) the leakage of SF 6 GAS.

  2. Empty the SF 6 GAS to the transport press (0.125 MP)

  3. Place the unit in the tighten position & fix the mechanism in this position

  4. Repeat all the above procedure for each pole

Measuring the following & Compare with Reference Values

  1. Timing operation of the CB (C-O-OCO) 

  2. Main contact resistance (micro ohm )

  3. Spring charging time (15 sec)

  4. Operating voltage ( MAX & MIN ) of the ( On / Off ) coils

  5. After carrying the tests put the circuit breaker into service

    Noting the operating signs of CB (on and off ) should be accommodated with normal operating position
