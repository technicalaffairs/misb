Introduction

This document outlines the minor maintenance procedures P1, to be performed every five years as indicated by Y5 in the title block

The first draft initiated by eng Samir Ayad at Sabtia 1, 220kv Substation. This document was updated at 12 Jan 2002 after the 1st approval date 08 May 2000 by adding some drawings and some necessary reference data.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Ensure CB isolated completely and grounded on both sides,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area,

  • Wear safety helmet, shoes, breathing mask, safety glasses,

  • Change Remote position to Local,

  • Shut off air valves,

  • Disconnect the control DC power for the CB.

  • Remove DC plugs for each pole.

Tools and equipment

  • Silicon grease, combination pliers, telephone pliers, mechanics pliers, adjusting pliers, machinist’s pliers,

  • Side-cutting pliers, Special tin snip, lacquer scraping tweezers/32 ribs, tweezers, cross leg soldering tweezers,

  • Screw drivers, screw starter, watch maker’s screw drivers, off set screw drivers, drip oilier,

  • Three square reamer, dust brush, hacksaw, measuring tape, hexagon sockets,

  • Compete set of tools, emergency light, gas detector, stop watch.

  • Dew point, acidity and air content measuring equipment.

  • Calibrated air and gas gauges.

Work to be Carried Out

 

Note:    Adjustment work on the pressure switches must be carried out only by AEG-TFK staff

  1. Check SF6 pressure on manometer.    (7 bar)

  2. Check air pressure on manometer.      (10 bar)

  3. Check compressed air manometer with control instrument.

  4. Check SF6 manometer with control instrument.

  5. Check adjustment and function of manometer contacts.     375F3 for air,    F1 for A, B, C for gas
    SF6 contact manometer, temperature-compensated (F1) fig 1, 208 fig 5
    SF6-warning, 1st stage              pe    =    6.0 bar    (p1)
    SF6-operation, blocking            pe    =    5.5 bar    (p2)

  6. Check SF6 moisture content (desired level <20oC).

  7. Check SF6 air content (desired level <2%).

  8. Check SF6 acid content (desired level <5-10 PPmg ).

  9. Check SF6 tightness at drive shafts,

  10. Check for air and gas leaks.     (audible noise or frequent refilling)

  11. Check that the air valves closed and then,

  12. Drain compressed air receiver.

  13. Check adjustment and function of compressed air pressure switch.   376F4   figure 4

    Note:    Adjustment work on the pressure switches must be carried out only by AEG-TFK staff

    Pressure switch of pneumatic system  (F4):       figure 4
    Contact 1: air refilling start pe =     9.3 bar (p6)
        stop pe =   10.0 bar (p7)
    Contact 2: auto-reclosing blocking pe =     8.5 bar (p8)
    Contact 3: "on" blocking   pe =     6.9 bar (p3)
    Contact 4: "off" (operation) blocking pe =     6.0 bar (p8)

     

  14. Check tightness of screw and pipe coupling.
    External diameter of pipe            Permissible moment
            10 mm                                        7 Nm
            12 mm                                      10 Nm
            15 mm                                      14 Nm
            18 mm                                      17 Nm

    15.

  15. Check refilling valve function: opened & closed and tightness.  (Refilling valve 373 (Y0) figure 1)

  16. Check auxiliary switch S1 for function and burning of contacts.    Figure 3

  17. Check screw coupling of electrical connections if necessary re-tighten.

  18. Open the air valves "shutoff valve 371 or 372     Figure 4

  19. Switch on control voltage for refilling valve. The pressure switch now controls refilling process until the receiver is at nominal pressure.

  20. Measure filling time as a standard value for the setting of the timing relay K2 on the instrument panel,   figure 3

  21. Check refilling time as on the time relay (K2) fig. 3, "on" delay for refilling time monitoring.  ( t set approx. 2 times the refilling time after O-C-O )

  22. Check SF6 pressure in the pole column and check the pressure of air in the compressed air receiver before switching.

  23. Carry out test switching operations:        
    - 10 C-O-switchings at one minute intervals
    - Including 2 O-    switchings with the manual emergency valve.

  24. Checking the pressure drop when switching as the following steps:
    -     Produce operating pressure.   10 bar
    -     Separate refilling valve from voltage or close shutoff valves (371/372).  Figure 4
    - Operate the circuit breaker electrically.
    -     Read off values on manometer (375) five minutes later.    Figure 4
       At 20 OC the difference is about 0.6 bar for opening, and about 0.3 bar for closing the breaker.
    - Markedly increased pressure drops point to a fault in the pneumatic system.

  25. Check the mechanical On, OFF position indicators.
