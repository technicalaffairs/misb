Introduction

This procedure details the equipment Inspection steps I1, which are performed on a daily basis as indicated by D1 in the title block. This procedure documents is the 1st. level of maintenance performed on this type of transformer.

Safety Precautions 

  • A work permit must be issued.

Tools and Equipment

  • Maintenance Record Data Sheet or mark on a copy of this document is required.

Work to be carried out

 

Oil Level

  1. Read and record the valves of the oil on the oil level gauges of the transformer and tap-changer and check that the oil levels are. Correct for the atmospheric temp.

Transformer Temperatures, Load And Voltages

  1. Read and record oil temperatures and check that the maximum temperature is acceptable by comparing it with past entries in the inspection log.

  2. Read and record load (MW and MVAR) from control room input voltage and output voltage check that load and voltage must be kept with in the specified limits, which are in the instruction manual. Oil temperature can be record from the thermo signal and be sure that the temperature on the thermo-signal Is less than 65c (65 o C alarm signal –75c trip).

Bushings

  1. Check the porcelain for cracking and other damage check that there is no leakage of oil.

  2. Check that there is no over heating of the terminals for heat discoloration using binoculars.

  3. Visually check the pressure of oil on the pressure gauges for 500 & 220& 132 kV for bushings and compare it the chart of pressure and temperature according to fig.1.

Oil Leakage

  1. Check for leakage from the transformer tank radiators, valves, pipes, gauges, gasket joints welds.

Dehydrating Breather

  1. The silica-gel Absorbent inside the breather bottle is normally magenta blue, but rarus:
    - Audible Noises
    - Loose by parts
    - Cooling equipment.

  2. Check that the brake of the wheels of the transformer on the railway is fixed.
