Introduction

    This procedure outlines the major maintenance procedure P2, which must be performed from eight to twelve years, Y8:12 as indicated in the above titled block.

    The first draft initiated at Demo substation, eng Abdel Tawab Ramadan. The first review at Nov 2007.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence with cautions marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Ensure that the CB isolated and proper earthing at the two sides of CB is required.

  • Interrupt remote control and motor circuits (this achieved by receiving the multi-pin plug at the left hand side at the operating mechanism)

  • Switch breaker ON and OFF manually in order to discharge the closing and springs. 

Tools and Equipments

  • Safety equipment "belt, shoes,...,etc".

Work to be carried out 

  1. Drain the SF6 gas from CB until 0.15 bar

  2. Opening of CB as following:
     - Before opening CB insure that the CB is in the open position and that the closing spring is discharged.
     - Disconnect the operating linkage between CB and operating mechanism, leave it adjusted.
     - Withdraw the break with the aid of the maintenance trolley see fig (4)

  3. Coars cleaning: Vacuum clean the break; the inside of the CB enclosure and insulated components immediately.

  4. Important:
     - Person who perform this task must wear throw away overall ; gloves and gas mask
     - Avoid skin-contact with decomposition products.

  5. Cleaning of individual components as following:
      - Insulators: core insulators (8), supporting rods (20), pull rods (16) and tie rods (22) figure 1. These items are always
         to be cleaned first with vacuum cleaned and with isopropyl or ethylalchol.
      - Metallic component:- clean all items as soon as possible after opening the CB to reduce the affects of reaction 
         products the cleaning by vacuum cleaner and isopropyl or ethylalchol

  6. Adsorbed: Remove the adsorbed after opening the CB a new adsorbed must be replaced shortly before reclosing the CB.

  7. Contact reconditioning : as following (see figures 2, 3) 
      - Dismantling arcing rod (46) as follow
      - Remove cylindrical head screw (92) of blast - cylinder (52).
      - Shift blast - cylinder (52) toward rear.
      - Remove complete nozzle base (42)
      - Dismantle arcing rod (46).

  8. Dismantling arcing sleeve (39) as following.   figures 1, 2
      - Remove tie rod (22) 
      - Dismantle cylinder support (43)
      - Remove current carrying cylinder (38) loosen bolt (33)
      - Loosen nut (41). Remove arcing sleeve (39)

  9. Loose items
      - Push out blast nozzle (51) from cylinder support (43)
      - Flow guides (45)
      - O ring (53)

  10. Assessment of arcing rod (46):
       - Replace when permissible limit is exceeded fig (5) or when pitting at  tip or deep scores are visible ( frictional 
          traces are permissible )

  11. Assessment of arcing sleeve (39)
       - Replace when permissible limits is exceeded and when pitting at tip is visible see fig (8)
       - Blast - nozzle (51):
       - Replace when specified limit is exceeded or break down traces are visible along or across the nozzle see fig (7) 
       - Assessment of flow guide (54)
       - Replace when specified limit is exceeded or break downs along or across the guide.
       - Assessment of absorber (87); O-ring (53) always to be replaced.

  12. Assembly as following
       - Fit arcing sleeve (39) in current carrying cylinder (38) tightening torque 80 Nm for nut (41)
       - Fit flow guide (45) and arcing rod (46) to nozzle base plate (42); tightening torque for bolt (48) 40 Nm
       - The clamping plate (47) must not rotate when tightening the arcing rod (46). The spring pin (49) must fit into the 
          hold of clamping plate.
       - The flow guide (45) must have no play.

  13. Fit O ring (53); blast nozzle (51), complete nozzle base (420 and blast cylinder (52) to cylinder support (50)

  14. Insert complete current carrying cylinder into guide cylinder (29) and screw together with cross bar (37) at front. Don't tighten hex head bolts (33). Lubricate contact sliding surfaces.

  15. Refit cylinder support (50) and tie rods (22)

  16. Operate switching lever in close direction until arcing contacts (46) and (39) engage and current carrying cylinder (38) rests approx. 5-10 mm before the shielding cap. Tighten hex head bolts (33) fig (19)

  17. Measure contact resistance
      - Measurement: position as for (6-2-f) (current carrying cylinder not engaged )
      - Measurement: fully closed permissible value max 55 ohm
      - Different between 1 to 2 measurement < 30   ohm if contact resistance are too high.
      - Measure individual contact points and clean them thoroughly.
      - Assemble absorber (87); gasket-ring (81) and close the circuit breaker

  18. Check up stroke as mentioned before (operational check up after 5 years).

  19. Make vacuum for CB until approx. 4 mbar (about 6 hours)

  20. Check leakage.

  21. Fill SF6 gas up to rated pressure.

  22. Enter the recorded values in the inspection record.

  23. Repeat the steps (1) to (11) for the remaining two poles.

  24. Measure operating time for CB and compare with commissioning value and record the values in the inspection record.
