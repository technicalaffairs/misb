Introduction 

This document lists the inspection checks I1, which must be performed on a monthly basis as indicated by M1 in the title block.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance st off should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Safety rope with caution marks surrounding the work area is required,
  • Wear your safety helmet and safety harnesses is necessary.
  • Portable grounds must be applied on both sides of the work area.
  • The complete bay must be isolated and safety makers placed around the work location, also safety barrier must be placed around the work area.
  • Fall protection equipment (safety belt/harness) must be used while working aloft.
Tools and Equipment
  • Plastic brushes,
  • Wire brushes,
  • Fiber Glass extension ladder,
  • Complete set of substations hand tools,
  • Course cloth (burlap) for cleaning insulators,
  • Lubricating grease, and oil,
  • Air blower.
Work to be carried out

  1. Clean contacts with sandpaper; clean all dust and contamination from switch parts. This step is also carried out on the earthing switch.

  2. Smooth the contact surfaces using graphite powder and emery cloth.

  3. Inspect and lubricate all movable joints on the drive rod assemblies. During the inspection look for loose fasteners and bent shafts. This step is also carried out on the earthing switch.

  4. Check the condition of copper shunts (flexible conductor), check for signs of fraying and overheating.

  5. Inspect the stationary contact condition; also check the condition of the pressure spring to ensure it is not damaged. The pressure spring must also be lubricated (oiled).

  6. Inspect the support insulators for chips, cracks and signs of flashovers. Also clean the insulators with cloth, heavy contaminates must be cleaned by emery cloth or wire brush. Care must be taken not to damage the porcelain

  7. Clean the inside of the mechanism box with air blower.

  8. After the mechanism box has been cleaned, inspect the auxiliary contacts, clean if necessary. Check the terminal strips to ensure there are no loose connections.

  9. Lubricate the gearbox with grease.

  10. Check alignment of contact during manual close operation.

  11. Perform a close and open function test, listening for unusual noises during the operations, also check that the main contacts close in properly.
