Introduction

This document outlines the equipment checklist I1, to be performed on a monthly bases as indicated by M1 in the title block The first draft initiated by eng Emad Farouk Mostafa at Kattamia substation 220kv and tested at first of June 02

Note:   HB: SF6 circuit breaker for installation indoor

            24 : Rated voltage in kv

            06 : Rated current corresponding ti 630A

            25 : Rated braking current in KA

 

The circuit breaker type HB can equipped with:

    - Closing coil: provided the spring storage mechanism is still sufficiently charged

    - Shunt trip coil: for operating an "open" element,

    - Electrical pressure monitor,

    - Operating counter,

    - Rewind motor 220v ac: for charging the spring storage mechanism.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued,

  • The qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category, 

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area,

    •
  • Wear your safety helmet, shoes .... etc
  • The circuit breaker should be switched off.
  • The HV & LV terminals must be correctly disconnected and grounded.
  • The spring of operating mechanism must be blocked.

Tools and Equipment

  • Set of mechanicals tools, torque wrenches with hex, SF6 gas

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Check the operating pressure.

  2. Check the tightening of the equipment.

  3. Check the settings of the valves and relays.

  4. Check the degree of pollution of insulating medium.

  5. Check the screw connections after 5years.

    Caution:

    In order to prevent the damage to CB neither operating mechanism nor the contacts should be manipulated while the spring assembly is fully or only partially charged

  6. Ensure that the system is left in open position.

Handling the quenching Medium        Figure 4

  1. Check the status of SF6 (not flammable, non toxic, odorless, colorless, stable chemically and is inert up to approximately 50 oC )

  2. Check the gas pressure by looking at the manometer if it is diving the green and red zones further SF6 must be added.

  3. Never operate the CB without an adequate amount of gas in the gas compartment, as otherwise the pneumatic damping system will not operate and the CB may suffer mechanical damage as a result

Filling the SF6 Gas         Figure 4

  1. Screw the valve cap of the gas filling valve by hand.

  2. Open the gate valve of the cylinder,

  3. Flush the hose connection between the gas tank, the pressure reduction valve, and the lever plug connection to the gas filling valve.

  4. Set the pressure reduction valve so that the secondary pressure reduction corresponding to the rated filling pressure at gas temperature of 20 oC is 6 bar  (5) over pressure

  5. Regularly check the gas pressure on manometer 11, indicator needle should be near the upper limit in the green zone.

  6. Screw the valve cap back to the gas filling valve 10 by hand, ensuring a tight fit
