Introduction

This procedure details the items, which are checked on an annual base (Y1). They include testing and major maintenance (P2). In addition to the procedures listed, it is expected the checks, which are done more frequently, would also be completed at this time.

Safety Precaution

  • A work permit must be issued.

  • The work area must be fenced with caution marks.

  • The CB must be isolated and properly earthed at both sides, it should be in ON position.

  • Disconnect DC voltage.

  • Wearing safety helmet.

  • Use only fiberglass ladder.

  • Personnel carrying out the maintenance should be trained and qualified or directly supervised by some one who is.   

Environmental Recommendation

  • Do not throw any oil into the sewage because the oil prevents the sewage treatment process.

  • Collect the oil that drained out of the poles into a barrel and return it back to the vendor to recycle it to save money.

  • Collect the oil used in washing the poles in another barrel and do not re-use it for filling the poles unless filtered, dried and its dielectric strength measured with an oil tester (not less than 40 kV / 2.5 mm).

  • Do not burn cloth used in cleaning but collect it and send it to the dump. 

Special Safety Precautions

  • Before working ensure the crane operator is fully aware of neighboring apparatus and the safe limits of approach to over head lines or bus bars.

  • The mobile crane must be grounded.

  • Do not use the mobile crane for the actual lift but attach a rope block and use the crane hook as a gin pole.

Tools and Equipment

  • Mobile crane – Sufficient and suitable polypropylene rope to lower the arcing chamber and support insulator that covering the arcing chamber.

  • One set of open-end wrenches.

  • One set of screwdrivers.

  • Fine emery cloth.

  • Torque wrench.

  • Some of clean and dry cloth.

  • Benzene and Grease.

  • One barrel of new oil.

  • The special tools of CB.

  • Tin cans to hold bolts, nuts, studs, washers, …etc.

Work to be Carried Out

Inspection of the Came Oil Seal

Check for any oil leakage around the came and if there is a leakage of oil, the came oil seal has to be changed as follows:

  1. Open the drainage oil- plug to drain the oil out of the CB in a suitable container.

  2. Remove the cotter pin of the pin which fixing the came to the connecting rode (2fig.1) and pull the pin out.

  3. Release the connecting rode (3 fig.1) down to the ground, and take care not to damage the porcelain of connecting rode.

  4. Unscrew the screw that fixing the came to the came shaft (4 fig.1).         Use spanner 14

  5. Unscrew the 4 screws that fixing the flange of the came to the CB (2fig.3).  Use spanner 19

  6. Pullout the came with its flange using special clamp (1fig.5), (3 fig.2).

  7. Pull out the old oil seal and clean its seat with benzene and fine emery cloth and lubricate it, and replace the old oil seal with new one.

  8. Clean the came shaft using fine emery cloth and lubricate it.

Inspection of Fixed Contacts

  1. Open the drainage oil- plug to drain the oil out of the CB in a suitable container.

  2. Using polypropylene rope to hang the arcing chamber with proper crane, 
    don’t use the mobile crane for the actual lift but attach a rope block and use the crane hook as a gin pole.

  3. Unscrew the 6 nuts (spanner 19) (1 fig.5) which connecting the arc chamber to its base

  4. Raise up the arcing chamber and lower it aside on a wooden base and take it to the workshop for inspection.   

  5. Unscrew the 11 screws of the upper cover (1 fig.1) of the arcing chamber and pull it up.

  6. Unscrew the 4 screws that fix the fixed contact to the arcing chamber (1 fig.2) using socket wrench 14 with long arm.

  7. Pull the fixed contact up.

  8. Inspect the fixed contact segments, if it is in a good condition it means that the moving contact is probably also in a good condition.

  9. If its segments are slightly burnt it should be cleaned by a fine emery cloth.

  10. If it is burnt with punctures it means that both of the moving contact and fixed contact are in a 
    bad condition their contact surface should be changed.

  11. Clean the fixed contact with benzene and new oil.

Inspection of Moving Contact

  1. Unscrew the 12 screws (sp.19) (5 fig.1)to release the arcing chamber insulator and pull it out.

  2. Unscrews the 4 screws (sp.14) to pull out the moving contact Bakelite cylinder (3 fig.3) and
     sliding contacts (5 fig.3) inside the arcing chamber insulator.

  3. To release the moving contact shaft from the came at the lower part, turn over the moving contact case 
    to see the moving contact from its base.

  4. Unscrew the 4 screws (sp.17) that fix the moving contact seat (2 fig.2) to the moving contact case.

  5. Pull out the moving contact seat and change its two gaskets.

  6. Disconnect the 4 cotter pins which fixing the moving contact shaft with came rode          (1 fig. 6).

  7. Pull out the moving contact.

  8. Clean all parts with fine emery cloth, benzene, and new oil.

  9. Check the moving contact head surface and clean it with fine emery cloth, benzene, and new oil, 
    if it is burnt with puncture it has to be changed.

  10. Reassemble all parts to its original position and Reassemble the came with its flange to its previous 
    position using special tool (1fig.4), (4 fig.2).

  11. Screw in the oil drainage plug again and refill the arcing chamber with new oil from the upper plug and 
    drain it again from the lower plug for final cleaning.

  12. Close the drainage plug again and refill with new oil from the upper plug to the correct level.

  13. Perform timing and micro-ohm tests before putting the circuit breaker in service.

  14. Record all that has been done in the maintenance card.
