Introduction

    This document outlines the inspection activities I2 to be performed on a monthly basis M1 by substation maintenance staff. These activities are in addition to the weekly inspection, which would also be performed at this time. The compressor must be taken out of service to perform this inspection.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, qualified maintenance staff carry the suitable safety ID category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required,

  • Wear standard personal safety and healthy equipment as helmet, safety shoes, rubber gloves, ... etc.

  • Ensure compressor is properly isolated,
  • Do not wear loose clothing near the compressor while it is running,

Environmental Recommendation

  • Before using a bower to clean the air filter, wear your breathing mask to protect lung tissues from the dust particulate.
  • Using solvents such as gasoline and others for cleaning the oil filter is dangerous, it will expose lungs to very hazardous and carcinogenic chemicals. You have to wear your breathing mask.
  • Don't bum the cloth used in cleaning, collect it and send it to the dump.

Tools and Equipment
  • Cleaning material, rags, air blower, solvent.

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Complete all the activities included in MPIS document no. CP-SS-005- -r0 Weekly inspection list.
  2. Inspect the air filter cartridge, if it is dirty, blow it out with compressed air from the inside outwards. If the paper elements in the cartridge are damaged, install a new filter cartridge. Ensure the filter cartridge is properly seated after it is re-assembled.
  3. Check the compressor crank case oil level through the level indicator, ensure the level is between the minimum and maximum indication lines, this must be done with the compressor at rest.
  4. With the compressor running check for any unusual noise and or vibration.
  5. Check all pipes and connectors (air and cooling) for any leaks.
  6. Record the operation hours number.
  7. Put the unit in service.
