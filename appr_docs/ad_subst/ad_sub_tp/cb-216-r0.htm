Introduction

This document outlines the major maintenance procedure P2, which must be performed every six  years Y6 or after 3000 cycles.

The first draft initiated by eng. Mohamed Abd El Hady Eskander, tested by eng. Mohamed Abd El Dayem, approved by eng. Hamdy Radwan Aly general director, accepted by eng. Motawee Hassan Aly Head Sector of El Shawashna SS, certified by eng. Ahmed Said Abd El Aziz quality control general director, MPIS Coordinator.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required

  • Wear your safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, ... etc

  • The circuit breaker must be isolated and properly earthed at both sides, it should be in ON position.

  • Disconnect DC Voltage

  • Ensure that the CB isolated completely and grounded on both sides

  • Control power disconnected

  • Isolate the DC voltage

  • Change remote switch to local operation position

  • Proper well fixed ladder should be used for climbing on the breaker

  • Don't burn the cloth used in cleaning, collect it and send it to the dump ..

Tools and Equipment

  • Safety equipment "belt, shoes, .... etc"

  • Complete toolset, scratch, electrical grease, rag, and air blower

  • Dew point, air content, acidity equipment and contact resistance rquipment

  • Gas detector, and new bottle sf6 gas, breathing mask

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Operating mechanism : clean, lubricate and oil

  2. Latching unit : clean with a degreasing agent, lubricate and oil

  3. Gear motor : lubricate

  4. Closing spring guide : clean with a degreasing agent, lubricate
    The severity of operating conditions : such as corrosive atmospheres moisture or dust, will determine the frequency of the following cleaning and maintenance jobs

  5. Cleaning of insulators

  6. Application of zinc-based paint to corroded areas replacement of mastic beads

  7. Measure the operating times. (Operations requiring the de-energizing of the circuit breaker)

  8. Opening time at rated voltage 41 ± 5 m sec

  9. Closing time at rated voltage 66 ± 10 m sec

  10. Charging time depending on operating voltage 10 sec
        * If the operating times are out of tolerance, Check:
             - On the electromagnet supply voltage.
             - The electrical circuit resistors.
             - The electrical circuits resistor drive.

  11. Monitor the drive setting time.

Tests after 6 Years or 3000 cycles

  1. Measure the contact separation time

  2. Measure the contact closing time

  3. Check pole synchronization

Position of components

1. Pole
    * Clean and dust
    * Tighten connection terminals
    * Tighten earthing contact
    * Inspect frame
    * Inspect mastic beads

2. Density switch
    * Top up gas
   * Test operation
   * Check thresholds
   * Inspect mastic beads
     (version tripolaire)

3. Control cabinet
   * Inspect tightness, ventilation
   * Tighten connection terminals
   * Check operation of heating elements
   * Inspect low voltage connection terminals

4. Operating mechanism
   * Latching unit (4.1) clean with a
     degreasing agent lubricate and oil
   * Operating mechanism (4.2) clean,
     lubricate and oil
   * Gear motor (4.3) lubricate
  * Closing spring guide (4.4) clean with
  a degreasing agent lubricate and oil

Supplies
  * Baltane degreasing agent
  * Isoflex topas L 152 grease
  * Vacuoline 133 oil

Circuit Breaker out Service

   - Before checking the thresholds, calculate the corresponding real pressures (see table in  
      appendix) .
   - Measure the thresholds as the pressure drops.
   - Thresholds may not be adjusted : replace devices that are out of tolerance.
   - In doubt, repeat the test in an air - conditioned room, using a calibration pressure gauge.

Tolerances
    -(eff. pressure at 20ºC and 1013 hpa)
    -Effective filling pressure : 7000 hpa
    -1 st level alarm effective pressure : 6200 ±  250 hpa
    -2 nd level alarm effective pressure : 6000 ± 250 hpa
    -Difference (alarm 1 p - alarm 2 p) : minl 150 hpa, maxi. 300 hpa
