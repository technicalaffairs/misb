Introduction

The Micro Ohmmeter is designed for measuring the resistance of contacts on busbars and circuit breakers. The measurement is made at a high current, typically 100 amperes DC. The voltage drop across a contact under test is compared to the voltage drop across a known internal shunt resistance and the result is displayed.

Note: This step by step instruction is for Micro Ohmmeter "Multi-Amp" Model 830250.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category, 

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area is required,

    •
  • Wear your safety helmet, shoes .... etc
  • Breaker must be proper isolated and grounded.
  • Breaker must be in "closed" position.

Tools and Equipment

  • "Multi-Amp" Model 830250.  
  • Safety helmet, shoes .... etc
    •

Work to be carried out

Calibration

  1. Using the power cord, connect the instrument to a suitable 220 Volt AC supply

  2. Plug in the heavy current cables and connect the 2 large clamps together

  3. Connect the smaller potential cables and shorten it out by connecting the large clamps together

  4. Set the Current Range Selector Switch to 1 Amp

  5. Set the Resistance Range Selector Switch to the middle position

  6. Turn the power switch to the "on" position. The digital display should now read +/- 00.0 and the pointer of the analogue test current meter should be in the green area and the "m Ohm" LED should be on. To verify proper operation, connect the 60 A DC current shunt as shown in the drawing below.

 

  1. Set the Test Current Selector Switch to 10 Amps

  2. Set the Resistance Range Selector Switch to mid position

  3. Turn the power switch to "on" position. The digital read-out should show 0.99 and the milli Ohm LED should be on

  4. Switch the Resistance Range Selector Switch to high "up" position. The digital read-out should now show approx 995 and the micro Ohm LED should be on. To test a breaker, follow the connection diagram below

      Note:
  • The potential cables must always be connected inside of the current cables
  • Maintain always same polarity (red current cable to red potential cable)
  • The instrument will give the highest accuracy on the 50 and 100 ampere ranges, however the duty cycle at this range is one minute "on" 10 minutes "off"
  • The instrument requires a 2 minute warm-up time to stabilize the metering circuit
  • The breaker contacts are generally in good condition if the obtained reading does not exceed 100 micro ohm.
