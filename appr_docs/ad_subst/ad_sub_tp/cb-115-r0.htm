Introduction

This document outlines the equipment checklist I1, to be performed on an annually bases as indicated by Y1 in the title block. Preventive maintenance is a cycle of planned inspection, testes, overhauls and servicing activities designed to keep the network equipment in good operating condition and avoid breakdowns.. 

The first draft initiated by eng Abdel Rahman Hassan Abdel  Halem Seoody at Cairo North substation 220kv

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category, 

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area,

  • Circuit breaker isolation on both ends and earthling is required.

  • Change remote switch to local position.

  • Hydraulic system with out pressure.

Tools and Equipment

  • Vacuum pump, gas detector, blower, screw drivers, set of common keys, buzzer,

  • Manometer, Micro-ohmmeter, dew point and timing equipment calibrated density monitor equipment.

Work to be carried out

  1. Check the supply voltage AC, DC.

  2. Check the operation of mechanism cabinet heaters.

  3. Check the wiring and terminals in mechanism for tightness and corrosion.

  4. Check the aux. contact and its correct position.

  5. Clean and check all connection terminals (Remote and local cabinet) and re-tightened if necessary.

  6. Retighten all connection on the breaker.

  7. Check SF6 gas pressure in compartment (7 rated pressure, at 20 degree centigrade)

  8. Calibrate gas density monitor (6.2 Alarm, 6 block) and recording.

  9. Measure dew point gas % ratio "SO2 "acidity and compare with the previous results.

  10. Check level of oil, should be at the center of the indicator at normal pressure 480 bar.

  11. Check the oil leak.

  12. Check the number of pump starts through the day and record (Look to the permissible number) (less than 5 times).

  13. Check the oil pressure switch (480 bar operation, 390 bar blocking, 470 bar pump start, 485 bar pump stop).

  14. Check the DC motor and carbon brushes (carbon should be replaced after 800 to 1000 operation hours).

  15. Check the loading time of the motor from 0-480 bar not exceed 3 minute normal operation (55:65 sec)

  16. Check the oil filter condition.

  17. Lubricate the moving connection.

  18. Re-tight of the fixture bolts.

  19. Make ON-OFF and observe the position indicator, ensure they are synchronized with operation position.

  20. Measure and record Timing ON-OFF and analyze the result (~70ms ON, ~ 40ms OFF) maximum.

  21. Measure and record contact resistance, compare with the previous results ~ 210 micro ohm. This results including the two earthing switch and disconnect switch.

  22. Make trip operation by protective relays.
