Introduction

This document lists the equipment testing procedure checklist, which must be performed for power transformers. according to each manufacturer instructions.

Safety Precautions

  • A work order must be issued and the qualified test group staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area,

  • Isolate the transformer out of service and earth

  • Disassemble all clamp from bushings (Hi-voltage side, Low-voltage side and tertiary if exists)

  • The presence of current in load is signaled by flashing caution lamp and a display segment.

  • Because of the amount of energy that can be stored in a magnetic field, precautions should be taken before disconnecting the test leads from the transformer that is under test.

  • Never remove the leads during the testing process and always allow enough time (it may take several minuets) to completely discharge the transformer being tested.

  • Connect the earth of tester to common earth of Substation.

Tools and Equipment

  • Test equipment type WRT100  The company address:
    Electrical Testing Instruments Scarbough, Ontario Canada M1V1E7
    telephone 416-292-8181, fax: 416-292-8188

  • The test equipment is specifically designed for the measurement of dc resistance in high inductive alternator windings. It offers a choice of 4 full-scale resistance ranges in 2 channels.

Work to be Carried Out

  1. In case of star connection, connect the current test leads to each phase between HV bushing to neutral, and in case of delta connection put them between the two HV bushings

  2. Connect the voltage test leads inside the current test leads.

        Note that: Potential test leads must not touch the current leads

  1. Ensure that test/dumb switch is in dump position (green color).

  2. Connect the power cable and switch the test equipment ON.

  3. Depress the test switch and wait until the current indicator reaches the maximum and stop rising. The yellow ready lamp should be flashing.

        Note :

  • The yellow flashing ready lamp means current is flowing in the load

  • current leads Must not be Disconnected while the lamp is flashing

  • You can change the range of the resistance to get accurate results, but it is not permitted to change the current setting while the current flow. If you need to change the current setting you should terminate the test.

  • To terminate the test, depress the dump switch, wait for the ready lamp to stop flashing.

        Note: Don’t operate the tap-changer during the test otherwise the current will be disconnected.

  1. Record the result for each phase and neutral at each tap-changer position in case of tapchanger on the HV side.

  2. Repeat each step with each phase (R, S & T).

  3. Record the results in the record sheet

  4. Run the test with each LV winding and for each phase. In the same manner and the same precautions.

  5. Record the results in the record sheet.
