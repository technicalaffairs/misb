Introduction

This document outlines the major maintenance procedures P2, which must be performed every five years as indicated by Y5 in the title block.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and personnel carrying out the maintenance should be trained and qualified or directly supervised by some one who is.
  • CB must be isolated and proper earthling at the two sides of the CB is required.
  • Safety fence with caution marks around the work area is required.
  • Isolate control circuit (DC voltage).
  • Wearing safety helmet is required
  • Use only fiberglass ladders.
  • Close the through valve (1 fig 2).
  • Open the drainage valve (24 fig 1) to drain the air out of the tank and be sure that the breaker is free of pressure. The pressure can be read at the control pressure gauge placed in the control cubicle.

Tools and Equipments

  • Mobile crane - Triple sheaved - Sufficient and suitable polypropylene rope to lower arcing chambers and hollow support insulators to ground.
  • One set of metric open end box wrenches , one set of socket wrenches, Allen keys (No 5 & 6) - Adjustable spanner - open end spanners (No 41 & 45) - One set of screwdrivers -Torque wrench.
  • Fine file - Fine emery paper - Wipers.
  • Hammer ½ kg - Hammer 2 kg - Rubber hammer.
  • Special spanners 6003014 , 6003015 , 361024 and 361021 .
  • Tin cans to hold bolts, nuts, studs, washers…etc, complete set of gaskets and 3 injectors .
  • Previous overhaul report including all tests, Bituminous pain.

Special Safety Precautions
  • Before working ensure the crane operator is fully aware of neighboring apparatus and the safe limits of approach to over head lines or bus-bars.
  • The mobile crane must be grounded.

  • Don't use the mobile crane for the actual lift but attach a rope block and use the crane hook as a gin pole.

Environmental Recommendation

  • Don’t burn cloth used in cleaning but collect and send it to the dump for proper disposal.

Work to be Carried out

  1. Remove the protective cap at the end of arcing chamber which covers the valves(30- 31-32-fig 2)Use spanner 19

  2. Disconnect the copper tubes connecting the insulating tube (20-26-fig 2) and each of the valves (30-32-22 fig 2) and the injector (17 fig 2).Use spanner 24

  3. Disconnect the insulating tube (20-26 fig 2).

  4. with a proper rope hang the arcing chamber with proper crane.

  5. Release the nuts of the 12 stud bolts which connecting the lower flange of the arcing chamber with the upper flange of the support insulator .Use spanner 17

  6. Raise up the arcing chamber

  7. Take it to the workshop for checking and maintenance.

  8. Release the nuts of the 6 stud bolts (32 fig 3 ) gradually by half turn all around .Use spanner 19 and tube spanner 19

  9. Pull out the entire moving contact exactly parallel to the chamber center line, taking care not to break the porcelain screen head.

Inspection of the moving contact (13 fig 4 )

  1. If it’s surface is in a good condition it means that the fixed contact is probably also in good condition and there is no need to disassemble it.

  2. If its surface is slightly burnt it should be cleaned by a fine file and a fine emery paper, then placed again in its previous position into the arcing chamber.

  3. If it is burned and punctured to a depth of 1-2 mm it should be changed and overhaul maintenance should be made to all other parts of the arcing chamber to change the screen(19 fig 4), gliding segment contacts (20 fig 4), moving contact (13 fig 4) and fixed contact (6 fig 3).

Changing the screen (19 fig 4 )

  1. Release 4 bolts (22 fig 4 ) and bring them out of the cylinder ( 12 fig 4 ).Use screwdriver

  2. Unscrew the screen to release it and pull it out.

  3. Change it with another complete one.

  4. At the end screw in the 4 bolts and tighten them properly. Use screwdriver

Changing the sliding segment contacts: (20 fig 4)

  1. Release the control relays of moving contact ( 5 fig 4 ) by releasing the nuts

    2. (73 fig 4) of the studs. Use spanner 13

  2. Release the control outlet relay ( 4 fig 4 )Use spanner 13

  3. Release entire base of the valves (30-31-32 fig 2) by releasing the 4 nuts (23 fig 4 ) gradually by half turn all around.

  4. Pull out the entire base, moving contact spring (9 fig 4) ,moving contact tube (13 fig 4 ) and the cylinder (12 fig 4 ).

  5. Release the cylinder (12 fig 4 ) and pull out the segment contact (20 fig 4) along with the two springs which control the position of segment contacts .

  6. Inspect all the parts and change the moving contact tube ( 13 fig 4), the sliding segment contacts, the two rings of the cylinder (12 fig 4) and the internal gasket of the head of the cylinder.

  7. Assemble the whole set of the moving contacts once again to be ready to place it in its previous position after finishing the maintenance of the fixed contact and replace it in the arcing chamber.

Changing the fixed contact

  1. Insert the special spanner 6003014 into the arcing chamber to release the counter nut (33fig3).

  2. With the special spanner 6003015 unscrew the fixed contact.

  3. Unscrew the position screws ( 46 fig 5). Use screw driver

  4. Release the head (45 - 44 fig 5 ) by means of special spanner 361021.

  5. Change the head (44 - 45 fig 5 ) completely and connect it with contact tube (43 fig 5).

  6. Tighten the position screw ( 46 fig 5 ) properly.

  7. Replace the fixed contact in its place into the arcing chamber

  8. Before replacing the parts of arcing chamber clean all parts and the internal insulator surface of the arcing chamber with dry cloth, then grease the moving contact slightly with the contacts grease.

  9. Prior to definite tightening of the counter nut (33 fig 3), the measure (D fig 3) must be 391 mm.

  10. Replace the moving contact in its place into the arcing chamber

Disassemble the entire base of the valves (30-31-32 fig 2) to make maintenance as following:

  1. Release the six bolts (74 fig 4 ) by means of allen key No 6 to disconnect the outlet valve ( 31 fig 2) out off the base of the two control relays (30 fig 2 ).

  2. Change the gasket (26 fig 4) .

  3. Check the plate with gasket ( 25 fig 4) and change it complete if needs to be changed

 

Disassemble the outlet valve for maintenance (31 fig 2).

    1. Release the four nuts of the metal cover of the valve. Use spanner 17

    2. Release the nut (54 fig 4) to release the piston and the lower gate.

    3. Change the piston ring and the gasket of the gate. Use spanner 19

    4. Assemble the outlet valve once again .

Disassemble the outlet relay (32 fig. 2 also re. to fig. 7 for more detail in performing mtce)

    1. Release the two nuts of the studs which holding it over the outlet valve. Use spanner 13

    2. Raise it up.

    3. Release the four nuts which hold the parts and lift the cover (77 fig 7).Use spanner 10

    4. Change the gaskets (38 and 76 fig 7) and the gasket of the opening D take care that all gaskets are properly placed onto their seats .

Disassemble the control relay (30 fig. 2 also re. to fig. 6 for more detail )

    1. Release the four nuts which assemble its parts and lift the cover. Use spanner 10

    2. Change all gaskets and take care that all are properly onto their seats.

Maintenance of pneumatic relay: (1 fig. 9 also re. to fig. 10 for more detail )

    1. Separate the relay from its support

    2. Unscrew the guide (27 fig. 10) with special spanner (361024

    3. Unscrew the nut (28 fig. 10)

    4. Remove the cover (29 fig. 10)

    5. Change the gasket (25 fig. 10) and it must be glued to the body by “means of universal gum”

    6. Change the piston ring (28 fig. 10) and valve (26 fig. 10)

    7. Clean all the parts of the relay and all the openings.

    8. Reassemble the relay and no lubrication is required but care should be taken that the gaskets are properly laid in their slots.

Maintenance of the air tank and return pressure valve

    1. Remove the two covers of the inspection holes which one of them is provided with return pressure valve (26 fig 1)Use spanner 24

    2. Clean the tank from dirtiness and possible condensate by a flow of compressed air and vacuum machine.

    3. If the internal surfaces are rusty it should be cleaned by metal brush then twice coated with bituminous coating.

    4. Change the two gaskets of the inspection holes covers

    5. Repaint the outside surface of the air tank.

    6. Clean the return pressure valve, check the gasket (30 fig. 15)and spring (32 fig. 15) change them if it need

     

Regulating of the safety valve (28 fig1) see fig 17.

    1. Take off the cap (121 fig 17). Use spanner 19

    2. Release the counter nut (120 fig 17). Use spanner 10

    3. By rotating the nut (119 fig17) regulate the desired pressure which can be seen at the pressure gauge in the control cubicle.

    4. Clean the filter (27 fig17) and dried its filtering element (33 fig 18) at a temperature less than 80 ° C until all humidity has evaporated. Use spanners 24, 41, 45 & Allen 5

    5. After finishing the maintenance of all the CB parts reassemble it once again as in its previous position and recheck the tightening of all nuts and bolts by torque wrench.

    6. Recharge the CB with air and visually check all the feeding pipes.

    7. Before putting in service perform micro ohm and timing tests, record the results, compare with previous test results and report / investigate significant changes.

    8. Finally record all results on maintenance card.
