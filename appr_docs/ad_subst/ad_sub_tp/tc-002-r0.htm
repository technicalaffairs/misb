Introduction

We recommend a periodic inspection of the tap changer equipment to certain a high reliability in service according to manufacture instructions. The major maintenance procedures P1 should be carried out every five years Y5

We recommend that personnel well acquainted with the equipment should carry out the inspection. The Inspection should be carried out as quickly as possible with the most care and tidiness.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required

  • Caution marks in control room is required

  • Wear your safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes.... etc

  • The high village CB and voltage CB peeper earthling is inquired

  • Use fiber glass ladders

Tools and Equipment

  • Oil littering machine with tank, hoses and flushing pipe,

  • Mobile crane

  • Empty oil barrels and pails for flushing oil

  • Oil dielectric tester and oil sample bottles, Multi metes

  • One set of socket wrenches screwdrivers

  • The special tools for the tap lap changes,

  • New oil

  • Proper plastic garbage bag to catch oil drips

  • Rubber gloves, Small brushes, Cloth wipes

  • Spare pasts contacts bolts washers gasket cover glue.

  • Proper steel tray to wash the diverter with oil in. it.

Work to be Carried Out
 1

Drive on Load tapchanger type SAV 220/1600-3-12/1 to the adjusting position (position 4)
 2

Drain the oil from the oil compartment of the OLTC
 3

Open the OLTC cover.
 4

Loosen the holding screws of the upper rim of the internal cylinder.
 5

Lift the diverter switch, insert by using the existing lifting eyes.
 6

The diverter switch insert exists of the insolating cylinder with transition resistors and the diverter switch.

  1. Disconnect the insulating cylinder from the diverter. Switch after loosen the screws of the connection between insulating cylinder and diverter switch.

  2. Lift the insulating cylinder.
 7

Both parts should be thoroughly cleaned with new oil, Clean the parts with soft clothes on brushes.
 8

Check the diverter switch

  1. All contacts tightened, check by trying to move them by hand.

  2. Visually check all sprigs.

  3. Check if the fixing ball of the energy accumulator is in correct position.

  4. Check all connecting leads - they must be flexible and must be fixed at their fixing points

  5. Check all sliding contacts - if the contact surface is ok clean them

  6. Push all sliding contacts - check if there is enough pressure.

  7. Switch the diverter switch in both operating position for doing this check but remember the position your hair dismount the diverter switch from the oil compartment.

  8. Check the contacts for the resistor connection.

   
 9

Check visually the contacts at the resister and switching contacts.
 
                                     6mm                                                                                 2mm                                  
                      New contact                                                         Contact must be changed

 

 

 

                                        

 10

Insulating cylinder

  1. Measure resistor value the value should be written on the upper steel ring.

  2. Check sliding contacts and clean then.

  3. Check apposite posts at the sliding contacts in the oil compartment.

  4. Check visually the insulating cylinder for discharge traces.

  5. Check of the connection to the resistors are faced and tightened.

  6. Check visually the resistors for their condition.
 11

 12

Switch the diveter switch back to the position you hare dismounted it from the oil compartment.

Check if the diverter switch is in its position on the even side (if you dismounted at stop 4)
 13

Insulating cylinder

  1. Measure resistor value the value should be written on the upper steel ring.

  2. Check sliding contacts and clean then.

  3. Check apposite posts at the sliding contacts in the oil compartment.

  4. Check visually the insulating cylinder for discharge traces.

  5. Check of the connection to the resistors are faced and tightened.

  6. Check visually the resistors for their condition

 14

Oil compartment

  1. Clean thoroughly with new transformer oil.

  2. Clean and check sliding contacts.

  3. Check insulting cylinder for arcing, traces.
 15

  1. Insert the diverter switch unit into the oil compartment.

  2. Watch the guiding pin on the bottom plate it must fit into the diverter switch bottom.

  3. Watch the marks on the upper metal ring, it must fit to the mark on the rim of the tap-changer head,

  4. Screw the holding Screws to the upper ring of the insulating cylinder and fix it to the oil compartment

  5. Check with a hand crank on the motor driver.
 16

Fill new transformer oil into the oil compartment and the conservator.

(The break down voltage for oil sample 50 kv/2.5 mm)
 17

Close the OLTC cover and vent the cover. If the OLTC is equipped with a suction pipe vent the suction pipe:
1- Check safety diagram visually.
2- Check functions of protective relay (flow relay).

Motor drive inspection
 1

Check coupling of motor drive and OLTC, it should be symmetrically.
 2

Check by hand crank the switching of mechanical and electrical and position.
 3

Check contractors, breakers, housing sealing, terminals general condition.
 4

Make test operations by hand first then make test operations with electrical drive.
 5

Grease necessary parts of motor drive if necessary.

 

Drive shaft, Berel gear

 1

Open berel gear and check it visually.

 2

Grease ports if necessary.
 3

Check drive shafts and coupling visually and grease them if necessary.


Notes: The transformer must not be put into service again before one boor after filling the new oil.

The diverter switch should not be without oil more than 10 hours.
