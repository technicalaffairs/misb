Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection I1, to be performed every three months as indicated by M3 in the title block

The first draft initiated by eng. Ahmad Fathallah Abou Zahou, accepted by general director eng. Said Abd El Gawad, and certified by eng. Esam Sadeq: Alex. Network general director and MPIS coordinator.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued.

  • The qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable ID safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear your safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, ... etc.

  • Isolate the breaker and ground it

  • Proper well fixed ladder should be used for climbing on the breaker

  • Isolate the DC voltage

  • Change the operation position from remote to local

Tools and Equipment

  • Blower multimeter gas detector set of common keys screw drivers set

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Vacuum the interior of the circuit breaker control panel to remove all accumulated dust and dirt

  2. Clean panel exterior bushings bus bars and connector of dust and dirt

  3. Check control wiring for loose connections and any signs of overheating and tighten all connections as required

  4. Check and tighten all ground connections as required repair any damaged ground connections

  5. Check the operating mechanism of the circuit breaker inspect for loose bolts nuts or linkage (tighten or replace parts as required)

  6. Check for gas leak of the circuit breaker (check the gas gauge)

  7. Check the circuit breaker on/off indications and adjust its position as required

  8. Check the cabinet heaters

  9. Check the close spring changed indicator

  10. Check the trip and close springs indication visually note any remarks for repairing

  11. Through the operators check the circuit breaker functions from remote and local

Return C.B. to service

  1. Remove any tools or equipment which should be used in the maintenance

  2. Switch on the DC voltage

  3. Remove any fixed or proper ground

  4. Change the operation position from local to remote

  5. Finish the work permit

  6. Place a copy of the MPIS document in the maintenance file
