Introduction

This procedure details the minor maintenance procedure P1, to be performed on an annually bases or according to contact life as indicated by Y1 in the title block.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required

  • Wear your safety and health equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, safety glasses, ... etc.

  • Wear breathing mask to protect your lung tissues from the dust particles ( Before using a blower to clean the control cabinet and insulators )

    •

  • CB must be isolated completely and the earthing switch must be connected on both sides of the CB.

  • Isolate the DC voltage. (Switch off the control circuit and insert closing and tripping preventive pins.) 

  • Ensure that the CB grounded on both sides.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set

    •

  • Voltage indicator.

  • Micro-ohmmeter , Blower .

  • Gas detector , Acidity equipment , Air content equipment .

  • Pieces of clothes and rags.

  • Grease and cleaning liquid ( like gasoline ).

  • Hydraulic oil and SF6 gas if needed .

Work to be carried out

  1. Visual inspection of circuit breaker components.

  2. Check any abnormal noise.

  3. Check any rust at metallic frame and remedy it.

  4. Check the cabinet heaters.

  5. Check the door packing, replace it if necessary.

  6. Retighten the earth terminal.

  7. Clean the porcelain from dirties and check for cracks.

  8. Inspect the accumulator and interruption chamber for any cracks.

  9. Check registered number of operation counters and annotate it.

  10. Check Pump running timer settings : 3 min.

  11. Check oil leak from hydraulic pump and also oil level in reservoir.

  12. Check normal working pressure for hydraulic :  335 bar.
    Warning : don't operate the CB unless it is filled with SF6 gas and the pressure at 7 bar.

  13. Check the settings of gas pressure gauge, refer to gas reference values :
    Normal working pressure 7     bar  at 20°C
    Gas trip lock out pressure   5.9  bar  at 20°C  ( Reset : 6.2 bar )
    Gas alarm , gas lockout pressure   6.3  bar  at 20°C  ( Reset : 6.6 bar )
        

  14. Test the time for closing and opening operation:
    Closing time must be about 65     m sec.
    Opening time must be about 27.5  m sec
        

  15. Test the maximum timing spread of main contacts :
    Closing                    10  ms over 3 phases
    Opening                      3  ms over 3 phases.
        

  16. Retighten of hydraulic parts.

  17. Read and annotate the number of operation of accumulator and annotate it.

  18. Check the Nitrogen pressure ( about : 200 bar ).

  19. Check the safety valve for SF6 gas:
    must open when the pressure gas reach 7.7 bar According to Doc. No. CB-019-r0

    Note: New SF6 gas should have a moisture content less than 125 ppmv equivalent to 15 ppmg.
    Used gas should have a moisture content less than 500 ppmv (62 ppmg), and if it is more, the gas should be exchanged with new gas or in the case of GIS equipment (a very large quantity of gas) it is more economical to use the gas truck to re-condition the g    as.

  20. Test SF6 moisture content  < 62 PPMw, must change the gas if more than that .

  21. Test SF6 Oil content  < 10 PPMw.

  22. Test SF6 Hydrogen Flouride ( HF ) content  < 1 PPMw.

  23. Test SF6 Sulpher dioxid ( S02  ) content  <   0.05 %.

  24. Measure SF6 percentage volume    ≥   99 %

  25. Measure the contact resistance  ≤ 100 m W according to Doc. No. CB-004-r0

Checking the Number of Pump Restarts

Note: The number of pump restart reflects the effectiveness of the seals on the unit. It depends upon the amount of leakage, the accumulator volume and working tolerance of the pressure switch.

  1. Calculate the volume injected at each pump restart = the running time in minutes x 2 (l/m).

  2. Notice from the number of restarts the rate of leakage is deduced. (A leakage of 4 liters per day should be investigated).

Checking the Oil Level in the Reservoir

  1. Check the oil level visually. If the level has dropped the oil must be topped up and investigate the reason of the fall.

  2. Normal: This mark corresponds to the oil level when the circuit breaker is in the open position, with the maximum pressure in the accumulators and a temperature of + 15 oC

  3. Warm: This mark corresponds to the oil level for the above conditions but at a temp. of +40 oC

  4. High: At high temperatures the pre-inflation pressure rises, the oil volume is reduced in the accumulators and the oil level rises in the tanks.

  5. Cold: This mark corresponds to the oil level for the above conditions but at a temperature of -25 oC

  6. Low: At low temperatures the pre-inflation pressure rises, the oil volume is reduced in the accumulators and the oil level lowered in the tanks.

  7. Intermediate: At Intermediate temperatures rises, the oil level should be adjusted proportionally to the temperatures between warm and normal or between normal and cold.

  8. When the oil level is checked every 12 months, the temperatures should be taken into account, in order to assess whether there has been any loss of oil.

  9. When topping up the oil, use only an oil complying with the required specifications.
