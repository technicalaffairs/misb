Introduction

    This procedure outlines the major maintenance procedure P2, which must be performed every ten years as indicated by Y10 in the above titled block, on the circuit breaker type BHG 112 see figure 6. The first draft initiated at Demo substation by eng Abdel Tawab Ramadan. The first review at Nov 2007.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable ID safety category.

  • Safety fence with cautions marks surrounding the work area.

  • CB must be isolated and proper earthing at the two side of CB.

  • Interrupt remote control and motor circuits (this achieved by receiving the multi-pin plug at the left hand side at the operating mechanism)

  • Switch breaker ON and OFF manually in order to discharge the closing and springs.

Tools and Equipments

  • Safety equipment "belt, shoes... etc"

Work to be carried out 
 

The following inspections are to be carried out in addition to the annual inspections and the 5 years inspection

  1. Remove cover for the operating mechanism; operating rod (6-4) and lever cover (6-5)
    Caution: the safety bracket (6-6) above the belt-crank (6-2) must not be removed. Danger of accident see figure 2.

  2. Verification of the tightening torque: with the aid of a torque spanner check the torque with which locking-nuts at operating rods have been tightened.

  3. Operating rods (6-1): 142 Nm; tie-rods (6-3): 106 Nm

  4. Switch-on breaker by operating the manual-release.

  5. In the "ON" position. Measure dimension "C" with a depth-gauge resting on stud (6-7)

  6. Trip breaker by operating the manual release.

  7. In the "Off" position Measure dimension "B" with a depth-gauge resting on stud (6-7)

  8. Stroke "C-B" is satisfactory if the value recorded is equal 170 + 1 mm, see figure 4.

  9. Measure dimension "E" from stud (5-5) to the rim of the dash pot housing with depth gauge, see figure 5, compare with the value indicated on the dash pot housing. permissible tolerance + 0.5 mm

  10. Uncouple the operating rod (6-1) at the bell-crank (6-2) by removing pin (6-7), insert pin (6-7) in bell-crank (6-2), with hand hand lever (6-8) jack breaker up to the "OFF" stop and measure dimension "A" with depth gauge resting on pin (6-7)  see figure 2

  11. Compare value "B-A" (= over travel "OFF") with the value indicated on bracket (6-6).The permissible tolerance is < 0.5 mm    see figure 4

  12. With hand lever (6-8) jack breaker up to the "On" stop and measure dimension "D" with depth - gauge resting on pin (6-7). The different between measured value and value indicated on the bracket (6-6) must be < 0.5 mm  

  13. The overall stroke "D-A" must be < 186

  14. With hand lever (6-8) jack breaker in the "Off" position; couple-up operating rod (6-1) to bellcrack (6-2) with pin (6-7)

  15. Fit protecting components and casing.

The BHG 112 is a single pressure puffer type SF6 gas circuit breaker with one break per phase. It is designed for incorporation in the B212 metal-clad switchgear concept and meets the requirements of appropriate standards. the three horizontal lying circuit breaker poles and the motor-wound spring operating mechanism FKF 2-6 incrporate one unit. A three-phase operating mechanism is employed. The operating mechanism and the supporting structures are  fixed to the circuit breaker enclosures,

The break employed for the BHG 112 is identical to that of the HGF100 puffer type circuit breaker . Owing to the simple design, the breaker requires little maintenance and is very reliable in operation.
