Introduction

    This document outlines the inspection and maintenance procedures to be performed on an annual basis as indicated by the Y1 in the title block. These activities in addition to the frequent intervals weekly and monthly and 6 months inspection would also be performed at this time.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, qualified maintenance staff carry the suitable safety ID category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required,

  • Wear standard personal safety and healthy equipment as helmet, safety shoes, rubber gloves, ... etc.

  • Ensure compressor is properly isolated,

  • Do not wear loose clothing near the compressor while it is running,

Environmental Recommendation

  • Using solvents such as gasoline and others for cleaning the strainer is dangerous, it will expose lungs to very hazardous and carcinogenic chemicals. You have to wear your breathing mask.

  • Don't bum the cloth used in cleaning- collect it and send it to the dump,

  • Before using a bower to clean the air filter cartridge from dust, wear your breathing mask to protect lung tissues from the dust particulate.

  • Measure the Noise Level and Vibration for the cooling fan. Be sure that it does not exceed the standard levels (40-60 dB in industrial workshop). Fix any problem to prevent the noise pollution. 'For any question refer back to the manufacturer.

Tools and Equipment

  • Cleaning material, rags, emery cloth, air blower, solvent, and Benzene,

  • New compressor oil, 12 liters,

  • New air filter cartridge, Grinding post, and gasket set.

  • Complete set of substation maintenance hand tools.

Work to be Carried Out

  1. As indicated in the introduction, complete all the steps outlined in MPIS document no. CP-SS-009- -r0, Minor Maintenance (P1-M6)

  2. With compressor completely isolated, change the crankcase oil by removing the drain plug and attached oil strainer. Also clean the strainer with solvent.

  3. Note: Drain the oil into a container ensuring proper disposal of the scrap oil and the solvent used to clean the strainer. The manufacture recommends oil be changed after 2000 hours of operation or 2 years, which ever comes first.

  4. Fill the compressor with new approved oil.

  5. After the oil change operation is complete, the compressor must be started several times in quick succession to prime the oil pump. This is required as the oil lines were drained with the crankcase.

  6. Inspect the air filter cartridge, if it is dirty, blow it out with compressed air from the inside outwards. If the paper elements in the cartridge are damaged, install a new filter cartridge. Ensure the filter cartage is properly seated after the cartridge is re-assembled.

  7. With the compressor at rest, check the oil level in the crankcase. The oil level should never fall below the sight glass.

  8. Remove first stage cylinder head and suction valve, remove carbon and dirt using emery cloth. Repeat this step for the second and third stages of the compressor.

  9. Remove and inspect the unloading valve block, this should also be cleaned to prevent it from sticking.

  10. Switch on the compressor to blow out any carbon deposits in the supply lines of each successive stage.

  11. Check the operation of the oil pump by observing the correct oil pressure on the oil gauge it should read between 2.5 bar – 4 bar.

  12. Check the operation of the cooling fan listen for excessive noise and signs of imbalance

  13. Check all pipes and connectors (air and cooling) for any leaks.

  14. With all suction and supply lines connected, test run the compressor, observing the correct gauge pressure from each stage, they read 1st stage 3.1 - 3.5 bar, 2nd stage 11 - 12.5 bar and 3rd stage max. 40 bar.

  15. Check the operation of the non-return valves between the main air receiver tank and the compressor unit.

  16. Check the compressor air tank, and momentarily open the drain valve to drain off any condensed.

  17. Check the operation of the compressor room thermostat controlling the exhaust fan, ensure proper operation of the room’s exhaust fan.

  18. Clean the compressor room floor.

  19. Put the unit in service.
