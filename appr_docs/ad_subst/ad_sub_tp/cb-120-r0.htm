Introduction

This document outlines maintenance procedure P1, to be performed after 1500 operations indicated in the title block. The first draft initiated by eng Mohamed Aly Aly Hassan, El_Shabab Gas Turbine Power Station and tested at 11 April 2002

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category, 

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area,

  • Switch off, the CB and disconnect switches on both sides. (busbar and line)

  • Block the CB drive and disconnect switch on busbar, and on line side

  • Switch off the control voltage 125v, and effectively grounded before work on the CB

Tools and Equipment

  • A complete set of tools, Toppingtrolly for insulating and are quenching gas systems

  • SF6 gas cylinder (40 kg approx.)

  • Filter material Y-AL 203 (activated alumina)

  • Pump, Filter, sheet alumina, and grease SKF

  • Sealing ring (130a), density relay, and 7 pieces of O-ring (123a)

Work to be carried out   

  1. Clean the condition of the winding motor and replace brushes.

  2. Check the correct operation of safety devices.

  3. Check the hydraulic oil level is normal +6 mm

  4. Check the correct operation of operating coils and aux. switch.

  5. Replace any damaged or warm parts.

  6. Check the source voltage control value.

  7. Check the operation of the circuit from manual position.

  8. Check the operation of the circuit from remote position.

  9. Check any gas leaks.

  10. Check the gas density relay insulating gas.

  11. Check the gas pressure 6 bar abs at 20 degree Centigrade.

  12. Measure the closing time of the CB < 50 ms

  13. Measure the tripping time of the CB < 60 ms

  14. Check the operating mechanism

  15. Check the DC resistance.

The following directive/instructions are developed for MBW Dew point Measuring Instrument

 

Important

The most accurate test results will always be obtained at close to atmospheric pressure, a pressure reducing device should be used when testing SF6 gas under system pressure, Sampling lines must be clean an dry, the lines and instrument must be purged for several minutes with the gas to be tested before the actual test is carried out.

Test Setup

 

 

1.  SF6 gas cylinder or switchgear compartment 6.   Quick connect coupling
2.  Pressure reduing device 7.   Internal instrument gas flow path
3.  Special coupling adaptor 8.   Flowmeter valve
4.  Flexible teflon sample gas line 9.   Flowmeter
5.  Control valve 10. Measuring head

 

  1. Adjust the pressure reducing valve at the sampling point to approximately 1 bar above atmosphericpressure. Install the flowmeter on top of the instrument , connect the sample gas line starting from thesampling point to the Gas In connection on the instrument. Make sure that all connections are tight and that all valves are closed .

  2. Open the main valve of cylinder or gas compartment slightly Open flowmeter valve (8) completely, open control valve (5) slightly until the flowmeter indicatesa gas flow through the instrument of 30 L/H.

  3. Press Mains switch, the button should be illuminated . Set Mode switch to Measurement, the button is in the released position. The indicator needle will first remain in the No Dew position ( red field ). After a short time it will move into the green field (Correct position) and remain there. This indicates that the cooling capacity is sufficient and the instrument will correctly display the dew point. If the indicator needle remains inside the red field, the effective dew point lies below the cooling capacity of the instrument.

 

Notes

  1. Observe the digital read-out until it has stabilized, this can sometimes take a considerable amount of time, make sure the gas flow through the instrument constant throughout the test.

  2. Once you have obtained a stable dew point reading, use the chart on the next page to convert your dew point reading to moisture content in ppm by volume or ppm by weight. With a ruler draw a straight line from 1 bar on the right column through the point in the center column of your obtained reading. Extend the line to the left column and read off moisture content of the gas under test in ppm by volume.

  3. If there is no means of reducing the breaker or GIS compartment pressure, use the same test hook- up but ignore the pressure reducing valve. However you will now use the flowmeter valve to control the gas flow throug the instrument. First open valve (5) all the way, then open the valve (8) on the flowmeter slightly until you have the desired gas flow of 30 L/H .To obtain a ppm reading use the chart again but this time start with the exact pressure you are applying to the ( Gas In ) point of the instrument e.g .3.5 bars or 6.2 bars..

  4. New SF6 gas should have a moisture content of approximately 125 PPMv equivalent to 15 PPMg a reading of 500 PPMv (62 PPMg) or higher indicates problems the gas should be exchanged with a new gas or in case of GIS equipment (a very large quantity of gas) it is more economical touse the gas truck to re-condition the gas OR (treatment the gas)

  5. Important Before test, clean and dry the lines and instrument must be purged for several minutes the gas to be tested before the actual test  carried out.

  6. For further information on instrument calibration and cleaning of the internal dew point mirror consult the manufacturers instruction manual .

Interpetation of test results

New SF6 gas should have a moisture content of approximately 125 ppmv equivalent to 15 ppmg . A reading of 500 ppmv  (62ppmg) or higher indicates problems. The gas should be exchanged with new gas or in case of GIS equipment (a very large quantity of gas ) it is more economical to use the gas truck to re-condition the gas.

Properties of SF6

Sulpher Hexaflouride SF6 as a gas is approximately five times heavier than air and possesses excellent properties. At room temperature and normal pressure (20ºC,1bar) it is gaseous and has adensity of 6.0 89/L its critical temperature is 45.6ºC for ease of transportation and treatment SF6 can be liguified by compressing as apure gas it is colourless, odourless, non-inflammable, non - toxic and inert

 

 

Electrical Characteristics

SF6 has excellent dielectric properties. The dielectric strength of SF6 gas at 2 bare is more than twice that of air at the same pressure and exceeds that of insulting oil the dielectric strength increases as gas pressure is increased.

Filling the circuit breaker         reference to figures no. 3, 4     For  reference to figures no. 3, 4

This avoids the necessity to evacute the C.B poles on site. consequently after erection it is only necessary to increase the gas pressure in the breaker poles to the rated filling perssure of 6 bar gauge refer to 20 ºC corresponding to the black mark on the green range of the density relay and the filling pressure curve

After completing maintenance the breaker poles are reflled with SF6 gas as follows:

  1. Connecd the vacuum pump with pressure gauge to the filling and start the vacuum in the C.B down to a pressure of 0.133m bar with the pump running continue pumping for a nother 30 minutes then stop the pump and shut it off

  2. Leave for two hours and note the pressure and record

  3. Check the pressure again after at least 10 hours, record the value

  4. Comparing the two values it is possible to determine whether the C.B interrupting chambers are sufficiently

  5. After establishing the C.B to be gastight couple the filling hose of the topping up gas unit

  6. Charge the SF6 gas up to pressure of 6 bar gauge referred to 20 ºC corresponding to the black mark on the green field of the density relay scale

  7. Disconnect the filling hose and screw the cover cap

  8. It is Recommended to leave the manual stop valves open during normal service
