Introduction

The purpose of this procedure is to provide detailed safe method for substations maintenance staff to minimize the possibility of error causing personal injury or damage to equipment.

This document outlines the Major Maintenance Procedures steps (P2) required for the Sf6 Outdoor Circuit Breaker 132KV type FX11 GEC Alsthom., this procedure to be performed every 8:12 year (Y8:12) or after 2500 switching cycles as indicated in the above title block  

The Preventive maintenance is a cycle of planned inspection, tests, overhauls and servicing activities designed to keep the Network equipment in good operating condition and avoid breakdowns.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued.

  • CB must be isolated and proper Earthing at the both sides

  • Interrupt the control circuit (DC voltage).

  • Discharges cloning spring 70.02 and tripping spring 40.04 by operating manual lever trip and manual lever close.

  • Wearing safety helmet is required.

Tools and Equipment

  • Densimeter test device W 41, Leak detector W 51 ,

  • Thermometer W 52

  • Contact ohmmeter W 68

  • One set of metric open and box wrenches.

  • Cleaning cloths, Lubricating grease, Circuit indicator (L.figure3) AVO meter.

Work to be carried out

  1. Inspect the state of circuit breaker according to MPIS docs. no. CB-041-r0 & CB-042-r0

  2. Check the condition of the contact material and the wearing caused by arcing without opening the gas compartments, the maximum admissible wear should be determined as follows:
    1.     Dimension (x) at the moment of electrical contact separation has been determined for poles a, b , and c of each circuit breaker on Completion of erection and recorded on the label in motor wound spring mechanism  (70.00 frg.1)
    2.     During service period xa, xb and xc can be differs from its new - condition value, Where x: Dimension from outer edge of chassis (60.00 fig.1) to the left and of linkage bracket pull (61.01 fig.1)

  3. Lift off the cover of the operating mechanism.

  4. Mount the slow close device W37 (Fig. 2).

  5. Connect circuit indicator (AVO) to primary high voltage terminals of pole a (fig. 3).

  6. For measuring of x, phase a slowly move the circuit breaker with the help of slow close device W 37 from the on position towards the off - position.

  7. Measure the dimension xa upon electrical contact separation and record it.

  8. Repeat measurement at least twice.

  9. Repeat measurement for poles b and c.

  10. The limiting value K = x-xo should be less than 6 mm if limiting value K of 6 mm has been reached. Recondition the contacts is necessary.

  11. Using the slow close device put the C.B in a completely open position.

  12. Remove slow close device W 37 and mount roof of motor wound spring mechanism 70.00 figure.

  13. Check the state of rated current carrying circuit without opening the arcing chamber we must measure the resistance of the rated current carrying circuit (ohm ? -test in the on- position as below:

  14. Get circuit breaker in on -position.

  15. Clean the surface of the tested points using metal brush.

  16. Connect contact - ohmmeter w 68 to the primary high voltage terminals of pole a (fig.4)

  17. Measure transition resistance directly at the connecting surfaces and record it.

  18. Connect contact - ohmmeter w68 to pole (b), then to pole (c), and conduct measurements, reference value = 40 micro ohm.

  19. If transition resistance at one of the three poles is bigger than the desired values conduct 5 to10 mechanical operations and repeat measurement

  20. If the transition resistance is still bigger than the desired value, conduct reconditioning.

  21. Measure transition resistance directly at high voltage terminal A and the corresponding user terminal K. (fig. 5) Reference value = 2 micro ohm.

  22. Measure transition resistance directly at user terminal and high voltage cable S (fig.6)

  23. Depending on the design of terminals K the transition resistance will be 2 micro ohm and 10 micro ohm If terminals K are designed for carrying the full breaker current rating, transition resistance must not exceed 5 micro ohm.

  24. If transition resistance at a measurement point exceeds the specified value, dismantle the terminal clean and grease the connection surface and the then connect it again.

  25. Make time test for the CB
