Introduction

This procedure details the major  maintenance Procedure P2 of the circuit breaker should be carried out every 12 year as indicated by Y12 in the above title block.

This inspection could be considered as a detailed inspection in addition to routine in inspection Y6.the inspection items in patrol inspection M1 and routine inspection Y6 should also be done during routine inspection Y12. The stationary arc contact (2100)should be checked during this inspection . If the wear of the stationary arc contact (2100) , the moving arc contact (2120)and nozzle (2130) should be replaced .

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • The circuit breaker must be isolated completely and grounded on both sides,

  •  Ensure that the earthing switch must be connected on both sides of the DS.

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area.

  • Isolate the DC voltage. (Switch off the control circuit and Insert closing and tripping preventive pins.)

  • Wear safety helmet, shoes, breathing mask, safety glasses.

  • Before using a blower to clean the control cabinet or when cleaning the insulators, wear breathing mask to protect your lung tissues from the dust particles.

  • Don't burn the cloth used in cleaning, collect it and send it to the dump.

Tools and Equipment

  • Safety helmet, shoes, breathing mask, safety glasses.

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks.

  • Tool set of  drivers, screw drivers, cloth

 

Work to be Carried Out

Circuit Breaker Proper Location at Exterior

  1. Check the tightness of anchor bolts.

  2. Check the tightness of ground terminal and other installation.

  3. Check the abnormal noise.

  4. Check the rust or damage of components.

Circuit Breaker Proper Location Inside Tank (Interrupter assembly)

  1. Cleaning inside enclosures, wipe away contamination and dust with clear cloths.

  2. Renewal of absorbent, refer to Fig .3 and PI-028

  3. Measure the contact resistance of main circuit.

  4. Inspect the stationary arc contact.

  5. Measure the stroke and contact wiping.

  6. Renewal of  stationary arc contact, moving arc and nozzle, in according with the result of the inspection of the stationary arc contact.

  7. Measure the insulation resistance of main circuit.

Gas System, Circuit Breaker proper

  1. Check the blind cover for gas feeding inlet

Gas System, Operating Mechanism Housing

  1. Check the gas pressure and temperature measurement within rated gas pressure.

  2. Check the setting of gas pressure gauge, refer to reference values

    1. 63 GA (ON) P1=0.45 + 0.03 MPa      (4.6 + 0.3   kgf/cm2 )

    2. 63 GL (ON) P2=0.40 + 0.03 MPa      (4.1 + 0.3   kgf/cm2 )

    3. P1-P2 = 0.05 + 0.025 MPa                 (0.5 + 0.25 kgf/cm2 )

  3. Check the gas stop valves in positions setting of gas pressure gauge, refer to reference values.

  4. Check gas leak, inspect disassembled parts refer to PI-013

Air System, Operating Mechanism Housing

  1. Check the air pressure.

  2. Check the setting of air pressure switches, refer to gas reference values

    1. 63 AR (ON) 1.40 + 0.03 MPa             (14.3 + 0.3 kgf/cm2 ) 

    2. 63 AA (ON) 1.23 + 0.03 MPa            (12.5 + 0.3 kgf/cm2 )

    3. 63 AL 1,2 (ON) 1.18 + 0.03 MPa       (12.0 + 0.3 kgf/cm2 )

  3. Check air stop valves in position.

  4. Check air leak

  5. Drain the water from air reservoir

Operating Mechanism

  1. Lubricate the mechanism, pins where disassembled, refer to fig 8, 9 and PI-006

  2. Check the leakage of chock absorber oil., visually, outside

  3. Check the looseness of retainer ring and nuts

  4. Replacement of some parts refer to item 11

Control System, Operating Mechanism Housing

  1. Check the auxiliary switch contacts, inspect the contact condition of operating segment.

  2. Check disconnection of space heaters.

  3. Check position of position indicator.

  4. Check registered number of operation counters.

  5. Check condensation and rust inside housing and water leak into housing

  6. Check the door packing, replace if aged.

  7. Check the tightness of bolts in housing and terminals.

  8. Lubricate the lever system, [Refer to PI-006].

Operating Test (Check of function)

  1. Manual opening and closing operation.

  2. Check of air reservoir capacity, from rated pressure to lockout pressure less than 150ms.

  3. Check the closing time and opening time, please refer to routine test report. Less than 20 ms.

Inspection and Replacement of Arc Contact and Nozzle

The inspection of the stationary arc contact of the interrupters is performed according to routine Inspection Y12 sequence shown in Fig .1(b) The inspection is made with the breaker contact in the open position.

Safety requires the close preventive pin to be instead, DC power disconnected and air exhausted from the mechanism.

The procedure of the inspection and replacement of the arc contacts,

and nozzle of the interrupter is as follows:

  1. Open the inspection cover (2010) by unscrewing the M20x100 bolts as shown in fig. 3.

  2. Before starting the inspection work inside the main enclosure (2000) the density of the oxygen must be carefully checked. The density of oxygen over 18 percent in the main enclosure (2000)is definitely required for a safe work.

  3. Unscrew the two socket bolts (2276) mounting the stationary contact holder (2105) by using the bar spanner (7013) and torque wrench (7020) as shown in fig .4. Reach in and take out the stationary contact holder (2105).

  4. Check the wear of the stationary arc contacts (2100). If the wear of the parts exceeds the service limits shown in fig. 2 replace with a new one. If the wear of the parts is slight, grind the surfaces of the parts by the fine sandpapers and apply very light film of micro-carbon grease, and reassemble the above contact in the reversed procedure and skip to 9 (b).

Replacement of the arc contacts and nozzle is as follows: 

  1. Set the inspection bar A(7011)on the nozzle (2130) as shown in fig .5(a), (b). 

  2. Unscrew the nozzle (2130) by using the inspection bar B (7012) and torque wrench (7020)and take out the nozzle(2130)by pulling the inspection bar A(7011).

  3. Set the inspection bar B(7012) on the moving arc contact (2120) as shown in fig .6(a), (b). 

  4. Unscrew the moving arc contact (2120)by using the inspection bar B(7012) and torque wrench (7020) and take out the moving arc contact (2120) by pulling the inspection bar A(7012).

  5. Clean the new stationary arc contact (2100) and new moving arc contact (2120) using solvent and apply a very light film of micro-carbon grease to the arc contacts.

  6. Clean the new nozzle (2130) using solvent ethyl alcohol.

  7. Reassemble the above contacts and nozzle in the reversed procedure of the above dismounting work with special care on the following points:

    1. When mounting the nozzle (2130), the nozzle (2130) can be twisted lightly and becomes a little heavy to turn. when it’s twisted further, it becomes light to turn again. After it has become light, the nozzle (2130) should be twisted only by the inspection bar A (7011) (not by a handle) to the final stop.

    2. Replace the absorbent (zeolite F9) (2021).

    3. Renew the O-ring (2500) and the gas sealer on the inspection cover (2010).

    4. Clean the inside of the main enclosure (2000) carefully.

    5. Close the inspection cover (2010) .the lightening torque for the M20 bolts (2015) on the main enclosure (2000) is 2,200kg cm.

    6. Evacuate the air in the main enclosure (2000). It should be evacuated down to1mmhg. After reaching 1mmhg, leave the vacuum pump working for at least 30 min. to minimize the humidity remaining inside the enclosures.

    7. Fill the enclosures (2000) with SF6 gas up to the rated gas pressure.

CAUTION:

An enclosure with a high concentration of SF6 can be hazardous causing asphyxiation from lake of oxygen. before entering enclosure, ventilate well and clean interior of powders. A continuous flow of dry, fresh air would improve personal conditions and help to keep the atmosphere air from entering the enclosure. Refer to the section on SF6 gas for precautions in SF6 environment.

Replacement of Absorbent and Rupture Disk

Replacement of absorbent

  1. The absorbent assembly (2020) is mounted on the inspection cover (2010) of the main enclosure (2000) as shown in Fig .3.The weight of the absorbent (zeolite F9)(2021) is approximately 3kg per phase. 

  2. The procedure of replacement of the absorbent (zeolite F9)(2021) is shown in Fig.3 (refer to PI-028). 

  3. After renewing the absorbent (zeolite F9)(2021), close the inspection cover (2010) within one hour and evacuate the air in the enclosure (2000) as soon as possible, as the absorbent (zeolite F9) (2021) might absorb too much moisture.

Replacement of Rupture Disk (option)

Rupture disk assembly (1030) (Fig. 7), when specified is provided on the inspection cover (2010). Rupture disk (1031) is to be replaced every 12 years . Be sure to replace the O-ring (1035) (1036), when the rupture disk is being replaced.

Replacement of the parts every 6 years   (refer to Fig. 8)

It is recommended to replace the valve seat (3128) and valve seal (3318) of the pneumatic operating mechanism every 6 years. The circuit breaker should be out of service before coming this work.

Replacement procedure of the parts is as follows:

  1. Close the circuit breaker.

  2. Switch off the control circuit.

  3. Exhaust the compressed air in the air reservoir (3090) from the drain valve.

  4. Insert the tripping preventing pin (3360) (refer to Fig. 8A).

  5. Remove the wiring of the trip coil assembly (3380).

  6. Remove the pin (3132) (refer to Fig .9(a))

  7. Remove the air piping (3353). (Refer to Fig. 9 b).

  8. Remove the bolts A (3398) and bolts B(3399) .

  9. Take out the control valve assembly (3300).

  10. Replace the valve seat (3128).

  11. Remove the nut (3326) and nylon nut (3324) from the driving rod (3352).

  12. Replace the valve seal (3318) together with the cylindrical valve (3322).

Replacement of the Control Valve Assembly     (Refer to Fig. 9)

It is recommended replace the control valve assembly of the pneumatic operating mechanism every 5,000 times of operation.

The circuit breaker should be out of service before commencing this work.

Replacement Procedure is as follows:

  1. Close the circuit breaker.

  2. Switch off the control circuit.

  3. Exhaust the compressed air in the air reservoir (3090)from the drain valve .

  4. Insert the tripping preventing pin (3360) (refer to Fig. 8A).

  5. Remove the wiring of the trip coil assembly (3380).

  6. Remove the pin (3132) (refer to Fig. 9a)

  7. Remove the air piping (3353). (refer to Fig. 9a).

  8. Remove the bolts A (3398)and bolts B(3399) .

  9. Take out the control valve assembly (3300)

  10. Remove the bolts D (3396) and the trip coil assembly (3380).

  11. Replace the control valve assembly (3300)
