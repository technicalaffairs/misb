Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff must carry the suitable ID safety category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required,

  • Wear the appropriate safety and Healthy equipment as safety shoes, helmet, breathing mask, safety glasses,

  • The circuit breaker should be switched off, fully isolated,

  • Earthing switches must be closed on both sides of the breaker,

  • Switch off the MCB for the DC of the CB.

Environmental Precautions

Before using a blower to clean the outside compartment of the circuit breaker from dust, wear your breathing mask to protect your lung tissues from the dust particulate.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete set of substation maintenance hand tools including screw driver set, torque wrench,
  • Emergency light, air blower,
  • 220 Vac supply, emergency light,
  • SF6 gas leakage detector,
  • Portable pressure gauge standard.
Work to be Carried out

Note: Although not always specified, all checks are performed on all three-breaker phases.

  1. Inspect visually all components of the circuit breaker for signs of overheating in the mechanism boxes, signs of electro static discharge, small oil leaks, weld cracks, foundation, fixation of circuit breaker.… etc.

  2. Check for AC and DC supply source.

  3. Check the hydraulic oil pressure, the pressure should be 330 bar at normal condition.

  4. Check the operation of the hydraulic oil pump by reducing the hydraulic oil pressure (bleed a quantity of oil back into the reservoir until the pump starts).

  5. Check and record the number of hydraulic pump operations as indicated on the operation counter and compare with the previous month.

  6. Clean all outside compartment of circuit breaker from dust, oil and other things using blower and rags.

  7. Retighten all bolts and earth connection parts if necessary.

  8. Clean all parts of the mechanism .

  9. Check oil level of main tanks for three phases.

  10. Check high-pressure pipes to ensure they are in their proper locations and sealing properly.

  11. Check the hydraulic oil reservoir tank to ensure the cap is sealing properly.

  12. Check oil leaks for all connections.

  13. Check and record the operation of the heater circuit of mechanism.

  14. Check door gaskets for all panels.

  15. Check the SF6 gas pressure this value should be 7 bar at 20 c.

  16. Using the leak detector check the entire CB switch for SF6 gas leak.
