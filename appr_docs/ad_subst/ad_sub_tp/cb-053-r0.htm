Introduction

    This document lists the inspection checks I1, which must be performed on monthly bases as indicated by M1 in the title block. This procedure will be done as a first level of inspection (Patrolling). 

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Wearing Safety helmet is required.

  • Wear a musk.

Tools and Equipment
  • Complete tool set, SF6 gas detector, Blower and Musk.

Work to be Carried Out

Gas Pressure

  1. Read and record the gas pressure , temperature and convert them on P/T  characteristic curve in Fig 1at the temperature for 20 0C .The rated pressure is 6 kg/cm2 at 20 0C.

  2. If SF6 Gas leak occur, supply SF6gas before the gas pressure drops to the alarm pressure. according to the following steps:-

Note: This supply dose not required any evacuation work Cutions:

  1. Be careful not to inhale SF6 gas when discharging the gas for the purpose of replacing air in the supply hose with 
    SF6 gas

  2. Be careful not to allow ingress of moisture into hose when connecting or disconnecting it.

  3. Connect stop valve (904) using Fig 2 and SF6 gas cylinder using the gas supply hose.in this case loosely tighten the connecting fitting (923) of cylinder and hose to such an extent that gas leaks.

  4. When installing gas C.B, open stop valve (904) Fig.2 and tighten stop valve of cylinder(923) while discharging the gas through the fitting part for connecting the cylinder and hose so as to replace the air in the hose with SF6 gas.

  5. When supplying the gas, open stop valve (904) Fig.2 for about 5 seconds to replace the air inside the hose with SF6 gas and then tighten connecting fitting (923).

  6. Open stop valve 921 of SF6 gas cylinder and supply SF6 gas .

  7. When pressure reaches the working pressure (6 kg/cm2 for 500 kV circuit brekers,5 kg/cm2 for 220 and 132 kV circuit breakers ) , close stop valve 921 ,904 and disconnect the supply hose .

  8. Mount protective cover to stop valve 904 after disconnecting the hose without fail.

Air Pressure

  1. Check whether or not the pressure gauge indicates the specified value (18 kg /cm2 for 500kv Circuit Breaker , 15k.g/cm2 for 220kv, 132kv Circuit Breaker) if not, check whether the discrepancy is due to a pressure gauge or pressure reducing valve disorder. 

  2. Check whether or not a leakage noise audible around the sealed piping joints.

Operation Counter

         Check and record the operation number.

Heaters

When humidity is high, the temperature drops sharply, or the temperature is low, turn on the heater for the control Box  and control cabinet.

Drainage

Open the drain valve of circuit breaker’s and air compressor receivers and drain once every two weeks.

Others:

Check for signs of abnormal noises in appearance.
