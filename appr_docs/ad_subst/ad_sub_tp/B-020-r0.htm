Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, qualified maintenance staff carry the suitable safety ID category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required,

  • Use standard personal safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, goggles, protective clothing, rubber gloves

    • ... etc.

  • The door of the battery room must be provided with NO Smoking signs .

  • The battery instructions must be displayed visibly on the wall..

  • Water source must be available in the battery room.

  • When working in the battery room bewares of parts under voltage.

    •

  • Disconnect DC switch of the battery from the main bus bar

Tools and Equipment

  • Suitable tool set.

  • Hydrometer.

  • Digital voltmeter.

  • Thermometer

  • Vaseline

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Keep the batteries clean and dry protect them against solid, liquid and gaseous contamination.

  2. Disconnect AC supply to Battery charger

  3. Inspect terminals of battery and clean any salt deposits with distilled water.

  4. Clean battery room floor with water.

  5. Clean top of the cells.

  6. Check for correct operation of battery room ventilation fan.

  7. Check acid level 10 mm above separators or between max / min levels.

  8. Write down the voltage temperature and acid density of pilot cells.

  9. Read over all voltage of bank and record on battery maintenance card.

  10. Inspect the outside of cells for cracks and leakage electrolyte.

  11. Check that the screws are well tightened 10 NM of cu poles.

  12. Protect the cell connectors and screws against corrosion using Vaseline.

  13. Measure the internal resistance of the battery by protection group.

  14. Connect the switch of the battery to auxiliary bus bar and discharge it for 10 hours until acid density reach’s 1.11g/cm3.

  15. Place battery bank on float charge for 10 hours until acid density is 1.24 g/cm3.

  16. When step No 13 is completed place selector switch to normal charge and return battery bank to regular service.

  17. Compare the reading you have taken to the readings recorded in previous years.
