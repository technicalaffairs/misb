Introduction

This procedure details the steps of Correction of Densimeter Switching Points to adjust the Circuit Breaker 132kv type FX11 GEC Alsthom after doing the maintenance a in MPIS document no. CB-043-r0 to ensure the equipment is in a good operating condition and avoid beakdowns.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category, 

    •

  • Safety fence rope with caution marks surrounding the work area,

  • Appropriate Personal protective equipment must be used,

    •

  • Circuit breaker must be fully isolated.

  • Earthling switch must be closed on both sides of the breaker to ensure it is properly grounded.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete tool set kit, drying agent

  • Grease type L-42, cleaning cloth,

  • Safety shoes, safety Helmet, Breathing mask, Safety glasses

Work to be carried out

  • The densimeter switching points SI and S2 are the points at which densimeter act where.

      • S1=alarm signal 1.
      S2=trip and lock signal 2.
       

  • If the value of S1 and which determined by the densimeter test devices W41 are different from the theoretically determined pressure value.
               S1act=S1th ± 0.2 bar.
                  S2act=S2th ± 0.2 bar.

  • If (S1-S2 = 0.3 ± 0.1 bar) or the pressure difference (S1-S2) within 0.2 to 0.4 then the CB is in a good condition.

  • But if the deviation exceeds the tolerance. Then we should correct the Densimeter switching points as below:

  1. Loosen bolts S and remove Densimeter cover (80.21 figure 1) bolts S and washers U are to be replaced.

  2. For signals S1 and S2 respectively, loosen nuts M1 and M2 then remove locking washers, figure 1b.

  3. Pressure is lowered by turning bolt 81.15 figure 1b where bolt 1 for correction of S1 and bolt 2 for correction of S2. Pressure is lowered by approx. 0.1bar per 45° turn (¼ turn) in clockwise direction and vice versa if the pressure is needed to be increased.

  4. After correction, tighten nuts M1 and M2 respectively and check pressure again with densimeter test device W 41.

  5. If the pressure does not to be corrected again apply locking varnish to setteing bolts 1, respectively 2.(81,15 figure 1b) including nuts.

  6. Insert new drying agent ( 81.05 figure 1c) from spare part set in cover (80.21 figure 1c)

  7. Clean sealing surfaces at Densimeter housing 80.20 and cover 80.21-fig (1) with clean cloth.

  8. Apply thin coating of grease type L-44 to O - ring (80.11 figure 1) from spare part set and place O - Rang on sealing surface of Densimeter housing 80.20 figure 1).

  9. Apply grease type L-42 to blots S and washers U from spare part set.

  10. Mount cover 80.21 and tighten with bolts S and washers U.
