Introduction

This document outlines the testing procedure P1, which performed every 2 years as indicated by Y2 in the above title block.

The capacitance and dissipation factor test set is used for shop and field  testing of high voltage electrical insulating systems at 50 /60 HZ by applying test  voltage up to 12kv the test set evaluates. The condition and quality of  electrical insulation materials and manufacturing  processes and reveals contamination. Fractures, punctures and other defects accompany the aging of  insulation.

The first draft initiated by: eng. Fahmy Fathy Abdel Khalek, tested at 05 May 2003 by eng. Hanan Mahmoud El Badrawy, revised by eng. Adel Abd Alkdar El Nagar: EDEPCo quality control, accepted by general director eng. Mahmoud El Saied Saker and approved by eng. Mostafa Alam Head Sector of Talka PS.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the maintenance staff should be qualified and  carry the suitable safety category .

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear safety helmet, and safety shoes.

  • The ground connection must be the first made and the last removed.

  • The transformer out of service.

Connect the test set to the desired transformer winding

Warning: During this test a voltage will be induced in all windings of the transformer .Treat all terminals as energized

  1. Make a short circuit on HV and LV transformer

  2. Set the UST / GST test mode selector switch to the required position for the particular test to be performed ( normally UST-3 ) .  

  3. Energize the test set by closing the main breaker switch.

  4. Close the external interlock switch.

  5. Set the HV ON / OFF switch to ON.

  6. Advance VOLTAGE CONTROL to desired test voltage.

  7. Read the value of transformer excitation current on the output millimeter.

Note : If the main breaker trip when advancing the VOLTAGE CONTROL, the output capabilities of the test set have been exceeded. Measure the transformer excitation current at a lower test voltage . The current capability is doubled by using the 5 KV voltage range .

  1. When the test has been completed, gradually reduce the test voltage to zero, open the external interlock switch or switch the HV ON / OFF switch OFF,  then the main breaker to off .

  2. Remove a short circuit on HV and LV transformer

IN CASE OF EMERGENCY

Power can be interrupted immediately by either switching the main breaker off or opening the external interlock switch.
