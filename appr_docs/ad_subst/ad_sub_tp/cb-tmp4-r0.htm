Introduction

This document outlines the major maintenance procedures P1, which must be performed every    years as indicated by Y  in the title block

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required

  • Wear your safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, ... etc

  • The work must be done in a cleaned area or in workshop.

  • The circuit breaker must be isolated and properly earthed at both sides, it should be in ON position.

  • Disconnect DC Voltage.

  • Close and open the breaker to make sure that the opening and closing spring are released before starting the work.

Environmental Recommendation

  • Don't throw any oil into the sewage because the oil prevents the sewage treatment  process.

  • Collect the oil that drained out of the poles into a barrel and return it back to the vendor to recycle it to save money.

  • Collect the oil used in washing the poles in another barrel and do not re-use it for filling the poles unless filtered, dried and its dielectric strength measured with an oil tester (not less than 40kv/2.5 mm).

  • Don't burn cloth used in cleaning but collect it and send it to the dump

Tools and Equipment

  • One set of metric open end box wrenches size 13mm to 24mm.

  • Adjustable spanner, one set of screw drivers, torque wrench, fine file, fine emery cloth, and wipers.

  • Complete set of gaskets for the circuit breaker.

  • One barrel of new oil (its dielectric strength not less than 40kv/2.5)

  • Dielectric strength oil tester.

  • Some of gasoline, lubricating oil, grease.

  • Clean pails for changing oil.

  • Tin cans to hold bolts, nuts, studs, washers, ... etc.

Work to be Carried Out

Inspection of Fixed Contact & Moving Contact

After the breaker is put out of service, without draining the oil out, the following operations are to be done on each pole. 

  1. Make sure that the breaker is open.

  2. Drain the oil out by removing the oil drain plug and allow the oil to drain out of pole into a container placed under each pole.

  3. Unscrew the metal cap to the insulating cylinder and raise it up with the upper connection.

  4. Lift the fixed contact, unscrew the screw of the insulating cover of the fixed contact

  5. Take off the insulating cylinder.

  6. Inspect the fixed contact elements and the contact springs.

  7. If the contact elements and the contact springs are in good condition and not deteriorated, clean them with new oil and clean cloth, then reassemble the fixed contact as follows:

  8. Place the insulating cylinder to the porter of contact.

  9. Fit the screw to fix insulating cylinder.

  10. Place the fixed contact into the pole in its previous place.

  11. Remove the 4 screws that fixing the insulating cylinder to the lower terminal part and unscrew the screw fixing the insulating cylinder to the circuit breaker back cover.

  12. Remove the 4 screws fixing the insulating cylinder to the oil pump.

  13. Lift the insulating cylinder and lay it gently on a clean board, take care not to damage the lower face.

  14. Unscrew the screw that fixing the arc chamber to the Aluminum cylinder base and pull out the arc chamber.

  15. If the arc chambers are not deteriorated, clean them with new oil and clean wipers, change it, pull out the sliding contact, then locate the new one with the sliding contact in the insulating cylinder.

  16. Unscrew the two screws that fixing the Aluminum cylinder to the lower terminal and pull out the Aluminum cylinder.

  17. Pull out the lower terminal with the sliding blades for the inspection of the moving contact rod.

  18. If the tip of the contact rod is badly deteriorated it must be replaced.

Oil Refilling

As the dealing experience with the type of circuit breakers we find that it can undergo four opening operations at short circuit replacing oil, but you have to replace oil after five short circuit as follows:

  1. Before starting drainage the oil out of the poles be sure that the breaker is in open position, then remove the outlet plug and allow all oil to drain out of poles into container replaced under each pole.

  2. Close the outlet plugs, open the filling plugs at the top of each pole and pour oil into each pole.

  3. After oil has reached the oil level indicators close and trip the breaker three times to wash the arc chambers, and contact.

  4. Remove the outlet plugs and allow all oil to drain out of the poles.

  5. Repeat the previous steps until you find the drainage oil to be cleaned.

  6. Refill the poles with oil of dielectric strength not less than 30kv/mm, during filling the breaker must be in open position and oil must be poured into each pole till the right mark on the oil level indicators.

  7. Wait a few minutes and if the oil level has lowered, fill up to the right mark.

Mechanical Checking

Note: All mechanical test operations must be carried out only when the 3 poles are filled with oil.

  1. Before putting the circuit breaker into service, it is necessary to carryout some hand closing and opening operations to make sure that the operating mechanism works satisfactorily and carryout some electrical closing and opening operations to make sure that connection are right and that the closing operating mechanism operates satisfactorily.

  2. For hand closing operation, insert the detachable crank into its place and manually rotate the main shaft in clockwise until the free rotation of the shaft, the state of springs is indicated by a colored indicator on the cover of the operating mechanism (Red spring loaded, white spring released), then turn in clockwise to direction of the mark "1". The state of the breaker is indicated by a colored indicator on the cover of the operating mechanism (Red spring closed, white spring released ).

  3. To trip the breaker turn the crank in anti clockwise to the direction of the mark "O".

  4. Check that the releases trip the breaker satisfactorily.

  5. Check that the auxiliary switch works satisfactorily.

  6. If closing springs are released, the motor when energized loads the springs and stops automatically as soon as springs are fully loaded.

  7. If the breaker works with the series releases, adjust the trip setting of releases.

  8. Clean the mechanism parts by means of a paint brush drenched with diesel oil and petrol, be sure that the breaker is open and springs are released before starting cleaning.

  9. Clean the metal parts by means of a clean dry duster and clean the insulating parts by means of another dry duster.

  10. After making sure that the operating mechanism is in good working condition and that the pole are filled with oil at the right level, the breaker can be put into service.

  11. Finally record all that has been done on the maintenance card of the circuit breaker.
