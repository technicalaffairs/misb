Introduction

This document outlines the major maintenance procedures P2, to be performed every 6 years Y6, or after 2000 switching cycle.

The preventive maintenance is a cycle of planned inspection, tests, overhauls and servicing activities designed to keep the network equipment in good operating condition and to avoid breakdowns.

The first draft initiated by eng. Gamal Sayed Soliman from Assuit 500kv Substation, ME zone.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category, 

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area,

  • CB must be fully isolated,

  • Earthing switch must be closed on both sides of CB to ensure it is properly grounded.

  • Interrupt the control circuit "DC" voltage.

  • Personal protective equipment must be used (safety shoes , Helmet , breathing mask, safety glasses) .

  • Close the Airing valves and exhaust the compressed air.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete tool set including screwdriver set torque wrench and Blower.

  • SF6 gas leak detector, 220 Vac supply

  • Portable pressure gauge standard.

  • Contact resistance tester, Timing equipment.

  • Dew-point equipment (moisture monitoring).

  • Gas quality testing instrument (air content)

 

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Make Y3 inspection for the CB according MPIS doc # CB-056-r0

Stationary contact   no. 118 Figure 4

  1. Visually inspect through handhole. When contact surface is found uneven, remove it from interrupter , and correct it with sand paper. When the length of the contact (L 1) is equal or less than 166mm , replace the contact with new one.

  2. Apply thin coat of grease to the contact. to do that follow the following steps:
    a) Remove the handhole cover no 152.
    b) Remove the contact by loosing 3 x M10 Bolts.
    c) Replace the contact with new one.

Arc contact no. 157 Figure 4

When surface is found uneven, polish the surface with a smooth – out file. If you need to change it, remove the contact by loosing 8xM8 socket Bolts, 4xM10 nuts and 4-M6x25 Bolts.(use socket wrenches 6,8,10)

Insulation nozzle and insulation cover  No 152,153 Figure 1

  1. Clean the interior and exterior surfaces. Using pure Alcohol and clean wipers. 

  2. Measure the nozzle inner diameter (D). Replace it with new one if diameter is 42mm or over. When the cover inner diameter (d) is 39 mm or over. replace it with a new one. to do it ,loosing 8xM8 socket bolts, 4xM10 nuts and 4-M6x25 bolts, measure the dimension of nozzle and cover.

Absorbent

  1. Replace with a new one just before handhole cover is closed, so that the absorbent may not absorb the moisture in the atmosphere.

  2. Don’t expose the absorbent in the atmosphere for more than 30 minutes, to do that detach the handhol cover then detach fixing bolts from absorbent case .

Oil dashpot  no. 800

  1. Replace the bush no C805 if the number of operation reaches 2500 switching operation ,also replace O ring.
  2. Replace with new oil type JIS-02320-2 by removing the pin, 4xM20 bolt

Control mechanism (400) Figure 2

  1. Check pins, links, levers, and operating rod and split pins.
  2. Check nuts and lock plates for possible looseness, Apply grease to pin.

Closing link mechanism   Figure 2

  1. Clean and apply a small amount of grease to the hook, pins and roller.

  2. Check nuts and bolts for looseness.

  3. Check the state of hook engagement under both close and open positions.

Electro-magnetic valve  See Figure 3

  1. Clean and apply turbine oil to the hook,

  2. Check nuts and bolts for looseness. When the armature is depressed with fingers, the hook is disengaged.

Auxiliary switch

  1. Check each part for normal tightening,

  2. Check the marks of open and closed state.

Air pressure gauge

  1. Use a standard pressure gauge or calibrating apparatus for calibration. Be careful not allow ingress of of dust into pressure gauge through the connection from which the pressure gauge is disconnected.

Pressures switch

  1. Check the lock pressure and release pressure on calibrated gauge.
  2. Setting values as follows:

    3. - Alarm and closing lock out ON 16.5 ± o.2 ,OFF 17.7 ± 0.2
    - Alarm and opening lock out ON 16.0 ± o.2 ,OFF 17.2 ± 0.2
    - Reclosing condition ON 17.6 ± o.2 ,OFF 17.95 ± 0.2

Trip-free contractor

    When contacts are solid or roughened considerably, correct with smooth cut file or very fine sandpaper.

Carryout the following:

  1. Measure the contact resistance according to MPIS document CB-004-r0. The contact resistance permissible value is 300 micro ohms. This value includes every thing in compartment.

  2. Measure the dew point of the insulating gas using moisture tester, it should be not greater than 15 PPM as indicated in MPIS document TD-SS-004-r0.

  3. Measure S02 the acid content of the insulting gas, using acidity tester, this value should not be > 0.5%(5-10ppmg)

  4. Check the content of air in the insulating gas, using air content tester, this value should be 2 % (value of SF6>98%).

  5. Check the complete circuit breaker for gas leaks, using SF6 leak detector.

  6. Check all bolts and fasteners to ensure they are properly tightened, including mechanism bolts.

  7. Check that all cable plugs are secure in their receptacles so proper contact will be made.

  8. Measure the gas pressure inside the CB, using a portable pressure gauge. The value should be 7 bar at 20 oC.

  9. Check the earting points of the circuit breaker, to ensure they are secure and have not been damaged.

  10. Check the operation of the pressure switch by draining off a small amount of gas, while observing the portable pressure gauge. The switch alarm contacts should operate at 6.2 bar and the blocking contact should operate at 6 bar.
