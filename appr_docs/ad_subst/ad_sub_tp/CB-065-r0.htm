Introduction

This documents out lines the check list I1, which must be performed one or two times a year as indicated by M6: Y1 at the above titled block.

Safety Precautions

  • Work order must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should the suitable safety category

  • Safety fence with cautions marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear safety helmet, shoes, torque wrenches

Tools and Equipments

  • Persenal protective equipment,

  • Complete set of tools, torque wrenches,

  • A400 emery paper, brush, solvent, polyurethane lacquer, primer

Work to be carried out 

 

General Condition of the Circuit Breaker

  1. Check visually of switchgear: visual aspect (corrosion, paint,...) if patches of corrosion are found begin reconditioning of the affected parts (matching surface finish and protection)

  2. Support: - Galvanized steel, painted or not, Painted aluminum alloy,

  3. Hot-dipped parts:
            - Thorough brushing of oxidised parts, - Degreasing with solvent,  - Application of a coat of zinc paint,

  4. Painted parts:  With light scratches:

            - Thorough degreasing with solvent, - Application of a coat of lacquer, using a brush

  5. With deep scratches or flaking:

            - Scour the smooth surface with A400 emery paper,

            - Thorough degreasing with solvent, - Application of a coat of primer, left to dry for 24 h,

            - Application of a coat of polyurethane lacquer using a brush

SF6 Gas Pressure

  1. For a circuit-breaker equipped with a "dial-type" SF6 gas densimeter, make sure that the intersection of the needle and the temperature circle is in the green zone.

  2. If the intersection is in either the yellow or red zone, for the same disposition as above, without any particular indication from the electrical contact densimeter, verify the concordance of different information sources to find the defective element and then replace it.

  3. Replacement of a dial densimeter is described in the section "REPLACEMENT OF ELEMENTS OR SUB-ASSEMBLIES"

  4. If observation confirms the information "topping up" (yellow zone) perform a topping up operation in accordance with "SF6 GAS TOPPING UP", or in the case of abnormally low voluminal mass (red zone) find the origin of the leak and repair it.

Air Vents and Heater Operation

  1. Clean air vents, free of dust, and unobstructed. If necessary, clean using a solvent.

  2. Make sure that the permanent resistors are working properly by checking that these give off heat and that there are no overheating marks (a zone notorious for this)

  3. For thermally controlled resistors, check that energizing and de-energizing takes place correctly at voltage supply terminals using a thermostat (recommended thermostat temperature: + 5 oC. If necessary, change resistors found to be defective.

General Tightness of Hydraulic Circuit

  1. Check that there are no remnants of oil (seeping) under hydraulic connections. Should this be necessary, re-tighten screws on the connections concerned to the recommended torque are given in paragraph of screw tightening torques   (figure 6).


    CAUTION: This type of intervention should always be performed with the circuit-breaker de-pressurized. before acting, bring the hydraulic circuit pressure to zero procedure described in section "pre-commissioning inspections and testes"

  2. Check for substantial leak (drip after drip) if detected during a maintenance visit, a repair operation should be programmed as quickly as possible in accordance with the lock-out possibilities of the apparatus. The following operations must be performed with the apparatus out of service, mechanically and electrically isolated and earthed:

  3. Bring the hydraulic circuit to zero pressure,

  4. Remove the faulty union,

  5. Clean the parts,

  6. Re-install union with a new seal

  7. Check for internal leak due to a number of pump starts above the permissible average (4 per 24 hours). Do not count starts following circuit-operations. Quickly get in touch with GEC ALS-THOM T&D S.A. Aftersales Service

Receiver Oil Level

  1. Check that the receiver oil level should always be between the limits given by the column level indicator. If oil lacks, top up using hydraulic oil complying with regulations AIR 3520/B or USA-MIL 05606.

CAUTION:

If the level observed very low (below limits) check tightness of all hydraulic circuits to detect eventual leaks and make inspections more frequently, until the leak is found and repaired.

Operation Counter

  1. Read indications given by the counter(s) and note these on the inspection card "maintenance visits". The number of circuit-breaker operations recorded influences future maintenance operations.

Pump-start Counter

  1. Read pump-start counter indications and note these on the service card "maintenance visits"

  2. If the average number of pump-starts between 2 maintenance visits is higher than 4 per 24 hours, do not count starting after circuit breaker operations- with no other signal but "pressure decrease", an internal leak exists in the hydraulic system. Quickly get in touch with GEC ALS-THOM T&D S.A. After sales Service
