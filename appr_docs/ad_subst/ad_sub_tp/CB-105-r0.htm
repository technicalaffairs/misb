Introduction

This document outlines the minor maintenance Procedure P1, to be performed every five years Y5 as indicated in the above title block  The first draft initiated by eng. Mohamed Sayed Esmaiel, tested by Hanafi Kamel Mohamed Saleh at 20 Oct 2002, El_Tibeen 500kv substation, Cairo zone

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,
  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area,
  • Wear safety helmet, shoes, breathing mask, safety glasses …
  • Circuit breaker must be fully isolated and earthing switch must be closed on both sides of the breaker to ensure it is properly grounded.
Tools and Equipment
  • Safety helmet, shoes, breathing mask, safety glasses, and tool set, (screw drivers, common set of keys)
  • Timing equipment,
  • Dew point equipment "moisture monitoring"
  • Acidity testing equipment,
  • Gas quality testing instrument "air content"
  • Contact resistance tester and portable pressure gauge standard,
  • Screw driver set, torque wrench,
  • Emergency light and 220kv supply,
  • 220volt ac supply and SF6 gas detector.
Work to be carried out

  1. Complete a visual inspection of all components of the circuit breaker, look for signs of electrostatic discharge, small oil leaks, weld cracks...etc

  2. Perform the annual inspection as in MPIS doc. no. CB-077-r0

  3. Read and record the accumulator serial number.

  4. Change the hydraulic oil and the oil filter.

  5. Test the pre–charging pressures in the accumulators and record the ambient temperature.

  6. Check and record the operation of the hydraulic oil pump by reducing the hydraulic oil pressure (bleed a quantity of oil back into the reservoir until the pump starts) and check the function of the pressure switch operation and record the stage of pump operation
    Pressure Switch Operation Pressure in Bars at 20 ºC
    Pump Start 325
    Pump off 342
    Closing Blocking 292
    Auto Trip 255

  7. Perform timing test  "trip time 18:25 ms, closing time 55:65 ms"

  8. Measure contact resistance ref. MPIS document no. CB-004-r0 "Permissible value 300 micro ohms. This value includes every thing compartment

  9. Measure the dew point using moisture tester of the insulating gas, the value should be not greater than 15 ppm ref. TD-SS-004-r0

  10. Measure SO2  using acidity tester, the value should be not greater than 0.5% (5-10 ppmg)

  11. Check the content of air in the insulating gas using air content tester, the value should be less than or equal than 2% (SF6 gas value greater than or equal 98%)

  12. Using SF6 leak detector check the complete circuit breaker for gas leaks

  13. Check all bolts and fasteners to ensure they are properly tightened, including mechanism bolts

  14. Check that all cable plugs are secure in their receptacles so proper contact will be made

  15. Using a portable pressure gauge to measure the gas pressure inside the circuit breaker. 
    The value should be 6 bar at 20 ºC 

  16. Check the earthing points of the circuit breaker, ensure they are secure and have not been damaged

  17. Check the operation of the pressure switch by draining off a small amount of gas, while observing the portable pressure gauge. The switch alarm contacts should operate at 5.2 bar and the trip contact should operate at 5 bar

  18. Ensure the pressure should 330 bar at normal condition.

  19. Check and record the number of hydraulic pump operation as indication on the operation counters (station operators do this daily).

  20. Check the operation of the heater circuit of the mechanism

  21. Check and record the pressure in the accumulator ensure that the initial pressure ~ 200 bar

  22. Check high pressure pipes to ensure they are in their proper locations and sealing properly

  23. Check the hydraulic oil reservoir tank to ensure the cap is sealed properly
