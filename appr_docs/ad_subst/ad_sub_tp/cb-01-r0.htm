Introduction

This document lists the hydraulic pressure testing PI, which must be performed every two years as indicated by Y2 in the title block

Safety Precautions
  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,
  • Safety fence with cautions marks surrounding the work area is required.
  • Breaker must be isolated,
  • Earthing cables applied,
  • Control power disconnected.

  • Personal protective equipment must be used (safety shoes, Helmet, breathing mask, safety glasses)

Tools and Equipment
  • Hydraulic test gauge, SF 6 Test gauge, buzzer, multi meter, micro-ohmmeter, blower, dew point equipment, acidity equipment, air content equipment and Timing equipment.
  • Hydraulic Oil and SF6 Gas if needed

Work to be Carried Out

General Work

  1. Externally Clean the Control Cabinet and breaker using a blower.

  2. Clean the inside of the control cabinet and inspect for oil leaks, loose terminals, equipment mountings and heater operation, also check door gasket condition.

  3. Generally inspect the breaker for any defects. this includes inspection of the grounding of the breaker, electrical connections, porcelain condition, deteriorating hydraulic hoses and electrical cables.

SF6 in interrupters

  1. Check each pole for the proper SF6 pressure. the normal pressure is 6 bar at 20 'C Record the actual pressure on the attached inspection form using a portable test gauge.

  2. For each pole check that:
    a) The alarm pressure relay closes at a pressure equal to 4.5 bar at 20 'C to alarm low SF6 pressure.
    b) The trip pressure relay closes at a pressure equal to 4.1 bar at 20 'C to block a close operation and trip the breaker. to perform these tests , isolate the pressure relay switch from the pole if possibly this will release the amount of SF6 discharged during testing adjust if necessary.

  3. Measure and record the dew point of SF6 gas in each pole using portable dew point tester, the dew point should not exceed 400 ppmv.

  4. Measure and record the air content of SF6 -as if the air content of gas is -greater than 2% the acceptable amount of air contained in SF6 gas is 2% using portable equipment also.

  5. Measure and record the acidity content of SF6 gas using acidity test equipment. the acidity level amount not exceed 5 ppm.

Electrical Operation and Checks 

  1. Close the breaker and test the contact resistance of the main breaker using the micro-ohmmeter and record the results on attached form acceptable value of contact resistance 40 micro-ohm.

  2. Measured and record timing switching operation for main breaker. the acceptable value of each case using a portable timing equipment.

    Close                           120 m second
    Trip                                40 in second
    Close trip                       70 in second     

  3. Do all the control function checks and record. the results

Hydraulic Pressure Testing

  1. Close all valves on hydraulic pressure tester (Fig. #1) and install to fitting A-E in control cabinet. Refer to Fig. # 3 for location of fitting AE.

  2. The plastic drain tube from the tester should be placed into reservoir D (Fig. #2). 
    Eensure all control voltage supplies have been de-energized    .

  3. Connect a buzzer or multi meter to the contacts of PS 0 1 of pressure switch J (Fig. # 3), slowly open valve K2 (Fig. #2) on the tester so that the pressure gauge reads full system pressure.

  4. Slightly open valve K3 (Fig. #2) The pressure gauge on the tester should indicate decrease. When the pressure reaches the cut-in point for the pump motor the PS 0 1 contacts will close. Record this pressure and adjust if necessary to 315+ 5 bars (See graph Fig. #4) Using electrical contact enable of Pressure-Switch (J) for POS 1.

  5. Close valve K3 (Fig. #2) and move the buzzer or multi meter connections to the contacts of PS02 of pressure switch (J).. (See Fig. # 3).

  6. Slightly open valve K3 (Fig. #2) again and observe the pressure gauge until the PS02 contacts make. Read and record this pressure. Adjust PS02 if necessary to 295 + 5 bars (See Fig. #4). Using electrical contact enable of Pressure-Switch (J) for POS2

  7. Close valve K3 (Fig. 42) and move the buzzer or multi meter to the contacts of PS03. Slightly open valve K3 (Fig. #2), again observe the pressure gauge until the PS03 contacts make. Read & record this pressure. Adjust PS03 if necessary to 200 (5 bar (see Fig. #4). Using electrical contact enable of Pressure-Switch (J) for POS3.

  8. Fully open valve K3 (Fig, #2) to allow the pressure to go to zero gauge. When no pressure is indicated on the gauge, close valve K3, but leave K2 open.

  9. The oil filter must be changed and cleaned.

  10. Install the handle of the hand pump and increase the pressure manually.

  11. The pressure will increase very quickly and first. When the pressure reaches a point where pumping does not increase the pressure, read & record the pressure from the pressure gauge.

    NOTE: The pressure just recorded is the pre-charge pressure of the lowest nitrogen accumulator 200 + 5 bars.
     

  12. Restore control power to the breaker, start the electric pump to charge the breaker to normal operation pressure (approx. 335 bars). Record this pressure and the time it takes for the pump motor to reach the above cut-off pressure. This must be under 3 minutes.

  13. Close valve K2 and remove the tester from the fitting in the control cabinet.

  14. Clean the pressure tester and wipe off any hydraulic fluid inside the control cabinet. Store the pressure tester and the plastic hose inside the wooden shipping box.

  15. Check for the proper hydraulic oil level and add if necessary. Use ES SO Univis J 13 if needed and check for the cause of the low oil.
