Introduction

This document outlines the second level of inspection I2, to be performed on an annually bases (Y1) or after 200/500/1000 working hours, which comes first  as indicated in the above title block. The first draft initiated by eng. Mostafa Abd_Alla Beni Suef East 220kv Substation and published at 27 Feb 2002

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area,

  • Wear safety equipment.

  • Ensure that the CB isolated completely and grounded on both sides

Tools and Equipment

  • Safety equipment "belt, shoes,...,etc".

  • Ammeter clamp, recorder multimeter, air pressure reference gauge

  • One set of three complete closing and opening valves, as shown in figure 4, with seals 211

  • One set of Allen keys one set of screwdrivers torque wrench clean rags, solvent (e.g. clorotene)

  • Anderol 495 lubricating oil (approx. 0.4 L for compressor).

  • Gasket for plugs 171, 173 fig 3, Seals for container cylinder193 fig 7, seals for condensate drain filter plug 

  • Two sets of 5.5 mm to 36mm spanners, one set of Allen keys, clean rags.

  • Chronometer, tool Kit (head gasket, 2 compression ring, cylinder gasket, cap gasket, oil scraper ring, complete valve block, air pressure reference gauge)

  • One 20 L gas cylinder for topping up.

 

 

Works to be Carried Out

The Compressor Off    (Figure 1)

  1. Carryout the steps stated in doc no. CB-088-r0 the monthly inspection.

  2. Check for air leaks,

  3. Check operation of the safety valve,

  4. Check setting of the pressure gauge and pressure switch,

  5. Check operation of the anticondensate resistance,

  6. Clean the condensate drain container and filter,

  7. Clean the suction filter,

  8. Change the lubricating oil,

  9. Dismantle and clean the suction and compression valves

  10. Check the system loading time. If the loading time is excessively long, i.e. 15% above the first installation time: dismantle the compressor cylinder head and body, replace the seals and the suction and compression valve block, for more details:

    1. Disconnect the delivery pipe and suction filter from the compressor head,

    2. Remove the 4 cap fastening screws and remove the cap and valve block

    3. Remove the suction valve membrane

    4. Remove the 4 cylinder body fastening nuts and remove the body.

    5. Replace the compression and oil scraper rings.

    6. Reassemble and replace the cylinder gasket, head and cap gasket, complete valve block

    7. Tighten nuts and screws to the torques given in figure 2  

The Circuit Breaker

Operating Mechanism

  1. The circuit breaker in Off position,

  2. Check the state and tightening of the accessible screws and nuts.

  3. Check operation of the anticondensate resistances,

  4. Inspect, clean and lubricate the control connecting rod assemblies of the position indicator and auxiliary commutators,

  5. Compare closing and opening operating times with those recorded during initial testing,

  6. If operating times are found to be outside the limits or the discrepancy between the phases exceeds 3 ms during opening and 5 ms during closing, the set of three complete closing and/or opening valves must be replaced,

  7. Check the operation of the auxiliary contacts, from the control cabinet terminal board in open and closed position, check the continuity of the auxiliary circuits with respect to wiring diagram using circuit testers.

  8. Check the air consumption, for more details: Starting from motor compressor cut-out pressure, check that when running the rated cycle, no signals or locks intervene due to low pressure.

Gas Control Circuit

  1. Check that the auxiliary cabling terminals are correctly tightened.

  2. Check the correct operation of the auxiliary relays.

  3. Check the operation of the anticondensate resistance at the control cabinet, using an ammeter clamp, check the resistance absorb current.

Extinction Chamber

  1. Check the circuit breaker in off position.

  2. Read and record the pressure and density meter and record the pressure of the SF6 gas.

  3. Check the tightening of the foundation bolts, of pole supporting structures using the torque wrench.

  4. Check the state and correct tightening of all the accessible nuts and screws including connections to the areial lines.

  5. Check the External finishes, if the finishes is lacking or damaged, clean and treat the damaged points, and touch up the painted surfaces with polyurethane-based paint and the hot galvanized surfaces with cold zinc.

  6. Measure the humidity content in the SF6 gas, should not exceed 250 ppm in the volume

  7. Clean the insulators (frequency of this operation must also take account of ambient conditions).
