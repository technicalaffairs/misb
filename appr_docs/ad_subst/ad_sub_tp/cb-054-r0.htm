Introduction

This document lists the Minor Maintenance P1, which must be performed each three years as indicated by Y3 in the title block or 500 switching operation which come first

 Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued.

  • CB must be fully isolated.

  • Throughly exhaust comprised air from the C.B air receiver.

  • Earthing switch must be closed on both sides of CB to ensure it is properly grounded.

  • Personal protective equipment must be used (safety shoes, Helmet, breathing mask, safety glasses)

  • Maintenance staff should be well trained and qualified or directly supervised by some one who is.

  • Caution marks must be hanged on a work place, safety rope around a work place.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete tool set including screw driver set torque wrench. and Blower.

  • SF6 gas leak detector.

  • 220 Vac source.

  • Portable pressure gauge standard.

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Inspect visually all parts of circuit breaker's operating mechanism.

  2. Using SF6 gas leak detector to detect the leaks for piping joints and sealed parts .

Air piping

  1. Check for any Air leakage and retighten union nuts and bolts.

  2. Calibrate the pressure gauge by using standard gauge .

 Control box

  1. Electro-magnetic valve See Fig. 1, clean and apply turbine oil to the hook, Check nuts and bolts for looseness. When the armature is depressed with fingers, the hook is disengaged.

  2. Closing latch see Fig. 2, clean and apply a small amount of grease pins, an roller. Check nuts and bolts for looseness.

  3. Check each part of auxiliary switch  for normal tightening and check the marks of open and closed state.

Control cabinet

  1. Calibrate the air pressure gauge by useing a standard pressure gauge or calibrating apparatus .

  2. Pressures switch:  check the lock pressure and release pressure on calibrated gauge.

  3. Trip-free contactor: when contacts are solid or roughened considerably, correct with smooth cut file or very fine sandpaper.

Oil dashpot

Remove the plug, check the oil level, oil level should be as high as the plug level.

Operating rod

  1. Check nuts and lock plates for looseness, check split pins and joint pins.

  2. Check for all Terminals and tight it if needed.

  3. Record the maintenance has be done and all parts which have been changed in maintenance card.
