Introduction

This document lists the equipment inspection steps I2 for GIS Circuit Breaker 66Kv type 8DN8 EGK and GIS Circuit Breaker 220kv type 8DP3 Siemens, which must be performed on an annual bases as indicated by Y1 in the title block .

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued

  • Maintenance staff should be well trained and qualified or directly supervised by some one who is.

  • Caution marks must be hanged on the work place, safety rope around the work place.

  • Personal protective equipment must be used (safety shoes, Helmet, breathing mask, safety glasses)

  • CB must be fully isolated, and Control Power disconnected.

  • Grounding switch must be closed on both sides of CB to ensure it is properly grounded.

Tools and Equipment

  • Vaccum cleaner up to 2000 watt

  • Tourqe Wrench, Pressure Gauge.

  • Hydralic oil, Compete Set of Tools.

  • Gas detector, Emergency Light.

  • Dew point, Acidity and Air Content Equipment.

  • Paint and paint bruhes.

Work to be Carried Out

  1.  Record number of make-break operation (as per operation counter or requested by system management).

  2. Carry out visual check.

  3. Record any special events (as requested by system management).

  4. Check hydraulic operating system for external leakage (pipes, valves, etc.).

  5. Check hydraulic operating system for internal leakage in on and off positions (pressure loss per hour at nominal pressure and with oil pump set switched off).

  6. Check gas filling of main module using a precision pressure gauge.

  7. Top with gas, if necessary.

  8. Trace and eliminate leak, if necessary.

  9. Check readings of pressure gauges 45.129 (1-1510) for gas and 45.209 (1-1510) for pressurized oil at nominal pressure against readings of precision pressure gauge.

  10. Check gas density monitor 45.135 (1-1510) using the service unit of the SF6 filling device A2 {"Loss of SF6 "and "general lockout (SF6)"}.

  11. Gradually reduce oil pressure in hydraulic system to zero. Checking the operation of pressure monitors for lockouts and signals. 
        -No.5 "General lockout (oil)"
        -No.6"Lockout (oil)"
        -No7"Oil delivery faulted".

  12. Check oil level in oil tank 5.27 (1-0410) (hydraulic operating system pressure less. Off state).

  13. In this state check pipe couplings of hydraulic operating system retighten as necessary using a torque wrench (see"General installation instructions").

  14. Vent hydraulic operating system including oil pump.

  15. Start oil pump set, 5.137.321, + 5.137.339, (1-1210) and check priming pressure of nitrogen filling in hydraulic storage cylinder 5.33 (1-0410) using a precision gauge.

  16. If necessary replenisch nitrogen filling in hydraulic storage cylinder 5.33 (1-0410).

  17. With rising and felling oil pressure check pressure monitors for correct operation (lockouts and signals): 
        -No.4"General lockout (N2)"
        - No.5"General lockout (oil)" 
        - No.6"Lockout (oil)"
        - No.7 "Oil delivery faulted"

  18. Check safety valve 45.249.11 (1-1510) (opening and closing pressure).

  19. Replace position switch S2, part  No 5.137 .303.9 (1-1210), of volume switch 5.137.303 on electric motor 5.137.321 (1-12100of the oil pump set.

  20. Lightly oil sliding surfaces of mechanism parts between main valve 137.141 (1-1210) and auxiliary switch 137.369 (1-1210), and also On.Off indicator 137.429 (1-1210). Check all mechanism parts.(Do not oil auxiliary switch.).

  21. Check the closing magnets 137.51, and tripping magnets 137.69. (1-1210) of the control valve blocks: terminals, condition, aluminum ring (setscrew), magnets firmly tightened.

  22. Check effect of electric heaters, e.g. 45.199 (1-1510), without carrying out measurement.

  23. Check to see that all connections to terminals and contractors, etc .are tight.

  24. Test for correct functioning : 
    Operate breaker,check signals and lockouts, anti-pumping feature, On-Off indicators,etc. 
    (This test normally extends to the control room console)

  25. Measure pressure of gas filling (final check).

  26. Check all remade joints (covers and flanges) for any signs of leakage. (Final check).

  27. Check oil level in tank (final check).

  28. Check hydraulic operating system for external leakage (final check).

  29. Secure pressure release valve 45.249.17 (1-1510) (final check).

  30. Measure moisture content of the gas (final check).

  31. Measure air content of the gas (final check).

  32. Paint finish and anti-corrosion protection to be touched up by customer's personnel.

    The following alarms occurs at 

    - Laking oil pressure alarm at 303 bar.

    - Blocking of  ON and OFF alarm at 273 bar. 

    - Nitrogen (N2) leakage alarm at 345 bar.

    - Critical oil pressure alarm 253 bar.
