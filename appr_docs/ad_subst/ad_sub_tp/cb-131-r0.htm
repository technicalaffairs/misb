Introduction

This document outlines the major maintenance procedures P2, to be performed four years as indicated by Y4 in the title block  

The first draft initiated by eng. Wagdy Mekhaiel Barsom and tested at 06 May 2003 at Malawy substation 132kv, ME zone. 

Safety Precautions

  • A work Permit must be issued.

  • Switch off the circuit breaker and be sure that the pressure gauge pointer reads 20 atm.

  • CB must be isolated and proper earthing at the two sides of the C.B is required.

  • Isolate control circuit (d.c voltage).

  • Safety fence with cautions marks around the work area is required.

  • Use only fiberglass ladders.

  • Wearing safety helmet is required.

  • Close the air stop cock in the distributing cabinet.

  • Open the drainage valve (13 fig 1) to drain the air out of the tank and be sure That the C.B is free of pressure

  • Personnel carrying out the maintenance should be trained and qualified or directly supervised by some one who is

    •

Tools and Equipment

  • One set of metric open end box wrenches, one set of socket uvenches, one set of screwdrivers and torque wrench.

  • Tin cans to hold bolts, wachers .........etc.

  • Pure alchol - wippers - fine file and fine emery paper .

  • Previous periodical maintenance report including ale previous tests and manufacture test certificates.

  • Complete set of gaskets.

 

Work to be Carried Out 

Distributing cabinet

  1. Dismantle the reducing valve (A fig 22) use sp 12.

  2. Dismantle pneumatic valve block (b fig 22) to main tain relief valve (No1) - filter (No.2) - non - return valve (3) valves (4) - stop cock (5) - use sp 14, 17.

  3. Dismantle push - button device (4 fig 22).use sp 12 and screwdriver.

  4. Clean all parts with pure alcohol and reassemble all parts once again

Control  cabinets for the 3 poles (fig 12)

  1. Dismantle the opening valve block (1) and closing valve black (8) use sp 12.14.17

  2. Dismantle tripping and closing electromagnet (2,5) to clean and main tain it  them.

  3. Dismantle the ayxilairy switch (3) and auxilairy switch actuator (4) and fig 16 in details to clean and maintain (use sp.12,14 )

  4. Clean all parts with pure alcohol and reassemble all parts once again.

  5. Clean the electrical terminals block (10) with flow of air

  6. Check the safety valve (9)

Arc extinction chamber blast valves, Figure 10 for the 3 pales

  1. Dismantle the casing (1)

  2. Dismantle the air cleaners (5)

  3. Dismantle the valve (2) ,stem (3) , piston (4).

  4. Clean all parts with pure alcohol.

  5. Change the 3 gaskets.

  6. Reassemble all parts once again

Isolator blast valves (fig (11) for the 3 poles

  1. Dismantle the cover (1)

  2. Clean and maintain piston (2), stem (3), exhaust valve (5), valve (4) and non - return valve (6).

  3. Change the 3 gaskets.

Arcing chamber ( 4 fig. 1 and fig 4, 5 in details )

  1. Disconnect the three joints between the arcing chamber 4 fig1 and the shunt resistance 5 fig1.

  2. Remove the protective cap at the end of arcing chamber (use screw drive).

  3. With a proper rob hang the arcing chamber with proper crane.

  4. Release the nut of the studs that connecting the lower flange of the arcing chamber to the upper flange of the supporting insulator.

  5. Raise up the arcing chamber and cover the hole of the supporting insulator with sheet of plastic ,then lower the arcing chamber in the wooden movable workshop cabinet through its movable ceiling to make maintenance or take it the work shop mtc make maintenance with special attention during raising the arcing chamber up take care not to break the porcline of the shunt resistance .

  6. Dismantle the parts of the first arcing chamber the exhaust valve (1 fig 5), stationary contact (2), moving contact mechanism (3), insulator (4), and the middle flange (5).

  7. Pull out the moving contact exactly parallel to The chamber center line

  8. Dismantle the moving contact cover 1 fig 4, casing (2), sliding contact (3), gasket (4), spring (5), ring (6), piston ring (7) , contact (8), dumper (9), ring (10), spring (11), bush ring (12) and locking screw (13) .

  9. Inspect moving contact surface
             a)      if its in a good condition it means that the fixed contact is also in a good condition.
                 b)  if it its surface is slightly burnt clean it with a very fine emery cloth and taking care
                 (do not remove the siliver coating) .
                 c) If it is burnt with a pantures you have to chang it.
             d) If 40% of surface silver coating is removed you have to change it also.

  10. Clean the internal insulator surface of the arcing chamber with pure Alcohol.

  11. Reassemble the hole set of moving contact mechanism once again and replace it in its previous  position after clining it with pure alcohol and greasing with very fine film of medical vasline .

  12. Using the crane locate the complete arcing chamber in its previous position on the support insulator after removing the sheet of plastic that has been covered the hole of supporting insulator

  13. Tighten the nut connecting the two flanges of the arcing chamber and supporting insulator .with torque wrench with a torque of 1.8 kg.m

  14. Repeat all steps from 6 to 13 for the other to arcing chamber.

  15. Reassambel the two arcing chamber and put them in its position on the supporting isalator.

Isalator Chamber (8 fig. 1) and fig. 6, 7 in details

  1. Disconnect the three joints between the isolator chamber and the capacitor (7 fig 1).

  2. With a propper robe hang the isolator with the crane

  3. Release the nuts of the studs that connecting the lower flange of the insulator chamber and the upper flange of its supporting insulator

  4. Raise up the isolator chamber and cover the hole of supporting insulator with a sheet of plastic to prevent the ingression of dust or any impurities then lower the isolator chamber in a wooden work shop cabinte through its movable sealling to make maintenancce or take it to the workshop for maintenance .during rasing up the arcing Isolator chamber take care not to break the porcelain of the capacitor.

  5. Disassemble the complete stationary contact mechanism (fig 8) and dismantle all its parts stop washer (1) adjusting washer (2), bush (3) stopper ring (4), screw (5), dashpot (6), adjusting washer (7), spring hosing (9), spring (10) , and contact (11).

  6. Inspect moving contact surface
           a)      If its in a good condition it means that the fixed contact is also in a good condition.

           b)  If it its surface is slightly burnt clean it with a very fine emery cloth and take care (don't remove the siliver coating).

       c) If it is burnt with a pantures you have to chang it.

       d) If 40% of surface silver coating is removed you have to change it also.

  7. Clean the internal  insulator surface of the isolator chamber with pure Alcohol.

  8. Reassemble the hole set of moving contact mechanism once again and replace it in its previous  position after clining it with pure alchol and greasing with very fine film of medical vasline

  9. Using the crane locate the complete isolator chamber in its previous position on the support insulator after removing the sheet of plastic that has been covered the hole of supporting insulator .

  10. Tighten the nut connecting the two flanges of the isolator chamber and supporting insulator with torque wrench, with a torque of 1.8 kg.m

  11. Repeat all steps from 5 to 10 for the other isolator chamber.

  12. Reassambel the two isolator chamber and put them in its position on the supporting isalator

  13. Tighten all nuts of supporting isolator chambers, O ring chambers by torque wrench with a torque of 1.8 kg.m.

Complete the following steps:

  1. Check the accuracy of the pressure gauages.

  2. Close the drainage valve and recharge the CB with air.

  3. Check for any air leakage.

  4. Close and trip the CB two times and be sure that the pressure gauge reads 20 ata when the CB is open and zevo at a when the CB is closed.

  5. Check the pressure drop
                Pressure drop            Self closing                Good contact
       from     2.9 atm.                     7    atm.                      4     atm.
                   3.1 atm.                     9    atm.                      6     atm.

  6. Perform micro ohm and timing tests, record the results, compare it with previous test results and report investigate significant changes.

  7. Record all parts that has been changed and test results on maintenance card.

  8. Disconnect the joints between the shunting resis tance (5 fig 1) and arcing chamber (4 fig 1)
    to measure the resistance of shunting resistance for the 3 poles it must be (120 + 5 ohm).

  9. Disconnect the joints between isolator (8 fig 1) and capacitors (7 fig 1) to measure by megger

  10. Before putting the CB in service clean the CB control cabiner with flow of air then check and tighten all ter minals of the terminal block (10 fig 12)           

  11. Clean and check all auxiliary contacts.

  12. Check colosing and trip coil and there contacts.

  13. Clean and check the air pressure manometer contacts and adjust the air pressure settings as fallowing:
                        16 atm           for        colosing
                        19 atm           for        auto reclosing

  14. Clean and check the auxiliary relays contacts in the CB cabinets.

  15. Check the position indication of the CB

  16. Before putting the CB in service, clean all its metal parts, clean working area, and don’t forget any wipers on the CB

  17. Before putting the CB in service, make a solograph test.

  18. Record the maintenance which has be done and all parts which have been changed  in maintenance card.

Resistance Test

  1. Disconnect the parallel joints between the shunting resistance and arc chamber

  2. Measure each resistance, 120 ohm ± 4 ohm

  3. Reconnect the parallel joints.

Condensers Test

  1. Disconnect the parallel joints with isolator chamber

  2. Measure the condenser insulation using megger 2.5kv

  3. Measure the tan delta for condenser (0.5 % at 20 : 30 ºC) w.r.t. temperature factor

  4. Reconnect the parallel  joints.

Contact Resistance Test

  1. Measure the contact resistance for each phase alone (290 micro ohm) if the value increased, you should measure each insulator or arc chamber alone to determine which chamber has a great value and change the contacts.

  2. The value of each contact 27 micro ohm for each chamber alone.

  3. The value of contact resistance for connectors between isolator and arc equal 130 micro ohm
