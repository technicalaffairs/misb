Introduction

This document outlines the battery inspection I1, which must be performed for every six months as indicated by M6 in the above title block.

This patent concerns the maintenance procedures of indoor acidic battery 220Vd.c, of capacity 468 A/H, and its impedance test by battery impedance test set type EBITE - AVO

The first draft initiated by eng. Maysa Ahmady, tested at 27 Jan 2003 by eng. Iman Rizk El–Ashry, reviewed by quality control engineer eng. Adel  Abdel Kader EL-Nagar of EDEPCo, accepted by general director eng. Mahmoud Sakr, and approved by eng. Mustafa Alaam Talkha Power Station Sector Head.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, qualified maintenance staff carry the suitable safety ID category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required,

  • Use standard personal safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, goggles, protective clothing, rubber gloves

    • ... etc.

  • No smoking is permitted in battery room

  • Use the proper insulated tools and don’t place any tools on the top of battery cells ( S.C. may occur )

  • Water source should be available in battery room.

  • Be aware of parts under voltage.

 

Tools and Equipment

  • Hydrometer

  • Digital voltmeter, Thermometer

  • Vasline.

  • EBITE: Enhanced battery impedance test equipment (tester)

 

 

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Check the room ventilation.

  2. Disconnect the AC supply to battery charger.

  3. Inspect the outside of cells for cracks and leakage of electrolyte.

  4. If you found a detective cell, replace with a new one. (follow the recommended procedure)

  5. Inspect electrolyte level, record density for each cell  and add distilled water if level is below normal

  6. Inspect inter-cell connectors and terminals of cells and clean any salt deposits with distilled water

  7. Check terminal connectors for tightness.

  8. Lubricate terminals of cells with a thin film of vaseline.

  9. Clean top of cells then read voltage per cell and record on battery maintenance card.

  10. Measure and record the specific gravity of the   electrolyte in each cell.

  11. Cheek and record the temperature of the electrolyte in each cell.

  12. Read and record overall battery voltage immediately after A.C power is switched off.

  13. Connect the AC power again to battery charger and connect a load (floating condition).

  14. Use the EBITE tester to measure the internal impedance of the battery group.

  15. Compare the EBITE results with the previous ones to check the performance of each column.

  16. Clean battery room with water and check the exhaust fan

  17. Print the test sheet from tester figure 1

 

Pro – Maintenance Procedures

  1. Dismantle the tester and other equipments.

  2. Leave the battery room from any matters ( i.e. cloths )

  3. Close the battery room tightly.

  4. Return the work permission.

 

N.B.  These procedures are the same for testing batteries in the location either steam or gas as well as GIS sectors although the battery technical data is different for each sector
