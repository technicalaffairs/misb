Introduction

    This procedure details the items, which are checked on an annual base. They include testing and routine Inspection. In addition to the procedures listed, it is expected the checks, which are done more frequently, would also be completed at this time.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, qualified maintenance staff carry the suitable safety ID category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required,

  • Wear standard personal safety and healthy equipment as helmet, safety shoes, rubber gloves, ... etc.

  • Before doing maintenance, inspection or service work on the unit be sure that the starting switch is in OFF position or the wiring disconnect from the line to prevent the unit from accidentally starting.

  • Personnel carrying out the maintenance should be trained and qualified or directly supervised by someone who is.

Environmental Recommendations

  • Fix any oil leaks to prevent pollution and save money of wasted oil. Oil leaks to the ground have very bad impacts on the environment, mainly on soil and ground water and eventually on the eco-system. "It takes only I liter of Hydrocarbon spilled in an aquifer to render 1,000,000 liters of potable - water use less for human consumption"

  • Don't throw any oil into the sewage because the oil prevents the sewage treatment process.

  • Collect the oil that drained out of the poles into a barrel and return it back to the vendor to recycle it to save money.

  • Collect the oil used in washing the poles in another barrel and do not re-use it for filling the poles unless filtered, dried and its dielectric strength measured with an oil tester (not less than 40 kV / 2.5 mm).

  • Don't burn cloth used in cleaning but collect it and send it to the dump.

Tools and Equipment

  • One set of open ends spanners - One set of socket wrenches - One set of screwdrivers.

  • Fine grinding paste - Fine emery cloth - wipers.

  • Tin cans to hold bolts, nuts, studs, washers, ...etc

  • Some benzene for cleaning - machine, oil grease.

  • Complete set of the compressor gaskets.

Work to be Carried Out

Air intake Filter        Figure 6

  1. Unscrew the nut of air cleaner cover and remove the cover (No 1).

  2. Remove the filtering element (No 2) and clean it with benzene and dry it with flow of air.

  3. Raise the air intake circular channel (No3), drain the oil out and clean with benzene.

  4. Check the air cleaner gasket (No5) and change it if needed.

  5. Unscrew the oil drain plug (34 fig1) to drain all oil out.

The Cooler Unit

  1. Disconnect all air pipes connections between stages and the air cooler.

  2. Unscrew the eight nuts connecting the base of cooler unite (fig 7).

  3. Withdraw the cooler unit out and clean its tubes with flow of sodium hydroxide solution using closed circuit and pump, then dry it with flow of air.

First Stage Cylinder Head and Valve      Figure 1 & 3

  1. Unscrew the six nuts of the cylinder cover studs and raise the cover (4 fig. 1) up.

  2. Remove the valve boards (3 fig.1) and remove the two gaskets (8 fig. 1).

  3. Unscrew the two fitted bolts (3 fig 3) and raise the upper valve board (2 fig.3) up.

  4. Remove the suction plates (6 fig 3) then remove the delivery plates (8 fig.3).

  5. Clean all valve parts with benzene.

  6. Make smoothing to the upper and lower board surfaces of the valve using very fine grinding paste.

  7. Clean the upper and lower boards surfaces of the valve.

  8. Put the suction and delivery plates in their places and check its clearances #9; #9; (must be not more than 0.2 mm) OR replace if needed.

  9. Collect the valve and tighten the two fitted bolts and check the movement of the plates.

Second Stage Cylinder Head and Valve        Figure 1 & 3

  1. Unscrew the six nuts of the cylinder cover studs and raise the cover (22 fig 1) up

  2. Remove the valve boards (24 fig1) and remove the two gaskets (19 fig 1).

  3. Unscrew the two fitted bolts (3 fig 3) and raise the upper valve board (2 fig 3) up.

  4. Repeat the previous steps from item 4.4 to item 4.9 (the second stage and the first stage is the same)

Third Stage Cylinder Head and Valve         Figure 1 & 4

  1. Unscrew the eight nuts of the cylinder cover studs and raise the third stage cylinder cover (10 fig 1).

  2. Remove the copper sealing ring (No11fig 1).

  3. Raise the third combined valve (fig 4).

  4. Unscrew the nut (8fig4) and remove the body, upper portion (1fig 4).

  5. Remove the circular suction plate (3fig 4) and delivery circular plate (5fig 4).

  6. Remove the suction valve spring (2fig 4) and delivery valve spring (6fig 4).

  7. Clean all parts of the valve and clean all suction and delivery plates holes.

  8. Make smoothing to the suction and delivery circular. Plates with grinding paste.

  9. Clean all parts with benzene and collect the valve.

Crank Shaft and Connecting Rod       Figure 1

  1. Unscrew the screws of the crank case hatch cover ( 35 fig 1 )

  2. Remove the lower block of the connecting rod of the three stages.

  3. Check the bearing and the crankshaft. And if they are not in good condition change this routine maintenance to overhand maintenance to change and maintain all parts.

Tension Adjusting Roller (59 Figure 2) of the Belt

  1. Release the grease cup (65).

  2. Release the tension adjusting roller screw and remove the two belts.

  3. Remove the axial shaft of the tension-adjusting roller.

  4. Remove the two ball bearings from the shaft.

  5. Clean and check them and change if needed.

  6. Collect the tension-adjusting roller and grease the ball bearings.

Note: In some cases the fan don’t use the belt for working but using special motor (3 Phase –2.2KW- 220/380V–1450 rpm) and its maintenance as follows:

  1. Check the tightness of the motor base bolts and the fan .

  2. Check the fan motor-ball bearings.

  3. Check the tightness of the cover of the motor.

The Fan       Figure 2

  1. Release the grease cup (65).

  2. Unscrew the adjusting screw (63).

  3. Withdraw the fan shaft (62fig 2) with the fan impeller (60 fig 2).

  4. Remove the two ball bearings of the shaft.

  5. Clean check the bearings and change if needed.

  6. Collect the fan shaft with bearings and grease them.

Motor Drive     Figure 2

  1. Disconnect the back cover of the fan by unscrewing the 4 screws sp.17 and remove the fan key from the motor shaft then pull out the motor fan.

  2. Unscrew the 8 screws that fix the cover of the back ball bearing and check the bearing then clean it with benzene and put new grease if it is in good condition.

  3. Unscrew the 8 screws of the front cover of the rolling bearing withdrew it toward the fly wheel, check the bearings, clean it with benzene and put new grease if it is in good condition.

  4. Reassemble the two bearings cover of the motor.

  5. Reassemble the fan on the motor shaft and fix its cover.

  6. Rotate the motor manually and check for any abnormal sounds.

Assembling

  1. Collect all parts of the compressor and connect it in their original places after finishing its maintenance and changing all gaskets.

  2. Collect all air pipe connections (inlet and outlet for all stages).

  3. Screw the oil draining plug (34fig 1) and refill oil to the correct level between the Max and Min marks of the lube dipstick.

  4. Rotate the unit by hand and check that is rotate smoothly and without any abnormal sound.

  5. Reassemble the air intake filter (fig.6) to the inlet of the 1st. stage.

  6. Put the starting switch in ON position and operate the comp.

  7. Check every stage pressure on pressure gauges.

    8. First stage 3 atm
    Second s 12 atm
    Third stage 40 atm

  8. Check for any abnormal sound during operation.

  9. Check oil pressure 2:4 atm.

  10. Check for any oil or air leakage.

  11. Record all the maintenance that have been done and all spare parts that have been changed in maintenance card.

  12. Record the number of operation hours

Before Putting the Unit in Operation You Have to do the Following Electrical Test:

  1. Switch off the main supply of 0.4kV.

  2. Switch off the control switches.

  3. Check all termination.

  4. Check the terminals of cables 0.4 kV.

  5. Check and clean the contacts of auxiliary relays in the control cabinets of the compressor.

  6. Check and clean the contacts of air pressure and oil pressure manometers.

  7. Check and clean the contacts of starting contactors.

  8. Check the signal relays.

  9. Check the starting time relay.

  10. Check the control circuit.

  11. Check the electrical valves and testing them.

  12. Adjust the air / oil pressure stages as follows:

    13. First stage 3 atm
    Second stage 12 atm
    Third stage 40 atm

  13. Oil pressure 2 to 4 atm. and oil temp. 80 Co

  14. Test the operating of compressors automatically from control board.

  15. Put the compressor in service.
