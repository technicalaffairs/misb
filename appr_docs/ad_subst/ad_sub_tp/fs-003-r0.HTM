Introduction

This document lists the Inspection checks I1 which must be performed every six months as indicated by M6 in the title block

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should be carry the Suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear your safety and healthy equipments safety helmet, safety shoes …. etc

  • Transformer outage is required.

  • Never perform any work on the transformer before isolating the fire protection system.

  • You must carry portable fire extinguisher when entering the transformer room after the fire.

  • Vent the flooded transformer room before entering it after the fire.

  • Any person inside the transformer room must immediately leave it after hearing the fire alarm. 

  • Any doors leading to the room must be kept closed.

  • To prevent the accidental discharge of the system, do not touch the pilot cylinder weighing device inside the transformer room.

Tools and Equipment

  • Safety Helmet, Safety Shoes.

Work to be Carried out

Note: You must perform a mechanical/manual test of the tripping circuit. In order to prevent any CO2 discharge to the water storage tank you must carry out step 1 and step 2 very carefully

  1. At the CO2 cylinder installation, secure the CO2cylinder valve head with a locking pin

  2. Disconnect the tie-rods from the saw- toothed slide by removing the connecting pins

  3. At the deluge valve stations, remove the glass from the manual pull-station Pull the handle

  4. Observe the operation of the electromechanical triggers at the deluge valve station and that the CO2 cylinders have correctly released the operating weights. Reset all stations for normal operation

  5. At the CO2 cylinder station, move the saw-toothed slide on the trigger so the releasing system is in its original or starting position.

  6. At the deluge valve station, lift up the operating lever of the deluge valve in order to put the valve in the closed position. Make sure that the weight is properly attached to the trigger box.

  7. Check all CO2 spray nozzles, ensure they are not obstructed (plugged). This can be done by inserting a thin nail in each nozzle

  8. Lightly lubricate all moving parts and bearing points of the mechanical trigger mechanism with a thin coating of grease.
