Introduction

This document lists the equipment Inspection checklist I1, which must be performed from month to 3 months as indicated by M1:3 in the above title block. The first draft initiated by eng. Emad Farouk Moustafa at El-Katamia substation Cairo Network

Safety Precaution 

  • A work order must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Transformer isolation and proper grounding on all its sides is required.

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area,

  • Wear your safety helmet, safety shoes, and safety belt when cleaning suspension Insulator.

Tools and Equipment

  • Safety helmet, safety belt, safety shoes, and tool set

  • Blower, voltmeter, screwdriver set, and fiberglass PVC ladder.

  • Voltage indicator, Portable earth for discharging,

  • Fine cloth for cleaning purposes,

Environmental Recommendation

  • Before starting the cleaning of all porcelain surfaces of all transformer wear your breathing mask to protect lung tissues from the dust particulate.

  • Don't burn the cloth used in cleaning, collect it and send to the dump.

  • Fix any oil leaks to prevent pollution and save money of wasted oil.

  • Oil leaks to the ground have very bad impact on the environment, mainly on soil and ground water and eventually on the eco-system. "It takes only I liter of Hydrocarbon spilled in an aquifer to render 1,000,000 liters of potable water useless for human consumption

 

Work to be Carried out

  1. Open the room of the transformer, listen and check for any abnormal sounds from the main tank and accessories.
    A rattling sound indicates loose bolts

  2. Visually inspect bolted connections in the transformer main and auxiliary circuits, foundation bolts and all other bolted connections to ensure that all parts are secure

  3. Read and record oil and winding temperature, check that the maximum temperatures are not being exceeded the limits saved, also check that the temperature is acceptable by comparing it with past entries

  4. Check for leakage from the transformer tank, pipes, gauges, gasket joints, welds, retighten any loosen screws if needed 

  5. Check the cables of transformer for H.V. side and L.V. side for any abnormal position

  6. Check the oil level in the gauge of conservator tank, be sure that the oil level is in same position between max. and min level

  7. Check the oil sampling and drain values for any leakage, retighten if needed

  8. Check the earthing screues and retighten if needed

  9. Clean the all body of the transformer by cleaning clothes, and use the blower to remove the dust

  10. Check the light of the transformer room,

  11. Be sure that the door of transformer room is good closed after inspection finished.
