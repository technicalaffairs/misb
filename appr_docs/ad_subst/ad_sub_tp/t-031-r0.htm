Introduction

This document lists the equipment Testing Procedure checklist, which must be performed for Power Transformers according to each manufacturer Instructions. (eng Khaled initiate the first draft)

Safety Precautions

  • The transformer frame must be securely grounded.
  • All neutral bushings should be filled to securely grounded.
  • All oil filled bushings must be filled to normal required oil level.
  • All oil compartments of the transformer under test must be filled with oil to the normal required oil level.
  • Current instrument transformers must have their secondary windings shorted together and grounded.
  • Test areas should be completely enclosed with a protective barrier that will prevent unauthorized access.

     Note: The load loss expressed in watts should not vary from the original test value (If available) by more than  + 7%

 Tools and Equipment

  • Ammeter, Wattmeter
  • Voltmeter (RMS)
  • Average voltmeter (calibrated in RMS)
  • Current transformer with proper ratio
  • Voltage transformer with proper ratio

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Review the transformer control plan. check any information available on the original factory test date uses it as guide.

  2. Record the transformer's ambient oil temperature.

  3. Determine the winding and tape position to be used for the test.

  4. Determine the voltage required for the test. (From the nameplate).

  5. Determine the metering required for the test.(i) voltage (ii) ammeters (iii) wattmeter's

  6. Ground the transformer tank.

  7. Check that oil is at required levels and in all oil filled bushings.

  8. Ground solidly all neutral bushings of the transformer to be tested.

  9. Short and ground the secondary windings of all current transformers.

  10. Connect leads between adjustable AC power source and the bushings of the winding selected for test. All remaining bushings are open and not connected.

  11. Clear the test area of personal, then energize the transformer increase the voltage slowly. verify that voltage are balanced and that meter values are reasonable, tank reading, then reduce the applied voltage and switch off the supply circuit.

Calculation No Load losses and Excitation current measurement

For single phase:          
                                WA=I1Ø *E1Ø       

                         I1Ø = Single phase Line Amp
                    E1Ø = Single phase applied volts.

For three phases:

                        WA = (I3Ø * E3Ø ) /1.732 

 Where:              I 3Ø  = Sum of three line amps
                         E3Ø  = Three phase applied volts

Percent excitation current:

                % Iex = (IAV / Ir ) *  100 % 

Where:          Iex  Percent excitation current

                        I AV = Average line current
                        Ir     = Rated line current (from nameplate).
