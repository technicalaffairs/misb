Introduction

This document details the items, which are checked every 5 years Y5, they include testing and minor maintenance P2, in addition to the procedure listed it is expected the checks, which are done more frequently, would also be completed at this time

Safety Precautions

  • Work order must be issued.
  • Circuit breaker must be fully isolated.
  • Earthling switch must be closed on both sides of the breaker to ensure it is properly grounded.
  • Appropriate personal protective equipment must be used (Safety shoes, Helmet, Breathing mask, Safety glasses.
Tools and Equipment
  • Timing equipment.
  • Dew-point equipment (moisture monitoring).
  • Acidity testing equipment.
  • Gas quality testing instrument (air contact).
  • Contact resistance tester.
  • Screwdriver set, torque wrench.
  • SF6 gas leak detector.
  • Portable pressure gauge standard.
  • Emergency light, 220 Vac supply.
Work to be carried out

Note: Although not always specified, all checks are performed on all three-breaker phases.

  1. Complete a visual inspection of all components of the circuit breaker, look for signs of Electro static discharge small oil leaks weld cracks etc.

  2. Perform timing tests trip time must be between 15:18 ms closing time must be between 50:60 ms.

  3. Contact resistance must be measured according to MPIS document CB-SS-004- -r0. The contact resistance permissible value is 300 micro ohms. This value includes every thing in compartment.

  4. Using moisture tester to measure the dew point of the insulating gas it should be not greater than 15 ppm as indicated in MPIS document TD-SS-004-r0.

  5. Using acidity tester to measure (SO2) the acid content of the insulting gas this value should not be greater than 0.5% (5-10ppmg)

  6. Using air content tester to check the content of air in the insulating gas. This value should be = 2 % (i.e. value of SF6 gas > 98%)

  7. Using SF6 leak detector check the complete circuit breaker for gas leaks.

  8. Check all bolts and fasteners to ensure they are properly tightened, including mechanism bolts.

  9. Check that all cable plugs are secure in their receptacles so proper contact will be made.

  10. Using a portable pressure gauge to measure the gas pressure inside the circuit breaker. The value should be 7bar at 20oC.

  11. Check the earthling points of the circuit breaker, ensure they are secure and have not been damaged.

  12. Check the operation of the pressure switch by draining off a small amount of gas, while observing the portable pressure gauge .the switch alarm contacts should operate at 6.2 bar and the blocking contact should operate at 6 bar.

  13. Ensure the pressure should 330 bar at normal condition.

  14. Check the operation of the hydraulic oil pump by reducing the hydraulic oil pressure (bleed a quantity of oil back into the reservoir until the pump starts). Check the function of the pressure switch operation and record the stage of pump operation.

  15. Check and record the number of hydraulic pump operation as indication on the operation counters (station operators do this daily).

  16. Check the operation of the heater circuit of the mechanism.

  17. Check the oil level of the parallel resistors through the oil level indicators on the hydraulic mechanism of the resistors also check the level of hydraulic oil in the breaker operation mechanism.

  18. Check and record the pressure in the accumulator ensure that the initial pressure ~ 230 bar.

  19. Check high pressure pipes to ensure they are in their proper locations and sealing properly

  20. Check the hydraulic oil reservoir tank to ensure the cap is sealed properly.

  21. Check and record the operation pressure of the pressure switch by observing the operation of oil switch contacts as the pressure is drained off.

  22. The shut off pressure for the pumps must be checked during pump up.

  23. Also during pump up the time taken to for the pumps to completely charge their respective systems should be record this value is typically around 400 seconds.
