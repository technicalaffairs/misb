Introduction

This document details the steps required to check the nitrogen pressure for SF6 Switchgear (Siemens type 8D.4/220kv)

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category, 

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area,

    •
  • Wear your safety helmet, shoes .... etc
  • Breaker must be isolated.

Work to be carried out

Note:  Refer to diagrams and pictures in Siemens Instruction Manual No. (SW 8518a) or (SW 8364-220)
The nitrogen pressure is the priming pressure of the "stored energy" accumulator, it is factory set and should be approx. 200 bars at an ambient temperature of +20oC

During the measuring procedure the oil pressure will be reduced to zero and the breaker will be in "lock-out" mode.

  1. Measure and record the ambient temperature inside the GIS building.

  2. Switch off the oil pump by tripping magnetic circuit breaker (F201) inside the control panel.

  3. Unscrew the locking screw (14.39) inside the control panel.(5mm allen key required)

  4. Using a 13mm special pipe socket with "T" handle, carefully and slowly open the pressure equalizing valve (14.37).

  5. Observe the hydraulic oil pressure gauge very closely. At first the pressure will decrease very slowly from 320 bars to a certain value, then the pressure will suddenly drop to zero. The nitrogen priming pressure can be recorded at the intermediate point, when the pressure suddenly begins to drop. Under normal conditions the priming pressure should be 200 bars at + 20 C.

Note:    The pressure will vary according to ambient temperature as indicated in the table below.


  1. Close the pressure-equalizing valve.

  2. Carefully re-tighten the locking screw.

  3. Switch on the oil pump and carefully observe the hydraulic pressure gauge.
    The pressure should rapidly climb to a certain value (priming pressure), note and record this pressure. The pressure will then slowly increase to full system pressure of approx. 320 bars.

  4. Compare the two readings in step 5 and in step 8
