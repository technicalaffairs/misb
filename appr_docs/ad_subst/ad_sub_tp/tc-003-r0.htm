Introduction

We recommend a periodic inspection of the tap changer equipment to certain a high reliability in service according to manufacture instructions. Also, we recommend that personnel well acquainted with the equipment should carry out the inspection. The Inspection should be carried out as quickly as possible with the most care and tidiness.

The first draft was initiated by eng. Emad Mabrouk Mohamed, and tested by eng. Wagdy Mechaiel Barsoum at El-Minia132kv substation, accepted by eng Metawie Aly Hassan General director, and approved by eng. Ahmed Saied MPIS and QC General Director at ME zone

The maintenance of the OLTC consist of 3 stages:

1) Inspection:

On the rating plate, inspection once a year, is recommended. This principally concerns the motor-drive mechanism and refers to a visual inspection inside the motor cabinet to check that nothing is loose, the heater is functioning the contactor contacts are not burned in the motor-drive mechanism a counter registers every tap-changer operation. During inspection the counter is read. If possible, motor and counter are tested by operating one step and than back. If the OLTC has its own oil conservator, the breather and the oil level indicator are checked. The inspection is carried out while the transformer is in service

2) Overhaul

The contact life and the frequency of operations determine the time interval between overhauls. The number of operation run by the OLTC is recorded by a counter, housed in the motor. Mechanism cabinet the registered number of operations should be noted at every inspection and overhaul. The OLTC should normally be overhauled regularly at intervals of 1/5 of the estimated contact life. Therevelant information is stated on the rating-plate. Hereby, the contact wear can be followed and necessary preparations can be made for replacing the contacts.

3) Contact Replacement

On the rating plate of the OLTC the calculated contact life at rated load is stated the contacts will withstand a very larg number of switching operations for normal power transformers the number of operations of the diverter switch is approximately 20 per day, which means that exchange of the contacts is not normaly necessary during the life of the transformer

Caution: The number of operations must be in no case exceed 500,000 due to weakening spring tension of the contacts.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued,

  • Personnel carrying out the maintenance should carry the suitable safety category, should be trained and qualified or directly supervised by someone who is.

  • Safety rope with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear your safety helmet, gloves, glasses and shoes,

  • The high voltage CB, low voltage CB of the transformer must be isolated & proper earthing at the two sides is required

  • Use only fiberglass ladders

    • .

  • Be sure to ground the transformer bushing terminals with on-site grounding equipment and with ancillary grounding cable of a thickness specified by on-site regulations.

  • Caution marks in control room is required.

Tools and Equipment

  • Mobile oil filtering machine with tank, hoses adapters, and flushing pipe.

  • Mobile crane or davit (suitable for tap changer model).

  • Empty oil barrels and pails for flushing oil.

  • Oil dielectric tester and oil sample bottles.

  • Meager and Multimeter.

  • One set of metric open-end box wrenches, one set of socket wrenches, screwdrivers, venier calipers (metric).

  • The special tools for the tap changer.

  • Tin cans to hold bolts nuts and washers.

  • Two barrels of new oil.

  • A proper plastic garbage bag to catch oil drips.

  • A plastic ground sheet to cover the work area.

  • A proper plastic or steel tray to wash the diverted and selector with oil in it

  • Rubber gloves, Small brushes, Cloth wipes

  • Spare parts, contacts, contact springs, bolts washers gasket for cover glue and Vaseline for gasket 0 ring

  • Tap changer support, Operating key for removal

  • Special tap changer brushes, 400 mm, 140 mm and handle

  • Inspection mirror, Lubricating grease

Work to be Carried Out

 1.

Operate the tap changer electrically in both directions from the operation box of the transformer and from control room to prove motor operation

 2.

Place local remote switch to the local position to prevent any unauthorized operations.

 3.

Determine the neutral position or adjustment position (centre position of the tap changer)

 4.

Operate the tap changer to the adjustment position

 5.

Cut off AC current of the tap changer operation motor

 6.

Confirm operating position in inspection glass on the tap changer cover

 7.

Close valve between the tap changer and conservator tank

 8.

Connect the flange of filtering machine drain hose to the flange of tap changer drain valve

 9.

Open the tap changer bleeder valve in the head cover.

 10.

Start operating the pump of the filtering machine when the bleeder has been opened and. removes on third of the oil from the tap changer compartment.

11.

Remove the guard plate of the horizontal transmission shaft by releasing the two clamps that fix the beginning and the end of the cover and slide them to the bevel gear end of the shaft

 12.

Remove the transmission shaft by releasing the coupling clamp that connects it with the tap changers.     Figure 1.

 13.

Clean the surface cover for the tap changer and the area of the main cover for the transformer that surrounding the tap changer cover.

 14.

Unscrew the 24 screws of the tap changer cover after ensuring the oil is lowered enough and collect all screws. Washers, nuts and lock washers in a tin can.

 15.

Drain 2/3 of the oil quantity and leave the rest in the diverter switch oil compartment for the first cleaning, and shut down the filtering machine.

 16.

Erect the davit or mobile crane into position. Before proceeding with erection of the davit or moving the mobile crane, ensure the crane operator is fully aware of neighboring live apparatus and the safe limits of approach to overhead lines or bus bars, and the crane is grounded.

 17.

Lift the cover of the tap changer complete with qualitrol safety and set it securely to one side, be careful not to allow something fallen into the oil compartment device and check the aluminum explosion vent for signs of corrosion or damage.

 18.

Check red marks on the gear plate, gear and tap changer head

 19.

Line up tap changer head and cog wheel, plate and Geneva wheel and dial marks to ensure proper alignment.

 20.

Undo suction pipe connection being careful not to drop the washer. Move the suction pipe to the right or left direction, to loosen it, then pull the nipple out of the pipe.

 21.

Release the two energy springs by raising the two locking pins by using an 8mm screw. screw the screw into the head of the locking pin and pull the blocking pin from it's slot to release the spring.     Figure 12, 13, 14.

 22.

Release the upper gear unit by releasing 5 screws, every screw has one washer and one lock washer Be careful not to allow them to fall into the oil compartment and check gear marks on the upper gear unit to detect it's position before raising it.(use socket wrench 13)

 23.

Take not of the 2 guide pins in the tap changer head and the two triangle marks on top of the tap changer insert for reinstallation.

 24.

Remove suction pipe using screwdriver or key to pry off pipe from the bottom bearing. Fig. 15

 25.

Very carefully raise up the suction pipe vertically, taking care not to break it.

 26.

Attach operating key to the insert coupling face in order to centralize the moveable changeover selector contact and facilitate removal of the insert.    Figure 16, 17

 27.

Using the davit or crane attach the chain hoist and 1/2 inch shackles to the lugs on the diverted switch.           Figure 18

 28.

Carefully and very slowly lift the diverter switch clear of the oil compartment by turning it clockwise and counterclockwise to miss the contacts on the housing and the switching unit from hitting one another and wait for several minutes to allow the oil to drip.

 29.

Pull a plastic garbage bag over the diverter to prevent drips while lowering to ground level.

 30.

Drain oil compartment and clean with a 400-mm brush, flush with new transformer oil and drain.

 31.

Examine oil compartment with mirror and check for electrical damage such as tracking, partial discharge and mechanical failures.

 32.

Replace the tap changer head cover to prevent ingress of moisture, dust or something else

 33.

Work must be done in a clean area with the ground covered with a clean sheet of plastic and a proper steel or plastic tray over the plastic sheet is required to clean the diverted switch and selector with oil in it.

 34.

Clean suction pipe with 40 mm brush

 35.

Check spring movement of roller contacts on switching element.

 36.

Check wear of roller contacts and measure with a vernier calibrator. Compare measurements to chart for type Y tap-changers. Replace all contacts if one contact does not meet the minimum requirement or if contacts will wear below the minimum requirement before next overhaul.

 37.

Measure transition resistor from the middle roller to either side. Deviatioshould not exceed 10% of the resistor value.    Figure 36

 38.

Remove carbon from the contacts with a scubbing pad.

 39.

Reinstall the OL TC insert in the reverse order of its removal using the operating key to lift the insert.

40.

If the tap changer has a change over selector switch move the OL TC to a position where the moveable change over selector contacts will be situated between phases after the installation.

 41.

If the tap changer does not have a change over selector switch move the OL TC insert so that it will be approximately in the adjustment position after its installation.

 42.

Lower the OL TC insert into the oil compartment making sure the clearance between the grading rings as well as parts of the switching elements and the fixed parts of the oil compartment is sufficient.

 43.

Remove the operating key and insert suction pipe. OLTC insert will be centered in the oil compartment by pressing the top end of the suction pipe.

 44.

Reinstall the gear unit setting it at the adjustment position in accordance with the position indicating disk and the gear markings. Attach gear unit with bolts.

 45.

Install the tap changer cover and replace gasket if necessary.

 46.

Check the adjustment position through the inspection window in the cover.

47.

Refill the tap changer with oil

 48.

Reconnect the drive shaft and guard cover torching coupling bolts to 9nm. Check entire drive shaft for wear and binding. Clearance between couplings must be approximately 2-3 mm.

 49.

Operate the tap changer by hand to ensure correct installment.

 50.

Check the number of turns between tap changes by cranking the tap changer from a raise to a lower and back to a raise position. The turns must not be greater than one. If the turns are greater than one use the following steps:

a) Turn the crank handle clockwise from position 10 to position 9 until the diverter switch operates and count the crank handle revolutions necessary for the red mark to appear in the green section in the middle of the inspection.

b) Turn the crank handle counterclockwise from position 9 to position 10 until the diverter switch operates and count the crank handle revolutions necessary for the red mark to appear in green section in the middle of the inspection glass.


c) Note the difference between the counted crank handle revolutions


d) To correct any revolutions greater than one by divided by 2 and turning the crank in the direction of the most turns after uncoupling the drive shaft.


e) Re-couple the motor drive at vertical part of the driving shaft   Figure 1.


f) Repeat steps I and 2 to verify the same number of turns in each direction

 51.

Operate the OL TC mechanically through the complete operating cycle to check its function

 52

When satisfied with the mechanical operation check tap changer electrically.

 53.

Restore tap changer to tap position before maintenance

 54.

Place tap changer selector on remote operation.

Lubrication of the drive shaft system

Note: The outer bevel gears are lubricated with grease at delivery and need no further lubrication.

  1. Check the couplings of the outer shaft system.

  2. For access to the couplings loosen the hose-clips and push the protective tubes to getber. Check and lubricate with grease if necessary. Recommended types of grease are GULF-718 EP synthetic grease. Mobil grease 28, Shell - Aero Shell Grease 22 or similar Remount protection tubes

Checking the motor-drive mechanism:

  1. Before closing the cover of the OLTC, make sure that no foreign odjects, tools wires, rags etc, are left in the diverter switch housing

Checking the motor-drive mechanism:

  1. Check and lubricate the motor-drive mechanism at the same time as the OLTC.

  2. Check the heater function

  3. Check the Toothed belt tightness

  4. Check the Motor protection function

  5. Check the Motor contactors for wear

  6. Check the Functions of position transmitter and other position switches

  7. Check the Counter function

  8. Check the Brakes function

  9. Check the Lubrication

  10. Carry out the operation test

Heater

  1. Check (by hand) that the heating element is functioning

Toothed Belt

  1. Check that the toothed belt is sufficiently tight. If tightening is required adjust the motor support.

  2. The tightness of the belt can be checked by a spring balance attached to the belt haifway between the pulleys. At a 10 N load the belt should yield about 5 mm

Motor protection

  1. Check the function of the motor-protective switch. For three-phase AC motors one of the phase conductors is loosened and the function time of the motor protective switch is checked by a raise or lower operation.

  2. The motor protective switch shall release within 60 secoods at a current setting equal to the rated current of the motor at actual voltage

Motor contactors

  1. Check that the contacts are not burned out

 

Position transmitter and other position switches

  1. Clean the contact plates and arms (fig.16) from dust and dirt with a dry cloth.

  2. Check the contact function with the moving contact arm in all positions.

  3. Check and adjust the resilience of the moving contacts in the multi-position switch for the motor-drive mechanism. The moving contacts shall in all positions have a clearance between the nut and the contact arm of .04-1.2mm. Adjustment is made with the nuts on the contact (fig. 16)

Figure 16. Contact arm play

Counter

  1. Check that the counter is functioning at RAISE and LOWER operations and that the arm of the operation counter is firnly fixed to the shaft

The Disc Brake

  1. Wipe the brake disc free from grease.

  2. Check that the cam disc on the "one-turn" shaft iose not affect the driving roller in the maintaining contact after an operation. The moving energy in the motor and the toothed wheels should be absorbed by the brake, and the motor-drive mechanism should stop with the red mark 25º from the centre of the brake pads. This can be adjusted by tightening both of the spring bolts on the brake.

  3. Using the hand-crank, operate the motor-drive mechanism until the bake is fully open. At this point the length of the springs must not be less than 35 mm. If the brake still dose not function, oil or grease has entered the brake linings, which will then need cleaning.

Clean as follows:

  1. Using a pair of tongs, remove the roll pins that hold the supporting shafts on the brake blocks.

  2. Then remove the shaft and the brake blocks.

  3. Clean the brake linings on the two brake with degreasing agent.

  4. When refitting the brake. Check that the brake disc is completely free from grease.

  5. Adjust the spring force of the brake until the motor-drive mechanism stops within the tolerances given above.

  6. Check that the brake for the maintaining contact prevents the arm system on the maintaining contact shaft from swinging beyond its normal position when the roller on the driving arm moves free from the cam disc. At the end of the operation, contacts for operation in the opposite driection shall not move when the arm system swings back to wards its normal position.

  7. Un permitted swinging should be prevented by raising the braking force, i.e. tightening the spring bolt. An increasing spring force on the brake makes the maintaining contact arm swing back faster, and the brake for the maintaining contact must be adjusted.

Lubrication

The bearing points of the brake blocks and the links should be sparingly lubricated with oil. (Use oil for plain ball and roller bearings.) The spur gears, the genevs wheel with the limit stop, the cam discs and the cam bar are sparingly lubricated with grease when necessary. (Us lithiurn grease for ball and roller bearings in low temperature (-50-+ 80ºC)(-58-+176ºF) Other bearing points do not need lubrication

Note: Protect the brake disc and the brake linings against lubricarts. Wipe off excess lubricant

Operation test

  1. Operate the driving mechanism first by manual operation and then electrically between the limit positions.

  2. Check the limit stops by operating the OLTC to one of the end positions. When trying to operate it electrically beyond the end position, the motor should not start.

  3. Check the mechanical end stop by trying to hand crank it beyond the end position. After a couple of turns on the hand crank it should be mechanically stopped.

  4. Operate the OLTC electrically to the other end position and carry out the same test procedure as above

Motor Drive Protections

  1. Thermal overload protective switch for motor.

  2. Limit switches (in both control and motor circuits).

  3. Mechanical end stops.

  4. Interlocking contact in the control circuit to prevent electrical operation during manual operation.

  5. Interlocking contacts in raise and lower control circuits to prevent operation in wrong direction of rotation (with wrong phase sequence).

  6. Motor contactors are electrically interlocked.
