Procedure:
 Measuring the dew point of SF6 Gas
Using Showmeter type SADP		 Approved Date:    23 Nov 1998
Date to be Reviewed:   Nov 2003

Introduction

This Procedure is carried out when measuring the Dew Point of SF6 gas using Showmeter type SADP figure 1 is required during any minor or major maintenance.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, qualified maintenance staff carry the suitable safety ID category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required,

  • Wear safety and healthy equipment as helmet, safety shoes ..etc

  • Circuit breaker must be out of service and isolated and grounded.

Tools and Equipments
  • Showmeter type SADP

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Check the battery of the meter using button in (BATT) position and replace it in a safe area when check reading is outside the green checkmark.

  2. Pump head up and down 3 times for evacuation and removing any remaining gas or air inside the meter.

  3. Switch on the meter and leave it for a minute allowing the pointer to move to zero otherwise it has to be calibrated till zero position using a small screwdriver in automatic calibration control.

  4. Switch off the meter and wait for 15 minutes or more before using.

  5. Connect the meter hose to the SF6 valve from one terminal and let the other terminal free and open the valve slowly to get rid of the air or any gas in the hose

  6. Connect the free terminal of the hose to the meter.

  7. Pump the head up and down for 3 or more times.

  8. Adjust the flow rate of the SF6 gas through the meter by place finger tip over head outlet then remove finger from outlet and wait until the pointer stop and steady.

  9. Record the reading of the meter by putting the button on READ position

  10. Record the ambient temperature using a thermometer.

  11. By these two readings and using the table of the meter, determine the ppm/ unit weight of moisture in the gas.

Note :     The ppm/ unit weight must not be more than 80 ppm/ unit weight.
