Introduction

This procedure details the outlines steps required for dismantling and overhauling station equipment for the major maintenance activities P2 which are performed every 10 years as indicated by Y10 in the above title block.

Safety Precautions

  • Work permit must be issued.

  • Circuit breaker must be isolated and properly earthed.

  • Isolate the DC control voltage.

  • Wear safety, Helmet shoes --------- etc.

  • Caution marks must be hanged on a work place.

  • Safety robe around a work place.

Environment Recommendation

  • Before using a blower to clean all compartment of the circuit breaker from dust, wear your breathing mask to protect your lung tissues from the dust particulate.

  • Don’t burn the cloth used in cleaning, collect it and send it to the dumb.

  • The fans must be operated & the doors must be open.

  • SF6 leaks also have bad impact on the environment.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete tool bag.

  • Grease gun, Lockotite

  • Crane.

  • Wool wires.

  • Portable gauge, Portable Gas leakage detector.

  • Wrench for coil, all special tools,

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Make sure that the CB is isolated from all the AC & DC sources.

  2. Remove the relays which cause a suddenly trip due to under pressure of the gas in the other breakers.

  3. Discharge the SF6 gas from the chamber.

Disconnecting the Auxiliary Contacts

  1. Disconnect the contact of the pressure switch from the control cables.

  2. Using spectral tool no. 32 to raise the moving contact and fix it by ruler so the arc chamber and hydraulic mechanism have separated and the moving at on position.

Disassembling the Pole

  1. Disassemble the support shunt of current transformer Figure 52.

  2. Conductor bar of 3 phase must be fixed along current TR side in balanced position using special tool no 37 Figure 53, Figure 54.

  3. Flanges to current transformer have to be disassembled Figure 55.

  4. Through top flange the ARC chamber must be tied and all fixation bolts of arc chamber base disassemble Figure 56.

  5. All CT's flange bolts have to be dismantled.

  6. Using the crane shaft the arc chamber to up about 2-mm Figure 57.

  7. Using the crane take the arc chamber out of its location about 2.5 cm Figure 58.

  8. To fix the conductor bar you have to be used special tool No 40 to circuit breaker side Figure 60, 61.

  9. Using plastic sheet you have to be covered all opining parts to avoid the dust.

  10. Using the crane take the arc chamber out its location to maintenance location putting it an wood support Figure 62, 63.

Maintenance of Arc Chamber Parts

  1. Dismantle the top flange of arc chamber.

  2. Tie the fixed contact and take it out of the chamber (Figure 1a) and put it on the maintenance table Figure 1b, 1c.

  3. Dismantle the lower flange to reach to moving contacts and disassemble it. Take moving contacts out of arc chamber, both arc chamber house and moving contact are separate. Good inspection for moving and fixed contacts. A good cleaning for all parts and the arc chamber cylinder. Using plastic sheet to cover all components of the arc chamber to a void dust accumulation.

  4. The lower block nut no. 36 of circuit breaker must be dismantling at make you able to draw the insulator drive through the cone.

  5. All gas sealing must be change according to its drawing position for the arc chamber fig 60, 62, and 63.

  6. Using white alcohol all parts you have to ensure that the surface contacts for moving and fixed are good and there is ma deterioration.

  7. Using smooth sandpaper, remove and smooth contact surface deterioration.

  8. Inner diameter of arc chamber nozzle must be checked according to manual instruction values and change it is necessary you have to ensure that there is no evaporation in tungsten part.

  9. All arc chamber cylinder gaskets must be changed.

  10. Grease gas seal chamber using grease gun.

  11. Put the chamber casing on the moving contact using the crane you have to.

  12. All lower flange gaskets no. 112 B, no. 113 B must be changed.

  13. Assemble the fixed contact and all tighter flange gaskets must be changed.

Assembling the Circuit Breaker

  1. To facilitate the smelling used water balance to adjust the ARC chamber level after raising by the crane.

  2. All circuit breaker and CT conductor must be cleaned by alcohol wet clothe to avoid dust and impurities accumulate.

  3. Take care during transfer the arc chamber to its location. Using the crane to adjust the CT and circuit breaker together in vertical and horizontal position.

  4. Arc chamber lower flange and circuit breaker base must le tied together in correct horizontal position with required momentum.

  5. Assemble and fix the accumulator in its location-using crane and also connect the accumulator flange and hose.

  6. Ram of arc chamber and motor shaft of hydraulic mechanism must be connected using special tool no. 22a, to remove the ruler and draw the arc chamber, ram all bolts and nuts must he well tied using key 19.

  7. Fill the 3 oil tanks for circuit breaker to standard level of oil on each tank.

  8. At first you have to start oil pump operation for short time

  9. Anther once yew have to start oil pump operation and open the air output valve to release the air during oil pump operation to get a required pressure of the pump.

  10. Oil bleeding have to be done through a concern valve for low pressure consequently through high-pressure valve for several times to remote air from oil during switches on & off.

  11. Check the oil pressure during the previous procedures and also oil pressure settings for pressure switch according to manufacturer instructions as follow:
      - Normal pressure 330 bar
      - Pump start 320 bar
      - Pump stop 335 bar
      - Off automation 227 bar
      - Block on 270 bar Safety valve open on 390 bar Safety valve close on 345 bar

Note: All previous pressure values have permissible percentage about ± 5% according to manual instructions

 

SF6 GAS Charging

  1. Start a vacuum procedure for circuit breaker until 0.5 m bar for 24 hours. Pressure gauge have to be checked periodically to ensure there is no leak and you can wait for half hour if there is leak you have to herised all bolt and flames righting it if it necessary.

  2. Charge nitrogen gas to 2 bar for 12 hours. Reduce the nitrogen gas pressure until 1 bar and you have to unite for 12 hours after that discharge the nitrogen to reach zero bar.

  3. Then respite the vacuum procedures until 0.5 for 12 hours. After that restart to charge the SF6 gas to normal operation pressure 6.55 bar for circuit breaker.

SF6 GAS Circuit Breaker Tests

  1. Timing test

  2. Contact resistance test

  3. Dew point test

  4. Gas percentage test

  5. Acidity test
