Introduction

This document lists the equipment inspection, which must be performed on a monthly bases as indicated by M1 in the title block.

Technical Specification:

No. of plates              : 21

No. of Cells                : 58 

Normal Voltage           : 116

Ampere houre rating    : 1690 at rate of 15 min .

Electrolyte required per cell 4.8 gall S.G1 1.2-1.22

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued,

  • The qualified maintenance staff carry the suitable ID safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Use standard personal safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, goggles, protective clothing, rubber gloves

    • ... etc.

  • No smoking is permitted in battery room.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete tool set.

  • Digital voltmeter.

  • Hydrometer.

  • Gloves.

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Disconnect AC supply of battery charger

  2. Inspect outside battery containers for cracks and leakage of electrolyte

  3. Inspect contact area of inter connection, lugs, terminal connections, tightening and clean by plastic bristle brush, then coated with a thin film of No - ox - ID grease.

  4. Clean top of battery containers with clear water and neutralized acid spilt only with a solution of sodium bicarbonate baking soda.

  5. Inspect and record electrolyte level, density for each cell ( level at 1/2" lowered from full mark level, 1.2 - 1.22 ).

  6. Lubricate terminals for cells with a thin film of vaseline.

  7. Record voltage / cell, it should be ( 2.05 ± 0.04 ) at 1. 21 , 77 ºF .

  8. If cell voltage more than 0.04 below min. float voltage put battery under equalize charge.

  9. Record overall voltage of battery it should be 2.05 x NO. of cell / battery if it is below put under equalizing.

  10. Replace defective battery.

  11. Check ventilation and clean battery house
