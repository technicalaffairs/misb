Introduction

This document outlines the major maintenance procedures P1, which must be carried out every five years as indicated by Y5 in the title block

We recommend a peiodie inspection of the tap changer equipment to certain a high reliability in service according to manufacture instruction. Also, we recommend that personnel well acquainted with the equipment should carry out the inspection. The inspection should be carried out as quickly as possible with the most care and tidiness

The first draft initiated and tested by eng. Moustafa Abd Allah Eid, approved by eng. Mohamed Lamloum  general director, accepted by eng. Khairy Eshak Head Sector, certified by eng. Ahmed Said Abd El Aziz quality control director, MPIS ME zone Coordinator.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Personnel carrying out the maintenance should be qualified or directly supervised by someone who is

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding work area is required.

  • Wear your safety equipment as helmet, shoes, breathing mask, safety glass .. etc

  • The high voltage CB, low voltage CB of the transformer must be isolated & proper earthing at the two sides is required

  • Use only fiberglass ladders

  • Be sure to ground the transformer bushing terminals with on - site grounding equipment and with ancillary grounding cable of a thickness specified by on - site regulation

  • Caution mark in control room is required

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Drive on load tap changer to the adjusting position (9).

  2. Drain the oil from the oil compartment of the OLTC.

  3. Open the OLTC cover (24 hex heads screws, M10, wrench size 17, with lock washers) take care of the cover gasket (round rubber gasket)

  4. Remove the position indicator dial (remove the clip from the shaft end keep washers for reasembly)

  5. Make a note of red - marked area not covered by gear plate of the diverter switch unit.

  6. Remove the fixing nuts of the diverter switch (5 nuts, M8, wrench size 13, lock washers).

  7. Carefully withdraw the diverter switch unit fron the oil compartment. Keep the diverter switch in a vertical position.

  8. Put the diverter switch to a clean table in workshop.

  9. Clean the diverter switch with new oil.

Diverter Switch

  1. Check all contacts tightened, check by trying to move them by hand

  2. Visually check all springs

  3. Check all connecting leads, they must be flexible and must be fixed at their fixing points.

  4. Check all sliding contacts, if the contact surface is ok clean them.

  5. Push all sliding contacts, check if there is enough pressure.

  6. Switch the diverter switch in both operating position for doing this check but remember the position you are dismount the diverter switch from the oil compartment.

  7. Check the contacts for the resistor connection.

  8. Check visually the contacts at the resistor and switching contacts.

Insulating Cylinder

  1. Measure resistor value.

  2. Check sliding contacts and clean them.

  3. Check visually the insulating cylinder for discharge traces.

  4. Check of the connection to the resistors are faced and tightened.

  5. Check visually the resistors for their connection.

Oil Compartment

  1. Clean thoroughly with new transformer oil

  2. Clean and check sliding contacts.

  3. Insert the diverter switch unit into the oil compartment.

  4. Watch the guiding pin on the bottom plate it must fit into the diverter switch bottom.

  5. Watch the marks on the upper metal ring, it must fit to the mark on the rim of the tapchanger head.

  6. Screw the holding to the upper ring of insulting cylinder and fix it to the oil compartment.

  7. Check with a hand crank on the motor driver.

  8. Fill new transformer oil into the oil compartment and the conservator
    (The break down voltage for oil sample 50 kv / 2.5 mm).

  9. Close the OLTC cover and make sure that rubber gasket in the cover is in its proper position. Equally fasten the 24 screws M 10 (wrench size 17), max. Torque 34 Nm motor drive inspection.

  10. Check oupling of motor drive and OLTC. it shoud be symmetrically.

  11. Check by hand crank the switching of mechanical and elactrical and position.

  12. Check contactors, breakers, housing sealing, terminals general condition.

  13. Make test operation by hand first then make test operation with electrical drive.

  14. Grease necessary parts of motor drive if necessary.

Drive Shaft, Bevel Gear

  1. Open bevel gear and check it visually.

  2. Grease parts if necessary.

  3. Check drive shafts and coupling visually and grease them if necessary.

Measurements before Return Tap Changer into Service

  1. Measure transformer winding temperture        degree C.

  2. Measure the DC resistance.

  3. Measure the turns ratio on all winding.

  4. Measure (R 60 , R 15).

  5. Set tap changer to original tap position.
