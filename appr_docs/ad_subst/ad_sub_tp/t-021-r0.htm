Introduction

This procedure details the minor maintenance procedure, which are performed on a 3-month basis as indicated by the M3. This procedure documents the 2nd. level of maintenance performed on this type of transformer.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounded the work area is required.
  • Before switching off the transformer listen and check for any abnormal sounds from main tank, De hyolrating breather, control cabinet of transformer, control cabinet of Tap changer and accessories this means that:   
      - A rattling sound indicates loose bolts.     
      - Repetitive scraping sound indicate a warn fan or oil pump bearing.
  • The low, medium, high voltage CB's of the transformer must be isolated and proper earthing at the 3 sides required.
  • Wearing safety helmet and safety belt.
  • Use only fiberglass ladder.
  • Personal carrying the work must be trained and qualified by some one who is.

Tools and Equipment
  • Complete tool kit/set, including a torque wrench,
  • Air blower, Lubricating grease (light grade),
  • Fine cloth for cleaning purposes,

Work to be Carried Out

Bushing

  1.  Check the porcelain for cracking and other damaged and clean it.

  2.  Check that there is no overheating of the terminals and any part at the bushing.

  3.  Check for any oil leakage from the bushings.

  4.  Check the pressure of oil in the oil pressure gauges 500, 220, 132 kV bushings and compare it with chart of pressure and temperature (P-T) chart, add oil if the pressure is lower than the reference curve limit (M.N curve) fig.1. The case must be to be located between MN and BC curves.

Oil level, Oil Leakage

  1. Read and record the value on the oil level gauge of the transformer and tap changer check that the oil level readings are correct for the atmospheric temperature.

  2. Check that their is no oil leakage from the transform tank, valves, pipes, gagues, gaskets joints, welds, radiators etc and retight any loosen connection.

De Hyolrating Breather

  1. The silica gel absorbent inside the breather bottle is normally nagenta blue, but turns pink if excessive moisture is present, dry or replace the silica gel if more than 70% in pink.

  2. Check that the sight-glass level of the sealing oil in the oil cup is between the high and low mark, it must be corrected it all times.

Control Cabinet

  1. Clean the control cabinet with air (using blower).

  2. Inspect the terminally and check for any loose connections.

  3. Check the gasket of the control cabinet door and change it if needed terminally for 500 kV transformer and operate the fans manually (and oil pumps for 500kv transformer individually and check that they operates in good condition

Tap changer

  1. Check the oil level of tap changer.

  2. Check the oil level in the gearbox or grease and fill if needed.

  3. Operate the Tap changer and check for any abnormal sounds or vibration during operation.

  4. Check that the step position indicator is positioned within the safety zone and check that the step No.’s the same is in the indicator at the tap cover of tap changer. Record the No. of tap changer operation from the counter and check that the operation counters function correctly.

  5. Check the gasket of the control cabinet of the Tap changer and change it if needed.

  6. Prepare the transformer to be put in service once more.
