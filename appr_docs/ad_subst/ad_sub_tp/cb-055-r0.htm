Introduction

This document outlines the major maintenance procedures P2, to be performed every 6 years Y6, or after 2000 switching cycle. The preventive maintenance is a cycle of planned inspection, tests, overhauls and servicing activities designed to keep the Network equipment in good operating condition and avoid breakdowns.

The first draft initiated by eng. Gamal Sayed Soliman from Assuit 500kv Substation ME zone.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category, 

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area,

  • Wear safety helmet, shoes, breathing mask, safety glasses …

  • CB must be fully isolated.

  • Thoroughly exhaust comprised air from the CB air receiver.

  • Earthing switch must be closed on both sides of CB to ensure it is properly grounded.

  • Interrupt the control circuit DC voltage.

  • Personal protective equipment must be used (safety shoes, Helmet, breathing mask, safety glasses) .

  • Close the Airing valves and exhaust the compressed air.

Tools and Equipment
  • Complete tool set including screwdriver set torque wrench and Blower.
  • SF6 gas leak detector.
  • 220 VAC supply.
  • Portable pressure gauge standard.
  • Contact resistance tester, Timing equipment.
  • Dew-point equipment (moisture monitoring).
  • Gas quality testing instrument (air content)

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Collect SF6 gas in the interrupting tank

Stationary Contact no.151 Fig.1

  1. Visually inspect through handhole. When contact surface is found uneven, remove it from interrupter, and correct it with sand paper. When the length of the contact (L1) is less than 166mm, replace the contact with new one. Apply thin coat of grease to the contact. to do that follow the following steps :
    -     Remove the hand hole cover.
    - Remove the contact by loosing 3xM10 Bolts.
    - Replace the contact with new one.

Arc Contact no.157 Figure 1

  1. When surface is found uneven, polish the surface with a smooth – out file. If you need to change it, remove the contact by loosing 8xM8 socket Bolts, 4xM10 nuts and 4-M6x25 Bolts.(use socket wrenches 6, 8, 10)

Insulation nozzle and insulation cover no. 152,153 Figure 1

  1. Clean the interior and exterior surfaces. Using pure Alcohol and clean wipers. Measure the nozzle inner diameter (D).

  2. Replace it with new one if diameter is 42 mm or over. When the cover inner diameter (d) is 39 mm or over. replace it with a new one. to do it ,loosing 8xM8 socket bolts, 4xM10 nuts and 4-M6x25 bolts. Dimension of nozzle and cover to be measured are indicated below.

Absorbent

  1. Replace with a new one. just before handhole cover is closed, so that the absorbent may not absorb the moisture in the atmosphere.

  2. Don’t expose the absorbent in the atmosphere for more than 30 minutes. to do that detach the handhol cover then detach fixing bolts from absorbent case.

Oil dashpot no. 800

  1. Replace the bush no C805 if the number of operation reaches 2500 switching operation ,also replace O ring.
  2. Replace with new oil type JIS-02320-2 by removing the pin, 4xM20 bolt

Control mechanism (400) Figure 2

  1. Check pins, links, levers, and operating rod and split pins.
  2. Check nuts and lock plates for possible looseness, Apply grease to pin.

Closing link mechanism Figure 2

  1. Clean and apply a small amount of grease to the hook, pins and roller.
  2. Check nuts and bolts for looseness.
  3. Check the state of hook engagement under both close and open positions.

Electro-magnetic valve See Figure 3

  1. Clean and apply turbine oil to the hook,

  2. Check nuts and bolts for looseness. When the armature is depressed with fingers, the hook is disengaged.

Auxiliary switch

  1. Check each part for normal tightening and check the marks of open and closed state.

Air pressure gauge

  1. Use a standard pressure gauge or calibrating apparatus for calibration .Be careful not allow ingress of dust into pressure gauge through the connection from which the pressure gauge is disconnected.

Evacuation

  1. Use a vacuum pump to evacuate to 1mmhg, and longer for 1.5 hours under 1mmhg.

  2. Fill with gas

  3. Fill with air by opening the stop valve

Pressures switch

  1. Check the lock pressure and release pressure on calibrated gauge.
  2. Setting values as follows:
    - Alarm and closing lock out ON 16.5 ± o.2 ,OFF 17.7 ± 0.2
    - Alarm and opening lock out ON 16.0 ± o.2 ,OFF 17.2 ± 0.2
    - Reclosing condition ON 17.6 ± o.2 ,OFF 17.95 ± 0.2
  3. Check for any leakage for air and gas

Trip-free contractor

  1. When contacts are solid or roughened considerably, correct with smooth cut file or very fine sandpaper.

  2. Make micro ohm resistance test and timing test and compare with the factory test.
  3. Using moisture tester to measure the dew point of the insulating gas. it should be not greater than 150 PPM as indicated in MPIS document TD-SS-004-r0.
  4. Using acidity tester to measure (S02) the acid content of the insulting gas. this value should not be greater than (5-10ppmg)

  5. Using air content tester to check the content 0f air in the insulating gas. This value should be 2 % (i.e. value of SF6 gas =98%).

  6. Using decomposition product device.

  7. Record the maintenance has be done and all parts which have been changed in maintenance card.
