Introduction

This document outlines the testing procedure P1, which performed every  year as indicated by Y1 in the above title block.

The Micro Ohmmeter PME-100 is designed for measuring the resistance of contacts on busbars and circuit breakers. The measurement is made at a high current, typically 100 amperes DC. The voltage drop across a contact under test is compared to the voltage drop across a known internal shunt resistance and the result is displayed.

Note: This step by step instruction is for Micro Ohmmeter PME-100.made by ( SMC  Italy )

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified testing staff should carry the suitable safety categories.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear Safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes ... etc.

  • Circuit breaker must be fully isolated.

  • Circuit breaker must be properly earthed on both sides of it, using earthing switch if present and temporary local earthings.

  • Breaker must be in "closed" position.

Tools and Equipment

  • Micro Ohmmeter PME-100.

  • Safety helmet, shoes.... etc.

Work to be Carried Out

Note : The potential cables must always be connected inside of the current cables

  1. Maintain always same polarity (red current cable to red potential cable).

  2. The instrument will give the highest accuracy on the 50 and 100 ampere ranges, however the duty cycle at this range is : one minute "on" & 10 minutes "off".

  3. The instrument requires a 2-minute warm-up time to stabilize the metering circuit.

  4. The breaker contacts are generally in good condition if the obtained reading does not exceed 100 micro ohm.

  5. Using the power cord, connect the instrument to a suitable 220 Volt AC supply.

  6. Plug in the heavy current cables (14m long) and connect the two large clamps including the unknown resistance across the CB contact terminals.

  7. Connect the potential cables (14m long) as close as possible to terminals of resistance under test, and Potential cables must always be connected inside of the current cables as in figure (2).
                       

  8. Make sure that contact points are clean and connections are well tight at both sides of the cables .

  9. Select the 200 micro ohm range or 2000 micro ohm range as required by measurements.

  10. Turn the AC power switch ON, Turn the 100 A switch ON.

  11. Wait until the reading becomes stable and make measurement by reading the display. The breaker contacts are generally in good condition if the obtained reading does not exceed 100 micro ohm.

  12. Turn the 100A switch OFF before disconnecting the test cables and turn the AC power switch OFF.

  13. Before closing the case, make sure that range switch is left in the CHARGE position .
