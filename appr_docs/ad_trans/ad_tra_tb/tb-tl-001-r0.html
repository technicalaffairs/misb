Equipment:
 Use of Arresting Rope in Vicinity of Energized Conductors

 Document No:  TB-TL-001-r0

 		 Approved Date:       02 Sep 1996

Introduction

The temporary fall protection systems for climbing towers require the use of a 16 mm nylon rope. This rope is not qualified as a live line rope and thus must be kept outside the absolute limits of approach to an energized conductor.

Information

In some wind conditions or tower configuration it may be difficult to control the temporary fall protection rope so that it is kept outside the absolute limits of approach to an energized conductor. In such cases, a full length rope should be used so that its weight will require higher wind pressure to blow it close to a conductor.

To further control the movement of the rope, it should be fed through a large karabiner connected to the tower leg near the energized conductor.
