Introduction

The purpose of this procedure is to provide detailed safe method for transmission lines maintenance staff to minimize the possibility of error causing personal injury or damage.

Safety Precautions

  • Wear your safety personnel protective equipment.

  • Wear your full body harnesses include chock absorber.

  • Ensure that the Retractable Life Line is installed above the work location.

  • Maintain the safe limits of approach to energized conductors.

Tools and Equipment

  • Safety personnel protective equipment.

  • Full body harnesses.

  • Dynamometer.

Work to be Carried Out

General Inspection before each use

  1. Before each use carefully inspect the harness and associated equipment for signs of wear or deterioration, or evidence of impact loading.

  2. Visually inspect for loose threads, pulled rivets, burns, cuts, abrasions, or evidence of chemical or physical deterioration that may have weakened the material or assembly.

  3. Check all snap hooks, buckles and D-rings for malfunctions and racks

  4. Immediately destroy and replace any component (warn/damaged), which does not pass inspection

 

Harnesses Inspection

  1. Inspect the tongue and billet end first. They are subject to the most wear as a result of repeated opening and closing.

  2. If your harness is equipped with grommets, inspect for loose, broken or distorted grommets.

  1. Check the Stitching for broken, burned, cut or pulled Stitches, broken strands of webbing appears as tufts on the surface.

  2. To inspect, hold the harness with your hands six to eight inches apart. Bend the webbing in an inverted "U" fashion to cause surface tension, exposing problem areas, inspect the entire length of the webbing. Damage caused by cuts, abrasions, corrosives, heat or chemicals should be apparent.

  1. Check all D-rings for distortion, D-ring attachment points should be checked for unusual wear or damaged fibers. Badly D-rings indicate chemical corrosion, and the equipment should be destroyed and replaced immediately.

  1. For stitched attachment points, check that stitching is not broken, burned, cut or pulled.

  2. Check all riveted attachment points for tightness, especially those at D-ring wear pads. Badly-pitted rivets indicate chemical corrosion, and the equipment should be destroyed and replaced immediately.

  1. Check all tongue buckles for distortion, sharp edges and cracks. The tongue should move freely and overlap the frame. Rollers should not be distorted and should roll freely.

  1. Check all friction buckles for sharp edges, cracks, distortion and to see that outer bars and centre bars are straight. Also check corners and attachment points for wear and cracks.

        

  1. If evidence of excessive wear, deterioration or mechanical malfunction is observed, replace the harness immediately. Never work with worn or damaged equipment. Using damaged or worn equipment can cause serous injury or death.

Important Note:

OSHA specifies that all covered by Occupational Safety and Healthy act are responsible for inspection and maintenance of all tools and equipment used by employees ñ weather owned by the employees or by the company. Personal protective equipment should be inspected before each use, and immediately removed from service if any sign of wear or damage is found.

Maintenance Procedures

  1. Clean and maintain equipment in accordance with recommended practice. Wash nylon harness only in warm water and mild cleanser avoid harsh chemicals such as degreasing compounds, turpentine, paint thinner, gasoline and other solvents.

  2. Allow nylon objects to dry naturally. Donít use heat to speed up the process. Inspect and lubricate all snap hooks after cleaning to make sure they operate properly and close securely. Use Klein Cinch aerosol lubricant or light motor oil.

Cautions: Harnesses

  • Read warning tags attached to your harness.

    A set of warning tags and labels is attached to each harness. For clarity, they are not shown in the illustrations in this booklet. These highly durable warning and instruction tags/labels are designed to be permanently attached. If they should become unattached or damaged call the company.

  • Harnesses are for use by properly trained professional only.

  • Looking hooks are Highly Recommended.

  • Always destroy harness if it has been subjected to any impact loading.

  • Always visually check that all buckles are properly closed before each use.

  • Always visually check that:

    1. Each snap hock freely engages D-ring or anchor point.

    2. Keeper is completely closed before use. Never rely only on the feel or sound of a snap hook engaging.

  • Before each use check that:

    1. Fabric is free of burns, cuts, broken stitches or excessive wear

    2. Rivets are not bent, loose or missing.

    3. Buckles and D-rings are not distorted or cracked.

    4. If tongue buckle, tongue does not bind on buckle and buckle holes are not damaged.

    5. Remove from service and destroy harness if it does not pass this inspection and replace immediately.

  • For personal use only, Not towing or hoisting

  • Snap hooks attached to D-rings must have less than 3/4'' (19mm) throat opening. Never attach ladder or rebar hooks onto D-rings.

  • Only attach connecting devices meeting government standards to D-rings.

  • Never attach foreign objects to D-rings. They may prevent or falsely indicate snap hook  engagement.

  • Never attach multiple snap hooks onto a D-ring.

  • Never punch holes in or alter harness in any way.

  • Never work without independent fall arrest protection if there is danger of a fall.

  • Never rely on a retrieval or other harness for fall arrest protection unless it has a fall arrest D-ring

  • During rebar work, always anchor the positioning assembly hook to vertical reinforcing rods only.

  • Fall-arrest anchor points must support a minimum of 5,000 Ibs (2,250kg) per attached worker and be independent of worker support. Positioning, suspension or retrieval anchor points must support your weight plus any job related load.

 

Test the Full Body Harnesses Semi annually

  • Belt tension test from waist 1200kg

  • Shoulders and legs tension test 1000kg

  • Rope tension test 1000kg

  • Hook tension test 1000kg

  • Issue a certificate that the harness and the rope pass the test.
