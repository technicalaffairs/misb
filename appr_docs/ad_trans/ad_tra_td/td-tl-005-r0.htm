Introduction

Inframetrics Company recommends the following directives when cleaning all optical components. The first draft initiated by eng Samy Mohmed Mahmoud Bassous Cairo, tested at 10 April 02

Information

Handling

  1. All components must be handled in such a way that no foreign matter, particularly grease, touches the surface.

  2. Components shall also be protected as much as possible from dust.

Cleaning

  1. Normally the best advise on cleaning optical components is "DONT" unless a slightly dirty surface has an appreciable effect on the performance of the component. (Any cleaning operation has some associated risk of damage).

  2. Excess dust can often be removed by blowing with clean and dry air. A more effective method of removing dust is with compressed gas. The camera must be held STRICTLY UPRIGHT to avoid liquid from being released. NEVER blow on the components with your breath, 

  3. If cleaning becomes necessary, the following materials are recommended:
           a- Clean lens tissue,
           b- Good quality porous cloth,
           c- Micro filter optics cleaner,
           d- Acetone, reagent grade,
           e- Alcohol: This should be 95% pure (190 proof) distilled ethy1 alcohol. Do not use 100% pure or
               "reagent grade", which has the last 5% water removed by absorbents. The absorbents leave a residue.
           f- Dispensers for both acetone and alcohol: You must use a dispenser designed so that alcohol or acetone
              dose not return to the main reservoir. This is to prevent contamination.

 

Directives 

  1. Fold a cotton pad in 1/2 to form a rectangle and then in 1/2 again to form a square

  2. Spray optics cleaner directly onto a cotton pad. Using the cotton pad "gently" rub surface until it is completely wet

  3. Use another clean and dry cotton pad to wipe component dry

  4. If there still seems to be some grease or dirt on the surface, perform the following two steps:
    - Fold a cotton pad as outlined in step 1
    - Dampen (do not saturate) the folded edge with acetone. Starting at the top edge of the surface, slowly and gently pull  the pad down the surface in one motion. You may repeat this several times. If heavy streaks appear, change to a clean pad and acetone

  5. Final cleaning follow step 2 except substitute alcohol in place of the acetone. The alcohol should remove any small streaks or residue. Repeat if necessary
