Introduction

This document listed the rules for temporary grounding of isolated overhead transmission lines to ensure that the line has been properly isolated, no hazardous potentials are present, or can develop in the work area.

Rule

To ensure employee safety, portable earths shall be applied to isolated line conductors before work is performed. The earths shall be of appropriate size to handle, without failure, the maximum fault current available at the work location.

Directives

Temporary Grounding

  1. Lines shall be isolated in accordance with the E.E.A. Electrical Safety Rules prior to the installation of temporary earths. Temporary earths shall be applied on isolated conductors at the point of work before work is begun. A potential check shall be done prior to the installation of temporary earths. Temporary earths shall be installed, relocated and removed using an approved live line tool, while wearing flash protection and maintaining the appropriate limits of approach In the following situations:
          -     Conductors to be cut or jointed
          - Loops to be opened or closed
          - Line switches to be maintained

  2. The open end shall be bonded (jumpered across) and earthed or both sides connected to a common earth.

  3. All phases within the safe limits of approach shall be earthed.

Earth Size Requirements:

  • The minimum size of portable temporary earth cable shall be 75mm2 copper.

  • Minimum temporary earth requirements for all 500 kv lines and lines in very high fault current right-of and high fault current stations (25 kA - 40 kA) shall be one 150 mm2 copper or two 75 mm2 copper earths.

  1. Care shall be taken when attaching the earth cable clamps to ensure the clamps make good electrical contact. The earthing cable shall be kept uncoiled and clear of any contact with tower steel or other earthed conducting objects. Approved earthing procedures shall be followed.

  2. Transmission lines should be isolated and earthed by the operating authority wherever system earthing facilities exist. At least one earth switch shall be a guaranteed item for the work protection. When practical, station earth switches shall be closed at all terminals.

  3. In high energy corridors induced voltages may be very high. For this reason, at least one permanently installed earth switch shall be closed before temporary earths are applied or removed. Whenever practical, permanently installed earth switches should be closed on both sides of the work .location before temporary earths are applied or removed.

  4. When applying or removing temporary earths with permanently installed earth switches closed on both sides of the work location an arc can be expected. With the use of only one permanently installed earth switch, the arc length, when installing or removing a temporary earth, can be much larger.

  5. All temporary earths shall be removed from a line before the permanently installed earthing devices are opened.
