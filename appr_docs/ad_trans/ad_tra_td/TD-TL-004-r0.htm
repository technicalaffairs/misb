Introduction

This document is used as a technical directive for Russian Power-washing equipment prior to start washing Live Transmission Lines 500KV Suspension and Tension Insulators.

Directions

  1. A work permit must be issued comprising of names of workers, date, and time, as well as the working site and the towers numbers. If the work location is at a great distance, the issuing authority can issue a verbal work order by telephone to the work supervisor.

  2. The workers must pass an annual examination/test in live line washing so as to ensure their complete understanding of how each piece of equipment operates and all the live line washing procedures to ensure their capability of performing the job. This must also be written down in workers examination registers and the industrial safety card of each worker.

  3. Workers must pass an annual health -check and it must be written down in their registers.

  4. Auxiliary protective equipment must be used, such as waterproof overalls, non-conductive gloves, goggles, and binoculars.

  5. A written permission given by the supervisor and registered in the work permit is essential for any person or piece of equipment to be present at the working site.

  6. In case of any change in water pressure the washing technician must immediately move the water column away from the insulators and the tower.

  7. It is essential to have a constant and direct communication between the work location and the nearest station capable of performing communication between the work supervisor and the specific control center.

  8. The following instructions for the live line washing must be taken into consideration

  1. Earthing of the equipment used (MPIS document no. TL-023-r0, item 7) Detecting the wind speed and direction (MPIS document no. TL-023-r0, item 4).

  2. The ground where the work takes place must be leveled and smooth .The inclination of the telescope pillar must not exceed 3 degrees.

  3. The diameter of the nozzle, water pressure, and the length of the water column, as well as the specific resistance, the length of the water hose and the inclination of the nozzle must be as required.

  4. The distance between the telescope and the tower, as well as that between the basket and the conductors must not exceed or be less than what is required.

  5. The washing process must take place according to the set procedures.


     

  1. The washing process must stop in any of the following cases:

    1. Rain / thunder / lightning

    2. Extreme wind speed /storms or mist

    3. Night fall or vision not clear

    4. De-energization whether it was caused by the washing process or any other reason.

    5. Changing the water pressure.

  1. Workers must not touch or get close to the equipment during the process. They must also stay away from any wet ground.

  2. All workers must stay away from the telescope by a distance not less than 4.5 m.

  3. Work must take place according to the procedures set for cleaning each type of tower. Work sequence must take place according to the wind direction as well as the instructions of the supervisor.

  4. The supervisor must make sure that each insulator string is entirely clean before moving to another one.

  5. Work must be done according to procedures set, and each piece of equipment must be place in its proper position.
