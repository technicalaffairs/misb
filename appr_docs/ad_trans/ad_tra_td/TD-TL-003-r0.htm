Introduction

The following tables of safe minimum distances from exposed energized electrical equipment shall be maintained in order to provide a safe electrical environment for EEC workers, electrical contractors and the general public.

Each table applies to specific circumstances corresponding to equipment design and work procedures and shall be interpreted by authorized EEHC staff accordingly, as outlined in the “EEC Electrical Safety Rules”.

This document was amended according to "Official Journal" issue no. 26 at 27 June 1991 regard to Law no. 204 for 1991 ''Rights of Way's Law''. so table no. 6  was amended at 16 Oct 2005.

 

Definition 

Exposed Energized Electrical Equipment is defined as any electrical apparatus or conductor and attached hardware that is not protected by a cover, jacket or barrier and is dynamically charged.

This equipment shall be considered energized until isolation has been verified and approved earthing procedures have been completed.
