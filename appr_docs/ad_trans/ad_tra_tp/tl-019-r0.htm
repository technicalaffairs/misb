Introduction

The purpose of this procedure is to provide detailed safe method for transmission lines maintenance staff to minimize the possibility of error causing personal injury or damage to equipment for washing Live Transmission Lines 500kv Suspension Insulators Using Russian Equipment.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued including the towers numbers, the working site, the date and Time as well as the names of the numbers of the working teams and degree of safety rate for every one of them.

  • Ensure the pressure of the water pump, must not be less than 10 atmospheric pressure (BAR).

  • Ensure the specific dielectric resistance of water used in the washing process, must not be less than 400 ohm-cm.

  • Ensure the wind speed meter should be established at a distance of 20 m from the tower on a high spot free of obstacles, given that the wind speed must not exceed 10 m/sec.

  • Ensure the adequacy of means of communication with control center.

  • Check for broken insulator disks prior to washing. Washing must not take place where there are Less than 17 disks of good condition in the string.

  • Work should stop immediately in case of any change in weather conditions such as storms, mist or rain.

  • Work should stop immediately if the circuit becomes de-energized due to the washing process or any other reason.

  • All Equipment should be provided with an earth cable of cross section not less then 150 mm2 or two cables not less than 75 mm2.

Tools and Equipment

  • Telescopic tower car (26 meter height) branch with 17mm nozzles, fixed on a special support in the basket of telescope and covered by protective shield-cage, rubber hose rated at 10 atm. and 40 meter long.

  • A pump to draw water from the tank and drive it through the hose with a pressure not less than 10 atmosphere press. (BAR).

  • A vehicle equipped with a water tank of a capacity about 5 m3.

  • A waterproof coat.

  • A wind meter.

  • Field glasses (Binoculars).

  • Goggles.

  • Non conductive gloves for the working individuals.

  • Earth cables to ground the washing equipment (Telescope, Water truck, and water pump)

Work to be Carried Out

  1. The supervisor should specify the proper position of each piece of equipment especially the telescopic vehicle as illustrated in (figure 1). He should also identify the work sequence on each phase according to the wind speed and direction which be detected even if the wind was completely still, given that the water stream must flow in the same direction of the wind.

  2. The telescopic vehicle jacks must be lowered to the ground , the vehicle raised and leveled to the ground and vehicles must be connected to the tower earth by approved earth cables.

  3. The water hose must be connected to the water pump.

  4. The washing technician will climb to the telescope basket and the telescope pillar should be raised taking care that its inclination from the main axis should not exceed (three degrees) as illustrated by the pillar’s pointer.

  5. The telescope height should not exceed 14 or15 meters from the ground in case of suspension towers type P or 19-20 meters in the case of suspension towers type P+5 and in both cases the distance from the hose nozzle to the insulator string should be not less than 8 meters.

  6. The pump is operated when the washing technician signals to the supervisor. The required water pressure must be detected .The washing technician then directs the water column to the lower insulator disk nearest the conductor and progresses upwards avoiding the over spray of water drops to the other insulator string of the same tower which are yet to be cleaned.

  7. The water is directed upwards along the insulator string (as illustrated in figure 1) by raising the telescope pillar slowly according to the signals made by the technician in the basket.

  8. The water must move along the insulator string (comprising of 30 disks) in a time not less than 40 seconds to avoid wetness of the insulator string causing the current to be cut off.

  9. The water column should be directed away from the tower as soon as the uppermost disk in the string is cleaned.

  10. In the case of double suspension insulator strings the water should be directed to 3 to 5 consecutive disks in the first string then to the same number in the parallel string at the same level and the cleaning process must proceed upwards.

  11. The water pump is turned off after the cleaning process is completed as the basket technician signals, then the telescope is lowered.

  12. The work supervisor must make sure that all the disks in string are quite clean before moving the equipment to another location.

  13. The telescope vehicle is removed to the next position to wash the following string according to the wind direction taking into consideration that the vehicle velocity should not exceed 4 km/ hour.

  14. All the strings on the same tower are washed in the same manner with the same procedures taking care not to disconnect the earth cables between the equipment, vehicles and the tower. The connections should also be checked after washing each string and before moving the telescopic vehicle.

  15. After washing all the strings of a tower the water valves on the pump and the tank are closed, the earth cables are disconnected from the tower and those of each piece of equipment are rolled on their specific reel.

  16. After the technician comes down, the telescope is moved back to its initial position and then moved to the next tower. The supervisor should also take care not to move the cars under the energized conductors.
