Introduction

The purpose of this procedure is to provide detailed safe method for transmission lines maintenance staff to minimize the possibility of error causing personal injury or damage to equipment for Lowering 500KV Middle and Side Bundles to the Ground for Repair.

EEHC_TL engineers in head office and zones using their background field experience and EEHC documentation to create this procedure.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued.

  • Ensure isolation using a potential indicator, then connect local earthings at the work location refer to MPIS doc. no LI-TL-013-r0.

  • Use approved fall -arrest procedures as well as personal protective equipment

  • Approved safety wire rope cables must be used, and the tractors winch cables must be frequently checked.

  • All equipment used must be checked and approved prior to work .

  • Workers uniforms must include a color indicating the disconnected circuit.

Tools and Equipment

  • Two bulldozers equipped with a winch at the back.

  • Two 6 ton Russian blocks.

  • Two 7.5 ton tower leg snatch blocks together with their specific wire rope cables (5m in length, 22mm in diameter).

  • An 80 meter long steel winch cable, 16 mm in diameter, for raising and lowering equipment

  • A hand line and an aluminum pulley for raising and lowering equipment.

  • A 70 m long steel wire rope cable (19 mm in diameter ) to be attached to the conductors.

  • Tool bag.

  • Approved fall arrest and personal safety equipment.

  • Two grips suitable for a conductor of a conductor of cross sectional area of 500 mm²

  • Nine 6 ton shackles.

  • 50 m long conductor of a cross sectional area of 500 mm²

  • A press with various dies.

  • Two steel and aluminum middle press joints.

  • A measuring tape.

  • Aluminum cutters.

  • Vernier calipers.

  • Iron saw

  • Isolating stick

  • Temporary earth cables of cross sectional area of 150 mm² .

  • A metal brush.

  • Insulating gloves.

  • Potential indicator.

Work to be Carried Out

  1. A worker must climb up the tower 1 to ensure isolation and apply earthings on the three phases of towers no.1&3 Fig.1, given that work will be carried out on tower no. 2. The middle and side phases of tower no.2 must be earthed directly before reaching the ground to be discharged.

  2. Two climbing technicians must climb the tower up to the conductor where the work is taking place and install one hand line at the cross arm angle next to the middle phase and another at the angle next to the side phase. Two 6 ton Russian block must also be raised one of which is fixed above the middle phase plate, while the other must be fixed above that of the side phase. Another snatch block must be attached at the tower leg.

  3. The bulldozer winch cable (80 m in length, 16 mm in diameter) must be raised and attached to the L joint of the middle phase through the tower leg snatch block and the middle phase Russian block.

  4. Using the bulldozer winch cable, the insulator string must be slackened then the L joint pin must be released and both the strings and the conductor lowered to the ground , given that they must be earthed immediately before they reach the ground as previously mentioned. Then untie the bulldozer winch cable.

  5. As for the side phase, the bulldozer winch cable must be attached to the side phase L joint through the tower leg snatch block, which must be conveniently placed at the side phase Russian block.

  6. The side phase is similarly lowered and earthed before reaching the ground.

  7. Two grips must be installed on the worn out conductor at a distance of 30 m from the tower, after the spacers of whole three spacers group at either side of the insulator strings must also be released.

  8. One of the grips must be attached to the front side of the bulldozer by a steel wire ,while the other grip must be attached to the wire at the back of the bulldozer as illustrated in the Fig.(2).

  9. The bulldozer is operated in order to apply tension on both wires slackening the part of the conductor between both grips. The worn out conductor is then cut at a distance of at least 25m at either side from the tower center.

    10. N.B:  Before cutting the damaged conductor section, temporary earthing jumpers must be installed across the entire conductor section to ensure that no induced potential voltage or current arises between two open points Fig.(3 a,b).

  10. The ends of the old conductors where the cutting took place must be entirely cleaned , and the new conductor is brought given that the measurement must be accurately performed.

  11. Apply bandage at both terminals of the conductor (the terminal of the old conductor as well as the new one) at a distance of 5 mm from each side as shown in Figure (4-a).

  12. The conductor is then inserted in the middle aluminum joint as shown in Figure (4-b).

  13. Apply another bandage at a distance of 145 mm from conductor end at the both side as shown in Fig. (4-c). Peel the aluminum strands off the steel core for a distance of 145mm from the bandage at both ends

  14. Insert both ends of the conductor into the steel joint till they touch at exactly the midpoint of the steel joint Fig.(5 – a).

  15. Press at the midpoint of the joint (i.e. at the point of contact of the conductor ends), using a press with the suitable dies. Press on at both ends of the conductor till the end of the joint, given that each two presses must overlap by 25% Fig.(5 - b).

    16.      N.B: The joint diameter must be (27+0.3mm) after pressing.

  16. File the steel joint then apply conductive grease on it.

  17. Slide the aluminum joint on the steel one, till the latter becomes at the middle of the aluminum joint.

  18. Replace the steel press dies by aluminum ones, then press once more starting at both ends of the steel joints till the end of the aluminum joint.

    19. N.B: The diameter of the aluminum joint must be (52+0.3mm) after pressing as shown in Fig.(5).

  19. Install another joint at the other end of the conductor following the same procedures mentioned above. The conductor is thus entirely repaired.

  20. The conductor and the grips must then be released, the bulldozer moved away and the conductor re-connected to the insulators. All spacers must then be re-fixed and local earthings disconnected.

  21. The work location must be cleared of all workers and equipment, and the side phase must then be raised and installed once more in its initial position.

  22. Earthing of the middle phase must also be disconnected.

  23. The tower must be cleared of all equipment and the local earthings of the other towers disconnected.

  24. The work permit must be completed and the control informed that that circuit is ready for re-connection.
