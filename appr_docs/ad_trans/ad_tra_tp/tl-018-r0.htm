Introduction

The purpose of this procedure is to provide detailed safe method for transmission lines maintenance staff to minimize the possibility of error causing personal injury or damage to equipment to repair short section of damaged conductor with armor rod or open sleeve.

This procedure was developed by EETC TL engineers at zones using their background field experience and EETC documentation to create this procedure.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued.

  • Ensure isolation using a potential indicator, then connect local earthings at the work location refer to MPIS Doc. No. LI-TL – 013-r0.

  • Use approved fall -arrest procedures as well as personal protective equipment

  • Approved safety wire rope cables must be used, and the tractors winch cables must be frequently checked.

  • All equipment used must be checked and approved prior to work.

  • Workers uniforms must include a color indicating the disconnected circuit.

  • Communication between the control unit, the supervisor and the crewmembers must be ensured.

Tools and Equipment

  • Climbing tools

  • Approved long ground cable and another short cable

  • Armor rod - open sleeve -press set.

  • Choir Hoist – Three snatch blocks –Tension machine.

  • Five nylon slings - rope ladder.

  • Complete hand line - 16-mm. wire rope.

  • Cleaning conductor tools (steel brush, steel wool, above sire cloths- and Grease)

  • Six shackles.

Work to be Carried Out

  1. A worker must climb up the tower to ensure isolation and apply earthing on the three phases of towers no B and D, given that the work would take place at tower no. C.

  2. Attach the hand line on the tower C fig (1).

  3. Lift the equipment required to tower arm.

  4. Install wire rope and also rope ladder as in fig (2, 3).

  5. Operate tension machine step by step to obtain slak between suspension clamp and insulator.

  6. Disconnect the insulator string from conductor.

  7. Lower the conductor to ground and it must be earthed directly before reaching the ground at both sides of damaged section of conductor as in figure no.2.

  8. Clean the damaged part very good using steel brush and abrasive cloths.

  9. Then insert two half’s of the open sleeve on the damaged part and compressed it using hydraulic press (open sleeve must be have the same size of conductor outer diameter.

  10. In case of using armor rod install the armor rod on the damaged section after cleaning the conductor instead of open Aluminum sleeve

  11. When finished the work lift the conductor again by using wire rope and tension machine.

  12. Connect again the insulator string to the suspension clamp using rope ladder.

  13. The work location must be cleared of all workers and equipment,

  14. The tower must be cleared of all equipment and the local earthings of the other towers disconnected.

  15. The work permit must be completed and the control informed that that circuit is ready for re-connection
