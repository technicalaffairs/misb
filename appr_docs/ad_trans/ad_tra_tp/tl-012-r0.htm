Introduction

The purpose of this procedure is to provide detailed safe method for transmission lines maintenance staff to minimize the possibility of error causing personal injury or damage to equipment ,and Washing Live Transmission Lines 500KV tension Insulators Using Russian Equipment.

Safety Precautions
  • A work permit must be issued comprising the work location, towers numbers, date and time, as well as names of the linemen and the degree of safety rate for each.
  • Ensure the pressure of water pump, must be not less than 10 atmospheric pressure.
  • Ensure the specific dielectric resistance of water used, must be not less than 400 ohm/cm
  • Ensure the wind speed meter must be established at a distance of 20 m outside the tower.
  • Ensure the wind speed and direction, must not exceed than the following values:
    • 8 m/sec for ordinary tension towers and angle towers of inclination from 0 to 20 degrees
    • 6 m/sec for angle towers of inclination from 20 to 45 degrees
    • 4 m/sec for angle tension towers of inclination greater than 45 degrees.
  • Check the adequacy of communication means with the specific control center.
  • Work should stop immediately in case of any change in weather conditions such as storms, mist or rain.
  • The supervisor must make sure that there are no broken or damaged insulator disks in the insulators strings. The cleaning process must not take place where there are less than 17 desks of good condition.
  • Work should stop immediately if the circuit becomes de-energized due to the washing process or any other reason.
  • All Equipment should be provided with an earth cable of cross section not less then 150 mm2 or two cables not less than 75 mm2.

Tools and Equipment
  • Telescopic tower car (26 meter height) branch with 17mm nozzles, fixed on a special support in the basket of telescope and covered by protective shield-cage, rubber hose rated at 10 atmospheric pressure and 40 meter long.
  • A pump to draw waters from the tank and drives it through the hose with a pressure not less than 10 atmospheric pressure (BAR).
  • A vehicle equipped with a water tank of a capacity about 5 m3.
  • A waterproof coat.
  • A wind meter.
  • Field glasses (Binoculars).
  • Goggles.
  • Non conductive gloves for the working individuals.
  • Earth cables to ground the washing equipment (Telescope, Water truck, and water pump)

Work to be Carried Out
  1. The supervisor should specify the proper position of each piece of equipment especially the telescopic vehicle as illustrated in figure 1. He should also identify the work sequence on each phase according to the wind speed and direction, as mentioned in safety precautions.
  2. Work must proceed depending on the wind direction so that the water would flow avoiding any water splash on the insulators, which aren’t cleaned yet.
  3. Washing of tension towers insulators is usually completed as the telescope is moved along 8 Positions as in Figure 2. Start the washing process at the farthest insulator in the direction of wind given that water must flow in a direction opposite to that of the wind to avoid any splash of water on the insulators yet to be cleaned.
  4. Place the telescope vehicle and the rest of equipment at the line comprising location no. 1 at a distance of 15 m from the towers axis given that it is the farthest line in wind direction as for the telescope vehicle it must be placed at location no. 1. (i.e. 6.5 m away from each phase).
  1. The telescopic vehicle jacks must be lowered to the ground, and the earth cables of the equipment are connected to the tower legs. The hose attached to the water pump must also be connected to the telescope.
  2. The washing technician must climb to the basket of the vehicle. raised and leveled to the ground and the telescope pillar is then raised taking into consideration that its inclination from the main axis must not exceed three degrees. Also take care that its height must not exceed than:
    • 12 to 13 meters in the case of tension towers type A
    • 17 to 18 meters in the case of tension towers type A+5

In both cases, the length of the water column must never be less than 8m in length, and the height of the telescope must make it possible for the washing technician to have a full view of all three insulator strings in a parallel position.

  1. Turn on the water pump when the washing technician gives a starting signal to the work supervisor. Then the cleaning process will takes place by washing the first half insulators strings of phase A, facing the High Dam, which are insulators no 1, 2, 3 consecutively (the insulators strings are numbered according to the numeration of the High Dam - Cairo Line 500KV as illustrated in document no. TD-TL-004- -r0. The water stream must flow starting from the conductor towards the towers body to the midpoint of the insulator string, and three or four disks of the three strings are washed consecutively till half the disks are cleaned taking into consideration that each 30 disks are cleaned in 40 seconds.
  2. Turn off the water pump when the washing technician gives a stopping signal to the supervisor. The supervisor must ensure that all insulators are well cleaned using field glasses from a spot where it is possible for him to see all the insulator disks.
  3. Wash the insulators no. 4, 5, 6 at location no. 2 similar to insulators no 1, 2, 3 at location no.1 following the previous procedures and taking into consideration the same Safety Precautions used before.
  4. Washing the no. 2-1, 3-1, 4 Loop suspension insulators string of phases A, B, C facing the High Dam must take place at sites 2, 3, 4 consecutively given that the water column must be moved upwards along those strings from the conductor up to the towers top.
  5. After washing all the insulators facing the High Dam, the taps of the pump and the tank must be turned off and the hose disconnected from the vehicle. All earthing cables that hinder the transfer of equipment must also be disconnected and equipment are then moved slowly, (i.e. with a velocity not more than 4 km/hour), to the other side of the tower (that facing Cairo)
  6. Insulators strings facing Cairo direction must washed from sites no 5, 6, 7, 8 consecutively. Taking the same steps and precautions except for the direction of the water column movement, as it must be moved along the tension strings in a direction opposite to that of the wind according to the common rule.
  7. After washing all the towers insulators mentioned in the work order, taps of the pump and the tank must be turned off, the hose and the earthing cables be disconnected. Then the equipment moved away from the towers outer phase by 25 m, then the telescope is then restored to its initial position to be moved to another tower taking into consideration that the vehicles must not be moved under the energized lines conductors.
