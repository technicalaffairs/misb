Introduction

 The purpose of this procedure is to provide detailed safe method for transmission lines maintenance staff to minimize the possibility of error causing personal injury or damage.

Safety Precautions

  • Ensure that the local grounding on both sides of the tower are located

  • Wear your safety personnel protective equipment (Helmet, Glasses, Gloves and Shoes)

  • Wear your full body harnesses include chock absorber.

  • Ensure that the Retractable Life Line is installed above the work location.

  • Maintain the safe limits of approach to energized conductors.

Tools and Equipment
  • Hand line, safety helmet, glasses, gloves and shoes

 

 

Work to be Carried Out

Using a hand line for rescuing a person who has fallen and is suspended in his full body harness from a retractable lifeline. Refer to figure 1

  1. Climb to a location above the victim and belt the pole strap to an adequate steel member of the structure.

  2. Attach the block of the hand line to a steel member above the victim and disconnect the hand line hook from the snap into two parts.

  3. Wrap the snap end of the hand line 2 times around an adequate steel member above the victim.

  4. Connect the hand line hook to the back “D” ring of the victim’s body harness

  5. It will require at least 2 people to raise the victim on the hand line to a position near the rescuing lineman on the tower.

  6. The lineman on the tower disconnects the retractable life line from the “D” ring and attaches the snap end of the hand line (rescue line) to the “D” ring instead.

  7. Take all the slack from the rescue line, release the hand line and remove hand line hook from (D) ring on harness.

  8. Lower the victim by unsnapping and paying out the rope to control the victim’s descent to the ground.

  9. Attend to the first aid needs of the victim.

Notes:

  1. The victim was out of reach for handling by the lineman on the tower attach the hand line block in a position that enables you to bring the victim to a position for better handling for the rescuing lineman on the tower.
  2. In some cases this method is impractical so a rope ladder must be used to reach the victim to rescue who will be rescued.
