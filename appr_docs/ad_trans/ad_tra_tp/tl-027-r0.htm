Introduction

The purpose of this procedure is to provide detailed safe method for transmission lines maintenance staff to minimize the possibility of error causing personal injury or damage to equipment.

The Transmission Line engineers in head office and zones using their background field experience and EETC / EDF documentation to create this procedure. The document was updated in HTML Format at Feb. 2004.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued.

  • Visually inspect all equipment (sticks, ropes, slings)

  • Inspect the insulation of insulating ropes using leakage surface tester.

  • Clean all poles by silicon cloth.

  • Be sure that all sticks have been tested and the date of last inspection not more than six months refer to the inspection certificate.

  • Earthing resistance for the line is in permissible value (black soil < 10 ohm, Rocky soil < 30 ohm, sandy soil < 20 ohm) .

b) Special Operating Mode (S.O.M)

  • Automatic reclosing unit on the line must be on the position “OFF".

  • If the line Switched off during the work, don’t switch it “ON” again before telling the work supervisor to lower the line man to the land.

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Check the radio link.

  2. Define the line, tower and parking car location.

  3. Define the rope (handline) location.

  4. Put Racks and bring out the materials from the car and inspect collective and individual materials.

  5. The first line man climb the tower with lifeline (by using safety tools, fall arrest and hook stick.

  6. The other lineman on the ground will plan steel pickets and pulley and complete hand line installation.

  7. Left the measuring rod up and check distances refer to distance table.

  8. Left up hook ladder and the extension unit, install it in a distance equal to 1.7m from the guard piece.

  9. The second line man climb the tower with another insulating rope and pulley.

  10. In case of porcelain insulators, check the state of insulator using mechanical tester.

  11. Collect and record the data necessary for calculation as illustrated in the check list form attached.

  12. The lineman descends on the ladder and takes the pole with brush to start to clean the string from top to bottom see fig. (2).

  13. In case of Porcelain String, check string by using mechanical tester - recalculate the LC on checklist if defective insulators are found.

  14. In case of Cap & Pin type, raise the brush using double brush and begin to clean the string from top to bottom.

  15. Repeat these steps for all phases.

  16. In case of double circuit towers move all equipment to the second circuit.

  17. Lowering all equipment to the ground and put them in the car.

  18. Return the live working authorization.
