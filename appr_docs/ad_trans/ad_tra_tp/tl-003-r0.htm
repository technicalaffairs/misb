Introduction

Horizontal and outboard movements may involve walking across a tower arm or the bridge or a shaft of the tower. These movements may also involve the use of suspension platforms, rope Ladders. This Procedure outlines some of the options that may be used for temporary fall protection during these movements to access the work location on the tower.

Safety Precautions

  • A Work permit must be issued.

  • Local earthing on both sides of work is required.

  • Wear your safety Helmet.

  • Wear your safety Belts

Work to be Carried Out

Horizontal Movement on the Tower - Double Belting System

  1. The double belting system which is comprised of a shock absorbing lanyard and pole strapis the most common fall protection system used when making horizontal movements on towers.

  2. Attach a shock absorbing lanyard to the back “D” ring of the harness and then connecta large karabiner to the other end of the lanyard.

  3. Move horizontally out the arm or across the tower bridge or shaft by alternately relocating the karabiner and the pole strap to provide continuous attachment to the tower members See Fig 1.

 

                                Figure 1   Using a Double Belting System

Retractable Lanyard

  1. Install a nylon sling on a cross member of the tower shaft or leg of the structure.

  2. Connect the retractable lanyard housing to the sling to allow the line to be fed out vertically.

  3. Thread the line through a large karabiner or sling on the leg or a member below the housing to change the direction of the line form vertical to horizontal. See Fig 2.

  4. Attach the end of the lanyard to a shock absorber on the back “D” of the body harness.

  5. Install Karabiners over the line and attach them to the members as you move along to minimize any pendulum motion if you should slip. Inserting the line through karabiners will also prevent the lanyard from excessive rubbing on the steel members as you walk across the arm.

  6. This system is useful when moving across bridge sections and tower arms when descending to de-energized conductor levels on a rope ladder.

Rope Anchored above the Walkway Location

  1. Belt in to the tower leg or ladder at the height location where horizontal movement towards the end of the arm will begin.

  2. Attach a 16 mm(5/8 in) nylon rope to the back side of an anchor hook and then install the hook with the switch sticks to a suitable location above the path of horizontal movement. Choose a location to minimize pendulum motion in the event of a slip.

    Note    : If there is a possibility that the nylon rope could encroach on the limit of approach to live conductors, substitute an approved live-line rope for the nylon rope.

  3. Where possible, use the main horizontal members of the arm above the anchor point.

  4. Install the fall arrester onto the rope in such a way as to minimize slack but still allow movement

  5. out of the arm. See Fig 3.

  6. Un belt from the tower steel or ladder and proceed to move horizontally along the tower arm.

  7. Belt to an arm member when the anchor hook must be relocated.

 

Figure 3: Using a Rope Anchored above the Walkway

Temporary Restraint Rope

  1. A temporary restraint rope may be installed along the arm or across the bridge when many trips are required to the work location.

  2. Tie one end of 16mm (5/8 in) nylon rope to a sling or karabiner installed near the end of the tower or bridge.

  3. String the rope through karabiners or nylon slings that have been installed 3 m to 4.5m (10 to 15 ft) apart across the arm or bridge. The karabiners or slings should be installed at a convenient height so that the shock absorber from the body harness can be attached to the rope that is strung through them.

  4. Tie the other end of the rope to the shaft or other end of the bridge or string it across to the other arm when required .See Fig 4.

  5. Attach a karabiner to the shock absorber on the back “D” of the body harness.

  6. Connect the karabiner to the rope when moving horizontally across the arm.

  7. Belt to a tower member when relocating the karabiner around the members or the karabiners holding the rope.

  8. Only one person at a time may use the rope for fall protection. Other persons line maintainers must be belted to the tower members when the rope is in use.

 

Figure 4: Using a Temporary Restraint Rope

 

Outboard Movement On The Tower

Retractable Lanyard

  1. Connect the retractable lanyard housing to the tower and move along the arm in the same manner as outlined in Section 3.2.

  2. When a suspension platform is required for access, install a sling and karabiner at the end of the arm and feed the lanyard through the karabiner before proceeding out on the platform. See Figure 5.

  3. By changing the direction with a karabiner, the retractable lanyard can also be used when descending to the de-energized conductor level on a rope ladder.

Figure 5: Using a retractable Lanyard When accessing the Work Location on a Suspension Platform

Rope Anchored above the Work Location

  1. Install the anchor hook and rope in a similar manner to the method outlined in last section

  2. Now, un belt from the tower member and proceed to the work location, allowing the fall arrester to move along the rope.

  3. Lock the fall arrester onto the rope, at the work location to minimize the fall distance.

  4. Reverse this sequence when proceeding back to the arm.

  5. Another method of using a rope for outboard fall protection, is to have a second ok attach a rope to the next tower arm or shield wire peak above the work location. The transmission line maintainer below can attach himself to the rope and proceed outboard to the work location. Slack should be kept out of the rope to minimize pendulum motion should a slip occur.

Safety Summary

The following hazards and precautions are emphasized to highlight some of the safety considerations related to using temporary fall protection systems on a tower. Other hazards which are site specific or of a general nature must be identified during the tailboard conference before the work begins.

 

 

Hazards

Precautions

Body Mechanics

- Minimize the slack in all fall protection systems.

Electrical

- Observe the limits of approach when using fall protection systems.

Mechanical

- Inspect all fall protection equipment before use.

 

- Replace any component in the system that has arrested a fall.

 

- Always have a full structure length rope or hand line available

 

  as well as karabiners for possible rescue at the work location.
