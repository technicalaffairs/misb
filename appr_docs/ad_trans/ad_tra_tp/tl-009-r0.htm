Introduction

This procedure describes five different methods for lifting and attaching suspension platforms, to enable the work to be done with minimal stress on the body.

General
          The five lift methods are:
           - Manual balance and slide method. See figure 1 &2
           - Handline supported from tower above work location. See figure 3.
           - Handline supported tension from insulator string. See figure 4.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued.

  • Local temporary earth on both work sides is required.

  • Wear your safety helmet, and safety belts.

Tools and Equipment

  • Handline sheave block, Handlines.

  • Handline sheave block assemble with a loop of rope

Work to be Carried out
 

Manual Balance and Slide Method

  1. Secure handline block at a suitable position, on tower crossarm, to raise platform. See figure 1.

  2. At ground level tie handline around platform above balance point so that the conductor hook end will hang as the low point. See figure 1. Tie handline onto platform to prevent slippage.

  3. Raise Platform.

  4. Tilt platform againts crossarm upper cord (feather) and maneuver platform onto top of insulator string. Conductor hook should be pointing downward. Platform should be straddled between the tower crossarm and insulatro string. See figure 2.

  5. Slide platform along insulator string, and down tower crossarm, until conductor hook is clear of insulator string and dead end hardware.

  6. Rotate platform and hook over the conductor.

  7. Adjust and attach both chains (or nylon straps) to convenient tower member.

  8. Spread chains (or nylon straps) to stabilize platform.

Handline Supported From Tower Above Work Location

  1. Secure handline block and handline to structure above work location.

  2. At ground level, with platform right way up (conductor hook facing up), tie handline around platform near balance point, so that conductor hook end will hang as the low point. Tie handline onto platform to prevent slippage. See figure 3.

  3. Raise platform.

  4. Grab tower end of platform with one hand maneuver other end of platform to hook over conductor.

  5. Adjust and attach both chains (or nylon straps) to convenient tower member.

  6. Spread chains (or nylon straps) to stabilize platform.

      Figure 3

Handline supported From Tension Insulator String

  1. Locate a small ladder on the crossarm, close to the tendion insulator string. Tie a handline from the ladder to an anchor point, to prevent swinging. See figure 4.

  2. Install a handline block and handline on the insulatro string using a rope sling. The rope sling is to be large enough to slide along the insulatro string. Climb down the ladder and slide the handline to a suitable location along the insulator string. A suitable line tool may be used.

  3. At ground level, with platform right way up (conductor hook facing up), tie handline around platform near balance point so that conductor hook end will hang as the low point. Tie handline onto platform to prevent slippage. See figure 4.

  4. Raise platform.

  5. Step down on ladder to suitable location.

  6. Grab tower end of platform with one hand and maneuver other end of platform to hook over conductor.

  7. Adjust and attach both chains (or nylon straps) to convenient tower member.

  8. Spread chains (or nylon straps) to stabilize platform.

Safety Summary
Because of their importance, the following hazards and precautions are emphasized in this summary. Other hazards which are site specific and of a general nature must be identified during 6the tailboard conference before the work commences.

 

Electrical

apply earths to the conductor in accordance with approved earthing procedures.

Mechanical

Check the platform, all tols, rigging and equipment are in proper working condition.

Work Aloft

At one time shall the platform be subjected to a load in excess of weight of two men and necessary tools.

Back Injury

Use approved fall protection equipment.
