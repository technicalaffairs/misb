Introduction

The purpose of this procedure is to provide detailed safe method for transmission lines maintenance staff to minimize the possibility of error causing personal injury or damage.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area must be used.

    •

  • Wear safety helmet, safety shoes, safety belts, and masks

    •

  • Local earthing on both sides of work is required.

Tools and Equipment    

   Qty                                           Tools & equipment 

    1               Handline Earths (size and number of sets depending on the fault current available)      

    1               Clamp stick to install the earths.

    1               Chain hoist - capable of holding the conductor tension.

    2               Shackles - twisted or anchor - type capable of holding the conductor tension.

    2               Strain links - capable of holding the conductor tension .

    1               Conductor grip to fit the conductor

    1               Wire rop sling - capable of holding the conductor tension.

                     12 mm (1/2 in ) lashing ropes.

    1               Suspension platform to access the outer hot end yoke plate.

    1               Capstan hoist or other lifting device

Material Required

     Replacement insulators of the appropriate size.

Work to be Carried Out

 Preliminary work

  1. Obtain a work permit on the circuit.

  2. Calculate the conductor tension to determine the size and rating of the chain hoist a rigging

  3. Attach the handline to the tower leg or arm for convenience raising the equipment.

  4. Install the capstan on a leg near the base of the tower to raise the equipment.

  5. Test the conductor for isolation and apply earths in accordance with approved earthing procedures

  6. Test porcelain insulators to determine the total number of defective units that should be replacedin the strings.

  7. Install the suspension platform for the outer end of the insulator string.

 Installing the chain hoist between the Insulator hardware yoke plates 

 

To use this method, the holes in the hardware yoke plates must be able to accept the correct size and rating of shackles .As well, the chain hoist must fit between the strings to prevent damage to the insulator units.

  1. Install a shackle in the center hole of the inner end and outer end hardware yoke plates and then insert a strain link into each shackle . See Figure 1.

  2. Connect a chain hoist between the strain links and take the slack out of the chain.

  3. Lash the insulators to the chain hoist, leaving the defective units free to be removed.

  4. Replace the defective units in the insulator string.

  5. Release the tension on the hoist to place conductor tension on the string while checking that all the insulator sockets and keys are properly positioned.

  6. When the work has been completed, remove the equipment and lower it to the ground.

Figure 1: Installing the chain hoist between the Insulator hardware yoke plates

Safety Summary

The following hazards and precautions are emphasized to highlight some of the safety considerations related to this job. Other hazards which are site specific and of a general nature must be identified during the tailboard conference before the work commences.
    Hazards                       Precautions 

Electrical              - Obtain a work permit on the circuit.

                           - Test the conductor for isolation and apply temporary earths using approved
                              earthing procedures

Mechanical         - Carefully calculate the conductor tension to check the loading
                             on the chain hoist & the rigging

                           - Check that all the insulator sockets and keys are properly positioned.

Work Aloft            - Use approved fall protection equipment.

Communication   - Good verbal communication is required of all crew members.
