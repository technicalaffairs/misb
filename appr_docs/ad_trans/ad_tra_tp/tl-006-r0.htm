Introduction

The purpose of this procedure is to provide detailed safe method for transmission lines maintenance staff to minimize the possibility of error causing personal injury or damage.

Safety Precautions

  • A Work Permit must be issued.

  • Local earthing on both sides of work is required.

  • Wear Your Safety Helmet and your Safety Belts.

Work to be Carried Out

Preparation

  1. Calculate the conductor tension to determine the size of rigging required

  2. Obtain a work permit on the circuit.

  3. Attach the handline to the tower arm or leg for convenience in raising the equipment.

  4. Attach the capstan hoist to a leg near the base of the tower to raise and lower the equipment.

  5. Test the conductor for isolation and apply earths in accordance with approved earthing procedures.

  6. Install the suspension platform by placing the hook over the conductor and connecting the chains to the angle steel on the bottom of arm . See Figure 1.

  7. Test porcelain insulators to determine the total number of defective units should be replaced in the string

  8. Attach a nylon or wire rope sling around the tower tension plate holding the insulators. The  sling should be protected from being cut by the steel members 

     Note:  A sling may be attached to the tension plate using a shackle if the hole diameter in the plate is large enough. 
    The sling and shackle must have a rating capable of holding the conductor tension 

  9. Install a grip on the conductor between the end span terminal and the hook of the suspension  platform.

  10. Attach the chain end of the sling and the hoist body to the grip. See Figure 1  

 

Figure 1:  Installing the Rigging

Replacing Individual units

  1. Lash the insulators to the chain hoist. Leaving the defective units free to be removed.

  2. Take up on the hoist to allow enough slack in the insulator string to disconnect the defective units

  3. Replace the defective units and remove the lashing ropes.

  4. Release the tension on the hoist checking that all the sockets and keys are properly positioned.

  5. Remove the equipment and lower it to the ground.

Replacing The Complete Insulator String

  1. -Install a second grip on the conductor and connect a snatch block to the grip. See figure 2.

  2. -Attach a second snatch block to the arm near the tension plate using a sling.

  3. -Tie a 19 mm (3/4 in ) rope to the third insulator from the lin or outer end and insert the rope through the two snatch blocks as shown in figure 2.

  4. -Lash the conductor end span terminal to the chain hoist to avoid bending the conductor near the grip.

  5. -Take up on the chain hoist to slacken the insulator string.

  6. -Attach the rope to the capstan hoist and then take up on the rope to hold the line end of the insulator string.

  7. -Disconnect the line end insulator from the conductor end span terminal.

  8. -Slacken the rope allowing the insulator string to swing below the tower plate.

  9. -Remove the rope from the snatch block that is attached to the conductor grip.

  10. - Install the insulator lifting yoke under the third insulator from the tower end.

  11. -Tie the rope to the insulator lifting yoke when enough slack is obtained in the rope as shown in figure 3 .

  12. -Take up on the rope to allow the insulator string to be disconnected from the plate.

  13. -Disconnect the insulator string from the capstan Hoist - Reverse this procedure.

  14. -Reverse this procedure to return the insulator string to its normal position.

Safety Summary

The following hazards and precautions are emphasized to highlight some of the safety considerations related to this job. 

Other hazards, which  are site specific and of a general nature must be identified during the tailboard conference before the work commences.

Hazard                                                    Precaution

Electrical                       - Obtain a work permit on the circuit.

                                    - Test for isolation and apply temporary earths using approved earthing procedures.

Mechanical                   - Calculate the conductor tension to check the loading on the rigging.

                                    - Check that all the insulator sockets and keys are properly positioned .

Work Aloft                  - Use approved fall protection.

Communication           - Good verbal communication is required of all crew members.
