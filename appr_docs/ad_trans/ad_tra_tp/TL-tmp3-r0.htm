Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection I1, which must be performed on an annually bases as indicated by Y1 in the title block.

The first draft initiated by eng. Abd El-Satar, EEHC technical inspection & quality control head sector, and from TL instructions document, this document certified by by eng. MPIS zone coordinators

Safety Precautions 

  • A work permit must be issued and the maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Maintenance staff should be well trained, qualified or directly supervised by some one who is.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear the personal protective equipment must used as safety shoes, safety helmet, earplug, and gloves pairs...etc

  • The transmission lines must be isolated and grounded on the work area.

Tools and Equipment

  • Safety and healthy equipment as safety harnesses, cotton suit,... etc

Work to be Carried out

  1. Be sure that the die corresponds to the right size

  2. Make certain that the end body and jumper terminal bores are thoroughly clean

  3. Be sure that the conductor end is not damaged if it is cut off the damaged portion

  4. Clean thoroughly all strands of  conductor portion having to be compressed. This is particularly important and is to  be made by unwinding the various wire layers, especially in case of greassed or old and in the first case a suitable solvent may be used in the latter case use emery cloth or  metallic brush having steel wires 2 to 3/10 of mm long

  5. Put a reference mark on the conductor at a distance equal to the length of dead end body bore, then insert the conductor into the dead end and slip it in thoroughly (fig. 17-a) making sure that above mark is reached

  6. Compress the dead end starting from the reference close to the lug (fig. 17-b) and continue compressions toward the conductor overlapping one half of each compression being sure every time that the two half dies barrel together

  7. Clean accurately as mentioned at point 4 all wires of the end of conductor having to be put into the jumper terminal

  8. Insert the conductor thoroughly into the jumper terminal then start compressing from lug side (fig. 17-c) going on according to previous instructions

  9. Clean perfectly the contact surfaces of dead end lug and of jumper terminal abrade them with emery - cloth or metallic brush then bolt the two parts with each other

Midspan Joints for Monometallic Conductors

  1. Be sure that the die corresponds to the right size

  2. Make certain that the joint bore is perfectly clean

  3. Be sure that the conductor ends to joint are not damaged if they are cut off the damaged portion

  4. Clean thoroughly all strands of conductor parts having to be compressed. This is particularly important and is to be made by unwinding the various strand layers especially in case of a greassed or old and blackened conductor. In the first case, a suitable solvent may be used in the latter case use emery - cloth or a metallic brush having steel wires 2 to 3/10 of mm. long

  5. Put a reference mark on each conductor at a distance equal to half the length of the joint then put the conductors into the joint making them slip up to the marks made before (fig. 18-a)

  6. Compress the joint starting from the centre (fig. 18-b) and continue compressing alternately toward one end and toward the other overlapping one half of each compression making every time sure that the two half dies barrel together
