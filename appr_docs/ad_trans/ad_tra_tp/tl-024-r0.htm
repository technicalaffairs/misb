Introduction

The purpose of this procedure is to provide detailed safe method for transmission lines maintenance staff to minimize the possibility of error causing personal injury or damage to equipment for earthing the Transmission Lines 500KV.

Safety Precautions

  • Work Permit must be issued.

  • Use approved fall arrest procedures

  • Use personal protective equipment: Flash glasses-safety helmet-safety boots - gloves

  • Ensure that the circuit is de-energized

Remember! ...“The Line is never dead until it’s been Properly Earthed !”

Tools and Equipment

Qty                         Item

1         Handline (more than 100m long )

1         Complete fall arrest equipment including retractable lifeline.

1         Clamp stick (length according to voltage rating)

3         Portable temporary earth cable min. cross section 150 mm2 (4/0) two parallel cable 75 mm2 (2/0)

1         Steel conductor cleaning brush with universal fitting

1         Insulated operating stick with universal fitting

1         Potential Tester / Voltage Indicator

1          Flash glasses for each worker on the tower

Work to be Carried Out

Installing the Earths

  1. Climb the tower using approved fall arrest procedures and maintaining the minimum limits of approach to the conductor.

  2. Install the hand line at an appropriate position near the tower shaft to maintain limits of approach while raising equipment.

  3. Test the potential indicator on the ground to ensure its reliability.

  4. Carefully raise the potential indicator on the hand line to the worker on the tower arm.

  5. Test the potential indicator again and then check the conductor for isolation verification.

  6. Lower the potential indicator to the ground on the hand line.

  7. Attach the steel conductor cleaning brush to the insulated operating stick and raise to the worker on the tower arm.

  8. Clean the conductor with the brush in the area where the conductor clamp will be installed.

  9. Clean the tower steel with the brush in the area where the flat - faced tower clamp will be installed.

  10. Raise the clamp stick and coiled earthing cables carefully on the hand line to the worker.

  11. Install the flat - faced tower clamp to the cleaned tower steel area and tighten with a screwdriver or adjustable wrench.

  12. Install the conductor clamp on the clamp stick hook and secure. Ensure that the throat of the conductor clamp is fully opened to fit over the conductor on contact.

  13. The worker must position himself directly above the conductor and install the conductor clamp quickly in one movement to avoid tearing the conductor.

  14. Tighten the conductor clamp securely and remove the clamp stick from the conductor clamp eye.

  15. Tie off any excess slack in the earthing cable to the tower steel or insulator string with a length of rope as shown in (Figure # 1). This will prevent the earthing cable from whipping and injuring workers if the circuit is accidentally energized.

  16. Complete the required earthing by applying earth cables to the other conductors of the circuit.

  17. When the earthing has been completed, you may begin your work in the area safety.

Removing the Earths

  1. When the work has been completed the supervisor in charge of the work must make sure that all men and equipment are clear of the conductor and that the workers have been informed that the earth will be removed.

  2. The earth cables must be removed in the opposite order than they were installed to avoid confusion and prevent a hazardous work area from occurring.

  3. If they were installed from the bottom conductor to the top conductor, they must be removed from the top conductor to the bottom conductor.

  4. Untie the ropes holding the excess earth cable slack for ease of removal.

  5. Loosen the conductor clamp of the first earth cable and quickly remove it from the conductor. 
    Be careful that the earth cable does not come in contact with the conductor after removing it to protect against arcing.

  6. Remove the flat - faced tower earth from the tower steel. Coil the earth and lower it carefully to the ground on the hand line.

  7. Remove the other earth cables in sequence as described.

  8. Lower all earthing tools to the ground carefully and store them in a clean, dry area.

  9. Climb down the tower using approved fall arrest methods and maintain the minimum limits of approach to the conductor.

  10. Close the Work Permit.
