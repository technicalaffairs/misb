Introduction

This procedure outlines some of the options that may be used to rescue a victim from a tower. Trades persons, who may be called upon to climb and work on towers, should be given opportunity to practice some of these methods using a dummy or mannequin. Rescue procedures should also be practiced yearly by those line crew-members who work on towers.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued.

  • Local earthing on both work sides is required.

  • Wear your safety helmet and wear your safety belts. 

Tools and Equipment

Qty                                  Tools

1     16 mm (5/8 in) nylon rescue rope long enough to reach from the victim’s location to the ground
        If  the line staff on the ground are going to control the victim’s rat of descent, the rope must be twice
        the length of the structure height. A handline may be substituted if the nylon rope is not available.

1       Anchor hook and switch sticks .

Work to be Carried Out

Using the Anchor hook And Rescue Rope

  1. Climb to a location above the victim and belt to a steel member above the structure.

  2. Wrap the rescue rope around an adequate steel member above the victim. See Figure 1.

  3. Attach the end of the rescue rope to the anchor hook and then connect the hook to one or two lengths of switch stick .

  4. Lower the anchor hook with the switch stick and insert the hook into the “D” On the back of the victim’s body harness. The hook can also be inserted around the harness shoulder straps near the “D” location.

  5. Take up the slack in the rescue rope and snub it or tie it to a steel member. See Figure 1.

  6. Cut or disconnect the victim’s fall protection rope or his lanyard. Watch that the ends of the switch sticks do not hit anyone when the rescue rope takes the victim’s weight. If the victim must be tagged during his descent, tie a knot above the fall arrester on the victim’s own fall protection rope so that it can be used to steer the victim around any abstacles below.

  7. Loosen the rope snub or untie the rope from the steel member and lower the victim to the ground by paying out the rope to control his descent. Care must be taken when lowering the victim to ensure that the rescue rope does not walk along the steel and become jammed at a joint in the tower members.

  8. Rescue ropes that have been used for practice must not be used as fall protection ropes again since the strands may have been damaged from the friction generated during the lowering operation.

 Using a Fall Arrester on the Victim’s Fall Protection

  1. Climb to a location above the victim and belt to a steel member of the structure.

  2. Wrap the rescue rope around an adequate steel member above the victim.

  3. Attach a fall arrester to the end of the rescue rope.

  4. Install the fall arrester that is now attached to the rescue rope upside down on the victim’s own fall protection rope. 
    The fall arrester must be installed upside down to ensure that it holds on the victim’s fall protection rope. See Figure 2.

  5. Push the arrester as far down on the victim’s fall protection rope as possible.

  1. Take up on the rescue rope to hold the weight of the victim.

  2. Snub or tie the rescue rope around a steel member to keep the rope tight.

  3. Cut the victim’s own fall protection rope above the fall arrester that was installed on the rope.

  4. Tie a knot in the fall protection rope above the fall arrester to ensure that the rope does not slide through the arrester.

  5. Loosen the snub in the rescue rope or untie it from the steel member and lower the victim to the ground by paying out the rescue rope to control his descent .

 Using the Hand line

  1. Climb to a location above the victim and belt to a steel member of the structure.

  2. Attach the hand line block to a steel member above the victim and split the handling.

  3. Wrap the snap end or the hook end of the hand line around an adequate steel member above the victim.

  4. Connect the snap or the hand line hook to the back “D” of the victim’s body harness. See Figure 3.

  5. Take the slack out of the hand line and snub or tie it to a steel member.

  6. Cut or disconnect the victim’s fall protection rope or lanyard.

  7. Loosen the snubbing hitch or untie the hand line from the steel and lower the victim by paying out the rope to control his descent. If the open hook was attached to the harness, the victim will have to be carefully controlled to prevent the hook from coming out of the “D” on the back of the victim’s body harness.

Safety Summary

The following hazards and precautions are emphasized to highlight some of the safety considerations related to tower rescue. Other hazards, which are, site specific or of a general nature must be identified during the tailboard conference before the work begins.

Hazards Precautions

Body Mechanics:

  • Use fall protection equipment when accessing the victim.

  • Prevent slack in the rescue rope and carefully control the descent of the victim.

Electrical:

  • Observe the limit of approach when using fall protection and rescue ropes near energized equipment.

  • When planning a rescue on lines with de-energized circuits, ground the phase conductor below the rescue location before lowering the victim.

Air:    Administer immediate resuscitation to a victim who is not breathing.

Mechanical:       Replace any component in the system that has arrested a fall.
