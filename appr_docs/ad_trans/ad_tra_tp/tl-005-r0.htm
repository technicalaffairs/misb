Introduction 

The purpose of this procedure is to provide detailed safe method for transmission lines maintenance staff to minimize the possibility of error causing personal injury or damage to the transmission line components

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued on the circuit.

  • Local earthing on both sides of work is required.

  • Wear your safety Helmet.

  • Wear your safety Belts.

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Connect the capstan hoist to a leg of the tower to aid in raising and lowering the equipment.

  2. Attach a handline to a convenient location on the tower in order to raise the equipment.

  3. Test for isolation and apply earths in accordance with approved earthing procedures.

  4. Test porcelain insulators to determine the total number that must be changed.

  5. Attach a nylon or wire rope sling to the end of the arm near the insulator suspension point and then connect the hook on the chain end of the hoist to the sling. See Figure 1.

  6. Attach a rope ladder to the bottom of the arm to access the conductor. A taut line hitch connected between the tower arm and the cross-over lowering link of the harness can also be used to access the conductor .

  7. Connect the hook on the body of the hoist to the conductor using a nylon sling or conductor lifting shoe.

  8. While keeping any slack chain in the hoist from binding in the tensioned chain, raise the conductor to slacken the insulator string.

Replacing Insulators near the bottom of the string

  1. Lash the insulators to the chain hoist below the units requiring replacement. Disconnect the string between the defective insulators and the units lashed to the chain hoist .

  2. Raise the replacement insulators in a tool bag and replace the defective units.

  3. Reconnect the insulators, checking for proper positioning of the sockets and keys , and then slacken the tension on the chain hoist.

Replacing Complete Insulator String

  1. Attach a snatch block to the tower arm near the insulator suspension point and insert the

  2. 19 mm (3/4 in ) rope through the block .

  3. Tie the end of the rope to the insulator lifting yoke and then place the yoke under the third insulator from the top of the string as shown in Figure 1 .

  4. If a separate rope and insulator lifting yoke are not available, a handline may be attached directly to the insulators .

  5. Raise the insulator string using the rope or handline attached to the capstan hoist.

  6. When the insulator string has been slackend, disconnect the top end of the string from the tower .

  7. Lower the insulator string to the ground and replace the defective insulator units or the complete string .

  8. Reverse this procedure to reconnect the insulator string.

Safety Summary

The following hazards and precautions are emphasized to highlight some of the safety considerations related to this job. Other hazards which are site specific and of a general nature must be identified during the tailboard conference before the work commences.

Hazards                                         Precaution

Electrical                - Obtain a work permit on the circuit .

                               - Test for isolation and apply earths using approved earthing procedures.

                               - Observe the limits of approach to adjacent energized circuits.

Mechanical             - Calculate the weight of the conductor to check the loading on the rigging.

                               - Check that all insulator sockets and keys are properly positioned.

                               - Keep any slack in the hoist from binding in the tensioned chain.

Work Aloft            - Use approved fall protection .

Communications    - Good verbal communications are required of all crew members.
