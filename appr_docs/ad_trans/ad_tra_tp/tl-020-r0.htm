Introduction

The purpose of this procedure is to provide detailed safe method for transmission lines maintenance staff to minimize the possibility of error causing personal injury or damage to equipment to measure the Earthing resistance using Megger.

EEC TL engineers in head office and zones using their background field experience and EEC documentation to create this procedure.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued.

  • Inspect the used cables before use.

  • Wear safety shoes and rubber gloves.

  • Don’t touch any bare conductor during measuring operation.

Tools and Equipment 

  • Megger Earth tester (crank type).

  • Two probes.

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Put a jumper between the earth lead and the tower leg then separate the earth lead from the tower leg .

  2. Release the jumper from the tower.

  3. Connect the system as in figure no. (1) by putting the two probes and the earth at a straight line perpendicular to the direction of the line to prevent any error due to induction

    4. Note:   In case of angle tower put the propes in the line that bisects the out side angle.  

                 If it is not possible we can put them on the line that bisect the inside angle.

  4. Adjust the pointer of the megger to the zero point

  5. Running fastly the megger crank by hand.

  6. Read and record the value of the resistance from the scale.

  7. Repeat the measuring two times to ensure correct measuring.

  8. Connect a jumper a cross the tower leg and earth lead then connect the earth lead to the

           tower then disconnect the jumper from the two sides.

    N.B    The tower Earthing resistance must be less than or equal to 0.5 W .
