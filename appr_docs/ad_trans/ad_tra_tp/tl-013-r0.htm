Introduction

The purpose of this procedure is to provide detailed instructions on a safe method for earthing the transmission lines 66 to 500kv.

Safety Precautions

  • Obtain an authorized Work Permit

  • Use approved fall arrest procedures

  • Use personal protective equipment: (Flash glasses, Safety helmet, Safety boots, Gloves)

Remember! ......"The Line is never dead until it's been Properly Earthed !"

Tools and Equipment

(1)     Handline

(1)     Complete fall arrest equipment including retractable life line.

(1)     Clamp stick (length according to voltage rating).

(3)     Earthing cables rated for system voltage (see table # 1).

Work To Be Carried Out

Installing the Earths

  1. Climb the tower using approved fall arrest procedures and maintaining the minimum limits of approach to the conductor. (See Table # 2)

  2. Install the handline at an appropriate position near the tower shaft to maintain limits of approach while raising equipment.

  3. Test the potential indicator on the ground to ensure its reliability

  4. Carefully raise the potential indicator on the handline to the worker on the tower arm.

  5. Test the potential indicator again and then check the conductor for isolation verification.

  6. Lower the potential indicator to the ground on the handline.

  7. Attach the steel conductor cleaning brush to the insulated operating stick and raise to the worker on the tower arm.

  8. Clean the conductor with the brush in the area where the conductor clamp will be installed.

  9. Clean the tower steel with the brush in the area where the flat - faced tower clamp will be installed.

  10. Raise the clamp stick and coiled earthing cables carefully on the handlineto the worker.

  11. Install the flat - faced tower clamp to the cleaned tower steel area and tighten with a screwdriver or adjustable wrench.

  12. Install the conductor clamp on the clamp stick hook and secure. Ensure that the throat of the conductor clamp is fully opened to fit over the conductor on contact.

  13. The worker must position himself directly above the conductor and install the conductor clamp quickly in one movement to avoid teasing the conductor.

  14. Tighten the conductor clamp securely and remove the clamp stick from the conductor clamp eye.

  15. Tie off any excess slack in the earthing cable to the tower steel or insulator string with a length of rope as shown in (Figure # 1). This will prevent the earthing cable from whipping and injuring workers if the circuit is accidentally energized.

  16. Complete the required earthing by applying earth cables to the other conductors of the circuit.

  17. When the earthing has been completed, you may begin your work in the area safely.


 Removing the Earths

  1. When the work has been completed the supervisor in charge of the work must make sure that all men and equipment are clear of the conductor and that the workers have been informed that the earths will be removed.

  2. The earth cables must be removed in the opposite order than they were installed to avoid confusion and prevent a hazardous work area from occurring. If they were installed from the bottom conductor to the top conductor, they must be removed from the top conductor to the bottom conductor.

  3. Untie the ropes holding the excess earth cable slack for ease of removal.

  4. Loosen the conductor clamp of the first earth cable and quickly remove it from the conductor. Be careful that the earth cable does not come in contact with the conductor after removing it to protect against arcing.

  5. Remove the flat - faced tower earth from the tower steel. Coil the earth and lower it carefully to the ground on the handline.

  6. Remove the other earth cables in sequence as described.

  7. Lower all earthing tools to the ground carefully and store them in a clean, dry area.

  8. Climb down the tower using approved fall arrest methods and maintaining the minimum limits of approach to the conductor.

  9. Close the Work Permit.

Special Earthing procedures for tension towers

    Installation of portable earthing cables on tension towers follow the same procedures as outlined previously in this document except when the following circumstances are encountered or maintenance work is being performed.

Terminal Point of the line section or conductor loop removed

    If portable earths must be installed on a terminal point tower or a tower where the conductor loop has been removed, the portable earth will have to installed on the conductor, outside or "down the line" from the tension insulator assembly.

    This installation method requires that an all - angle conductor clamp be installed with the clamp stick in the horizontal position and parallel to the tension insulator assembly. The aid of an additional insulating stick or handline to support the weight of the clamp stick and earthing cable may be required depending on the overall length of the tension insulator assembly. (See Figure # 2 below)

installing and removing a conductor loop terminal from a conductor line terminal

When a conductor loop terminal is to be installed or removed from a conductor line terminal, special earthing precautions must be taken to ensure that all voltage and current potential differences are eliminated between the all conductor terminals and the line worker. Before removing the conductor loop terminal ensure that a temporary jumper is installed across the connection as shown in (Figure #

The jumper will provide earthing continuity and protect the worker from electrical injury when he is separating the connection by hand. If the conductor loop is to remain disconnected from the conductor line, the earth jumper will then have to be removed from the disconnection point by the use of an insulated clamp stick.

Parallel groove loop clamp maintenance

    Maintenance on parallel groove clamps may include either cleaning and tightening, replacement or installation of a compression joint if required. During any of these procedures the clamp will have to be opened. Temporary earth cables that are connected together at a common point such as the same tower arm must be installed on either side of the clamp before disconnecting it to provide earthing continuity and there by eliminating the possibility of a potential difference across the opened clamp.

Earthing procedures on 500KV systems

    The procedure steps for installing temporary earths on 500KV are the same as outlined previously in this document but because the temporary earthing cables are much longer and heavier, special installation methods may have to be used. Supporting the insulated clamp stick by use of another clamp stick from below or handline from above such as shown in (Figure # 2) are good options as long as the minimum limits of approach from the conductor are maintained.
