Procedure:  
Replacement of 500KV Suspension Insulator String Using Bulldozer Russian Equipment		 Approved Date:    22 June 1998
Date to be Reviewed:  June 2003

Introduction

The purpose of this procedure is to provide detailed safe method for transmission lines maintenance staff to minimize the possibility of error causing personal injury or damage to equipment for replacement of 500KV-suspension Insulator string.

EEHC-TL engineers in head office and zones using their background field experience and EEHC documentation to create this procedure.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued.

  • Ensure isolation using a potential indicator, then connect local earthings at the work location refer to MPIS no. LI-TL-013-r0

  • Use approved fall-arrest procedures as well as personal protective equipment.

  • Approved safety wire rope cables must be used, and the tractors winch cables must be frequently checked.

  • All equipment used must be checked and approved prior to work.

  • Workers uniform must include a color indicating the disconnected circuit.

Tools and Equipment

  • A bulldozer or a tractor equipped with a winch at the back.

  • Two 6 Ton Russian pulleys Fig.(1)

  • An 80 meter long winch cable (16 mm in diameter), for raising and lowering equipment

  • Two 7.5 ton tower leg snatch block together with its specific wire rope sling (5m. in length, 22 mm in diameter).

  • Two 80m long hand line (18 mm in diameter) together with an aluminum pulley for raising and lowering equipment.

  • Seven 6 ton shackles.

  • Tool bag.

  • Insulating gloves capable of insulating at least 20 KV.

  • Approved fall arrest and personal safety equipment

  • Temporary earthing set and Potential indicator.

Work to be Carried Out

  1. The line must be isolated according to EEHC rules.

  2. A worker must climb up the tower to ensure isolation and apply earthing on the three phases of towers No 1 and No 3, given that the work would take place at tower no. (2). The middle and side phases must be earthed directly before they reach the ground in order to be discharged.

  3. Two climbing technicians must climb the tower up to the conductor where the work is taking place and install two hand lines. One at the middle phase and the other at the side one .Two 6 ton Russian blocks must also be raised, one is to be fixed above the middle phase plate while the other must be fixed above the side phase plate. Another snatch block must also be attached to the tower leg.

  4. The bulldozer winch cable (80m. in length, 16mm in diameter) must be raised, through the tower leg snatch block and the middle phase Russian block, and attached to the L joint of the middle phase suspension insulator assembly.

  5. Using the bulldozer winch cable, the middle insulator string must be slackened, then the L joint pin must be released, and both the string and the conductor lowered to the ground given that they must be earthed immediately before they reach the ground as previously mentioned. Then release the bulldozer winch cable.

  6. As for the side phase, the bulldozer winch cable must be raised, through the tower leg snatch block and the side phase Russian block, and attached the joint of the side phase insulator assembly as illustrate in Fig.(2).

  7. The side phase is similarly lowered and earthed before reaching the ground.

  8. The insulator string is then replaced by a new one and all cotter keys must be checked.

  9. The bulldozer winch cable must be attached to the new insulator string and the side phase is then raised .The temporary earthing is disconnected when it becomes at a distance of about one meter above the ground.

  10. The new string Fig.(3) is then raised up the tower to be fixed at the cross arm replacing the other insulator string.

  11. The middle phase is similarly raised, its earthing disconnected at a distance of approximately one meter above the ground and fixed once more at its initial position.

  12. The work location must be cleared from all workers and equipment.

  13. Remove all the temporary earthing .

  14. The work permit must be completed and the control unit informed that the circuit is clear of workers, equipment, and the circuit is ready for reconnection.

N.B:  If the insulator string of the middle phase only is to be changed, only the middle phase is lowered as the tower still maintains its balance.
