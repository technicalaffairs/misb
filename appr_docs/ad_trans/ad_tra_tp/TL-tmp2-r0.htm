Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection I1, which must be performed on an annually bases as indicated by Y1 in the title block.

The first draft initiated by eng. Abd El-Satar, EEHC technical inspection & quality control head sector, and from TL instructions document, this document certified by by eng. MPIS zone coordinators

Safety Precautions 

  • A work permit must be issued and the maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Maintenance staff should be well trained, qualified or directly supervised by some one who is.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear the personal protective equipment must used as safety shoes, safety helmet, earplug, and gloves pairs...etc

  • The transmission lines must be isolated and grounded on the work area.

Tools and Equipment

  • Safety and healthy equipment as safety harnesses, cotton suit,... etc

Work to be Carried out

  1. Make certain that the dies for aluminium and steel correspond to the prescribed sizes

  2. Check whether the bores of the aluminium and of the steel joint are thoroughly clean

  3. Be sure that the conductor end is not damaged if so, cut off the damaged portion

  4. Slip the aluminium joint over the conductor for about one meter

  5. Serve the conductor (with metallic wire, or suitable tape) at about 3 cm. off the end, and with a hacksaw cut off the aluminium mantle wires near the serving (fig. 8.a) taking care not to nick steel core wires

  6. Serve the core end (fig. 9-b)

  7. Serve the conductor mantle at a distance from the core end equal to half the steel joint length plus 25%

  8. Cut off the aluminium wires just in front of the last serving (fig. 9-c) always taking care not to nick the core wires. To avoid this saw the outer layers of the mantle and cut only for about 2/3 of diameter the  aluminium wires of the inner layer which are then detached by a repeated bending movement

  9. Put a reference mark on the core (now spilled of aluminium wires) at a distance equal to half the steel joint length. Put the core into the steel joint as much as 2 cm, making the serving slipl then remove serving and push the core in up to the reference mark made before. To practically carry out this operation hold the joine by pressing it into the die fitted on compressor

  10. Repeat operations mentioned at poines 5,6,7,8,9) on the end of the other corductor to be jointed and be sure that the steel joint is in the position as indicated on fig. 9-d

  11.  Compress the steel joint starting from the centre (fig. 9-d) working first toward one and then toward the other end overlapping one half of each compression and making sure every time that the two half dies barrel together

  12. Clean thoroughly all alhaminium wires of  conductor parts to be compressed. This is particulary important and shall be made by winding the various wire layers, especially if conductor is greassed or old and blackened in the first case a suitable can be used, in the second case use an emery cloth or a metallic brush with 2 to 3/10  mm long wires

  13. Starting from the centre of the steel joint already compressed put a reference mark on each conductor at a distance eequal to half the aluminium joint length slip the latter over the steel joint removing obviously the serving and stop at the reference marks thus being sure that the aluminium joint centre corresponds to that the steel joint (fig. 9-a)

  14. Compress the joint starting from the marks on the aluminium sleeve (fig 9-e) and work as per point 11
    Assembly of joints with filter marked on the tables with cross (+) is carried out as above mentioned with the change of the following points
    Put one conductor into one filter and into the joint making it slip for about one metre put the other conductor into the second filter

  15.  Put the two filters into the joint then compress the latter starting from the marks on the aluminium sleeve (fig. 6-a) and continuing the compression as mentioned at point 11

  16. Dead ends joints and repair sleeves for monometallic conductors aluminium or aluminium alloy

  17. Compression filttings for aluminium or aluminium alloy conductors are generally similar to those used for aluminium / steel conductors

  18. However the steel terminals have not the tubular compression part as the conductors have no steel core Aluminium body can be supplied (up on request) already compressed onto the steel terminal in this case the position of the steel terminal must be stated

  19. Joints have not, obviously the steel inner part and repair sleeves are identical to those supplied for aluminium steel conductors
