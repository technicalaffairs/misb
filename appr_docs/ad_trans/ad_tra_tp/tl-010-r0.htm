Introduction

The purpose of this procedure is to provide detailed safe method for transmission lines maintenance staff to minimize the possibility of error causing personal injury or damage.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued.

  • Local earthing on both work sides is required.

  • Wear your safety helmet, safety belts.

Tools and Equipment 

    Qty                                               Tools & Equip.

     1                         Transmission line maintainers’ full body harness .

    1                         Anchor hook .

    2                         Lengths of switch to attach to the anchor hook .

    1                         16 mm (5/8 in ) structure length nylon rope with a green tracer -

                                Ontario Hydro Central Stores number - 362-A0136.

                                                              OR

    1                         16 mm x 8 m (5/8 in x 26 ft) of the same nylon rope .

    1                          Shock absorber to attach to the back “D” of the body harness .

    1                          Fall arrester to connect the shock absorber to the rope - the current

                                approved model is the RTC 2300 .

    1                          Container for the rope .

 

Work to be Carried out

Assembly and Use Of Temporary Fall Protection Using the Nylon Rope

  1. Cut or select the length of rope to be used . When more than one person must climb the tower, a full structure length rope will likely be more convenient as the additional people may then use the same rope . If only one person is needed or if tower inspection is taking place, a shorter length of rope may be more convenient. When an 8m (26 ft) piece of rope is used, a knot must be tied at the bottom end of the rope to prevent the fall arrester from sliding off.

  2. Attach one end of the rope to a small karabiner using an eye splice or a bowline knot.tape the tail of the bowline to the standing rope if this is the method of attachment.

  3. Connect the karabiner, with the rope attached, to the hole in the anchor hook. The karabiner should be connected from the backside of the anchor hook so that it will not interfere when installing the hook on the tower 

  4. Put on the full body harness and attach a shock absorber to the back “D” ring of the harness. Use a small karabiner to attach the shock absorber if it does not have its own method of Attachment .

  5. Connect the fall arrester to the shock absorber using a small karabiner.

  6. Attach the tow lengths of switch together and connect them to the anchor hookm see figure1.

  7. Raise the anchor hook , with the rope attached , and install it around a horizontal or diagonal structure member approximately 3 m (10 ft) above your position .

  8. When installing the anchor hook , attach it around the larger members and where possible ,install the hook at the intersection of the steel members . Always check that the hook is well secured around the member . The anchor hook may be installed on ladder rungs but do not attach it to step bolts .

 

                               

Figure 1: Climbing a Tower Using the Rope Method of Temporary Fall Protection

  1.  Climb the tower diagonals, ladder or step bolts to a level near the anchor hook.

  2.  While maintaining good footing, belt to a tower member or ladder using a pole strap .

  3.  Remove the anchor hook and relocate it to a suitable member within reach of the switch sticks, see figure 1.

  4. Un belt from the tower member or ladder and climb to the anchor hook location.

  5.  Repeat this sequence until the work location is reach.

  6.  When a full length of rope is used , the first power line maintainer may require assistance in raising it to the work location. When help is required , once past the midway mark, secure the anchor hook and lrt a second person climb up to the midway mark while his fall arrester is attached to the rope .

    NOTE

    Only one person must use the rope for fall protection at any given time. Other power line  maintainers must be belted to the structure while someone else is using the rope for fall protection.

  7.  Once the second person is belted in near the midway point , he can hold the weight and  feed the rope while the first power line maintainer continues to relocate the anchor hook and climb to the work location .

  8.  If additional power line maintainers are required work on the structure , they may climb , one at a time , using their fall arrester on the rope already installed .

  9. When the work is completed , each person descends in turn while his fall arrester is attached to the rope.

  10. When everyone is off thw structure , the last power line maintainer descends to a location where he can still reach the bottom of the switch stick.

  11. Belt to the structure and remove the anchor hook from the member and then relocate it to a member at approximately waist height .

  12. Unbelt from the structure and descend to a location where the bottom of the switch sticks can still be reached and relocate the anchor hook again .

  13. This sequence is repeated to the base of the structure.

 

Safety Summary

The following hazards and precautions are emphasized to highlight sum of the safety

considerations related to climbing a structure . Other hazards which are site specific or of

a general nature must be identified during the tailboard conference before the work begins .

 

Hazards                                 Precautions

Body Mechanics           - do not over reach when installing the anchor hook.

                                        The weight of the anchor hook and rope could

                                       overbalance the sticks .

 

Electrical                        - Observe the limits of approach to energized

                                        conductors when using the nylon rope for

                                        temporary fall protection .

 

Mechanical                     - Inspect all fall protection equipment before use.

                                      - Replace any component in the system that has

                                        arrested a fall .

                                       - Always have a full structure length rope or

                                         handline as well as karabiners available for possible

                                          rescue at the work location .
