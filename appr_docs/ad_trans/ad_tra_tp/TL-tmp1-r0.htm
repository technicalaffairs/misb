Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection I1, which must be performed on an annually bases as indicated by Y1 in the title block.

The first draft initiated by eng. Abd El-Satar, EEHC technical inspection & quality control head sector, and from TL instructions document, this document certified by by eng. MPIS zone coordinators

Safety Precautions 

  • A work permit must be issued and the maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Maintenance staff should be well trained, qualified or directly supervised by some one who is.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear the personal protective equipment must used as safety shoes, safety helmet, earplug, and gloves pairs...etc

  • The transmission lines must be isolated and grounded on the work area.

Tools and Equipment

  • Safety and healthy equipment as safety harnesses, cotton suit,... etc

Work to be Carried out

  1. Make certain that the dies for aluminum and steel correspond to the prescribed sizes

  2. Check whether the bores of the individual elements dead end body, steel terminal and jumper terminal are thoroughly

  3. Be sure that the conductor end is not damaged if so, cut off the damaged portion

  4. Insert the conductor into the dead end and slip it over the conductor for one meter approx

  5. Serve the conductor (with metallic wire, or suitable tape) at about 3 cm. off the end, and with a hacksaw cut off the aluminum mantle wires near the serving (figure. 8.a) taking care not to nick steel core wires

  6. Serve the steel core end (figure. 8-b)

  7. Serve the conductor mantle at a distance from the core end equal to the depth of the eye end liole plus 25%

  8. Cut off the aluminum wires just in front of the last serving (figure 8-c) always taking care not to nick the core wires. To avoid such nicking, saw the outer layers of mantle cut only for 2/3 of diameter the inner layer of aluminum wires which will be detached by a repeated bending movement

  9. Mark the core (new undressed from aluminum wires) at a distance equal to the depth of the eye end bore, insert the core 2 cm into the steel terminal and remove serving put off the serving then by means of a plier and insert thoroughly the core making sure that it has entered up to the mark made before to carry out easily this operation, keep hold of the steel terminal by pressing it slightly between the dies fitted on the compressor

  10. Compress the cylindrical part of the steel terminal starting 8-d) and continue the compressions towards the conductor overlapping one half of each compression and making sure every time that the two half dies barrel together

  11. Clean thoroughly all aluminum wires of the conductor portion that has to be compressed. This is particularly important and is to be made by winding the various wire layers in case of greased or old and blackened conductor in the first case a suitable solvent may be used in the latter case use an emery cloth or metallic brush having steel wires 2 to 3/10 mm long

  12. Slip the dead end body over the eye end, taking off obviously the serving, until the polygonal part of come is thoroughly inserted in the corresponding dead end seat (fig. 8-e) ensure the right orientation of the steel terminal in respect to the position of the lug  

  13. Compress the first portion of the dead end, namely that on the steel terminal side (fig. 8-f) starting from the reference line marked on purpose overlapping one half of each compression as written at point 10

  14. Compress the second portion of the dead end starting from the reference mark and continue compressions toward the conductor (fig. 8-g) proceeding as written at points 10 and 13

  15. Clean very accurately as mentioned at point 11 all aluminum wires of the conductor end having to be inserted into the jumper terminal

  16. Insert thoroughly the conductor into the jumper terminal then start compressions from lug side (figure 8-h) and proceed as previously described

  17. Clean perfectly the contact surfaces of dead end lug and of jumper terminal by abrading with emery-cloth or metallic brush and finally bolt the two parts with each ether.
