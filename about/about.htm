Organization            Document Development Process

What is the MPIS?

A process designed to provide EEHC with a systematic approach to documenting and implementing proper maintenance and repair procedures throughout the utility (network).

MPIS Goal Statement

Provide EEHC Network Operations staff with a sustained capability to perform their maintenance activities safely, effectively and consistently in a timely and efficient manner, through the development of concise, accurate technical information delivered via state of the art, user friendly paper and electronic systems.

Maintenance Policy

Specifies for each piece of equipment:

  • What maintenance activities are required,

  • How often each activity is to be carried out,

  • What equipment condition may trigger additional maintenance,

  • Which materials are suitable for the task, which cannot be used and why.

Maintenance Policy is usually established on the bases of:

  • Through analysis of maintenance,

  • Corporate reliability standards,

  • Saving due to reduced equipment breakdown,

  • Value of reliable service to customers.

MPIS Products
Procedures, detailed step by step instruction on how to perform tasks, including safety precautions, resources required to perform the procedures and information regarding environmental sensitivities.

Bulletins, information about system/equipment problems, this is a type of warning system to exchange information regarding equipment problems or safety hazards experienced among zones.

Directives, specify what maintenance activities are required, when and at what frequency, directives provide consistency as to when and how often procedures are performed on special types of equipment throughout.
Maintenance Procedures                "How to do the job"

                    * Tell maintenance staff        
                        - Why the work is needed,
                        - Exactly how each step should be carried out,
                        - What safety hazards exist,
                        - What tools and test equipment,
                        - How to use the tools and test equipment.

                    * May contain photographs, sketches, and engineering illustrations.

* Based on manufacturer's manuals but add valuable information specific to electric utility that is not available  in the manufacturer's manual

Maintenance Job Check Lists         "What must be done"
                - Are used by trained staff who have done the work before,
                - They provide a method of monitoring the completion of each maintenance activity.
                - Specify the order of steps to be carried out,
                - Contain safety and may be environmental notes,
                - Contain space for recording readings taken, observations and problems encountered in carrying out the maintenance activity.
