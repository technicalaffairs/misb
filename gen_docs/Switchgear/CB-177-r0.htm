Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection check list I2, which must be carried out on an annually bases as indicated by Y1 in the title block.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear safety helmet, shoes, breathing mask, safety glasses.

  • The circuit breaker must be isolated completely and properly earthed from both sides,

  • DC control power should be disconnected.

  • Before using a blower to clean the control cabinet or when cleaning the insulators, wear breathing mask to protect your lung tissues from the dust particles.

  • Don't burn the cloth used in cleaning, collect it and send it to the dump.

Tools and Equipment

  • Safety helmet, shoes, breathing mask, safety glasses.

  • Hydraulic test gauges, hydraulic oil, and SF6 gas if needed,

  • SF6 test gauge, micro ohmmeter, air blower, brush, and rags

  • Dew point equipment, acidity equipment, air content equipment, and timing equipment

Work to be Carried Out 

Operating Mechanism

  1. Check and clean the surface of the internal device with a dry brush or rags .

  2. Check for distorted or oxidized springs and replace the damaged ones.

  3. Check for locking rings out of place and refit in their housing if needed.

  4. Check for loose nuts or screws and tighten if needed.

  5. Check for wires and relative fasteners detachment and replace if needed or reconnect the detached wires correctly .

Medium Voltage Part

  1. Check and clean the surface of  the insulating parts with a dry brush or rags.

  2. Check for locking rings out of place and refit in their housing if needed.

  3. Check for loose nuts or screws and tighten if needed.

  4. Check for distortion or cracking of the insulating parts and ask ABB-SACE for replacement of the damaged parts if needed.

  5. Check for oxidized isolating contacts (only for draw out C.B.) and clean with a rough rag soaked in suitable solvent and lubricate moderately with neutral grease.

  6. Check  for traces of over heating or loose screws on the connection to the C.B. terminal.(only for fixed version circuit – breaker), clean the connections and breaker terminals with a rough rag soaked in suitable solvent cover with neutral grease and tighten the screw.

Earthing Isolating Contact (only for draw – out Circuit Breaker when fitted)

          Check for traces of oxidation and clean with a rough rag soaked in suitable solvent and lubricate moderately with neutral grease.

Earthing Connection

  1. Check for traces of oxidation and/or loose nut (only for fixed version circuit breaker) and clean with a rough rag soaked in a suitable solvent

  2. Tighten the earthling connection fully and cover it with neutral grease.

Auxiliary circuit supply Voltage

  1. Check the supply voltage of the operating mechanism electrical accessories. The releases and the electrical interlocking devices must work correctly for values between 85% and 110% of the relative rated voltage. 

Operating and Control Elements

  1. Carry out the working tests.

  2. Replace the damaged or faulty elements (if necessary, ask ABB SACE)

Gas Pressure

Checking the Gas Pressure

Note: The following procedure is valid for HA1 – HA2 circuit breakers only. For HA3 circuit breaker, operations of gas filling pressure checking and its eventual reset are not required.

  1. Check that the circuit – breaker is out of service (both primary and secondary circuits isolated).

  2. Make sure that all the circuit – breaker parts are at ambient temperature.

  3. Remove the protection cover (2) and the plug (1) from the gas filling value (see fig. 3)

  4. Apply the gas pressure checking device (see fig. 4) to the gas filling value and measure the pressure.

  5. The correct pressure value is 500KPa (absolute value) at an ambient temperature of 20 ْ C.

  6. For different ambient values, use the correction curve shown in fig. 1.

  7. Pressure values between 400 and 500KPa (absolute value at an ambient temperature of 20 ْ C) do not jeopardize the correct operation of the breaker.

  8. Pressure reset must only be carried out if the pressure value drops below 400KPa (absolute value).

  9. This limit also corresponds to the operating threshold of the pressure switch (if fitted)

 

In case of a completely discharged circuit – breaker (relative pressure zero) it must be sent back to ABB SACE for repair.

Filling with Gas

  1. To carry out gas filling, it is necessary to have a SF6 gas cylinder complete with pressure reducer as well as pressure checking and adjustment device which must be adjusted to a value of 500KPa (absolute value) ( see curve in fig 1)

  2. A Teflon or stainless steel pipe, of adequate length and complete with the necessary screw – type and fast – on couplers, must be connected to the out let of the checking and adjustment device (see fig 2) the SF6 gas must comply with requirement indicated in the following paragraph.

  3. Let gas out of the cylinder into the air for at least 20 seconds in order to clear the connection pipe of air pockets.

  4. Insert the fast – on coupler in the gas filling by supplying the gas slowly.

  5. At the end of this operation, remount the plug (1) and the sealable protection cover (2) (see fig 3)

 

Requirements for the SF6 gas

The gas must comply with IEC Standards, Publication Nr.376 (Specification and acceptance of new SF6 gas) and any further revisions, as well as with the corresponding Italian CEI Standards, Publication nr.410. it must also be remembered that impurities will impair the gas characteristics and compromise the functionality of the circuit – breaker.

Lubrication

All the sliding parts subject to friction must be lubricated at the established time with 5RX MOLY grease or equivalent .
