Introduction

This document outlines the minor maintenance procedures P1, which must be performed every fifteen years or 5,000 mechanical CLOSE/OPEN operations as indicated by Y15 in the title block

Competence Requirement: 

Personnel authorized by ABB to carry out assembly, commissioning and maintenance work

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear safety and health equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, safety belts ... etc.

  • Isolate and ground both sides of CB.

  • Switch off and isolate the control circuits i.e. de-energized

  • Closing springs discharged, circuit breaker in open position and grounded.

  • There is danger of falling off the top of the CB stack when cleaning insulators or checking connections wear your safety belt.

Tools and Equipment

  • Safety and health equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, safety harnesses ... etc.

  • Voltage indicator.

  • Complete tool kit, cleaning tools.

  • Thermovision camera.

  • Electronic timer.

  • Gas filling equipment

  • Spray equipment, clothes and wrenches

  • Resistance Bridge, test current min. 200 A DC

  • Variable DC voltage source

Work to be Carried Out

Important Note:

All HPL circuit breakers are completely hose-proof, i.e. circuit breakers in operation can be cleaned with high-pressure water without any risk of water entering the circuit breaker and causing damage to essential parts.

Ocular Inspection

  1. Clean the circuit breaker insulators from salt and other contaminant deposits. The time interval for cleaning depends on the local conditions.

  2. Check external cleaning, heater element function, gas pressure, valves and drainage holes .

  3. A new or overhauled circuit breaker should be checked for tightness by reading the density monitors after about 1 month and then at 1-2 year intervals in connection with the regular ocular inspection.

Rating Plates

Make a note of the serial number.

Operation Counter

Make a note of the number of operations.

SF6 Gas

  1. Filling of gas (if necessary)

  2. Dew point at normal pressure in circuit-breaker (i.e. Dew point maximum -5 º C at rated filling pressure and + 20 º C temperature).

Insulators

  1. Visual inspection and cleaning

  2. Spray and rub the insulators clean

  3. Check cementing, bolts and nuts 

Main Current Circuit (Resistance Check)

  1. Check resistance of CB when circuit breaker only operating >100 CLOSE/OPEN operations/ year after 6-8 years or 2500 mech. CLOSE/OPEN operations.                                                                                      

Important Note:  Main circuit resistance per breaking chamber < 38µΩ.

Contact System (Opening position)

Inspection of reference holes in outer arm and mechanism housing.           

Operating Characteristics

  1. Measurement of operating times:
     

    Important Note:     Closing time < 65 ms                       Opening time coil I  20 ± 2 ms   

                                         Opening time coil II  20 ± 2 ms        Close-Open time max. 55 ms 

  2. Phase discrepancy. 
     Important Note: Max. Phase discrepancy between phases: at closing 4 ms; at opening 3 ms. Max. Discrepancy between breaks in the same phase: at closing 3 ms; at opening 2ms.

Operation Availability

  1. Pull rod system: checking of the tightening torque of nuts on the pull rods. (torque 300 Nm)

  2. Safety of release of operation:  Measurement of min. operating voltage.                     
    Closing coil: < 85% of nominal voltage.        Opening coil: < 70% of nominal voltage.

  3. Anti-pumping device: (Checking anti-pumping function) The circuit-breaker in open position, the operating mechanism charged, CLOSE and OPEN impulse given simultaneously (the close button shall be pressed). The circuit-breaker will make a CLOSE - OPEN operation and may not close when the operating mechanism is charged.

Motor Current

  1. The motor current is to be measured at the end of the charging operation.

  2. Maximum allowed motor current is 110% of value measured at routine test.

  3. The charging time is allowed to change up to 30% from value measured at routine test.

  4. The motor shall be able to change the spring at min. 85% of nominal voltage (IEC). 

Dash Pots

  1. Checking of oil-level or recording of damping during operation alternatively. Recording of damping during operation is made by means of circuit breaker analyzer with transducers connected to:          

  2. Main shaft of the circuit breaker mechanism when checking the opening dash-pot.

  3. Main shaft (cam shaft) of the opening mechanism when checking the opening dash-pot.

    Important Note: These measurements are to be carried out only by specially trained personal.

Catch Gears

  1. Visual control of the adjustment

  2. Play of gaps in the catch-gears acc. To manual 1HSB515409-100

  3. Greasing of shafts and catch surfaces with grease "L" 

    Important Note: Grease ,, L,, is Low-temperature grease suitable for lubricating precision components (Grease ,,L,, special  Lubricants 1HSB445409-1).

  4. Chang the signal from coil 1to coil 2.

Coils

The resistance of coils and coil currents are allowed to differ ± 10% from value measured at routine test.

Mechanical Interlocking

  1. In OPEN position the distance between the interlocking arm and the interlocking disc shall be min. 2 mm.

  2. In CLOSE position there must be full contact between the arm and disc (No gap is permitted).

Heaters                  

Checking of resistance: If applicable, check signal for loss of power.

Central Control Cubicle

Checking of resistance:

- Small cubicle:                 48 W, 220 V        R = 1000 Ohms ± 10%

- Middle or large cubicle: 140 W, 220 V        R = 346 Ohms ± 10%

Thermostat

If applicable, check that the thermostat is adjusted to requested value.

Terminal Blocks

Tightening of screws or clamps

Direct on Line Motor Starter

If applicable, check signal for loss of power.

Tightness of SF6 Gas

  1. Density monitoring :  Check the density switches and signal/blocked circuits .

  2. Accuracy test of the pressure gauge must be done at standard reference atmosphere i.e. +20 ◦c and 101.3 Kpa (1013 mbar)

  3. Remove the density switch from the circuit-breaker for checking of function. 

Important Note: Min. permissible voltage for test of the circuits in the density switch (30 V).

Blocking Relays

The relays shall close when a density switch is pulled out from a pole pressurized to filling pressure.

Corrosion

  1. Outside: Visual check, particularly check protected components in non heated compartments, pull-rod system, clevis couplings, springs

  2. Treat with anti-rust oil if necessary

Lubrication

  1. Operating mechanism sliding surfaces of spring bridge with Grease "M" and chains with Oil "A"  

    Important Note:

Grease ,,M,, is Low-temperature grease for long-term and permanent lubrication of worm gears, spur gears and other machine elements. Counteracts wear and corrosion. (Grease ,,M,, special  Lubricants 1HSB445409-1).

Oil ,,A,, is thin , fully synthetic lubricating oil for precision components in the Operating mechanism and circuit breaker.  (Oil ,,A,, special  Lubricants 1HSB445409-1).

Tightening Torque

Check of tightening torque on screw joints.( tightening torque for grounding 79 Nm)
