Introduction

This document outlines the reconditioning procedures P3, which must be performed after 2000 OP as indicated by OP 2000 at rated current in the title block.

Competence Requirement: to be carried out by trained staff or ALSTOM specialist. take the circuit breaker out of service, an opening of the pole columns is necessary .

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff must carry the suitable ID safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear safety and health equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, safety belts ... etc.

  • The circuit breaker must be isolated and grounded from both sides..

  • The position indicator of the closing spring shows " discharged "

  • Isolate the service voltage and control voltage.

  • The appropriate local safety regulation should be observed.

  • Pump the Sf6 gas in the service unit and evacuate the column then ventilate the pole column.

  • Wear rubber gloves , protective clothes , gas mask and breathing equipment during the opening and cleaning.

  • Remove decompositions products after having opened the breaker (pole ) or any subassembly.

  • Wipe off decompositions products with a piece of clothes or use a vacuum  cleaner.

  • Don't agitate decomposition products unnecessarily.

  • Neutralize deposits (arc products), adsorption  filter and used clothes with a 3 % soda solution for 24 hours ,or dispose of as harmful substance.

Tools and Equipment

  • Cleaning tools.

  • Safety belt, safety helmet.

  • No special tools needed.

Work to be Carried Out

Replacement of the Arcing Contacts

Preliminary Remakes

  1. Replace the arcing contact in closed, dry and dust free room only.

  2. Do no replace the arcing contacts in the substation in inclement weather conditions or in windy conditions.

  3. Clean all functional elements with piece of cloth that has been soaked in alcohol, and greases according to the greasing instructions. Replace parts when necessary.

  4. Replace the securing elements of bolt connections if it's unscrewed during work.

  5. Exposure of SF6 gas compartments to the atmosphere should be kept to minimum.

  6. Water / rain must be prevented at all circumstances.

Procedures

  1. Open the pole column in closed, dry and dust – free rooms only .the arcing contacts can easily be replaced by using the revision trestle (see fig. B3).

  2. Breaker in end position (off)

  3. Closing spring "discharged"

  4. Store the SF6 gas in a service unit and evacuate the columns .then ventilate the pole columns.

  5. Dismantle the density monitor.

  6. Uncouple the operating mechanism.

  7. Dismounted the linkage cover sheet

  8. Fix a rope to the upper HV terminal of the pole columns (fig. B1)

  9. Undo the four fastening bolts of the support / crank case connection.

  10. Lift the pole column, transport to the work shop and mount them on the revision trestle in upright position

Notes: if the workshop is not available, the replacement of the arcing contacts can be done in the substation but only under dry and windless conditions. A dismounting of the pole column from the support is not necessary then.

Dismounting the Complete Breaking unit from the Support unit

Preconditions

  1. Drive rod and lever (3) uncoupled.

  2. Linkage cover sheet dismounted

  3. This procedure is described in the first part A of the operating instructions.

  4. Mount the revision set (2.07) at the support and couple it with the lever (3) see chapter (6) (measure the length L1) to an accuracy of + or – 2 mm .

  5. Dismount the filter housing (fig B1).

  6. Loosen the bolt (1.08.34) by approx. 1.5 turns, pull the securing pin (1.08.33) upwards and fasten the bolt again. The of the pins are now unsecured.

  7. Pull the pin (1.12.06) out of the opening spring connection (1.1106) .

  8. fix a rope to HV  terminal of the pole columns .

  9. Undo bolts M16*50 (fig B1) , take care that no bolt falls. The support porcelain may be damage falling bolts.

  10. Move the interrupter unit manually into dismounting position by using the revision set. The dismounting position is reached if the measure (L2= L1+78mm) the accuracy of L2 IS ± 2mm. pull the pin out of the opening spring connection beforehand.

  11. Lift the interrupter unit by using a crane until split of (60…..90mm) is reached between the bottom of the support for contact (1.05) and the top of the flange of the upper support porcelain.

NOTE: The equipment may be damage when the split is > 130 mm.

  1. Move the interrupter unit in direction end position off by turning the stretching bolt (2.07.03) until the locking pin (1.12.02) is uncovered (fig. B1) is covered. The movement is limited by locking position of interrupter unit. The internal lever follows the movement, if necessary, insert opening spring connection (1.11.06) into the internal lever (1.080.10).


                               Figure B1: Disassembling the breaking unit from the support unit

  2. Pull out the locking pin (1.12.02).

  3. Lift the breaking unit until the connection piece (1.12.01) and the cap nut (1.12.04) are threaded out of the connecting tube (1.03.07) and place it on a suitable base. Do not damage the gasket .

  4. Protect the support unit and mount the cover to provide water penetration.

Change of the Arcing Contact

  1. Loosen the expansion bolts at the fixed contact (1.01) and proceed as described in chapter 6.2 (fig. B2).

  2. Lift the fixed contact upwards out of chamber porcelain, place it on dry pad ,clean the surfaces and remove any adhering decomposition products.

  3. Replace the fixed arcing contact (1.1.02). Renew the bolt (torque 65Nm) and (PTFE) shield.

  4. Undo the connection chamber porcelain support or contact (1.05) by thrust piece (1.01.08) loosening the bolts (1.26) (see fig. B2)

  5. Lift the chamber porcelain, place it in a dry pad and clean the inner surfaces from any decomposition products.

  6. Pull the complete interrupter unit (1.03) out of support for contact.

  7. Mount the new interrupter unit in support for contact (1.05).

  8. Replace (guide strips PTFE – locking pin – gasket).

  9. Mount the chamber porcelain .fasten the bolts (1.26) and tighten with 210Nm

  10. When mounting the fixed contact on the chamber porcelain the contact has to be positioned with respect to the interrupter unit.

  11. Place the preassembled unit (chamber porcelain and support for contact) on a suitable assembling trestle . for mounting  the closing buffer (10.03) (fig. B3), a sufficient space is necessary.

  12. Couple the closing buffer (10.03) and the connecting tube with the locking pin (1.12.02) and pull them down  until they lock.

  13. Undo the six expansion bolts of the fixed contacts , the locking  bolts remain in the interrupter unit screw three centering pins (10.02) into the fixed contact. Spread them evenly (see fig. B3).

  14. Put there spacers evenly spread on the upper flange of the chamber porcelain.

  15. Guide the fixed contact (1.01) into the  chamber porcelain and put it on spacers (the centering  pins will center  the fixed contact).

    NOTE:- do not forget the gasket (1.04) take notice of the position of the HV terminal pads .
     

  16. Move the interrupter unit (1.03) by pushing the closing buffer  (10.03) up to the lower support into centered position and secure with to bolts (M16) .

  17. Remove the spacers (10.01) and drop the interrupter unit.

  18. Mount expansion and locking bolts. (Before removing the centering pins, fix the fixed contact with the locking bolts ) .

  19. Pull the closing buffer (10.3) with the connecting tube and the locking pin (1.12.02) down until they lock. The position of the axle of the locking pin is now a bout 15 mm blow the fastening pad of the support  porcelain  (fig. B3).

Mounting of the Breaking unit on the Support unit

Preconditions: Revision set mounted and adjust to measure L2 in accordance with (chapter B).

  1. Remove uncovered gasket (1.07) of the support porcelain.

  2. Lift the breaking unit and guide the connecting tube (1.03.07) onto the connecting pieces (1.12.01) , the drilling of the pins of the connecting tube and of the connecting pieces must be in line (fig.B1).

  3. Make sure of the correct positioning of the HV terminal pads, correct angular of the drilling of the connecting tube , turn the tube if necessary

  4. Mount the locking pin (1.12.02).

  5. Drop the breaking unit and fix it with bolts (1.15) and washers (1.14) on the support unit fasten the bolts with 210Nm. (take care no bolts falls. the support porcelain may be damaged by falling bolts).

  6. Move the interrupter unit in end position (off ) by using revision set the end position "Off" is reached. If the drilling of the internal lever (1.8.10) and opening spring connection (1.11.06) are in line.

Caution: - during the movement, the opening spring connection has to be guide into the internal lever.

  1. Mount the pin (1.12.06) of the opening spring connection (1.11.06) (see fig. B1) .

  2. Lock the securing pin (1.08.33) by loosen the bolt by (1.08.34) approx. 1.5 turns , push the securing pin down and fix the bolt (1.08.34) with (13 Nm) .the heads of the pins are now  partially covered by the securing pin .

  3. Dismount revision set and mount linkage cover sheet.

Replace the Adsorption Filter

  1. Undo the filter hosing (1.08.21), remove securing ring (1.08.20) and perforated sheet (1.08.05) .

  2. Pull out the used filter cartridge (1.08.04), insert a now cartridge and replace securing ring and perforated sheet.

  3. Replace o-ring (1.08.36).

  4. Mount the cover and fasten the bolts with (10Nm).

  5. Evacuate the breaker, top up with sf6 to the rated pressure and put it into operation. The procedure is described in port A chapter (4).

  6. With SF6 leak detector. Check all flanges – connection opened during the revision.
