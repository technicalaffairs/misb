Introduction

This document lists the equipment inspection I1, which must be performed on an annually bases  as indicated by Y1 in the title block

The first draft initiated by eng. Sawsan El lethy, and tested by eng Wael Taha at 11 April 2003, revised by Quality Control eng. Adel Abd El Kader El Naggar at EDEP Co. accepted by General Director eng. El Araby Ali Said and approved by Awad Mohamed.Fathi Head sector of Port Said Gas Turbine Power Station.

 

Safety Precautions

 

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required,

  • Wear your safety helmet and safety shoes.

  • Circuit breaker must be disconnected

  • All control circuits must be disconnected

  • Ensure that  the CB removable from the metalclad unit

  • Ensure that  the spring of CB discharging

Notes:
Don't work on the breaker or mechanism while the spring are charged unless they are secured in that position by the maintenance

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Remove the box barriers

  2. Clean all insulating parts from smoke deposit and dust by using dry cloth

  3. Clean the bushings by using dry cloth

  4. Clean the inside of the box barriers by using dry cloth

  5. Clean all flat parts in the throat area of the interrupters by using sandpaper

  6.  Check the Breaker and Mechanism adjustments as the following

    (For easing in reviewing the adjustments, the following are recapitulated)

  1. Primary contact wipe : 1/4" to 5/16"  ....... (fig. 4)

  2. Arcing contact wipe : 5/16" or greater gap at primary contacts  ....... (fig. 5)

  3. Primary contacts gap: 5-1/8" to 5-9/16"  ....... (fig. 4)

  4. Trip latch wipe: 3/16" to 1/4" with trip latch resting against stop pin ....... ( 6 fig.2 ) & ( fig. 7)

  5. Trip armature travel 7/32" to 9/32"

  6. Closing latch wipe : 3/16" to 1/4" ....... (  fig.3 ) & ( fig. 6 )

  7. Closing latch - monitoring switch : maximum clearance 1/32" ....... ( 13 fig.3 )

  8. Motor and relay switch max. clearance 1/32" ....... (  fig.3 ) & ( fig. 6 )

  9. Interlock switch: max. clearance 1/32" ....... (  fig.6 ) & ( fig. 8 )

  10. Driving and latching pawl: max. clearance to ratchet teeth 0.015" ....... ( 2 fig1 )

  11. Latch checking switch contacts make when the gap between the trip latch and the stop pin is 5/16" ..... fig.3

  1. Check all nuts, washers, bolts, cotter pines, and terminal connections for tightness

  2. Inspect all wiring to make sure that no damage has resulted during installation, and test for possible grounds or short circuits

  3. Lubricate all bearing surfaces of the mechanism

  4. Operate the breaker slowly with the manual charging wrench and there is no excessive binding or friction and that the breaker can be moved to the fully opened and fully closed positions

  5. See that any place where the surface of the paint has been damaged is repainted immediately

  6. Tighten the box barriers

  7. Check the operating voltage at the closing coil, trip coil, and motor terminal. (125 volt), if the voltage at the terminals of the coil or motor does not fall in the specified range, check the voltage at the source of power and line drop between the power source and breaker

  8. Check the closing speed of the arcing contact. It should be a minimum of 11 feet per second. Time of closing is (0.022 sec)

  9. Check the opening speed of the arcing contact. It should be a minimum of 15 feet per second. Time of  opening is (0.016 sec)

  10. Remove all tools and equipments

  11. Return the CB into the metal clad unit

  12. Test the operating time (closed time........m sec. &  open time.......m sec.)
