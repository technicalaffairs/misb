Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear your safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes ... etc

  • Remove the CB from service before performing any maintenance work on the breaker

  • Discharge the accumulator pressure to assure safe working conditions, work accumulator pressure is not required for maintenance

Tools and Equipment

  • Safety helmet, safety belt shoes, and tool set

  • Blower, voltmeter

  • Fine cloth for leaning purposes

  • Oil pump, gasket seal, Filler

  • Micro ohmmeter

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Open the red maintenance - positioning valve until the hydraulic pressure reaches approximately the pre charge. Press to prevent the breaker from closing

  2. Check the condition of the hydraulic oil in the sump tank

  3. Check the level of the hydraulic oil in the sump tank

  4. Check the pressure switch - pressure settings

  5. Check operation of the hydraulic pump

  6. Check the trip latch clearances

  7. Check the opening coil when the breaker is opened electrically during maintenance checks on the breaker

  8. Check the trip air gap

  9. Lubricate all operating mechanism moving parts

  10. Clean all interior and exterior cabinet surface

  11. Make sure all electrical connections are tight

  12. Make sure all fasteners are tightened.

  13. Connect a top of bushing terminal to the terminal resistance test on each of the three breaker poles with the breaker closed.

  14. Open the breaker and remove the oil from the three tanks.

  15. Open the three tanks manholes.

  16. Perform maintenance check on the entrance bushing.

  17. Check the bushing current transformers visually to make sure the leads are in good condition.

  18. Clean the interiors of the three tanks using clean lint free rags and insulating oils.

  19. Make sure water has not leaked in to the tanks.

  20. Clean the rotating tube and the contact supporting arms using clean lint free rags and insulating oil.

  21. Make sure all fasteners are tight.

  22. Use the operating mechanism red maintenance positioning valve slowly to close the breaker unit, the movable contact members are about to enter the interrupter chambers on center. If a movable contact member is not on center adjust the contact member alignment.

  23. Continue to slowly close the breaker with the main movable contact members.

  24. Aligned with the interrupter chambers and as the breaker is closing check the top two  main movable contact members to make sure that entry in to interrupter chambers is centered  and the movable contact member are set in a vertical line.

  25. Check the penetration of each main movable contact member in to its stationary contact.

  26. Make sure all electrical connections  through out the contact assembly are intact.

  27. Check all three manhole door gaskets to make sure that they are in good sealing condition.

  28. Coat all three manhole door gaskets with grease to prevent the gaskets from sticking bolt the three manhole closed

  29. Fill each tank with tested oil through the drain valve.

  30. Use the operating mechanism maintenance positioning valves slowly to  close and slowly to  open the breaker three times ending with breaker in the full open position.

  31. Use the movable closing lever fast to close the breaker.

  32. Use the movable trip lever fast to open the breaker.

  33. Use electrical contacts to close the breaker.

  34. Use electrical contacts to open the breaker.

  35. Reset the C.B in to service
