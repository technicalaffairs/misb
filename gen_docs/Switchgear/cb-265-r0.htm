Introduction

This document outlines the major maintenance procedures P2, which must be performed every twenty years as indicated by Y20 in the above title block.

Condition of CB during Work: 1- Circuit breaker out of service and must be isolated.

                                                    2- Gas compartments need not be opened.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff must carry the suitable ID safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear safety and health equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, safety belts ... etc.

  • The circuit breaker must be isolated and grounded from both sides..

Tools and Equipment

  • Gas filling device.

  • Cleaning tools.

  • Safety belt, safety helmet.

  • Normal tools are needed.

  • Two precision pressure gauge one for oil (0-600) bar and the other for SF6 gas (0-15) bar (class 0.6).

 

 

Work to be Carried Out

General Check

Follow the same procedures that have been carried out in the minor maintenance procedures in addition to the following   procedures :-

Drawing off the SF6 Gas

  1. It is recommended to draw off the SF6 with service unit so that it can be used again almost completely for the future filling.

  2. The service unit has all the facilities for drawing off and filling the SF6 gas.

  3. The condition of the sf6 gas in the service unit should be checked in advance.

Open one interrupter unit and remove the contact tube

Visual Check of contact system and Blast Cylinder

If any irregularities are found proceed as in next step (3)

  1. This check is made with out the interrupter unit having to be dismantled.

  2. Contact support with contact tube is removed only.

  3. Only open and check one interrupter unit of the circuit breaker.

  4. If the electrical stressing is known, check the interrupter unit with the highest load.

  5. If the examination of the contact / guide tube shows that both must be renewed, the gas gap must subsequently be checked, along with the off – centre condition and the proper alignment of the contact system.

If any Irregularities are Found in step (2) open all Interrupter units and Remove the Contact tube then proceed as in step (2)

Replace Filter

  1. It is necessary to protect the filter material against the atmospheric humidity

  2. The material must not be directly exposed to the open air for prolonged period of time.

  3. It is supplied in closed tin vacuum that is produced which is recognizable by hissing when opened.

  4. If the tin is leaky, no vacuum will be produced so must not be used because filter capacity will be inadequate for maintenance interval stated.

To Change the Filter

  1. Unscrew the filter chamber 

  2. Remove the disc  

  3. Insert the filter bag in the filter chamber 

  4. Install the chamber with the disc using the new sealing ring supplied 

  5. The circuit breaker must be evacuated. 

 

Filling the SF6 Gas

  1. The breaker may be filled only by appropriately qualified personal, and accordance with the SF6 filling curve ( see under 1-00800- technical data) .

  2. To fill the breaker with gas , service unit can be used since this contains all the necessary facilities .

  3. If the service unit is not available, the SF6 can be taken direct from the gas cylinder using the SF6 filling device.

  4. We must use safety valve with the nominal opening pressure of 8 bar with the filling device that prevent the pressure compartments being overstressed due to an impermissible high pressure.

  5. A vacuum pump must be available for evacuating the breaker prior to SF6 being filled in .

Filling by using a Service Unit

  1. Connect the service unit to the maintenance flangeW1 figure 1 .

  2. Evacuate the breaker to ≤ 20 mbar 

  3. Switch over the service unit to filling.

  4. The values of the filling pressure and the operating dependent on temperature that taken from the diagram (SF6 filling curve and operating values of density monitor – section 1-0080, technical data)

Figure 1

 

Filling by using Gas Cylinder

Siemens offers a complete filling device of type w 348 for filling the breaker with SF6 gas from a gas cylinder (fig. 2).

  1. Connect the maintenance flange of filling device to maintenance flange W1 of the breaker.

  2. Open slowly the valve on the pressure reducer with the vent valve closed to prevent the fitting icing up. (Check the filling pressure by reference to the precision pressure gauge.

  3. At the ambient temperature other than +20 C, the SF6 filling pressure must be taken from the diagram (section 1-0080, technical data )  (care must be taken that the filling pressure depend on the ambient temperature is correct)

  4. The filling tolerance may be up to 0.3 bar over the nominal pressure curve.

  5. When filling is complete, unscrew the filling device and close maintenance flange

NOTE: Make sure all parts are cleaned.

NOTES:

  1. Only pressurize the operating mechanism when the circuit breaker has filled with sf6 gas.

  2. Trial operations must not be made without the breaker having been filled with sf6 gas at nominal pressure.

Check of the breaker for sf6 leaks: - for nominal pressure of sf6 gas (see table in sec. 1-0080, technical data)

  1. When the breaker has been filled , all connected elements should be subjected to a leakage test by using:

  2. Leakage detector

  3. Leakage detector spray from WOBST

  4. Soap solution (if this is not available)

Check all terminal blocks

Check the terminal connection for firm seating and terminal for damage

Measure of the humidity  and air content of the SF6 filling.

Measuring the SF6 gas humidity content

  1. Before putting the breaker into operation , the humidity of the SF6 must be checked   for this a commercially dew point measuring instrument can be used .

  2. The maximum permissible dew point temperature at operating pressure corresponding to the permissible humidity are stated below

  1. If the dew point temperature is below  -100 C during the operation but not below -15 0  C  the measurement should be repeated after two or three months in order to test the effect of the static filters

  2. If there is no reduction in the humidity , we must use a new filter

Measuring the SF6 gas air content

  1. When the breaker has been filled with a new gas and following maintenance the  air content should not be more than 5% to be measure with the sf6 percentage instrument (3-027) from Dilo

  2. This instrument measure the sf6 volume (at least 95%) .
