Introduction

This document outlines the general equipment inspection steps I1, to be carried out every six months as indicated by M6 in the title block.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear safety helmet, shoes, breathing mask, safety glasses is required.

  • The circuit breaker must be isolated completely and properly earthed from both sides,

  • DC control power should be disconnected.

  • Before using a blower to clean the control cabinet or when cleaning the insulators, wear breathing mask to protect your lung tissues from the dust particles.

  • Don't burn the cloth used in cleaning, collect it and send it to the dump.

Tools and Equipment

  • Safety helmet, shoes, breathing mask, safety glasses.

  • Hydraulic test gauges, hydraulic oil, and SF6 gas if needed,

  • SF6 test gauge, micro ohmmeter, and Air Blower.

Work to be Carried Out

Manual Operating Mechanism

  1. Carry out a few closing and opening operations.

  2. Supply the under voltage release with the relative rated voltage (if provided).

Motor Operating Mechanism 

  1. Supply geared motor for spring charging at the relative rated voltage to see if the springs are charged correctly if the signal are correct the geared motor cuts off when the springs are charged.

  2. Carry out a few closing and opening operations, the geared motor recharges the springs after each closing operation
    N.B. Supply the under voltage release with the relative rated voltage (if provided)

Under Voltage Release (If Provided)

  1. Supply the under voltage release at the relative rated voltage and carry out the circuit-breaker closing operation

    if the circuit-breaker closes correctly the signal changes over

  2. Disconnect the power supply from the release the circuit-breaker opens the signal changes over.

Shunt Opening Release (If Provided)

  1. Close the circuit-breaker.

  2. Supply the shunt opening release at the relative rated voltage the circuit-breaker opens correctly the signal correct

Shunt Closing Release (If Provided)

  1. Open the circuit-breaker.

  2. Supply the shunt closing release at the relative rated voltage the circuit-breaker closes correctly the signal correct

Key Lock With Possible Accept Contact (Bch) to the Closing Circuit (If Provided)

  1. Open the circuit-breaker

  2. Turn the key and remove it

  3. Attempt the circuit-breaker closing operation neither manual nor electric closing take place

Auxiliary Contacts In the Operating Mechanism

  1. Insert the auxiliary contacts into suitable signaling circuits.

  2. Carry out a few closing and opening operations of the circuit-breaker to see if signals occur correctly.

Auxiliary Transmitted Contacts

for Indicating that the Circuit Breaker is Open/Closed Connected Isolated

  1. Insert the auxiliary contacts into suitable signaling circuits with the circuit-breaker inside the enclosure carry out a few closing and opening operation.

  2. Put the circuit-breaker in the withdrawn position to see if the signals of the relative operations occur correctly

Insulation Resistance

  1. With a 2500 V megger, measure the insulation resistance between the phase and between phases and exposed conductive part of the medium voltage circuit. The insulation resistance should be at least 50 MΏ, and in any case. Constant in time if values are different, find out the cause.

  2. With a 500 V megger (installed equipment permitting) measure the insulation resistance between the auxiliary circuits and the exposed conductive part. The insulation resistance should be a few MΏ and, in any case. Constant in time

Electromechanical Interlocking Device (If Provided)

  1. With no supply to the interlocking device try to rack-in the circuit-breaker into the enclosure .Racking-in is not possible

  2. Supply the interlocking device at the relative rated voltage

  3. Carry out the racking-in operation .Racking-in is possible.
