Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection I1, which must be carried out on an annually bases as indicated by Y1 in the title block.

To avoid the degrading to dangerous insulation levels. It is advisable to perform a first inspection six months after  putting into service, to define the inspection frequency and the maintenance schedule, taking into account only the switchboard prescription but also those of each component.

CB Technical Data:

Rated voltage                        17.5kv

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding work area is required.

  • Wear your safety equipment as helmet, shoes, breathing mask, safety glass .. etc

  • The personal shall perform the safety operation so as all the live parts are de-energized, earthed and equipped with warnings before removing the protection and reaching main circuit.

  • Wear safety belt when cleaning insulations.  

Tools and Equipment

  • Blower and brushes

  • Voltage indicators, portable earth for discharging

  • Fine cloth for cleaning purpose

 

Work to be Carried out

  1. Perform on each component under the safety condition specified by the relevant instruction, all maintenance operations.

  2. Remove dust and dirt if possible with vacuum cleaner.

  3. Remove dust from the insulation parts with dry clean rags.

  4. Perform a sight inspection of the insulating blocks and the bus- bar joints.

  5. Remove dust from the ventilation holes with a dry brush.

  6. Check the correct working of the levers and if necessary grease the parts subject motion.

  7. Inspect the insulating contacts removing blackening if any from the silver coated surfaces with a local then protect again the surfaces.

  8. Perform some connection and insolating operation of the circuit breakers.

  9. Perform some operations on the earthing switches.

  10. Ascertain the correct working of locks and inter locks.

  11. Check the correct working of the resistors, the thermostate's rating ( ascertain that these have been in service for four at least).

  12. Check the correct working of ventilators if any.

  13. Measure the insulation resistance.

Return the Circuit Breaker into Service

  1. Put the circuit breaker on service position.

  2. Disconnect the portable grounds on both sides of circuit breaker.

  3. Return the circuit breaker of the generator on service position

  4. Lift up the safety fence with caution mark surrounding the work area

  5. Finish the work permit with coordination with shift engineer.

Service Reset

After maintenance remount all protection and perform the operations necessary for putting into service by shift engineer.
