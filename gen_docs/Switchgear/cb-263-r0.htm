Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection I1, which must be performed every five years as indicated by Y5 in the above title block.

Inspection and maintenance (service – general). The circuit breaker can only function reliably if it is correctly maintained.

The purpose of the inspection and maintenance are:

  • To determine to what extent certain parts have warm and to assess their state.

  • To ensure that parts still in a good condition are kept in this state.

  • To replace certain parts in good time.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff must carry the suitable ID safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear safety and health equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, safety belts ... etc.

  • The circuit breaker must be isolated and grounded from both sides..

Tools and Equipment

  • Cleaning tools.

  • Safety belt, safety helmet.

  • Normal tools are needed.

  • Sf6 filling device type w348.

  • Oil precision gauge.

Work to be Carried Out

General Check

This check covers  visual inspection of the circuit breaker without it having to be dismantled visual inspection covers:

  1. The pressure of the SF6 and N2 filling.

  2. The tightness.

  3. Contamination of insulating parts.

  4. Leaks in the hydraulic system.

  5. Damage in the porcelain body and inadequate earth.

Note: The number of make – and – break operations (if counter is present) and any particular occurrences such as:

  • Impermissible switching sequence.

  • Incorrect closing and opening of the  circuit – breaker.

  • Loss in the SF6 and N2 gas.

  • Oil leaks in the operating mechanism.

Check the SF6 Filling

  1. Connect the precision pressure gauge (class 0.6) (0-15) bar to maintenance flange w1 (fig. 6).

  2. Check the SF6 gas that may be left in the service unit.

  • If the measured value not more than 0.3 bar below the filling curve specified , the filling must be correct .

  • If the measured value more than 0.3 bar below the filling curve specified, a leak test must be carried out and the leak must be repaired. then the filling must be correct.

Visual Check of Tightness of the Hydraulic System

The visual inspection covers all screwed connections visible from out side and the connections of the hydraulic system, moreover,  hydraulic monitoring unit, the valve block, hydraulic storage cylinder and the operating mechanism must be checked for visible leaks.

Check for Possible Corrosion on the Hydraulic Storage Cylinder

  1. Check of releases for fixed seat and damage

  2. Check the lever of closing and opening solenoids for damage . check the solenoids , the   setscrews on the lever and the aluminum ring nut for fixed seating (fig. 9)

Check of Oil Level and Oil Condition   if Necessary, Refill

  1. Un pressurize the hydraulic system with the circuit – breaker only in the off position,

  2. Open the pressure release valve (14.37 fig. 10).

  3. With the oil pressurized and in a steady state , the oil level must be visual down to the bottom edge of the filling – in strainer of the oil tank (fig. 12)              

Check the priming Pressure of Nitrogen

  1. Fig. 16 shows the N2 priming pressure as a function of the temperature.

  2. It is necessary to measure the N2 temperature by measurement on the storage cylinder.

Measuring the N2 Priming Pressure on the Oil side as follows:

  1. Connect the precision pressure gauge (class 0.6 ) to the  measuring connection M2 (fig. 10) of the control unit with the oil un pressurized.

  2. Close the pressure release valve 14.37 (fig. 10).

  3. Switch on the oil pump. Shortly after the oil pump has been switch on, the pointer jump to the priming pressure value.

  4. If the  priming pressure is more than 5 bar below the tolerance, the N2 filling should be corrected.

  5. The gas side of the hydraulic reservoir may only be filled with  (N2 purity 99.99% , O2 content less than 50ppm , H2O content 30ppm)

  6. No other gases may be used for the hydraulic reservoir.

  7. Owing to the permissible leak rate of approximately (2-5)bar the priming pressure can drop below the nominal filling pressure during the operating  period. for example: Circuit breaker has been in operation for 5 years, check of the priming pressure in the storage unit shows a measurement of 183 bar.

The permissible leakage rate has not been exceeded. even if  the priming pressure deviates.

  1. For filling in the N2 gas a pump set is required whet can be bought or borrowed from the erecting department (ANL.A4) in erlangen (fig. 18).

  2. The procedures of the N2 gas filling are:

  1. Un screw the nut 13.2.18 (fig. 17).

  2. Fit the connection of the N2 filling device (fig. 18).

  3. Correct the N2 filling pressure according to (fig. 16).

Check of the Opening and Closing Pressure of the Safety valve

  1. Operate contactor K9 in the control cubical (fig. 11) electrically.

  2. The oil pump increases the oil pressure in the hydraulic system beyond the nominal pressure.

  3. The safety valve operate and passes the oil from the high pressure section to the unpressurized part of hydraulic system

  4. The operating pressure must be between (375 – 412) bar.

  5. when the pump switched off , determine the closing pressure of the safety valve (the closing value must be at least 10 bar above the upper pump on value (320bar).

  6. if the pressure values are outside these limits, a new safety valve must be used. The breaker must not be  put into operation before this has been done. The new safety valve must be the same type as the old one.

Check the Operating values of the SF6 Density Monitor and Oil Pressure Monitor

SF6 Density Monitor Operating Values Check                                              

  1. To determine the operating values of the SF6 density monitor follow these procedures:

  2. Connect the sf6 gas to the maintenance flange w1 through a distributor with t drain value fixed on it a precision SF6 pressure gauge class 0.6 (0-10) bar and a safety valve as show in (fig. 6).

  3. Open the drain valve of the distributor to reduce the sf6 pressure of the circuit breaker.

  4. Read and record the values of the precision gauge at which the sf6 density monitor function (alarm, lock out)during the pressure density process.

  5. Close of the distributor drain valve and open sf6 cylinder regulator valve to the increase the SF6 pressure inside the circuit breaker.

  6. Read and record during this process values of the values of the precision gauge at which the sf6 lock out and alarm are cleared.

Oil Pressure Monitor Operating Value Check

  1. Open the pressure release valve 14.37 ( fig. 10) to unpressurized the hydraulic circuit (for this switch off the out pump CB).

  2. Connect the test pressure gauge to M2.

  3. Close the valve 14.37 again and switch on the oil pump to bring the system to the Nominal Pressure.

  4. Reduce the pressure with the valve 14.37 and chick the operating values of the pressure switch using the pressure monitor (see section 1-0080 under “technical data”).

Check of the Heating Effect of the Anti Condensation Heater

  1. Check the function of the external monitoring circuit, if provided, for the anti–condensation heating.

  2. If this heating is not monitored, the current consumption should be measured.

Check for Corrosion Protection and Touch up where Necessary

  1. Check the paint finish of the circuit breaker for damage.

  2. Defective parts must be derusted provided a priming coat and varnished.
