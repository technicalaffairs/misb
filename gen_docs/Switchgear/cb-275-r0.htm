Introduction

This documents outlines the major maintenance procedure P1, to be performed every ten years as indicated by Y10 at the titled block.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff must carry the suitable ID safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear safety and health equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, safety belts ... etc.

  • The circuit breaker must be isolated and grounded from both sides..

  • Isolate the service voltage and control voltage.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set

  • Contact resistance tester

  • Megger (500 – 5000) volt

  • Multimeter

  • Air blower

  • Cleaning solvent 

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Check SF6 pressure on manometer

  2. Check SF6 manometer with control instrument

  3. Check air pressure on manometer.

  4. Check compressed air manometer with control instrument.

  5. Check setting and function of manometer contacts.

  6. Check SF6 moisture content desired level: ≤ - 20 ْc.

  7. Check SF6 air content desired level: ≤  2 %.

  8. Check SF6 acid content desired level: ≤ 5 … 10 ppmG

  9. Check SF6 tightness at drive shafts (sniffing drill-hole).

  10. Drain compressed air receiver.

  11. Check high pressure flexible tubes, if necessary replace

  12. Check adjustment and function of compressed air pressure switch

  13. Check tightness of screw and pipe couplings

  14. Check refilling valve for function and tightness

  15. Check refilling times, in comparison with reference value from commissioning.

  16. Check auxiliary switch for function and burning of contacts.

  17. Check screw couplings of electrical connections, if necessary re tighten

  18. Check fixed main contact and if necessary replace

  19. Check movable main contact and if necessary replace

  20. Check insulating nozzle and if necessary replace.       

  21. Replace molecular sieve filter

  22. Carry out test switching operations.

  23. Check times of switching operations.

  24. Check pressure drop when switching, compare with reference values from commissioning.

  25. Check mechanical position indicators.

  26. Closing the CB manually

  27. Opening CB manual manually

  28. Closing time of breaker

  29. Tripping time of breaker
