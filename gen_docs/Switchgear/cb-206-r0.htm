Introduction

This document outlines the minor maintenance P1, which must be performed from eight to twelve years and after 2500 switching cycles 1 bases as indicated by Y8:12 in the title block

The technical specification of the CB Max. Rated voltage = 145kv, rated voltage = 132kv, Rated current = 1250 A, short-circuit current 31.5 KA

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, qualified maintenance staff carry the suitable safety ID category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required,

  • Wear standard personal safety and healthy equipment as helmet, safety shoes, rubber gloves, ... etc.

    •

  • Switch off the disconnectors on CB sides and grounded the CB sides

  • Switch off DC & AC supply (CB is electrically dead)

  • Switch off the charging current DC motor wound spring

  • The CB in not under pressure (all compressed air is released)

Tools and Equipment

  • Portable air blower, metal brush

  • Set of common screw drivers, cleaning material (arg, cloth), water pump, metal wires

  • Densimeter test equipment, double open-ended spanner

  • Set set to refill connector

  • Lubricating grease for sf6 L... 44

  • Ohmmeter and slow close device

    •

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Check and presence of any abnormal noise

  2. Check and presence of surface defects or cracks at breaking unit and supporting insulators

  3. Check the tightening of bolts and nuts

  4. Check the mechanism of operation

  5. Measure the bending of drive rod (< 3mm)

  6. Inspect the functioning of heaters

  7. Record counter reading of operations counter

  8. Clean the insulations and check for defects or cracks

State of Arcing Contacts (Determine the maximum admissible wear)

  1. Connect circuit indicator to primary H.V terminals of pole A

  2. For measuring of xa, slowly move the C.B with the help of slow close device from the on - position towards the off - position

  3. Measure dimension xa upon electrical contact separation (beep tone stops, lamp goes out) and record it

  4. Repeat measurement at least twice and record xao

  5. Repeat steps 1,2,3 and 4 for poles B and C

  6. For every pole evaluate the admissible limiting value K < 6mm (K=Xa - Xao)

  7. If K of 6mm has been reached, reconditioning of contacts is necessary

Densimeter Switching Points

  1. Open isolating terminal for signals S1 (signal 1" top - up") and S2 (signal 2 witch and lock) wound spring operating mechanism

  2. Connect terminal for S1 to line tester

  3. Connect densimeter test device to densimeter support

  4. Open the nozzle of densimeter test device

  5. When line tester reacts close nozzle and record reading S1/bar

  6. Compare S1 with the theoretically determined pressure value and repeat measurement

  7. On apparatus side, connect line tester to terminals for S2

  8. Conduct measurement for S2 according to measurement for S1 with repetition

  9. Nominal values:   
    Pressure difference  S1 - S2 = 0.3     ± 0.1 bar       
                                  S2 = S2 (according to calculation) ± 0.2 bar

  10. If the deviation exceeds the tolerance, densimeter should be correct

  11. State of rated current carrying circuit (measurement the DC resistance of contacts by using the ohmmeter) (reference value < 40 µ Ω
