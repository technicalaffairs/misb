Procedure:
Measuring SF6 Moisture Rate
Using SHAW Tester         (P1 –Y3)		 Date Approved:    24 May 2004
Date to be Reviewed:  May 2009

Introduction

New gas has 15 ppmg moisture rate,

In circuit breakers and components equipped with dryers moisture < 40 ppmg. 

In other component without dryers: moisture < 80 ppmg.

This document lists the testing procedures P1, which must be performed every three years as indicated by Y3 in the above title block.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category, 

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear your safety helmet, safety shoes .... etc

    •

  • The circuit breaker under test must be out of service.

  • Circuit breaker must be isolated & proper earthing at the two sides of CB area is required

  • Isolate control circuit (DC voltage)

  • Change remote/local switch to local position.

    •

Tools and Equipment

  • Dew point meter ( ºC ) type SHAW England

  • Thermometer  ( ºC )

  • Screw driver, and set of common keys.

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Switch the instrument on and check the battery condition.

  2. At the sample point to be tested open the sample valve gently and check that no dirt or condensate is expelled.

  3. Connect the PTFE sample pipe supplied with the instrument between the sample tapping point and the instrument using either of the connections of the head / sensor assembly.

  4. Adjust the sample value to give a gentle flow through the instrument (5-10 L/min) is an ideal flow but the flow rate is not particularly critical. Don't exceed the flow rate of about 20 L/min.

  5. Place a finger over the outlet connection of the instrument head and the sample pressure will cause the head to open. Remove the finger when the head is fully open. (if there is insufficient sample pressure, fit a length of  1 m or  2 m of flexible pipe to the outlet connection of the instrument head and a slowly  raise the head by hand).

  6. The instrument reading will move ip to the scale and then stabilize. When there is no further change in reading, note the final result. If there is a doubt about the stability if the sample flow rate or the material of the sample pipe being used, increase the sample flow rate after the final reading has been obtained.

    If the instrument moves to a drier reading , this indicates that the original flow rate was too low or that there is a leak in the system allowing ambient moisture into the sample or that the sample pipe itself is still wet.

  7. After the test is complete , close the instrument head ready for the next test.
