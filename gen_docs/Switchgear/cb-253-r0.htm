Introduction

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff must carry the suitable ID safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear the appropriate safety equipment as safety shoes and helmet

  • All maintenance work must be done with both primary and control power circuits are de-energized

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete tool kit

  • Brush, vacuum cleaner

  • Megger1000 volt or 2500 volt

  • Lubricant 0282A2048P009

 

 

Work to be Carried Out

Switchgear Cells

No. Inspection items Yes No N/A
1.

Clean the equipment

     
2.

Inspect the bus bars and connections carefully

      
3.

Measure the bus insulation resistance to ground and between phases (use a MEGGER 1000 or 2500 volt)

      
4.

Clean racking mechanism and lubricate jack screws and gears with lubricant 0282A2048P009

      
5.

Check primary disconnecting device contacts, apply a thin coat of contact lubricant 0282A2048P009

      
6.

Check tightness and continuity of all control connection and wiring

      
7.

Check that all heaters are energized and operating correctly

      
8.

Check the earthing points

      

 

Switchgear Breakers

No. Inspection items Yes No N/A
1.

Perform a visual inspection of breaker and check for any loose or damaged parts

      
2.

Perform slow closing test operation manually

      
3.

Check the erosion, wipe and gap indicator

     
4.

Perform the vacuum interrupter integrity test

      
5.

Lubricate the breaker operating mechanism

     
6.

Check the electrical operation using the test cabinet (if available)
7.

Examine the  movable contact rod of the vacuum interrupter and re-lubricate the movable contact rod with 0282A2048P009 grease
8.

To clean the interior of the breaker from dust and any strange materials, use a compressed air or blower with a maximum pressure 4 kg/cm2, since high pressure can cause damage of internal parts, and the compressed air should be free of moisture.
