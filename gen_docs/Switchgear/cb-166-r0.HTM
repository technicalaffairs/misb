Procedure:
Checking the Nitrogen Pressure (I1-Y3)

Approved Date:    07 Aug 2004

Date to be Reviewed:  Aug 2009

Introduction

This document outlines procedures P1, which must be performed every three years as indicated by Y3 in the title block.

The nitrogen pressure is the primary pressure of the stored energy accumulator.

The factory set (180 + 15 ) bar at 15 ºC, and the accumulator should be replaced when pressure losses exceed 10 %.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, qualified maintenance staff carry the suitable safety ID category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required,

    •

  • Wear your safety helmet, shoes .... etc.

  • The circuit breaker under test must be out of service.

  • Circuit breaker must be isolated & proper earthing at the two sides of CB area is required

  • Isolate control circuit (DC voltage)

  • Change remote/local switch to local position.

    •

Tools and Equipment

  • Hand pump, thermometer

  • Pressure gauge with it’s delivered connection.

Work to be carried out

  1. Put the circuit breaker in open position.

  2. Switch off  the power supply of the pump motor of the circuit breaker.

  3. Measure and record the ambient temperature.

  4. Install the pressure controller as shown in the following figure:

  5. Observe the hydraulic oil  pressure very closely. At  first  the pressure decrease very slowly from 325 bar to a certain value ,then the pressure suddenly drop to zero. The nitrogen pressure can be recorded at the point when the pressure suddenly begins to drop.

  6. Close k3 and use the hand pump to charge the hydraulic circuit and carefully observe the pressure gauge. The pressure increases rapidly to a certain value which is the nitrogen pressure. Read and record this value. The pressure will slowly increase to full system pressure of approximately 325 bar.

  7. Carryout several charging and discharging cycles and record the nitrogen pressure as indicated in step 5 and step 6. calculate the average pressure during the charging and discharging cycles as the following diagram:

Pre-charging pressure at test temperature = (P rising  + P falling) / 2.

  1. Express the pressure for a temperature of 15 ºC, A 10 % pressure variation will be obtained for a 20 ºC variation in temperature.

  2. Read and record the various hydraulic pressure of the system as the following:

For 66KV

                           Par      = Cut - out pressure of pump motor = ( 326 à   346 ) bar

                           Pdr      = Cut - in pressure of pump motor = ( 306 à   326 ) bar

                           VE     = Closing lock out  pressure = ( 261 à   277 ) bar

                           DVE  = Closing unlocking  pressure = ( 271 à   297 ) bar

                           PVD  = Electrical auto-opening pressure = ( 242.5 à   257.5 ) bar 

 

For 220KV

                           Par  = Cut - out pressure of pump motor  = ( 310 à   336 ) bar

                           Pdr  = Cut - in pressure of pump motor    = ( 306.5 à   325.5 ) bar

                           VE      = Closing lock out pressure  = ( 265 à   281 ) bar

                           DVE  = Closing unlocking  pressure= (268 à   291) bar

                          PVD = Electrical auto opening pressure = (242.5 à   257.5) bar

  1. Place  the Circuit Breaker back into normal operation condition.
