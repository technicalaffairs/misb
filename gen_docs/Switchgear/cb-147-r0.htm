Introduction

The micrometer is designed for measuring the resistance of contacts on busbar and circuit breakers.

The measurement is made at a high current, typically 100A DC. The voltage drop across a contact under test is compared to the voltage drop across a known internal shunt resistance and the result is displayed.

This document lists the testing procedures P1, which must be performed every three years as indicated by Y3 in the above title block.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category, 

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear your safety helmet, safety shoes .... etc

    •

  • The circuit breaker under test must be out of service.

  • Circuit breaker must be isolated & proper earthing at the two sides of CB area is required

  • Isolate control circuit (DC voltage)

  • Change remote / local switch to local position.

    •

Tools and Equipment

  • Safety helmet & safety shoes.

  • Micro-Ohm meter type Electrona MRC4 see figure 1.

  • Maintenance tool kit  includes screw drivers set ... etc

 

 

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Join the apparatus to 220v, 50Hz by the main leads delivered with it. (regulation  knob “I” on null).

  2. Move the switch “Arret-Marche” to “marche” in order to admit extern tension and let under tension for about 15 minutes (for thermal stabilization of electronically components).

  3. Join the current circuit by using the power cords (35 mm²) delivered with apparatus at the terminals (I+) and (I-).

  4. Join the measuring circuit by using the measuring armored cords at the terminals (U+) and (U-) with regards to the respect polarities.

  5. Regulate the intensity across RX with control knob.

  6. Wait for the value indicated on the Amp. meter to be stabilized then read and record directly the resistor value.

  7. Compare the reading for each type with the reference values if  OK then, are up to 200microohm for both 66kv and 220kv

  8. Disconnect the test unit and prepare the CB to be back into normal operation condition.
