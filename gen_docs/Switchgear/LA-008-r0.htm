Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued,

  • The maintenance staff must be trained, qualified, and carry the suitable ID safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear your safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, shoes, breathing mask, ... etc.

  • Surge Arrester out of service.

  • There is danger of falling off the top of the arrester when cleaning insulators or checking connections wear your safety belt.

  • Isolation and proper earthing is required

Tools and Equipment 

  • Safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, safety belt ... etc.

  • Tool set for tightness, cleaning tools as cloth .... etc

  • Rags, Fiberglass ladder

  • Voltage indicator, thermo vision gun

  • Megger.

 

 

Work to be Carried out

In addition to the two monthly inspection check list, perform the following tasks below:

  1. Check that there is no voltage, clean the insulators of Surge Arrestor.

  2. Check insulators for cracks.

  3. Disconnect bus or conductor and inspect the insulation resistance by 1000v megger (the value should not be less than 1000 M.ohm),

  4. Replace defective units revealed by megger test

  5. Correct any suspected primary connections ( hot spots) that might have been revealed during thermo vision test.

  6. Reconnect bus or conductor and check for tightness of line and earth bus connection

  7. If discharge counter is fitted, check for proper insulation of arrester's base to earth.

  8. Put the Surge Arrester into service and carry out thermo vision test for hotspot for its connection with network.
