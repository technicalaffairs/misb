Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection I1, which must be performed on an annually bases as indicated by Y1 in the above title block

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staffmust carry the suitable ID safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear the appropriate safety equipment as safety shoes and helmet

  • All maintenance work must be done with both primary and control power circuits are de-energized

  • Inspect the panel board once every year or after any severe electrical faults

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete tool kit

  • Brush, vacuum cleaner

  • Megger1000 volt or 2500 volt

  • Lubricant 0282A2048P009

    •

Work to be Carried Out

Load Centre Cells

No. Inspection items Yes No N/A
1.

Clean out the panel board by using a brush, vacuum cleaner and avoid blowing dirt into the panel

      
2.

Look for any moisture or signs of previous wetness or dripping inside the panel board

      
3.

Inspect the bus bars and connections carefully

      
4.

Measure the bus insulation resistance to ground and between phases (use a MEGGER 1000 or 2500 volt)

      
5.

Clean racking mechanism and lubricate jack screws and gears with lubricant 0282A2048P009

      
6.

Check primary disconnecting device contacts, apply a thin coat of contact lubricant 0282A2048P009

      
7.

Check tightness and continuity of all control connection and wiring

      
8.

Check that all heaters are energized and operating correctly

      
9.

Retighten plug fuses and closely examine fuse clips

      
10.

Check fuses to make sure they have the proper Ampere rating and interrupting rating

      
11.

Visually inspect deterioration in insulating material and replace such insulating parts

      
12.

Check the earthing points

      

 

400V Load Centre Breakers

No.

Inspection items

 Yes No N/A
1.

Perform a visual inspection of breaker and check for any loose or damaged parts
2.

Perform slow closing test operation manually

      
3.

Check the erosion, wipe and gap indicator

     
4.

Check operation of all mechanical components by operating each circuit breaker several times to make sure that all mechanisms are free and in proper working order

      
5.

  • Check the breaker operating mechanism.

  • Check the integrity of all electrical and mechanical interlocks.

  • Examine switch operating mechanism.

  • Whenever possible, check all devices for missing or broken parts, proper spring tension, free movement, rusting or corrosion and dirt.

  • Adjust, clean and lubricate or replace parts as required.

      
6.

Clean and dress copper electrical contacts blades and jaws, avoid removing metal from silver contact surfaces

     
  
No.

Inspection items

 Yes No N/A

7.

  • Measure the insulation resistance (use a MEGGER 1000 or 2500 volt) if:

  • A severe electrical fault has occurred.

  • It has been necessary to replace parts or clean the insulating surfaces.

  • The panel board has been exposed to high humidity condensation or dripping moisture.

 

 

 
8.

To clean the interior of the breaker from dust and any strange materials, use a compressed air or blower with a maximum pressure 4 kg/cm2, since high pressure can cause damage of internal parts, and the compressed air should be free of moisture

     
9.

  • Lubricate the operating parts of switch mechanism

  • Use a clean non metallic, light grease parts

  • Do not oil or grease parts of molded case circuit breakers

  • If no instructions are given on the devices sliding copper contacts operating mechanism and interlocks, it may be lubricated with clean light grease
