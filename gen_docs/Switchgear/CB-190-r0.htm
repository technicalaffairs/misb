Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection checklist P4 which must be performed every three - years bases as indicated by y in the title block. The technical specifications of the CB. Rated current = 600A - 230 MVA - 20 KA - 6.6 KV

The first draft is initiated and reviewed by eng. Nagy Bekheit Saleh, accepted by eng. Badr Ibrahem Abdel Gawad general director, and approved by eng. Abdel Karem Abu Zied Ismaiel; Head Sector of High Dam Hydraulic Power Station,  Hyd.EPCo.

This document certified by eng. Kamel Ragheb Hakem MPIS Hyd.EPCo.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable category.

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear safety and healthy equipments as safety helmet, safety shoes ..etc

  • Switch of the CB, and withdraw it from its cell

  • Switch of DC current

Tools and Equipment

  • Tool set, cleaning material (cloth. - benzene)

  • Oil for lubricating

  • Grease for gears

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Remove interphase partitions

  2. Drain the oil and check oil gauges

  3. Disconnect insulation rods from the poles and remove the poles

  4. Remove lower covers with stationary contacts and take out spacing cylinders and chambers

  5. Remove upper covers and take out oil separators

  6. Disconnect the mechanism housing from insulation cylinder, dismantle the mechanism of roller contacts

  7. Repair the contacts and explosion chambers

  8. Inspect the mechanism and details located inside the CB

  9. Clean all parts, check the fastenings

  10. Check the condition of the oil damper

  11. Check the CB main shaft turns freely with disconnecting tripping springs

  12. After assembling the CB, fill it with pure oil transformer

  13. Join the CB with the operating mechanism by means of a drive rod

  14. Start adjusting the CB by setting its main shaft in the off - open position

  15. When the breaker is open install the tripping spring

  16. Adjust the CB without upper covers on the pole and without oil separators

  17. By an ordinary electric circuit with lamps determine the moment of contacts touching of each CB pole

  18. Close and open the CB as far as possible with the help of external levers

  19. Adjust the length of insulation rods so that the marks on the control rods corresponding to the opened state of the CB

  20. With the help of the operating mechanism manual, bring the CB moving contacts to touch the stationary ones. Difference in contacts touching shouldn't exceed 5 mm. Adjust the contacts touching by changing the length of insulating rod

  21. Close the CB completely, total travel of the moving contacts should be within 240 - 245mm, travel inside the contacts 60 + 3. 60-5 mm and the difference of travl between the closed and the extreme positions shouldn't be less then 4mm

  22. Test the mechanical parts of the CB by several make and break operation

  23. Test the CB operation by slow manual breaking

  24. Measure the resistance of the breaker between terminals

  25. Before putting it into service. Wipe it dry all insulation parts
