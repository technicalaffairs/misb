Introduction

This document outlines the minor maintenance procedures P1, which must be performed every ten years as indicated by Y10 in the above title block.

Condition of CB During Work: 1- Circuit breaker out of service and must be isolated.

                                                    2- Gas compartments need not be opened.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance stuff must carry the suitable ID safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear safety and health equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, safety belts ... etc.

  • The circuit breaker must be isolated and grounded from both sides..

Tools and Equipment

  • Gas filling device.

  • Cleaning tools.

  • Safety belt, safety helmet.

  • Normal tools are needed.

  • Two precision pressure gauge one for oil (0-600) bar and the other for sf6 gas (0-15) bar (class 0.6).

Work to be Carried Out

General Check

  1. This check covers visual inspection of the circuit breaker without it having to be dismantled visual inspection covers:

  2. The pressure of the SF6 and N2 filling.

  3. The tightness.

  4. Contamination of insulating parts.

  5. Leaks in the hydraulic system.

  6. Damage in the porcelain body and inadequate earth.

Note: The number of make – and – break operations (if counter is present) and any particular occurrences such as:

  1. Impermissible switching sequence.

  2. Incorrect closing and opening of the circuit – breaker.

  3. Loss in the sf6 and N2 gas.

  4. Oil leaks in the operating mechanism.

Check the SF6 Filling

  1. Connect the precision pressure gauge (class 0.6) (0-15) bar to maintenance flange w1 (fig. 1).

  2. Check the SF6 gas that may be left in the service unit.

  3. If the measured value not more than 0.3 bar below the filling curve specified, the filling must be correct.

  4. If the measured value more than 0.3 bar below the filling curve specified, a leak test must be carried out and the leak must be repaired. then the filling must be correct.

Measure of the N2 Priming Pressure on the Oil Side as Follows:

  1. Connect the precision pressure gauge (class 0.6) to the  measuring connection.

  2. M2 (fig. 6) of the control unit with the oil un pressurized.

  3. Close the pressure release valve 14.37 (fig. 6).

  4. Switch on the oil pump. Shortly after the oil pump has been switch on , the pointer jump to the priming pressure value.

  5. If the  priming pressure is more than 5 bar below the tolerance, the N2 filling should be corrected.

  6. The gas side of the hydraulic reservoir may only be filled with (N2 purity 99.99%, O2 content less than 50ppm, H2 O content 30 ppm).

  7. No other gases may be used for the hydraulic reservoir.

  8. Owing to the permissible leak rate of approximately (2-5)bar the priming pressure can drop below the nominal filling pressure during the operating  period . for example :- circuit breaker has been in operation for 5 years, check of the priming pressure in the storage unit shows a measurement of 183 bar.

  9. The permissible leakage rate has not been exceed ed. even if  the priming pressure deviates.

  10. For filling in the N2 gas a pump set is required whet can be bought or borrowed from the erecting  department (ANL.A4)in erlangen (fig. 7).

Check of the Opening and Closing Pressure of the Safety Valve

  1. Operate contactor K9 in the control cubical (fig. 5) electrically.

  2. The oil pump increases the oil pressure in the hydraulic system beyond the nominal pressure.

  3. The safety valve operate and passes the oil from the high pressure section to the un pressurized part of hydraulic system.

  4. The operating pressure must be between (375 – 412) bar.

  5. When the pump switched off, determine the closing pressure of the safety valve (the closing value must be at least 10 bar above the upper pump on value (320 bar ) approx.

  6. If the pressure values are out side these limits , anew safety valve must be used . The breaker must not be  put into operation before this has been done . The new safety valve must be the same type as the old one.

Check the Inner Sealing of the Hydraulic System in both Switching States

  1. The measurement should be made with the circuit breaker in the "on" and "off" position and the hydraulic system at nominal pressure (320bar)

  2. Measure the oil pressure by using the precision pressure gauge and repeat the measurement after 30 minutes with the pump switched off and after the temperature has reached an adequate level (approx. 15 minutes ) .  the leakage rate must not exceed 5 bar/ hour. Temperature variation accruing during the measurements must be allowed for with approx. 1bar.

Check the Operating values of the SF6 Density Monitor and Oil Pressure Monitor

SF6 Density Monitor Operating Values Check

  1. to Determine the operating values of the SF6 density monitor follow these procedures:  

  2. Connect the SF6 gas to the maintenance flange w1 through a distributor with t drain value fixed on it a precision SF6 pressure gauge class 0.6 (0-10) bar and a safety valve as show in (fig. 1).

  3. Open the drain valve of the distributor to reduce the SF6 pressure of the circuit breaker.

  4. Read and record the values of the precision gauge at which the sf6 density monitor function (alarm, lock out) during the pressure density process.

  5. Close of the distributor drain valve and open SF6 cylinder regulator valve to the increase the sf6 pressure inside the circuit breaker.

  6. Read and record during this process value of the values of the precision gauge at which the sf6 lock out and alarm are cleared.

Oil Pressure Monitor Operating Value Check

  1. Open the pressure release valve 14.37( fig. 6 )to unpressurized the hydraulic circuit  (for this switch off the out pump CB).

  2. Connect the test pressure gauge to M2 .

  3. Close the valve 14.37 again and switch on the oil pump to bring the system to the nominal pressure.

  4. Reduce the pressure with the valve 14.37 and chick the operating values of the pressure switch using the pressure monitor (see section 1-0080under “technical data”).    

Check of Breaker Pressure Gauge of SF6 and Pressurized Oil

  1. The difference between the measured values of the  test pressure gauge (class 0.6 ) and the service pressure gauge  (class1.0 ) must not be larger than the sum of the  permissible deviation `1of the two pressure gauges .

  2. This means that the deviation of the `test pressure gauge  of (class 1.0) with measuring range of 600bar (oil ) or 15 bar (gas ) with respect to the service pressure gauge must not be larger than 12 bar (oil ) or 0.3 bar (gas )

  3. Deviation above 5 bar (oil ) and 0.2 bar (sf6 ) should be noted on the service pressure gauge (by hand on adhesive film)

Check of the Heating Effect of the Anti Condensation Heater

  1. Check the function of the external monitoring circuit , if provided , for the anti – condensation heating

  2. If this heating is not monitored, the current consumption should be measured .

Check of the Release Circuit Enforced Triple Poles Operation, anti pumping Feature and Lockouts.

Auto Recluse Lockout

  1. Switch off the pump motor by tripping CB.

  2. Lower the storage pressure by means the pressure release valve 14.37 (fig.6) until pressure monitor B2 (fig. 6) activates the auto reclose lock out via contactor K4 (in the control unit).

  3. Read off the operating pressure on the test pressure gauge (precision gauge ).

  4. Check the indication and effectiveness of the auto – reclose lockout feature.

Reclosing Lockout

  1. Slowly reduce the storage pressure further until the pressure monitor B2 (fig. 6) and contactor K2 (in the control unit) operate.

  2. Read off the operating pressure on the test precision pressure gauge.

  3. Check the indication and effectiveness of the auto – reclose lockout by means of electrical "on" command,  the breaker must not be close .

Function Lockout

  1. Switch on the pump motor and increase the pressure until the reclosing lockout is cancelled

  2. Close the breaker and switch off the pump motor .

  3. Slowly increase the storage pressure until pressure monitor B2 and contactor K3 operate.

  4. Read off the operating pressure gauge ( see technical dada ) on the test pressure gauge .

  5. Check the indication and effectiveness of the function lockout by means of electrical  "off" commands in all tripping paths

  6. The breaker must not open.

Enforced Triple – Pole Operation Feature (only Version E)

Breaker with Separate Hydraulic Operating System

  1. Close all three poles of the breaker and open one of them by actuating the operating trip  Y2 (fig.3)

  2. After the time set on enforced triple poles operation relay on the plant side has elapsed , the other two poles must also be tripped by enforced triple- pole operation contactor .

  3. Carry out this test in the similar manner on the other two poles.

  4. Close one breaker pole by manually actuating the closing coilY1 (fig.3)

  5. When the reset time has elapsed , the pole must reopen .

  6. This check should be carried out on al three poles.

Tripping

Check the tripping action of the circuit breaker via all the existing tripping paths.

Anti Pumping Feature

Breaker in "Open "State

  1. Give" close "command and keep button pressed in (maintained command).

  2. Give "open" command while the close command is being applied.

  3. Breaker may only close and then open; it must not reclose despite the presence of the close command .

Breaker in "Close" State

  1. Give close command and keep button pressed in (maintained command).

  2. Give open command after approximate 15 s while the close command is being applied.

  3. Breaker may only open (must be reclose)

Delayed Function lockout on N2 Loss

  1. The time relay is set in the factory to a reference delay time of three hours.

  2. Check the time setting and correct if necessary.

  3. Any deviation time settings require an agreement between the customer, the sales department and the manufacture.

  4. Check for corrosion protection and touch up where necessary.

  5. Check the paint finish of the circuit breaker for damage.

  6. Defective parts must be derusted provided a priming coat and varnished.
