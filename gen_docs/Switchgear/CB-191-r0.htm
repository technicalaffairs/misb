Introduction

This document outlines the maintenance procedure P1 which must be performed every 10 years bases as indicated by Y10 in the title block.

 The technical specifications of the circuit breaker : Rated current = 2000A and short circuit 40kA/3 second, pneumatic mechanism.

The first draft is initiated and reviewed by eng. Soleiman Abd Elfattah Soleiman on Feb 2002, accepted by eng. Badr Ibrahem Abdel Gawad general director, and approved by eng. Abdel Karem Abu Zied Ismaiel; Head Sector of High Dam Hydraulic Power Station,  Hyd.EPCo.

This document certified by eng. Kamel Ragheb Hakem MPIS Hyd.EPCo.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding work area is required

  • Wear safety helmet, shoes, safety glasses ...

  • Switch off CB.

  • Switch off the DC & AC supply. (CB is electrically dead)

  • Switch off the disconnectors on C.B sides and grounded the C.B sides

  • The CB in not under pressure (all compressed air is released)

Tools and Equipment

  • Portable air blower, metal brush

  • Suction - evacuation service cart

  • Telescope crane & lifting crane

  • Set of common, screw drivers, and cleaning material (rag, cloth)

  • Graphite, scilicon, vaseline and berulub FK - 33 greases for lubricating

  • Electronic breaker timing analyzer (type TM 1600/MA 31) micrometer and water pump

Work to be Carried Out

Preparation

  1. Isolate the terminals of (closing, opening) coils of CB from main cabinet for all CB phases

  2. Connect the external DC supply to (closing & opening) coil for CB phases

  3. Connect the AC supply of air compressors

  4. Measurement (close-open) times, of CB

  5. Disconnect AC supply of air compressors and test the air protection devices during release the compressed air from the CB

  6. Switch off the external DC supply and AC supply

  7. Record counter reading of operation counter and no of operation hours of compressors

  8. Measurement the DC resistance of contacts of CB

  9. Test (dew point, air volume percentage & decomposition products) of the SF6 gas

  10. Suction the SF6 gas from the CB

Steps of Dismantling and Maintenance

  1. Dismantle the terminals from the both sides of CB

  2. Dismantle the twin head, dismantle the capacitors and transport the twin head to work shop

  3. Measure the capacitance of the capacitors

  4. Measure the (closing & opening) strokes and record it

  5. Dismantle the parallel (preinsertion) resistors from twin head end measure the resistance

  6. Check and maintenance the mechanism and moving contacts of resistors

  7. Check the presence of overheat and flashover on breaking units components

  8. Check the presence of surface defects or cracks on breaking units components

  9. Dismantle the (moving stationary) contacts of interrupters, clean and lubricate the (puffer cy1., arcing pins, crown)

  10. Inspect the external insulator of breaking units

  11. Replace the old O - rings by new O - rings before mounting the all parts of twin head and parallel resistors

    Steps before Mounting the twin head on Circuit Breaker

  12. Measure the (closing, opening) strokes and compare it with the reading of step 4 in the previous steps

  13. Measure the resistance of parallel resistors and compare it by the previous reading

  14. Dismantle and maintenance (drive group, closing value, opening value, main piston) of pneumatic mechanism (lubricate all parts and new - o - ring)

  15. Change the gas. SF6 isolating flange by new one

  16. Transport twin head from workshop to site of CB and mantle it

  17. Mantle all parts of pneumatic mechanism of step (14)

  18. Check and inspect the insulation of support column and its tightening

  19. Repeat all steps (1 to18) every twin head and pneumatic mechanism of all C.B phases respectively

Steps of main ten ma of air compressors

  1. Check the air values and change the oil

  2. Check the air filter and safety valves

  3. Check the all air pistons

  4. Change o-rings and gaskets for air - pipelines

  5. Check air dryer

  6. Check the all components of control cabinet

  7. Switch on air compressors to fill the receivers by compressed air

  8. Check the pneumatic mechanism for air leaks

  9. Perform the vacuum for all breaking units and support columns of CB

  10. Fill the all phases of CB by SF6 gas and test (the gas protection stages, dew point, air volume percentage and decomposition test)

  11. Measure the (closing & opening) times for each phase

Preparation of switch on the Circuit Breaker

  1. Connect the terminals on CB sides and clean the insulation of CB

  2. Switch on DC supply control of CB

  3. Switch on the CB several times

  4. Record counter reading of operation counter and no of operation hours of compressors

  5. Measure the hot spots of all part of CB
