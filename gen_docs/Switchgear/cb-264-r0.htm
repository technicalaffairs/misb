Introduction

This document outlines the contact system check to be done for circuit breaker up to 50 KV rated short circuit breaking current every time the circuit breaker is operated (open or close ) the contact system is subjected to mechanical or electrical stress.

The electrical stress is evident by virtue of erosion on the components. The intensity of thus erosion depends on the amplitude of the current switched with frequent switching and / or high breaking currents, the stress dependant wear determines. The point in time when a contact system check must be implemented:

  • The max. permissible number of the interruption: the relation ship between the breaking current and max. permissible no. of interruptions (shown in fig. 11). If the interruption  take place with higher current, the no. of possible interruptions decrease (as shown as in the fig. 2)

NOTE: The contact tube is already with down.

Condition of CB during work:  Circuit breaker out of service and must be isolated.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff must carry the suitable ID safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear safety and health equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, safety belts ... etc.

  • The circuit breaker must be isolated and grounded from both sides..

  • Isolate the service voltage and control voltage.

  • The appropriate local safety regulation should be observed.

 

Tools and Equipment

  • Cleaning tools.

  • Safety belt, safety helmet.

  • Special tools needed.

Measuring the Nozzle Distance

  1. Use et (22.1) to the nozzle (22.11.1 (fig.3).

  2. Record the measurement using the depth gauge , measure the distance between the nozzle (22.0-9.1) and the plate/ cowl (22.31) previously dismantled .

  3. the difference between dimension  (a ) and dimension (b ) is the distance (d ) between the nozzles . this measurement must be recorded in case the contact tube or the guide tube has to be replaced .if the contact carrier dismantled , its position in relation to the plate , together with the four metal brackets , must be marked on reassembly all parts must be refitted in their original position .

Removing the Moving Contact Tube        

  1. To detach the moving contact tube (22.7 fig.5 ), remove the bolts (22.7.5) and take out the fork (16. 9.1). Withdraw the moving contact tube from the guide tube (22.11).

  2. When the moving contact tube has been removed, undo the four screws from the guide tube and replace the tube.

  3. The moving contact tube and the blast cylinder must be replaced in pairs.

Assembling the Interrupter Unit

  1. The interrupter unit is assembled in reverse order as described for dismantling with due attention to marks provided during dismantling.

  2. Smooth the contact face of the flange plate with a brush only used for copper surfaces and apply a thin film of acid – free Vaseline. Screw down the guide tube and grease its out side with a very thin film of centoplex 24 DL.

  3. Slide the moving contact tube (22.7) over the guide tube in such a manner that the boreholes for the bolt (22.7.5 ) and the slots of the moving contact tube are in line .enter the fork  (16.9.1) from the flange (22.23) plate and fix to the moving contact tube by means of bolt (22.7.5). Make sure the centering pin (16.9.5)  has the correct position with respect to the lower terminal plate (22.22)

  4. Apply a very thin film of the centoplex 24 DL to the outer cylinder surface of the moving contact tube(22.7). plug the assembled( blast cylinder complete) nodule with the studs through the borehole of the moving contact, making sure that it fits properly , and screw down to the flange plate (22.23) . slide the blast cylinder(22.5) over the moving contact tube. following this mount the porcelain jacket (22.1) and measure the centre offset.

 

Measuring the Free Gas Path and the Centre offset

After replacement of the contact and guide tubes , the free gas path and the centre offset must be measured .

Measuring the Free Gas Path

  1. Using a depth gauge ( measuring  range ≥ 1000mm ), determine clearance "b" between plate (22.31) and arc – quenching nozzle (22.9.1 fig. 8 page 5- 0150/6).

  2. Likewise by means of a depth gauge , determine clearance "c" between sealing surface of the porcelain jacket and the blast cylinder ( 22.5 fig .4 page 5-0150/3 )

  3. The difference between "b" and "c" is the clearance in gas.

  4. The nominal dimension (e.g 20,30, or 40mm) is subject to a tolerance of -2/+3 mm if this tolerance is exceeded,  the approved free gas path must be reestablished by exchanging the parts.

Measuring the Centre offset

  1. Mount the plate kowl (22.31) with the contact tube and the contact tube support.

  2. Remove the cover from the plate kowl  and fit mounting plate 1 . in its place . Introduce the rod 2 . through the mounting plate .

Measuring Principle

  1. The front end of the rod is fitted with a spike that matches the inside diameter of the nozzle.

  2. With the approved centre offset, the spike can be pushed into the graphite nozzle of the guide tube .

  3. Like all other special tools, the centre offset testing device W371 can be obtained on loan from the filed services department ANL A4 erlangen.
