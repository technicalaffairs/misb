Introduction

This document outlines the visual inspection I1, which  must be carried out every six months as indicated by M6 in the title block.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding work area is required.

  • Wear your safety equipment as helmet, shoes, breathing mask, safety glass .. etc

  • Before cleaning touching or executing any maintenance of apparatus or live parts in medium and high voltage switchgears the following precautions have to be taken into consideration:

    • Disconnected completely.

    • Secure against re-connection.

    • Verify that the installation is dead.

    • Carry out earthing and short – circuiting.

    • Provide protection against adjacent live parts.

Tools and Equipment

  • Safety helmet, safety beet, and tool set

  • DC 1000 V megger

  • Air blower and brushes

  • Fine cloth for cleaning purpose

  • PVC ladders, breathing mask, rags

 

 

Work to be Carried out

General Checks

  1. Wearing and maintenance labels present and well readable

  2. Operating and maintenance instructions on site and up to date

  3. Doors and fences around switchyard in proper condition.

  4. All cable channels protected by covers.

  5. Free access to emergency exits.

  6. Switchyard, indoor and emergency lighting in proper service conditions

  7. All operating and maintenance tools available

  8. Important spare parts available

Mechanical Checks

  1. Clean pedestals and steel structures, check no visible corrosion.

  2. Check that all metallic parts (switchgear, structures, and fences) are well connected to earthing grid.

  3. Check that  porcelain insulators are without cracks and pollution. No traces from partial discharges (visual inspection with glass)

  4. Check that screwed connections are without missing or loose bolts and nuts.

  5. Inspect the high voltage connections by glass.

Electrical Checks

  1. Check that there is no standing alarms or unusual signals.

  2. Check of indication signals by lamp test.

  3. Check that there is no unusual noises or smells on the switchgears.

  4. Check that the indicating and measuring instruments are working.

Return the Circuit Breaker into Service

  1. Disconnect the earthing and short circulating.

  2. Ensure that there is no any tools or any cleaning materials in the work area.

  3. Lift up the safety fence with caution mark surrounding the work area.

  4. Finish the work permit with coordination with shift engineer.
