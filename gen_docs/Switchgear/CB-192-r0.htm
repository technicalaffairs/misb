Introduction

This document lists the testing procedures P1, which must be performed every two years as indicated by Y2 in the title block.

This is a multi-channel microprocessor based event event recorder primarily intended for timing circuit breakers. Specially designed input stages makes the event recorder virtually unaffected by the various sources of interference, normally found in the high voltage switch yards.

The instrument registers the making and breaking time of main and auxiliary contacts. Live contacts operation can also be measured i.e. dc contacts used in control circuits. The test results are printed out on a built-in thermal printer in plain language. The paper is standard thermal - sensitive - paper and is easily replaced

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category, 

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear your safety helmet, safety shoes .... etc.

  • Circuit Breaker must be out of service.

Tools and Equipment

  • The timer (TM-16)

  • Four long wires for the connection between (TM-16) and the two sides of circuit breaker as shown in the figure

  • Avometer, Jampers

  • Wires for signals of triping and closing.

 

 

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Switch on the power supply (220Vac) to the timer

  2. Connect the DC voltage to input voltage of the instrument (TM-16) (220v or 110v) (-,+ ve)

  3. Connect the signal wires for closing coil (-,+ve) and press push-button (ready) until the red led light then press push-button (start)

  4. Remove the signal wires for closing coil and connect these wires for tripping coil (1) (-,+ve) and press push-button (ready) until the red led light then press push-button (start)

  5. Remove the signal wires for tripping coil (1) of circuit breaker and close the circuit breaker manually

  6. Connect the signal wires for tripping coil (2) (-,+ ve) of the circuit breaker to instrument (TM-16) and press push-button (ready) until the red led light then press push-button (start)

  7. Keep the printed-out sheet of the timer which is the measuring time for closing coil and tripping coil (1,2)

Return Circuit Breaker into Service

  1. Switch off the power supply for timer.

  2. Remove the signal wires between the timer and the tripping coil or the closing coil.

  3. Remove the bridges on the timer and the four long wires between the timer and the two sides of the circuit breaker.

  4. Keep the printed-out sheet in the maintenance file.

  5. Close the work permit.
