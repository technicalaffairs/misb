Introduction

This document lists the equipment inspection I4, of emergency diesel engine, which must be performed every 2500-operation hr base as indicated by H 2500 in the title block.

The first draft initiated by eng.: Hesham M. Hassan, reviewed by Maintenance director: eng. Saad Mostafa, revised by eng.: Samir Abdel-Gelil at EDEPCo., accepted by maintenance general director: eng. Ali Mostafa Al-lithy, and approved by eng. Mostafa Alam Head  Sector of TALKHA steam power station 2 x 210 MW.

Safety Precautions

  • Work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff must carry the suitable ID safety category,

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area,

  • Wear appropriate personal protective equipment must be used: safety shoes, helmet, breathing mask, safety glasse

  • Isolate the circuit breaker for the diesel engine.

    •

Tools and Equipment

  • Test equipment level "dip sticks",

  • Multimeter, and common sets keys,

  • Cleaning material "rags, cloth" and screw driver set

  • Complete maintenance tool set.

  • Cleaning fluid.

  • Feeler gauge.

  • Suitable lifting device.

After isolated the diesel engine mechanically and electrically

  1. Check the oil level in the tank and / or refill it from the storage tank up to the top mark on the level gauge.

  2. Turn the handles of the coarse (lamination type) oil cleaner fitted in the oil circuit.

  3. Check the clogging of the fine oil cleaners acc.to the differential pressure gauges data.

  4. Check the amount of leakage from stuffing box of fuel pump and prevented it by retighten the gland bolts or replace the sealing ring { it must be dropped some drops to cool the gland}.

  5. Check the temperature of the crankshaft bearings by touching the side lids of the upper engine case, thus the safety of operation is increased.
            .  At higher temperature it is necessary to dismantle the lids and to check the condition of bearing cca 10 min after engine stopping.

  6. Check the suction pipe for tightness. Imperfect tightness will manifest itself in the output of the engine And remove any leakage if found.

  7. Remove the cylinder heads and inspect the following parts.

Suction and exhaust valves

  1. Check the suction and exhaust valves for tightness

  2. Remove the carbon deposits from the exhaust valve seats.

  3. Re-machine the valve on the milling machine and grind them

Starting valves

  1. Clean the starting valves of rust, impurities and water.

  2. Re-grind the valves if necessary.

  3. During assembly, smear the valves guide with thick grease.

Note: If water is found in the starting valves, this means that the water has not been drained from the starting air cylinder properly.


Pressure relief valves

  1. Check mainly the valve seats for tightness( by pouring fuel into them).

  2. Check the condition of the spring and replace it by new one if needed.

  3. Re-adjust the relief valve again.

The Pistons and piston rings

  1. Dismantle the pistons and remove piston rings.

  2. Carry out a visual inspection of the pistons and clean them of carbon.

  3. Clean carefully seized parts of pistons with a smooth file.

  4. Clean also the grooves of the piston rings of carbon.

  5. Check the rings wear, measure the clearance in the piston ring lock , with the ring inserted into the cylinder liner.

    - Care is to be taken that the rings are measured always in the cylinder in which they were run -in.

  6. Change the upper rings with the lower ones. If the clearance of the two upper rings is larger than the wear limit {See table of clearance Table(1)}.

  7. Check scraper rings wear during operation which causes increasing in the oil consumption.

  8. Be sure when cleaning the grooves for scraper rings it is necessary to check whether the oil drain holes under rings are not clogged.  

  9. Check the slide surfaces of big end bearings and crank pins during dismantle the pistons.

  • Remove any defect on the bearing by scraping, on the big end journals with a smooth file.

  •  Care is to be taken that the file dust may not penetrate into the oil holes in the crankshaft.

  1. Check visually the cylinder heads, at the inspection of the pistons.

  • Remove the carbon deposits.

  • Clean any seized points with a fine grindstone.

  • Take care to prevent the grinding dust from falling into the case.

  1. Check the tightness of the cylinder head’s, incase of un-tightness, tighten the bolts of cylinder heads.

  2. De-water the air vessel with head.

  3. Check the lubrication of rocker arms and valve guides and adjusted the regulation valves of lubrication if needed.

  4. Check whether the suction and exhaust valve clearance are correct by means of gauges.

At a cold engine

  • Check the clearance between the valve and the rocker arm roller with the responding cam in its basic position.

  • Adjust the clearance if necessary.

During operation (Immediately after stopping)

  • Check whether the clearance is sufficient by checking with cam in their basis position, whether the valves push rods can be turned.

Important:: If the valve push rod cannot be turned, the clearance is not correct and the valve dos not close fully.

  1. Clean the crankcase and the oil tank.

  2. Change the lubricating oil if necessary (See major maintenance procedure).            

  3. Check the fuel injection pump, check particularly the pump plunger wear.

  • Uncouple the fuel injection pump from the fuel injection valves and close it tightly. (recommended to use a plug screw for this propose)

  • Take out the pump by hand by means of the lever supplied with the engine tools.

  • Fit the lever into the hole in the tappet guide body above the fuel injection pump tappet.

  • If there is a hard resistance, it is a sign that the piston clearance is correct.

  • If there is a soft resistance, (with the lever slowly dropping), it is a sign that the piston clearance is large and piston must be replaced.

  1. Check the nuts tightening of injection cams (See major maintenance procedure) .

  2. Clean and flush with fuel the suction strainer in the oil tank.

  3. Let the sludge and water out of the fuel tank.

  4. Clean the fine double fuel cleaner, located on the upper part of the crankcase, at the front of the engine:

  • Wash the cleaning insert in the fuel oil and flush out cleaner casing.

  • For servicing during operation, any of the two units can be putout of service by turning a control pin. 

  1. Clean the fine oil cleaner acc. to the instruction supplied with the cleaner.

  2. Drain the sludge from the coarse (lamination – typed) oil cleaner and / or remove the inserts.

  • Clean the body of cleaner.

  • Wash the inserts in fuel oil.

  1. Check the flow of water through the engine, be sure that there is no clogging by impurities, throttling or clogging with scales on the water transfer pipe and remove the scales if found in the cooling spaces of the engine.

  • Before cleaning the water spaces, flush the engine, if possible, with a flow of water admitted in the direction opposite to that of normal cooling.

  • However, it is first necessary to connect the water pipe so that the impurities flushed out may not penetrate into the water electric pump.

  • Drain the water from the cooling spaces of the engine.

  • Remove the water-collecting pipe from the engine and other plugs and covers of the cooling spaces of the engine.

  • Remove mechanically the mud, sand and other deposits from the cooling spaces.

  • Clean mainly the water passages from the upper case to the transfer pipe and from that pipe to the cylinder head.

  • Having cleaned the water spaces, flush them with a flow of water

  1. Clean the air cleaner of the turbo-blower.

  • Wash it in fuel oil.

  1.  Clean the air distributor and smear it with grease.

  2. Check the fuel injection valves or removal of the valves from the cylinder head (by means of a nozzle tester )

  • Check the setting of the opening pressure.

  • Check the fuel is sprayed by the valve through all holes and clean these holes if it is clogged by means of the nozzle cleaner.

  1. Check the function and the reliability of the signaling equipment. When checking and adjust the signaling equipment, it is absolutely necessary to check continuously the operating conditions of the engine specially the oil pressure.

  2. Keep the surface of the engine clean.

Important:

* If you need any help please go to the major maintenance procedure.
* After successful assemblies, coordinate with the shift operation engineer to prepare 

   the diesel for re-starting to check that every thing is Ok.

* Read all the available record value and putted in table (1) to compare between the

   values before and after Inspection.
