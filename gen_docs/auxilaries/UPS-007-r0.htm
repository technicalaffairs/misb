Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff must carry the suitable ID safety category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required,

  • Wear the appropriate safety equipment as safety shoes, helmet,

  • Maintenance and repair of the equipment should only be undertaken by trained, experience and authorized electrical personnel.

  • Use only insulated tools, particularly when connecting a battery. Stand on a rubber material.

  • Personal effects of metal such as rings, watches, bracelets and necklaces which may cause a short circuit and metal subjects (pens, tools etc.) which may fall from open pockets while bending over batteries or chargers should be removed.

  • Isolate all AC supplies, auxiliary supplies and the battery (open isolator, remove battery fuses or remove a battery link) before working on the equipment. And never handle a component or assembly without personally confirming that no voltage is applied pr present.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set .

  • Digital voltmeter.

  • Brush., Air Blower

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Check all alarms and status indicators

  2. Check meter readings are normal and compare them with the test certificate.

  3. Check the cause of any significant changes as the change of load or recent discharge.

  4. Check ventilation devices are clean and clear of obstructions.

  5. Visually check electrical connections and components for signs of overheating or corrosion and rectify as necessary.

  6. Check painted and plated components for signs of damage or corrosion.
