Safety Precautions

  • Work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff must carry the suitable ID safety category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required..

  • Wear standard personal safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, hearing protection, rubber, gloves, safety glasses ... etc.

  • Isolate the circuit breaker for the Diesel Engine.

  • The pump must be isolated mechanically by closing suction and delivery valves.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set

  • Cleaning fluid, Soft brush

  • Suitable lifting device, Feeler gauge

  • Hammer, Torque wrench, Manual puller

  • Dial indicator.

  • K&N air filter cleaner

  • K&N air filter oil

Work to be Carried out

Disassembly

Preparatory Work

  1. Remove all dirt and mud from around the engine.

  2. Remove drain plug and drain oil from engine.

  3. Prepare a stable stand which will prevent the engine from falling over, then put the engine securely in the stand.

Starting Motor

  1. Remove starting motor.

Engine Block

  1. Install bracket A1 to engine assemble 1 onto engine repair stand A using bracket  

  2. For The Engine, Install A1 to the Engine First

  3. For The Transmission, Install A1 to the Stand First

Alternator

  1. Loosen mounting bolt and nut of alternator plate, and mounting bolt and nut of alternator.

  2. Move alternator towards cylinder block, and remove belt 2.

Attention:

be careful not to catch your fingers or damage the alternator when moving it towards the cylinder block.

  1. Remove alternator.

  2. Remove oil cooler.

  3. Remove exhaust manifold.

  4. Pull out pilot portion, then remove water pump.

Fuel Filter

  1. Disconnect hoses between pump and filter at pumped end.

  2. Remove fuel filter

Fuel Injection Pipe

  1. Remove clamp, then

  2. remove sleeve nut, then remove fuel injection pipe.

Spill Tube

Remove Spill Tube

Notice:

When removing the nozzle holder, the inserted part is easierto pull out if the spill tube is removed after the nozzle holder is removed.

Fuel Injection Pump

  1. Remove oil supply tube.

  2. Remove fuel injection pump holder bolt.

  3. Remove gear and nut, then remove fuel injection pump.

  4. The is no bolt installed from from the fuel injection pump side , so do not forget to remove it.

  5. to prevent dirt or dust from entering , fit caps our use tape to cover the oil and fuel inlet and outlet ports 

Intake Manifold

Remove intake manifold

Nozzle Holder

  1. Remove nozzle holder.

  2. When removing the nozzle holder, clean around the nozzle holder and fit ablind plug to prevent dust or dirt from entering.

  3. Be careful not to damage the tip of the nozzle holder when removing.

  4. Mark the nozzle holders with tags showing the cylinder no. and keep in a safe place, if there is no abnormality in the nozzle holder, install in the same position when assembling.

  5. Remove cylinder head cover.

  6. Remove rocker arm assembly.

  7. When removing the rocker arm, loosen the locknut and turn the adjustment screw back 2 to 3 turn

Push Rods

  1. Remove push rods from cylinder head.

  2. Mark the push rods with tags to show the places of installation and keep in asafe place. if there is no abnormality in the push rod , install in the same position when assembling

Cylinder Head Assembly

  1. Remove mounting bolts of cylinder head assembly.

  2. Fit lifting tool in mount on cylinder head, then lift off cylinder head assembly with crane.

  3. Lay card board on the floor to protect the bottom face of the cylinder head from damage.

Disassemble the Cylinder Head Assemble   as follows:

  1. Using spring pusher B, compress valve spring, then remove valve cotter.

  2. Loosen spring, and remove spring seat 3 and valve spring.

  3. Raises cylinder head and remove valve.

Note:

  • Mark the valves with tags to show the place of installation and keep in a safe place.

  • Using remover C, remove valve guide.

Gear Case Cover

  1. Remove gear case cover.

  2. Remove front oil seal.

  3. Remove oil pan.

  4. Remove oil pump.

Idler Gear

  1. Remove bolts then remove plate.

  2. Remove gear.

Cam Shaft Assembly

  1. Turn over cylinder block, so that cylinder head mounting face is facing down.

  2. Remove mounting bolts through casting hole in gear.

  3. When removing, rotate the camshaft lightly and be careful not to damage the bushing.

  4. Remove oil suction pipe.

Piston Connecting Rod Assembly

  1. Checking connecting rod cap number

  2. Turn over cylinder block, and set with cylinders horizontal.

  3. Check that the number stamped on connecting rod cap matches cylinder number.

Note:  If there is no number stamped on the connecting rod cap, stamp a number at the camshaft end.

Removing Carbon using Fine Sand Paper

  1. Remove Carbon at top of cylinder

  2. Be careful not to damage the inside of the cylinder.                           

  3. If necessary, measure the end play of the connecting rod with dial gauge before removing the piston and connecting rod assembly.

  4. Connecting rod cap

  5. Rotate the crankshaft to set the piston at bottom dead center.

  6. Remove mounting bolts of connecting rod cap.

  7. Tap cap with plastic hammer and remove connecting rod cap together with bearing.

  8. Be careful not to damage the thread of the mounting bolts.

  9. Piston, connecting rod assembly: push a wooden bar handle through from oil pan side. Support piston at cylinder head end, then push bar in further from oil pan side to remove piston and connecting rod assembly.

Disassemble Piston and Connecting Rod Assembly as Follows:    

  1. Using pliers, remove snap ring on one side.

  2. Remove piston pin, and separate connecting rod and piston.

  3. If the pin does not come out, soak in hot water.

  4. Remove snap ring on other side in the same way.

  5. Using piston tool, remove piston ring from piston.

  6. Using push tool, remove bushing of connecting rod

Crankshaft

Remove crankshaft, be careful not to hit the crankshaft against the cylinder block and damage the sliding surface.
