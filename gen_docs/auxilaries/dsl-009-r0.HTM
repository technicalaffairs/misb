Introduction

This document lists the major maintenance procedure P2 of emergency diesel engine, which must be performed every 25000 operation hours as indicated by H 25000 in the title block.

The technical specifications for the diesel stated at the end of the document.

The first draft initiated by eng.: Hesham M. Hassan, reviewed by Maintenance director: eng. Saad Mostafa, revised by eng. Samir Abdel-Gelil at EDEPCo., accepted by maintenance general director: eng. Aly Mostafa Al-lithy and approved by eng. Mostafa Alam Head  Sector of Talkha steam power station 2 x 210 MW.

Safety Precautions

  • Work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff must carry the suitable ID safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area.

  • Wear appropriate personal protective equipment must be used: safety shoes, helmet, breathing mask, safety glasses

  • Isolate the circuit breaker for the diesel engine.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set.

  • Cleaning fluid, Feeler gauge,

  • Suitable lifting device.

After isolated the diesel engine mechanically and electrically

 

Fig (1) & fig (2) help you to disassemble well

  1. Clean carefully the dismantled point and the surfaces close to it with fuel oil, at complete dismantling of the engine, the whole engine is cleaned.

  2. Care is to be taken to preserve cleanliness when dismantling the engine.

  3. Wash every part in fuel oil and wipe it with clean rags or with blast of compressed air

  4. Use only suitable tools for disassembly.

  5. Take good notes of its position on the engine so that reassembly may be facilitated, before dismantling any part from the engine.

  6. Be sure take note of any marks or items no. on them , acc. to which they should be re-assembled. Before disassembling the parts and assemblies,

  7. Protect the engine from dirt, to close the open points, cover the outlets with oiled paper and wrap the pipe ends with paper. 

  8. Check whether the parts are in good condition or whether they are to be repaired or replaced with new ones. 

  9. Unscrew the nuts of all bolts a turn or more and then only remove all the nuts. When dismantling parts or assemblies,

  10. Be sure screw in all the bolts and nuts in their original position after the parts or assemblies so that they may not be lost or exchanged .Tie the shims and washers together and mark them so that they may not be lost or exchanged during the re-assembly. 

  11. Take care no excessive force should be applied to dismantle any part. If a big force is required, it is necessary to ascertain the cause and to remove it.

  12. Check whether the parts are suitable for further use, by examining and measuring the parts, determining the clearance (or interference) between contact surfaces, testing for tightness, etc     
        - Replace damage or worn parts by new ones.
        - Make sure that the correct clearance or interference is observed when replaced the parts    
        - Record the values of clearances, which measure during, disassemble for all parts of engine in table (3). 

  13. Clean every part and lubricated the moving parts with clean oil before starting to reassemble.

Complete Engine Disassembly

  1.  Drain the water, lubricated oil and fuel oil from the engine and all pipes.

  2. Close the air inlet.

  3. Disconnect the engine from the driven machine.

  4. Dismantle the glass thermometer, the instrument board and other measuring instruments and the transmitters of electric instruments from the engine.

  5. Loosen and remove all water, oil, air and fuel pipes, which would hinder the dismantling of the engine.

  6. Remove the suction tube and exhaust piping from the engine cylinder heads, also the charging elbow at the supercharged engine.

  7. Dismantle the double the double fuel cleaner.

  8. Remove the starting equipment with feed pipe.

  9. Dismantle the front cover.

  10. Dismantle the flywheel from the crankshaft.

  11. Dismantle the lateral lids and covers on the engine.

  12. Dismantle the cylinder heads.

  13. Dismantle the valve push rods and the valve push rod covers.

  14. Dismantle the turbo-supercharger with the bracket (at supercharged engine).

  15. Dismantle the connecting rods with the pistons.

  16. Dismantle the governor

  17. Dismantle the rear cover.

  18. Dismantle the upper engine case.

  19. Dismantle the lids with shells of all the main bearings of the crankshaft

  20. Dismantle the crankshaft

Disassembly of Individual assemblies
 

Dismantling of the Front cover with Pump

  1. Dismantle the water, oil, fuel and air piping.

  2. Dismantle the fuel pump.

  3. Dismantle the oil pump from the front cover of engine.

  4. Dismantle the lid with tachogenerator drive.

  5. Dismantle the front cover from the crankcase.

Disassembly of Cylinder Head

  1. Remove the exhaust gas thermometer, the cooling water thermometer and the indicator cock.

  2. Remove the cylinder head cover.

  3. Remove the valve push rods by raising the valve rocker arms.

  4. Remove the bolts joining the sucking tube with the cylinder head.

  5. Loosen and remove the bolts of the exhaust pipe flanges at the cylinder head.

  6. Dismantle all pipes which would hinder the disassembly of the corresponding cylinder head (water collecting pipe, air inlet pipe, fuel inlet / outlet pipes and oil inlet pipe And / or instrument board).

  7. Loosen gradually the nuts of the cylinder head bolts for one turn and then remove them.

  8.  Screw in two suspension bolts, suspend the cylinder head, moves it above the studs and place it aside.

Disassembly of the Connecting Rod with Piston

  1. Dismantle the corresponding cylinder head.

  2. Dismantle the corresponding lateral cover of the upper part of the crankcase.

  3. Remove the split pins of connecting rod bolts.

  4. Loosen and screw off the connecting rod bolt-nuts. At the same time hold the lower big end lid to prevent it from falling into the crankcase. 

  5. Moving out the connecting rod bolts.

  6. Remove the Lower big end lid with the shell through the hole in the lower lid of case.

  7. Screw the suspension of piston firmly on the piston crown. Clean the threads of bolts before.

  8. Remove the pistons with the connecting rod shank and shell and place it aside by means of lifting device. When removing the piston, watches through the hole in the lower lid of casing that the connecting rod flange does not catch the side of cylinder bush and does not damage it.

  9. Dismantle the piston after the circlips have been removed and gudgeon pin has been driven out by means of special fixture.

  10. Dismantle all the pistons from engine one after another from the 1st to the 6th cylinder.

  11. Turn the crankshaft on removal of the flywheel, by means of a wooden beam or an iron bar placed between 3 to 4 bolts inserted into the holes in the crankshaft flange.

CAUTATION:

After the crankshaft has been turned into the TDCs for dismantling the 2nd, 3rd, 5th and 6th cylinders, it is necessary to secure in this position against swinging which might cause injury to the operator or damage to the engine. This may be done by securing the crankshaft in the crankcase by wooden beams or by two iron bars inserted between the flywheel bolts and bearings upon the floor.

Disassembly of Crankshaft

  1. Separate the upper and lower engine case.

  2. Remove the lids with shells of all crankshaft bearings.

  3. Dismantle the counterweights.

General instruction for Partial or Complete Assembly 

 

Fig. (1) & Fig. (2) help you to disassemble well

  1. Wash the parts with fuel oil by means of brush, wipe them with a rag which does not leave behind any fibers or clean them with a blast of compressed air. 

  2. Remove defects of all parts and assemblies.

  3. Check the fitting areas of the parts

  4. Blow all pipes or ports through with compressed air to remove all impurities settled in them

  5. Record the values of clearances, which measure after assemblies for all parts of engine in table 3

Assembly of Engine Parts

  1. Lubricate the friction surfaces of the parts.

  2. Protect the engine so that no dust and impurities may penetrate in.

  3. Maintain the working site clean and in good order.

  4. Take good notes of the marks and items Nos. on the parts or assemblies in order to fit them in the same position as before their disassembly.

  5. Tighten the bolts and nuts crosswise and gradually .Do not tighten them firmly one after another to avoid any deformation of the parts.

  6. Do not mount any other parts, unless the previous part is fitted or attached correctly.

  7. Check whether the assemblies are assembled correctly and whether they can be freely turned or moved.

  8. Use only new split pins and look- washers, undamaged and new gaskets and packing.

  9. Use only the necessary tools and fixtures.

  10. Assemble every part in reverse sequence of disassembly

Assembly of Crankshaft Bearings

 

Assembly of the main Bearings

  1. Clean thoroughly the seating surfaces and lubricate properly the main bearing bolts threads, nuts and bearing shell.

  2.  Insert the lower half of bearing shell into the clean bore in the lower part of the crankcase.

  3.  Insert the Crankshaft. Fit the upper half of bearing shell with the cap ( Observe the numbering of the caps and shells)

  4. Tighten firmly the nuts (So that the shell seats down), loosen them and tighten again.

  5. Tighten alternately and uniformly the nuts to the line of marking of nut and shell marked by marker.

  6. Check the clearances. In vertical plan the clearance must be within 0.17 - 0.256 mm in the plan measured 10 mm from the dividing plan of both sides and on both bridges it must be for 0.02 - 0.1 mm larger than the clearance measured in the vertical plan.

  7. Assemble every part in reverse sequence of disassembly.

Assembly of the Big end Bearings

  1. Clean thoroughly the seating surfaces and lubricate properly the connecting rod bolts.

  2. Measure the connecting rod bolt in loose condition by using the measuring yoke supplied with the engine tools and indicated by the engine No. and the nominal length of bolts which inserting feelers between the recessed end of bolt and the seating surface of the yoke .Fig(3)

  3. Fit the connecting rod lid with shell and inserted connecting rod bolts to the crankshaft pin and connecting rod shackle and connecting rod bolts are lightly tightened by nuts

  4. (Observe the numbering on the connecting rod and bolts)

  5. Tighten the nuts (acc. to the tightening of main bolt joints).

  6. Assemble every part in reverse sequence of disassembly.

Assembly of Cylinder Heads

  1. Be sure the gaskets used must be straight, not damaged and annealed.

  2. Place the gasket on the cylinder liner and centered accurately so that it may not protrude over the edge of the liner and fits properly in the recess.

  3. Mount the cylinder head on the bolts and fit it to the cylinder liner so that the gasket is not shifted or reformatted.

  4. Check the seating of the cylinder head and gasket whether the gasket is not squeezed at any point. Replace the gasket if damaged.

  5. Set the valve clearance see clearance table (Table No. 1).

  6. The nuts of cylinder head bolts should be tighten (acc. to the tightening of main bolt joints).

  7. Assemble every part in reverse sequence of disassembly.

Tightening of main Bolt Joints

 

Tightening of cylinder Head Bolt Nuts

  1. Be sure the nuts of cylinder head tightened crosswise by means of the cylinder head spanner extended with a bar.

  2. Do the tightening in two steps.
    - First, all cylinder head nuts are tightened for the bolt prolongation of approx. 0.15-mm.
    - The next, tightening is done for the prolongation of bolt of 0.25-0.3 mm. After the engine has been started a check for tightness is made

  3. Bolts prolongation is measured by depth gauge with vernier accuracy of 0.02 mm.

  4. During measurement, arms of gauge are supported on the upper surface of nut and the movable part is moved out so that it touches the ground bolt surface in nut.

  5. The difference between measured values before and after the tightening of the cylinder head bolts means the value of bolt prolongation.

Tightening of connecting Rod Bolt Nuts

  1. Check the connecting rod surface from the mechanical break of surface or any defect. If the mechanical break is larger, bolts must be exchanged.

  2. Replace all bolts with found permanent deformation larger than 0.1 mm (permanent bolt prolongation at free state).

  3. Measure the length of bolt at free state by means of measuring shackle by underputting the feeler gauge between the recessed bolt end and shackle seating (See Fig 4). The clearance “A” can be found from the clearance table (Table 1).

  4. Assemble the connecting rod bolts acc. to prescript numbering as shown in Fig (3).

  5. Be sure the nuts of the connecting rod Bolts are crosswise tightened to the line of marking of nut and bolt which corresponds to prescript prolongation of connecting rod bolts (0.2 – 0.28 mm) up to position of a cut in a nut and a hole in a bolt for a split pin.

  6. Measure the connecting rod bolts prolongation by measuring shackle and feeler as shown in Fig (4).

  7. After tightening the nuts of the connecting rod bolts they are ensured by a split pin which must be not move after its opening. 

Tightening of Bolt Nuts of Main Bearings

  1. Be sure the upper shell and the bearing lids are assembled.

  2. Tighten all nuts crosswise to the line of nuts and shell marking for the given prolongation (0.22 to 0.26 mm) marked by manufacturer.

  3. Check the clearance.

  4. Lock the nuts against loosening by a lock.

  5. Be sure prolongation check is not carried out, unless at bolt exchange by means of dial indicator as shown in Fig (5).

  6. Mark identically the nuts and bolts again by a line.

  7. Be sure under this tightening the bolts must have an extension of 0.22-0.26 mm.

Tightening of bolts of crankshaft counterweights

  1. Tighten the bolts of crankshaft counterweights with a tightening torque of 245 – 343 Nm.

Tightening of bolts of Injection Cams

  1.  Tighten the bolts of divided injection cams for prolongation of 0.09 to 0.12 mm (It means turning the nut for 60°).

  2.  Be sure all nuts are locked as shown in Fig (6)

Tightening of bolts of Fundamental Frame to engine Case

    1.    Tighten the bolts of fundamental frame with a tightening torque of 350 – 450 Nm.

Pressure Test

    1.    Tested every part according to the pressure test table (Table No.2).

Re-lubrication

    1.    Use the recommended oil type or its equivalent.

Cleaning of lubricating oil

  1. Clean the oil either by means of two type RHO 3a centrifugal cleaners at the discharge of oil pump of a capacity of 600 l/hr, at a pressure 0.2 Mpa which operate continuously or by an independent electric centrifuge included in the engine room equipment, which is started at certain interval acc. to its out put , so that the cleaned quantity corresponds to the quantity cleaned by two RHO 3a units with this alternative the RHO 3a centrifugal cleaners are not necessary.
