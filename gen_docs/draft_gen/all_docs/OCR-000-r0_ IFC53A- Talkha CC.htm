Introduction

This document lists the testing procedure P1, which must be performed on an annually bases as indicated by Y1 in the above title block

The type IFC53A Relays are extended range, single phase very inverse time over current relays. This type is likely to provide faster over all protection.

 The variation in the magnitude of fault current thru the relay is dependent upon the location of the fault with respect to the relay. The applications of these relays requires three relays for multiphase fault protection one per phase, and a separate relay connected for single phase to ground faults.

The first draft initiated by eng. Fayza El-Sharkawy, tested by eng. Shirin Hamdy & eng. Azza Salama, revised by EDEP Co. Quality Control eng. Adel El-Nagar, accepted by General Director eng. Mahmoud Saker and approved by eng. Moustfa Alam Head Sector of Talkha Power Station.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • The generator must be out of service.

Tools and Equipment

  • Test Plugs.                                

  • Test Leads.

  • Secondary Injection test set.

Work to be Carried Out

Preparation

  1. Make sure PT secondary circuit are opened at the incoming terminal blocks before isolating the relay voltage circuit for test

  2. Look at field maintenance file and compare the position of the setting switches on relay with those shown on the setting sheet.

Testing the Relay

  1. Connect the test leads between the relay current terminals ( 3-4 ).

  2. Connect the test leads between the relay contacts ( 1-2 ) .

  3. Increase the current slowly until the relay picks up.

  4. Record the pick up value in the test sheet.

  5. Increase the current to the values ( 2 In, 5 In & 10 In) where In is the rating current, until the relay trips at every current value.

  6. Record the trip time value at each current value in the test sheet
