Introduction

This document lists the testing procedure P1, which must be performed every six months as indicated by M6 in the above title block

This relay is  a negative – phase – sequence time over current relay used primarily to protect generators from unbalanced phase currents, it is providing essentially back – up protection it is recommended this procedure to be carried out every six months.

The first draft initiated by eng. Fayza El-Sharkawy, tested by eng. Shirin Hamdy & eng. Azza Salama, revised by EDEP Co. Quality Control eng. Adel El-Nagar, accepted by General Director eng. Mahmoud Saker and approved by eng. Moustfa Alam Head Sector of Talkha Power Station.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • The generator must be out of service.

Tools and Equipment

  • Test Plug handle.                                 

  • Clamp ammeter.

  • Secondary injection test.          

  • Test Leads.

Work to be Carried Out

Preparation

  1. Make sure CT secondary circuit are shorted at the incoming terminal blocks before isolating the relay current circuit for test

  2. Look at field maintenance fill and compare the position of the setting switches on relay with those shown on the setting sheet.

Testing the Relay

Phase to Phase Test

  1. Connect the test leads between current terminals (3 – 5).

  2. Connect the test leads between the trip contact (1 – 2).

  3. Increase the current slowly until the relay pick up (start).

  4. Record this current value as the start value in the test sheet.

  5. Increase the current to the values (2 In & 5 In & 10 In) from start.

  6. Record the current values & the time values at which the relay trips at every current value at the test sheet.

  7. Repeat the following steps at the current leads (3 – 7).

Phase to Earth Test

  1. Connect the test leads between the current terminals (3 – 4) & (5 - 6) & (7 – 8).

  2. Repeat the steps from 2 to 6 for the terminals (3 – 4) & (5 - 6) & (7 – 8).
