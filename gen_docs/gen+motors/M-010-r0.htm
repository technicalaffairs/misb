Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection I3, which must performed every six months as indicated by M6 in the title block.

This motor is used for the following pumps: 

Boiler feed water pump, desalination water supply pump, fire fighting motor, closed cooling motor, service water pump, circulating water pump, condensate extraction pump

Safety precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff must carry the suitable ID safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear the appropriate safety and healthy equipment as safety shoes, helmet ... etc

  • Motor is out of service for some works if necessary.

  • High temperature may arise under operating conditions on the motor surfaces so that touching should be prevented or avoided.

Tools and Equipment

  • Kit, safety and healthy equipment

  • Megger for insulation resistance measurement

 

 

 

Work to be Carried out

Motor Windings

  1. Measure the insulation resistance ref to standard value.

  2. Inspect of coil end as follows:

    - Grease and dust accumulated on coils may cause insulation deterioration and poor cooling effect.

    - Moisture must not accumulate, coils must be kept warm when motor is used.

    - Discoloring and this is mainly caused by over heat.

  3. Ensure there is no untoward change of wedges from original position.

  4. Ensure that the binding at the coil end is in its normal position.

Clean the Interior of the Motor

  1. After a motor is in operation for some time, accumulation of dust, carbon powder and grease etc., are unavoidable and may cause damage.

  2. Points to be noted during cleaning.

If using Compressed air or a Blower

  1. Compressed air should be free of moisture.

  2. Maintain air pressure at 4 kg/cm2, since high pressure can cause damage to coils.

If using Vacuum

  1. Vacuum cleaning can be used, both before and after other methods of cleaning to remove loose dirt and debris. It is a very effective way to remove loose surface contamination from the windings without scattering. Vacuum cleaning tools should be non metallic to avoid any damage to the winding insulation.

Wiping

  1. Surface contamination on the winding can removed using a soft lint free wiping material. If contamination is oily, the wiping material can be moistened with a safety type petroleum solvent. In hazardous locations, a solvent such as inhibited methyl chloroform may be used but must be used sparingly and immediately removed. While this solvent is non flammable under ordinary conditions, it is toxic and proper health and safety precautions should be followed while using it.

  2. Keep core ducts completely clean. The difference in temperature rise could be around 10 oC before and after cleaning.

Clean the Exterior of the Motor

  1. On open ventilated motor, screens and louvers over the inlet air openings should not be allowed to accumulate any build up dirt, lint, etc. that could restrict free air movement.

  2. If the motor is equipped with air filters, they should be replaced or cleaned and reconditioned at a frequency that is dictated by conditions. It is better to replace or recondition filters too often that not often enough.

  3. Totally enclosed air to air cooled totally enclosed fan cooled require special cleaning consideration. The external fan must be cleaned thoroughly since any dirt build up not removed can lead unbalance and vibration. All of the tubes of the air to air heat exchanger should be cleaned using a suitable tube brush having synthetic fiber bristles.

Note:  Air Filters must be Cleaned every Week

Bearing Characteristics for Closed Cooling Water Pump Motor

Re-Greasing

It is advisable to regress when the motor is running to allow the new grease to be evenly distribute inside the bearing. before regressing, the inlet fitting should be thoroughly cleaned to prevent any accumulated dirt from being carried into the bearing with new grease. The outlet of grease drainage should be opened to allow the proper venting of old grease. Use grease gun to pump grease through grease nipple into bearing. After regressing operate the motor for 10-30 minutes to allow any excess grease to vent out.

Drive & Opp. Drive Bearing (Re-greasing Bearing )

Kind of grease : LITHIUM BASE NLGI#3

Period of re-greasing each  3000  hours

Selected grease  MOBILUX 3   (260 GM)
