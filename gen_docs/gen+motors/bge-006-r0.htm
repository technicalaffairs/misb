Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection I1, which must be performed on a weekly, semiannual, and annually bases as indicated by W1, M6 and Y1 in the title block.

The first draft initiated and tested by eng. Haytham Hemdan, reviewed by eng. Mohamed Abbass Ahmed, revised by EDEPC quality control eng. Adel Abdul Kader El Naggar, approved by eng. Tarek Youssef Ali, Head Sector of Ayoun Moussa Power Station.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance stuff should carry the suitable ID safety category

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required

  • Wear the appropriate safety equipment as safety shoes and helmet

  • It will be necessary to wear high voltage proof gloves for brush holder removal from or insertion into the carrier ring while the unit is in excited condition

  • To ensure proper field current transfer during checks and maintenance activities, remove only one plug-in brush holder at a time for each polarity. Removing the next brush holder is only permissible after the first one has been reinserted and positively participated in the field current transfer.

  • Unit in service

Tools and Equipment

  • New Carbon Brushes

  • Gage Stick with several notch marks

  • Dry linen cloths

  • Cleaning fluid (Rivolta or equivalent)

Work to be Carried Out

Carbon Brushes

  1. The excitation power requirements for the respective loads should be monitored continuously and entered into the operating log at regular intervals together with any special occurrences.

  2. The wear of each individual brush can be checked during operation without removing the brush holder by inserting a gage stick of insulating material into the bore of the telescopic plunger. The gage stick is provided with several notch marks showing the useful length of the carbon brush. see figure 1.

  3. The brush is worn down to the permissible limit and must be replaced when the gage stick can be inserted into the bore so that the inner end face of the stick handle beds against the telescopic plunger.

Replacing Carbon Brushes

  1. Replacing carbon brushes is possible during all operating and load conditions of the unit.

  2. To remove a plug-in brush holder, press the handle toward the telescopic plunger the carbon brush is thus locked in its guide through a clamping pin and the brush holder released from its lock in the carrier ring.

  3. The carbon brushes themselves should be replaced a way from the slip rings to avoid any hazard from this work during operation.

  4. After installing the new carbon brush, the first notch mark of the inserted gage stick will coincide with the top of the telescopic plunger.

  5. See figure 2

Removing Worn Carbon Brushes

  1. Remove plug-in brush holder, keeping the handle pressed toward the telescopic plunger.

  2. Maintain brush in its guide by a firm touch of the finger against the force of the compression spring.

  3. Release handle.

  4. Allow brush to slide out (as far as possible).

  5. Loosen and remove telescopic plunger.

  6. Remove brush from its guide.

  7. Disconnect pigtail lug.

  8. See figure 3.

 

Inserting a New Brush

  1. Insert brush into guide.

  2. Reconnect pigtail at cable lug.

  3. Refit telescopic plunger.

  4. Insert brush into guide against the force of the compression spring.

  5. Press handle.

  6. Insert plug-in brush holder into carrier ring.

  7. See figure 4.

Checking Slip Rings

  1. Heavily contaminated slip rings have to be cleaned during unit outages.

  2. Clean brushes and dry linen cloths leaving no fluff should preferably be used for this purpose.

  3. The use of a cleaning liquid (Rivolta or equivalent) may be helpful.

  4. See figure 5.

Shaft Grounding Brushes

  1. The carbon brushes should be checked at regular intervals.

  2. During operation, the useful length of each individual carbon brush can be determined by a visual inspection.

  3. The carbon brushes should be replaced against new ones having contact faces which match the rotor shaft contour.

  4. See figure 6.

 

 

Weekly maintenance Procedures

  1. Check shaft grounding brushes

  2. Check carbon brush

  3. Check exciter air filter is cleaned

Semi Annual maintenance Procedures

  1. Check shaft grounding brushes

  2. Check carbon brush

  3. Check exciter air filter is cleaned

  4. Check slip ring surface is cleaned (at rotor standstill)

Annual maintenance Procedures

  1. Check shaft grounding brushes

  2. Check carbon brush

  3. Check exciter air filter is cleaned

  4. Check slip ring surface is cleaned (at rotor standstill)

  5. Measure rotor insulation resistance
