Introduction

This document outlines the major maintenance procedure P1, which must be performed every ten years as indicated by Y10 in the title block.

Safety Precautions

  • A work order must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear your safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes ... etc.

  • Before starting any work on the machine, check that it is switched off and blocked up for switching on again.

  • Isolation and proper earthing is required

  • The unit should be out of service.

Tools and Equipment

  • Tool set for tightness, cleaning tools as cloth.... etc

  • Lifting device, press-on and detaching device.

  • AVO meter, Isolator test device.

  • Oil for bearing

 

 

 

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Visual examination, measure insulation resistance for stator winding

  2. Clean interior, retighten screws for terminal boxer earthing.

Changing the Bearings (A)

Removal of Bearings Shell (A1)

  1. Remove the split guard (drive end only)

  2. Loosen bearing assembly bolts, upper with lower section and upper section with bearing bracket

  3. Remove cover of bearing housing

  4. Loosen bearing shell bolts

  5. Remove top bearing shell

  6. Open lubricating oil ring joint and pull out ring

  7. Raise shaft using lifting device

  8. Twist bottom bearing shell free and remove

Bearing Shell Installation (A2) 

  1. The installation of bearing shell is done in reverse order to the sequence of removal described in (A1).                                                                                                          

  2. By assembling it, care should be taken not to damage the bearing ring seals (half rings). They should be checked before installation and replaced if necessary.

Disassembly - Assembly (B)

Disassembly of Motor Uncoupled (B1)

  1. Drain bearing oil on both sides

  2. Remove the bearing shell at the drive end as per (A1 without point 1)

  3. Disconnect and remove bearing thermometer on both sides

  4. Loosen the bolts in the lower section of the bearing housing jointed to the bearing bracket and remove the lower section of the bearing housing

  5. Insert a sheet 0.5 mm thick between the bearing bracket ring seal and the shaft and carefully lower the rotor

  6. Remove the bearing shell at the non-drive-end as per (A1)

  7. Proceed as per (B1 point 4)

  8. Unscrew and remove side covers of stator housing and dismantle piping of pressurized sealing chamber

  9. Loosen bearing bracket dowel pins and withdraw them

  10. Loosen bearing bracket bolts and press the bearing bracket off the stator housing using 2 bearing bracket bolts via the two threaded holes

  11. Carefully lower the rotor together with the bearing bracket into the stator bore

  12. Remove bearing bracket

  13. Lift rotor on the drive end side

  14. Proceed as per (B1point 9, 10, 11 and 12)

  15. Dismantle air guides

  16. Lift rotor on both ends and carefully remove it from the stator. Take particular care for the stator winding when rotor is handled

Assembly (B2)

  1. The assembly of motor is done in the reverse order to the sequence of disassembly described as per (B1)
