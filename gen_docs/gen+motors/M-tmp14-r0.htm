Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection I1, which must performed every six months as indicated by M6 in the title block.

This document for the following motors:

Boiler feed water pump motor, Desalination water supply pump motor, Fire fighting motor, closed cooling motor, Service water pump motor, Circulating water pump motor, and Condensate extraction pump motor.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding work area is required.

  • Wear your safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, shoes, breathing mask, safety glass ... etc.

  • Motor is out of service, and switch off the AC power supply for the motor.

  • High temperature may arise under operating conditions on the motor surfaces so that touching should be prevented or avoided.

Warning:

Disconnect all power sources to the unit and discharge. All parts which may retain an electrical before attempting any maintenance or repair. Screen and covers must be maintained in place when unit is in operation failure to observe this warning may result in personal injury.

Motor for use in hazardous locations class I & II in stations: repairs of these motor must be the manufacturer or manufacturers authorized service shop. Those are approved to repair U. L listed motor. To maintain the U. L listing the U. L listing applies to the electric motor only not to the belt or gear transmissions or other devices the devices that connection to the motor.

 

Lubrication

Unit with grease lubricated bearings are pre-lubricated at the factory and normally do not require initial lubrication reduplicating in travel depends upon speed. Type of bearing and service, notes that operating environment and application may dictate more frequent lubrication.

Motor must be at rest and electrical should be locked open to prevent energizing while motor is being serviced. If motor is being taken out of storage. For preparation instruction. To re-lubricate bearing. Remove the drain plug. Inspect grease drain and remove any blockage with a mechanical probe or scavenger. Under no circumstances should a mechanical probe or scavenger be used while the motor is in operation. Add new grease at the grease inlet. New grease must be compatible with grease already in the motor

Work to be Carried Out

Inspect units at regular intervals. Keep unit clean and ventilation opening clear of dust or other debris. Lubricate unit per this operating instruction folder and instruction plate on unit. Excessive lubrication may damage the unit do not over grease.

Motor Winding

  1. Measure the insulation resistance wrt standard to determine quality of the insulation resistance.

  2. Inspection of coil end:
    - Grease and dust accumulated on coils may cause insulation deterioration and poor cooling effect
    - Moisture must not accumulate. Coils must be kept warm when motor is used.
    - Discoloring and this is mainly caused by over heat.

  3. Ensure there is no untoward change of wedges from original position.

  4. Ensure that the binding at the coil end is in its normal position.

Clean the Interior of the Motor

  1. After a motor is in operation for some time, accumulation of dust, carbon powder and grease etc., are unavoidable and may cause damage.

  2. Points to be noted during cleaning:

  3. If using compressed air or a blower:
    Compressed air should be free of moisture
    Maintain air pressure at 4 kg/cm2, since high pressure can cause damage to coils.

  4. If using vacuum:
    Vacuum cleaning can be used, both before and after other methods of cleaning to remove loose dirt and debris. It is a very effective way to remove loose surface contamination from the windings without scattering. Vacuum cleaning tools should be non metallic to avoid any damage to the winding insulation.

 

 

Wiping

Surface contamination on the winding can removed using a soft lint free wiping material. If contamination is oily, the wiping material can be moistened with a safety type petroleum solvent. In hazardous locations, a solvent such as inhibited methyl chloroform may be used but must be used sparingly and immediately removed. While this solvent is non flammable under ordinary conditions, it is toxic and proper health and safety precautions should be followed while using it.

Keep core ducts completely clean. The difference in temperature rise could be around 10 Co before and after cleaning.

Cleaning the Exterior of the Motor

  1. On open ventilated motor, screens and louvers over the inlet air openings should not be allowed to accumulate any build up dirt, lint, etc. that could restrict free air movement.

  2. If the motor is equipped with air filters, they should be replaced or cleaned and reconditioned at a frequency that is dictated by conditions. It is better to replace or recondition filters too often that not often enough.

  3. Totally enclosed air to air cooled totally enclosed fan cooled require special cleaning consideration. The external fan must be cleaned thoroughly since any dirt build up not removed can lead unbalance and vibration. All of the tubes of the air to air heat exchanger should be cleaned using a suitable tube brush having synthetic fiber bristles.

  4. AIR FILTERS MUST BE CLEANED EVERY 7 DAYS
