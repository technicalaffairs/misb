Introduction

This document lists the equipment inspection I1 for hydrogen cooled Generator, which must be performed on an annually bases as indicated by Y1 in the title block.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Maintenance staff should be well trained, qualified or directly supervised by some one who is

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area is required,

  • Personal protective equipment must be used (safety shoes, Helmet, breathing mask, safety glasses).

Tools and Equipment

  • Personal protective equipment as safety shoes, Helmet, breathing mask, and safety glasses

  • Brushes, Blower, Megger.

  • Cleaning fluids such as freon - TF, and chlorothene.

Period

Procedure

Result

Daily

  1. Check sight glasses after level detectors for liquid level.

  2. Check gas consumption.

 

Weekly

  1. Check hydrogen cooler vent.

  2. Check shaft-grounding brushes.

  3. Check carbon brushes.

  4. Check seal oil cooler in operation for proper venting.

  5. Check seal oil supply lines and shut off valves for leakage.

  6. Check degree of contamination of seal oil filter.

  7. Check oil level and contamination of oil in vacuum pump.

  8. Drain liquid from drain traps before and after vacuum pump.

 

Monthly

  1. Change over seal oil pump 1&2.

  2. Perform functional check of stand by seal oil pump.

  3. Change over vapor exhausters 1&2.

  4. Check intake strainer in take port of vacuum pump.

  5. Check exhaust filter at vacuum pump.

  6. Re-grease shaft seal of vapor of exhauster.

 

Every 3 Month

  1. Check air filter at fan motor of gas dryer on dirt.

 
Every 6 Month

  1. Check exciter end generator bearing and shaft seal insulation.

  2. Re-greasing devices of vapor exhauster shaft seals with grease.

  3. Drain liquid from compressed air filter.

  4. Check slip ring surface. (At rotor standstill)

 
Yearly

  1. Measure insulation resistance.

  2. Check shut off valve of gas supply for function and free movement.

  3. Check gas supply line, shut off valves and solenoids on gas tightness.
