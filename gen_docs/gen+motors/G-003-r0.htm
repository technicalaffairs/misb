Introduction

This Document details major maintenance procedure P1 to be performed after 65,000 operations hours as indicated by H65,000 in the title block.

This Document details the programs for removal or insertion of the rotor, and stator visual inspections of the two ring and winding.        

The first draft initiated by eng. Mohmed Aly Aly Hassan, tested by eng. Al Shahat Ahmed Mousa, revised by EDEPC. quality control eng. Tawfik El abasery, and approved by AL Shabab power plant general director eng. Hamdy Melegy and certified by eng. Aly Mostafa AlLathy EDEPC. Quality control and Inspection Head Sector.

Safety Precautions

  • Work order must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area.

  • Wear safety helmet, shoes, gloves ... etc.

  • Gas turbine out of service at cold state.

Tools and Equipment

  • Device for insertion and removal of Rotor

  • Megger, thermometer, and voltmeter

  • Air blower, special tools as mention at List No.1

Works to be Carried Out

Removal of Exciter

  1. Removal the Exciter housing , including the collector brush rigging

  2. Disconnect all oil lines, protecting the openings against entrance of dirt

  3. Disconnect all electrical connections, pushing the cable ends below the floor line if possible.

  4. Remove all bolts and dowels holding the exciter base to the foundation

  5. Check to see that all parts are free and that no Interference excite.

Disassembly of Rotor

  1. Remove brush – holder rigging and complete direct connected exciter

  2. Remove the coward (side the turbine) .

  3. Remove the outside air shield rings and outside shields

  4. Remove the inside air shield rings and inside air shields

  5. Remove the pedestal cop. Oil deflectors  and bearing lining.

  6. Place a jacking device or support under the coupling end of the Rotor.

Program for Removal OR Insertion of the Rotor

Caution:

  • Don't suspend or set down the rotor in the vicinity of the bearing surface (damage to the rotor).

  • Place Protective material (thick cardboard press pan or sealing material).

  • Put the strips of wood between the two wire ropes (no slipping the wire and adjusting the middle when loaded angle between loops equal 45 o C).

  1. Insertion of sliding plate. You see the fig .1- Item 1 , fix the sliding plate on the rotor fan hubs in 12 O'clock position , pull through the  stator bar  the sling ( item 2, fig. 1 ) by using the  copper wire.

  2. Insert the sliding plate on the tensed sling between the supports ( item 4, fig. 1 ) in the upper position when fitted, remove the sling  ( item 2, fig 1) and the adjusting screws (item 3 , fig. 1) press at both ends the sliding plate with the supports ( item 4 , fig .1 ) to the erection device, turn the rotor until the sliding plate is reaching the 6 O clock position loosen, the screws ( item 5, fig . 1 ) until the sliding plate lays in the stator bar. Remove supports (item 4, fig.1) at driving side.

  3. Fix the inter- mediate piece ( item 7, fig. 2 ) for the sliding pad on the fan hubs at driving  end in the 12 O'clock position .

  4. Turn the rotor until the inter- mediate piece is reaching the position of the sliding plate : by the same, the sliding plate erection device has turned again in the 12 O clock position and can now be removed

  5. Dismantle bearing brackets and upper bearing parts.

  6. Mount the lifting jack (item 8, fig 2) at the B end.

  7. Lift the rotor as high as possible using the crane at A end and the lifting jack at B end, make sure that the rotor does not touch the stator windings and iron core.

  8. Pull tow lengths of string through the stator bar and use them to pull the sliding pad (item 9, fig. 2) from the  B end to the A end (  to keep connect the air pipe line to the B end ), the distance between the pad and the end of the stator iron core should  be at  150  mm.

  9. Put  the second sliding pad (item 10, fig –2) unclear the fan hub with the inter-  mediate piece ( to keep connect the air pipe line to the A end )

  10. Lower the rotor at both ends until the A end lies on the pad ( item 9, fig – 2) (in this position the rotor is 1.5 mm above the centre line)

  11. With the aid of the crane, dismantle the lower bearing shell and the bearing rings at both ends.

  12. Assemble bearing  pedestal, bracket and two – part ring ( item 11) with threaded bolts ( item 12 ) and nuts ( item 13) to the rotor shaft at the B end { all items Nos. refer to ( fig.2 section c-c ) }

  13. Change on B end of the rotor from the lifting ,jack to the crane sling . then check with a spirit level that the rotor remains at  horizontal position

  14.  Open the compressed air valve and take out the  rotor with slight lateral movements, advacing the crane accordingly

Attention 

  • Watch the second sliding pad ( item 10, fig – 2): as soon as this pad is full contact with the stator core , the connection to the air supply must be changed over .

  • Continue the withdraw of the rotor until its center of gravity has approx. reached the midpoint of the bearing bedestal support the gravity center line is approx 250 mm besides of the rotor midpoint, i.e. the slot wedges centering screws .

  • Lower the rotor on the wooden support ( item 14, fig 3 ) and set down the sling on the bearing pedestal support.

  • Dismantle lifting jack at the B end with the crane.

  • Lift the rotor with the sling and carefully pull it right out of the stator bore.

Note:     For insertion of the rotor. Proceed in the reversed sequence.
