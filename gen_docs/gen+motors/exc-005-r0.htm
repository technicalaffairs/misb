Introduction

This document outlines the major maintenance procedure P2, which must performed  after eighty thousands operating hours as indicated by H 80000 in the title block.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff must carry the suitable ID safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear the appropriate safety and healthy equipment as safety shoes, helmet ... etc

  • Unit must be out of service and generator is grounded.

Tools and Equipment

  • Tool set kit

  • Safety and healthy equipment

  • Dry linen cloth

  • High voltage-proof gloves

  • Telescopic plunger (gage stick)

  • Spanners, screw drivers …etc

  • Dry air source.

Work to be Carried Out 

The field current transmission system for the generator type consists of:

  • Slip ring of the generator

  • Brush holder.

  • Carbon brushes. (See Fig.1)

Required Maintenance

  1. Check the carbon brushes.

  2. The carbon brushes length should be checked during operation without removing the brush holder by inserting a gage stick of insulating materials into the bore of the telescopic plunger.

  3. The gage stick is provided with several notch marks showing the useful length of the carbon brush. (See Fig.2)

  4. By visual inspection, make sure that no spark (brushes are matched with slip rings).

Note: for unusual condition check the carbon brushes length should be checked during operation daily.

Precaution

  • It will be necessary to wear high-voltage proof gloves for brush holder removal from or insertion into the carrier ring while the unit is in excited condition.

  • To insure proper field current transfer during checks and maintenance activities while the unit is excited, remove only one plug in brush holder at a time for each polarity.

Removing Worn Carbon Brushes

  1. Remove plug in brush holder, keeping the handle pressed toward the telescopic plunger.

  2. Maintain brush in its guide by a film touch of the finger against the force of the compressing spring.

  3. Release handle.

  4. Allow brush to slide out (as far as possible).

  5. Loosen and remove telescopic plunger.

  6. Remove brush from its guide.

  7. Disconnect pigtail lug. 

Inserting a New Brush

  1. Insert brush into guide.

  2. Reconnect pigtail at cable lug.

  3. Refit telescopic plunger.

  4. Insert brush into guide against the force of the compression spring.

  5. Press handle.

  6. Insert plug-in brush holder into carrier ring.

Return the equipment into Service

  1. Disconnect the ground from the generator winding.

  2. Lift up the tools, materials used, and the safety fence with caution mark surrounding the work area.

  3. Finish the work permit.
