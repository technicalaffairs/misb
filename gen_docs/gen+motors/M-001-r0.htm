Introduction

This document lists the equipment inspection I1, which must be performed every six monthly bases as indicated in the title block for motors

Safety Precautions 

  • A work permit must be issued and the maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Maintenance staff should be well trained, qualified or directly supervised by some one who is.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear the personal protective equipment must used as safety shoes, safety helmet, earplug, and gloves pairs...etc

  • The motor must be out of service.

  • Circuit breaker of the motor in test position .

Tools and Equipment

  • Suitable tool set, Air Blower, Megger device

  • Greasing machine, Multimeter.

  • Fine cloth for cleaning purposes

  • Brushes, (soft brushes)

Work to be Carried out

  1. Measure and record the insulation of motor winding, repair if required

  2. Clean the body of the motor and ventilation holes using air blower and brushes

  3. Remove the motor bearing bracket.

  4. Remove the rotor, avoid touch the stator end winding by the rotor.

  5. Check the ball bearings, grease / lubricate or replace if needed. (bearings and grease must be kept free of dirt)

  6. Clean motor winding using a soft brushes

  7. Reassemble the motor.

  8. Check all the tightens of the power and control circuits connections .

  9. Measure and record the dc resistance of motor winding.

  10. Connect the power source to the motor

  11. Run the motor and check the ball bearing or roller bearing housing temperature not more than 80oC

  12. Check lubrication and alignment if temperature exceed this limit

  13. Check vibration or noises, tighten the bolts if required

  14. Check operation of the motor from local and remote operation on  test  position

  15. Check all protection of the motor by protection group ( O.C. , S.C., U.V. )
