Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding work area is required.

  • Wear your safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, shoes, breathing mask, safety glass ... etc.

  • Motor is out of service, and switch off the AC power supply for the motor.

  • High temperature may arise under operating conditions on the motor surfaces so that touching should be prevented or avoided.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete tool set

  • Cleaning materials (cloth - benzene), grease for bearing

  • Recommended grease quality according to NLGI N3 specification:

  • SHELL ALVANIA G3 or equivalent.

  • Oil bath

Work to be Carried Out

Maintenance Interval

Deadline

Maintenance

Every 100-1000 hours

Exterior cleaning of the bearing

Bearing in reversing operation every 5000 operating hours.

Bearing in continuous every 8000 operating hours.

Oil change
During prevention maintenance work for the installation immediately if:
The bearing temperature exceeds 15K over the indicated value.
Unusual changes of the lubrication oil become visible.
Increased oil level in the case of bearing type EFW.

Bearing inspection

Permanent Checks

  1. Check that there is no abnormal operation (heating, abnormal noise and abnormal current).

  2. The cleaning of the inside of the machine of the rotor and stator ventilation ducts and, of the winding must be carried out with dry, oil free compressed air with maximum pressure 3 bars.

  3. If the machine is too dirty, it can be cleaned with a cloth impregnated with turpentine and check the insulation after cleaning.

Stator Insulation Check

  1. Before the first stator-up or after along stop or a dismantling, check the insulation of the stator winding to earth.

  2. To carry out the measurements with a minimum margin of error, all the pipes, tubes, cables must be disconnected and the out side surfaces of the machine should be carefully cleaned.

  3. To measure the insulation resistances of the stator winding, use a megohmmeter with a 2500 V magneto.

Variation in the Insulation Resistance

  1. Measure the insulation resistance every 3 : 4 hours during drying of the motor,

  2. Be careful and notes that at the beginning of the operating the insulation resistance drops and then slowly increases and the drying can be stopped 2-3 hours after the insulation resistance has reached the required value. Also remember that the drying can take quite along time up to 48 hours.

  3. When the drying is finishing, stop the current supply and leave the machine to cool.

  4. When room temperature is reached, recheck the insulation resistance and if the measured value is correct, remake the normal connections and restart.

  5. Measure the insulation resistance after cooling and notice the value will be higher than the value measured when the machine is hot at the moment it has been stopped.
