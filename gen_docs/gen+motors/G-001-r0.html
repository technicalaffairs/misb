Work to be Carried Out

Removal of Exciter

  1. Remove the exciter housing , including the collector brush rigging

  2. Break the flexible coupling as described the instructions for Flexible Couplings in the exciter section

  3. Disconnect all oil lines , protecting the openings against entrance of dirt

  4. Disconnect all electrical connections, pushing the cable ends below the floor line if possible

  5. Remove all bolts and dowels holding the exciter base to the foundation

  6. Check to see that that all  parts are free and that no interference exist

  7. Slide the exciter back clear of the generator shaft . Refer to the section on handling in Direct - Connected Exciter instructions for precautions to be observed in handling the exciter

Disassembly of Rotor

  1. Remove brush - holder rigging and complete direct - connected exciter (see instructions on Direct-Connected Exciter)

  2. Remove the coupling guard

  3. Remove the outside air shield rings and outside air shields

  4. Remove the inside air shield rings and inside air shields

  5. Remove the pedestal cap, oil deflectors, and bearing lining

  6. Place a jacking device or support under the coupling end of the rotor

  7. Remove the generator and turbine coupling bolts

  8. Take a hitch at ( X ) as shown in position "B" of Fig 1; protect the shaft with a fiber or copper shim

  9. Disengage the rabbet on the coupling

  10. Raise the collector end of rotor , with the sling at ( X ) as far as possible without touching or damaging the stator end windings, fingers , or stator core

  11. Insert protective shim ( 5 ) in the air gap. Coat the top of the steel air gap shim ( 4 ) with paraffin or hard wax and assemble in the air gap from the collector end. The steel air gap shim should be securely wired to the turbine end to prevent its moving

  12. Attach cords or wires to the rotor shoe ( 3 ) and place the shoe in the bore at the collector end. Pull it through to the coupling and as shown in position " B "

  13. Lower the coupling end of the rotor until it rests on the rotor shoe

  14. Lower the collector end of the rotor until the rotor is centered in the stator bore as shown in position " B " slide the rotor through the stator bore until the rotor shoe is near the collector end of the stator bore as shown in position " A"

  15. Place a support at the collector end of the rotor at ( Z ) Remove the sling at ( X ) .

  16. Place sling around the middle of the rotor as shown in position "A" and raise the rotor so that it does not rest on the rotor shoe or the support (Z) protect the finished surfaces the frame with a block of wood at (Y) use orcopper shims around the rotor to prevent damage when using the sling

  17. Remove the rotor shoe at the collector end . Do not allow the retaining ring to touch the air gap shim or punching .

  18. Remove the rotor taking care not to allow any part of the rotor to damage the stator end -winding insulation

  19. Remove the air gap shims

Armature Core

  1. The armature core should be inspected , particularly for evidences of hot spots or damaged punching. if such damage is observed , this requires very careful and expert repair.

  2. If the core is in need of painting , all surfaces should be cleaned and punching sprayed with general electric epoxy paint the inside of the generator should be checked for evidences of oil which would indicate oil leakage past the oil deflectors

Caution

No attempt to grind or repair core damage should be under taken except by a man who has had previous training . an index perienced man can easily cause additional damage . and result in a core burnout.

Armature Windings

  1. Inspect the ends of the armature winding for deposits of oil and evidences of corona discharge , or mechanical damage such as vibration , chafing , loose block and ties etc.

  2. If cleaning is necessary , inspect the winding and insulation care fully after ward. under ordinary conditions. it should not be necessary to repaint the end winding more frequently than once in fire years. to repaint the end winding, it is recommended that general electric epoxy paint be sprayed on after the winding have been thoroughly cleaned and dried

Cleaning the Armature Core

  1. In many cases the cleaning fluids may be applied to the winding by means of a spray as an alternative to the use of brushes or clothes .

  2. The spray nozzle should be held close to the work so as to obtain the greatest benefit from the cleaning fluid .

  3. Two or three application of the cleaning fluid may be necessary depending upon the amount of oil and dust and after each application , the parts should be wiped dry .

  4. In case hand scraping of parts is necessary , wooden sticks should be used instead of metal scrapers to avoid damaging the insulation .

  5. After the winding have been thoroughly cleaned , a finish coat of varnish may be applied , if necessary . the varnish may be sprayed while observing every precaution tow in imize fire hazard . spray a small test area first to be sure that the cleaned area provides a good bonding surface for the varnish . all insulating varnishes have a low flash point and ighition may be caused by a spark .

Cleaning

  1. The out surface of all component of the exiter should be cleaned by using clothes and cleaning fluid as required to remove any remaining dust, dirt or oil film

  2. Clean all parts of exciter, using compressor air.

Inspection

  1. If vibration is present, all SCREW - tybe connections should be checked regularly . Normally, the static components should require no further attention .

  2. All contactors and relays should be regularly inspected .

  3. The automatic and manual regulator voltage adjuster contact brushes should be inspected and appropriate repair or replacement performed as required .

  4. Silicon diodes are used in the power circuit of the static exciter - these diodes are not at this time known to age ; There fore, they are either good or should be replaced .

  5. Individual diodes can be checked with an ohmmeter and if good, should have a low resistance in the forward direction and high resistance in either .

  8. If it becomes necessary to replace a faulty diode use the following procedure:

  1. Shut down the equipment .

  2. Discharge all capacitors in the AC Machine field circuit.

  3. Disconnect generator field ground detecting

  4. Remove diodes.

  5. Screw in new diode after first applying a small amount dow corning Molykote M77 paste or similar silicon grease with Mos2 to approximately the first threeds.

Shaft - Grounding brushes

Introduction

  1. Shaft - grounding brushes are supplied on turbine generator units to prevent damage to bearings by electrical discharge through the oil films.

  2. Shaft - to - ground voltage occur and turbine - generator or shaft in two ways :

  • Charge separation in wet - steam stage of condensing turbines can cause a build up of electrostatic voltage ( DC ) between shaft and stationary parts .

  • On units with static excitation systems the out put of the three - phase full wave bridge rectifiers is not pure DC but contains a 360 hertz ripple of appreciable magnitude .

  1. Shaft - grounding brushes provide a low - impedance path to ground so that : 

  • Build up of electrostatic voltage is prevented

  • Current flow due to static excitation voltage ripple flow harmlessly to ground by passing the bearings .

Procedure for Measuring Shaft to Ground Voltage

  1. The voltmeter or oscilloscope should be connected solidly to ground on one terminal ; the other terminal to an insulation probe containing a metal - graphite or bronze brush. To read shaft voltage , press the probe firmly against the shaft at a point adjacent to the grounding brush assembly, but preferably not in the track of the grounding brush

  2. The DC component of shaft -to-ground voltage is usually below 10 volts, and this DC component is not greatly affected by the type of meter which is used. Be sure to connect the meter to get an up- scale reading.

  3. The AC component ( or peak-to-peak voltage ) is of greatest interest . if the true peak -to -peak voltage is 10 volts or less, the indication is that the grounding brush assembly is functioning satisfactorily . A peak-to-peak voltage above 10 volts indicates that maintenance is required.

Maintenance

  1. Remove the brushes from their holders , one at a time . clean brush and holder so brush may move freely .

  2.  Scrape brush face with a knife to remove glaze .

  3. Use a soft brush seating stone or a canvas- padded bort to remove excess film from shaft .

  4. Clean brush clip contacts .

  5. Check spring force. It should be at least 33/4 pounds , and even higher force may be advisable in some instances

  6. Replace brushes before the pigtail is in contact with top of brush box. Fig 5, Fig 6.

Far - Air Filters

Cleaning

     When to Clean

In general, filters should be cleaned when the pressure drop across the filter bank reaches 0.25 inches W.G. However, this figure may vary from installation to installation, depending upon the velocities encountered and upon the type of dust and dirt being removed from the air stream, and the original design conditions.

     How to Clean

Under ordinary conditions, far - air filters may be readily cleaned with water . A garden hose with pressure nozzel and water pressure of 30 to 40 psig is satisfactory. Higher pressures are desirable although pressures as low as 15 to 20 psig may be used but with less satisfacory results. Hot water will speed up the cleaning operation . A nozzle with hose adaptor is preferable to an ordinary pressure nozzle .

The stream of water should be directed to the dirty side of the filter so that the full energy of the water jet will break the dirt mass loose from the media and surplus water will flush the loosened dirt out through the herringbone - crimped channels .

     Alkaline Cleaning Materials

When dirt loads are heavy or tenacious, or where a considerable number of filters are involved and economy demands a faster cleaning operation, the filters should be presoaked in an alkaline cleaning solution. Recommendation of the exact material to use should be obtained from the manufacturers of cleaning products. Typical alkaline cleaners are

      Dry Compounds

Three generally satisfactory, inexpensive dry alkaline materials are turco no. 16, oakite penetrant and kelite super S. From 2 to 4 oz. Of this material should be used per gallon of water

      SOLVENTS:

Prepared alkaline solvents are more expensive than dry compounds, and usually cannot be justified except for removing contaminants having a large percentage of tenacious, greasy substances .

Caution : All of the alkaline solution should be thoroughly washed out of the filters with fresh water

 

Maintenance Notes

  1. It is often economical to purchase spare filters for installation when the dirty filters are removed for servicing.

  2. It is not generally necessary to clean a Far-Air filter so thoroughly that the bright electroplated surface of the screen media is exposed. Alight residual film of discolored oil in no way changes the filter's performance after it has been reoiled

  3. It may be necessary to front-wash and back - wash filters that have heavy dirt and lint loads . It is not necessary to wash out the last traces of lint which seem to float within the media . However , if a large quantity of dirty lint is permitted to accumulate the filters may have to be oiled periodically in a highly alkaline solution to remove it .

  4. The condition of the felt on the filter holding frames should be checked whenever a filter panel is removed, and repairs made when necessary .

  5. A draft gauge installed to measure the resistance across an air filter bank best indicates when the filters need cleaning .

  6. Air filter maintenance organization using far - air automatic or manual air filter cleaning and oiling equipment often can do better job more economically than the user. Check with your far - air representative for the name of a reputable service company in your area .

Resistance Temperature Detectors ( R.T.D )

Description

A resistance temperature detector (RTD) is a resistance element , usually made of copper, and adjusted to 10 ohms at 25 C. Operation of the RTD is based on the principle that the electrical resistance of a metallic conductor varies linearly with its

temperature ( See Fig. 7 )

 

Fig. 7 Resistance temperature curve of copper RTD (10 ohms at 25 C )

 

                      

Application

The RTD and its associated equipment is designed for use with generators, transformers, and other apparatus to determine winding, gas, and liquid temperatures. The equipment consist of two parts : the switchboard equipment which usually includes a temperature meter, test resistor, transformer switch, and leads: and the machine equipment which usually includes the resistance temperature detectors, leads, and terminal block with grounding connections.

Tests

A test resistance is usually provided for periodically checking the calibration of the temperature meter. Test resistance has value of resistance of 11.73 ohms, which corresponds to the resistance of a detector at temperature of 70 C. A transfer switch is used to connect the temperature meter to any one of several detectors in the machince. The design is such that the contacts are opened and closed in proper sequence when transferring from one detector to another. Another position connects to the calibrated test resistor to check the temperature meter at the 70-degree point. The construction of RTD'S is shown in Fig. 6. Each detector has three leads designated as A,B and C the resistance of the element itself is the total resistance from A to B or A to C minus the lead resistance B to C.

Shaft bearing insulation

Introduction

The magnetic flux path of an ac generator may be sufficiently non-uniform to cause a small ac voltage to be induced in the circuit ( one turn 1oop ) made up of the generator shaft, bearings and the generator casing. Even though this induced voltage is small, it could cause a large ac current to flow if some insulation were not inserted in the circuit if the current were allowed to flow, it would cause pitting of the bearings the circuit is opened by insulating the collector end bearing .

Test

The insulation may be checked by the following method use a 0-200 amps-low impedance ac ammeter with a non-metallic contact, such as a carbon brush to connect one side of the ammeter to the shaft. Connect the other side the ammeter to the casing to obtain a measure of the current that might flow with on bearing insulation present . Disconnect the ammeter from the casing and connect it to the test terminal of the end shield. if, in this case, the ammeter indicates even a small current flow, the insulation is not satisfactory and must be replaced or repaired .

Other units with end shield bearing or pedestal type bearing do not have means of making electrical contact with the bearing inner liner . on these units the insulation between the bearing inner and outer ring should be check at installation and when the unit is shutdown for major inspection . the procedure for checking bearing insulation is as follows:

  1. Remove bearing cap at collector end .

  2. Lift the rotor slightly at the collector end , to clear the bearing using a shaft jacking device as shown in instructions "Assembly and disassembly of rotor" found elsewhere in the manual . take care not to damage the oil deflectors .

  3. Using a high resistance ( at least 5000 ohms per volt ) volt -ohm meter or vacuum tube volt-ohm meter , measure the resistance across the insulation between the bearing inner and outer rings.

  4. The insulation resistance should be 100,000 ohms or more ; however , a minimum of 10,000 ohms may be considered satisfactory.

  5. If the resistance across the bearing insulation measures less than 10,000 ohms, remove the upper and lower bearing halves . as before , measure the resistance across the insulation between the inner and outer rings of the bearings halves .

  6. The bearing should be replaced or repaired when the resistance across the bearing insulation measures less than 10,000 ohms.
