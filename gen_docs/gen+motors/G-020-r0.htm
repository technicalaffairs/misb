Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection I1, which must be performed every six months as indicated by M6 in the title block.

The first draft initiated by eng.Haytham Hemdan, tested by eng. Mohamed Abbass & eng. Tamer Awad Abdul Rahman, revised by EDEPC quality control eng. Adel Abdul Kader El Naggar, approved by eng. Tarek Youssef Ali, Head Sector of Ayoun Moussa Power Station.

This document accepted by eng. Samir Abd El Gilil EDEPC inspection & quality control general director, MPIS coordinator, and certified by accountant Mahmoud Soliman EDEPC admin & financial inspection & quality control's general director and supervisor of inspection & quality control head sector.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, qualified maintenance staff carry the suitable safety ID category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required,

  • Wear standard personal safety and healthy equipment as helmet, safety shoes, rubber gloves, ... etc.

  • Ensure engine starting circuits are disabled before commencing service or maintenance procedure.

  • Isolate any anti-condensation heater supply.

Tools and Equipment

  • Safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes ...etc

  • Set of wrenches, cleaning material, and air blower.

  • Grease Mobilux no. EP2 or Shell Alavania R3.

Work to be Carried Out

 

Windings Condition

  1. Measure the winding insulation resistance to earth, ensure that the AVR was disconnected during this test and any resistance temperature detector (RTD) leads grounded. A 500V megger or similar instrument should be used.

  2. Notice that the insulation resistance to earth of all windings should be in excess of 1.0 Mega ohm and should the insulation resistances be less than these values, drying out of generator windings is essential.

  3. Carry out Drying out drying by directing warm air from a fan heater into the generator air inlets and / or outlets.

 

Bearings

  1. Check for overheating, noise, vibration

  2. If excessive vibration develops after a period of time this may be due to bearing wear. The bearing should then be examined for damage or loss of grease and replaced if necessary.

  3. Replace the bearing after 40 thousands hours in service.

Fault Finding

No voltage build up when starting set

Check link k1-k2 on auxiliary terminals. Follow separate excitation test procedure to check machine and AVR

Loss of voltage when running

First stop and re-start set. If no voltage or voltage collapses after short time, follow separate excitation test procedure

Generator voltage high followed by collapse

Check sensing leads to AVR.

Refer to separate excitation test procedure

Voltage unstable, either no load or with load

Check speed stability

Low voltage on load

Check speed if correct check ‘UFRO’ setting

Excessive voltage/speed dip on load switching

Check governor response

Sluggish recovery on load switching

Check governor response

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

  Before commencing any fault finding procedure examine all wiring for loose connections.

 

Separate Excitation Test Procedures

  1. Check the generator windings, diode assembly and AVR using the appropriate following section:

  • Generator windings, rotating diodes and permanent magnet generator (PMG).

  • Excitation control test

NOTE         Incorrect speed setting will give proportional error in voltage output.

Checking PMG

  1. Start the set and run at rated speed

  2. Measure the voltage at AVR terminals P2, P3 and P4. These should be balanced and within the following ranges:
    50HZ generators- 170-180 volts.              60HZ generators- 200-216 volts.

  3. Should the voltage be unbalanced stop the set, remove the MPG sheet metal cover from the non drive end bracket and disconnect the multipin plug in the PMG output leads.

  4. Check leads P2, P3, and P4 for continuity.

  5. Check the PMG stator resistances between output leads. These should be balanced and within +/-10% of 2.3 ohm. If resistances are unbalanced and /or incorrect the PMG stator must be replaced.

  6. If the voltages are balanced but low and PMG stator winding resistances are correct the PMG rotor must be replaced.

 

 

 

Checking Generator Windings and Rotating Diodes

  1. This procedure is carried out with leads X and XX disconnected at AVR or transformer control Rectifier Bridge and using 12 volt d.c supply to leads X and XX.

  2. Start the Set and Run at Rated Speed.

  3. Measure the voltage at the main output terminals U, V and W.

  4. If voltages are balanced and within +/-10 % of the generator nominal voltage, check voltage at AVR terminals 6, 7 and 8 these should be balanced and between 170-250 volts.

  5. If voltages at main terminals are balanced but voltages at 6, 7 and 8 are unbalanced, check continuity of leads 6, 7 and 8. Where an isolating transformer is fitted (MX321 AVR) Check transformer winding.

  6. If faulty the transformer unit must be replaced.

Balanced Main Terminals Voltages

  1. If all voltages are balanced within 1 % at main terminals, it can be assumed that all exciter windings, main windings and main rotating diodes are in good order, and the fault is in the AVR or transformer control.

  2. If voltages are balanced but low, there is a fault in the main excitation windings or rotating diodes assembly, proceed as follows to identify.

Rectifier Diodes

  1.  Check the diodes in rectifier assembly with a multimeter, disconnect the flexible leads at the terminal end for each diode

  2. Check the forward and reverse resistance, a healthy diode will indicate a very high resistance (infinity) in the reverse direction, and low resistance in the forward direction, a healthy diode will give a full deflection reading in both directions with the test meter on the 10.000 ohms scale, or an infinity readings in both directions.

  3. On an electronic digital meter a healthy diode will give a low reading in one direction, and high reading in the other.

Replacement of Faulty Diodes

Note: The rectifier assembly is split into two plates, the positive and negative, and the main rotor is connected across this plates.
  Each plates carries three diodes, the negative plate carrying negative biased diodes and the positive plates carrying positive biased diodes.

  1. Ensure that the correct polarity diodes are fitted to each respective plate,

  2. When fitting the diodes to the plates they must be tight enough to ensure a good mechanical and electrical contact, but should be over tightened, the recommended torque tightening is 4.06 - 4.74 NM (36 -42Ib in).

Surge Suppressor

  1. The surge suppressor is a metal-oxide varistor connected across the two rectifier plates to prevent high transient reverse voltages in the field windings from damaging the diodes

  2. This device is not polarized and will show a virtually infinite reading in both directions with an ordinary resistance meter. If defective this will be visible by inspection, since it will normally fail to short circuit and show signs of disintegration, Replace it fully

Unbalanced Main Terminal Voltages

If voltages are unbalanced, indicates a fault in the main stator windings or main cables to the circuit breaker.

Notes: 

  • Faults on the stator windings or cables may also cause noticeable load increase on the engine when excitation is applied. Disconnect the main cables and separate the winding leads U1 – U2, (U5 – U6), V1 –V2, (V5 –V6), W1 –W2, (W5 –W6) to isolate each winding section.

  • Leads suffixed 5 and 6 apply to 12 wire winding only

Excitation Control Test

AVR Function Test

All types of AVR’s can be tested within this procedure:

  1. Remove exciter field leads X and XX (F1 & F2) from the AVR terminals X & XX (F1 & F2)

  2. Connect a 60w 24v household lamp to AVR terminals X& XX (F1 & F2)

  3. Set the AVR volts control potentiometer fully clockwise

  4. Connect a 12 volts, 1.0A DC supply to the exciter field leads X & XX (F1& F2) with X (F1) to the positive

  5. Start the generating set and run at rated speed

  6. Check that the generator output voltage is within +/- 10% of rated voltage

Notes:

  1. If the generator output voltage is correct but the voltage on 7-8 (or P2 – P3) is low, check auxiliary leads and connection to main terminals

  2.  The lamp connected across X-XX should glow. In the case of MX321 AVR’s the lamp should glow for approximately 8 sec. and then turn off. Failure to turn off indicates faulty protection circuit and the AVR should be replaced

  3. Turning the “volts” control potentiometer fully anti-clockwise should turn off the lamp with all AVR types

  4. Should the lamp fail to light the AVR is faulty and should be replaced

  Important!    After this test turn volts control potentiometer fully anti-clockwise.
