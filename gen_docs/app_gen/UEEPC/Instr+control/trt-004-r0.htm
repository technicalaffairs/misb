Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection I1, which must be performed on an annually bases or at unit shutdown as indicated by Y1 in the title block.

The equipment function is to Feed water flow transmitters

The first draft initiated by eng. Mohsen Kamel, revised by eng. Hassan Shimi, approved by eng. Ibrahim Al_Shahat Ibrahim; Head sector of Power Station and certified by eng. Gamal Abd_Elwahab Inspection and quality Control Head Sector.           

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued.

  • The qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable ID safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, shoes, breathing-mask, safety glasses ... etc.

  • The unit is out of service and shutdowned.

Tools and Equipment

  • Safety helmet, shoes ... etc

  • Toolbox

  • Piece of cloth

  • Multimeter

  • Calibration Pump (rang:0-2 bar)

Work to be Carried Out

  1.  Open the work permit to start the maintenance procedure and receipt the equipment place from the operation after work permit finished.

  2. Check the equipment after that close isolation valves of both sides (HI&LO) for transmitter. figure no. 1

  3.  Open vents of both sides (HI&LO) for transmitter.

  4. Connect the calibration pump to HI side and compress air to clean the transmitter from any water

  5. Close balance valve

  6.  Install multimeter to take mA reading in the test point or by connect it series in the mA circuit. figure no. 2

  7. Check ZERO of the transmitter with atmospheric pressure at the two sides (HI&LO).

  8. Adjustment ZERO if the reading of multimeter is less or larger than 4 mA. Turn the ZERO screw of transmitter with a minus screwdriver clockwise or counterclockwise consequently (refer to figure no. 9).

  9. Apply input press of 100 % (300 mmH2O) by the calibration pump to HI side.

  10.  Adjust the SPAN - if the reading of multimeter is less or larger than 20 mA. Turn the SPAN screw of transmitter with a minus screwdriver clockwise or counterclockwise consequently (refer to figure no. 9).

  11. Check ZERO and SPAN more than one reading to adjusting at 4 & 20 mA exactly.

  12. Take reading at (0 – 25 – 50 – 75 – 100) % and make sure it at (4 – 8 – 12 – 16 – 20) mA consequently.

  13. Then if the relation between input and output is within specified limit the calibration is finished.

  14. Remove multimeter from transmitter and connect the terminals.

  15. Remove the calibration pump from HI side of the transmitter.

  16. Open the isolation valves for transmitterand make a good vent from two sides HI & LO to purge any air inside the transmitter

  17. Close the two vents, now the transmitter in service.

  18. Return the equipment place to the operators after maintenance schedule completed.

  19. Close the work permit and return the safety working at the normal position.
