Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection I1, which must be performed on an annually bases as indicated by Y1 in the above title block.

The first draft initiated by eng. Safwat Mahrous Masoud, tested by eng. Kamel Tawfeq Gad, accepted by general director eng. Abed El Azem Gomaa Hagras, and approved by eng. Hussein Ahmed El-Mahdy Head Sector of Assuit Power Station. and certified by eng. Gamal Abdel Wahab: UEEPC inspection & quality control Head Sector and MPIS coordinator

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued

  • The qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable ID safety category, 

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear your safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes .... etc

  • Personnel carrying out the maintenance should be trained and qualified.

    •

  • Switch off the circuit breaker and be sure that the pressure gauge pointer reads 15 atm.

  • CB must be isolated and proper earthing at the two sides of the CB is required.

  • Isolate control circuit (d.c voltage).

  • Use only fiberglass ladders.

  • Close the air stopcock in the distributing cabinet.

  • Open the drainage valve to drain the air out of the tank and be sure that the CB is free of pressure

Tools and Equipment

  • Maintenance tool kit, screw driver

  • Safety helmet, and safety shoes

  • One set of metric open-end box wrenches, one set of socket wrenches, one set of screwdrivers and torque wrench.

  • Tin cans to hold bolts, washers ..... etc.

  • Pure alcohol - wipers - fine file and fine emery paper.

  • Previous periodical maintenance report including ale previous tests and manufacture test certificates.

  • Complete set of gaskets.

 

 

 

Work to be Carried Out

Distributing Cabinet

  1. Dismantle the reducing valve

  2. Dismantle push - button device

  3. Clean all parts with pure alcohol and reassemble all parts once again

Control Cabinets for the 3 Poles

  1. Dismantle the opening valve and closing valve.

  2. Dismantle tripping and closing electromagnet to clean and maintain them.

  3. Clean all parts with pure alcohol and reassemble all parts once again.

  4. Clean the electrical terminals with flow of air.

  5. Check the safety valve.

Isolator Blast Valves 

  1. Dismantle the cover.

  2. Clean and maintain piston, stem, exhaust valve and non - return valve.

  3. Change the 3 gaskets.

Complete the following Steps:

  1. Tighten all nuts of supporting isolator chambers, O-ring chambers by torque wrench with a torque of 1.8 kg m.

  2. Check the accuracy of the pressure gauges.

  3. Close the drainage valve and recharge the CB with air.

  4. Check for any air leakage.

  5. Close and trip the CB two times and be sure that the pressure gauge reads 15 atm. when the CB is open and zero atm.  when the CB is closed.

  6. Perform micro ohm and timing tests, record the results, compare it with previous test results and report investigate significant changes.

  7. Record all parts that have been changed and test results on maintenance card.

  8. Disconnect the joints between the shunting resistance and arcing chamber to measure the resistance of shunting resistance for the 3 poles.

  9. Disconnect the joints between isolator and capacitors to measure by megger.

  10. Before putting the CB in service clean the CB control cabinet with flow of air then check and tighten all terminals of the terminal block (10 fig 12)

  11. Clean and check all auxiliary contacts.

  12. Check closing and trip coil and their contacts.

  13. Clean and check the air pressure manometer contacts and adjust the air pressure settings as fallowing:
                                        11 atm       for     closing
                                        12 atm       for     auto reclosing

  14. Clean and check the auxiliary relays contacts in the CB cabinets.

  15. Check the position indication of the CB.

  16. Before putting the CB in service clean all its metal parts, clean working area and don’t forget any wipers on the CB

  17. Record the maintenance that has been done and all parts that have been changed in maintenance card.

Resistance Test

  1. Disconnect the parallel joints between the shunting resistance and arc chamber

  2. Measure each resistance.

  3. Reconnect the parallel joints.

Condensers Test

  1. Disconnect the parallel joints with isolator chamber

  2. Measure the condenser insulation using megger 2.5kv

  3. Measure the tan delta for condenser (0.5 % at 20: 30 ºC) w.r.t. temperature factor

  4. Reconnect the parallel joints.

Contact Resistance Test

  1. Measure the contact resistance for each phase alone if the value increased, you should measure each insulator or arc chamber alone to determine which chamber has a great value and change the contacts.

  2. The value of each contact for each chamber alone

  3. The value of contact resistance for connectors between isolator and arc.
