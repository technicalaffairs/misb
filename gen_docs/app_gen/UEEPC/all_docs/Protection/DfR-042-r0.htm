Introduction

The relay characteristics allow a great accuracy to recognize internal and external faults in generator / step up transformer / unit auxiliary transformer. It is manufactured by GE

This procedure details the steps required to completely check the proper operation and calibration of the TTS100B121G00 relay. It is recommended this procedure be carried out on an annually bases

The first preliminary steps to prepare for relay testing describes the activities required prior to testing. Some of these steps could be performed days before the actual test. The second section preparation for testing describes the steps to set up the equipment for performing the actual tests. The third section is the actual test

Safety Precautions

  • A work order must be issued.

  • The maintenance staff should be qualified and carry the suitable safety ID category.

  • Wear safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, anti static hand probe to avoid electrical shock …etc

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required       

  • The generator must be isolated and work permit required.

  • Make sure that CT secondary circuits are shorted before isolating the current circuit for test current injection

  • Switch off DC power supply before inserting or withdrawing any card from the relay module.

Tools and Equipment

  • Test set MANTA

  • Clip on ammeter

  • Voltmeter

  • Test leads

 

 

Work to be Carried Out

Preliminary steps to prepare for relay testing

  1. Obtain outage for generator protected by this relay

  2. Inspect the CT secondary circuits and all cabling supplying the relaying

  3. Use the station drawings to isolate the following

  • Breaker trip coils

  • Disturbance alarms and any recorders associated with the relay

Testing the relay and protection outputs

  1. Carefully follow these test procedure steps

  2. Verify the test results match the relay setting, if not consult technical services

Preparation

  1. This procedure is used to perform off load relay testing

  2. Look at the panel nameplate and check that this system designation agrees with the generator that has been taken out of service for maintenance

  3. Check there is no AC current from CT`s by using a clip on ammeter

Testing procedures

Pick up current test

Phase A

  1. Connect the circuit as in fig1 and adjust the tester (MANTA) to I1 low,Φ-N and A-N

  2. For the first winding, the current is injected at the terminals C1, D1

  3. Press current (I)

  4. Press dynamic then press fault

  5. Raise the current slowly from zero until the relay picks up

  6. Record this current, this is the pick up current and checks that current is in the accepted range i.e ± 10% setting

  7. Repeat the steps 1 to 6 for the second winding and the third winding

Note    For the second winding the current is injected at the terminals C2,D2
            For the third winding the current is injected at the terminals C3, D3

  1. Repeat this test for phases B and C

 

 

 

Operating time test

Phase A

  1. Connect the circuit as in fig 1 and adjust the tester (MANTA) to I1 low, Φ-N and A-N

  2. Short circuit the output of I1

  3. For the first winding, the current is injected at the terminals A1,B1

  4. Press current (I)

  5. Press dynamic then press fault and adjust the current to more than the pick up current

  6. Press static then removes the short circuit of I1 output

  7. Connect the output of I1to terminal 25-27

  8. Press time (toggle the time button to have time in seconds)

  9. Press dynamic then press fault

  10. The relay picks up and the tester is stopped after the operant time on the LCD

  11. Record this time

  12. Repeat the steps 1 to 11 for the second winding and the third winding

Note   For the second winding the current is injected at the terminals A5, B5
           For the third winding the current is injected at the terminals C1, D1

  1. Repeat this test for phases B and C

Restraint characteristic test

First winding with second winding

  1. Connect the circuit as in fig 2 and adjust the tester to I1 & I2

  2. Short circuit the output of I1

  3. Short circuit the output of I2

  4. Press I

  5. Press dynamic then press fault

  6. Adjust I1 to 0.6In

  7. Press I and adjust I2 to 0.6In

  8. Press Φ and adjust the angle to 0

  9. Press static

  10. Remove the short circuit of I1 & I2 output and connect the leads as follow:
    for I1 red at A1, black at B1 & for I2 red at B5, black at A5

  11. Press dynamic then press fault.

  12. Decrease I2 slowly until the relay picks up and the tester is stopped.

  13. Record I1 & I2 then calculate.

  14. Mean bias = (I1+I2) / 2

  15. Differential current = I1 - I2

  16. Using the curves (curve Is = setting) and at the calculated mean bias determine the theoretical differential current and compare it with the calculated differential

  17. Press reset of the tester

  18. Repeat the steps from 1 to 11

  19. Increase I2 slowly until the relay picks up and the tester is stopped

  20. Calculate the mean bias and get theoretical differential current and compare it with the calculated differential current

  21. Repeat this test for first winding with third winding for I1 red at A1, black at B1 & for I2 red at D1, black at C1

  22. Repeat the test steps from 1 to 21 for phase B and phase C

Second harmonic restraint test

As the pick up test, but the injected current is 0.6 A with second harmonic with high level and then decrease the level of second harmonic until the relay picks up then record this ratio of the second harmonic

  1. Connect the circuit as in fig 3 and adjust the tester to I4

  2. Short circuit the output of I2

  3. Press I

  4. Press dynamic then press fault

  5. Adjust I2 to 0.6 A

  6. Press I and adjust the second harmonic ratio to 30%

  7. Press static

  8. Remove the short circuit of I2 output

  9. For the first winding the current is injected at terminal C1 - D1

  10. Press dynamic then press fault

  11. Decrease the level of second harmonic until the relay picks up and records the ratio of second harmonic

  12. Repeat the steps 1 to 11 for the second winding and the third winding


Note    For the second winding the current is injected at the terminals C2,D2
            For the third winding the current is injected at the terminals C3,D3

  1. Repeat this test for phases B and C

Return relay and associated equipment to service

  1. Verify the test results match the relay setting, if not consult technical services

  2. Trip the circuit breaker associated with the relay

  3. Return relay to service by removing the test leads from the relay terminals block and the test set. Make sure CT secondary circuit is in normal condition

  4. Any equipment associated with the relay which has been isolated, must be returned to service by the following restoration steps

  •  Breaker trip coils

  • Disturbance alarm and fault recorder associated with this relay.
