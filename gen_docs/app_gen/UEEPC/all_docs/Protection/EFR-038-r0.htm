Introduction

The MCGG is a microprocessor based over-current relay available in single, two, three or four pole versions manufactured by GEC Alsthom. Each pole is provided with a choice of  four inverse time characteristics and three definite time characteristics as well as a trip test position.

An instantaneous element is also available on each phase. The MCGG 22 is the single-phase version.

Work to be Carried Out

 

Preliminary Steps to Prepare for Relay Testing

  1. This procedure is used to perform off load relay test.

  2. The test is to determine the pick up current and time.

  3. Take a copy of the relay setting.

Test Procedures

  1. Connect the circuit as shown in fig. 1.

  2. Switch off the DC supply feeding the relay.

  3. Adjust the time setting to zero.

  4. Switch on the DC supply feeding the relay.

  5. Switch on the Sverker.

  6. Raise the current from zero slowly until the relay picks up and the red led lights.

  7. This is the pick up current.

  8. Switch off the Sverker.

  9. Switch off the DC supply feeding the relay.

  10. Adjust the time setting to its normal value.

  11. Switch on the DC supply feeding the relay.

  12. Short circuiting the leads of the current

  13. Switch on the Sverker.

  14. Adjust the current to little more than the pick up current.

  15. Switch off the Sverker.

  16. Remove the short of the current leads.

  17. Switch on the Sverker to apply the adjusted current suddenly.

  18. The relay trips after a time this is the pick up time.

  19. Compare the pick up current and pick up time with the settings.
