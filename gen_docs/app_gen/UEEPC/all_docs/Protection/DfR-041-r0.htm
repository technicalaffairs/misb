Introduction

The type SBD11B is a single phase, high speed, high impedance, static differential relay. It was specially designed to provide differential protection for high and low voltage AC busses. However, because of its design and sensitivity, this relay may also be used for shunt reactor differential protection. While the normal bus scheme requires three SBD11B relays – one per phase –the number of relays required for the protection shunt reactor depends on the scheme employed.

The first preliminary steps to prepare for relay testing describes the activities required prior to testing. Some of these steps could be performed days before the actual test. The second section Preparation for testing describes the steps to set up the equipment for performing the actual tests. The third section is the actual test.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety ID category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear your Safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, shoes, safety belt.... etc

  • The generator must be isolated and work permit required.

  • Make sure that CT secondary circuits are shorted before isolating the current circuit for test current injection.

  • Switch off DC power supply before inserting or withdrawing any card from the relay module.

Tools and Equipment

  • Test set SVERKER 650

  • Ammeter

  • Voltmeter. 

  • Test leads.

 

Work to be Carried Out    

Preliminary Steps to Prepare the Relay Testing

  1. Obtain outage for generator protected by this relay.

  2. Inspect the CT secondary circuits and all cabling supplying the relaying.

  3. Use the station drawings to isolate the following:

  • Breaker trip coils.

  • Disturbance alarms and any recorders associated with the relay.

Testing the Relay and Protection Outputs

  1. Carefully follow these test procedure steps.

  2. Verify the test results match the relay setting, if not consult technical services.

Preparation

  1. This procedure is used to perform off load relay testing.

  2. Look at the panel nameplate and check that this system designation agrees with the generator that has been taken out of service for maintenance.

  3. Check there is no AC current from CT’s by using a clip on ammeter.

Testing Procedure (s)

Pick up Voltage Test

  1. Connect the circuit as in Fig1. 

  2. Adjust the sverker to 95 volt AC (voltage tap = 50).       

  3. Apply this voltage to the relay as shown in fig. 1.

  4. Increase the voltage until the voltmeter begins to decrease and the ammeter to increase.      

  5. Record the value before the voltmeter begins to decrease (100 ± 4).

 Pick up Current and Output Test

  1. Connect the circuit as in Fig 2

  2. Adjust the tape voltage setting to 50 and the tape current setting to 0.5

  3. Increase the current until the lamp light.     

  4. Record the ammeter reading.

Tap Voltage Test

  1. Connect the circuit as in Fig 3

  2. Adjust the voltage tape setting to 50.

  3. Apply 5 volt AC (10% of tape voltage) between the terminals 5&6.

  4. Read the volt between 5&16 (5V ± 2%)

  5. Repeat the above four steps but with voltage tape setting = 100.

  6. Repeat the above four steps but with voltage tape setting = 150.

Cautions

If the DC supply is removed, studs 5&6 should be shorted by pulling the connection plug

Don’t apply voltage to the relay longer than 10 seconds and don’t re-apply voltage more often than once every 2 minutes

Return Relay and Associated Equipment to Service

  1. Verify the test results match the relay setting, if not consult technical services.

  2. Trip the circuit breaker associated with the relay.

  3. Return relay to service by removing the test leads from the relay terminals block and the test set. Make sure CT secondary circuit is in normal condition.

  4. Any equipment associated with the relay which has been isolated, must be returned to service by the following restoration steps:

  • Breaker trip coils.

  • Disturbance alarm and fault recorder associated with this relay.
