Introduction

This procedure details the testing procedures P1, which recommended to be carried out every six months as indicated by M6 in the title block.

The purpose of the relay is basically to give an indication that an inter turn fault occur to earth in the rotor winding or turn-to-turn fault which give alarm only.

The relay contains over current relay set at 0.05 amp, timer operates at one-second-delay time, two capacitors to prevent DC from returning to the relay and path the AC and voltage transformer supplied with 110Vac from the voltage transformer of the rotor winding as the generator operate and 100 v from the testing transformer when the unit off, the other winding of the transformer one terminal is earthed through the rotor brush and the other is connected to the coil of the relay as the fault occur (the circuit is completed through the brush and allowed the current to path to the relay and it operate to give alarm signal.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety ID category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear your Safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, shoes, safety belt.... etc

  • The line must be out of service

  • Isolate the relay from PT`s & CT`s

Tools and Equipment

  • Special test key.

  • Avometer and connecting leads

  • Test transformer to give the test voltage (100v).

  • Selector switch to get the test voltage from the test transformer to the relay and isolate the voltage coming from the main unit transformer.

 

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Turn the supply voltage switch from the off position to the auxiliary voltage position.

  2. Insert the special test key into the test selector switch (b6).

  3. Change the position of the test selector switch by the test key on the test panel to the test delay action position.

  4. Press on the test key button until the signal appears.

  5. Release the button of the test key and return the selector back to its first position (action).

  6. Return the supply voltage selector switch back to its off position.

  7. Reset the rotor earth leakage relay to its normal position.

  8. Record the values (results) on the test sheet.
