Introduction

This procedure details the testing procedures P1, which recommended to be carried out every six months as indicated by M6 in the title block.

The purpose of the relay is basically to prevent damage on the turbine, if the driving torque less than the total losses in the generator and the prime mover, the generator start to work as a synchronous compensator, taking the necessary active power from the network

The reverse power relay is provided with one step, the relay shall be set at 10% of the generator rated power and shall give a trip after a time delay of 10 sec. The relay contains one directional current unit and one time delay unit.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety ID category, 

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear your Safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, shoes, safety belt.... etc

  • The line is out of service

  • Isolate the relay from PT`s & CT`s

Tools and Equipment

  • Special test key.

  • Avometer and connecting leads

  • Test transformer to give the test voltage (7v).

  • Selector switch to get the test voltage from the test transformer to the relay and isolate the voltage coming from the main unit transformer.

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Turn the supply voltage switch from the off position to the auxiliary voltage position.

  2. Insert the special test key into the test selector switch (b5).

  3. Change the position of the test selector switch by the test key on the test panel to the test delay action position.

  4. Press on the test key button until the signal appears.

  5. Release the button of the test key and return the selector back to its first position (action).

  6. Return the supply voltage selector switch back to its off position.

  7. Reset the reverse power relay to its normal position.

  8. Record the values (results) on the test sheet.      

Remarks

Our relay has a special working mechanism with two coils one coil for the current sample and the other for the voltage, during the generator working the relay is supplied from the voltage and current transformer of the generator along two auxiliary contacts of a selector switch (b5) and during the test (the unit is out of service) the relay is supplied from test transformer, which give (7v) to the terminals 1,2 of the relay along the voltage selector switch which disconnect the other auxiliary contacts which getting the voltage from the generator and connect them to the testing transformer to give (100 v) to the terminals 3,4 of the relay as shown in the figure and the 110 volt (d.c) is connected through the terminals 5,6.As the injection value from the testing transformer reached to the setting the relay operate with time delay (10 sec).
