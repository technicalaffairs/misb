Introduction

This procedure details the testing procedures P1, which recommended to be carried out every three months as indicated by M3 in the title block.

The purpose of the relay is basically to give an indication that a serious fault within the transformer, this means the gas generation is in much more quantities and the oil is displaced in the relay by the gases, to the conservator tank, with the result that the lower float is now no more under the buoyancy of the coil and falls down under the effect of counterweights, this tilts the mercury switch shorts contacts of the alarm then trip circuit.

The relay is also useful in indicating any loss of oil that a transformer might suffer because the loss of oil will cause oil level to drop in the relay then the top float will indicate it by dropping and shorting the alarm contacts and if the oil level keeps on falling the lower float will be affected and will close the trip circuit contacts.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety ID category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear your Safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, shoes, safety belt.... etc

  • The transformer must be out of service

Tools and Equipment

  • Avometer and connecting leads.

  • Safety and healthy equipment.

  • Special test blower.

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Ensure that the transformer out of service.

  2. Connect the special test blower into the test valve and blow air slowly until the alarm contacts close and so on until the trip contacts close, the trip circuit contacts thereby isolating transformer.

  3. Press on the test button until the (Alarm, Trip) signals appears.

  4. Reset the test button into original position.

  5. Release all signals in the dangers board.

  6. Record the values (results) at the MRDS.
