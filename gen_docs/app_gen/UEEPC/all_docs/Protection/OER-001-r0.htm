Introduction

This document outlines the testing procedure P1, which must be performed on an annually bases as indicated by Y1 in the title block.

The GTT relay and an associated type VAA auxiliary follower relay are designed to prevent over fluxing of the 500MVA auto transformer 500/220/11kv.

The over fluxing protection operates when the ratio of terminal voltage to frequency exceeds a pre-determined setting, and resets when the ratio falls below 95 to 98% of the operating ratio.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and maintenance staff must carry the suitable safety category,

  • Safety rope with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Safety and healthy equipment must be used as safety shoes, safety Helmet ...etc.

  • The transformer must be isolated, earthed from the three sides.

  • Switch off the DC power supply before inserting or withdrawing the relay.

Tools and Equipment

  • Secondary injection test set type SVERKER 650

  • Test leads, and Multi-meter (AVO)

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Check the minimum operating voltage.

  2. Check the time delay.

  3. Checking the tripping of all breakers associated with the relay.

Preparation

  1. The AC test supply should be at a known fixed frequency and adjustable voltage.

  2. Prepare the DC supply.

  3. Withdraw the relay from its case.

  4. Prepare the circuit as in Figure 1.

  5. Using Sverker 650 inject in the secondary circuits & record the pick-up frequency, reset frequency, the pick-up voltage, and the time.

Checking the Minimum Operating Voltage

  1. Connect the variable AC source to points 9 & 10 ... see figure 1

  2. Adjust the voltage to the normal voltage.

  3. Slowly increase the voltage until the left auxiliary relay picks up after about 0.5 to 1 sec.

  4. Record this voltage value (Minimum operating voltage).

  5. Calculate the V/F ratio and compare it with the setting.

  6. Slowly lowering the applied voltage until the auxiliary relay resets.

  7. Record this voltage value (resetting voltage).

  8. The resetting voltage should be greater than 95% of the measured operating voltage.

Checking the Time Delay

  1. Connect the output contact 3 & 4 ... see figure 1 to the timer contact of the test set.

  2. Re-apply the minimum operating voltage; the timer will stop once the right auxiliary relay has picked up.

  3. Record this time and compare it with the time delay setting.

  4. Remove all the leads from the relay.

  5. Insert the relay in its case.

Checking the Tripping of all Breakers Associated with the Relay

  1. Close all breakers associated with this relay.

  2. Disconnect the wires from points 9 & 10 from the relay.

  3. Apply the minimum operating voltage on 9&10 until the relay operates after the time delay and check the breakers are tripped.

  4. Re-connect the wires to points 9 & 10 and reset the relay.

Return the Relay and the Associated equipment to Service

  1. Disconnect the Sverker 650 & all connection leads from the relay

  2. Make sure that every disconnected wires are connected to their positions.

  3. Check that the VT connections are in normal connections.

  4. Reconnect any alarm or disturbance signal you have disconnected.

  5. Check that there are no alarms on the relay panel or control panel.

  6. Return relay to its normal place.
