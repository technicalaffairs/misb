Introduction

This document outlines the instrument testing procedures I1, which must be carried out on an annually bases as indicated by Y1 in the title block.

This document for all types of pressure, temperature, flow, and level indicators.

The first draft initiated by eng. Khalid Abd Allah Mohamed & eng. Mostafa Ali Kheder, accepted by technical expert eng. Hassan El-Behiry, and approved by eng. Fathy El Sayed Head sector of El-Kuriemat Power Station.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the maintenance staff should be qualified and carry the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear the personal protective equipment must used as safety shoes, safety helmet, earplug, and gloves pairs...etc

  • Close manual valve for Indicator line.

Tools and Equipments

  • Safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes ... etc.

Work to be carried out

Steps to Prepare for Test

  2. Obtain copy of indicator setting values and record it in its MRDS.

Gauge Problems

Chattering

Chattering is a violent fluctuation of a gauge's pointer that can seriously damage a gauge's internal parts to prevent this throttling down the supply line until the chattering stops.

Excessive Heat in Steams Damage a Gauge and affect its accuracy to Prevent this :

  1. Close manual valve for pressure indicator line.

  2. Remove the pressure indicator from its location.

  3. Make Seal loops like the one pictured in figure 1 .

  4. This seal reduces the amount of heat that reaches the gauge. It also protects the gauge from rapid pressure fluctuation.

 

  

                        Figure 1:  Seal Loop

 

Return indicator to service

  1. Return the pressure indicator back to its location.

  2. Open manual valve for pressure indicator line.
