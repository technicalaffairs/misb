Introduction

This document details the check list, which should be carried out on a weekly, monthly bases and every three months bases as indicated by W1, M1, M3 in the title block.

Tawfeq El-abasey, accepted by general manager eng. Mohamed Abd Elghaffar, and certified by eng. Moustafa Al Lethy; EDEPCo. Inspection & QC head sector.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear protective safety helmet, shoes, safety clothes, goggles, and rubber gloves.

Tools and Equipment

  • Safety helmet, shoes,

  • Hydrometer, Digital voltmeter

  • Thermometer, Filler.

Work to be Carried Out

Important Notes

  • When cells are supplied filled with acid they must be placed on float charge or given an equalizing or freshening charge within 90 days from the date of shipment.

  • Never add acid to increase specific gravity readings.

  • Gases given off by batteries on charge are explosive!

  • Don't smoke or permit open flames near batteries or do anything to cause sparks.

  • The room in which the battery is installed should be well ventilated and its temperature as moderate as the climate will allow.

  • The temperature of the electrolyte should preferably never exceed 38 OC.

 

Weekly

  1. Keep containers, lids and battery rooms perfectly clean.

  2. Wipe out salt and acid stains on terminals, connections and lids with a wet sponge.

  3. Never clean containers and lids with solvents, detergents, oil or similar products.

  4. Wash caps water and dry them before putting them back on the battery.

Monthly

  1. Check total voltage at battery terminals. It must equal: N * U

          - N = number of battery cells
          - U = floating voltage per cell to be applied according to cell type and annual average room temperature.

  2. Measure voltage, specific gravity and temperature of a few reference cells.

  3. Check electrolyte levels.

  4. Measure room temperature.

Quarterly (every 3 months)

  1. Measure total voltage of battery and ambient temperature.

  2. Measure all cells voltages.

  3. Measure specific gravity of a few cells with varying voltages as well as electrolyte level and temperature.

  4. Apply equalizing charge.

An Autonomy Test can be Applied Once or Twice Year

Water Topping up

  • Filling up with distilled or de – ionized water must be carried out on a charged  battery, before electrolyte reaches minimum level.

  • When adding water, never exceed maximum level.

  • Time intervals between two water topping up vary from 1 to 6 years.

  • Type of alloy.

  • Cell type.

  • Room temperature.

  • Battery age.

  • After having checked electrolyte levels, it is necessary to apply an equalizing charge to homogenize electrolyte until nominal specific gravity is obtained.

Log Book

We recommend that a log book be kept in wish the following information is recorded:

  • Periodical measurements.

  • Capacity tests.

  • Storage times and conditions.

  • Filling – up dates.

  • Power cuts, etc...

Advice for Great Reliability and Longer Life of Batteries

A battery is generally the last protective barrier against power cuts; battery maintenance is therefore very important for it conditions its life and it consists mainly in:

  • Correctly adjusting floating voltage.

  • Topping up with distilled or de – ionized water at intervals depending on cell technology (NEVER ADD SULFURIC ACID).

  • Keeping the battery clean after adding distilled water.

  • Keeping connections perfectly clean, clean them it necessary.

  • Applying equalizing charges, essential to maintain a proper state of charge and homogeneous electrolyte.

Constant Current Charge

  1. Must be carried out under watching.

  2. Charging current will if possible be restricted to 0.03 C10.

  3. If temperature reaches 45C, charging must be stopped and resumed when electrolyte temperature is 35 C

  4. The battery is charged when every cell without exception reaches the specific gravity nominal 20C with electrolyte midway between max and min level.
