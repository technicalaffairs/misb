Introduction

This document details the check list, which must be performed on a weekly, monthly and every three months bases as indicated by W1, M1, M3 in the title block.

This first draft is initiated by eng. Samy Mohamed Hassan, tested by eng. Al Sayed Mahmoud Al Ashry at 18 Jan 2004, revised by EDEPC quality control eng. Tawfik El-Abasery, accepted by  eng. Mohamed Abd El-Ghaffar General director of Al Aresh PS, and certified by eng. Aly Mostafa Al Lethy EDEPC Inspection and quality Control Head Sector.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area is required,

  • Wear protective safety helmet, shoes, safety clothes, goggles, and rubber gloves,

  • The battery instructions must be displayed visibly on the wall..

  • Water source must be available in the battery room.

  • Battery group must be out of service.

Tools and Equipment

  • Safety helmet, shoes, Hydrometer,

  • Thermometer, Filler, Digital voltmeter

Work to be Carried Out

Important Notes:

  • When cells are supplied filled with acid they must be placed on float charge or given an equalizing or freshening charge within 90 days from the date of shipment.

  • Never add acid to increase specific gravity readings.

  • Gases given off by batteries on charge are explosive!

  • Don't smoke or permit open flames near batteries or do anything to cause sparks.

  • The room in which the battery is installed should be well ventilated and its temperature as moderate as the climate will allow.

  • The temperature of the electrolyte should preferably never exceed 38oC.

Procedures

  1. Check the electrolyte levels in all cells regularly and if necessary top up with distilled water.

  2. Never allow the electrolyte level to fall below the "MIN" line.

  3. Don't over fill the cells.

  4. Set the charger to "equalize" for about 30 minutes to help mix the electrolyte when water has been add.

  5. Keep the battery and surroundings clean and dry.

  6. Wipe the cells with a clean soft cloth dampened with clean water.

  7. If necessary, a small amount of mild detergent may be added to the cleaning water to remove any greasy film.

  8. Don't use any special powders or solvents for cleaning the battery cells, as scratching or damage to the plastic could occur.

  9. Make sure that bolted connections are tight.

  10. Keep connectors, posts and bolted connections covered with "No oxide" grease for protection against corrosion.

  11. Should any corrosion of the connections occur because of spilled acid ete.

  12. Carefully remove corrosion materials, thoroughly clean and neutralize with dilute ammonia or backing soda.

  13. Dry the parts before coating them "No- oxide" grease to protect from further corrosion.

  14. Don't let the neutralizing solution enter the cell.

  15. Keep the battery at the recommended charge voltage.

  2. Whenever the battery is subjected to discharge of more than 5 to10 percent of its rated capacity, it should be recharged as soon as possible.

 

 

 

Cell Appearance

  1. Healthy cells, when fully charged, show a marked contrast between the dark brown positive and the light grey negative plats.

  2. For cells in transparent jars (SMF, SMBF, SMZA, SD, SDH, SGL, SGH), it is extremely use full to inspect the appearance of each cell in the battery at regular intervals.

  3. Any cells not showing a healthy plate coloration, or having a specific gravity or voltage noticeable lower than the other cells, or in which the plates bubble unevenly or not at all, should be regarded as suspect.

  4. Such cells should be carefully examined for internal short- circuits, such as may be caused by small pieces of scale bridging across the plates.

  5. A period of charging will normally restore such cells to the condition of the remainder of the battery, but if it does not, expert advice should be obtained immediately form FIAMM.

Pilot Cell

  1. For regular monitoring of the battery condition select one cell near the middle of the battery as a" pilot" cell (for batteries comprising more than 60 cells, select one pilot cell for every 60 cells).

  2. The electrolyte specific gravity of the pilot cell ( s ) will be indicative of the state of charge of the whole battery.

Maintenance Record Data Sheet

Written records should be kept of battery maintenance, so that long-term changes in battery condition may be monitored.

The following Inspection Procedures are Recommended

Weekly

Check and record the overall float voltage at the battery terminals (not at the charge!), and measure the pilot cell voltage.

Monthly

Record the battery voltage and the voltage, specific gravity and the temperature of the pilot cell (s).

Quarterly (every 3 months)

Record the voltage, specific gravity and temperature of all cells.

  • It is a good practice to give the battery a full discharge test at 5 year intervals until signs of degradation are observed or until the battery has reached 85 percent of its original capacity

  • Once this stage has been reached, yearly capacity tests should be conducted until the battery reaches the end of its useful life.

  • See IEEE standard 450-1980 for recommendations concerning test procedures and battery replacement criteria.
