Replacement of Defected Battery Cell		 Approved Date:    19 Mar 2005

Introduction

This document outlines procedures P1, which performed to replace the defected cell by a new one, only when it is necessary according to the measured values of the cells.

The first draft initiated by eng. Abdallah Elsayed M, revised by EDEPC quality control eng. Adel  Abd EL kader El Nagar, accepted by eng El Sayed Attiah general director, and approved by eng. Awad Soliman Head Sector of Talkha PS.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, qualified maintenance staff carry the suitable safety ID category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, safety goggles, rubber gloves, and rubber apron, nose mask ... etc

  • No smoking or using any material cause ignition or fire in battery room.

  • Use the proper insulated tools.

  • Don't use tools used at maintaining Alkaline batteries to maintain acid batteries and vise versa

Tools and Equipment

  • Safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, ... etc

  • Suitable and isolated tools to perform tight and loose of different screws.

    •

  • Digital multimeter special for battery

  • Hydrometer

  • Suitable suit against acid

 

Work to be Carried out

  1. Wear the safety and healthy protective equipment.

  2. Prepare the new cell by filling it with electrolyte 1.22 gm/cm3 up to the mark indicating maximum level as follows:

  3. Wash the cell with distillate water before putting the solution by putting quantity of distillate water with grams of potassium hydroxide with small qty Lithium hydroxide (to save battery life and fixing the solution) in glass vessel and continue mixing, measure density, add distillate water in case of increasing of density or add potassium hydroxide in case of decreasing of density to reach 1.22 gm/cm3

  4. Charge the cell using suitable 2V charger, with great attention to the temperature of the cell.

  5. Put the battery out of service by coordination with shift engineer.

  6. Connect a suitable insulated cable (bypass) with terminals clamps between cells before & after the cell to be changed, take care of polarity to make the voltage difference between point of disconnect and previous point equal zero.

  7. Ensure that the cable of bypass is good connected because of working on direct current.

  8. Disconnect the old cell and remove it from its position.

  9. Loose strap connecting between (cell 1), (cell 2) and between (cell 2), (cell 3).

  10. Lift cell no. 2 and put the new cell in place.

  11. Tight perfectly the loosen straps.

  12. Place the battery back into normal operating condition.

  13. Record the battery readings in the MRDS Form
