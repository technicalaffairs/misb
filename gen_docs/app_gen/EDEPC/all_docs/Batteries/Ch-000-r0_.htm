Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff must carry the suitable ID safety category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required,

  • Wear the appropriate safety equipment as safety shoes, helmet,       

Work to be carried out

Daily or once a Week

  1. Check all alarm and status indicators.

  2. Check meter readings are normal. Compare them with the test certificate.

  3. Check the cause of any significant changes for example,

  4. Change in load, recent discharge

  5. Record all abnormal occurrences in the service log

Weekly or once a Month

  1. Complete daily check.

  2. Check ventilation apertures are clean and clear of   obstructions 

  3. On vented cells check the battery electrolyte levels and top up if necessary in accordance with the manufacturers instructions

At Least 4 times / Year

  1. Check ventilation grilles

  2. Visually check electrical connections and component for signs of overheating or rectify as necessary.

  3. Check painted and plated components for signs of damage or corrosion.

  4. Rectify as necessary

Annually

  1. Carry out a test discharge of the battery for the specified autonomy.

  2. Note the test should be carried out at a time of low risk and followed immediately by a full recharge.

  3. Consult the battery manufacturers instruction for guidance on frequency of testing, depth of discharge etc.

Every Five Years

Fans are rated for a 5-year life it is recommended that fan failures be preempted by renewing them after 4 years
