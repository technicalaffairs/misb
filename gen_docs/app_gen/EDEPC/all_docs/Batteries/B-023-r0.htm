Introduction

This document lists the equipment inspection I1, which must be performed on an annually bases as indicated by Y1 in the title block

Type: Lead Acid Battery – Maintenance Free

No. of Cells: 24

Cell Voltage: 2v

Capacity: 300 Ah

The first draft initiated by eng. Abdallah Elsayed M, revised by quality control eng. Adel  Abd – EL kader El nagar at EDEP Co., Accepted by eng El Sayed Attiah, and approved by eng. Awad Soliman Head Sector of Talkha PS.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff must carry the suitable ID safety category,

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area,

  • Wear appropriate personal protective equipment must be used, safety shoes, helmet, breathing mask, rubber gloves

  • The door of the battery room must be provided with NO Smoking signs.

  • The battery instructions must be displayed visibly on the wall..

  • Water source must be available in the battery room.

  • When working in the battery room be aware of parts under voltage.

  • The battery set is out of service if necessary.

Tools and Equipment

  • Digital multimeter   ( AVO – METER )

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Make sure that the cells are clean and dry, externally clean the caps.

  2. Check the connections are properly tightened lubricate and fully screw them.

  3. Check and read the voltage of each cell.

  4. Voltage must be homogeneous within  ± 0.02v.

  5. Make partial discharge with nominal power during a time equal to the half of the battery autonomy.

  6. For that stop the floating charge ( rectifiers ) and note regular times equal to 1/10 of the autonomy the total  voltage and the using current.

  7. Before stopping the discharge, note the voltage of each cell. it will show if  the autonomy is still good and if any cell is not working bad.

  8. Measure the battery internal resistance of the battery ref. to MPIS doc no. B-021-r0

  9. Place the battery set back into normal operating condition
