Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection I3, which must be performed every six months bases as indicated by M6 in the title block.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, qualified maintenance staff carry the suitable safety ID category

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required

  • No smoking is permitted in battery room.

  • Use standard personal safety equipment as helmet, safety shoes, safety goggles, and rubber gloves, and rubber apron.

  • Use the proper insulated tools.

  • Disconnect the power supply fuse.

  • Don't place any tools on top of the battery cells.

Tools and Equipment

  • Standard personal safety and healthy equipment

  • Brushes, distilled water, rags, safety goggles, rubber gloves and rubber apron.

  • Standard screwdriver set.

  • AVO meter

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Clean dust of battery.

  2. Check that connections are tightened and clean any salt deposits with distilled water.

  3. Check for the correct position of all vent covers.

  4. Check the electrolyte level and add distilled water if necessary.

  5. Check the ventilation of the battery room to ensure their correct operation

  6. Clean battery room floor with water.

  7. Measure the internal resistance of the battery.

  8. Replace the defect battery in all units

  9. Rearrangement state: Connect all items which are disconnected.
