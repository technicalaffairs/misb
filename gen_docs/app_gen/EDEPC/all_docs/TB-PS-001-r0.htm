Trouble Shooting Tables

    These trouble shooting will help maintenance staff to overcome most of problems may be occurred during carrying out the maintenance procedure.

Trouble

Possible Reasons

Remedy

Defects at start up

1. When engine is started with compressed air, the Crankshaft does not make full revolution or does not rotate at all.

- shut-off valve or starting or starting air cylinders and starting air pipe closed

-  Open the valves

- Too low starting air pressure in air cylinders

-   Pump air to the required pressure

- Indicator cocks not closed.

-   Close.

- Starting valve stuck in.

-   Test starting value.

- Open position or not sealing in hole.

-   Examine incorrect valve , release or grind in if required.

2. When started with air, engine develops sufficient speed for starting, but when passing to fuel, does not fire or misses.

-  Fuel not supplied to fuel injection pumps.

-  Check whether there is fuel in service tank and whether valves are open on fuel line. Fill pipe with fuel and pump the fuel injection pumps. Then move the fuel control fuel to its normal position and bleed the system.

-  Air in fuel pipe and pump.

-  Bleed fuel injection system, check the screw of injection valve and pump by hand by the fuel injection pump tappet lever.

- Fuel injection valve needle and delivery valve of some cylinder seized, owing to which air penetrated into main pipe.

-  Check the beginning of fuel injection.

-  Delayed fuel supply to cylinder.

-  Release fuel injection valve needle and delivery valve, or replace.

-  Great quantity of water in fuel.

-  Drain water from fuel service tank , fuel pipe and cleaner. Fill tank with clean fuel , remove fuel injection valves from cylinders and operate fuel injection pump by hand.

-  Governor stuck in “stop” position and disconnected fuel injection pumps.

-  Release governor lever and pull rods, which act, on fuel injection pump. Remove cause of sticking.

-  Pressure for fuel atomization not ensured by fuel injection pump.

-  Check the pump with pressure , clean valves, or grind in. If pump plunger clearance too great .Replace plunger and cylinder by new fitted ones

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Trouble

Possible Reasons

Remedy

 

- Fuel injection valve needles not sealing in closed position or nozzle holes clogged.

- Disassemble fuel injection valve, grind-in needle, clean nozzle holes or fit new nozzle. When fitting check the spring tension by maximeter and atomization of fuel by a tester.

- Insufficient compression pressure in cylinders.

- Check clearance under exhaust and suction valve rocker arm roller, set the clearance. Check valve tightness. Remove sticking, if any, by grinding in, or replace. Check compression chamber. If gasket between cylinder head and cylinder not sealing, tighten the cylinder head.

- Temperature in engine room too low and engine is cold.

- Warm up cylinders by filling engine with warm water.

       ×          Defects during operation

1.     Engine stop.

-  No fuel supplies to the fuel injection pump.

- Check whether there is fuel in service tank and whether valves are open on fuel line. Fill pipe with fuel and pump the fuel injection pumps. Then move the fuel control fuel to its normal position and bleed the system.

- Water in tanks, from which fuel is supplied pumps.

- Drain water and fill main fuel line with clean fuel and operate fuel injection pump by hand with fuel injection valve, check screw open.

- Air in suction chamber of fuel injection pump owing to untight pipe or pump.

- Force air out of the fuel injection pump by pumping fuel by hand with check screw of fuel injection valve open. Remove air leaks in joints.

2.     Engine speed decreases.

 

 

Exhaust gas temperature rises and exhausts smoke increased.

-  Engine over loaded.

- Throw load of engine and set speed to rated.

-  Fuel injection valve needle cone untight in hole or seizing.

- Grind in needle or replace nozzle.

Lower exhaust gas temperature.

- Fuel supply reduced.

- Check level of fuel in service tank.

 

 

Trouble

Possible Reasons

Remedy

1. Knocks in engine cylinders and moving parts of engine.

-  Increased fuel injection advance , increased fuel supply / manifests itself in knocks in cylinder at piston position in T.D.C

- Cut out fuel injection pump of defective cylinders and if knocking stops, reduce injection advance- if exhaust gas temperature was high , reduce fuel feed . If knocking continues after fuel injection advance and fuel feed have been reduced, it may be detonation of fuel, which indicates that fuel of unsuitable grade, is used.

-  Fuel injection valve needles seizing / manifests it self in knocks in cylinders at position of piston in T.D.C.

-  Check fuel injection valves. Remove defects.

- Main or big –end bearing or gudgeon bearing clearance excessive or bolts are loose / manifests itself in dull, increasing or squealing metallic impacts, engine speed remaining constant.

- Stop engine, check clearance and condition of surfaces. If clearances are excessive, the corresponding bearing should be replaced. Replace worn gudgeon pin or shell by new one.

-  Worn bearings / knocking of big –end bearings, engine speed decreases.

- Stop engine, examine big-end bearings and shaft journals. Remove defects. Mount spare bearing on clean shaft journals. If cracks are found on crankshaft journals, operation of engine is not allowed.

- Large clearance between gear teeth or heavy wear of teeth / manifests itself in knocks in gearing.

- Remove excessive clearance in teeth or replace worn gears.

       ×          Defects on some assemble.

1.  Non –uniform operation of individual assemblies.

- In sufficient final compression pressure in some cylinder.

-  Check whether gases do not escape in a large amount in some cylinder via piston rings. Turn suction and exhaust valves occasionally to clean seating surface of carbon particles.

 

 

Trouble

Possible Reasons

Remedy

 

- Fuel injection leaking at inlet.

- Check pump operation, remove defects. If air penetrates into pump or water penetrates with fuel into the suction space, operate the pump by hand lever, check where air penetrates, remove the defect. Drain water from service tank and cleaner as frequently as possible.

- Fuel injection valve needle seizing, due to distortion.

- Disassemble fuel injection valve, remove defect. Check before mounting on engine. Clean fuel injection valve, or replace.

       ×          Engine smokes

1.   Dark exhaust gases.

- Great quantity of fuel supplied to cylinder.

- Reduce supply of fuel at fuel injection pump of corresponding cylinder.

- Fuel injection valve needle seizing or nozzle holes clogged.

- Check the fuel injection valve delivery pipe by hand, if hard impacts and abnormal heating are felt, stop engine, clean valve nozzle or replace nozzle and operate fuel injection pump by hand.

- Delivery valve of fuel injection pump leaking or its spring broken.

- Examine and grind in delivery valve , replace spring by new one.

- Insufficient compression pressure.

- On the first occasion adjust pressure in cylinder to normal value.

- Delayed fuel delivery.

- Set beginning of fuel delivery.

- Injection valve nozzle holes heavily worn.

- Replace worn nozzle.

2. Exhaust gases of blush colour.

- A great quantity of oil penetrates into cylinder.

- Scraper rings incorrectly mounted or heavily worn. On the first occasion replace worn rings.

3. Exhaust gases of whitish colour.

-  Partial or imperfect combustion.

- Low temperature in combustion chamber , some fuel evaporated due to incomplete combustion. Increase load and on the first occasion adjust compression to normal.

- Influent fuel contains water escapes from cylinder heads.

- Drain water from fuel filter and service tank more frequently. Cylinder heads with cracks should be replaced as soon as possible.

 

 

Trouble

Possible Reasons

Remedy

×     Safety valve blow

- Remnant of fuel oil in injection valves before starting due to excessive priming or increased fuel oil supply.

- After closing regulation screws of injection valves prime fuel oil in an amount corresponding to 1-2 measured deliveries check if during turning engine with air fuel oil penetrates into injection valve.

- Combustion pressure higher than normal or safety valve spring tension insufficient if a safety valve blow after each working stroke.

- Reduce fuel injection advance and adjust tension of safety valve spring or remove seizing.

- Cylinder overload, water penetrates into cylinder during suction.

- Reduce delivery of fuel, examine and test cylinder head with pressure and if cracks are found replace cylinder head by new one.

- End of injection nozzle defective.

- Replace defective nozzle by spares one.

- Safety valve on the cylinder head blows

- Check or adjust the combustion pressures. If the combustion pressures are correct, the defect should be looked for in the valve.

×      Defects in lubrication system

 

1.    Defects in oil system operation.

- Oil pump not delivering oil.

- Check level of oil in tank, replenish if necessary, examine suction pipe and all joint. If axial and radial clearance of oil pump gears are too large, remove pump cover and check axial and radial clearance between gears and pump casing. If clearances are too large due to heavy wear of gears, replace gears and remove also other defects which found during inspection.

- Oil pump not developing necessary pressure.

- If oil leaks at delivery pipe/in engine or beyond it/, stop engine, subject pipe to pressure test and simultaneously check main and big-end clearances. If pressure-reducing valve of oil pump does not seal, it is necessary to pass over to the other pump on the engine or a spare pump. On the first occasion examine and check the valves.

 

Trouble

Possible Reasons

Remedy

 

- Oil cleaner not operating normally.

- If the difference in pressure gauge readings / pressure gauges before and after cleaner / is small, the cleaner operates correctly. If the difference in pressure before and behind cleaner is too great, the cleaner is choked. Clean the cleaner elements by closing the cock put out cleaner out of service and clean it.

-  Water in lubricating oil.

- The presence of water manifests itself in which colour of oil. All the oil should be changed. The cause of the defect should be found and the defect removed. Operation of engine wit oil mixed with water is not allowed.

 2. High temperature of oil flowing from engine.

- Insufficient oil cooling.

- Check oil temperature before and after cooler. Open valve and increase flow of water througt oil cooler.

- Insufficient Quantity of circulating oil.

- Check level of oil in tank, replenish if necessary, examine suction pipe and all joint. If axial and radial clearance of oil pump gears are too large, remove pump cover and check axial and radial clearance between gears and pump casing. If clearances are too large due to heavy wear of gears, replace gears and remove also other defects which found during inspection.

- Insufficient pressure in lubricating system.

- If oil leaks at delivery pipe/in engine or beyond it/, stop engine, subject pipe to pressure test and simultaneously check main and big-end clearances. If pressure-reducing valve of oil pump does not seal, it is necessary to pass over to the other pump on the engine or a spare pump. On the first occasion examine and check the valves.

- Excessive bearing temperature rises.

- Stop engine, check clearance, mounting of shafts flow of oil through lubricating system and remove defects which have caused bearing temperature rise. Starts engine again and check.

Possible Reasons

Remedy

×          Defects in cooling system. 

1.     Great difference in inlet and outlet water.

- Closed valve on water pipe.

- Open valve

- Insufficient quantity of water in tank of closed circuit.

- Top up with fresh water.

-  Incorrect operation of water pump.

-  Repair pump.

- Scales in cooling water spaces in cylinders, in cylinder heads and in water cooler spaces or these spaces are clogged.

- Clean cooling spaces of cylinders, cylinder heads and water cooler.

2.     High outlet water temperature of engine.

-   Engine over loaded, high exhaust gas temperature.

-  Check exhausts gas temperature at individual cylinders and reduce engine speed.

-  Insufficient water supplies.

-  Take measure to increase water supply.

Speed fluctuation.

1.     Engine gains speed exceeding the rated value.

-  Seizure of governor pulls rod.

-  Repair pulls rod.

-  Governor spring controlling fuel oil lever has slipped out.

-  Remount spring back in its correct place.

-  Governor is out of order.

-  Check the governor

2.     Engine operates with great speed variation due to governor being out of order.

-  Low lubricating oil temperature at governor inlet less than 20 °C.

- Increase lubricating oil temperature.

1.     Gas penetrates into the engine casing.

Broken or seized piston rings.
