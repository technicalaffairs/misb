Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes ... etc

  • Isolate the vessel from the feed water system.

  • Be sure that the heater has been completely drained.

  • Check the pressure gauges show zero pressure.

  • Check thermometers and assure that temperature is less than 38 °C.

  • Provide adequate scaffolding lighting, platforms or ladders.

  • Make certain that all safety tags are replaced when maintenance and inspection are complete.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set.

  • Cleaning articles (fluid, rags.… etc)

  • Feeler gauge, suitable lifting device.

Work to be Carried Out 

Internal inspection for evidence of corrosion, scaling crack or broken or worn parts.

Spray Valve

  1. Valve must seat firmly and check for debris.

  2. Valve nuts should be tight with no evidence of leakage under gasket. If a disc appears to hang down, the spray valve can easily be replaced.

Spray Nozzles

  1. Spray nozzle should likewise be checked for foreign matter and see that all holes are clean and clear.  

  2. Check packing of controllers and valves, replace if necessary.

  3. Check man way gasket, replace if there is evidence of leaks or deterioration of the gasket.

  4. Check operation of all controllers; they should move freely. Make any necessary adjustments, paying attention to the overflow valve and controller as this is not used frequently and may have a tendency to corrode and freeze into position.

  5. Open and close all gate valves that have not been used since last inspection.

  6. Inspect thermometers, pressure gauges and any other instruments.

  7. Inspect all piping connection for corrosion. Inspect insulation,

  8. After unit is returned to service, oxygen testing should be performed to ascertain that the vent setting is correct.
