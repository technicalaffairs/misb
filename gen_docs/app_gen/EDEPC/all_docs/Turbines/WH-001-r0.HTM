Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, hearing protection ... etc.

  • Isolate the vessel from the feed water system.

  • Be sure that the heater has been completely drained.

  • Check the pressure gauge shows zero pressure.

  • Check thermometers and assure that temperature is less than 40 C°.

  • Provide adequate scaffolding, lighting, platforms or ladders.

  • Make certain that all safety tags are replaced when maintenance and inspection are complete.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set

  • Cleaning articles (fluid, rags.… etc)

  • Manual puller, 4x6 hard wood blocks

  • Micro meter

  • Main crane, Temporary jack

  • Arc-air gouging

Work to be Carried Out 

  1. Check any leakage of the flange.

  2. Check level of water in the shell side.

  3. Check Temperature in and Temperature out.

Tube Leak Test                                                       

  1. Follow the shut down procedure. After the tube side has been drained the channel cover and plate cover should be removed to expose the tube end.

  2. A hydraulic test should then be put on the shell side not exceeding the test pressure illustrated in the technical specification.

  3. Observe any leak which occurs and mark their location, if there it is necessary to replace the tube or plug both tube's ends.

NOTE: The number of plugged tubes shouldn't exceed 10% of the tubes number.
