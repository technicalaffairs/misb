Introduction

This document outlines the hot gas path inspection procedure P2, which must be performed for hot gas path inspection every 48000 operation hours as indicated by H48000 in the above title block.

The first draft initiated by eng. Ayman El-Mor & eng. Ali Mohamed Fahmy at Port Said Gas Tutbine, tested by eng. Abd El-Hamid Abu Zaid at 02 March 2003 , revised by EDEPCo. Inspection and Quality control eng. Abouda El-Bana,  , accepted and approved by Port-Said plant general director eng. El-Aaraby Ali Said.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance stuff should be carry the suitable safety category

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area is required

  • Wear your safety helmet, safety shoes …. etc

  • Unit must be out of service.

  • Fire fighting system should be isolated.

  • Fuel system should be isolated.

  • Oil system should be isolated.

  • All switches in motor control center panel should be isolated.

  • Generator must be fully isolated. 

Tools and Equipment

  • Two wrenches set size 3/8″ to 1 1/2″

  • Two sockets set drive 3/4″ size 3/8″ up to 1 1/2″

  • Two Allen wrench size 3/32″to 1″

  • Four impact socket wrench drive 1″ size 1 1/8″,  1 5/16″, 1 ½″, 1 11/16″, 1 7/8″ &2 ¼″.

  • Power hand drill, Misc. grinding machine.

  • Four chain hoists 1 1//2 tons.

  • Chain hoists 5 tons, Chain hoists 8 tons, and Chain hoists 10 tons.

  • Eye bolts & shackles.

  • 0.0015 to 0.040 inch feelers.

  • 0 – 1 inch micrometer.

  • 1 – 10 inch outside micrometer.

  • 2 – 12 inch inside micrometer.

Work to be Carried Out 

DISASSEMBLY

Turbine alignment check

  1. Remove the upper half of the load coupling guard at the load gear and at bearing NO. 2.

  2. Remove the generator coupling covers to allow the turbine rotation.

  3. Remove the coupling guard from the accessory coupling.

  4. Take run outs reading at the forward and aft. Ends of the load coupling and accessory gear shafts.

  5. Record the run outs reading on appropriate sheets.

  6. Remove the load coupling and accessory coupling.

  7. Mount the alignment fixture and take alignment reading on the turbine to load gear and accessory gear to the turbine and record the results in the attached sheet

Combustion Component and Piping Disassembly

  1. Combustion chamber accessories and pipes removal

  2. Remove the atomizing airlines from the fuel nozzle.

  3. Remove the fuel lines from the fuel nozzle.

  4. Disconnect the spark plug leads.

  5. Disconnect the flame detector.

  6. Unlock and remove all fuel nozzles take in consideration to mark the nozzles with its proper Chamber number.

Combustion Liners Removal

  1. Mark each cover with the relative chamber number.

  2. Unbolt and remove the combustion covers.

  3. Remove the combustion liner no 1 as described below: 

  4. Remove the crossfire tube clamps from chamber No 1.

  5. Remove the crossfire tube clamps from adjacent chamber N0 2 and No 1.

  6. Push the crossfire tube into adjacent chambers No 2 & No 10.

  7. Remove the liner No 1.

  8. Remove the crossfire tube with the adjacent chamber NO. 2  & NO. 10.

  9. Repeat steps (a) to (e) to remove other liners.

Combustion Component Inspection

  1. Inspect the crossfire tubes & retainers for (wear, cracks& overheating) and record the results in the attached sheet

  2. Inspect and maintain the fuel nozzle according to the appropriate procedure and record the results in the attached sheet

  3. Inspect the cap and liner if it should be reused, repaired or scraped according to the criteria and record the results in the attached sheet

  4. Inspect the transition pieces with strong beam of light and records should be made of any visible cracks, deposits or erosion and record the results   in the attached record sheet.

Turbine Compartment Accessories Removal

  1. Remove the turbine compartment roof with the diesel engine exhaust manifold & CO2 fire fighting piping connected to this compartment

  2. Remove the turbine compartment stand beams adjacent to the turbine shell

  3. Prepare four lifting lugs on the chimney wall above the center lines of the first stage nozzle,    the second stage nozzle, right and left side of the shell

  4. Install three support screw jacks below:
         =     Discharge compressor forward to after casing flange
         = Discharge casing to turbine shell flange
         = Turbine shell to exhaust     hood

  5. Remove atomizing air & gas manifold and fuel oil lines.

  6. Remove cooling air pipes as necessary for removal of the shell

  7. Remove the two second-stage nozzle axial holding bolts located at 45º & borescope plugs from the upper shell half

  8. Remove the wheel space thermocouples & junction boxes adjacent to turbine shell.

The Upper Shell Half Disassembly

  1. Remove the vertical bolting between the turbine shell and compressor discharge shell

  2. Remove the vertical Allen head bolts between the turbine shell and the exhaust hood

  3. Remove the turbine shell horizontal bolting

  4. Jack the turbine shell at the jack bolts holes provided to lift the shell case (it is preferable to use four guides during the shell lifting).

  5. Lift the shell casing using mobile crane, lifting wires and hoists as described in the weight and center of gravity drawing

  6. Remove the eccentric pins for positioning the 1st & 2nd nozzles upper halves after removing the upper shell case

Turbine Internals Disassembly

  1. Check and record the clearances of the 1st & 2nd nozzle holding clamps (attachment # 3).

  2. Remove the upper portion of the 2nd stage nozzle holding clamps and the horizontal joint bolting and lift the upper half of the 2nd stage.

  3. Remove the Allen-head horizontal joint bolts from the inner diaphragm upper half and lift it

  4. Remove the combustion outer casing except the combustors No 1, 10, 9

  5. Remove the transition pieces by removing the bolts with the first stage nozzle and side L seal 

  6. Push these transition pieces clear of nozzle into the outer combustion chambers.

  7. Remove the first stage nozzle clamp and the horizontal joint bolting

  8. Lift off the upper half of the first stage nozzle careful attention should be paid to the L-shaped seals between the segments

  9. Remove the upper half of supporting ring.

  10. Take a complete set of turbine clearance and record the results in the attached sheet

  11. Remove the first and second stage nozzle lower centering pins & second stage axial holding bolts located at 45º & bore scope plugs from the lower shell half.

  12. Untight the seal ring bolts from the first stage nozzle inner support ring and the two axial nozzle clamps.

  13. Remove the first stage nozzle support lugs.

  14. Roll out the first stage nozzle by hooking the tow nozzle sides to two-hoist chain hooked on the lifting lug above the first stage nozzle Transvaal axel with the nozzle swinging free.

  15. Raise one hoist cable and lowering the other side until the nozzle brought out above the turbine shaft, and then lift the stage up.

  16. Remove the second stage nozzle diaphragm lower half packing by bushing it out of its groove.

  17. Tight a rope with wooden blocks of dimension 7*7*12 cm with distance between block of 20 cm around the turbine shaft at the labyrinth packing groove.

  18. Remove the lower half second stage nozzle supporting lugs then remove it by rolling out using tow chain hoist hooked on the lifting lugs above the stage axel the lower half of the nozzle and diaphragm must be lifted as one piece.

Turbine Internal Inspection

  1. Inspect the transition pieces with strong beam of light and records should be made of any visible cracks, deposits or erosion and record the results in the attached record sheet

  2. Inspect the first and second stage nozzle for cracks, distortion, blocked cooling holes, loss of metal and corrosion. Repair is mandatory for nozzles exceed the following limits:

Cracks:

Sidewall:

  1. Five cracks per nozzle segment 0.5 inch deep and spaced less than 1.0 inch.

  2. Vane trailing edge:

  • any number of cracks parallel to sidewall and less than 1.0 inch provided that distance between any adjacent cracks is greater than the longer of the two cracks.

  • Two converging cracks less than 1.0 inch with a part distance less than the total sum of the two cracks.

  • Two diverging cracks less than 1.0 inch and the length of the crack is less than the distance between the between the end of crack and sidewall.

  1. Vane leading edge: two cracks less than .375 inch and a part distance less than 1.0 inch.

Corrosion / Erosion:

  • bowing of the trailing edge in excess of 0.035 inch with no more than one incident per segment.

  • No other partition shall be bowed.

  1. Record the inspection results and record the results in the attached record sheet

Buckets Inspection

  1. Inspect the first & second stages buckets for cracks, torn metal, dents & erosion of loading and trailing edges.

  2. Compare the inspection results with the manufacture limits and change if necessary.

First Stage Bucket Removal

  1. Cut out the heads of lock twist pins by sharp chisel

  2. Bush the bucket in the direction of removed head pay attention to the axial & radial seal pins.

  3. Clean out the bucket groove and remove any proud metal caused by cutting chisel or securing  peen marks.

Exhaust Hood Disassembly

  1. Remove the flex plats from the aft end of the exhaust plenum.

  2. Remove the flex plats from the upper forward half of the plenum.

  3. Remove the feed and drain line to bearing NO.2.

  4. Remove the exhaust turning vanes (diffuser) from the exhaust plenum slide the back against the load gear.

  5. Enter the exhaust plenum and remove the horizontal joint bolting insulation shields then complete the removal of horizontal joint bolts of the inner and outer flanges of the exhaust hood

  6. Break the exhaust hood halves by using jack screws (holes are provided in the outer flange for this purpose) lift the exhaust hood vertically (using 2 ton trolley hoist) until it clear the air baffle which is trapped between the seal of bearing NO2 and the hood.

  7. Lift the hood into the stack and then bush it against the load gear direction.

 Second stage Bucket Removal

  1. Cut out the heads of lock twist pins of the second stage bucket by sharp chisel.

  2. Push the buckets toward the removed pins head gradually as group pay attention to bucket shield slots.

  3. Clean out the bucket groove and remove any proud metal caused by cutting chisel or securing peen marks.

Bearing No. 2 Disassembly

  1. Remove the upper half of the bearing housing.

  2. Record the clearance of journal bearing & oil seal with rotor.

  3. Remove the upper half of journal bearing liner and take reading of the lower half clearance to the rotor with feeler.

  4. Lift the turbine rotor and roll lower half.

Bearing No.2 Inspection

  1. Inspect the bearing housing and casing joints for leaks, warping and gouge.

  2. Inspect oil seal packing teeth for oil, dirt, wear and broken seals.

  3. Check air groove for cogging.

  4. Check for indication of shaft/ seal rubbing.

  5. Inspect bearing liner thoroughly for bad scratch, loose Babbitt and high spots.

  6. Check the inside diameter of bearing liner at centers and at ½ inch from either side at the horizontal joint.

  7. Record the bearing liner diameters reading and to be compared against the data shown on drawings in   attached   sheet

  8. Change the bearing if the inspection show scratches, high spots, excessive wear out of tolerances.

Bearing No. 2 Re-assembly

  1. Clean the bearing liners using kerosene and clean rags. Don’t use rags, which may leave lint deposits on the bearing surface. Besides avoid contacting of hard objects to the bearing surfaces.

  2. Apply a thin film of turbine oil to the lower half of bearing liner & role it out to its place while the turbine rotor is still lifted.

  3. Release the hoist lifting the rotor and take reading of shaft / liner clearances at four points (front / rear / right / left).

  4. Compare the clearances with the drawings recommended tolerance to avoid tilting or twisting of bearing liners, to achieve the recommended clearances flow these steps:

  5. Put 2 mm sheet of asbestos on the upper half of the rotor.

  6. Lift the rotor with 8-ton hoist just to release the liner lower half.

  7. Put the bearing liners upper half to its position and tight both halves with liners´ bolts.

  8. Release the rotor-lifting hoist &remove the liner upper half.

  9. Check the clearances and record the results in the attached sheet

  10. Measure the total clearance of bearing liner putting 1 mm tin wire on the shaft then re-assemble the upper half of bearing liner and measure the thickness of the tin wire at bearing forward & aft direction. 

  11. Re-assemble the liner’s upper half and bolt the liner.

  12. Re-assemble the seal and record the clearances in   attached sheet

  13. Re-assemble the upper half of journal bearing housing and bolt it.

  14. Check the GIB keys of bearing NO 2 housing.

Bearing No. 1 Disassembly

  1. Remove the L-shaped air intake duct.

  2. Remove the upper half skin of the inlet plenum.

  3. Remove the upper half of compressor forward casing. Using two guide bolts is recommended to avoid hitting of the compressor blades.

  4. Remove the upper half of the inlet casing three Allen-head bolts are located in the passage on each side of this casing must be removed.

  5. Remove the upper half of bearing housing.

  6. Measure bearing and seal clearances and record the results in the attached sheet

Bearing No. 1 Inspection

  1. Check the loaded thrust bearing “ pads thickness “.

  2. Apply axial force in the aft direction to measure forward bearing clearances.

  3. Check the unloaded thrust bearing “ land taper “ and compare the taper measures with the drawing limits if the measured taper falls outside the tolerances the bearing must be replaced.

  4. Inspect Babbitt surface of journal & thrust bearing for cracks, chips, scratches, wiping and high spots and record the results in the attached sheet.

  5. No cracks are allowable.

  6. Scratches that less than 0.001 to 0.005 inch deep and less than one inch long can be repaired by scraping down any raised metal and lightly blending out the babbit surface with “0000” steel wool.

  7. Excessive temperature should be not exceeding 5 percent of the lower liner half area.

  8. Check the inside minor diameter of the journal bearing liner at vertical center and the major diameter ½ inch from either side of the horizontal joint and compare these reading with the allowable limits.

  9. Inspect oil seals and packing teeth for oil, dirt, uneven wear, and broken seals   take in consideration that bearing NO1 is most critical, since oil leakage could cause compressor fouling & possible loss of output.

Bearing No. 1 Re-assembly

  1. Clean the bearing liners using kerosene and clean rags.

  2. Rollout the lower half of the liner and adjust the bearing to shaft clearance (right, left, front, rear) to avoid liner tilting or twisting.

  3. Measure the total vertical clearance of bearing liner using 1mm tin wire.

  4. Assemble the upper liner half and bolt the two halves.

  5. Assemble the seal’s lower halves and record their clearances.

  6. Assemble the loaded and unloaded thrust bearing and record their clearances. 

  7. Clean the bearing housing thoroughly and assembly the bearing housing covers and bolts it.

Compressor Casing Disassembly

  1. Remove the upper half of the compressor discharge casing “hence the inner barrel upper half must be freed from the discharge casing - remove the three Allen bolts located slightly forward from the junction of the bulked head struts”.

  2. Use 4 guide bolts when lifting compressor discharge casing – when removing the horizontal joint bound bolts they should be driven upwards a step in the lower casing prevent them from being driven down.

  3. Remove the upper half of the compressor casing.

  4. Inspect the compressor rotor and stator blades for deposit, erosion or corrosion pitting.

  5. Clean the inlet guide vanes thoroughly and perform a liquid penetrate test especially to the braze area between the blade itself and the inner shroud ring and to the blade root area at the dovetail.  

  6. Clean the compressor blading by using organic chemical against.

  7. Roll out the bottom rows of compressor fixed blades with the rotor in place. In those stages with individual blades there may be spacing shims between some blades. These should be carefully marked and replaced in their original position at re-assembly.

Compressor Inspection

  1. Inspect the compressor rotor and stator blades for deposit, erosion or corrosion    pitting.

  2. Inspect the external side of compressor casing to be sure there are no cracks.

  3. Check for cracks in the blade root and dovetail by using liquid penetrate test.

 

Compressor Casing Re-assembly

  1. Before re-assembly the lower half casing should be thoroughly clean and free of all cleaning   residue.       

  2. Reassemble the loose blades of the compressor stator lower stages.

  3. Pay attention to the position of the blade in relation to the casing.

  4. Reassemble the upper half of inlet casing. Don’t overlook the six Allen-head bolts located in the inner barrel directly in the air stream.

  5. Record all compressor clearances in attachment record sheet

  6. Cheek visually the compressor to verify That no foreign object or debris is teft in the turbine remember the three axial angular bolts holding the inner barrel to the compressor discharge casing Re Assemble the discharge casing of compressor

  7. Assemble the compressor after casing

  8. Assemble the compressor forward casing

  9. Re assembles the inlet plenum upper half skin.

  10. Assemble the L –shaped air intake duct.

Inspection of First & Second Stage Shrouds

  1. Check the turbine shrouds for clearance with buckets forward; middle, and aft end of blade tip record the amount and location of all rubbing on field clearance report.

  2. Check for build up on the shrouds (remove by grinding).

  3. Check the first stage turbine wheel to the stator position by using one marked first stage turbine bucket. Take four-point feeler gage check and replace the upper half of turbine shell.

  4. Change the shroud segments if the radial clearance between bucket tip and the shroud exceed the upper limits.

  5. Arrange the shroud segments accordance to the knocked numbers in the shroud metal.

  6. Load shrouds in the flow direction and mark the dowel pin hole by drilling.

  7. Remove the shroud segments and drill the dowel pinholes on power full machine.

  8. Re-assemble the shroud segments and their dowel pin.

  9. Check the bucket tip and the shroud radial clearance and record the results in the attached sheet

Second Stage Bucket Re-assembly

  1. Arrange the bucket in accordance to the balance sheet.

  2. Place the lock twist pins in their slots and assemble each bucket with its seal pins. (Anti size   compound is recommended to use for pines)

  3. Push the first bucket into the rotor grove (2cm) and then install the next bucket, push the tow buckets into their grove (0.5 cm) and assemble the third bucket, push the buckets gradually paying attention to the seal pins.

  4. Rotate the lock twist such that it secure the bucket from moving axial, then punch the lock twist with the two diametrically opposite peen marks with around nosed punch.

First Stage Bucket Re-assembly

  1. Arrange the bucket in accordance to the balance sheet.

  2. Place the lock twist pins in their slots. (Anti seize compound is recommended to use for pines)

  3. Assemble buckets with their radial & axial seal pins.

  4. Rotate the lock twist such that it secures the bucket from axial movement.

  5. Punch the lock twist heads with around nosed punch to lock it in the   rotor slots with two diametrically opposite peen marks.

Exhaust Hood Re-assembly

  1. Lower the upper half of the exhaust hood and tight the horizontal joint bolts of inner and outer flanges be sure that new gaskets are used on the horizontal joint of the inner gas path cone & the air baffle is properly located in the groove of the seal of bearing NO 2 before lowering the hood.

  2. Assemble the horizontal joint bolting insulation shield.

  3. Assemble the exhaust turning vanes (diffuser) ensure the position of gas seal plates between the diffuser & stack.

First & Second Nozzle Re-assembly

  1. Clean thoroughly the turbine shell and discharge casing

  2. Coat the seal-second stage nozzle spring seal with anti seize

  3. Roll in the second-stage nozzle when in position and supported on the lifting rig insert and bolt the support shims

  4. Lower the second stage nozzle lower half onto the supporting lugs and inserts the bottom eccentric pin.

  5. Assemble the two lower axial holding pins at 45°.

  6. Assemble the lower half inter stage packing (labyrinth) and spring adjusts the clearances as recommended by the manufacture drawing.

  7. Roll in the lower half of the first stage nozzle and while the nozzle is still supported on the lifting hoist insert the horizontal supporting lugs and bolt tightly and then lower the nozzle.

  8. Assemble the first stage nozzle bottom eccentric pin.

  9. Tighten the first nozzle bottom holding clamp and check their clearance.

  10. Take a complete set of turbine clearance and record the results in the attached sheet

    ** Note that the clearances on the packing and the second stage nozzle must be taken while the nozzle must be waged against the second stage shrouds (this is its running position).

  11. Assemble the upper half of the supporting ring.

  12. Assemble the upper half of the first stage nozzle.

  13. Assemble the upper half of second stage nozzle diaphragm. 

  14. Assemble the upper half of second stage nozzle.

  15. Assemble the clamps to the first stage nozzle horizontal joint and check the clearance of the lug U clamp record this clearance on the attached sheet

  16. Assemble the second nozzle clamps and record the results in the attached sheet.

  17. Assemble the two packing strips into the outer seal groove of the first stage nozzle; verify that the lap of the strips is not in line to minimize the possibility of leakage.

  18. Assemble the transition pieces. Starting from the chamber No 5 and work upper wards from that point be sure that the transition pieces are fully engage in the first stage nozzle before tightening the center holding bolts.

  19. Assemble the side seals (L seals) and locking bolt washer combination between each transition pieces.

Shell Casing Re-assembly

  1. Check thoroughly turbine shell cleaning.

  2. Coat the mating surfaces of the turbine shell hood and discharge casing with a light film of anti-seize.

  3. Check that all is secure within the shell area before assemble, rotate the turbine rotor with ratchet mechanism check if there were any abnormal sound.

  4. Assemble the shell’s upper half using 4 guide rods and the two jack bolts mechanism pay attention to the turbine shell level during lowering with jack bolts.

  5. Tighten the shell bolts according to the bolting sequence.

  6. Assemble the first and second stage nozzle upper eccentric pins; second stage axial holding bolts at 45º and the bore scope plugs.

  7. Assemble the outer combustion casing using new gasket on the casings and fix them in the groove with grease in two spots.

  8. Remove all support jacks from the unit.

  9. Remove all support jacking located under the turbine.

Combustion Component Re-assembly

 A - Cleaning

  1. Clean all mating flange faces of any gasket material. 

  2. Clean all hardware bolts & nuts with wire-brush to remove traces of anti-seize or rust.  

  3. Lock maintained fuel nozzle with steel lock wire.

B - Combustion Liner

  1. Assemble liner No 5 to the chamber. Slide the crossfire tubes from chamber No 6 & No 4 into liner No 5

  2. Assemble liner No 4. 

  3. Slide crossfire tube from liner No 5 into position between liners No 4 & No 5 and lock it with retainers.         

  4. Follow the a/m procedure for the remaining chambers.     

  5. Energize the ignition transformers and observes that the spark plugs are properly.

  6. Check the flame detector works properly by expose the scanner to suitable light.

  7. Assemble the spark plugs and the flam detector. 

Fuel Nozzles & Aaccessoires Pipe

  1. Assemble the combustion covers take in consideration that the two larger bolts in the flanges are to be mated with the holes in the cover and casing.  

  2. Reassemble the fuel nozzles but do not tighten bolts firmly.

  3. Reassemble the fuel piping and when mating with the nozzle flanges. Tight the nozzle bolts firmly. Check the gas lines flange for leakage with soapy solution.

 

 

Re-assemble the Turbine Compartment Roof and CO2 Fire Fighting System Pipes

Turbine / load gear alignment check

  1. Mount the alignment fixture on the load gear flange.

  2. Rotate the load gear shaft from the generator coupling. Record the face and rim reading in the sheet (attachment # 6) The alignment reading should be within following Tolerance. 

  1. Take in consideration the face reading referenced to 12 flange diameter and correction should be made if the face reading has been taken at less diameter.

  2. Repeat the alignment reading at moderate temperature (preferable after midnight)

  3. Re-assemble the load coupling and torque the bolts in sequence as described in sheet (attachment # 7).

  4. Re-assemble the load coupling guard and its drainpipe

  5. Re-assemble the generator coupling cover.

Turbine / accessory gear alignment check

  1. Mount the alignment fixture on the accessory gear flange.

  2. Rotate the accessory gear shaft and record the face and rim reading in sheet (attachment # 6), which should be within the following tolerances:

  1. Take in consideration the face reading referenced to 12 flange diameter and correction should be made if the face reading has been taken at less diameter.

  2. Re-assemble the accessory coupling and torque the bolts in sequence as described in sheet (attachment # 7).

  3. Re-assemble the coupling guard.

  4. Check generator the unit component.

  5. Perform the cranking checks on the turbine.

Preparation the unit for start after inspection

  1. Revise & check the clearness & cleanness of working site.

  2. Check the availability of DC & AC power supplies.

  3. Check batteries voltage should not be less than 105 V.

  4. Check the lube oil level and the fuel oil tank of starting diesel engine.

  5. Check the level of main tank lube oil in normal level.

  6. Operate the emergency lube oil pump check direction of rotation and the discharge Pressure.

  7. Check lube oil flow through sight glass at bearing NO. (1, 2, 3).

  8. Check the level of cooling water tanks.

  9. Check valves position of fuel supply system in operation position.

  10. Check the position of cooling air dampers.

  11. Check readiness of unit (No trip signal is initiated) and the ready light signal is on.

Operation checks

  1. Operate the unit at different speeds (200 r.p.m, crank speed, fire position, full speed no load)     

  2. check the unit for:
       a.  Leakage of fuel gas line (test with soapy solution).
       b.  Leakage of cooling and seal air pipes.
       c. Abnormal noise or excessive vibration.

  1. Operate the unit at (different loads 0%, 25%, 50%, 100%). Take complete operation reading   set record at the (attachment # 8).

Criteria to determine if the combustion chamber caps liners should be reused, repaired, or scrapped.

 

Note:

  1. Combustion chamber liners are typical, therefore, they will not be discussed by specific drawing numbers

  2. Tungsten inert gas is the preferred welding process. The coated rod process and stick welding is acceptable when performed by competent personnel.

  3. Deterioration less than maximum allowable limits indicates that re-use in the "as is" condition is acceptable, based on the premise that consideration has been given to previously recorded data pertaining to the operating hours, firing conditions, and the subsequent operations expected from the liners

Checking and sizing of louver openings is not generally recommended except for those louvers which have been repaired.

Repaired louver openings should be sized to ± .005" of drawings or comparison dimension, with care taken to maintain a .350" to .375" minimum width of louve opening
