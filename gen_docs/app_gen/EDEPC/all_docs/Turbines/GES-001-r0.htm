Introduction

This document outlines the major maintenance procedures P1, which must be performed every five years as indicated by Y5 in above title block.

The gland exhauster system prevents sealing steam from escaping past the outer shaft packing rings to atmosphere at each end of the turbine rotor by maintaining a slight vacuum in the outer leak off chamber between the end packing rings, the system continuously removes a mixture of this sealing steam and air ( which tends to enter the turbine along the rotor shaft ) and discharges the resultant condensate to a suitable drain.

As shown in figure1 the basic system consists of a condensate collecting tank, motor driven air blower, and water spray chamber connected to the leak off piping in the packing glands.

Safety Precaution

  • A work permit must be issued and qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required 

  • Wear the safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, shoes, gloves … etc.

  • Isolate the circuit breaker of the air blower.

  • Disconnect electric terminal of the air lower.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set.

  • Cleaning fluid

  • Feeler gauge & dial indicator

  • Rages

Work to be carried out

Air Blower in Service

  1. Measure vibrations level for bearing and compare it with the setting value, then analyze it and determine what to do (if it is needed).

Air Blower out Service

  1. Check alignment and compare it with the setting value.

  2. Check bolts fixation, then re-tightens (if it is needed).

Disassembly

  1. Move the unit by lifting under the steel base.

  2. Preferably machine should be set on a level floor in a clean dry place.

  3. Remove shipping skids and set unit on base provided four under the corners

  4. Two under the center of the I beam base members.

  5. Remove shipping cover and connect piping.

  6. Use extreme caution to prevent foreign material entering blower.

  7. Use flexible connection between unit and piping system.

  8. A dial indicator is the best device for checking alignment for most couplings a straight edge may be used on top and side of coupling.

  9. Raise or lower motor by shim adjustment or shaft side ways to remove both parallel and angular misalignment (not exceed 1/64 inch).

  10. Remove up – end blower with shaft extension up.

  11. Remove # 9 bearing cap.

  12. Remove # 20 and # 21 locknut and lockwasher.

  13. Remove # 11 bearing housing, # bearing and # 14 grease slinger together.

  14. Remove # 26 or # 27 labyrinth.

  15. Remove # 5 & # 6 tie rods.

  16. Remove # 16 inlet head.

  17. Remove # 18 & # 19 locknut and lockwasher.

  18. Remove # 1 impeller.

  19. Remove # 3 intermediate section.

  20. Turn shaft back into horizontal position.

  21. Remove cap screws holding # 12 bearing housing to # 17 outlet head and # 26 or # 27 labyrinth to # 17 outlet head and slip # 17 head off shaft.

  22. Remove free bearing (drive end) - Pull coupling half do not knock or pry it off shaft.

  23. Remove bearing cap bearing locknut and bearing lockwasher

  24. Remove cap screw holding bearing housing to inlet head.

  25. Pull bearing housing, bearing, and grease slinger off shaft attach puller to housing.

  26. Remove bearing from housing. If necessary, use extreme care and knock bearing out from back of housing.

  27. Remove thrust bearing (opposite drive end) - removing parts in the following order:
        * Bearing cap # 10.
        * Bearing locknut # 22 and bearing lockwasher # 23.
        * Bearing housing # 12, bearing # 8 , and grease slinger # 15.

  28. Adjusting leakage flow ( spray chamber ) refer to figure (3,4):

  29. Connect a manometer to one of the pressure taps in the leak off piping (refer to the diagram of the gland exhauster system included in the descriptive portion of this manual)

  30. Loosen gland nut (5) on the same side of the chamber as the manometer connection and close damper vane (3) until a slight steam flow from the rotor shaft is noted record the vacuum reading at this setting.

  31. Open damper vane (3) until an additional (6 inch) of water vacuum is obtained then tighten gland nut (5).

Inspection

  1. Check bearing housing of Air Blower :for wear, cracks and damage.

  2. Check thrust bearing of Air Blower for damage.

  3. Inspect the collecting tank for leakage or damage.

 

 

Assembly (refer to fig 2)

  1. Apply good cleaning for all air blower parts.

  2. Bolt bearing housing (11) firmly to inlet head (16) and tighten the cap screws evenly.

  3. Firmly against the shaft shoulder

  4. Slide the new bearing into place in housing (7) if necessary, tap it carefully into place but take care not to hit the balls or retainer.

  5. Install lockwasher (21) and locknut (20) and tighten firmly .

  6. Coat the bearing balls and retainer sparingly with clean grease.

  7. Fill bearing cap (9) no more than one – half full of grease and assemble the cap to the bearing housing make sure the shaft turns freely by hand.

  8. Install the coupling half on the blower shaft.

  9. Tap the coupling half into place carefully with a lead hammer and rotate the shaft while tapping to distribute the load on the thrust bearing.

  10. Install the drive motor

  11. Adjust alignment of the coupling halves carefully using a dial indicator or straight edge along the coupling haves, Raise or lower the drive motor as necessary by shim adjustment, or shift the motor sideways to correct for parallel or angular misalignment.

  12. Reinstall upper casing half.

  13. Rebolt upper and lower casing halves.

  14. Reinstall front and rear bearings.

  15. Reassemble motor – pump coupling.

  16. Recheck alignment between pump & motor shafts.

  17. Reinstall coupling cover. 

Starting the Air Blower

  1. Open the inlet and outlet valves of the air blower .

  2. Connect electric terminal of the air blower motor.

  3. Put the circuit breaker of the air blower motor in service.

  4. Check the direction of the pump.

      

 

Attendance and Checking during Operation

  1. During operation of the air blower, the following should be checked: 

  2. The bearing temperatures

  3. Coupling alignment after the blower has been running for several hours.

  4. Excessive vibration

  5. Adjust leakage flow in the piping on opposite side of the chamber.
