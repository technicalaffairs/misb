Introduction

This document outlines the equipments inspection I1, which must be performed every six months as indicated by M6 in above title block.

A shaft sealing system is required to seal the turbine casing so that vacuum may be established at startup. After startup, sealing must be maintained so that air will not leak into sub-atmospheric section of the machine, and excess steam in the high-pressure section of the machine does not blow out into the turbine room, or into bearing housing to contaminate lubricating oil. At startup, Sealing is achieved by introducing steam between intermediate packing rings, and venting between outer packing rings to condenser at sub-atmospheric pressure.

Safety Precaution

  • A work permit must be issued and qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required 

  • Wear the safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, shoes, gloves … etc.

  • Shutdown the unit.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set.

Work to be carried out

The Steam Regulator in Service

  1. Check the pressure of steam seal header (not exceed 4 psig).

 

The Steam Regulator out of Service

  1. Open drain cocks on air supply filter-regulators to remove any condensate.

  2. Verify that air supply filter-regulators are holding the supply pressure to the regulator instruments at 35 psig.

  3. Verify that the controller sensing line above the water seal is full of water by removing header pressure gauge. Also verify that needle valve in sensing line is not fully closed.

  4. With the air supply to the controller on push the cleanout wire on the controller relay to clean the relay orifice.

Attendance Checking during Operation

  1. Check steam seal header pressure.

  2. Check for any leakage from pipes, flanges ... etc.
