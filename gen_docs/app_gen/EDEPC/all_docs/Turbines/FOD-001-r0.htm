Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection I1, which performed on an annually bases indicated by Y1 or at fuel oil leaks as indicated in the title block

The distributor pumping distributes the light fuel oil with its 10 pump elements to the 10 corresponding turbine fuel nozzles and proportional to the speed at which the unit operates

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required

  • Wear your safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety, shoes, eye glass ... etc

  • Ensure that the equipment shutdown.

  • Light fuel oil not under pressure and no flow rate..

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set as (suitable wrenches set, screw driver, feeler gage adjustment, lift hook, plastic plugs)

  • Rags, cleaning fluid.

Work to be Carried Out

Light Fuel Oil Leaks

  1. The equipment must be shutdown and ensure that the bypass valve is opened and not under pressure.

  2. Tighten fittings without using Teflon tape as manufacturer instructions.

  3. Take care in tightening in order to prevent cracking and or stripping threads.

  4. No excess weight (loads) can be placed on the equipment during retightening.

The Equipment does not Pumping

  1. Remove socket head plug in face plate and turn shaft with screw driver.

  2. If the shaft is broken the equipment is reinstalled by a new one.

  3. Change the spare part element by a new one if damaged.

  4. Check (bypass valve, main pump, clutch).

Magnetic Pickup Clearance

  1. Remove pipe plugs located below ends of each magnetic pick up, clearance between each sensor and gear indicator must be within the limits specified in the manufacturer instructions from 0.005 to 0.010 inch (max 0.015 inch)

  2. Torque seal do not exceed 15 ft Ibs
