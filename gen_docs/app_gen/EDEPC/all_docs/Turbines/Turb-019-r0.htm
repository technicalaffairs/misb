Introduction

This document outlines the major maintenance procedures P1, to be performed every five years as indicated by Y5 in the title block

The high pressure steam turbine is a part of the steam turbine matrix which is used in generating electricity. It uses the steam that comes from HRSG as a working medium in its operation. The HP turbine rotor consists of 13 stages.

Safety Precaution

  • A work permit must be issued and qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required 

  • Wear safety helmets, shoes, ear plug and gloves pairs … etc.

  • Be sure that the turbine is out of service and turning gear is off.

  • Be sure that the all electrical & instrumentation cables are completely isolated.

  • Be sure that the fire fighting system is isolated.

 

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set.

  • Set of wires.

  • Cleaning articles.

  • Fillers, Vibrometeres, dial gauges and slip gauges.

  • Special tools for clamping the rotor.

  • Hydraulic pump for unbolting the couplings.

Work to be Carried out

  1. Before Disassembly, you must record all measurements of the unit during normal operation.

  2. Stop and isolate oil system, cooling system, fire fighting system, electric connections and turning gear.

Disassembly

  1. Dismantle the connecting pipes between HP upper casing and stop / control valve block.

  2. Dismantle the connecting pipes between HP and LP upper casing.

  3. Break the cement insulation.

  4. Cut and remove  the iron net around the turbine.

  5. Remove the thermal wood insulation.

  6. Dismantle the upper cover of bearing pedestal.

  7. Check the run-out of the two couplings (tightened couplings).

  8. Uncouple the coupling between HP &LP rotors. Use a hydraulic pump to unbolt the coupling bolts.

  9. Check the run-out of the two couplings (loosen couplings).

  10. Check the alignment between the two couplings.

  11. Lock the lower part of the turbine by four raising bolts (four knees) and raise the body(0.05mm).

  12. Unbolt the bolts and nuts of the turbine casing by heating (unbolt 2 bolts and let 1). We use two types of heating plugs (Large 23V – 2250W – 40A) & (Small 23V – 7500W – 20A).

  13. Remove the screws & bolts fixing bearing No,2 bearing –block cap on the lower bearing –block.

  14. Remove the screws & bolts fixing bearing No,1 bearing –block cap on the lower bearing –block.

  15. Remove the screws & bolts fixing thrust bearing –block cap on the lower bearing –block.

  16. Disassemble the three bearings blocks caps fitted with the rotor  groundings.

  17. Measure the clearances of journal and thrust bearings and record them.

  18. Remove the screws & bolts fixing journal-bearing & thrust-bearing straps.

Dismantle Bearing no. 2 as follows

  1. Remove the journal bearing strap.

  2. Remove the two supports fitted with their sensors.

  3. Dismantle the thermocouple.

  4. Remove the upper half journal – bearing.

  5. Measure the clearances of journal bearing and bearing straps.

  6. Dismantle the oil supply connection from the rotor jacking.

Dismantle the Thrust Bearing

  1. Dismantle the thermocouples.

  2. Remove the thrust - bearing strap.

  3. Remove the screws and the bolts at the thrust – bearing box junction.

  4. Remove the upper thrust - bearing box.

  5. Disassemble the upper thrust – bearing stops and rings.

  6. Measure the clearances of the bearing straps.

Disassemble the Turning Gear

  1. Remove the screws fixing the turning gear on bearing block.

  2. Sling the turning gear.

  3. Remove the turning gear.

  4. Disassemble the turning gear motor to be checked.

  5. Disassemble the turning gear, inspect its components and replace the damaged.

  6. Disassemble bearing No,1 as mentioned before for bearing No,2.

  7. Unbolt the remain bolts and nuts of the turbine casing by heating as used before.

  8. Sling the turbine upper casing.

Remove the Turbine Upper Casing

  1. Make sure that the guide bolts are installed.

  2. Lift the upper casing by 1 mm, using extraction screws.

  3. Raise the upper casing slowly with, keeping it horizontally until it leaves the posts.

  4. Lay the casing on the provided area.

Remove the upper Diaphragms and Packing Carriers

  1. Suppress the screws and bolts locking at the joint and remove the bolts.

  2. Install the eyebolts in the holes tapped for this purpose.

  3. Sling and pull upwards to loose the horizontal joints with a screwdriver used as a wedge.

  4. Once the joint is released, lift the upper part without leaving the centerline.

  5. Measure the rotor axial & radial clearances and record them.

  6. Check the rotor runout by dial indicator (the limit is 0.07).

  7. Measure the clearances of the steam seal straps.

Raise the Rotor

  1. Sling the rotor at the given locations (the rotor is lifted by 2 mm).

  2. Make sure that the slings are correctly stretched.

  3. Lift the rotor at very low speed and right in the axis, so that it doesn't touch the casings, until the largest impeller is freed.

  4. Lay the rotor on the prepared cradle supports.

Remove the Lower diaphragms and Packing - Carriers

  1. Sling the 1/2 packing – carriers using two eyebolts screwed in holes specially tapped at the joint.

  2. Remove the keys securing – screws at the joint.

  3. Lift the half – packing. Remove the keys. After they are released from the turbine horizontal joint, remove the packing.

  4. Disassemble the pin if it is necessary, in that case, correctly mark the flat parts orientation.

  5. Disassemble the inlet and outlet half – packings by slinging them and rotating them in their own housings.

Dismantle the Lower part of Bearing no. 1

  1. Sling the lower part of the bearing by using two eyebolts in the distinct position. 

  2. Lift the lower part of the bearing by using rotating it in its own housing.

  3. Dismantle the lower part of bearing No, 2 and the thrust bearing as mentioned for bearing No, 1.

 

Inspection

Cleaning

  1. Degrease all turbine parts (turbine casing, turbine cover, bearing blocks,….) with non corrosive product and rags.

  2. Clean carefully all the HP turbine parts with adequate products and solvents.

  3. Clean all screws, pins and nuts by solar

  4. Clean all steam seal segments by solar.

Visual Examination

Examine visually the turbine components of wear, cracks, deformations, foreign bodies, etc….. .

Checking the Bearing Blocks

  1. Check the sliding planes condition by N.D.T for wear, cracks, ….. .

  2. Check the journal bearings surface condition by N.D.T for wear, cracks, ….. .

  3. Check the journal bearings mating  - faces by N.D.T for wear, cracks, ….. .

  4. Check the thrust collar surface condition by N.D.T for wear, cracks, ….. .

  5. Check the thrust bearings shoes mating  - face by N.D.T for wear, cracks, ….. .

  6. Check the separation of the journal and thrust bearings by N.D.T.

N.D.T Inspection

  1. Inspect the diaphragms by magnetic test.

  2. Inspect the steam seal segments by penetration.

  3. Inspect the rotor and its blades by magnetic test.

  4. Check the elongation of the turbine bolts.

Sandblasting

  1. Cover the coupling, journal bearings, seal stripes and thrust bearing to prevent them from damage.

  2. Sand blast the turbine shaft.

  3. Sand blast the upper and lower diaphragms.

  4. Sand blast the steam seal segments.

  5. Sand blast all the bolts, screws and nuts of the turbine (cover thread).

Assembly

Before the assembly, we must coat the mating faces, pins, keys and screws active faces with COPASLIP.

Assemble the Lower Diaphragms Packings

  1. Pull the packings into the diaphragm, each in its marked position after putting the springs in their places.

  2. Screw the packings in the diaphragm.

  3. Pull down using a crane the lower diaphragms in their places.

  4. Put the keys lock the diaphragms into the horizontal joint.

  5. Screws the bolts to lock the keys.

  6. Make the blue test between the turbine lower part and the bearings pedestals to actuate the surfaces of contact.

Assemble the Lower Part of the Bearing no. 2

  1. Pull down the lower part of the bearing in its grooved place.

  2. Tight the screws, fix the lower part with the pedestal.

  3. Assemble the lower part of bearing No.1 as mentioned for bearing No.2.

  4. Assemble the lower part of thrust bearing as mentioned for the bearings No.1&2.

  5. Put lead wires on the lower diaphragms. 

  6. Put the rotor very slowly till it rests in its position.

  7. Raise the rotor again and measure the clearances between it and turbine casing.

  8. Unlock the lower part of the turbine by unbolting the four raising bolts at both inlet & exhaust sides , and descend the body(0.05mm) which raised in disassembly.

  9. Put the rotor very slowly till it rests in its position again.

  10. Measure the all radial & axial clearances using a filler and record them.

  11. Sling the turbine cover.

  12. Paint the lower part of the turbine with special recommended paint to make the blue test that used to actuate the surfaces of contact of the two halves of the turbine.

  13. Put the turbine cover using its four guides on the lower part of the turbine to execute that test.

  14. Raise the turbine cover again and remark the result of the test.

  15. Assemble the upper diaphragms packings as done for the packings of the lower diaphragms.

  16. Put the upper diaphragms in their places and lock them by screws.

  17. Put the turbine cover again.

  18. Screw the bolts of the turbine cover (bolt 2 and let 1).

  19. Check and adjust the alignment between HP / LP shafts (loosen couplings).

  20. Measure and adjust the clearances of the journal and thrust bearings before assembly.

Assemble the upper Part of the Bearing no. 2 and its Casing

  1. Put the upper part of the bearing on the rotor and tight it.

  2. Assemble the upper part of bearing No, 1 and its casing as mentioned in bearing No, 2.

  3. Assemble the upper part of thrust bearing and its casing as mentioned in bearing No, 1 & 2.

Assemble the Turning Gear

  1. Sling the turning gear.

  2. Put the turning gear in its place.

  3. Tight the screws fixing the turning gear on bearing block.

  4. Assemble the turning gear motor and fix it.

  5. Install a new main oil pump, if necessary, and adjust it.

  6. Connect all steam junctions between HP / LP turbines and HP / stop and control valves and fix them.

  7. Bolt the coupling between HP/LP turbines. 

  8. Bolt the turbine cover bolts by heating. Use the same heating plugs used in disassembly.

  9. Insulate the turbine with glass wool at first.

  10. Cover the turbine with metallic net.

  11. Insulate the turbine with thermal cement.

Preparation of the unit for start up

  1. Be sure that all electrical & instrumentation cables are completely connected.

  2. Operate the fire fighting system.

  3. Operate the cooling system.

  4. Operate the oil system.

  5. Operate the turning gear and check any noise and abnormal sound.

 

Attendance and checking during start up and Operation

  1. Check the vibration on the bearings.

  2. Check the oil pressure.

  3. Check the bearing temperature.

  4. Check the vacuum in condenser.
