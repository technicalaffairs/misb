Introduction

This document outlines the major maintenance procedures P1, which must be performed every five years as indicated by Y5 in the title block.

The intermediate pressure turbine (1MAB) is a double flow 2. turbine with 17 reaction stage per flow incl. 3 twisted stages

The steam turbine converts the thermal energy of the steam generated by the boiler into mechanical on the shaft.

Technical Specification of HP Turbine:

IP turbine bearings

The IP rotor is supported in two bearings, a combined journal and thrust bearing directly adjacent to the coupling to the HP rotor at its front end and a journal bearing at its end.

Safety Precaution

  • A work permit must be issued and maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required 

  • Wear the safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, shoes, gloves … etc.

  • Shut down the unit.

  • Operating personnel must be notified before starting and maintenance, inspection or repair work.

  • Before starting work on the steam turbine ensure that steam turbine has come to a complete stand still and secured against start up.

  • Shat down systems (pneumatic, hydraulic, etc.) and depressurize relevant sections.

  • Operate turning gear till T = 100 ºc then Open systems carefully.

  • Steam turbine components may remain hot even after coast down and cause burns. Allow sufficient time for components to cool down.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set.

  • Cleaning fluid, Rag

  • Manual puller, Feeler gauge

  • Dial indicator, Vibrometer

Work to be Carried out

While Unit in Service

  1. Measure absolute and relative vibrations level for bearings by the measurement devices set on bearing and compare it with the setting value, then analyze it and determine what to do.

  2. Measure absolute and relative expansion value for shaft and casing by the measurement devices set on bearing and casing and compare it with the setting value.

  3. Check temperature difference between upper and lower casing halves by temperature sensors set on the upper and lower casing halves.

  4. Check bearing temperature by the thermocouple set on bearing and compare it with the setting value.

  5. Check bearing oil temperature by the thermocouple set on the entrance and exit of bearing oil and compare it with the setting value.

  6. Check the upper margin and the lower margin for casing.

  7. Check seal strips and flanges for leakage.

While Unit in ShutDown

  1. Disassemble bearings, check bearing seats in bearing housing.

  2. Inspect bearing guides for wear and bindings by X-rays.

  3. Check bearing surfaces, babbit bonding and bearing clearance which is listed down in the MRDS.

  4. Check seal rings and replace seal rings if it is required.

  5. Check condition and performance of shaft lift oil system, by checking the exit of lifting oil from the lower half of bearing, check for crack of the nozzle by ultrasonic waves or X-rays.

  6. Check condition and performance of temperature monitoring system by putting the thermocouple in oil or water and heating the fluid to the alarm and trip temperature and observe the thermocouple temperature from DCS

  7. Check bearing pedestal anchor preloads.

  8. Open handholes of HP casing, perform boroskop inspection for blades.

  9. Check bolts fixation, then re-tightens it (if it is needed).

  10. Discharge the oil from the main tank and clean it.

  11. Check the validity of oil and add or change it (if it is needed).
