Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should be carry the Suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required

    •

  • Wear safety helmets, shoes, earplug and gloves pairs.

  • Isolate the screen mechanically and electrically.

  • Isolate the circuit breaker of the pump motor.

  • Disconnect electric terminal of the pump motor.

  • The pump must be isolated mechanically by closing delivery and suction valves

Tools and Equipment

  • Maintenance tool set.

  • Grease (multi purpose grease).

  • Wires to raise up the screen cover.

  • Cleaning fluid, Rages

Work to be Carried Out

The Oil Coolers in Service

  1. Check the pressure of oil pumps.

  2. Check the difference between outlet and inlet temperature in the oil coolers (not exceed 4.3 oC).   

The Oil Coolers Out of Service

  1. The cold medium should it be necessary to stop the cold fluid first.

  2. Isolate the oil coolers by closing the inlet and discharge valves.

Disassembly       as shown in figure 1

If it is noticed that the outlet temperature of oil is increased to 6 oC more than usual. (we should interfere to check clogging of the tubes of the inlet water side) as the following :

  1. Insure that the other oil cooler is in service.

  2. Isolate the oil cooler from both sides oil and water and drain oil in an empty barrel.

  3. Dismantle the reversing bonnet of the cooler and take it out.

  4. Dismantle the bolts of flange connecting the cooler shell with its base.

  5. Dismantle the bolts of inlet and outlet oil flanges.

  6. Tight the shells from its eye bolt and lift it to an empty place.

  7. Clean tubes by using a soft rod or water lance one by one till you be insured that all tubes internally cleaned.

  8. Clean the surface of tubes to remove the greasy oil and other impurities by using acid cleaning.

Assembly

  1. Insure that the area around the tubes bundle is clean.

  2. Lift the cooler shell and put it in its place after putting the gasket between the shell flange and its base flange.

  3. Tighten the shell flange bolts firmly according to their tighten torque with a torque wrench.

  4. For making hydro test to check for leak we should performed the following steps:

  5. Put a blind flange on the outlet or inlet flange which ever is the higher.

  6. Connect the discharge pipe of a hand pump to the other flange of cooler.

  7. Pressurized the cooler with clean water and notice any leaks from the upper side of tubes.

  8. Plug the cracked tubes from the upper side first by using useful wooden plug.

  9. Dismantle the discharge pipe flange of the hand pump and dismantle the flange connecting the shell with the base and other side of the cracked tubes firmly.

  10. Put the cooler shell again in its place and tighten its connecting flange with base.

  11. Tighten the bolts of the inlet and outlet oil flanges.

  12. Tighten the reversing bonnet of the cooler shell.

  13. Put the cooler in operation and notice the outlet temperature of oil and record it hourly.

Double "O" Ring Seal Protection

Permits tube bundle to expand and contract without harmful strain or intermixing of shell and tube fluids.

"O" Rings retain compression, without adjustment,
and are unaffected by vibration and temperature changes.
No danger of over-tightening. Telltale holes reveals any leakage.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

To Replace "O" ring Packing

  1. Remove bonnet or channel at floating tube shell end.

  2. Remove existing "O" Rings and  "O" Ring retainer

  3. Clean sealing surface including OD Ring of floating tube sheet.

  4. Assemble inner (shell side)  "O" Ring

  5. Assemble retainer ring and tighten to shell flange using the bolting that is threaded in the ring.

  6. Assemble outer (tube side)  "O" Ring.

  7. Replace floating head bonnet or channel.
