Introduction

This document outlines the major maintenance procedures P1, to be performed every five years as indicated by Y5 in the title block.

The basic function of the steam turbine in a multi shaft plant is to generate efficient electric power from heat which would other wise be wasted by the multiple gas turbine exhaust flows. The system accepts available steam from HRSG'S, which convert the waste heat energy from their associated gas turbine into usable steam energy.

Steam Turbine Technical Data 

STAG: Steam Turbine and Gas turbine, SCSF: Straight Condensing Single Flow, (Industrial) 14 Stage

Rating = 50,790 KW,            Initial pressure = 575 Psig.      Initial Temperature = 866 °F

Back pressure = 1.75 inches Hg Abs.

Rated speed 3000 RPM,        Primary Trip 3360 RPM,       Emergency Trip 3420 RPM

Thrust Bearing (Active and inactive): (Tapered),                 Journal bearings 1,2 ( Elliptical )

Turning Gear      AC 3 phase, 50 HZ, motor 380V, Speed= 3 RPM

Safety Precaution

  • A work permit must be issued and the maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear the safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, shoes, gloves … etc.

  • Shut down the unit.

  • Lock the condensate pumps (1&2) switches, close isolate valves and isolate the power.

  • Lock the vacuum pumps (1&2) switches, close isolate valves and isolate the power.

  • Isolate compressed air for all valves.

  • Switch off gland exhaust and oil vapor extractor.

  • Switch off ventilation system.

  • Generator should be isolated.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set includes screw driver set.

  • Special tools for turbine as shown in figure 5

  • Welding machine, Jacks (hydraulic, screw),

  • Filler gauge, Telescope gauge.

  • Electric heaters,

  • Dial indicator with magnet base.

Work to be Carried out

Preparation            before doing any overhaul:

  1. Make maintenance schedule complete with maintenance individuals for each job

  2. Revise the store for the available spare parts and check it for validity.

  3. Prepare a complete list of spare parts which should be purchased from the local market (not available in the store).

  4. Check all tools which will be used during performing maintenance such as dial indicators, feelers gauges micrometers , venire calipers make calibration for it if is required.

  5. Check validity of cranes and steel wires which will be used for hanging and carrying turbine parts.

  6. Prepare and clean the place which the parts of the turbine will be isolated for doing maintenance.

  7. Record all readings of the turbine at different loads such as vibration of bearings and bearings temperature, oil pressures and temperatures, and axial, relative displacement and eccentricity.

Disassembly

  1. Ensure the unit at shut down

  2. Remove the lagging and insulation.

  3. Lubricate the bolts to be removed

  4. Remove all thermocouples, resistance temperature detectors instrument,

  5. Measure the run out of the two coupling halves using dial indicators and record it (not to exceed 0.02mm) as attachment no. 13

  6. Remove the bolting from the main steam inlet flanges.

  7. Check alignment for coupling between turbine and generator (axial 0.01mm lopes down - radial 0.02mm) and record it.  as attachment no. 9, 16

  8. Dismantle coupling bolts with its nut and mark them.

  9. Check clearance  of packing (Labyrinth) [a=0.08, b=0.02, c=0.19inch] as attachment no. 24,25

    1. = distance between seal strip fixed and moving front side.

    2. = distance between seal strip fixed and moving rear side.

    3. = distance between seal strip and shaft or fixed part.

  10. Dismantle the horizontal shell joint by cal-rod electric heaters.

  11. Remove the upper half of the following parts:

    1. HP and LP shells,

    2. Upper half of diaphragms,

    3. Upper half of packing and nozzle plates.

  12. Measure axial and radial clearances between moving and fixed blades on both sides of the rotor and record them.

  13. Measure journal bearing clearances (not exceed 0.018 inch).

  14. Measure run out of the shaft by using four dial indicator or at four different places in the shaft (not to exceed 0.003 inch)

  15. Measure clearance of the thrust bearing by pushing rotor front and rear ( 0.010 – 0.015 miles ).

  16. Lift the rotor out fits place and clean using sandblast, then check for any cracks by NDT - ( x – ray ) die – penetrate – magnetic yoke – ultrasonic ).

  17. Lift the lower half of bearings and check the white metal for cracks or cavities 'Re-cast if it is repaired'

Cleaning of Turbine Section Components

Rotor and Coupling

  1. The rotor must blast cleaned with an air jet type injector using aluminum oxide 220 mesh or finer.

  2. Bearing journals, couplings, thrust runners, and emergency trip mechanism should be carefully wrapped and sealed to protect them from contamination and or abrasive damage during the blast cleaning operation – bearing journals, couplings, thrust runners trip mechanisms, should also be coated with alight coat of lubrication prior to wrapping to prevent rusting. Through clean buckets, tenons, covers, wheel bases, packing areas and all exposed areas of the rotor. The rotor metal will have alight gray color when blast cleaning is complete. Through inspection and good turbine efficiency when the unit is returned to services. After the blast cleaning process is complete, a through cleaning with compressed ( dry instrument type ) air is imperative

  3. The coupling should be cleaned using a solvent and scotch bright pads to remove dirt and oil residue. After thoroughly cleaning the coupling, the face should be lightly honed to remove any burrs or high spots. After completing the cleaning lubrication should be applied to prevent rusting.

Inner Shell       as shown in figure 2.

  1. A good visual inspection of the shell will determine the extent of cleaning required inside the inner shell. If no visual crack indications are found or excessive deposits are not present, then only general hand stoning, emery cloth cleaning and moderate hand scrapion on diaphragm fits, nozzle fits keys, key ways and spill strip areas is necessary. Corrosion deposits, blue blush and burrs should be removed for accurate inspection and reassembly of the inner shell and components

  2. Excessive hand scraping filing or honing should not be done on the nozzle face to avoid damage of proper contact fitting upon reassembly. Circular power brushes mounted on high speed air motors have been used very effectively to make a preliminary cleaning of the above areas. When using power brushes, always ensure the brush is rated at or above the air motors, rated rpm. Also wear protective equipment while operating the machinery. You should wear a face shield, gloves, and long sleeved shirt. On the upper inner shell it is faster and much more effective to blast clean all internal components and areas using aluminum oxide 220 mesh or finer after the blast cleaning process is complete, a through cleaning with compressed ( dry instrument type ) air is imperative.

Diaphragms and Nozzle Plates     as shown in figure 3

  1. Diaphragms and nozzle plates when removed from shell must be blast cleaned using aluminum oxide 220 mesh or finer. Special attention should be paid to the following areas, steam paths radial fits, crush pins, packing fits, joints, support blocks, lower center line centering pin fits and all steam sealing areas after blast cleaning, it may be necessary to hone all fits prior to reassembly.

  2. On diaphragm partition and nozzle partitions that are eroded thin on the trailing edges, it is possible to roll the trailing edges over by excessive blast cleaning. This should be avoided and brought to the blast cleaner operators attention before any blasting takes place. After the blast cleaning process is completed, a through cleaning with compressed air is imperative.

Packing Casings, Steam Shields and Water Boxes

  1. Packing casings, steam shields and water boxes which have been removed from their major components should be blast cleaned using the same procedures outlined in the previous sections.

  2. After blast cleaning, all sealing surfaces and fits should be honed to remove high spots and burrs. Exercise coition to ensure that all brass sections are protected during the blasting process to prevent damage. items listed above that have not been removed from their major components should be cleaned using wire brushes, emery cloth and hone stones, pay extra attention to sealing faces and fits.

Bolt Threads, Tapped Holes, Dowels and Fits Unit

Bolt Threads

Bolt threads should be cleaned using approved solvents and wire brushes. After a through cleaning with solvents and wire brushes, a die nut should be run over the threads, if one is available.

Tapped Holes

All tapped holes (horizontal joints on LP sections, nozzle areas, diaphragm horizontal joints and packing casing fits) should be cleaned with approved solvents. A good tap should be run inside to clean up threads. After tapping and cleaning, holes should be covered to avoid contamination and damage to internal threads.

Dowels and Dowel Fits

All dowels and dowel fits should be cleaned with solvents and lightly polished with a very fit grit emery cloth. Avoid removal of any dowel or dowel fit metal, this will cause a loose fit.

Nuts

All nuts should be cleaned with solvents and wire brush and then a good top should be run through each nut to ensure good threads.

Oil Deflectors

Oil deflectors should be cleaned, using wire brushes and solvents sealing surfaces and horizontal joints should be game over lightly with a hone stone to remove any high spots or burrs.

Bearing Journal Surfaces and Thrust Runners

Bearing Journal

  1. Wash Journal surfaces with a solvent and then strap lap with 400 grit or finer emery cloth rolls. The rolls can be purchased in 2 inch widths and are approximately 50 yards long.

  2. Follow this procedure to strap lap the journal. Pull with left hand and allow the right hand to go towards the rotor while keeping the emery cloth snug, then reverse the procedure move back and forth over total journal surface until the total area is strapped satisfactorily. Always keep even force on the strapping cloth to ensure even polishing.

  3. Don't use any thing coarser than 400 grit. Cloth should be torned in lengths long enough to wrap around the journal twice with enough left (approx. 4 feet total) to give sufficient pulling area with each hand. Better results are achieved if the emery cloth is kept moist with solvent while strapping. If burrs, nicks or scratches are found, it is acceptable to us on Arkansas stone, or any close texture extra hard stone to polish away high spots. When using a stone on journal surfaces, always move in a radial direction to prevent producing a flat spot . After a good finish has been accomplished with 400 grit emery, the journal should be polished using crocus cloth in the same manner as you used the emery cloth.

Thrust Runner

Wash the thrust runner with solvents and polish out any rough spots with a hard Arkansas stone, using radial movements. If a slight rusting or sludge build – up is present. It is acceptable to clean with scotch bright pads and solvents .12 - horizontal, vertical and all other metal – to metal sealing surfaces. Wash with solvent and use scotch bright industrial pads to remove dirt and oil residue. After cleaning thoroughly, go over all surfaces with a good grade, medium hardness hone stone to remove any nicks.

Gasket Fits

Gasket areas, including steam lead pipes and manhole covers need thorough cleaning  using a putty knife, wire wheel mounted on air grinder, or side grinder, and hand honing to give goad sealing surfaces after cleaning you should immediate coat with some type of preservative to prevent corrosion and rust. It is unnecessary to clean twice.

Steam Leak off Piping, Hydraulic Oil Piping Mechanical Hydraulic Control 'MHC', and Stainless Steel Tubing Electro Hydraulic Control 'EHC'

The steam leak – off piping should be blown through to ensure that it is open. Hydraulic oil piping internals should be washed in solvent and blown through with dry air to ensure that they are clean. Stainless steel tubing internals should be washed with enraptured alcohol to ensure clean lines after cleaning all piping and tubing, the ends should be closed to prevent contamination.

After the blast cleaning process has been completed, you must blow out dust and residue from the rotor, diaphragms, inner shells, nozzles and packing casing (using dray air supply). You should lubricate all journals, metal to metal fits, couplings and any other surfaces which you do not want to rust or corrode. Journals and diaphragm partitions should be covered or stored in areas to prevent damage from occurring to them.

Inspection to be done    

  1. Inspect rotor visually and by using die- penetrate, ultrasonic, magnetic yoke and x – ray devices to check rotor cracks and check erosion.    as shown in figure 1

  2. Inspect of 1P and LP shells by the same way (above).

  3. Inspect diaphragms and nozzle plates.

  4. Inspect packing, packing casings, water impeller casings.

  5. Inspect steam shields and oil deflectors.

  6. Inspect bolts, studs, dowel fits, dowels and nuts for cracks and elongation.

  7. Check the steam balance holes for evidence of cracking and erosion.

  8. Check the thrust runner for signs of fretting, scores on thrust faces and cracking. The faces should also be checked to ensure that they are parallel.

  9. Check specifically for rubbing or wear, scoring, out of round conditions tapering, cracking and discoloration.  Check all wheel and shaft fillets closely for cracks.

  10. Check for cracking, tightness, staking of balance plugs and the condition of open plug holes.

  11. You should none destructively test the couplings for cracks, scoring and high spots on faces. Check for bolt or stud hole galling or filled holes that are out of round. Ensure that the faces are flat and check for run out of the radial and axial.

  12. Check the dovetail pins for cracking, proper staking and looseness. Ultrasonic testing is the only acceptable method of checking dovetail pins for cracks.

Assembly    as shown in figure 4

  1. All parts of turbine should be cleaned accurately before reassembling.

  2. Install the nozzle plate in both the upper and lower shell and ensure that 95% of it contact on sealing and bolt head faces by using blue contact method.  as shown in figure 7

  3. Apply fed pro N-1000 to bolt threads and torque the bolts properly according to the recommend torque for each kind of bolt.

  4. Install lower parts of bearings, diaphragms, nozzles and shell plates very well and apply fed pro N-1000 to all fitted parts. Then tight all bolts carefully with their torque limits as attachment no.17:22

  5. Put rotor in its place and check clearance between moving and fixed blades and adjust the rotor clearances as recommended by the manufacturer.

  6. Measure the twist and tilt of the journal bearings No. 1,2 (not exceed 0.05 mm) and record these measurements as attachment no.14

  7. Ensure that the rotor is perfectly leveled and that all rotor components are clean.

  8. Install upper – half bearing and assemble the thrust bearing, square bearings to rotor journals and lubricate all bearings with turbine oil.

  9. Roll the rotor to ensure freeness of its movement.

  10. Install upper packing, diaphragms, steam seal and nozzle plates and packing casing ensure that the packing is clean, and the packing fits are clean.

  11. Turn the rotor to ensure that it is not binding and not rubbing. Ensure that all bearings are lubricated before turning.

  12. Tighten and stretch the horizontal joint bolting ensure that all nuts and studs are properly matched, fel N – 1000 has been applied to the reads as attachment no. 15

  13. Tighten the bolting from the center outwards the ends working both sides equally and after stretching the studs, the horizontal joints must be closed check them with feeler gauge.

  14. Roll the rotor to ensure there is no interference.

  15. Take coupling alignment checks and make any necessary adjusting.

  16. Install all upper and lower valve gears and stretch bolting.

  17. Align and couple steam inlet flanges and all other piping.

  18. Ensure that all bolting and nuts are matched correctly and that fel pro N-1000 has been applied.

  19. Install turning gear and cheek gears clearances and record them.   as attachment no. 26

  20. Remove any temporary blocking or changes and ensure that permanent hangers have the proper cold setting.

  21. Reconnect all linkages, wiring, piping and align to proper specifications.

  22. Reinstall all  thermocouples, pressure taps, shell alignment keys and gibes

  23. Reinstall all insulation on the shell and piping.

  24. Clean the area around the turbine hall and put unit on turning gear.

  25. Evacuate the oil tank and clean it and refill with new oil or purifying the used oil and make flushing of all oil pipes for bearings.

  26. Operate unit on the turning gear for not less than 48 hours and clean oil screens and filters.

Preparation of the unit for Start after Assembly

  1. Check the availability of DC & AC power supplies.

  2. Check the level of main tank lube oil in normal level.

  3. Operate the emergency lube oil pump check direction of rotation and the discharge pressure.

  4. Check lube oil flow through sight glass at bearing no. (1,2,3,4).

  5. Check the level of cooling water tanks.

  6. Check valves position.

  7. Check condenser level in normal after operation in ordinary level .

 

Check the unit for:

  1. Leakage of oil line

  2. Leakage of cooling and seal steam pipes.

  3. Abnormal noise or excessive vibration

  4. Operate the unit at different loads

  5. Take complete operation reading and record the results in the attached record sheet.

Attendance Checking During Operation

  1. Check and measure the vibration for bearings No. 1, 2, 3, 4

  2. Check and measure the average steam inlet temperature

  3. Read and record Lube oil pressure

  4. Read and record hydraulic oil pressure                                               

  5. Review the valves positions.
