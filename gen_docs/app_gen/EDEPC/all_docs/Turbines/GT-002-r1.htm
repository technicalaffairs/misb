Introduction

This document outlines the hot gas path inspection procedures P1, which must be performed every 24000 operation hours as indicated by H24000 in the above title block.

The first draft initiated by eng. Ayman El-Mor & eng. Ali Mohamed Fahmy, revised by EDEPCo. Inspection and Quality control eng. Abouda El-Bana, tested at 2nd march by eng. Abd El-Hamid Abu Zaid, accepted and approved by Port-Said plant general director eng. El-Aaraby Ali Said.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance stuff should be carry the suitable safety category

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area

  • Wear your safety helmet, shoes, and gloves pairs.

  • Unit must be out of service.

  • Fire fighting system should be isolated.

  • Fuel system should be isolated.

  • Oil system should be isolated.

  • All switches in motor control center panel should be isolated.

  • Generator must be fully isolated. 

Tools and Equipment

  • Two wrenches set size 3/8'' to 1 1/2''

  • Two sockets set drive 3/4'' size 3/8'' up to 1 1/2''

  • Two Allen wrench size 3/32'' to 1''

  • Four impact socket wrench drive 1'' size 1 1/8'', 1 5/16'', 1 ½'', 1 11/16'', 1 7/8'' &2 ¼''.

  • Power hand drill, Misc. grinding machine.

  • Four chain hoists 1 1/2 tons.

  • Chain hoists 5 tons, Chain hoists 8 tons.

  • Eye bolts & shackles.

  • 0.0015 to 0.040 inch feelers.

  • 0 – 1 inch micrometer.

Work to be Carried Out 

DISASSEMBLY

Disassembly of the Combustion Component and Piping

Combustion Chamber Accessories and Pipes Removal

  1. Remove the atomizing airlines from the fuel nozzle.

  2. Remove the fuel lines from the fuel nozzle.

  3. Disconnect the spark plug leads.

  4. Disconnect the flame detector.

  5. Unlock and remove all fuel nozzles take in consideration to mark the nozzles with its proper chamber number.

Combustion Liners Removal

  1. Mark each cover with the relative chamber number.

  2. Unbolt and remove the combustion covers.

  3. Remove the combustion liner no 1 as described below: 

  4. Remove the crossfire tube clamps from chamber No 1.

  5. Remove the crossfire tube clamps from adjacent chamber N0 2 and No 10.

  6. Push the crossfire tube into adjacent chambers No 2 & No 10.

  7. Remove the liner No 1.

  8. Remove the crossfire tube with the adjacent chamber NO. 2 & NO. 10.

  9. Repeat steps (a) to (e) to remove other liners.

Combustion Component Inspection

  1. Inspect the crossfire tubes & retainers for (wear, cracks& overheating) and record the results  in the attached record sheet

  2. Inspect and maintain the fuel nozzle according to the appropriate procedure and record the results in the attached sheet

  3. Inspect the cap and liner if it should be reused, repaired or scraped according to the criteria and record the results   in the attached record sheet

  4. Inspect the transition pieces with strong beam of light and records should be made of any visible cracks, deposits or erosion and record the results   in the attached record sheet

Turbine Compartment Accessories Removal

  1. Remove the turbine compartment roof with the diesel engine exhaust manifold & CO2 fire fighting piping connected to this compartment

  2. Remove the turbine compartment stand beams adjacent to the turbine shell

  3. Prepare four lifting lugs on the chimney wall above the center lines of the first stage nozzle,    the second stage nozzle, right and left side of the shell

  4. Install three support screw jacks below:
         = Discharge compressor forward to after casing flange
         = Discharge casing to turbine shell flange
         = Turbine shell to exhaust     hood

  5. Remove atomizing air & gas manifold and fuel oil lines.

  6. Remove cooling air pipes as necessary for removal of the shell

  7. Remove the two second-stage nozzle axial holding bolts located at 45º & borescope plugs from the upper shell half

  8. Remove the wheel space thermocouples & junction boxes adjacent to Turbine shell.

The Upper Shell Half Disassembly

  1. Remove the vertical bolting between the turbine shell and compressor discharge shell

  2. Remove the vertical Allen head bolts between the turbine shell and the exhaust hood

  3. Remove the turbine shell horizontal bolting

  4. Jack the turbine shell at the jack bolts holes provided to lift the shell case (it is preferable to use four guides during the shell lifting).

  5. Lift the shell casing using mobile crane, lifting wires and hoists as described in the weight and center of gravity drawing

  6. Remove the eccentric pins for positioning the 1st & 2nd nozzles upper halves after removing the upper shell case

Turbine Internals Disassembly

  1. Check the clearances of the 1st & 2nd nozzle holding clamps

  2. Remove the upper portion of the 2nd stage nozzle holding clamps and the horizontal joint bolting and lift the upper half of the 2nd stage.

  3. Remove the allen-head horizontal joint bolts from the inner diaphragm upper half and lift it

  4. Remove the combustion outer casing except the combustors No 1, 10, 9

  5. Remove the No 10 transition pieces by removing the bolts with the first stage nozzle and side L seal 

  6. Push these transition pieces clear of nozzle into the outer combustion chambers.

  7. Remove the first stage nozzle clamp and the horizontal joint bolting

  8. Lift off the upper half of the first stage nozzle careful attention should be paid to the L-shaped seals between the segments

  9. Remove the upper half of supporting ring.

  10. Take a complete set of turbine clearance and record the results  in the attached record sheet

  11. Remove the first and second stage nozzle lower centering pins & second stage axial holding bolts located at 45º & bore scope plugs from the lower shell half.

  12. Untight the seal ring bolts from the first stage nozzle inner support ring and the two axial nozzle clamps.

  13. Remove the first stage nozzle support lugs

  14. Roll out the first stage nozzle by hooking the tow nozzle sides to two-hoist chain hooked on the lifting lug above the first stage nozzle Transvaal axel with the nozzle swinging free.

  15. Raise one hoist cable and lowering the other side until the nozzle brought out above the turbine shaft, and then lift the stage up

  16. Remove the second stage nozzle diaphragm lower half packing (labyrinth) by bushing it out of its groove

  17. Light a rope with wooden blocks of dimension 7*7*12 cm with distance between block of 20 cm   around the turbine shaft at the labyrinth packing groove.

  18. Remove the lower half second stage nozzle supporting lugs then remove it by rolling out using two chain hoist hooked on the lifting lugs above the stage axel the lower half of the nozzle and diaphragm must be lifted as one piece.

Turbine Internal Inspection

  1. Inspect the transition pieces with strong beam of light and records should be made of any visible cracks, deposits or erosion and record the results in the attached record sheet

  2. Inspect the first and second stage nozzle for cracks, distortion, blocked cooling holes, loss of metal and corrosion. Repair is mandatory for nozzles exceed the following limits:

Cracks:

Sidewall:

  1. Five cracks per nozzle segment 0.5 inch deep and spaced less than 1.0 inch.

  2. Vane trailing edge:

  • any number of cracks parallel to sidewall and less than 1.0 inch provided that distance between any adjacent cracks is greater than the longer of the two cracks.

  • Two converging cracks less than 1.0 inch with a part distance less than the total sum of the two cracks.

  • Two diverging cracks less than 1.0 inch and the length of the crack is less than the distance between the between the end of crack and sidewall.

  1. Vane leading edge: two cracks less than .375 inch and a part distance less than 1.0 inch.

Corrosion / Erosion:

  • bowing of the trailing edge in excess of 0.035 inch with no more than one incident per segment.

  • No other partition shall be bowed.

  1. Record the inspection results and record the results in the attached record sheet

Buckets Inspection

  1. Inspect the first & second stages buckets for cracks, torn metal, dents & erosion of loading and trailing edges.

  2. Compare the inspection results with the manufacture limits and change if necessary.

First  Stage Bucket Removal

  1. Cut out the heads of lock twist pins by sharp chisel

  2. Bush the bucket in the direction of removed head pay attention to the axial & radial seal pins.

  3. Clean out the bucket groove and remove any proud metal caused by cutting chisel or securing peen marks.

 Second Stage Bucket Removal

  1. Remove the exhaust turning vanes (diffuser) from the exhaust plenum and slide them back against the load gear.

  2. Enter the exhaust plenum and remove the horizontal joint bolting insulation shields.

  3. Remove the horizontal joint bolts of inner and outer flanges of the exhaust hood.

  4. Use jackscrews to be separate the upper half of exhaust hood from the lower half.

  5. Use 2-ton hoist to left the exhaust hood upper half and hang it up into the stack.

  6. Cut out the heads of lock twist pins of the second stage bucket by sharp chisel.

  7. Push the buckets toward the removed pins head gradually as group pay attention to bucket   shield slots.

  8. Clean out the bucket groove and remove any proud metal caused by cutting chisel or securing peen marks.

Re-assembly

Second Stage Bucket Re-assembly

  1. Arrange the bucket in accordance to the balance sheet.

  2. Place the lock twist pins in their slots and assemble each bucket with its seal pins. (Anti size compound is recommended to use for pines)

  3. Push the first bucket into the rotor grove (2cm) and then install the next bucket, push the tow buckets into their grove (0.5 cm) and assemble the third bucket, push the buckets gradually paying attention to the seal pins.

  4. Rotate the lock twist such that it secure the bucket from moving axial, then punch the lock twist with the two diametrically opposite peen marks with around nosed punch.

First Stage Bucket Re-assemble

  1. Arrange the bucket in accordance to the balance sheet.

  2. Place the lock twist pins in their slots. (Anti size compound is recommended to use for pines)

  3. Assemble buckets with their radial & axial seal pins.

  4. Rotate the lock twist such that it secures the bucket from axial movement.

  5. Punch the lock twist heads with around nosed punch to lock it in the   rotor slots with two diametrically opposite peen marks.

 

Exhaust Hood Re-assemble

  1. Lower the upper half of the exhaust hood and tight the horizontal joint bolts of inner and outer flanges be sure that new gaskets are used on the horizontal joint of the inner gas path cone & the air baffle is properly located in the groove of the seal of bearing NO 2 before lowering the hood.

  2. Assemble the horizontal joint bolting insulation shield.

  3. Assemble the exhaust turning vanes (diffuser) ensure the position of gas seal plates between the diffuser & stack.

 

First & Second Nozzles Re-assemble

  1. Clean thoroughly the turbine shell and discharge casing

  2. Coat the second stage nozzle spring seal with anti seize

  3. Roll in the second-stage nozzle when in position and supported on the lifting rig insert and bolt the support shims.

  4. Lower the second stage nozzle lower half onto the supporting lugs and inserts the bottom eccentric pin.

  5. Assemble the two lower axial holding pins at 45°.

  6. Assemble the lower half inter stage packing (labyrinth) and spring adjusts the clearances as recommended by the manufacture drawing

  7. Roll in the lower half of the first stage nozzle and while the nozzle is still supported on the lifting hoist insert the horizontal supporting lugs, bolt then tightly and then lower the nozzle.

  8. Assemble the first stage nozzle bottom eccentric pin.

  9. Tighten the first nozzle bottom holding clamp and check their clearance.

  10. Take a complete set of turbine clearance and record the results  in the attached record sheet               

  11. Note that the clearances on the packing and the second stage nozzle must be taken while the nozzle is waged against the second stage shrouds (this is its running position).

  12. Assemble the upper half of the supporting ring.

  13. Assemble the upper half of the first stage nozzle.

  14. Assemble the upper half of second stage nozzle diaphragm. 

  15. Assemble the upper half of second stage nozzle.

  16. Assemble the clamps to the first stage nozzle horizontal joint and check the clearance of the clamp record this clearance on form GT-4656.

  17. Assemble the second nozzle clamps and record this clearance. and record the results  in the attached record sheet               

  18. Assemble the two packing strips into the outer seal groove of the first stage nozzle; verify that the lap of the strips is not in line to minimize the possibility of leakage.

  19. Assemble the transition pieces. Starting from the chamber No 5 and work upper wards from that point Be sure that the transition pieces are fully engage in the first stage nozzle before tightening the center holding bolts.

  20. Assemble the side seals (L seals) and locking bolt washer combination between each transition pieces.

Shell Casing Re-assemble

  1. Check thoroughly turbine shell cleaning.

  2. Coat the mating surfaces of the turbine shell exhaust hood and discharge casing with a light film of anti-seize.

  3. Check that all is secure within the shell area before assemble, rotate the turbine rotor with ratchet mechanism check if there were any abnormal sound.

  4. Assemble the shell’s upper half using 4 guide rods and the two jack bolts mechanism pay attention to the turbine shell level during lowering with jack bolts.

  5. Tighten the shell bolts according to the bolting sequence.

  6. Assemble the first and second stage nozzle upper eccentric pins; second stage axial holding bolts at 45º and the bore scope plugs.

  7. Assemble the outer combustion casing using new gasket on the casings and fix them in the groove with grease in two spots.

  8. Remove all support jacks from the unit.

Combustion Component Re-assemble

A- Cleaning

  1. Clean all mating flange faces of any gasket material. 

  2. Clean all hardware bolts & nuts with wire-brush to remove traces of anti-seize or rust.  

  3. Lock maintained fuel nozzle with steel lock wire.

B - Combustion Liner

  1. Assemble liner No 5 to the chamber. Slide the crossfire tubes from chamber No 6 & No 4 into Liner No 5.

  2. Assemble liner No 4. 

  3. Slide crossfire tube from liner No 5 into position between liners No 4 & No 5 and lock it with Retainers.        

  4. Follow the a/m procedure for the remaining chambers.     

  5. Energize the ignition transformers and observes that the spark plugs are properly work.

  6. Check the flame detector works properly by expose the scanner to suitable light.

  7. Assemble the spark plugs and the flam detector.

C- Fuel Nozzles & Accessoires Pipe

  1. Assemble the combustion covers take in consideration that the two larger bolts in the flanges are to be mated with the holes in the cover and casing.

  2. Reassemble the fuel nozzles but do not tighten bolts firmly.

  3. Reassemble the fuel piping and when mating with the nozzle flanges. Tight the nozzle bolts firmly.

Assemble the Turbine Compartment Roof and CO2 Fire Fighting System Pipes

Preparation the Unit for Start after Inspection

  1. Revise & check the clearness & cleanness of working site.

  2. Check the availability of DC & AC power supplies.

  3. Check batteries voltage should not be less than 105 V.

  4. Check the lube oil level and the fuel oil tank of starting diesel engine.

  5. Check the level of main tank lube oil in normal level.

  6. Operate the emergency lube oil pump check direction of rotation and the discharge Pressure.

  7. Check lube oil flow through sight glass at bearing NO. (1, 2, 3).

  8. Check the level of cooling water tanks.

  9. Check valves position of fuel supply system in operation position.

  10. Check the position of cooling air dampers.

  11. Check readiness of unit (No trip signal is initiated) and the ready light signal is on.

Operation Checks

  1. Operate the unit at different speeds (200 r.p.m, crank speed, fire position, full speed no load) 

Check the Unit for:

  1. Leakage of fuel gas line (test with soapy solution).

  2. Leakage of cooling and seal air pipes.

  3. Abnormal noise or excessive vibration.

  4. Operate the unit at (different loads 0%, 25%, 50%, 100%).

  5. Take complete operation reading and record the results  in the attached record sheet.

Attendance Checking During Operation
