Maintenance Procedures (P1: 10% Plugging)

Introduction

This document outlines the maintenance procedures P1, to be carried out in case of 10% plugging of the total tubes as indicated in the title block.

The function of these heaters to used the high temperature, which come from extractions from turbine.

Safety Precautions

  • A work order must be issued; the maintenance staff should be qualified maintenance manuals

  • Wear safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, anti static hand probe to avoid electrical shock …etc

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required       

  • Isolate the equipment and make good drain.

    •

  • The unit must be out of service

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tools set.

  • Safety and healthy equipment

  • Ropes and wooden blocks.

  • Scaffolding

Work to be Carried Out

Disassembly

Shell Removal

  1. Remove the insulation from the part to be handled.

  2. Disconnect all the shell external pipe connections

  3. Disconnect and remove all instruments as liquid level controllers, gauge glasses.

  4. Cut the shell by torch or grinding where indicated on general arrangement.

  5. A special protection ring is provided to avoid damage of the tube bundle.

  6. Remove the shell by pulling, while the shell is being removed, wooding blocks should be placed beneath is bundle, at the tube support plates not on the tubes. These act as supports when the shell is completely removed.

Channel Disassembly

When it is necessary to examine the tube sheet surface and internal box. Only the channel cover has to be removed.

Inspection

  1. Inspect to tubes to prevent any damage.

  2. Test for all tubes from any damage if any tube defected it must be plugging.

  3. Drill or grind out the tube.

  4. Insert the stainless steel plug and weld it.

  5. Check all welding points.

  6. Check all instruments as liquid level controllers.

  7. Clean gauge glasses.

Assembly

  1. Assembly all instrument and liquid level controller.

  2. Assembly low pressure and high pressure heaters as before.

  3. Connect all piping
