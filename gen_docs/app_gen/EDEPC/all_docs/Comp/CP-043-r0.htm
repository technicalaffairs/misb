Introduction

This document outlines the equipment major maintenance procedures P1, to be carried out every five years as indicated by Y5 in the title block.

Atomizing air compressor delivers air in high pressure to the fuel nozzles to atomize the fuel in the combustion chamber of the unit.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required 

  • Wear safety helmets, shoes, earplug and gloves pairs … etc.

  • The turbine must be shut down and ratchet off.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set.

  • Cleaning fluid, manual puller.

  • Feeler gauge, dial indicator

  • Micrometer, straight edge, rags.

Work to be carried out

  1. Remove the compressor from the turbine and securely mount to a stand or workbench using the four bolt holes of the oil drain flange.                    

  2. Remove inlet end bearing cover.

  3. Remove bearing retainer nuts and lock washers.

  4. Drive out the two bearing carrier flange dowels and remove inlet end bearing carrier cap screws.                                                                                                                              

  5. Using four (4) jack bolts through tapped holes in inlet end bearing carrier flange, pull carrier and bearings as an assembly. Be sure to pull evenly.

  6. Drive out two gear end cover dowels and remove cover.

  7. Remove shaft clamp plate and oil slinger

  8. Remove pinion locknut.

  9. Mark gear, gear hub and pinion for easier reassembly.

  10. Remove the gear is slip fit on hub by tapping lightly with a soft-face hammer.

  11. Jam block of hard wood between rotors to prevent rotation while pulling the gear hub and pinion.

  12. Pull gear hub and pinion. If gear hub pinion are pulled with jaw type puller as shown in figures 1 and 3, an adaptor plate must be provided. Pulling directly on the pinion teeth will damage teeth making timing difficult and cause gear wear. Pulling directly on the gear hub flange will distort the flange causing gear run-out. Always use a shaft protector. Adaptor plate dimensions are given in figure 2A

  13. Remove bearing clamp plate.

  14. Bearings are a light press fit on shaft. Press each rotor shaft through bearing (figure 4).  As shaft is pressed through, be careful that rotor does not fall and damage opposite end of shaft. Support the puller so it does not fall when rotor drops free of bearing. If puller or press is not available, tap rotors through which a block of hard wood and hammer.

  15. Drive out two gear end bearing carriers to housing dowels and remove carrier.

  16. Remove bearings and oil seals (lip seals) from both bearing carriers. Remove with care so as not to damage the bronze-filled Teflon lining of the rotor shaft seal since it may be reusable. If bearing are to be reused, keep the races in matched sets.

Inspection:

  1. Damage teeth in the gear and the pinion.

  2. Wear in bearings.

  3. Damage of the seal.

Assembly

  1. Install rotor shaft seals in either bearing carriers (figure 9). Press or drive seal about .005’’ below flush to prevent interference with end of rotor. .005’’shim stock (cut diameter of seal) placed over end of seal will allow it to be driven to proper depth. A flat driver as shown in figure 9 will prevent damage to seal. Oil O.D. of seal to prevent seizure in bore.Check clearance of spacer and seal.

  2. Install oil seal in bearing carriers, fig. 10 Lip of seal points towards bearing bore. Seal bottoms against shoulder in bore. Use sealing compound on O.D. of seal for ease of installation and prevent leakage around seal. Be sure the driver used is large enough to bear against the outer ring of seal case to prevent damage to case and distortion of seal lip. Use only seals shown in parts list as they are selected for blower service.

  3. Install inlet bearing carrier and .030’’ aluminum gasket on housing.

  4. Match the cavity on the flat side of the carrier with the inlet opening on the housing (figure 8 ). Insert dowel pins before pulling cap screws tight.

  5. Stand housing and bearing carrier on blocks as shown in figure 8. Slide bearing spacers on inlet end of the rotor shaft with grooves toward rotor. Be sure bearing spacer fits square against end of rotor. Tape bearing spacers to shaft to hold in place while lowering rotors into housing. Install gate rotor first. If main rotor is installed first the gate rotor bearing spacer O.D.  will drag the top of the main rotor lobes. Do not damage seals when inserting shaft. Blocks must be high enough so rotor shafts hang free and allow end of rotor to rest solidly on bearing carrier. Match timing marks on end of rotors when assembling rotors in housing (figure 9). Rotors must be used in matched pairs. A matching number is stamped on the O.D. of each rotor on the same lobe as the timing marks.

  6. Rotate rotors to be sure they are resting squarely on bearing carrier. Using a depth micrometer, measure the distance from the end of each rotor lobe to the face of the housing flange figure 9. Rotate rotors so all lobes can be checked and use greatest reading to the total end (inlet plus discharge) clearance shown in clearance chart (figure 10). This sum determines the amount of housing shims required at the gear end. Housing shims are made of aluminum and are furnished in varying thickness as a set. Each shim is market for thickness.

  7. With 0 to 2 inch outside micrometer, measure thickness from rotor side of carrier to bearing shoulder in bearing bore (figure 11). Record this measurement for each bore.

  8.  Measure the length of the two bearing spacers (figure 12). Match each bearing spacer length with thickness measurement made in step 7 to determine the discharge end clearance. If the spacers do not measure longer than the bearing carrier by the required amount shown in the clearance chart (figure 10) under discharge end, shims must be added at the end of the spacers to make up the difference.

  9. Slide bearing spacers over gear end of rotor shaft figure 14) with grooves towards rotor. Note that spacer and shaft was matched to individual bore (step 8); this relation must be maintained. To check this relationship match discharge end bearing carrier cavity (shown in figure 15) with discharge in housing.

  10. Place amount of aluminium shims, established in step 5, on end of housing (figure 14). Mike shims, to be sure of thickness.

  11. Drive two dowels in flange of housing. Lower discharge end bearing carrier in place. Do not damage lip seals as they pass over ends of shafts. Match discharge cavity in carrier with discharge opening in housing (figure 15). Be sure dowels are through both flange before pulling cap screws tight. Place shims, if used, established in step 8, over shaft and against end of bearing spacer.

  12. Check the total end clearance with dial indicator clamped on discharge end carrier flange, indicator button to rest on steel strap clamped between two bearing locknuts (figure 16). Set dial on zero and lift rotor with pry bar placed under locknuts. With rotor as it well go, the indicator reading should be equal to the total end clearance established in step 5. To check the clearance on the other rotor, rest indicator button on end of the shaft. Lift rotor by means of an eye bolt screwed into one of the tapped holes and pry bar through eye bolt.

  13. Assemble angular contact ball bearings on each rotor shaft at gear end as shown in figure 17. Marked face of the inner bearing is placed down in bore; marked face of outer bearing is placed up. The bearings are a slip fit in the bore and a press fit on the shaft. Use lubricant on both inner and outer races for easier installation. Assemble press plate, figure 18, and press bearings in bore. Figure 19 gives dimensions of press plate. Tighten nuts on jack screws evenly to prevent cocking of bearing on shaft and in bore. It is not recommended to drive bearings in place as damage to bearing will result. Bearings must be completely seated against shoulder in bearing carrier.

  14. Install bearing clamp plate and secure with "Nylok" type screws (figure 20).

  15. Check fit of keys in pinion and gear hub. Fit keys to both rotor shafts for installation of pinion and gear hub.

  16. For ease of disassembly and assembly, the rotor shafts and bore of pinion and gear hub are tapered. Jam piece of hard wood or leather belting between rotors to prevent turning while installing gear hub and pinion. Heat gear hub and pinion in oil bath until 350 F allow heat to penetrate for 30 minutes minimum. Slip the pinion on the main rotor, install locking device (nut or clamp plate) and pull up tight. Slip gear hub on gate rotor, install locking device and pull up tight. Where retainer plate is used, turn it upside-down to be sure hub is pulled tight before pilot of plate hits end of shaft (figure 21). Tightening the locking device is very important as this operation pulls rotor shaft through bearings until bearing spacer and shims are clamped solidly between rotor end and bearing, assuring a fixed position of the rotor. As pinion and hub cool, retighten retainer and locknut.

  17. After pinion and gear hub are cooled and tight, check discharge end clearance between ends of rotor and discharge end bearing carrier using a feeler gauge through discharge opening. Check rotor at inlet end with feeler gauge through inlet opening.  These clearances should coincide with figures listed in the clearance chart.

  18. Turn blower over to install bearings in inlet end carrier. Remove tape holding bearing spacer in place. Place a machined bar such as the base of a dial indicator across the bearing bore on the carrier plate. Be certain the face of the bore is smooth and the bar lies perfectly flat. With depth micrometer, measure the dis­tance from the bar to the shoulder in the bore and record figure for both bearing bores (Figure 22).

  19. Move bar and depth mike to the end of the bearing spacer (Figure 23). Bearing spacers must be tight against end of rotors. Add bearing spacer shims like those used in Figure 13, to the end of the bearing spacer so the depth reading is from .005" to .010" less than the reading obtained in Step 17. This clearance provides "float" for the bearing outer race.

  20. Install the ball bearing on the gate rotor and the roller bearing on the main rotor. Oil bore and shaft for easier assembly. Use press plate, shown in Figure 18, to press bearings in place. Install lock washers and nuts and pull up tight. Bend ear of lock washer into slot of nut.

  21. With depth micrometer measure distance from bearing to carrier face on both sides (Figure 24).

  22. With gasket in place, measure depth of counter bore in bearing cover (Figure 25). This measurement must be at least .010" greater than the largest reading obtained in Step 24 to provide for float of bearing outer race. If less than .010", add additional gaskets. Figure 25 shows the bearing cover for T5CDL12.

  23. Grease bearings, refer to "Lubrication" under Maintenance Section, and install covers.

Timing of Rotors

  1. Stand unit with gear end up. Slip gear on hub. Gear must be positioned so tapped holes in hub are near center of the holes in the gear. This is necessary to allow movement of the gear on the hub for timing. Tighten "Nylok" type screws against flat washers just enough to allow gear to slip radially on hub. Always use new washers when timing blower.

  2. Provide 3/8"-16 stud turned down on one end to fit indicator clamp for indicator support (Figure 26).Use flat metal stock bolted to end of shaft for indicator button bracket.

  3. Set indicator at zero. Hold gear under clockwise to take up all backlash. Gear backlash is.002/.004. While holding pressure on gear, rotate the rotor counterclockwise with Allen wrench in one of the shaft screws as lever. Do not rotate the assembly by moving gear; use wrench only. Make two complete revolutions; if at any time the indicator moves to the plus side of zero, reset indicator to zero and again make two complete revolutions. Note the smallest reading (this is the smallest number of thousandths from zero and not the smallest figure on the indicator dial). Continue rotation and stop at this point and reset indicator to zero. This is the closest clearance of rotors in this direction. If the indicator pointer flutters at any point during rotation, check for burrs or dirt on rotors or gear teeth.

  4. While holding gear under pressure counterclockwise (Figure 27), rotate rotor clockwise two complete revolutions, and note the smallest indicator reading. Continue rotation and stop indicator at smallest reading. This is the point of minimum total interlobe clearance.The interlobe clearance is equally divided

  5. Hold gear from turning (Figure 28) and move rotor counterclockwise with wrench just enough to divide the minimum indicator reading obtained in Step 27. For example: The indicator reading is +16. Then move rotor until the indicator reads +8.

  6. Tighten gear to hub screws evenly. Be sure the indicator reading of Step 28 does not change. Discharge side clearance is checked with a feeler gauge through the discharge opening in the housing. The rotors are held in time by the distortion of the flat washers into the gear holes and clamp action of the screws. Rotate blower several times to be sure timing has not slipped.

  7. Remove indicator equipment. Turn over retainer plate installed upside down in Step 15. Install oil slinger (if used) with clamp plate and tighten screws.

  8. Check drive shaft locknut for tightness and bend ear of lock washer into locknut slot.

  9. Oil bearings and gear to prevent dry start and/or rusting during storage. Replace shaft oil seal in gear end cover. Protect shaft key­way and oil shaft to prevent damage to the seal when installing the gear cover. Insert gear cover dowels before pulling cap screws tight.

  10. Clean gear case breather filter and install; replace if necessary.

  11. Fill gear case with oil (-refer to "Lubrication" in Maintenance Section).

  12. Mount the compressor in the turbine again.

 Attendance and checking during operation

  1. Measure the inlet pressure and temp.

  2. Measure the discharge pressure and temp.
