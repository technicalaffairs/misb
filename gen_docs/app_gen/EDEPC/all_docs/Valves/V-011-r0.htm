Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks must be surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear the safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, ...etc).

    •

  • Hearing and eye protection should be used when working on a valve which is under pressure.

    •

  • The boiler must be drained, and the boiler vents must be opend .

  • Never strike a valve which is under pressure premature actuation can result .

  • Never stand in front of the discharge out let of pressure relief valve which is under pressure.

    •

  • Always take a great care when you approach or use any pressure valve.

 

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set.

  • Cleaning fluid, manual puller.

  • Suitable lifting device feeler gauge .

  • Hammer air wrench.

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Check the safety valves for external leakage ( See annex 1= Fig 1.2 ).

  2. Check the safety valves internal leakage.

  3. Check the adjusting ring set screw.

  4. Check the forked level for damage.

  5. Check spring for any damage

  6. Check the setting pressure by using the air set device (it makes with boiler in operation).

Dismantling

  1. Remove the forked level pin (34)  and pull out the level loosen the cap setscrew and remove the cap (22) fig 1,2

  2. Remove the spring covers (21) (22) on marine valves.

  3. Remove the sprindle nut (5) from the spindle (12).

  4. Make a prick-punch mark on the side of the adjusting bolt head and another directly below this mark on the bonnet top (machined surface) measure the distance between these mark with a pair of dividers and keep the record until the valve is weady to reassemble.

  5. Loosen the adjusting bolt lock nut and the adjusting bolt. This releases the compression of the spring

  6. Remove the bolts or studs (19) that fasten the bonnet (17) to valve body .

    1. On low and medium pressure drum valves the bonnet and spring can be lifted up over the spindle.

    2. Nearly all super heater valves have cooling spools ans also deflector collars on the spindles. It is not necessary to break the joint between the bonnet and the cooling spool. It will, however, be necessary to remove the deflector collar locking device enough to let the collar move freely up and down the spindle. The bonnet, cooling spool and spring can then be lifted up over the spindle

  7. Remove the spindle, (12) disc (or disc holder (16) and insert ), guide (11), and guide (adj) ring (10) and inspect and clean them ( See Fig 3,4,5 ) .

  8. Mark the location of guide ring with respect to the guide, to prevent its being moved and upsetting the valve action when re-tested.

  9. Remove both setscrews (11), (4) mark carefully the location of the nozzle ring, (3) so that it can be returned to the same position, and then remove it.

  10. When the valve is completely dismantled, examine the seats on both the nozzle and the disc or disc insert. Determine how much lapping or grinding if any, is necessary to remove all scratches, nicks and dents from them I1 either seat is severely damaged, it may have to be re-machined or replaced.

 

Micro Finishing of Valve Seats

  1. When applicable, Safety valve seats may be lapped to a micro inch finish using special compounds. Prior to supper-finishing the valve seats should be lapped flat and to a fine surface finish in accordance with standard practice as specified above .

  2. The diamond lapping compound PDH-6 is used in conjuction with a lapping and cleaning thinner PDH-27 and should be used as described in the following procedure .

  3. Clean lapping block using varsol, Acetone or apply cleaning thinner PSH-27, then with a clean dry lint-free cloth or cotton prior to applying lapping compount

  4. Apply dots of PDH-6 on the lapping block approximately ½ to 1 inch apart (not less than four (4) per block ), circumferentially on the face of the lapping block and then apply a drop of lapping thinner PDH-27 to each dot of compound .

  5. Lap the valve seat, keeping the lapping block against the seat and applying slight downward pressure. During the operation the lapping compound may begin to get stiff and movement of the lapping block more difficult; remove lap from lapped surface and add a few drops of lapping thinner PDH-27 to the lapping block, replace on surface being lapped and continue to rotate exerting no downward pressure.

Cleaning

  1. Before a valve is assembled all parts and bearings (fig. 6) should be thoroughly cleaned. This is usually done with emery cloth and wibed clean with a reg. By this method, all foreign matter that might cause leakage or faulty action is removed. If valves are to be assembled without retesting, care must be taken not to mix the parts of different valves. figure 7

Assembling Valves

  1. When all the parts, guide, contact surfaces and bearings have been cleaned, the seats properly reconditioned and all lapping compound removed, the valve is ready to be assembled. This is done as follows :

  2. Screw the nozzle ring on the nozzle, making certain that it is below the top surface of the nozzle seat. If  the former position is known, return the ring to it, otherwise blow down adjustment. figure 8: "nozzle ring setting"

  3. Wipe the nozzle (3) and disc seats (6,7) clean with a cloth and then place the guide, guide ring (10) disc and spindle assembly in the valve bode. Be sure that the guide ring, is in the same position as when the valve was dismantled .

  4. Carefully lower the bonnet (17) and spring (20) into place, using care not to damage the seats or spindle. Then center the bonnet (17) and tighten it down evenly, to prevent any unnecessary strains or possible misalignment of the parts .

  5. Before applying any load to the spring rotate it rapidly to be sure that the spindle is free, it should rotate freely with the spring. If it does not, the cooling spool or the bonnet, or both should be shifted until any binding disappears .

  6. The adjusting bolt is now tightened until the distance between the prick punch marks on the bolt and the machined surface on the bonnet top is the same as before the valve was dismantled. Unless considerable lapping or machining has been done the set pressure should be almost the same as before reconditioning. Correction can be made for metal removed by machining .

  7. Tighten the guide (adj) and nozzle ring setscrews and if the valve is to be tested and adjusted on the boiler, refer to instructions
