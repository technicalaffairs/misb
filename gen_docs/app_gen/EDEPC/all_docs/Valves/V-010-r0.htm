Introduction

This document lists the equipment inspection I1 for all valve types, to be performed every two months as indicated by M2 in the title block

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks must be surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear the safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, ...etc.

    •

  • Hearing and eye protection should be used when working on a valve which is under pressure.

  • Isolate valve mechanically and electrically.

  • Drain the pipelines, which connected to this valve.

  • The boiler must be drained, and the boiler vents must be opened.

  • Never strike a valve which is under pressure. Premature actuation can result.

  • Never stand in front of the discharge outlet of a pressure relief valve, which is under pressure.

  • Always approach and use any pressure relief valve with great care.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set.

  • Suitable lifting device

  • Cleaning fluid

 

 

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Check any leakage form valve gland fit found, retighten on gland bolts or renew the gland pocking.

  2. Check any leakage from the pressure tight cover. If it found retighten the cover nut.

  3. Check any leakage if there is any internal leakage it must be carried out the maintenance procedure of this type to prevent the leakage.

  4. Check any leakage from the valve bodies (arracks or mechanical wear) remark the point of leakage and welded it with suitable electrode.

  5. Check the electrical drive of the valve for electrical type.

  6. Check the operation of the valve drive during closing /opening of the valve .

  7. Check the oil level of the gear-motor for electrical drive.

  8. Re-grease the valve spindle.
