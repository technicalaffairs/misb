Introduction

This document outlines the equipment major maintenance list P1, which must be performed for the high pressure steam turbine by-pass valve every five years as indicated by Y5 in the above titled block

The high pressure steam turbine by-pass valve is a station for reduction the steam pressure and temperature outlets from the main steam of high pressure steam boiler to very low pressure and temperature which be able to get in the condenser.

Safety Precaution

  • A work permit must be issued and qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required 

  • Wear safety helmets, shoes, earplug and gloves pairs … etc.

  • Isolate the by-pass valve mechanically and electrically. 

  • Be sure that the steam boiler is shutdown.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set.

  • Spray.

  • Wires for lifting.

  • Cleaning articles.

  • Filler.

Work to be Carried out

Before disassembly, you must measure the by-pass stroke and record it.

Disassembly

  1. Disconnect all electrical connections.

  2. Disconnect all hydraulic and pneumatic connections.

  3. Plug all opening immediately.

  4. Check the alignment between the actuator and the valve stem.

  5. Remove stem coupling between actuator and valve stem.

  6. Remove the actuator by the crane.

  7. Insert a cylindrical pin in the hole underneath the stem thread to prevent the stem from sliding through the stuffing box when the bonnet is removed.

  8. Disassemble of the valve components as follow:- see fig. 2

  9. Remove the gland nut (16).

  10. Remove the locking ring (17) and then the gland bush (15).

  11. Loosen and remove bolts(22) and lift off the yoke.

  12. Loosen the bolts (21) in the cover plate (18) and lower bonnet (7) into the valve body.

  13. Remove bolts (21) and cover plate (18).

  14. Remove segment ring (19) by driving through the four holes in the valve body with a mandrel.

  15. Pull the complete valve bonnet out of the valve body by means of hoist.

  16. Remove the plug from the bonnet.

  17. Remove the stuffing box (14) in case of leakage.

  18. Remove the stem guide (8) from the stem if required.

  19. Disassemble the spray nozzle as follow: see fig. 1

    1. Unscrew all bolts (5.1) and packings (5.2), remove flange (5.3).

    2. Loosen and remove nozzle holder (5), spacer ring (5.4) and packing (5.5) by mounting one of bolts (5.1)in the center hole of the nozzle holder and pull out the nozzle holder.

    3. Release the locking of the nozzle (4) by bending back the lock washer (5.6) and unscrew the nozzle.

Inspection

  1. Inspect the cracks in the valve interior. If there, remove them by grinding and recheck with fluid penetrate test (the limit of cracks is 3 mm).

  2. Remove by lapping with cast iron disc any small impressions in the seat sealing surface resulting from clamped-in foreign particles.

  3. Check corrosion inside the valve. If there, remove it by cleaning with no corrosive products or by lapping with cast iron disc.

  4. Inspect the valve seat, if there is any damages or cracks, retreat it by grinding or machining.

  5. Replace the valve seat if it cannot be reconditioning.

  6. Check the valve bonnet for damages and treat it by grinding or machining.

  7. Inspect packing (13) and replace it if required.

  8. Replace the stuffing box (14) in case of leakage.

  9. Replace the stem guide (8) if it is required.

  10. Put a new gasket (6) in the spray nozzle.

  11. Replace the packing (5.5) in the spray nozzle if it is required.

  12. Clean all parts before assembly.

  13. Change the plug.

  14. Clean the silencer by air hose, then by solar and rags.

  15. Rub all sliding surfaces carefully with a cloth containing suitable lubricant (Molycote).

Assembly

  1. Install the plug (12) in bonnet (7).

  2. Install the stem guide (8) in bonnet (7).

  3. Insert new stem packing rings (14).
    ► Do not overfill gland with packing rings. Leave at least height of one ring free to accept gland bush (15).

  4. Insert gland bush (15), gland nut (16) and snap ring (17). Tighten gland nut slightly.
    ►  Do not over tighten the gland nut.

  5. Secure stem from dropping through packing box by inserting a pin through the hole in the stem.

  6. Lift the complete bonnet assembly with the stem / plug and slide it carefully into the valve body.

  7. Inspect pressure seal gasket.
    ►Be sure that the stem / plug sets accurately on the valve seat.

  8. Insert spacer ring (20) and segment (19).

  9. Mount cover plate (18).

  10. Screw the bolts (21).

  11. Mount the actuator yoke by means of bolts (22) and tighten them.

Assemble the Spray Nozzle as Follows:

  1. Assemble the nozzle (4) and nozzle holder (5), secure the nozzle with spacer ring (5.6)

  2. Put a new gasket (6) on nozzle holder (5) and insert nozzle stud (5.7)

  3. Replace packing (5.5), mount them together with spacer ring (5.4) and fit flange (5.3) with bolts (5.1).

  4. Install the actuator of the valve.

  5. Check the alignment between actuator and valve stem.

  6. Tight the coupling bolts firmly.

  7. Mount the hydraulic and pneumatic connections.

  8. Mount the electrical connections.
