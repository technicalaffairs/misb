Introduction

This document outlines the major maintenance procedures P1, to be performed every five years as indicated by Y5 in the title block

The HP steam turbine stop valve is used to protect the turbine from any pressure drops. The valve is plugged by closing of a moving actuator on its seat.

Safety Precaution

  • A work permit must be issued and qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required 

  • Wear safety helmets, shoes, earplug and gloves pairs … etc.

  • Be sure that the turbine is out of service and turning gear is off.

  • Be sure that the all electrical & instrumentation cables are completely isolated.

  • Be sure that the fire fighting system is isolated. 

  • Isolate the valve mechanically and electrically.  

  • Be sure that the oil system is completely isolated.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set.

  • Spray.

  • Wires for lifting.

  • Cleaning articles.

  • Filler and vernier.

Work to be Carried out

Disassembly

  1. Dismantle the connecting pipes between HP upper casing and stop/ control valve block.

  2. Disconnect all electrical connections.

  3. Disconnect all hydraulic and pneumatic connections.

  4. Mark exact position of valve and actuator stem in the stem coupling to facilitate reassembly.

  5. Measure the stroke of the valve and record it.

  6. Remove stem coupling between actuator and valve stem.

  7. Remove the actuator by the crane.

  8. Remove the nuts (20012G).

  9. Remove the cup nuts (20012C) by heating.

  10. Unbolt all the bolts (20012A) fix the valve body with the turbine.

  11. Sling and lift the valve body to be disassembled.

Disassembly of the valve Components

  1. Disassemble the heat protector (20043A) by unbolting the screws (20043C) and lift it.

  2. Raise the part (20043E).

  3. Unbolt the bolts (20042W).

  4. Pull up the plug with the bonnet.

  5. Raise the plug from the bonnet by unscrewing it.

  6. Raise the part (20020U).

  7. Take out the bonnet(20020N).

  8. Raise the part (20041A).

  9. Raise the rings ( 20102N & 25102Q) respectively.

  10. Raise the part (20041C).

Inspection

Cleaning

  1. Degrease all parts of the valve with non corrosive product and rags.

  2. Clean carefully all the parts with adequate products and solvents.

  3. Clean  all screws, pins and nuts by solar.

Visual examination

  1. Examine visually the valve components for wear, cracks, deformations, foreign bodies, etc…… .

  2. Change the plug, metallic rings, steam seal rings and the bonnet.

  3. Machine the valve seat carefully.

N.D.T Inspection

  1. Inspect the valve parts by magnetic test.

  2. Make blue test between the bonnet and the seat.

Sand Blasting

  1. Sand blast the parts of the valve.

  2. Sand blast the bolts, screws and nuts of the valve.

Assembly

Before the assembly, we must coat the mating faces, pins, keys and screws active faces with COPASLIP.

  1. Assemble the part (20041C).

  2. Assemble the rings ( 20102N & 20102Q) respectively.

  3. Assemble the part (20041A).

  4. Lock the part (20041A).

  5. Screw the plug in the bonnet.

  6. Insert the bonnet with the plug in the valve body.

  7. Lock the plug in the bonnet.

  8. Make a blue test to actuate the clearances between the bonnet and the seat.

  9. Tight the screws (20042W).

  10. Put the heat protector (20043A).

  11. Put the part (20043E).

  12. Tight the screws (20043C).

  13. Tight the screws (20010A), put the cup nut (20012C) and tight the nut (20012G). Use a heating plug for tightening.

  14. Assemble the stem coupling between actuator and valve stem.

  15. Assemble the actuator and tight it.

  16. Measure and adjust the stroke of the actuator.

  17. Connect all hydraulic and pneumatic connections.

  18. Connect all electrical connections.

  19. Mantle the connecting pipes between HP upper casing and stop / control valve block.
