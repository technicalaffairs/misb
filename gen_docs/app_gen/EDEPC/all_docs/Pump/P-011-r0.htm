Introduction

This documents outlines the major maintenance procedure P2, which must be performed every five years as indicated by Y5 in the title block.

This pump is used to push the cooling water through heat exchangers to cool the auxiliaries equipment.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear safety and healthy equipment as safety helmets shoes, gloves, … etc

  • Isolated the circuit breaker of the pump motor 

  • Disconnect electric terminals of the Pump Motor.

  • Isolate the Pump mechanically by closing suction and delivery valves.

  • Be Sure that the pump is completely drained.

 

 

 

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set.

  • Cleaning fluid, Filler, Feeler gauge,

  • Rags, Grease

  • Vibrometer, Dial indicator.

Work to be Carried Out

Pump in Operation

  1. Measure Vibration level for bearing and compare it with setting values then analyze it and determine what to do

  2. Check the discharge, pressure of pump and compare it with the setting values the replace the worm components

  3. Check the temperature of bearing house (60°).

Pump out of Operation

Disassembly of the Pump

  1. Check alignment and compare it with setting value.

  2. Dismantle all attached Pipes to the Pump and aux. piping such as oil pipe (190), constant level oilier (251) and water seal pipe (102).

  3. Unscrew motor bolts and take it out.

  4. Disconnect coupling.

  5. Remove gland assembly from Stuffing boxes as follow:
     - Back of nuts (355).
     - Slip the upper washers’ (354) from bosses on gland.
     - Lifts the upper gland half out of stuffing box and removes the gland bolts and lower gland half.

  6. Jack and remove dowel Pins from upper half casing.

  7. Unscrew nuts from casing parting studs.

  8. Loosen upper half-casing (100).

  9. Use the lugs provided for lifting the upper half casing

  10. Unscrew nuts from bearing These cap studs and lift bearing cap (111) from unit.

  11. Caps must be replaced on the same end of Pump from which they were removed.

  12. They should be marked for identification before disassembly.

  13. Carefully lift rotating assembly from unit and Place on padded supports, which not injure the shaft sleeves.

    Note: the distance from the end of the shaft to the face of the Pump half coupling so that the coupling half can be correctly positioned when reassembled.

  14. Pull the coupling half from Pump shaft.

  15. Dismantle coupling key.

  16. Drain the oil from the bearing housing (134).

  17. Unscrew cap screws on each end cover (109) and remove end cover (109) from bearing housing.

  18. Preserve end cover gaskets (360).

  19. Dismantle snap ring (361) from shaft (122).

  20. Dismantle thrust and ball bearing (112 and 137) from shaft seat.

  21. Slide ball bearing collar (237) of the shaft (122).

  22. Slide ball bearing housings (134) from shaft (122).

  23. Dismantle deflectors (123) from shaft (122) and Slip casing wearing rings (103) from impeller (101) and off rotating   element.

  24. Slide packing (106), lantern rings (105) and stuffing box bushings (125) off ends of shaft (122)

  25. Smooth the exposed portions of the shaft at the end of the sleeves with fine emery cloth so that sleeve will not bind while being removed

  26. Mark the location of the keyway on the out side of both the impeller hub and the locking sleeve (104) at Point (A) and (B)

  27. Measure clearance of all diametral running of the pump

  28. Unscrew the shaft sleeves (126) from shaft (122) with a pin Spencer.

  29. Tap the Impeller (101) from shaft with lead mall.

  30. Dismantle Key (178) from Key way.

  31. Unscrew and remove shaft sleeve (104) by hand or with a strop wrench.

  32. Take care shaft to sleeve threads is right hand

Inspection

  1. Inspect the validity of thrust and ball bearing (112 -137) for wear and corrosion

  2. Check all parts for any worn – out and renewed if it is need.

  3. Check the suction and delivery Pipes for creep.

  4. Check all parts for cracks. and renewed it need

  5. Inspect the run – out of shaft (permissible limit)

  6. Check the impeller wearing rings dimension clearance and renewed if it is need

  7. Check the impeller for correct setting and renewed it if need

  8. Change (O) ring (412 A).

  9. Change (360) and oil seal (332, 333).

Assembly of the Pump

  1. Clean all contact surfaces before mounting.

  2. Determine the correct setting of the impeller (distance B) and the sleeves on the shaft in relation to the rotation of the Pump.

  3. Assemble the shaft sleeve (104) that has a keyway in the thread end.

  4. Place  O - Ring ( 412A ) in the groove in end of shaft sleeve  (104) Assemble first the shaft sleeve )

  5. Slide Impeller on shaft and tap in place against the sleeve with a Lead mall.

  6. Place  O - Ring  ( 412 ) in the groove in end of shaft sleeve ( 126 )

  7. Screw shaft sleeve on shaft up to key.

  8. Slip wearing rings on impeller.

  9. Place stuffing box bushing (125) on shaft.

  10. Place deflector’s (123) on shaft.

  11. Wipe a small amount of grease of on the oil seal lips in a bearing housing.

  12. Place bearing housing on shaft and slide them along unit they contact the shaft sleeves.

  13. Slide ball bearing collar (237) over thrust end of shaft (122) and push up against shaft shoulder.

  14. Install the thrust and coupling end ball bearing on either end of shaft.

  15. Place snap ring (361) in grove in shaft on thrust bearing end.

  16. Oil shaft extension and slide coupling and bearing and cover (119) with gasket (360) in place.

  17. Assemble the pump half coupling.

  18. Bolt the thrust end Bearing end cover to the bearing housing.

  19. Smooth up and clean casing wearing ring, stuffing box bushing and bearing housing seats in upper and Lower half casing.

  20. Check and measure clearance of all diametral running of the pump.

  21. Check the rotating elements for free turning.

  22. Assemble bearing caps (111) and tighten the nuts evenly.

  23. Check again for free turning of the rotating.

  24. Repack stuffing boxes and replace gland assemblies.

  25. Check coupling alignment.

  26. Connect coupling.

  27. Oil pump bearings.

  28. Connect auxiliary piping such as oil pipe (190) – constant level oilier (251) and water seal pipe (102)

Staring of Pump

  1. Check the direction of rotation of pump.

  2. Opens the cooling water to each gland and the quantity of water is sufficient.

  3. Checks the level of oil in constant level oilier for thrust bearing.

  4. Priming of the pump by open suction valve, close discharge valve and open vent valve until all airs expelled and water Flows through opening.

  5. Open the delivery valve gradually after the pump has been started to operate at its full speed.

Attendance and check during operation

  1. Check the discharge, pressure and vibration during operation.

  2. Check the oil level in the constant level oilier.

  3. Check the temperature of cooling water at discharge.

  4. Check the temperature of thrust bearing housing and thrust bearing end cover should not exceed 60°
