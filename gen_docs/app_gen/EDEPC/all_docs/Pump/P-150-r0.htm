Introduction

This document outlines the minor maintenance procedure P1, which must be performed every five years as indicated by Y5 in above title block.

This pump is used for lubricating bearings and to provide seal oil to the generator shaft seals.

Pump Technical Specification:

Type Vertically Sub Merged Lube oil Pumps Centrifugal - Single stage ( VCRE )
Rated pressure 2 Kg / cm2
Flow 50 gpm
Efficiency at rating 36.5 %
Temperature 60 °C ( 140 °F )
Pumps Speed 960 rpm
Bearing No. Group L02 double row bearing construction

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and qualified maintenance stuff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Shut down the unit.

  • Lock the condensate pumps ( 1&2 ) switches, close isolate valves and isolate the power.

  • Lock the vacuum pumps ( 1&2 ) switches, close isolate valves and isolate the power.

  • Isolate compressed air for all valves.

  • Switch off gland exhaust and oil vapor extractor.

  • Switch off ventilation system.

  • Generator should be isolated.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set

  • Cleaning fluid, manual puller,

  • Screw driver set

  • Filler gauge, Rags

  • Dial indicator with magnetic base

Work to be carried out

Pump in Service

  1. Record pump pressure reading

  2. Check discharge quantity

  3. Check and record the vibration level of the pump.

Disassembly ( Refer to figures 1 , 2 )

  1. Unbolt the driver and remove it.

  2. Remove the pump assembly from the oil tank.

  3. Remove bolts ( 102 ) and lock washers ( 124 ) holding strainer ( 59 ) to casing ( 2 ) remove strainer ( 59 ) .

  4. Remove discharge pipe bolts ( 108 ) and nuts ( 232 ) break seal at discharge flange .

  5. Remove oil circulation line ( 76 ) by loosing tube fitting (78). Do not allow the line to kink as it is used to lubricate the bearing and must be free of restriction.

  6. Loosen cap screws ( 102E ) .

  7. Tighten jack screws ( 242 ) evenly and break joint between casing cover ( 6 ) and casing ( 2 ) . Remove casing ( 2 ) .

  8. Remove tru  arc  retaining ring ( 83 ) .

  9. Remove flex loc nut ( 15 ) and washer ( 16 ) .

  10. Remove impeller ( 3 ) and feather key ( 17 ) .

  11. Remove cap screw ( 102 E ) .

  12. Remove casing cover ( 6 ) with guide bearing (11B) from connecting pipe (50).

  13. The lower guide bearing should be replaced when diametrical clearance is double the initial value of point (0.008 inches).

Upper Thrust Ball Bearing (27)

  1. Remove pump half of coupling (68). This may require a puller to use, be certain pull is even, steady, and parallel to the shaft to avoid possible damage to the shaft or other parts.

  2. Remove cap screw (102C). Connecting pipe (50) with shaft (7) and upper bearing (27) is now free of cover plate (56) and may be removed.

  3. Remove cap screws (102A), bearing cover (24), and disengaged and bearing locknut (29) removed.

  4. Remove shaft (7) with bearing assembly (27) from connecting pipe (50).

  5. Remove bearing (27) from shaft (7) with a shop press. Remove spacer(s) (41) and save them for possible reuse.

  6. Bearing seals (30) and (30A) should be removed and replaced.

Inspection

  1. Inspect the ball bearing (27) for damage.

  2. Inspect the wearing rings (9 & 10) for wear or damage.

  3. Inspect the impeller for wear or damage.

  4. Inspect the shaft for trueness with a dial indicator it must be true within (0.002 inches).

  5. Check shaft run-out (not exceed 0.002 inches).

Reassembly

  1. Install new wearing rings ( 9 ) and ( 10 ) in their respective bores in the casing (2) and casing cover (6).

  2. Tap bearing seal ( 30 A ) into bearing cover ( 24 ) using a resilient mallet. Lubricate the sealing lip with bearing grease.

  3. Slide bearing bushing ( 11 B ) in casing cover ( 6 ) and secure it with retaining clip ( 238B ). Lock washer ( 124 ) and cap screw ( 102 ).

  4. Attach casing cover ( 6 ) to connecting pipe ( 50 ) with cap screw ( 102 E ) , being sure the rabbet fit is properly engaged so that the gasket seat is square with the axis.

  5. Before mounting parts on the shaft. Clean shaft and parts thoroughly. Apply clean machine oil to the shaft surface on which the part is to be mounted.

  6. Install the thrust ball bearing ( 27 ). It should slide snugly into the bore in the connecting pipe ( 50 ) and have an inter furnace fit on the shaft ( 7 ).

  7. Slide shaft into connecting pipe ( 50 ) through the throat at the thrust bearing end and carefully enter it through the bearing bushing ( 11 B ). Lubricate the sealing lip on bearing seal ( 30 ) and tap it into the upper connecting pipe flange using a resilient punch.

  8. Tap the impeller feather key ( 17 ) into the key way in the shaft ( 7 ) being sure it does not extend beyond the shoulder at the thread

  9. Line up the key and key way in the impeller ( 3 ) and slide or tap it into the shaft all the way to the shoulder secure the impeller with the washer ( 16 ). Lock nut ( 15 ) and retaining ring ( 83 ) .

  10. Determine the required thickness for the spacer ( 41 ) such that the axial clearance between the casing cover ( 6 ) and impeller is 3/32" to 1/8" this is important to control axial bearing load . A piece of key stock can be used to make a tool for this purpose , Grind the ends so it will drop into the bearing bore in the connecting pipe and reset on the shoulder at the bottom of the bore while being close to the bearing seat on the shaft. Set the impeller at its proper location and measure the distance between the bottom of the bearing bore and shaft shoulder below the bearing. This then is the required thickness of spacer ( 41 ).

  11. The thrust bearing ( 27 ) must be packed with grease prior to assembly . The upper bearing cavity ( inside bearing cover ) should also be filled with grease prior to slipping the cover on the shaft .

  12. Lubricate the bore of the ball thrust bearing ( 27 ) with light machine oil and bump it into the shaft using a heavy mallet and tube with even contact on the inner race only or use shop press. Be sure it is all the way on and firmly against spacer ( 41 ) .

  13.  Install the bearing lock washer ( 28 ) and lock nut ( 29 ) be sure the lock nut (29) clamps the inner race of the ball thrust bearing ( 27 ) . Bend one tab on lock washer ( 28 ) fully into a mating slot on lock nut ( 29 ).

  14. Bearing cover ( 24 ) must limit end play to within 0.003  0.005 inches and also provide a water tight seal against gasket ( 65d ) . Install the bearing cover ( 24 ) with the proper thickness gasket ( 65D ) under it and secure  it with cop screws ( 102A ).

  15. Install grease cup ( 32A ).

  16. Pump clearance (not exceed 0.12 inch).

  17. Tap coupling key ( 17A ) into the key way in shaft (7) and slide pump half coupling ( 68 ) into shaft (7) and lighten the set screw (73) .

  18. Join the pit cover  motor stand ( 56 ) to the connecting pipe with lock washers ( 124 ) and cop screws ( 102C ) .

  19. Cement gasket ( 65 & 65E ) in place on the casing ( 2 ). Take scale measurements to be sure the wearing ring will engage the impeller journal by least 3/16".

  20. Install casing ( 2 ) and secure with bolt lugs ( 240 ) and cap screws ( 102F ) install and tighten discharge flange bolts ( 10G ) and nuts ( 232 ).

  21. Install oil circulation line (76) with fittings (78).

  22. Install strainer (59) and secure it with lock washers (124) or locking plates (131) and bolts (102).

  23. Rotate pump rotor by hand. It should rotate freely.

  24. Tap coupling key (17) into the key way in the motor shaft and slide coupling half (68) on the driver and bolt the driver to the pit cover motor stand.

  25. Install the assembled unit in the tank and connect the discharge pipe.

  26. Adjust the coupling alignment.

  27. Make the electrical connections pump the motor electrically and check for proper rotation.

Starting the Pump

Before starting the pump, the following steps should be checked:

  1. Check the direction of rotation of pump for starting the pump for the first time.

  2. Check the quantity of oil in the thrust ball  bearing pump.

  3. Open the inlet for cooling the oil bath of the thrust ball- bearing.

  4. Open the suction and delivery valves of the pump

  5. Connect electric terminal of the pump motor.

  6. Put the circuit breaker of the pump motor in service.

Attendance and Checking during Operation

During operation of the pump, the following should be checked:

  1. The bearing temperature

  2. Check pump suction & discharge pressure.
