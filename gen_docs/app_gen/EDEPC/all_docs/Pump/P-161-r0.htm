Introduction

This document outlines the major maintenance procedures P1, to be performed every five years as indicated by Y5 in the above title block.

Technical specification

Type of pump: 250-QVD-570-50-LU
Design:  Horizontal centrifugal pump
Pump capacity: 756 m3/h ( 210 l/s )
Specific energy 90 m w.c. ( 883 J/kg )
Pump Speed  980 rpm.
Pump power  315Kw
Bearing type: 6314 – 7314

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear safety and healthy equipments as safety healthy, safety shoes, ...etc.

  • Isolate the circuit breaker of the pump motor.

  • Disconnect electric terminals of the pump motor.

  • The pump must be isolated mechanically by closing delivery and suction valves.

  • Be sure pump must be completely drained.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set.

  • Cleaning fluid, Manual puller,

  • Feeler gauge, Dial indicator.

  • Vibrometer, Straight edge.

  • Rags

Work to be carried out

Pump in Operation

  1. Check discharge quantity and pressure and record them.

  2. Check the vibration level of the pump set complete with the motor then, record them.

Pump Out Of Operation

  1. Check alignment and record it.

  2. Disconnect the coupling between pump and motor.

  3. Disconnect the suction, discharge and auxiliary piping.

  4. Dismount the pump from the bedplate and place it on erection pads.

  5. Disconnect the pump half coupling.

  6. Disconnect the bearing housings.

  7. Disconnect the stuffing boxes.

  8. Disconnect the casing covers.

  9. Remove the complete pump rotor from the lower casing.

  10. Dismount the shaft sleeves from both side of the rotor.

  11. Dismount the impeller from the rotor.

  12. During disassembly, ensure that the individual parts are not damaged.

Inspection

  1. Check the validity of the anti-friction ball bearings.

  2. Check all parts for any worn-out and replace them by new ones.

  3. Inspect the run-out of shafts (allowance 0.03 mm).

Assembly

  1. Check the run-out of the rotor (must not exceed a value of 0.03mm during the measurement).

  2. Assemble the impeller, the shaft sleeves the gland follower with sealing rings and nuts.

  3. Check the run-out of the impeller sealing rings (must not exceed a value of 0.06mm) and the run-out of the gland bushes must not exceed 0.03mm.

  4. Assemble the bearings with the inserted ring.

  5. Fit the sealing rings into the pump covers.

  6. Lock the sealing rings by locking set screw.

  7. Secure the locking screws against spontaneous screwing out by centers.

  8. Mount the casing cover from the side of the drive including the stuffing box and the intermediate piece.

  9. Put the assembled rotor without bearings into the pump.

  10. Mount the casing cover from the opposite side of the drive including the stuffing box and the intermediate piece.

  11. Adjust the axial clearance of the rotor (0.1-0.2mm) via the spacer rings of the bearings.

  12. Assemble completely the pump including the bearing from the drive side after adjusting the rotor in the center position, then mount the spacing thrower ring on the side of the angular contact bearing.

  13. Cover the bearings and mount the pump half coupling.

  14. Lift the pump and put it on its place, then adjust the alignment (be sure pump rotating freely by hand, otherwise dismantle again and treat the causes).

Starting Of Pump

  1. Check the direction of rotation of pump before starting the pump for the first time.

  2. Check the quantity of grease in the thrust-bearing sump.

  3. Check whether the inlet for flooding the stuffing box is open.

  4. Check whether the inlet for hydraulic sealing of the suspension is open.

  5. Check whether the delivery sluice valve is closed.

  6. Open the delivery valve gradually after the set has been started to operate at its full speed.

Attendance and Checking During Operation

During operation, the following should be checked:

  1. The temperature of the anti-friction bearing in the bearing top which should not exceed 75°C.

  2. The temperature of cooling water at discharge.
