Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear safety and healthy equipments as safety healthy, safety shoes, ...etc.

  • Unit out of service.

  • Isolate the circuit breaker of the pump motor

  • Disconnect electric terminal of the pump motor

  • The pump must be isolated mechanically by closing delivery and suction valves

  • Be sure pump  must be completely drained

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set.

  • Cleaning fluid, manual puller, Rages

  • Feeler gauge & dial indicator

Work to be Carried Out

Disassembly

  1. Disassembly of the pump for maintenance, repair, or replacement should be done to the point required for the service desired.

  2. Drain system prior to any disassembly . complete drainage is not required drain only to level necessary to prevent leakage when disassembled . remove pipe plug # 31 B to drain pump .

  3. Remove seal ( 9 - ¼) inch circulating line with or without filter furnished by other than buffalo pump div. inspect line connection openings and filter if furnished for any possible clogging or build up or deposits which may have occurred prior to reassembly .

  4. Remove combination casing cover and suction elbow ( 5 ) by removing bolts and cap screws (102) care must be taken not to damage casing cover ring (10) when removing .

  5. Remove tru-arc snap ring (83) impeller lock nut (15) and lock washer .

  6. Remove impeler (3) the  5/16” balance holes have been tapped for an impeler  puller  Use plate type puller bearing an shaft extension .

  7. Removal of cartridge seal from shaft .

  8. Remove cap screws (102C) and remove shaft (7) using Jack Screws .

  9. Remove adaptor (60) this completes pump disassembly .

  10. Refer repair – replacement instructions prior to reassembly .

  11. Refill system and check for leaks before operating

Assembly

  1. Clean all parts thoroughly and check for burrs and damage before assembly .

  2. Install throat bushing with seal (11A) in adopter (60) with sealing ring pin (136) locking plates (131) and machine screws (156) after tightening machine screws bend tab of locking  plates against screw heads .

  3. Install g- inch diameter asbestos gasket (65C) on adopter (60) face , using graphite and oil on one side and shellac on the back . Assemble adopter (60) to mounting face of turbine accessory gear casing .

  4. Coat stub end of pump shaft (17) with a thin coat of clean white lead and mount shaft in  gear shaft socket with cap screws (102C ) tighten screws evenly

  5. Check pump shaft for excessvnout and proper clearance maximum all an able radial shaft run out at mechanical seal location in 0.005 inch .

  6. After shaft alignment and clearance have been set lock cap screws (102C) un place by up setting surrounding metal with a prick punch .

  7. Coat outside diameter of casing wearing ring (9) with thin coat of clean with thin coat of clean white lead and oil .

  8. Install john crane mechanical seal (82) this seal is designed to be installed as a complete  package unit and must not be disassembled prior to assembly into pump .

  9. Assemble impeller (3) to shaft unit lock washer (16) & lock nut (15) lock impeller assembly in place with truarc ring (83)

  10. Coat out side diameter of casing cover ring (10) with this coat of clean white lead and tap ring into bone of casing cover (5) be sure ring seated firmly and squarely .

  11. Inst all gasket (65A) 11”  O.D. on 3” pump and 11-3/4” O.D. on 5” pumps on casing cover (5) in the same manner as step above . Assemble casing cover (5) to casing (2) with cap screws (102) .

  12. Install air cock (67) and pipe plugs (31B , 74 and 74A )   .

  13. Seal circulating line , with or without filter furnished by other than buffalo pump div., must be installed before operation .

  14. This completes the assembly of the pump.

  15. Check shaft clearance as in step 4 above
