Introduction

This document out lines the major maintenance procedure P1, which must be performed for every five years as indicated Y5 in the above title block.

This Pump is used to transfer the coolant water to condenser for condensing the steam from turbine to condensate water and also transfer water for closed cooling system.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear safety and healthy equipment as safety helmets shoes, gloves, … etc

  • Isolated the circuit breaker of the pump motor 

  • Disconnect electric terminals of the Pump Motor.

  • Isolate the Pump mechanically by closing delivery valves.

  • Isolate cooling water for the pump.

 

 

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set.

  • Cleaning fluid, Filler, Feeler gauge,

  • Rags, Grease

  • Vibrometer, Dial indicator

  • Lifting equipment.

Work to be Carried Out

Pump in Operation

  1. Measure Vibration level for bearing and compare it with setting values then analyze it and determine what to do

  2. Check the discharge, pressure of pump and compare it with the Setting values the replace the worm components

  3. Check the quantity, pressure of cooling water for bearing of pump and compare it with setting value,

  4. And determine what to do

Pump out of Operation

  1. The pump consists of a bowl assembly, column assembly and discharge head assembly

  2. Bowl assembly includes the suction bell (086) the impeller case (077) the top case (076) the pump shaft (167) and the impeller (176)

  3. Column assembly implorers the bottom column (422) the column bearing (383) the column shaft (400) and the column shaft coupling.  

Disassembly of the pump

  1. Dismantle any auxiliary piping and  wiring that would interfere with disassembly

  2. Check of alignment of coupling

  3. Dismantle pump half coupling (530 ) from driver half coupling (529 )

  4. Unscrew motor bolts and take it out

  5. Measure the pump clearance and recorded it

  6. Dismantle adjusting plate (532 ), pump half coupling (530 ) and pump half coupling  keys (676- 3)

  7. Dismantle the main discharge piping from discharge head (465) and put flange

  8. Dismantle discharge head (465) from foundation plate (429)

  9. Lift the pump assembly a few feet and support it by I -beams placed across the foundation opening to catch   

  10. Dismantle the stuffing box packing assembly (050)

  11. The lifting lugs of the column

  12. Thread lifting eye bolt into head shaft (404) and attach lifting line with some slack

  13. Attach separate lifting lines to lifting lugs of discharge head (465) and disconnect discharge head from column (422) and lift discharge head only ( not shaft ) straight up l or 2 feet and disconnect the shaft coupling

  14. Lower the discharge head monetarily to take up slack in shaft lifting line.

  15. Lift the discharge head and shaft clear of the foundation

  16. Remove any remaining shaft coupling parts from the head shaft lower end

  17. Place discharge head to rest on wood blocks and disconnect its lifting lines

  18. Lift the head shaft clear of the discharge head

  19. Remove the remaining assembly from the pit. by using column lifting lugs,

  20. Place the assembly horizontally on suitable v- block supports

  21. Disconnect outer column (422) from top case (076) and carefully remove the column along the shaft

  22. Disconnect and remove coupling and column shaft

  23. Place the pump bowl assembly in operating position

  24. Resting the suction bell (086) on suitable wood block supports

  25. Dismantle the splitter plate (630)

  26. Detach and remove the top case (076) and lifting the case squarely to avoid binding and possible bearing damage

  27. Using a lifting eye bolt threaded into top end of pump shaft (167)

  28. Lift and remove pump shaft and attached impeller from assembled impeller case (077) and suction bell (086)

  29. Remove in sequence the socket head cap screws

  30. Dismantle sand cap (244), split ring (256-1) ,  impeller (176)and key (676-1)

  31. Dismantle retaining screws and use back off screws to free the parts

  32. Dismantle the pump bearings and wear rings

  33. Measure clearance of all diametral running of the pump and compare it with the setting value (such as   suction bell bearing (104), top case bearing (397), column bearing (383), stuffing box bushing (233), and wear ring).

Inspection

  1. Inspect all O-rings and packing rings removed during disassembly for wearing.

  2. Clean and wash all contact parts and dry it using compressed air

  3. Inspect each part for wear, erosion or corrosion and replace it if it is needed.

  4. Check the diametric running clearance specification and replace all excessively worn part

  5. Check all threads to insure that there are no incompletely cut thread, burr or metallic slivers and replace any bolting which does not pass visual inspection

  6. Check the run out of shaft and record it.

Assembly of the Pump

  1. Check and measure clearance of all diametric running of the pump 

  2. Install the pump bearing and wear rings

  3. Check that all retaining screws are securely tightened

  4. Install the impeller key (676-1) in shaft key way

  5. Slide the impeller into shaft past split ring groove

  6. Install split ring (256-1) and slide impeller down to seat against the split ring

  7. Install secure sand cap (244) to the impeller using socket head cap screws with lock washers

  8. Install suction bell (086) and impeller case (077)

  9. Install assembled suction bell and impeller case in operating position on wood block supports

  10. Using a lifting eye bolt in pump shaft (167)

  11. Install the assembled pump shaft (167) and impeller into the assembled impeller case (077) suction bell (086)

  12. Install tops case (076) over the pump shaft to mate with impeller case (077)

  13. assemble the retaining cap screws lock washers and nuts

  14. Place the pump bowl assembly horizontally as level as possible on wood block supports.

  15. Grease suction bell bearing (104) by removing pipe plug from suction bell bearing housing (grade2)

  16. Change the position of the pump bowl assembly from horizontal to vertical

  17. Assemble inner column connector (612) to pump top case

  18. Place two inner column -o-rings (747-1) into position on inner column connector (612)

  19. Thread lifting eye bolt into the tap and of column head shaft (400) and attach a lifting line

  20. Lift the shaft (400) and position it approximately two feet above the pump shaft (167)

  21. Install coupling part and assemble the column shaft (400) to the pump shaft (167)

  22. Threaded up tightly by the coupling nut (255)

  23. Remove set screw (795) and drill small holes in the shaft, on screw center line, large enough to dog point.

  24. Replace the set screw tightly and peen to prevent loosening.

  25. Attach lifting lines to the lifting lugs of the bottom column (422)

  26. Lift and position the bottom column over the top of the column haft (400).

  27. Align match marks and slowly lower the column into the flange of the bowl assembly.

  28. Assembly and torque the retaining cap screws, lock washers and nuts.

  29. Lift the pump bowl column assembly, lower it into the foundation opening and support it by I –beams placed     

  30. Across the foundation opening to catch the column lifting lugs.

  31. Dismantle lifting equipment.

  32. Install column bearing (383) and check the bearing retaining screws are securely tightened.

  33. Install two inner column -O- rings (747-1) into their grooves at the top end of the column.

  34. Thread lifting eye bolts into the top end of head shaft (404) and attach a lifting line.

  35. Lift the head shaft (404) and positions it two feet above the column shaft (400).

  36. Install coupling parts and assemble the head shaft to the column shaft

  37. Drill small holes for coupling set screws and peen the set screw to prevent loosing

  38. Attach lifting lines to the lifting lugs of the discharge head (465) lift and position it over the top of the head shaft (404)

  39. Check the mating flange is clean and free of damage

  40. Carefully lower the discharge head (465) to mate with the column

  41. Assemble and torque the retaining cap screws, lock washers and nuts

  42. Lift the pump assembly to remove the supports

  43. Check that top surface of foundation plate (429) and bottom surface of discharge head outer flange are clean and free damage  

  44. Lower the pump into position at foundation plate (429) and secure with lock washers and stud nuts

  45. Remove lifting equipment

  46. Install the stuffing box packing assembly (050)

  47. Install the gland studs nuts.

  48. Measure the pump clearance and recorded it.

  49. Support the driver vertically, shaft facing down and the thoroughly clean the shaft end meant surfaces

  50. Connect the main discharge piping to the discharge head out put flange

  51. Connect any auxiliary piping, wiring and instruments

  52. Install key in driver shaft key way, press the driver half coupling all the way up on driver shaft

  53. Insert split ring and pull the drive half coupling down to set firmly against the split ring

  54. Install key (s) in shaft, Slide on pump haft coupling and thread on adjusting plate (532)

  55. Install driver on pump and temporarily tighten the mounting fasteners

  56. Check the alignment of coupling and record it.

  57. Insert two of coupling bolts and tighten progressively until secure

  58. Thereby closing the gap above the adjusting plate and raising the impeller (s) to running position

  59. Insert the remaining coupling bolts and tighten all bolts securing

Starting of the Pump

Before The Starting the Pump the Fool Awing Steps Must Be Checked

  1. Open the cooling lines to gland and the quantity of water is sufficient (8 – 10 GPM)

  2. Check the direction of rotation of pump

  3. Check the level of the sump

  4. Priming of the pump by filing the tunnel between pump and condenser by screen travel pump

  5. Check the level of oil in bearing of driver

  6. Open the delivery valve gradually after the pump has been started to operate at its full Speed.

Attends and Checking During Operation

  1. Check the discharge, pressure and vibration during operation

  2. Check the temperature of cooling water at discharge

  3. Check the quantity and pressure for cooling water

  4. Check the leakage from stuffing box
