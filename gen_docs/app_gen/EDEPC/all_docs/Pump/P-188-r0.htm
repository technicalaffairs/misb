Introduction

This document outlines the major maintenance procedures P1, which must be performed every five years as indicated by Y5 in the title block.

Safety Precautions

  • A work order must be issued.

  • The maintenance staff should be qualified and carry the suitable safety ID category.

  • Wear safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes …etc

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required       

  • Unit out of service.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set

  • Torque wrench, Hammer, Dial indicator

  • Cleaning fluid, Manual puller

  • Suitable Lifting device, Feeler gauge

Work to be Carried Out 

Disassembly  ( see figure 1 )

  1. Remove the nuts from the gland bolts 17B and remove glands (17) from the shaft (6) The gland halves are separable .

  2. Remove all nuts or cap screws from the upper casing (1B) and from the bearing caps (41).

  3. Screw down the jack screws to separate the upper and lower casings. turn the jack screws back after the case halves have separated to avoid interference  at reassembly.

  4. Match mark and remove both bearing caps (41).

  5. Attach hoist to the eye bolt (S) to lift upper casing (1B).

  6. Place slings around the shaft near the bearing housings and lift rotating element from lower casing (1A) tap lightly on the underside of the bearing housings to separate the housings from the brackets.

  7. Place rotating element in a convenient work place.

Rotating Element

Disassemble

  1. Loosen set screw and remove the coupling half 2880550 tap from the back of the hub or use a puller. Remove coupling key (46).

  2. Take out cap screws to remove bearing covers ( 35 , 37 ) and the gasket (73B).

  3. Loosen and remove bearing locknut (22) and lock washer (69A).

  4. Remove housings (31), bearings (16) and deflectors (40A), as units with a bearing puller.

  5. Withdraw casing rings (7) on most pumps these may be withdrawn before removing the coupling half.

  6. Remove packing (13), lantern rings (29) and stuffing box bushings (63). Make note of the number of packing rings on each side of the lantern rings.

  7. Clean all metal parts ( except bearings ) with a solvent.

  8. Use a bristle brush ( not metal or wire ) to remove tightly adhering deposits, a fiber scraper may be used to remove the gasket and shellac from casing flanges.

    1. Blow dry with clean dry compressed air.

    2. Clean the bearing as described in bulletin No. 2880549.

Assembly

Rotating Element   (See figure 1 )

Reassemble

  1. Coat the shaft ( 6 ) lightly with oil.

  2. Place impeller keys (32) on shaft.

  3. Install O-rings in shaft sleeves ( 14 ), coat with oil and screw sleeves on shaft to loosely butt the impeller hub.

  4. Install thee stuffing box bushing ( 63 ) and lantern ring (29) on the shaft. If a mechanical seal is used, install according to the seal manufacturer’s instructions.

  5. Locate casing rings (7) on impeller.

  6. Place water deflectors (40A) on shaft.

  7. Insert bearings (16), shield side first, into housings (31).

  8. Press housing – bearing assemblies on shaft to bottom with water deflectors refer to bulletin no. 2880549 for procedure ( mounting bearings ).

  9. Install bearing lock washer bearing.

  10. Install gaskets (73B) on bearing covers. Cut replacement gaskets from 1/16 inch No. 444 vellumoid.

  11. Attach bearing covers (35, 37) in the assembled position the grease fittings must be located on top. Install coupling key; assemble coupling half on the shaft and tighten the setscrew.

Assembly

  1. Use the upper casing (1B) as a template to cut a gasket (73A) from 1/64 inch vellumoid. Machined surfaces of both casings must be perfectly clean and free from burrs and nicks.

  2. Affix the new gasket to lower casing (1A) with shellac.

  3. Use slings around the shaft near bearings to set rotating element into lower casing. Position the casing rings (7) and both bearing housings so that all dowel pins engage.

  4. Assemble both bearing caps and tighten the cap screws.

  5. Adjust the shaft sleeves (14) to center the impeller in the lower casing. Tighten the setscrews. Make sure that impeller is centered.

  6. Cover the top side of the casing gasket with a mixture of graphite and oil. Install the gland bolts. carefully locate the upper casing on the lower, making certain the dowel pins engage. Attach case nuts and alternately tighten at diagonally opposite positions rotate shaft by hand to check that it runs free.

  7. Withdraw the lantern rings (29) and push the bushings (63) to the rear of the stuffing boxes. Insert the same number of packing ring as were found during disassembly, on each side of the lantern ring. Insert each ring separately and stagger the joints of successive rings 90°. Insert the glands (17) and set the nuts finger tight – do not use a wrench.

  8. Rotate shaft by hand to check that it runs free.

  9. Replace all drain plugs removed during disassembly.

  10. Re-lubricate the bearings refer to bulletin no. 2880549.

  11. Follow instructions in bulletin No. 2880549 to check out the pump after repair and place the pump in service.
