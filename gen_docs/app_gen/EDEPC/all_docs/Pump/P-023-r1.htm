Introduction

This document outlines the major maintenance procedure P1, which must be performed on an annually bases Y1 or at pump pressure < 90 kg/cm2 as indicated in the above titled block

This pump supply hydraulic oil for steam valves.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance stuff should be carry the suitable safety category

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required

  • Wear your safety helmet, safety shoes …. etc

  • Isolate the circuit breaker of the pump motor

  • Disconnect electric terminals of the pumps motor

  • The pump must be isolated mechanically by closing delivery and suction valves

  • Be sure pump must be completely drained

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set

  • Cleaning fluid.

Work to be Carried Out

Disassembly

  1. Check pump pressure value major maintenance (Setting value 112 kg/cm2)

  2. Remove pressure compensator cartridge and remove volume limit assembly.

  3. Remove compensator control piston & volume limit control limit.

  4. Remove (8) 3/8" x 31/2" socket head cap screw and remove (8) copper washers

  5. Separate drive cover and port end cover

  6. Lift off drive end cover assembly and remove retaining ring and lift out

  7. Remove (7) pistons and hold down plate

  8. Remove thrust plate

  9. Remove seal retaining ring and remove drive shaft seal

  10. Remove drive shaft retaining ring

  11. Remove the drive shaft from drive cover assembly.

  12. Remove the trnnino nut assembly

  13. Remove the thrust washers

  14. Remove reaction plate

  15. Remove rationing ring

  16. Remove (2) roller bearing and needle bearing.

  17. Remove (7) piston springs from rotor and remove (7) piston seats from rotor.

  18. Remove valve plate from port end cover and remove O-ring from port end cover.

Inspection

  1. Inspect the piston and rotor for wear or damage.

  2. Inspect the end play of ball socket joint in piston and shoe for wear or damage.

  3. Inspect the back side shoe for wear or damage.

  4. Inspect the thrust plate for wear and deformation.

  5. Inspect hold down plate for wear and deformation.

  6. Inspect valve plate for wear and deformation.

  7. Inspect parts of compensatory for wear or damage. 

  8. Inspect of tapered roller and needle bearing for damage. 

Reassembly

  1. Install (2) roller bearing cups and cones in drive end cover.

  2. Install retaining ring and reaction plate and thrust washers on trunnion.

  3. Install trunnion nut assemblies.

  4. Tighten 2 3/8" trunnion nuts and insert drive shaft to point of inter perence fit.

  5. Install the shaft into the place.

  6. Install bearing retraining ring.

  7. Install shaft seal.

  8. Install thrust plate in reaction plate then install (7) piston and shoe assemblies and hold down plate and hold down plate retaining ring.

  9. Install position of retaining ring and install valve plate.

  10. Install (7) spring seats in bottom of piston bores then install rotor in position on valve plate

  11. Install (7) piston springs in rotor bores and install O-ring

  12. Install drive end sub-assembly to port end sub assembly.

  13. Install (8) 3/8" x 3½" socket head cap screw be sure copper washers are under the head of each cap screw.

  14. Install volume limit control piston in 1/2 " control bore install control piston and starter spring in 11/16" control bore.

  15. Install volume limit adjustment parts, and install compensator

  16. Check the direction of pump rotation before starting the pump for the first time.

  17. Check pump pressure after maintenance above (112 Kg/Cm2)

Starting of Pump

  1. Check the direction of rotation of pump before starting the pump for the first time.

  2. Open the suction and delivery valve.

Attendance and Checking during Operation

  1. Check the temperature of the anti-friction bearing in the bearing top.

  2. Check the temperature of cooling water at discharge.
