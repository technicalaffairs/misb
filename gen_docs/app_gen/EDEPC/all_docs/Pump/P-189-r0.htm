Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category

  • Safety fence with caution marks to surround the work area is required.

  • Wear safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes ... etc.

  • Isolate the circuit breaker for the pump motor.

  • The pump must be isolated mechanically by closing suction and delivery valves.

  • Unit out of service.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set

  • Cleaning fluid, Soft brush

  • Suitable lifting device, Feeler gauge

  • Hammer, Manual puller

  • Dial indicator

  • Torque wrench

Work to be Carried Out 

  1. Check the main bearing temperature.

  2. Check the pump for smooth operation

  3. Check all anchor bolts and support steel fasteners bolts, and tighten if necessary

  4. Check pump packing leakage and adjust as required

  5. Check pump / drive alignment .

  6. Check condition of a pressure relief valve

Maintenance Instruction

Lubrication

  1. Grease timing gears routinely when operating continuously

  2. Grease bearings at the same time as timing gears in step (A)
    Note: Zerk fitting do not have ball detente. This prevents bearings from
    being over-greased. Excess grease may run out of the zerk fitting when
    pump is in operation

Packing

  1. Use "John crane 1065 packing or equal" size required is 5/16 square.
    This packing gives: excellent wear, high chemical resistance great antifrictional value  

Pump Packing Removal and Repacking Instructions

Disassembly

  1. Remove packing nuts (19A and 19B-see fig A, pg. 7)
    Note: some pumps have a lock screw to secure the packing nut once it
    has been adjusted

  2. Use a packing pick to remove the old packing. Care must be used not
    to scratch or damage the shaft in the packing area while removing and
    installing packing

  3. Inspect the packing gland area. Be sure it is clean

Re-packing Procedure

  1. Select the desired packing. See step # 2 (A) above. Pre-formed
    packing will seat in more quickly

  2. Lubricate your packing rigs with "Triflon" or use a good grade of machine oil if "Triflon" is not available

  3. Insert the packing rings.
    Note : rotate each new packing ring so the split is 90 degrees away from the last split. A small screw driver can be used to press the packings into the gland area. Be careful not to damage or scratch the shaft

  4. Leave approximately 1/4 of threads clear of the last packing so that the adjusting nut (19 A and 19B) can be started

  5. Tighten the packing adjusting nuts (19 A and 19 B) a little more than "finger tight"

  6. Start pump up and adjust the packings as follows:

    1. Tighten the packings until the leakage is slowed to a small stream at each packing. Allow the pump to run for approximately 2 hours to start the seating process. Note : if the packings are over tightened so that no leakage occurs, the packing will burn and score the shaft. Always allow a little pump age to weep through the packing to insure cooling.

    2. After 2 hours of a rapid leakage of liquid through the packing, you may re-tighten the packing to a slow drip. Allow at least 15 minutes for the packings to form to the shaft in the packing area.

         

    3. Any further packing adjustment must be done with extreme care. Tighten the packing nut 1/8 turn, then immediately loosen the packing nut 1/16 turn. Are the packings still weeping a little moisture? (They should be). Allow the pump to run several hours at this setting

    4. For any subsequent packing adjustment, follow step 6c above and be aware of the burn comment made in step 6a
