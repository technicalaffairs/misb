Introduction

This document outlines the major maintenance procedure P1, to be performed every five years as indicated by Y5 in the above title block.

This pump is used to feed boiler with water

Pump Technical Specification:

Desgin

Multi Stage Horizontal Centrifugal Pump,  8 stages

Pump rate

379 t/h

Height

2286.3 m

Speed

5359 RPM

Bearing type

Journal bearing ø 90 mm

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear safety and healthy equipments as safety healthy, safety shoes, ...etc.

  • Isolate the circuit breaker of the pump motor.

  • Disconnect electric terminals of the pump motor.

  • The pump must be isolated mechanically by closing delivery and suction valves.

  • Be sure pump must be completely drained.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set.

  • Cleaning fluid, Manual puller.

  • Feeler gauge, Dial indicator.

  • Vibrometer, Straight edge.

  • Shims under pump feet.

  • Heating of connection bolts of stator.

  • Assembly flange.

  • Erection bolts. 

  • Equipment for heating up impeller hubs.

  • Gauges as: (Pressure, dial indicator with magnetic holder, Feeler gauges).

  • Special spanners for rotor and stator nuts.

  • Screw Jacks, Screw Jacks for rotor.

  • Standard spanners and other tools.

Work to be Carried Out

Disassembly

Pump in Operation

  1. Check discharge quantity and pressure and record them.

  2. Check the vibration level of the pump set complete with the motor then, record them.

Pump out of Operation

Dismantling of Bearings, Mechanical Seals and Balance Device

  1. Dismantle the cooling water, lubricating oil and sealing water pipes and the claw coupling guard.

  2. Dismantle the claw coupling spacer, check the alignment of the coupling and record it.

  3. Dismantle the thrust bearing cap, remove the nuts, the thrust bearing discs and the thrust bearing housing top figure 3.

  4. Screw the suspension bolts into the threads in the thrust bearing shell and pull the complete shell together with the thrust bearing disk off the shaft ( raise slightly ) by means of a lifting tackle.

  5. Disassemble the thrust bearing shell completely.

  6. Remove the upper half of the radial bearing shell figure 3.

  7. By means of a lever ( wooden or steel bar with a protective soft sleeve ) slightly raise the pump shaft, remove the lower half of the Radial bearing shell and check whether. 

  8. Remove the lower half of the thrust bearing housing.

  9. Remove the oil thrower, the seal flange cover and the mechanical seal flange together with the mechanical seal figure 4.

  10. Remove the seal cage on the discharge side, the mechanical seal nut and the seat.

  11. Remove the split ring sleeve, the split thrust ring and the spacer ring of the nut.

  12. Remove the balance disk space cover together with the sleeve and washer of the cover figure (8).

  13. Remove the balance disk, the thrust ring and the spacer ring.

  14. Remove the throttle ring of the balance disk, the bearing plate and the restriction bush.

  15. Remove the claw coupling hub and the coupling guard flange.

  16. Dismantle the radial bearing and the mechanical seal on the suction side in the same way as those of the discharge side.

 

Dismantling of Hydraulic Part of Pump

  1. Dismount the pump from the bedplate and place it on erection pads figure (2).

  2. Remove the thrust bearing including bearing housing, mechanical seal on the discharge side and the seal cage and the balance device

  3. Remove the radial bearing and the mechanical seal on the suction side ( without dismantling the seal cage on the suction side ).

  4. Reassemble the radial bearing on the suction side.

  5. Remove the pump cover and the thermal insulation.

  6. Place screw jacks under the last but one and the last stage ring sections.

  7. Remove the stator connection bolts.

  8. Remove the discharge casing.

  9. Remove the last stage impeller in the following way figure (1).

  10. Move the rotor of the extreme position toward the discharge.

  11. Check whether on the impeller is marked its position with regard to the shaft or mark this position. Cover a part of the shaft behind the impeller hub with asbestos fabric .Fit two levers in between the front impeller disk and the stage ring section. The levers should bear upon the impeller at the vans. By means of the lever apply slight pressure to the impeller and at the same time warm up the impeller hub quickly and uniformly either by means of an annular gas burner of the burner of an oxy – acetylene welding set.

  12. After heating up to a temperature of approx. 180 °C, when due to thermal expansion, the impeller hub is loose on the shaft, remove the protective asbestos fabric and pull the impeller off the shaft.

  13. Transfer the screw jack supporting the last stage section so that when the last stage section is dismantled it will support the two end stage sections on the discharge side.

  14. Dismantle the last stage section including the diffuser and the thrust ring of the last stage impeller.

  15. Remove the other impellers ( except the first stage impeller ) and the stage section the procedure is the same as for the dismantling of the last stage section and impeller.

  16. When dismantling the fourth stage section and impeller and the impellers of the other stages, it is necessary to support the shaft by means of a screw jack to prevent the pump from turning over .

  17. Remove the radial bearing on the suction side.

  18. Remove the shaft together with the first stage impeller.

  19. Remove the seal cage on the suction side.

  20. Remove the first stage impeller from the shaft.

  21. Remove the first stage diffuser and the other diffusers up to the last but one stage diffuser.

  22. Remove the sealing rings of diffusers, stage sections and of the suction casing.

Assembly

Assembly of Hydraulic Part of Pump

  1. Mount the diffuser, stage section and suction casing sealing rings, before mounting the sealing rings should be chilled .

  2. Secure the sealing rings by adjusting screws M 8 x 10.

  3. Fit the diffusers on the walls.

  4. Fix the suction casing on an erection pad.

  5. Mount the first stage impeller on the shaft figure (7).

  6. Fit the impeller keys in the keyway in the shaft.

  7. Place the impeller on a fire – clay block and warm up the impeller hub to a temperature of 180 °C by means of an annular burner.

  8. Fit the impeller on the shaft, drive it to the shaft shoulder and turn it to the right .

  9. Cover the impeller hub all around quickly with asbestos fabric.

  10. Hold the impeller pressed to the shaft shoulder for a period of approximately one minute.

  11. Remove the asbestos fabric from the impeller shaft.

  12. Mount the suction mechanical seal seat on the shaft.

  13. Mount the suction seal cage.

  14. Fit the lower half of the radial bearing on the suction end to the seal cage. Before tightening fully the nuts of the bolts by which the bearing housing is attached to the seal cage, seal cage by taper pins .

  15. Fit the pump shaft with the first stage impeller mounted on it into the suction casing. Support the shaft in the bearing journal on the discharge side by means of a jack.

  16. Assemble the radial bearing shells and housing.

  17. Carry out a check adjustment of the rotor ( axial and radial ) so that the suction casing face wall is adjusted to the impeller passage inner wall ( suction end ) figure ( 6 ).

  18. Measure the distance between the shaft end and the face wall of the radial bearing housing ( the measured value must be recorded ) .

  19. Move the rotor to the extreme position toward the suction and measure again the distance between the shaft end and the face wall of the radial bearing housing, ( the measured value must be recorded ) – measurement of the clearance to the suction .

  20. Mount the first stage ring section.

  21. Move the rotor to the extreme position toward the discharge and measure the distance between the shaft end and the face wall of the radial bearing housing.

  22. With type 150-CHM pumps, the minimum clearance to the discharge is for the first stage and up to the last but one stage impeller 3.2 mm, for the last stage impeller 5.2 mm.

  23. Place the impeller split thrust ring in the recess in the shaft behind the first stage impeller hub.

  24. Mount the impeller and the second stage to the last.

  25. Mount the discharge casing.

  26. The axial clearance and the basic adjustment of impellers must be measured at all the stages .

  27. Fit the connection bolts.

  28. Carry out the complete assembly of the entire pump.

Assembly of Bearings, Mechanical Seals and Balance Device

  1. The assembly of the above functional units, when their component by new ones.

  2. The following rules should be observed.

  3. When the balance device is replaced by a new one, the alignment of the rotor should be checked figure (8).

  4. When mechanical seals and thrust bearing are replaced by new ones, the adjustment of the balance must be checked figure (4).

  5. Radial bearings can be replaced independently of the baince device, seals and thrust bearing, provided however , that they are not damaged to such an extent that will render the alignment of the rotor impossible. Whenever the radial bearing are replaced, the alignment of the rotor with regard to the stator must be checked figure ( 3 ).

  6. If both radial bearings are to be replaced, replace first the radial bearing on the suction side.

Assembly of Radial Bearings

  1. Grind in the spherical surface of the shell of the radial bearing on the suction side with the bearing body fixed in a vice or on an assembly bench.

  2. Then the bearing housing and the lower bearing shell must be screwed together and dowelled.

  3. Into the lower bearing housing make sure the oil groove is on the side of both oil inlets in the bearing housing.

  4. Then the lower and the upper bearing housing halves must be screwed together and dowelled.

  5. Fix the lever for grinding in on the radial bearing shell and check wether the shell is an interference fit in the bearing housing. If the shell is loose in the bearing housing, it is necessary to grind or scrape the dividing plane ( in the upper or lower housing ) so that the shell is fitted in the bearing housing with a small interference .

  6. For grinding in, coat the spherical surface with fine grinding paste and grind in with a swinging movement of the grinding lever ( the upper and the lower bearing housing parts being slightly tightened by connection bolts ). Proceed in this way until approx. 70% of the spherical surface is ground in ( matte surface ).

  7. Remove the bearing shell from the bearing housing, remove grinding paste and clean, provide with a preservative coating.

  8. Assemble the shell with bearing housing and check whether, after the bolts have been tightened and the upper and lower bearing housing halves have been dowelled, the shell can be removed.

  9. For the radial bearing on the discharge side the procedure is the same.

Assembly of Balance Device 

  1. Before fitting the balance device, the radial bearing on the suction side must be completely assembled .

  2. Remove the bearing housing on the discharge side including the seal cage.

  3. Fit the bearing plate together with o-rings into the discharge casing ( fit by slight tapping on the plates )

  4. Align the rotor axially with regard to the stator i.e. adjust the shaft end on the suction side toward the bearing housing so that distance between them is equal to the value stamped on the bearing housing . secure the rotor in its position with regard to the stator.

  5. Measure the distance between the bearing plate face and the shaft shoulder “ 1 “ and the ring width “ s “ ( see figure (8) measure the values “ a “ and  “ b “ on the balance disk and adjust the spacer ring by grinding to the value “ x “ with tolerance ± 0.05

Assembly of Mechanical Seals

  1. Adjust the pump rotor its extreme position toward suction ( the balance disk is pressed against the bearing plate ).

  2. Clean thoroughly and coat with oil the cylindrical part of the flange of the mechanical seal on the discharge end, fitted, and the mechanical seal . fit . the seal on the flange  and adjust the seal ( when the balance disk bears against the plate, the mechanical seal springs are compressed by 3.5 mm ) secure the seal in its adjusted position by means of adjusting screws.

  3. Place the “O” ring in the recess in the seal cage and screw up the seal flange together with the mechanical seal to the seal cage.

  4. The procedure for the assembly of the mechanical seal on the suction end is the same.

  5. Tightening of the connection Bolts of the pump stator by the heating up of the bolts and the resulting expansion of the material.

Assembly of Thrust Bearing to Feed Water Pump

  1. Fit the front bearing shoes including guide shoes and bearing rings into the thrust bearing cage.

  2. Mount the rear thrust shoes and the bearing on the thrust bearing cage cover.

  3. Coat the thrust disk faces with paint and assemble completely the thrust bearing cage with the bearing disk and thrust bearing cage cover.

  4. Remove the thrust bearing cover and disk.

  5. Check the contact of the thrust shoe with the bearing disk and scrape, if necessary.

  6. Reassemble the thrust bearing cage, cover and disk measure the axial clearance of the thrust bearing disk in the bearing (the correct clearance is 0.25 to 0.4mm)

  7. Check the preload of the disk spring of the thrust bearing .

  8. Check the grinding in of the thrust bearing ring, the bearing thrust disk and the thrust bearing cage ( check with paint ) .

  9. Adjust the pump rotor into the extreme position toward the suction ( balance disk bears against the plate ) secure the rotor in this position.

  10. Measure the length of the shaft end ( dia. 55 mm including thread M ssx2 ) from the radial bearing journal face L1.

  11. Measure the overall length of the thrust bearing disk hub – L1.

  12. Assemble the bearing spacing plate, the thrust ring without the disk spring.

  13. Determine the thrust bearing spacing ring width.

  14. Adjust the thrust bearing spacing ring width to “ x1 “.

  15. Measure and / or adjust the axial clearance between the thrust bearing cage cover and housing cover .

  16. Dismantle the cage including the thrust bearing disk .

  17. Assemble the thrust bearing completely . secure the thrust bearing spacing plate and thrust ring in their positions by the adjusting screws on the bearing housing .

Starting of Pump

  1. Check the direction of rotation of pump before starting the pump for the first time.

  2. Check the quantity of oil in the thrust-bearing sump.

  3. Open the inlet for cooling the oil bath of the thrust bearing.

  4. Check whether the inlet for flooding the stuffing box is open.

  5. Check whether the inlet for hydraulic sealing of the suspension is open.

  6. Check whether the delivery sluice valve is closed.

  7. Open the delivery valve gradually after the set has been started to operate at its full speed.

 

 

Attendance and Checking during Operation

  1. Check the temperature of the anti-friction bearing in the bearing top which should not exceed 75°C

  2. Check the oil level in bearing top according to the mark on the oil gauge.

  3. Check the temperature of cooling water at discharge.

  4. Check the vibration of the pump figure (5).
