Introduction

This document outlines the major maintenance procedures P1, to be carried out every five years as indicated by Y5 in the title block.

This pump feed demi water from expansion tank for cooling generator, turbine lube oil and oil of feed water pump.

Safety Precautions

  • A work order must be issued.

  • The maintenance staff should be qualified and carry the suitable safety ID category.

  • Wear safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, anti static hand probe to avoid electrical shock …etc

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required       

  • Isolate the circuit breaker of the pump motor.

  • Disconnect electric terminals of the pump motor.

  • Close the suction and delivery valve and drain the pump completely.

  • The unit must be out of service

    •

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set.

  • Cleaning fluid, anti seizing grease.

  • Feeler gauge, dial indicator.

  • Rope and wooden blocks

 

Work to be Carried Out

Before Pump Shutdown

  1. Check discharge pressure and record it.

  2. Shutdown the pump.

  3. Check alignment and record it.

  4. Disconnect suction and delivery flanges.

  5. Dismantle the coupling.

  6. Lift the electric motor.

Disassembly

  1. Remove nuts and washers (50F,50G) and draw glands (50) of gland studs.

  2. Remove cap head screw and nuts securing top half casing (A1) to bottom half casing (B1).

  3. Presse tap out spring dowels.

  4. Screw in forcing set screws to break seal between casings.

  5. Lift top half casing off studs, place it to one side on clean flat surface.

  6. Remove gland packing (51) and lantern ring (46).

  7. Slide stuffing box insert (53) clear of packing area.

  8. Remove screws ( A62A, B62A) securing bearing housing to bottom half casing.

  9. Lift rotor assembly clear of bottom half casing, support on blocks.

  10. Remove case wearing dowels (23A).

  11. Remove thrust end bearing cap (63).

  12. Dismantle thrust bearing housing complete with bearing.

  13. Remove liquid thrower (72) and shoulder ring (77) (T.E)

  14. Remove (T.E) case wear ring (23).

  15. Pull sleeve (47) with O- ring seal (47R) off end of shaft (T.E)

  16. Remove impeller (21) and remaining case wear ring.

  17. Remove coupling end ( C.E) bearing cap.

  18. Dismantle bearing housing (C.E) complete with bearing.

  19. Remove liquid thrower and shoulder ring ( C.E).

  20. Pull sleeve (47) with O – ring seal (47R) off end of shaft (C.E).

  21. Remove impeller key (116).

Inspection

  1. Check the shaft run – out ( not more than 0.05mm).

  2. Check clearance between case wear rings and impeller ( 0.24 – 0.42 mm).

  3. Remove any roster scale from internal parts with iron brush.

  4. Check ball bearings and housing ( renew them if it is needed).

  5. Check all bolts (  renew them if it is needed)

  6. Examine all sleeves, O – ring seal (renew them if it is needed ).

Reassembly

  1. Lightly smear shaft (71) with grease.

  2. Fit impeller key in shaft.

  3. Push impeller (21) onto shaft centrally over key.

  4. Position O – ring (47R) on flange end of sleeve (47).

  5. Push sleeves (47) onto shaft to locate on key.

  6. Position case wear rings (23) on impeller.

  7. Thread stuffing box insert, lantern ring (46) onto sleeves.

  8. Slide glands onto sleeve.

  9. Slide shoulder rings (77) and liquid throwers (72) onto both ends of shaft.

  10. Pack both sides of bearings with clean grease.

  11. Mount bearings, secure with retaining nuts.

  12. Position glands, stuffing box inserts and case wear rings.

  13. Position case wear rings on impeller with dowels, fit case wear ring in bottom half casing

  14. Place rotor into bottom half casing, center line the impeller.

  15. Slide bearings housing into place, secure to bottom half casing.

  16. Check shaft turns freely by hand.

  17. Fill bearing caps with clean grease, position onto studs and secure with nuts.

  18. Place gasket on bottom half casing joint face, position top half casing above bottom

  19. Insert spring dowels, secure casings together.

  20. Check that shaft turns freely by hand.

  21. Put the pump in its place and connect suction and delivery pipe.

  22. Put the motor on its place

  23. Adjust the alignment between motor shaft and pump shaft and record it.

Starting the Pump

Before starting the Pump

  1. Open cooling water for gland packing.

  2. Open suction and delivery valves.

  3. Check the direction of rotation of the pump.

Attendance and Checking during Operation

  1. Temperature of bearings.

  2. Any water leakage.

  3. Discharge pressure, quantity.

  4. Vibration level.
