Introduction

Function of water air pump is to extract the air which has leaked into the condenser by various parts and to maintain the required vacuum inside .

This document outlines the major maintenance procedure P1, which must be performed for water jet air pump  every five year as indicated by Y5 in the above title block

Safety Precautions

  • Issue work order including the team work names.

  • Wear safety helmet –shoes- gloves, etc.

  • Use safety fence (Rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area.

  • Isolate the circuit breaker of the pump motor.

  • Disconnect electric terminals of the pump motor.

  • The pump must be isolated mechanically by closing delivery and suction valves.

  • Be sure pump must be completely drainage.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set.

  • Cleaning fluid, Manual puller.

  • Feeler gauge, Dial indicator

  • Vibrometer, Straight edge, Rags.

Work to be carried out

Disassembly

Pump in Operation

  1. Checks discharge quantity and pressure and record them.

  2. Check the vibration level of the pump set complete with the motor then, record them.

Pump out of Operation

  1. Check alignment and compare it with the setting value,

  2. Dismantle all attached pipes to the pump.

  3. Unscrew motor bolts and take it out.

  4. Lift pump from its place to the work place.

  5. Dismantle the gland of the pump and remove packing cords out.

  6. Dismantle the pump half coupling.

  7. Dismount thrust bearing complete with the hanger.

  8. Dismantle discharge elbow and shaft guide pipes.

  9. Dismantle the shaft joints by unscrewing them from their mafe couplings.

  10. Dismantle suction lantern if used, then take suction slide bearing out.

  11. Begin dismantling the hydraulic part from suction side .

  12. Dismantle impellers, sleeves and diffusers from the hydraulic part shaft after dismantling the pump clamping bolts.

  13. Check all dismantled parts for worn-out and replace by new one (if it is needed).

  14. Put the parts on a clean surface and be careful not to replace the same parts.

  15. Check the wear of:
        - Protecting sleeves of the shaft, contacting the bearings and gland packing.
        - Impeller ring.
        - Impeller.
        - Suction bell and transition.
        - Packing material.
        - Rolling – contact bearings (after disassembly).

  16. Replace the worn – out parts.

  17. Replace the protecting sleeve and the bearing if the rubber bearing clearance exceeds 0.6mm.

  18. Regrind the stuffing box bushing if it is worn-out , it is possible to regrind its roughness up to 0.4mm.

  19. Replace the bushing of the stuffing box if the bushing diameter is 2mm smaller than initial dimension.

Inspection

  1. Inspect the validity of journal bearings and thrust bearing (ball – roller – needle-white metal castings).

  2. Inspect the validity of the anti-friction bearings

  3. Check all parts for any worn-out.

  4. Check the suction and delivery pipes for creep.

  5. Check all parts for cracks.

  6. Inspect the run-out of shafts.

Assembly

  1. Clean all contact surfaces before mounting.

  2. Make paper gaskets (collo type printing paper or equivalent paper) for all flange joints.

  3. Grease both contact surfaces of the flange joint and place the paper gasket between them.

  4. Tighten the flange joint bolts.

  5. Screw the bearing housing with pressed-in bearing rubber bush into the diffuser.

  6. Screw the diffuser cover.

  7. Screw the joint ring on to the suction cover.

  8. Place the diffuser horizontally and insert the shaft (with pressed–on protecting sleeves )into the bearing.

  9. Put a key to the keyway.  

  10. Mount the impeller.

  11. Tighten the nut and fix it by a screw.

  12. Join the suction cover and suction bell.

  13. Attach the first guide pipe and the guide bearing housing with the pressed-on bearing bush on the protruding shaft end.

  14. Join the next shaft to the pump shaft by split coupling.

  15. Turn the shaft and check up its run-out.

  16. Mount all the guide pipes as far as the partition ceiling and the branch elbow.

  17. Put rubber packing to the partition ceiling frame and tighten the flange screws after aligning.

  18. Mount the stuffing box to the branch elbow.

  19. Discharge casing assembly is similar.

  20. Mount the dry element bearing housing with bottom cover, paper gasket and radial lip seal.

  21. Mount the rolling-contact bearing with tapered sleeve and secure it by a nut.

  22. Fill the bearing wholly, and 1/3 of the lid spaces with grease.

  23. Mount the top cover with paper gasket and radial lip seal.

  24. Screw the bearing tank with oil level indicator to the plate or stand.

  25. Screw the motor lantern and bearing tank to the discharge casing.

  26. Insert the packing cord rings-turn the adjoining rings through an angle of 180°C and tighten it slightly.

  27. Check its connection with the electric line and check whether the motor rotates correctly in the direction .

  28. Mount the lantern and the electric motor with coupling half keyed to it on the bearing top housing .

  29. Adjust alignment between the pump and the motor .

Starting of Pump

  1. Check the direction of rotation of pump before starting the pump for the first time .

  2. Check the quantity of oil in the thrust bearing sump .

  3. Open the inlet for cooling the oil bath of the thrust bearing .

  4. Check whether the inlet for flooding the stuffing box is open .

  5. Check whether the inlet for hydraulic sealing of the suspension is open .

  6. Check whether the delivery sluice valve is closed .

  7. Open the delivery valve gradually after the set has been  started to operate at its full speed .

Attendance and checking during operation

During operation, the following should be checked

  1. The temperature of the anti-friction bearing in the bearing top which should not exceed 75°C .
  2. The oil level in bearing top according to the mark on the oil gauge .
  3. The temperature of cooling water.
