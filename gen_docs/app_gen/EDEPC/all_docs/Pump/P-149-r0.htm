Introduction

This document outlines the minor maintenance procedure P1, which must be performed every five years as indicated by Y5 in above title block.

The working principle of the liquid ring vacuum pump is dependent upon a continuous supply of clean seal liquid which is normally water, the seal liquid enters the pump through a connection on the casing and is discharged from the pump.

Safety Precautions 

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear safety and healthy equipments as safety healthy, safety shoes, , gloves, etc.

  • Isolate the circuit breaker of the pump motor.

  • Disconnect electric terminals of the pump motor.

  • The pump must be isolated mechanically by closing delivery and suction valves.

  • Be sure pump must be completely drained.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set

  • Cleaning fluid

  • Feeler gauge & dial indicator

  • Rags

Work to be carried out

Shaft Bearing

The bearing should be flushed out each 3000 hours running time and repacked only  1/3 full with Grease, temperature Bearing about 60 oC.

Bearing Overheating due to

1 – Too much grease       2- Misalignment of coupling         3- bad bearing.

Bearing and Gland Packing list

Bearing:              Qty: 2                Type: 6304

Gland packing:    Qty: 6

Dimensions:   Inner Diameter  24      Outer Diameter  40

Gland Packing

  1. Slight gland leak:  Tighten the gland ring slightly.

  2. Excessive gland leak:  Repack the packing    ( tool, extractor )

  3. Remove the gland ring and packing outside lantern ring, to remove the inner packing, slide the lantern ring out of the gland then remove the packing with an extractor.

  4. Applying compressed air at the seal liquid connection can aid in removing this inner packing.

  5. Max. Air pressure = 30 psig.

Mechanical Seal

Should replace when:   1 – Worn         2 – scratched          3 – cracked                  

4 – Rotating portion of the seal does not firmly grip the shaft.

Replace Mechanical Seal

  1. Clean the shaft thoroughly where the previous seal may have bonded to the shaft.

  2. The seal faces must be protected during installation from particles which may scratch the surfaces.

  3. Do not run the seal dry.

Impeller end clearances and gasket Locations

  1. The gaskets between impeller casing and end plates determine the impeller end clearances.

  2. Clearance for standard materials = 0.004 inches.

Disassembly

** DE : Drive end,   ** NDE : Non drive end        

Refer to drawing for table No.(1)

  1. Drain the liquid from the pump.

  2. Mark casing .. etc, to ensure correct reassembly and alignment .

  3. Remove bearing cover N.D.E. No. 14 and N.D.E. hex bolts No. 27.

  4. Remove N.D.E. bearing seal housing No. 12.

  5. Remove all tie bolts No. 23 .

  6. Remove N.D.E. mechanical seal No. 21 from shaft No. 11.

  7. Remove Suction end casing N.D.E. No. 1 and inter suction casing No. 3.

  8. Remove impeller key 1st Stage No. 19 and impeller 1st Stage No. 7

  9. Remove enters delivery casing No. 4 and inters suction casing 2nd Stage No. 5.

  10. Remove impeller key No. 20 and impeller 2nd Stage No. 8.

  11. Remove enters delivery casing 2nd Stage No.6.

  12. Remove discharge end casing No. 2 with shaft intact from harness.

  13. Remove D.E bearing cover No. 13 & hex bolts No. 27.

  14. Remove bearing seal housing D.E No. 12.

Inspection

  1. Inspect the ball bearing for damage.

  2. Inspect the wearing rings for wear or damage.

  3. Inspect the impeller for wear or damage.

  4. Inspect the shaft for trueness with a dial indicator it must be true within (0.004 inches).

  5. Check shaft run-out (not exceed 0.002 inches).

Assembly      Refer to Fig. No. (1)

  1. The impeller must be placed on the shaft with blades pointing in the direction of rotation.

  2. The side clearance between impellers and impeller casing is extremely important using a depth gauge to measure the depth of 2nd stage inter suction part No. 5 and with a micrometer.

  3. Measure the width of 2nd stage impeller part No. 8.

  4. Install new abutment rings in their respective bores in the casing and casing cover.

  5. Tap bearing seal into bearing cover using a resilient mallet. Lubricate the sealing lip with bearing grease.

  6. Slide bearing bushing in casing cover and secure it with retaining clip, lock washer and cap screw.
      

  7. Attach casing cover to connecting pipe with cap screw, being sure the rabbet fit is properly engaged so that the gasket seat is square with the axis.

  8. Before mounting parts on the shaft. Clean shaft and parts thoroughly. Apply clean machine oil to the shaft surface on which the part is to be mounted.

  9. Install the thrust ball bearing. It should slide snugly into the bore in the connecting pipe and have an interference fit on the shaft.

  10. Slide shaft into connecting pipe through the throat at the thrust bearing end and carefully enter it through the bearing hushing, lubricate the sealing lip on bearing seal and tap it into the upper connecting pipe flange using a resilient punch.

  11. Tap the impeller feather key into the key way in the shaft being sure it does not extend beyond the shoulder at the thread.

  12. Line up the key and key way in the impeller and slide or tap it into the shaft all the way to the shoulder secure the impeller with the washer. Lock nut and retaining ring.

  13. Determine the required thickness for the spacer such that the axial clearance between the casing cover and impeller is 0.135" to 0.125" this is important to control axial bearing load.

  14. A piece of key stock can be used to make a tool for this purpose , grind the ends so it will drop into the bearing bore in the connecting pipe and reset on the shoulder at the bottom of the bore while being close to the bearing seat on the shaft.

  15. Set the impeller at its proper location and measure the distance between the bottom of the bearing bore and shaft shoulder below the bearing, then the thickness of spacer (not exceed 0.130").

  16. The thrust ball bearing must be packed with grease prior to assembly. The upper bearing cavity (inside bearing cover) should also be filled with grease prior to slipping the cover on the shaft.

  17. Lubricate the bore of the ball thrust bearing with light machine oil and bump it into the shaft using a heavy mallet and tube with even contact on the inner race only or use shop press. Be sure it is all the way on and firmly against spacer.

  18. Install the bearing lock washer and lock nut, be sure the lock nut clamps the inner race pf the ball thrust bearing. Bend one tab on lock washer fully into a mating slot on lock nut.

  19. Bearing cover must limit end play not exceed (0.003 – 0.005 inches) and also provide a water tight seal against gasket.

  20. Install the bearing cover with the proper thickness gasket, and secure it with cap screw.

  21. Install grease cup.

  22. Pump clearance (not exceed 0.12 inch).

  23. Tap coupling key into the key way in shaft and slide pump half coupling into shaft and lighten the set screw.

  24. Join the pit cover – motor stand to the connecting pipe with lock washers and cap screw.

  25. Install N.D.E mechanical seal on the shaft.

  26. Cement gasket in place on the casing. Take scale measurements to be sure the wearing ring will engage the impeller journal by least (0.130 inch).

  27. Install casing and secure with bolt lugs and cap screws install and tighten discharge flange bolts and nuts.

  28. Install strainer and secure it with lock washers or locking plates and bolts.

  29. Rotate pump rotor by hand. It should rotate freely.

  30. Tap coupling key into the key way in the motor shaft and slide coupling half on the driver and bolt the driver to the pit cover motor stand.

  31. Install the assembled unit and connect the suction and discharge pipe.

  32. Adjust the coupling alignment.

  33. Make the electrical connections pump the motor electrically and check for proper rotation.

Starting the Pump

Before starting the pump, the following steps should be checked :

  1. Check the direction of rotation of pump for starting the pump for the first time.

  2. Check the quantity of oil in the thrust ball – bearing pump.

  3. Open the inlet for cooling the oil bath of the thrust ball- bearing.

  4. Open the suction and delivery valves of the pump.

  5. Connect electric terminal of the pump motor.

  6. Put the circuit breaker of the pump motor in service.

Attendance and Checking during Operation

During operation of the pump, the following should be checked:

  1. The bearing temperature

  2. Check pump suction & discharge pressure.

  3. Check pump vibration.
