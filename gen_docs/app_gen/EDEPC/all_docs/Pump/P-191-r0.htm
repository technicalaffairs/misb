Safety Precautions

  • Work permit must be issued,

  • The maintenance staff must be qualified and carry the suitable ID safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required..

  • Wear the safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, ear plugs, rubber, gloves, safety glasses ... etc.

  • Isolate the circuit breaker for the pump motor.

  • The pump must be isolated mechanically by closing suction and delivery valves.

 

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set

  • Cleaning fluid, Soft brush

  • Suitable lifting device, Feeler gauge

  • Hammer, Manual puller

  • Dial indicator

  • Torque wrench

  2. Start to disassemble pump from timing gear end first

  3. Remove timing gear cover (2) this is done by removing cover bolts (3) clean exce grease from around gears, as well as gear cover

  4. Remove cotter pins from drilled hole in shafts. (Discard old pis and replace with ne 5/32" x 1-1/2" pin is used)
    Note : on new pumps, the cotter pins have been discontinued and Esna lock nut is us in place of slotted nut and cotter pin

  5. Remove timing gear nuts (8). If a wedge is used to keep pump from turning, use eitl soft brass or hardwood to keep from marring gears

  6. To remove the timing gears (10) a puller, as described below, should be used:

Assembly of edwards Pump

Preliminary precautions prior to assembly (all models)

  1. Be sure all parts are clean and free of grease, metal chips and that all gasket material remove from machined surfaces. Using a wire brush, buff threads on packing (19A. and 19B) and adjusting nuts (12)

  2. Using either a wire brush or round hole brush, remove any packing from threaded packing gland holes

  3. After cleaning parts, use a fine flat file, and keeping the file surface flat with machir surfaces, gently remove any burrs. Remove also any burrs on edges of pump parts, es cially end covers (2. and 5.) and end cases (15.)

  4. With 10 grit emery paper, remove any build up on counterbore surfaces of end ca (15.)

  5. Using the same type of paper as in step 4 remove foreign build up on the ins diameter of pump body (25). Be sure to deburr all edges using 100 grit emery pap

  6. Reassemble pump from drive end of pump as viewed from driver.

Prepare endcases for assembly (packed pump only): see fig 1

  1. With end case (15) liner counterbore side face down, install (one) packing (19A.) and (one) packing nut (19B) into the respective threaded holes. Ch to be sure that the threads have a loose fit and move freely. The packing (19A. and 19B.) should be able to be threaded all the way into the end case (1 with fingers only)

  2. Remove packing nuts and install the three pump packing rigs (19C) is end stuffing box

  3. When installing the packing (19C), be sure to stagger overlapped sections to prevent leakage

  4. After installing the three pieces of pump packing install the packing nuts (19A - 19B) into respective threaded glands (left hand 19A right hand 9B) and install them so that the initial one or two threads are started. Do not tighten packing nuts down

  5. After installing packing and packing nuts, install grease seals (14). Install grease seal with the lip side of the seal facing down toward the packing nut. Install grease seal so that the top edge of seal is level with the bottom edge of the bearing compartment area

  6. Using rotor / drive shaft (22 or 23). Insert the shaft through the packing gland rotating the shaft as it is being inserted. This forms packing to the shaft diameter and provides a smooth surface when placing end case o the shaft for assembly. Do the same procedure to both of the gland areas
    (Note : packing nut must righten in same direction of shaft rotation)

Pump Assembly

  1. Review rotation chart. (note : suction port is stamped "suction" discharge port is stamped "discharge"

  2. Install two liner segments (21) into pump body (25) Note : both liner segments should have "S" marked on them these go nearest to the suction of the pump. Note : there are holes drilled on suction side of liners. This side of the liner must always go toward the suction marked side of the body

  3. Install rotor / shaft (23) into pump so that the threaded end is away from drive end

  4. Install rotor / drive shaft (22) into pump so that the threaded end of shaft is away from drive end. Be sure the drive shaft is located in the proper position. See rotation chart (page 21)

  5. Position the two remaining liner segments (21) into the pump body in the same manner as step 2: and noting the suction hole position as in step 2 is facing the suction side of the pump

  6. Apply a thin film of grease on the gasket surface of the pump body and install the body gaskets (20) to both sides

  7. Apply a thin film of oil to packing area and seal area on the shaft. This will provide initial lubrication to both packing and grease seals

Special note:

When installing endcases (15) as they are positioned against the body (25) and liner segments (21) be sure liner segments fit into counterbore on endcases and that they are not cocked in any way. Damage to the endcases and liners can occur if this is not observed

  1. Starting with the drive end side install the endcase (15) which is labeled with a numb
    Check to be sure packing nuts (19 A & 19 B) tighten with the direction pump shaft will be turning 
    When installing endcase, be careful not to roll the packing or tear the lip seal installation (Note : a piece of .005" shim stock wrapped around each end of the shaft will ease installation of the endcase onto the shaft and over the shaft shoulders)

  2. Install bolts (16) 3/8" x 1-1/2" NC cap screws with washer (17) fastening endcase body. Do not tighten bolts (16) securely at this time

Mechanical seal only

  1. Install the snap ring on drive and idler shaft assemblies. Lubricate the shafts will elastomer compatible lubricant

  2. Place spring retainer, spring, boot and rotary on each shaft and push until the spring slightly compressed. Note : Install both seals without delay to prevent boot from taki set on shafts

  3. Immediately install endcase to body with bolts and washers. Do not fully tighten body at this time

LIP seals only

  1. Note : Seal cones are required to install lip sealed endcases. Place seal cones on body shafts

  2. Lubricate both shafts with grease or oil

  3. Install endcase to body with bolts. Do not fully tighten bolts at this time

  4. Repeat steps 7 through on the other side of the pump with the other endcase ; noting again for packed pumps that the rotation of the packing nuts, tighten with rotation of pump shafts. Do not rotate shafts until bearings have been adjusted

  5. Pack bearings (13) with a good quality lube. We recommend the chevron industrial grease - medium

  6. Prior to installing shaft bearings (13) apply a small amount of "Never - Seez" or equal to shafts (22/23) and bearing inner race (13) to prevent galling or pick-up on the shaft

  7. Use a bearing seating sleeve and gently tap bearings (13) into bearing compartment.
    Note that the bearings should tap into the compartment easily and no force should be used. Install bearings into both end cases

  8. Tighten body bolts to 35 foot-pounds torque

  9. After tightening install the four bearing adjusting nuts (12) in the endcases. Install adjusting nuts loosely; do not tighten

  10. The bearings are adjusted to center the rotor between the liners. This provides the same clearance on both sides of the rotor

  11. Adjust clearances in the pump as follows: starting with the upper shaft (22)

  1. Tighten one adjusting nut until rotor contacts the liner. Mount dial indicator to shaft and note reading. Loosen this adjusting nut and tighten nut on opposite side until the rotor contacts the liner. Note reading of dial indicator. (This is total clearance). Adjust nuts until half of total clearance is indicated. The rotor is now centered between the liners

  2. Once shaft and rotor are centered, and the bearing adjusting nuts are set, tighten the two set screws (11) to secure the bearing adjusting nut in the endcase.
    Warning : Do not tighten adjusting nuts too tight against the bearing this will cause excessive preload.

  3. Repeat this step with the other shaft.

  1. Installing the timing gears (10). The proper adjustment is as follows:

  1. Using a coarse grade of lapping compound, lap tapers of timing gears (10) to shafts (22 & 23)

  2. Clean excess compound away and wipe both the male and female taper half clean with a clean rag and suitable solvent. (Do not use oil based solvent)

  3. Install timing gear (10) onto idler shaft (23) by tapping gear onto shaft with a hollow sleeve. Taper should hold gear on tight.

  4. Install timing gear nut (8) onto the threaded end (23) of the idler shaft and tighten to approximately 175 # ft. torque.

  5. Run a piece of .006/.010 shim stock between rotors from the discharge port for clearance. Leave shim stock between rotors for clearance adjustment.

  6. Set timing gear (10) loosely onto the drive shaft (22)

  7. Rotate timing gear in the direction of rotation slightly while holding gear (10) on the drive shaft being careful that the shim stock remains in between rotors. Make sure that the timing gear on the drive shaft is touching or driving the timing gear on the idler shaft.

  8. Tap timing gear onto drive shaft tapered seat and secure with timing gear nut (8) as in step D
    Note : use a piece of brass or hardwood between timing gear teeth to prevent rotation when tightening timing gear nuts.

  9. Remove shim stock from pump compartment. Pump should turn freely and timing gear on the drive shaft should be driving the timing gear on the idler shaft

  1. Grease the face of endcase # 2 (15) and install cover gasket (4)

  2. Install timing gear cover (2) with bolts (3) (5/16" x 3/4" NC)

  3. Grease the face of endcase # 1 (15) and install cover gasket (4)

  4. Install front seal (7) into front cover (5) flush with the cover face. Be sure that the lip side of the seal faces outward (toward the coupling)

  5. Install front endcase cover (5) and secure it with bolts (3) (5/16" x 3/4"NC cap screws)

  6. Fill end cover (2) with lubricant (Chevron industrial grease - medium for pumps) to designated levels (approximately 1/2 full)
    Note : During initial break-in excess pressure can build up in the endcases which will cause excess grease to run out of the zerk fitting

  7. After assembly is complete, the packing gland adjustment should be performed during initial break-in. Tightening of packing glands should be done with caution. The packing nuts (19A & 19B) should not be over-tightened. Tighten packing nuts only enough to prevent excessive lrakage. A few droplets of pump age is all that should appear when pump is in operation.

    Note : When pump is not in operation, and suction-pressure is present on the suction side of the pump, excess leakage might appear through packing glands, do not tighten packing glands. This will cause excess wear and damage to packing glands and pump shafts.

    A shut-off valve should be installed on the suction side to eliminate pressure against the packing glands when not in operation to prevent the possibility of over-tightening the packing
