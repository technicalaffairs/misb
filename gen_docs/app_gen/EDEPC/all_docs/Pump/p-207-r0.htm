Introduction

This document outlines the minor maintenance procedures P1, which must be performed for every five years as indicated by Y5 in the above title block

The first draft initiated by eng. Ali Fahmy, tested by eng. El-Sayed Mahmoud El-Kholy, accepted by eng.  Sawsan El lithe general manager, revised by eng. Zakaria El-Shahat EDEPC MPIS coordinator and approved by eng. Mohamed Hussein Gomaa quality insurance head sector.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued

  • Wear your safety helmet, shoes  ... etc

  • Caution marks must be hanged on a work place, safety rope around a work place

  • Disconnect the DC & AC power supply

Work to be Carried Out

Disassembly

  1. Remove the pump from tank pump may have small quantities of oil trapped in internal, that will run out when removed from tank

  2. Loosen locking plates (131) and remove bolts (102) and locking plates (131) holding strainer (59) to casing (2), remove strainer (59)

  3. Remove discharge pipe bolts (108) and nuts (232). Break seal at discharge flange

  4. Remove oil circulation line (76) by loosening parker "F" fittings (78). Use care in removing, not kink so as to prevent reuse, this is oil line to bearing and must be free from restrictions

  5. Loosen locking plates (136) securing cap screws (102 f) and loosen cap screws swing lugs (240) 90° out of contact with drive side plate. It is not necessary to remove bolts ahd lugs - only to loosen

  6. Tighten jack - screws (242) evenly and break joint between casing cover (6) and casing (2) remove casing (2)

  7. Remove true - arc retaining ring (83)

  8. Remove flex loc nut (15) and washer (16)

  9. Remove impeller (3) and feather key (17) impeller will normally slide off shaft without use of appreciable force. Do not HAMMER on impeller under any circumstances, if use of wheel puller is required be certain pull is steady, evenly applied around shaft and parallel with shaft axis, in order to avoid damage to shaft or other parts, hydraulic balance holes may be used as jackscrew holes, by use of suitable tap and jackscrews (this is emergency procedure only when other means arc either not available or ineffective)

  10. Loosen locking plates (137) and remove cap - screws (102 E) and locking plates (137)

  11. Remove casing cover (6) with guide bearing (111B) from connecting pipe (50)

  12. Remove lower guide BEARING (11B)
          * Loosen locking plate (137A) and remove cap screw (102) locking plate (137A) and
             retaining clip (238B)
         * Guide bearing (11B) which is now free if bearing is to be reused and not replaced. It is
            suggested that it not be removed from casing cover
         * Suitability for bearing reuse visual inspection of bearing surfaces and examination of
            clearances on wearing rings and impeller rings should also require replacement of lower
            or guide bearing

  13. Remove upper thrust bearing (ball bearing (27))
          * Remove pump half of coupling (68) this may require use of wheel puller and care
             should be exercised to see that pull is even, steady and parallel with shaft to avoid
             possible damage to shaft or other parts
          * Loosen locking plates (131) and remove cap screws (102 C) CONNECTING PIPE
             (50) with shaft (7) upper bearing assembly and pump half coupling is now of cover
             plate (56) and may be removed
          * Separate bearing housing (38 B) from connecting pipe (50) remove shaft (7) with  
             bearing assembly from connecting pipe (50) 
          * Remove cap screws cap screws (102 A) and bearing cover (24) tab on bearing lock
             washer (28) may be disengaged and bearing lock - nut (29) remove
          * Remove bearing (27) and bearing housing (38 B) from shaft with bearing puller or shop
             press remove bearing (27) from housing (38 B)
          * Remove bearing seals (30 and 30 A) if required
             Note : Ball bearing (27) if removed from shaft should not reused except in case of
             emergency and should be replaced with new bearing at reassembly

  14. Remove & replace wearing rings (9) and (10) if clearance exceed 0.16 radial.
    Destruction original rings is required to remove from seats. Bronze rings should be drilled on opposite sides. Split and removed in two pieces. Graphitar rings should be broken out by use of cold chisel or punch and hammer

Reassembly

  1. Assembly wearing rings (9) and (10) in casing

  2. Assembly upper thrust bearing (ball bearing (28))
          * Assembly bearing seals (30 and 30 A) if required
          * Assembly bearing (27) in housing (38 B)
          * Assembly bearing (27) and bearing housing (38 B) in shaft with bearing puller or press
          * Assembly cap screws (102 A) and bearing cover (24) tab on bearing lock washer (28)
             may be disengaged and bearing lock - nut (29) removed
         * Connect pipe (50) into bearing housing (50)
         * Assembly upper bearing with shaft (7) connecting pipe (50) and assembly cap screws
            (102C) tighten locking plates (131)
         * Assembly pump half of coupling (68) this may require use of wheel puller and care
            should be exercised to see that pull is even, steady and parallel with shaft to avoid
            possible damage to shaft or other parts

  3. Assembly lower guide BEARING (11B)
          * Assembly guide bearing (11 B)
          * Assembly retaining clip (238) and locking plate (137 A) and cap screw and tighten
              locking plate (137 A)

  4. Assembly casing cover (6) with guide bearing (111 B) from connecting pipe (50)

  5. Assembly cap - screws (102 E) and tighten locking plates (137)

  6. Assembly lmpeller (3) and feather key (17) lmpeller will normally slide off shaft without use of appreciable force

  7. Assembly flex loc nut (15) and washer (16)

  8. Assembly tru-arc retaining ring (83)

  9. Tighten jack-screws (242) evenly and assembly joint between casing cover (6) and casing (2)

  10. Tighten locking plates (136) securing cap screws (102 f) and loosen cap screws swing lugs (240) 90° out of contact with drive side plate. It is not necessary to remove bolts and lugs - only to loosen

  11. Assembly oil circulation line (76) by loosening parker "F" fittings use care in removing not kink so as to prevent reuse this is oil line to bearing and must be free from restrictions

  12. Assembly discharge pipe bolts (108) and nuts (232) put seal at discharge flange

  13. Assembly strainer (59) to casing (2) holding strainer (59) and locking plates (131) and assembly bolts (102) tighten locking plates (131)

  14. Assembly the pump into tank
