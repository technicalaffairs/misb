Introduction

This document outlines the major maintenance procedures P1, to be performed every five years as indicated by Y5 in the title block.

The closed cooling pumps function to circulate the cooling water using for cooling the matrix components. They obtain from the compensation tank the water which flow to cool the components of the unit, pass to the heat exchanger and return to the pumps again in a closed circle

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued.

  • The maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear safety and healthy equipments as safety healthy, safety shoes, ...etc.

  • Isolate the CB of the pump motor.

  • Disconnect electric terminals of the pump motor.

  • Isolate the pump mechanically by closing the suction and delivery valves.

  • Be sure that the pump is completely drained.

 

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set.

  • Cleaning fluid and rags.

  • Filler, vibrometer and dial indicator.

  • Grease.

Work to be Carried Out

Before Disassembly

The Pump in Operation

  1. Check the vibration level of the pump and record it.

  2. Check discharge, pressure and record them.

Disassembly

The Pump out of Operation

  1. Check the alignment and record it.

  2. Disconnect the motor.

  3. Unbolt the coupling between the motor and the pump.

  4. Remove the motor.

  5. Dismantle every pipe connecting to the pump.

  6. Unscrew  the hex screw ( 1 ), raise the washer and raise the plate ( 3134 ).

  7. Unscrew  the screw ( 2 ) to raise the bearing cover ( 3261 ) and then remove the radial shaft seal ring  ( 4311 ).

  8. Remove the ball bearing ( 3011 ).

  9. Unbolt the oil dipstick ( 3859 ).

  10. Unbolt the nuts ( 3 ) and the studs ( 3 ).

  11. Raise the bearing bracket ( 3130 ).

  12. Remove the ball bearing ( 3012 ).

  13. Remove the radial shaft seal ring  ( 4311 ).

  14. Remove the bearing cover ( 3262 ).

  15. Remove the flinger ( 2540 ).

  16. Remove the stuffing box gland ( 4120 ) by unscrewing the screw ( 4 ).

  17. Remove the gland packing ( 4130 ), lantern ring ( 4134 ), the gasket ( 4551 ) and the shaft sleeve ( 2450 ).

  18. Unscrew the nut and the screw ( 5 ), raise the pump cover ( 1222 ) and then remove the gasket ( 4521 ).

  19. Raise the pump shaft with the impeller, remove the key ( 6710 ) and unscrew the impeller nut ( 2912 ) to remove the impeller.

  20. Remove the casing wear ring ( 1510 ).

  21. Now the pump is completely disassembled.

Inspection

  1. Clean all parts of the pump.

  2. Change all bearings of the pump.

  3. Check all parts for any worn-out , replace the damaged.

  4. Check all parts for cracks, treat the cracks by grinding or remachining.

  5. Inspect the run-out of the shafts.

  6. Change the wearing rings.

  7. Measure the radial clearances for the pump and adjust them.

  8. Grease the bearings of the pump.

Assembly

  1. Clean all contact surfaces.

  2. Assemble the impeller with the shaft by mounting the key ( 6710 ) and screw the impeller nut ( 2912 ) to fix the impeller with the shaft

  3. Put the lower wear ring ( 1510 ).

  4. Put the upper wear ring ( 4521 ).

  5. Assemble the pump cover ( 1222 ) and fix it with the pump casing by screw ( 5 ).

  6. Put the shaft gasket ( 4551 ), the shaft sleeve ( 2450 ), the gland packing ( 4130 ) and lantern ring ( 4134 ).

  7. Put the stuffing box gland ( 4120 ) and tight the screw ( 4 ).

  8. Assemble the bearing bracket ( 3130 ) by bolting the bolt and nut ( 3 ).

  9. Assemble the flinger ( 2540 ). 

  10. Assemble the radial shaft seal ring  ( 4311 ).

  11. Assemble the bearing cover ( 3262 ) and tight the screw ( 6 ).

  12. Assemble the ball bearing ( 3012 ). 

  13. Tight the oil dipstick ( 3859 ).

  14. Assemble the ball bearing ( 3011 ).

  15. Put the radial shaft seal ring  ( 4311 ), put the bearing cover ( 3261 ) and then tight  the screw ( 2 ).

  16. Put the plate ( 3134 ), put the washer and tight the screw ( 1 ).

  17. Mount the coupling between the pump shaft and the motor shaft, adjust the alignment and then fix the coupling.

  18. Now, the pump is completely assembled.

Starting the Pump

Before Starting the Pump

  1. Check the water level in the compensation tank.

  2. Open the suction and delivery valves of the pump.

Attendance and checking during Pump Operation

  1. Check for any water leaks.

  2. Check the discharge and pressure of the pump.

  3. Check the vibration level.
