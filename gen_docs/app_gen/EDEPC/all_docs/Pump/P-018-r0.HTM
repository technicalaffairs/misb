Introduction

This document outlines the major maintenance procedure P1, which must be performed for Condensate pump every five years as indicated by Y5 in the above title block.

The first draft initiated by eng. Gamal Mostafa which reviewed by Maintenance manager eng. Mohammed Farag and Revised by Inspection and Quality Control at EDEPC. eng. Abouda Elbana and tested on May30, 2003. by eng. Gamal Mostafa and Approved by General Manager Eng. Ali Mostafa El  lithy

Safety Precautions

  • Issue work order including the team work names

  • Wear safety helmet –shoes- gloves, etc.

  • Use safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Isolate the circuit breaker of the pump motor

  • Disconnect electric terminals of the pump motor

  • The pump must be isolated mechanically by closing delivery and suction valves.

  • Be sure pump must be completely drainage.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set.

  • Cleaning fluid.Manual puller.

  • Feeler gauge, Dial indicator

  • Vibrometer, Straight edge, Rags.

 

 

Work to be carried out

 

Disassembly

  1. Checks discharge quantity and pressure and record them.

  2. Check the vibration level of the pump set complete with the motor then, record them.

  3. Measure the pump alignment and record it.

  4. Dismount the motor after stopping the pump and dismantling the electric terminals of it then, take it out.

  5. Measure the axial clearance of the pump and record it.

  6. Dismantle the attached pipes to the pump

  7. Dismantle the pump from the Condensate tank.

  8. Raise the pump and take it to the work place.

  9. Take the cords of the stuffing box out.

  10. Unscrew the castle nut and take coupling out.

  11. Dismantle the lantern ring.

  12. Dismount thrust bearing (29326).

  13. Dismantle the clamping bolts of the pump stages.

  14. Dismantle the pump impellers starting from suction side up to the last stage.

  15. Dismount the guide pipe and the elbow.

  16. Dismantle the lower shaft from the half coupling.

Inspection

  1. The validity of journal bearings white metal casting.

  2. The validity of the anti-friction bearings

  3. Check the radial clearances for pump by measuring the gap between wearing rings of diffusers and the distance sleeves on the shaft and record it.

  4. Check all parts for any worn-out.

  5. Check all parts for cracks.

  6. Inspect the run-out of shafts.

 

 

 

Assembly 

  1. Make sure the contact surfaces of flanges and guide bearings are clean and not damaged.

  2. Install pump impellers on the shaft beginning from discharge side up to the suction side.

  3. Fit the suction journal bearing and tight all stages together with clamping bolts and Check the rotation of the shaft.

  4. Mount the first joint of the shaft to the protruding top end of the pump shaft and join them rigidly by the mafe coupling.

  5. Mount the first guide pipe and fix it to the pump casing.

  6. Mount the bearing housing on the top end of the first guide pipe from which the shaft protrudes.

  7. Install the lantern ring and mount stuffing box, then re-pack it.

  8. Mount the bearing (29326) and check whether the shaft has uniform play in the hole of the bearing and also whether it has not deflected to one side by the bearing.

  9. Lower the fully assembled pump on to the erection frame and fix it.

  10. Mount the pump half coupling.

  11. Adjust the axial clearance of the pump by lifting the entire rotor in the upward direction until it stops by means of the castle nut. At the same time, count the number of turns of the nut up to the moment when the nut is fully tightened.

  12. Set the rotor correctly by slackening the nut to half of the number of turns, which were necessary for its tightening.

  13. Secure the nut in its position by a cotter pin, or with type BKN by safety screw.

  14. Mount the electric motor on its place.

  15. Adjust the alignment between motor shaft and pump shaft and record them.

 

Starting the Pump

 

Before starting the pump, the following steps should be checked

  1. Check the oil level in the thrust-bearing pump bath

  2. Open the cooling water for the oil bath of the thrust bearing.

  3. Open the suction and delivery valves of the pump.

  4. Check the direction of rotation of the pump by pushing the electric button of the motor pump instantaneously.

 

 

 

Attendance and checking during operation

 

During operation of the pump, the following should be checked:

  1. The temperature of the anti-friction bearing (it should be not exceed 75°C).

  2. The temperature of cooling water.

  3. Any water leakage and oil leakage.

  4. The discharge quantity and pressures (suction and discharge) of the pump and record them.

  5. Check the vibration level and record it.
