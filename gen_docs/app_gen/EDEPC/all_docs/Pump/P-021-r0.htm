Introduction

This document outlines the major maintenance procedures P1, which must be performed every 41000 operating hours as indicated by 41000 H in the above titled block.

The first draft initiated by eng. Saad Samra, tested at 28 June 2003 by eng. Mohamed Abdel Azeem, revised by EDEPC quality control eng. Abouda Elbana, accepted by General Director eng. Mohamed Hamouda Al-Sarag and approved by eng. Moneer Mohamoud El-Wakeel Head Sector of Demiatta GT

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area.

  • Wear safety helmets, shoes, gloves, etc….

  • Isolate the CB of the pump motor.

  • Disconnect electric terminals of the pump motor.

  • Isolate the pump mechanically by closing delivery valve.

  • Be sure that the pump is completely drained.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set.

  • Cleaning fluid, Filler. Rags.

  • Vibrometer, Dial indicator.

Work to be Carried Out

Disassembly of Pump

  1. Measure vibrations level for bearing and compare it with the setting value.

  2. Check discharge, pressure and record their values in the table and compare these values with the setting values.

  3. Check the alignment and compare with the setting values.

  4. Disconnect pump motor.

  5. Unbolt and lift off motor.

  6. Disconnect piping connecting to turbine.

  7. Loosen bolts connecting the pump to the oil tank.

  8. Remove complete pump from oil tank. Transfer pump to workshop for disassembly.

  9. Remove coupling guard and pull off pump half coupling.

  10. Remove lube oil pipe connecting to antifriction bearing.

  11. Measure the clearances of the pump.

  12. Loosen hexagon nuts and remove suction cover with wearing ring.

  13. Loosen impeller nut and remove lock washer.

  14. Remove impeller from shaft and remove the key.

  15. Loosen bolts at discharge elbow and nuts at suspension tube and then remove volute casing from shaft.

  16. Remove locking ring and force out shaft with ball bearing from bearing skirt.

  17. Loosen nut and pull off ball bearing.

Note:

It may be difficult to remove the individual components of the pump rotor [impeller, nuts or bearings] from the shaft. Never use impact methods such as hammer, etc…

First use penetrating oil and pullers. If it is not successful, the respective parts should be slightly heated and then pulled off or pressed off, making sure that the shaft remains at the lowest possible temperature. After disassembly of the rotor elements by the application of heat, the shaft must be checked for run out.

Inspection Components

Rotating Components

  1. Measure impeller, wearing rings clearance. The clearance must be within the limit [0.3 – 0.8].

  2. Measure bearing bush and pump shaft clearance. The clearance must be within the limit [0.08 – 0.146].

  3. Examine impeller neck [suction and discharge sides] as well as wearing rings and bearing bush for signs of radial rubbing. If rubbing is detected, it may be remedied by remachining.

  4. Make sure that the resulting clearances are not exceeding the max. Permissible values.

  5. Install new parts and restore the original clearances, if larger

Shaft

  1. Examine the shaft for rubbing marks at the bearing bush

  2. Grind the shaft if there is rubbing or minor damage

  3. Make sure that the clearances are not exceeding the permissible values, unless we must replace the shaft.

  4. Balance the pump dynamically, if a new shaft is installed or one of impeller parts is replaced.

Ball Bearing

  1. If the removed bearings are good, we must wash them by oil, grease them, dry them and install them in the pump again.

  2. Use new bearing, if minor discolorations or minor damage are detected

Rebalancing Impeller

  1. Insert key and slide impeller over the shaft as far back on the shaft as it will go.

  2. Install lockwasher and secure in position on shaft by means of impeller nut.

  3. Prior to dynamic balancing, check pump rotor for run out at the impeller and the bearings [Max, permissible run out 0.03].

  4. Rebalance rotor at max. Operating speed.

Assembly

  1. Coat the shaft with a lubricant after installing the rotor elements on the shaft.

  2. Insert bearing bush into volute casing and lock against turning by means of a straight pin.

  3. Install wearing ring in volute casing and lock against turning by means of a grub screw.

  4. Slide shaft through the bearing bush in the volute casing.

  5. Insert key in the shaft and slide impeller into position. Install lockwasher and secure in position by means of impeller nut.

  6. Insert wearing ring in suction cover and lock it by the screw. Install suction cover in volute casing and tighten bolts.

  7. Insert pump unit with shaft into suspension tube. Bolt volute casing to suspension tube.

  8. Slide spacer ring over shaft collar and install ball bearing. Insert locking ring and lock bearing in position with grooved nut.

  9. Mount flexible coupling.

  10. Reconnect lube oil pump.

  11. Bolt discharge elbow to volute casing.

  12. Check the alignment of the pump rotor after assembly.

  13. Lock all bolts and nuts carefully.

  14. Clean pump and motor shaft ends and coupling. Install key in shaft keyway. Remove inserts from coupling. Heat hub and then mount it on the shaft end.

  15. Examine and install flexible inserts.

  16. Put complete pump in oil tank and tighten bolts, mount on drive motor, and connect piping.

  17. Check the alignment of pump – motor.

  18. Reconnect mean power, check pump for correct direction of rotation.

  19. Exchange main power leads if opposite direction of rotation is required.

  20. Vent equipment, and then the pump is ready to be placed into service.

  21. Measure the pressure, discharge and vibration and compare them with the setting values.
