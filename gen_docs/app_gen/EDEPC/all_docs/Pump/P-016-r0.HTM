Introduction

This document outlines the major maintenance procedure P1, which must be performed every 5 years as indicated by Y5 in the above title block.

The first draft initiated by eng Medhat Fouad, tested at 05 June 2003, revised by EDEPCo.quality control eng. Abouda Elbana, accepted by General Manager eng.Ali Mostafa El_Lithy and Approved by eng. Mostafa Alaam Talkha PS Head Sector.

 Safety Precautions

  • Issue work order including the team work names

  • Wear safety helmet –shoes- gloves, etc.

  • Use safety fence (Rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area.

  • Isolate the circuit breaker of the pump motor

  • Disconnect electric terminals of the pump motor.

  • The pump must be isolated mechanically by closing delivery and suction valves.

  • Be sure pump must be completely drainage.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set.

  • Cleaning fluid.Manual puller.

  • Feeler gauge, Dial indicator

  • Vibrometer, Straight edge, Rags.

Work to be carried out

Disassembly

  1. Checks discharge quantity and pressure and record them.

  2. Check the vibration level of the pump set complete with the motor then, record them.

  3. Measure the pump alignment and record it.

  4. Dismantle the electric motor and take it out.

  5. Dismantle all attached pipes to the pump.

  6. Dismount the half coupling of the pump shaft.

  7. Loosen the follower glands and take out the stuffing box packing cords.

  8. Discharge the oil from the bearing sump

  9. Loosen the bearing caps and remove them from the pedestal.

  10. Unscrew the sleeve nut of the bearing and take it out.

  11. Dismount the thrust bearing (29415) and the two journal bearings (2317K-1317K) of the suction and discharge sides of the pump.

  12. Screw out the nuts fixing the pedestal to the pump casing.

  13. Lift the pump to the work place and put it on its maintenance stand.

  14. Loosen the clamping bolts and remove them from the pump.

  15. Dismantle the impellers of the pump beginning from suction side up to discharge side

  16. Place suitable pad under the loosened shaft.

  17. Dismantle all worn-out sleeves by heating.

  18. If new rotor parts (bushes, impellers) are used, it is necessary to balance the assembled rotor dynamically.

 Inspection

  1.  The validity of journal bearing white metal castings.

  2. Check the radial clearance of the pump by measuring the clearances between the wearing rings which are installed in the diffusers and the distance sleeves on the shaft.

  3. The validity of the anti-friction bearings.

  4. Check all parts for any worn-out.

  5. Check all parts for cracks.

  6. Inspect the run-out of shafts.

Assembly

  1.  Place the suction casing with the bolted bearing pedestal on an assembly stand or on the base plate.

  2. Insert the shaft with mounted gland sleeves for the stuffing box and put it into the bearing pedestal through the stuffing box space of the suction casing and support the shaft from the other side.

  3. Build in the radial bearing.

  4. Fit the first impeller and ensure that the inner front wall of the impeller channel mates with the surface of the diffuser shim.

  5. Fit the other impellers by the same way up to the last stage.

  6. Check the clearance of the rotor in the longitudinal axis (3-5mm).

  7. Mount the thrust bearing so that the rotor is approximately on the center of this clearance.

  8. Rotate the pump rotor and ascertain that it is easy rotating otherwise dismantle the pump and solve the problem of axial clearance.

  9. Insert the stuffing box with new packing cords.

  10. Put the pump in its place and mount all attached pipes.

  11. Put the motor on its place.

  12. Adjust the alignment between motor shaft and pump shaft and record them.

Starting the Pump

Before starting the pump, the following steps should be checked: -

  1. Check the oil level in the thrust-bearing pump bath

  2. Open the cooling water for the oil bath of the thrust bearing and gland packing.

  3. Open the suction and delivery valves of the pump.

  4. Check the direction of rotation of the pump by pushing the electric button of the motor pump instantaneously.

Attendance and checking during operation

During operation of the pump, the following should be checked

  1. The temperature of the anti-friction bearing (it should be not exceed 75°C).

  2. The temperature of cooling water for bearing oil and gland packing.

  3. Any water leakage and oil leakage.

  4. The discharge quantity and pressures (suction and discharge) of the pump and record them.

  5. Check the vibration level and record it.
