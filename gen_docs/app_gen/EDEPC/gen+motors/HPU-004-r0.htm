Introduction

This document outlines lists the equipment inspection I1, which must be performed on monthly bases as indicated by M1 in the title block

The hydrogen electrolyzing plant generates hydrogen and Oxygen by the electrolysis of water. The installation will be constructed in order to fulfill the following functions

  1. Delivery of hydrogen of 0.7 MPa constant pressures for cooling the generators

  2. Filling of bottles with High-pressure hydrogen

  3. Safe discharge of Oxygen into the Atmosphere, if it should not be filled into Oxygen bottles

Production Capacity of the Technological installation

Hydrogen                        10 m3/h
Operation temp                80 °C
20 MPa

Circuit of electrolyte

For the electrolytic process a 28% solution of potassium hydroxide (KOH) will be used as an electrolyte. The electrolyte is prepared in a dissolving vessel of carbon steel, into which a pump pumps it from the electrolyzer. This pump is of cast iron. The vessel has a withdrawable basket for dissolving the KOH

 

Circuit of feed water

The dematerialized water needed for the electrolytic process is supplied by the piping into the equalizing and storing vessel. From here, the water is lead into the electrolyzer and / or into the dissolving vessel of the electrolyte. The vessel is made of PVC

The conductivity of the demi-water will be continuously measured at the intake. Conductance = (2μs/cm)

 

Circuit of hydrogen

The produced hydrogen is delivered into the gas reservoir. The reservoir will have a gas inlet and outlet and a water inlet and overflow. The level regulator controls the volume of hydrogen production by adjusting the electric current of the rectifier. A four-stage compressor cooled by air to 20 MPa outlet pressure presses the hydrogen from the reservoir. Into the suction part of the compressor, a filter for trapping eventual traces of KOH is inserted. The filter body is of carbon steel, the candle filter of polypropylene. The filling station will be at the same time for filling four high-pressure steel bottles with hydrogen to 20 MPa final pressures
