Introduction

This document outlines the major maintenance procedures P1, which must be performed every 15 years as indicated by Y in the title block

The first draft initiated by eng. Amr gaber, tested by eng. Mohammed Abbas & eng. Mohammed Elbayaa 

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category

  • Maintenance staff should be well trained, qualified or directly supervised by some one who is

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required

  • Personal protective equipment must be used (safety shoes, helmet, breathing mask, safety glasses ... etc)

  • The operator, who is inside the stator, is deprived from any metallic or magnetic object (phones, tape measure, chain)

  • Protect the inferior part of end winding and core, with a rubber carpet or plastic film which will move with a make progress expertise

  • Supply of necessity, by intermediate of separate cubicle, the eventual appliance, and lighting put on work inside the core stator

Tools and Equipment

  • Megger (500 - 5000) volt

  • Complete maintenance tool set

  • Air blower, cleaning solvent

  • Contact resistance tester

  • Multimeter

Work to be carried out

 

Inspection conditions

  1. Field winding rotor .

  2. Armature winding stator .

  3. Excitation system .

  4. Terminal box opened .

Frame inspection

  1. Check field support .

  2. Check movel parts .

  3. Check heating resistor wiring .

  4. Check frame body (cracks - corrosion).

  5. Check  volute and weld bolts .

 

Armature winding (stator) inspection

  1. Visual inspection .

  2. Check the cleanliness of the stator winding .

  3. Check the condition of connection between coils and poles .

  4. Check the coil wedging and binding .

  5. Measure winding insulation .

  6. Measurement of pole impedance .

Field winding (rotor) inspection

  1. Check the rotor bars.

  2. Check the core wedges and plate wedging .

  3. Check coil end winding .

  4. Measure winding insulation .

Excitation system

  1. Check positive and negative of Scr connection .

  2. Check Scr head nut .

  3. Measurement the Scr resistance .

  4. Retighten of the connection bolts .

  5. Replacement all the ventilation fans .

  6. Replacement the all filters .

  7. Check de-excitation and over voltage protection system .

  8. Check and cleaning and retightening the ac busbar (between the excitation transformer and generator and excitation cubicle) and dc busbar between excitation cubicle and carrier brushes .

  9. Visually check and measuring the insulation resistance of excitation transformer .

 

 

 

Exciter

  1. Remove the brushes of generator, exciter and earthing brushes from their holders

  2. Remove brushes holders

  3. Clean brushes slip ring contact

  4. Before and after cleaning brushes slip ring contact measure the insulation resistance between slip ring and earth by using megger device 500 V.D.C. "approximately ≥ 10 M Ω"

  5. Clean brushes holders.

  6. Before and after cleaning brushes holders measure the insulation resistance between holders and earth by using megger device 500 V.D.C. "approximately ≥ 10 MΩ"

  7. Put holders in their position and tightening it

  8. Check the clearance between brushes holders and slip ring "approximately 2 m.m"

  9. Replace the brush by a new one

  10. Check brushes must move freely in the holders and must sit to the rings by their full surface.

  11. Check brush pressure.

  12. Check connection of busbar of brushes must be tightened.

  13. Put the unit in service.

  14. Inspect the brush vibration.

  15. Inspect the sparking brushes.

  16. Measure the current in each brush (the current in any brush must be 12A/cm2 approximately.
