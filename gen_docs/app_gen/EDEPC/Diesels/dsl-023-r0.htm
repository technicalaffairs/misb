Introduction

This document outlines the major maintenance procedures P1, to be performed every 16000 operation hours to ensure good operation protection of all components as indicated by H16000 in the title block.

The first draft initiated by eng. Ezat Mohamed Osman El-Bake, reviewed by eng Azmy Soliman, approved by end. Mohamed Abd El-Ghaffar general director of El-Arish PS, and certified by eng. Samir Abd El Gilil; EDEPC inspection & quality control general director, MPIS coordinator.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued.

  • The maintenance staff should carry the suitable ID safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear the safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, shoes, gloves ...etc.

  • Isolate the circuit breaker of the pump motor.

  • Disconnect electric terminal of the pump motor.

  • The pump must be isolated mechanically by closing delivery and sactior valves.

  • Be sure pump must be completely drained.

  • Be sure oil must be completely drained.

Work to be Carried Out

Compressor End   fig 2 A,B

  1. Remove screw plug 46009 ad drain off the oil.

  2. Remove hex - headed screw 32163 washers 39165 pat-locking nut 32164 and holding spring 32162.

  3. Screw tight fixing tool 90030 to looking flange 32107 with hex headed screw 90031.

  4. Unlock cap nut 38001 and remove oil pump 36000 with C snaper 90240.

  5. Remove locking plate 32114.

  6. Un screw ring nut 32151 with box spanner 90050, remove fixing tool 90030.

  7. Pull out centrifing 32150 (using extractor 90070 and extracting bash 90120 if necessary).

  8. Remove hex - headed screw 76021 and locking edge achers 76022.

  9. Screw on the extractor 90070 to the inner bearing bush and withdraw bearing 32100.

  10. Don't remove guide bolt as it defines the position of the bearing.

  11. If the bearing 32100 has to be replaced the pump holder 32161 should be unscrewed from the dismantled bearing and be fixed to the new bearing with hex - headed screw 32152 and locking plate 32153. Dismantled part have to be kept clean.

  12. Clean carefully the bearing champer before inserting the new bearing it is of utmost importance before fitting the bearing that the shaft end is:
    - Thoroughly cleaned.
    - Coated with suitable coating to prevent sizning such as molykote dx or a similar product.

  13. All surplus paste must be removed in order to prevent a discoloring of the lubricating oil only white coloured products must be used

  14. The spare bearings at turbine end supplied by us or our service stations have a correct adjusted clearence in the radial damping springs 24105 (fig 5)

  15. Assemble in reverse order to dismantling as per when assembling insert the bearing 3-1100 by hand as far as possible

  16. Fit the centrifuge 34150.

  17. Screw in the ring nut 34151 by hand as far as possible with the side of the box spanner 90050 press the bearing 34100 and cenbrifuge 34150 onto the shaft shoulder

  18. Loosen the ring nut 34151 with the box spanner 90050 and retighten it by hand (until it sits close to the centrifuge)

  19. Slip on the box spanner 90050 with the engraved mark "O" on the top and apply a mark on the centrifuge in the radial elongation of "O" fig. 2

  20. Tighten the ring nut 34151 by knocking with a hammer on the tommy bar 90260 until the turbocharger size engrave on the box spanner 90050 matches with the mark on the centrifuge 34150 while doing so the box spanner 90050 must be supported by hand.
    Please Note: to prevent the  shaft from distorting the rotor must be turned by 180° after each stroke using the box spanner and rig nut 32151.

  21. Fit the locking plate 32114 on the shaft and check whether it possible to secure in this position if none of the tongues of the locking plate 32114 matches with the slats of the ring nut 32151 tighten the later a little move until the securing operation can be done blamelessly.

  22. Carefully fit the hex-headed screws 76021 and locking washers resp. locking edge washers 76022 and tighten then firmly.

  23. Damaged locking plates or locking washers have to be replaced by new ones.

  24. Measure the distance "K" at compressor and check whether it agrees with the figures shown on plates 46011 inside the bearing space cover 46000.

  25. Check the axial contact surfaces of oil pump 36000 before flitting it they must be absolutely clean and free of any damage.

  26. Check the true running of the pump, figure 2B

  27. Lock carefully cap nut 36001 and ring nut 32151 with the plate 32114, center the pipe of the pump with the spring 32162 in holder 32161 the pipe of the pump should not touch the pump holder.

Turbine End   fig 2A

  1. Remove screw plug 48009 and drain off the oil

  2. Remove hex-headed screw 51011 press off bearing space cover 48000 from gas inlet casing 51001 with puller screw 90900

  3. Remove hex-headed screw 34163 washers 34165 pal. locking nut 34164 and holding spring 34162

  4. Screw tight fixing tool 90030 to locking flange 34107 with hex-headed screws 90031

  5. Unlock cap nut 39001 and with "C" spanner 90250 remove oil pump 39000

  6. Remove locking plate 34113

  7. Unscrew locking nut 34151 with pox spanner 90050 remove fixing tool 90030

  8. Pull out centrifuge 34150 (using extractor 90070 and extracting bush 90120 if necessary)

  9. Remove hex-headed screw 51025 and locking washers resp. locking edge washers 51026

  10. Screw on the extractor 90070 to the inner pearing bush 34102 and withdraws bearing 34100

  11. Do not remove guide bolt 51022 as it defines the position of bearing

  12. If the bearing 34100 has to be replaced the pump holder 34161 should be unscrewed from the dismantled bearing and be fixed to the new bearing with hex-headed screw 34152 and locking plate 34153 Figure 5

  13. Dismantled parts have to be cleaned.

  14. Clean carefully the bearing chamber before inserting the new bearing

  15. It is of utmost importance before fitting the bearing that the shaft end is:
    - Thoroughly cleaned
    - Coated with a suitable coating to prevent sizening such as e.g.     molykote dx or a similar product

  16. All surplus paste must be removed in order to prevent a discoloring of the lubricating oil only white colored products must be used.

  17. The spare bearings at turbine end supplied by us or our service station

  18. A correct adjusted clearance in the radial damping springs 34105 (fig 5)

  19. Assemble in reverse order to dismantling as per fig 2A

  20. When assembling insert the bearing 34100 by hand as far as possible.

  21. Fit the centrifuge 34150

  22. Screw in the ring nut 34151 by hand as far as possible

  23. With the aid of the box spanner 90050 press the bearing 34100 and centrifuge 34150 onto the shaft, until they come to the stop at the shaft shoulder

  24. Loosen the ring nut 34151 with the box spanner 90050 and retighten it by hand (until it sits close to the centrifuge)

  25. Slip on the box spanner 90050 with engrave mark "O" on top and apply a mark on the centrifuge in the radial elongation of "O" (see fig 1)

  26. Tighten the ring nut 34151 by knocking with a hammer on the tommy bar 90260 until the turbocharger size engraved on the box spanner 90050 (eg  454 for VTR 454)matches with the mark on the centrifuge 34150 while doing so the box spanner 90050 must be supported by hand.
    Please Note: to prevent the shaft from distorting the rotor must be turned by 180° after each stroke using the box spanner 90050 and ring nut 34151.

  27. Fit the locking plate 34113 on the shaft and check whether it is possible to secure in this position if none of the tongues of the locking plate 34113 matches with the slots of the ring nut 34151 tighten the later a little more until the securing operation can be done blamelessly.

  28. Carefully fit the hex-headed screws 51025 and locking edge washers 51025 and tighten them firmly.

  29. Damaged locking plates or locking washers bare must be replaced by new ones.

  30. Check axial contact surfaces of oil pump 39000 before fitting it, they must be absolutely clean and free of any damage

  31. Check the true running of the pump (fig 2-A)

  32. Lock carefully cap nut 39001 and ring nut 34151 with plate 34113 center the pipe of the pump with spring 34162 in holder 34161 the pipe of the pump should not touch the pump holder.

  33. See remarks for refilling with oil

Oil Pump

  1. All oil pump must be examined for wear and leakage after they have run for 16000 hours this check and possible repairs are performed by our service station this check should be repeated after each further 16000 hours

  2. After the oil pumps have been fitted the true running must be checked in any case if the turbocharger is equipped with an oil flow monitor consider when sending the oil pump 36000 or 39000 to the service station for overhaul, always join the connecting cable 32205 or 34205after fitting the overhauled oil pumps set up the oil flow monitors 32211 or 34211
