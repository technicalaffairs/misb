Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, hearing protection, gloves, ... etc.

  • Isolate the circuit breaker for the diesel engine.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set.

  • Cleaning articles (fluid, rags.… etc)

  • Feeler gauge, suitable lifting device

  • Multimeter, and common sets keys

  • Test equipment level "dip sticks"

Work to be Carried Out 

Before Isolated the Diesel Engine Mechanically and Electrically

  1. Operate the engine and check the following steps and reads all the available record values in the MRDS table 1

  2. Check the lubricating oil level in the tank.  In between up to the top mark on the dip-stick

  3. Check the fuel level in the fuel tank

  4. Check the exhaust gas temperature         800 oC  ± 20  oC

  5. Check the exhaust gases color             should be Colorless

  6. Check the engine oil temperature                80 oC : 90 oC

  7. Check any leakage from the piping connections to the diesel engine or its accessories (water, lube oil, fuel, and air)

  8. Check (by listening) the regular operation of the engine and whether there is no abnormal sounds or knocks

  9. Check the engine water temperature            65 oC : 85 oC

  10. Check the operation of suction and exhaust valves, check the condition of the valve springs

  11. Check (by touch) the crankshaft and cam-shaft bearings temperature rise on the case covers of the cylinder blocks, at the points where the bearings are located

  12. Check the starting valves for tightness by touching the starting air pipes heating on cylinder heads

After Isolated the Diesel Engine Mechanically and Electrically

  1. Check the oil level in the tank and / or refill it from the storage tank up to the top mark on the dipstick.

  2. Turn the handles of the coarse (lamination type) oil cleaner fitted in the oil circuit.

  3. Check the clogging of the fine oil cleaners' acc. to the differential pressure gauges data.

  4. Check the amount of leakage from stuffing box of fuel pump and prevented it by retighten the gland bolts or replace the sealing ring {it must be dropped some drops to cool the gland}.

  5. Check the temperature of the crankshaft bearings by touching the side lids of the upper engine case, thus the safety of operation is increased.

    At higher temperature it is necessary to dismantle the lids and to check the condition of bearing cca 10 min after engine stopping.

  6. Check the suction pipe for tightness. Imperfect tightness will manifest itself in the output of the engine and remove any leakage if found.

  7. Check tightness of the cylinder heads, incase of un-tightness, tighten the bolts of cylinder heads. 

  8. De-water the air vessel with head.

  9. Check the lubrication of rocker arms and valve guide and adjusted the regulation valves of lubrication if needed.

  10. Keep the surface of the engine clean.

  11. Check oil viscosity.

  12. Check air filter.

  13. Remove the cylinder head and check the suction and exhaust valves for tightness

    1. Remove the carbon deposits from exhaust valve seats.

    2. Re-machining  the valve on the milling machine and grind them.
