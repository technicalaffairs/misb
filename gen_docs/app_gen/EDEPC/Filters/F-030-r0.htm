Introduction

This document out lines the equipment inspection I1 which must be performed  for the  full gas Strainer every six  months as indicated by M6 in the title block or  the periodic fuel gas analysis report  refers to abnormal or strange impurities or liquid contained with the fuel gas came from the purchasers lines

The first draft initiated by eng. Amr Ahmed Gad, tested by eng. El-Sayed Mahmoud El-kholy, accepted by general manager eng. Sawsan El Lithe, revised by  eng. Nasser Bahgat MPIS coordinator, and approved by eng. Solman Zaki Soliman general manager of inspection and quality insurance.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence with  caution marks surrounding the work area is required 

  • Wear your safety helmet, safety shoes, eye glass

  • Insure that no spark source or lighting cigarettes exist

  • Insure that the inlet valve firmly closed

  • Insure that the valve is opened and plugged  and the vented gas stopped

  • The strainer screen must be cleaned by solvent  detergent

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set.

  • Flat mirror

  • Rags, cleaning fluid.

 

 

Work to be carried out 

  1. During operation, clean by opening blow – down valve, (with exposing the flat mirror) from dirt, scale, etc.

  2. During shutdown if there is dirt, scale, other strange things you  must close the inlet valve and the line after the inlet valve vented to atmosphere, and open the blow-down valve

  3. Loose the strainer flange bolts 

  4. Take out the strainer and clean with the suitable solvent cleaner

  5. Reassemble the strainer and retighten the flange and flange bolts

  6. Close the blow - down   valve

  7. Open the inlet gas valve slightly until the line after the inlet valve and its priming and pressurized

  8. Close the vent valve and its plug

  9. Complete to open the inlet fuel gas valve
