Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection I2, which must be performed on yearly bases as indicted by Y1 in the title block.

 

The preventive maintenance of the water monitors should carry out monthly and once a year. When a tank is on fire, the foam system will protect the tank itself, and the water monitors will provide an adequate water cooling of the closed fuel tank (s) or to give coverage to fire fighting personnel and the salvage team.

Safety Precautions

  • Disconnect the power supply of the oil pumps

  • Check the main post indicator valve on the main network i,. (close) position

  • Check the water valves in (close) position

  • Wear the safety shoes, and the helmet

 

 

 

 

 

Tools and Equipment

  • Wrench set.

  • Screwdriver set.

  • Pliers.

  • Greasing gun.

 

Work to be carried out

  1. Be sure that the oil tank is filled-up with mineral oil.

  2. Be sure t/hat the electrical panel is correctly connected with the power supply switching on the main switch (380/3/50 Hz).

  3. The monitors must be tested in all their functions, controlling the movements utilizing the hydraulic power pack.

  4. Control the monitor movements in emergency condition operating directly on the monitor hand wheels.

  5. Control the opening / closure movements of the water interception valves either through the hydraulic control or through the manual one.

  6. Be sure that the water butterfly valves are easily movable by their emergency hand wheels.

  7. Operate alternatively the hand wheels (15)and control the horizontal and vertical movements for both monitors.

  8. Be sure that all piping connections and equipment's bolts are correctly tightened.

  9. Be sure that the oil tubing is correctly connected and fixed on its cable tray.

  10. Be sure that all rotating joints, external shaft, and ball bearings are suitably greased.

Oil filling

  1. Refill the oil storage tank with mineral oil, until the max. level is reached.

  2. Switch on the main pump.

  3. Set up the relief valve at 50 bar (clockwise = increase).

  4. Operate alternatively the oil distributors until obtaining a soft movement of the unit driven.

  5. During this operation the oil level decreases, due to piping filling thus, an adequate quantity of oil should be added.

  6. With the by-pass valve of the monitor in "MAN" position, the lines filling operation should be easier.

  7. In case of irregular movements of the monitor, due to air trapped on the lines each higher tubing connection must be loosen allowing a free air vent until arrival of pure oil.

  8. When all the movements are correctly operated and the oil level is stable the filling operation has been completed.
