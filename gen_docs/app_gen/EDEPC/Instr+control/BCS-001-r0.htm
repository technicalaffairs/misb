Introduction

This document outlines the testing procedures P1, which must be performed on an annually bases  as indicated by Y1 in the title block.

There are eight burners per unit manufactured by Forney Company. The major parts of each burner are (solenoid valve, limit switch and spark system). They are installed on the movable parts of the burner such as oil gun, igniter gun, fuel valve and air register.

They are used for moving these parts from “closed” position to “opened” position and vice versa and for giving the indication of these parts .In addition to that, the Spark Rod is used for igniting the light fuel with a High Voltage Electric spark for starting a Burning Operation.

Safety Precaution 

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff must carry the suitable ID safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes ... etc.

  • The boiler must be off.

  • Close manual isolating valves for all main fuel.

  • Disconnect power supply.

Tools and Equipment

  • Safety helmet, Shoes, and rags.

  • Screw Driver Kit.

  • Digital voltmeter.

  • 110 AC volt External Power Supply.

 

Work to be Carried out

Preparation

  1. Review Technical reference material and manufacture’s manual.

  2. Obtain copy of (solenoid valve, limit Switch and Spark System) field maintenance File.

  3. Remove Solenoid and Spark Transformer Terminals by disconnecting wires (in the Terminal Cabinet).

Testing Procedure (solenoid valve)

  1. Connect solenoid terminal to 110 AC Voltage external power supply.    

  2. Turn On the Power Supply Switch.

  3. Check the Air output from the Solenoid.

  4. If there is Air output equal to the supplied Air then the Solenoid is OK.

  5. If not, you have two estimated problems.
    The first is the damage of the solenoid coil and you can measure it's resistance with the Multimeter-if it is bad you must replace it with a new coil.
    The second is of the Solenoid parts (O rings, vent strainer & Spool).

  6. If the problem still exists, you have to replace all the solenoid with a new one.

Testing Procedure (Limit Switch)

  1. When the solenoid valve energized to move

  2. The movable parts (GUNES_VALVES) to the open

  3. Direction

  4. Check the open limit Switch point by voltmeter

  5. When the solenoid valve energized to move the movable parts (GUNES_VALVES) to the closed direction

  6. Check the closed limit Switch point by voltmeter

  7. If these points are ok the limit Switch is ok

  8. Else then you have two estimated problems.

  9. The first adjust the movable parts guide.

  10. The Second adjust the limit Switch lever. Or replace it with a new one.

 

 

Testing Procedure (Spark System)     

  1. Retract out the rod of the Spark           

  2. Connect Transformer Terminals to 110 AC Voltage External Power supply.    

  3. Turn On the Power Supply Switch.

  4. Check the generated Spark and note its color.

  5. If the generated Spark appeared good, then the Spark System is OK.

  6. If not, you have two estimated Procedures.

  7. The first is to clean the rod and its head.

  8. The Second is to replace all Spark with a new one.

Return Solenoid Valve and Spark System to Service

  1. Return solenoid valve and spark system to service by disconnecting it from the 110 AC voltage power supply. 

  2. Reconnect the wires to the cabinet termination.

  3. Connect main power.

  4. Open manual isolating valves.

  5. Place a copy of test result in the field maintenance file.

  6. Repeat all the previous steps for all other Burners.
