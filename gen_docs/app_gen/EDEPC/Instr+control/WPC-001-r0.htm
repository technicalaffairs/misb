Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection checklist I1, to be carried out every three months as indicated by M3 in the title block.

The Control & Protection of Feed Water Pump consist  of:

Release and Protection feed water pumps:

a- Level Switches

b- Flow Transmitters.

c- RTDs

d- Sol. Valves of Minimum Flow

e- Limit Switches on Suction and discharge Valves

f- Back rotation device

g- Pressure switches.

h- Local gauge.

Control of main flow valves

a- Position transmitters

b- Flow transmitters

c- Limit switch & torque switch

For testing protection system  perform the following:

Each protection loop have ( sensor, switch, software logic, I/O electronic modules and final control element test )

Safety Precautions

  • A work order must be issued.

  • The maintenance staff should be qualified and carry the suitable safety ID category.

  • Wear safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, anti static hand probe to avoid electrical shock …etc

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required   

  • The pump must be off

Tools and Equipment

  • Digital multi-meter.

  • Screw driver kit, manual tools.

  • Oil spry cleaner & Teflon roll.

  • Current injector.

  • Spanner.

Work to be Carried Out

Preparation

  1. Obtain a copy of ( limit switches, torque switches, pressure switches , RTD transmitter, level transmitter, flow transmitter, junction box ) maintenance manuals.

  2. Obtain a copy of schematic loop wiring diagram for any protection signal, and data sheet.

feed water pumps

  1. Check pressure switches of each pump and make maintenance for it ( clean micro switch, recalibrate ).

  2. Check flow transmitter for each pump and make maintenance for it (clean & function check, recalibration ).

  3. Check RTD on each pump ( measuring temperature, check the signal )

  4. Check local gauge for right calibration.

  5. Check limit switch & torque switch for each valves and make maintenance for it (clean & function check).

Solenoid valves

  1. Clean moving parts for sol .valves.                                                                                                     

  2. Check the "O" ring.

  3. Measure resistance for coil.

  4. Check wiring up to junction box.

  5. Check limit switches for valves.

  6. Check signal up to ovation screen and output command from ovation control.

  7. Check time for opening and closing valves.
