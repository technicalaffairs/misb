Introduction

This document out lines the testing procedures P1, to be performed every three months as indicated by M3 in the title block.

This document outlines how to check & schedule service CO%&CO2%&O2% measuring devices.

These gases are the exhausts in the boiler, which their percentages must be measured by these devices to process them before getting out to the atmospheric air by the chimney.

by getting samples from 2 levels from the boiler and get into the analyzer in the device, their results appear on the indicating meter and recorders.

Tools and Equipment

  • Suitable tool set,

  • Safety helmet and safety shoes, ... etc.

  • Nitrogen cylinder

  • Pressure meter

  • Test gas cylinder: [mixture of inert gas and a certain concentrations of the gas component which is to be measured by the analyzer, the concentration should be 60% : 100% of the FINOR measuring range.]

  • For testing CO % & CO 2% measuring

  • Air: for testing CO2 % measuring.

  • Blower, Screw driver.

  • Digital Multimeter type: Fluke 85.

  • Cotton

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Make a leakage test to check the sample gas lines inside, outside the gas analyzer, and the props inside the boiler as follow:

  2. FINOR to be switched off.

  3. "Sample gas out" connection to be provided with gas tight closure.

  4. Connect a pressure meter to the sample gas line.

  5. Apply a pressure of about 0.1 bar (nitrogen) to the sample gas line, and make the gas tight closure also on the gas inlet so that the entire sample gas rout is closed.

  6. The pressure drop in the closed system should be smaller than 0.5 bar for every 4 minutes of waiting time.

  7. If this is not the case, check the sample gas lines, filter leaking, screwing not correct, hoses porous.

  8. Correct to exactly 21Vol by means of the potentiometer.

  9. Carry out the monthly procedures as MPIS document no. CIS-001-r0.

Return the Device to Services

  1. Connect to sample gas and switch to desired measuring range.
