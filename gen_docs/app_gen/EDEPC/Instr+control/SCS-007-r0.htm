Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection checklist I1, to be performed after 30 days operation or 25 start with 5 fire mode as indicated in the title block

The first draft initiated by eng. Adel Abdel Aziz, tested by eng. Abdelel Meneim Ibrahem Haikal 8 June 2003, reviewed by eng. Abd El-Momem El-emam at EDEPCo. accepted by general director eng. Yousry Abd El-maksuod and approved by eng. Mostafa Allam head sector of Talkha PS

Safety Precautions

  • Unit must be shutdown.

  • Work order must be issued

  • Wear your safety helmet, shoes ... etc

    •

Tools and Equipment

  • 2 Spanner 25 mm

  • 2 Gasket

  • multimeter

  • megger

  • Spray contact cleaner

  • Sand paper (fine)

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Check the spark plug assembly for freedom from binding

  2. To disassemble the cylinder assembly

  3. Remove the four bolts from the top of the cylinder

  4. Remove the top of the cylinder and the piston

  5. Remove all foreign material such as dust, dirt or chips from the cylinder and piston assemblies

  6. Remove all oxidation and galling marks. correct the cause of galling

  7. Clean the electrodes, and set the gaps of the electrode between 0.070 to 0.100 inch

  8. Check the sparking  by applying ignition voltage to the spark plugs (16 kv ac) from ignition transformer

  9. Verify that the spark plug shell has been tack welded to the piston rod with 1/4" welds along every other flat of the hex section of the shell

  10. Check the cable isolation by megger

If arcing does not occur at the tip of the electrodes, perform the following

  1. Again clean the electrodes, and check the gaps of the electrode between 0.070 to 0.100 inch

  2. Check the voltage to the ignition transformer (116 V ac )

  3. Check the spark plug lead voltage. disconnect the lead from the spark plug and apply voltage to ground

If no arcing occurs between the lead tip and ground, the transformer or the lead is shorted and should be replaced. If arcing occur, the spark plug is faulty and should be examined for an open or short circuit, as follows

  1. check an open circuit by determining continuity between the center electrode and the input terminal

  2. Check a short circuit by determining circuit continuity between the center electrode and ground
