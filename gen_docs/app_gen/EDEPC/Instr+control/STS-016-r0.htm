Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category

    •

  • Personal protective equipment must be used safety shoes, safety helmet, ground strap ...etc.

    •

  • Unit must be shut down

Tools and Equipment

  • Suitable tool set,

  • Safety helmet and safety shoes, ... etc.

  • Digital voltmeter

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Remove two servo valves (stop & control) from the unit.

  2. Check the oil seal and change it if damage.

  3. Clean filters for two servo valves (stop & control).

  4. Clean slots for two servo valves (stop & control).

  5. Measure two coil resistor for each valve which must be in permissive limit (1.08 kW) and if reading is not correct change the valve.

  6. Assemble two servo valve ( stop & control ) again in unit.

  7. Measure output voltage from stop/speed ratio valve servo card (SSVD) in Speedtronic on LCTB1 (C 10,11 & C13,14) to check if o/p voltage (6.5 ± 0.2) VDC (operation limits for servo valve 90 SR )

  8. Measure the same o/p voltage from SSVD card beside stop / speed ratio valve in ( JB – 2 ) on point (19-20) & (17 – 18) to check if o/p voltage run servo valve 90 SR.

  9. Measure output voltage from gas control valve card (SSVG) in Speedtronic on LCTB1 (C 22,23 & C25,26) to check if output voltage (6.5 ± 0.2) VDC (operation limits for servo valve 65 GC ).

  10. Measure the same o/p voltage from SSVG card beside stop / speed ratio valve in (JB – 2) on point (21,22) & (23– 24) to check if o/p voltage run servo valve 65 GC.

  11. If there is different in reading o/p voltage of SSVD & SSVG cards from resistance R92 (3) FROM ssvd & resistance R90 (1) from SSVG

  12. If two cards SSVD & SSVG not respond we change two cards SSVD & SSVG and and repeat steps from (6 to 9)
