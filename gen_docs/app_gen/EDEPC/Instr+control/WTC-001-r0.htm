Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection checklist I1, to be carried out every six months as indicated by M6 in the title block.

Water Treatment is used in several purposes like drinking, industrial used and services

Water Treatment at Ataka Power Station consists of:

1) Demineralization system

2) Raw water treatment

3) Make-up water system

4) Drinking water

We focus on  Demineralization system which can be used to remove the salt content of the water after the KSU-reactor nearly completely so that it can be used as make up water for the boiler.

The Principal Parts of the Demineralization Plant are :

1. Activated charcoal filters (1x drinking water)

2. Caction exchangers

3. CO2-sprinklers

4. Double – stage anion exchangers

5. Mixing-bed exchangers

6. Regenerative station

7. Neutralization plant

For Testing Water Treatment Plant perform the following:

1. Check control valves on pipes and filters  tank levels.

2. Check tank levels

3. Check instruments & measuring devices (ph, conductivity) .

Safety Precautions

  • A work order must be issued.

  • The maintenance staff should be qualified and carry the suitable safety ID category.

  • Wear safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, anti static hand probe to avoid electrical shock …etc

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required       

  • The system must be off

Tools and Equipment

  • Digital multi-meter, Fluke 187

  • Screw driver kit, manual tools .

  • Oil spray cleaner & Teflon roll.

  • Pneumatic Calibrator.

  • External 24 Vdc power supply.

  • Current injector.

Work to be Carried Out

Preparation

  1. Obtain a copy of individual devices (Flow transmitter, pneumatic valve, PH Transmitter, Conductivity Transmitter, Local Gauge) maintenance manuals

  2. Obtain a copy of wiring diagram of system.

Procedures

  1. Check the flow transmitters of the main raw water lines.

  2. Check the PH transmitter

  3. Check the conductivity transmitters of each line.

  4. Check pneumatic solenoid valves.

  5. Check local gauges.
