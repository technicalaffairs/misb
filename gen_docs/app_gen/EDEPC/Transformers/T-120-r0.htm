Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection I1, which must be performed on monthly bases as indicated by M1 in the title block.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued

  • The maintenance staff should carry the suitable ID safety category

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required

  • Wear the appropriate safety equipment as safety shoes, helmet

  • Transformer must be out of service and isolated and grounded from all sides.

  • Don't go too close to energized areas on the transformer while it is operating too close, means as a general understanding within a spherical area whose center is the top of the bushing and whose radius is the full length from the top of the bushing flange (ground potential) of the bushing.

Tools and Equipment    

  • Complete maintenance tool set

  • Binocular or infra red scope

  • Voltage indicator, new silica gel

Work to be carried out

  1. General visual inspection on transformer and oil leaks and repair any exist damage

  2. Inspect all painted surface of the transformer and repair any exist damage

  3. Check oil level in the tank, conservator and oil filled bushings

  4. Make visual inspection of all cooling fans and repair any exist damage

  5. Check oil temperature indicator

  6. Check winding temperature indicator

  7. Visual inspection of transformer control cabinet

  8. Check the silica gel breather and replace the abnormal by new

  9. Check the correct pressure of bushing sealing oil indicator

  10. Check any alarms on control panel and replace any damage indicator lamps

  11. Check and test the indicator lamps on control panel and replace any damage

Transformer Temperatures, Load and Voltages

  1. Read oil and winding temperature, check that the maximum temperatures are not being exceeded, also check that the temperature is acceptable by comparing it with past entries in the inspection log.

  2. Voltages and load must be kept within the specified limits, which are in the instruction manual.

Oil Level

  1. Read the values on the oil level gauges, check that the oil level readings are correct for the oil temperature, in compliance with the graph on the nameplate located near them, (in nameplate) and this graph also appears in the transformers instruction manual.

Audible Noise and Loosely Bolted Parts           

  1. Listen and check for any abnormal sounds from the main tank and accessories as a rattling sound indicates loose bolts and a repetitive scraping sound indicates worn fan or oil pump bearings.

Loosely Bolted Parts

  1. Visually inspect bolted connections in the transformer's main and auxiliary circuits, foundation bolts and all other bolted connections to ensure that all parts are secure.

 Oil Leakage

  1. Check for leakage from the transformer tank, valves, pipes, gauges, gasket joints welds etc.

Dehydrating Breather

  1. The silica-gel absorbent inside the breather bottle is normally magenta blue, but turns pink if excessive moisture is present, dry or replace the silica gel if more than 70% is pink.

  2. Check that the sight-glass level of the sealing oil in the oil cup is between the HIGH and LOW marks, it must be correct at all times because excessive sealing oil in the cup may contaminate the silica-gel, while a shortage of oil may not seal the conservator properly.

Pressure Relief Device

  1. Check for cracks, oil or gas leakage or any sign of damage to the pressure plate.

Cooling Equipment

  1. Check for an excessive buildup of grime on the radiator surface and remove it.

  2. Check that any oil flow indicator installed in the system shows that oil is circulating.

  3. Check that radiator valves are in the open position.

Bushings

  1. If the bushings are equipped with an oil gauge, use binoculars to visually check the oil level and for signs of leakage.

  2. Check the porcelain for cracking and other damage.

  3. Check that there is no overheating of the terminals; inspect the terminals for heat discoloration using binoculars or infra red scope.

Protective Relays

  1. Check the transformer's protective relays at the site control room and check that they have not actuated.
