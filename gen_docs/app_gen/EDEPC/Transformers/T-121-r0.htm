Introduction

This document outlines the minor maintenance procedures P1, which must be performed every ten years as indicated by Y10 in the title block.

The first draft initiated by eng. Amr Gaber, revised by eng. Mohamed Abbas Ahmad, tested by eng Mohammed El-bayaa, accepted by general manager eng. Mohamed Abbas Ahmad, approved by eng. Mahmoud El-Naquib  head sector of Ayoun Moussa PS.

This document certified by eng. Mohamed Fayez Hassan EDEPC MPIS coordinator.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required

  • Wear safety helmet, shoes, breathing mask, and harness....

  • Transformer isolation and earthing on all its sides is required

  • There is danger of falling into the oil containment pit when working near large power transformer

  • A proper well fixed ladder should be used for climbing on tap of the transformer tank

Tools and Equipment

  • Megger (500 - 5000) volt

  • Multimeter

  • Air blower

  • Complete maintenance tool set

  • Cleaning solvent

Work to be carried out

Transformer oil system

  1. Check the oil temperature indicates whether the oil or winding temperature lies in a satisfactory range in relation to their values in the test report, load condition and ambient temperature. Overloading of transformer should be made only in accordance with applicable standard. 

  2. Check the flanged joints and welds for oil leakage

  3. All flanges seals and welds must be checked for oil leaks

  4. If any leakage found we must immediately seal by retightening the screws replacing the seals or by welding

  5. If the finishing coat is touched up in time, the paint coat in general needs not be completely renewed

  6. Clean the painted surface with a solvent soaked rag or a steam jet blower

  7. Paint all the service of the transformer

 OFF load tap changer

  1. The OFF load tap changer must be operated with the transformer is de-energized

  2. Traverse all taps for cleaning the tap changer contacts

  3. The cupper parts of the contacts are tarnishing when immersed in oil

Cooling system

  1. Check the flanged joints and weld for oil leakage

  2. All flanges seals and welds must be checked for oil leaks

  3. If any leakage found we must immediately seal by retightening the screws replacing the seals or by welding

  4. Inspect condition of all cooling fans

  5. Check for cracks in fan blades, test all fan motors

  6. Check for abnormal bearing noise 

  7. Cleaning the coolers:

  8. Use water pressure to clean radiators and transformer tank walls do not direct the stream of water directly at the cooling fans

  9. The unsealed wiring must be covered by plastic covering to avoid s.c in wiring system

  10. The coolers must be cleaned at least when the oil temperature tends to rise while transformer load and inlet temperatures of air or water remain constant

 Control cabinet

  1. Check the heating and lighting of the control cubicle

  2. The thermostat of the control cubicle heating

  3. Check the heating for correct functioning

  4. Check cubicle lighting for correct functioning (door contact)

  5. Inspect transformer control cabinet and blow out dust

  6. Check condition of door seal

  7. Check all electrical connection for tightness

  8. Check proper operation of space heaters

  9. Test operation of fan control

 Bushings

  1. Check for oil leaks bushings, bushing domes

  2. The pipes leading to the bushings must be checked for oil leaks

  3. Clean the porcelain surfaces on all bushing

  4. The intervals, which must be specified for each application, are largely dependent upon local air pollution

  5. The average cleaning interval is 1year

  6. Check the bushing terminals

  7. Check the bushing terminals for light fit

  8. Check the oil level indicators

  9. The oil levels in the conservator or in other vessels must be checked by reference to the sight glasses or oil level indicators

Buchholtz and Auxiliary Device

  1. Open bleed valves on buchholz relay unit a small amount of oil escapes test alarm and trip circuit using the test button next to the vent valve

  2. Check all electrical connection for tightness

  3. Check the corresponding signals in the control room

  4. Check all electrical connection for tightness

  5. Inspection of control circuit

  6. Operating test

High & Low Winding

  1. Take and record insulation resistance readings (megger readings), high voltage winding to low voltage winding, high voltage to earth, low voltage to earth

  2. Compare readings to historical data

  3. Take and record capacitance dissipation factor percentage tan Δ

  4. Compare readings to historical data

  5. Perform transformation ratio tests in lowest, highest and neutral tap position

  6. Measure high voltage windings (phase to phase)

  7. Measure low voltage windings (phase to phase)

  8. Compare readings to historical data readings taken at all points of tap changer

  9. Take and record DC resistance winding readings

            * Each high voltage phase to neutral

            * Each high voltage phase to phase

            * Each low voltage phase to phase

  10. Compare readings to historical data
