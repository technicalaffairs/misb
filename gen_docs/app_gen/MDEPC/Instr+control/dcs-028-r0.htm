Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection I1, which must be performed on weekly bases as indicated by W1 in the title block.

The IC687 Bus Transmitter Module (BTM) is used in a FIP single rack redundancy system. It provides a path for Redundancy communications when connected to the Redundancy Communications Module (RCM) in the other half of the two–section redundancy rack. Each section of this rack requires a Bus Transmitter Module and a Redundancy Communications Module. It also provides a high performance parallel interface to the programming device.

Features

  • Required for FIP Redundant Systems.

  • Two Bus Transmitter Modules required per system (in dual backplane redundancy rack).

  • Three LED indicators provide module, programmer port, and expansion port status.

  • No DIP switches to set, easy software configuration into MFC system.

  • High Performance Parallel Programmer Interface.

Status Indications

  • Three green LEDs at top of module provide module status information as described below.

  • The top LED (MODULE OK) LED is ON when the CPU software completes its power–up configuration of the BTM, and has polled (or attempted to poll) each expansion rack in the system. It is OFF when any of these conditions are not met.

  • The middle LED (Programmer Port Enabled) is the Programmer Port Active LED. This LED is either blinking or ON when the programmer and the MFC are communicating. It is OFF when they are not communicating. Note that this port is not used in communications between the Windows programmer and the PLC.

  • The bottom LED (Expansion Port Enabled) provides the status of the redundancy communications link. This LED is either blinking or ON when the BTM is communicating with the Redundancy Communications Module connected to it through the IC697CBL803 cable. It is OFF when they are not communicating.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued

  • Maintenance staff should be well trained and qualified.

  • Caution marks must be hanged on the work place, safety rope around the work place.

  • Removing a Module

    • First, make sure rack power is turned off. Then, use a medium-blade, slotted screwdriver to loosen the top and bottom holding screws just enough to free the module from the rack. Note: Screws are held captive in the module, so they do not have to be removed from the module.

    • Grasp the board firmly at the top and bottom of the board cover with your thumbs on the front of the cover and your fingers on the plastic rack clips on the back of the cover.

    • Squeeze the rack clips on the back of the cover with your fingers to disengage the clips from the rack rail, and then pull the board firmly toward you until it clears the rack.
