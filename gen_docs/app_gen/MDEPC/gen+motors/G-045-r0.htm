Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued

  • Personal protective equipment must be used (safety shoes, helmet, breathing mask, safety glasses)

  • Maintenance staff should be well trained and qualified

  • Caution marks must be hanged on the work place, safety rope around the work place

Tools and Equipment

  • Brushes

  • Blower

  • Cleaning fluids such as freon - TF, chlorothene

  • Megger. (500 - 5000 V)

Work to be carried out

 

Inspection and maintenance of stator

  1. Check the existence of oil and any other foreign matters remaining inside generator

  2. Check each coil for any distortion, insulation, deterioration, damage or soiling

  3. Check ventilation duets for any deformation, spacers that have slipped out, binding ropes loosened, wedges seasoned or loosened and connection parts discolored by overheating

  4. Check the stator core for any discoloration or damage by overheating clamping bolts, loosened and ventilation ducts soiled

  5. Insulation resistance of the stator core through - bolt to clamp is measured with 1000 V megger and the minimum acceptable value should be more than 100 mega ohms for operation

  6. Insulation resistance of the stator coil is measured with 1000 V megger and the acceptable value should be more than 50 mega ohms for operation

  7. Check if temperature detectors and lead wires are damaged or loosened

Inspection and maintenance of rotor

  1. Insulation resistance of rotor coil is measured with 500 V megger and the minimum acceptable value of more than 10 mega ohms for operation

  2. Check to see if balance weights and locking stoppers are loosened

  3. Check to see if journal parts of shaft are worn or damaged

  4. Leakage test for the center bore is conducted with nitrogen gas at radial lead and the pressure drop is considered suitable for operation

  5. After leakage test, the center bore should be bowled with nitrogen gas at 0.5 -1 bar gage sufficiently and closed keenly with plug on remaining the pressure at 0.5 bar gauge
