Introduction

This document outline the major maintenance procedure P1, which must be performed when the performance of the heat exchanger reduce (high pressure drop or insufficient heat transfer)

The heat exchanger function is to transfer heat from closed cooling water system to service cooling water system

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should be carry the suitable safety  category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required

  • Wear your safety helmet, shoes, gloves and glasses

  • Isolate the heat changer

  • Cool the heat exchanger

  • Drain the heat exchanger from the water (both sides : hot & cold)

  • Make sure the heat exchanger is not under pressure and empty before starting work

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set

  • No special tools are required. A ratchet spanner is all that is needed for maintenance and repair (wrench 80)

  • Cleaning fluid and nylon brushes

Work to be carried out

Opening the Heat Exchanger (Figure 2)

  1. Dismantle any connection to the cover

  2. Dismantle the heat exchange movable cover

  3. Clean the thread of the tightening bolts

  4. Apply a thin film of oil to the thread

  5. We advise painting a diagonal line cross the plate pack to ensure that the plates are reassembled in the right order

  6. Measure the current A-dimension and record it

  7. Remove bolts (1)

  8. Slacken nuts 2.3, and 4 alternatively so that the movable cover can move parallel with the frame plate

  9. Remove bolts 3 and 4

  10. Loosen nuts 2 alternatively

 

Figure 2

Taking Out the Plate

  1. If two or more plates have stuck together, they must be separated carefully so that the gaskets are kept on the correct plate

  2. The plates support each other in pairs. If a plate ha been so damaged, it must be replaced with an identical one, the adjacent plate must also be taken out of the heat exchanger

Cleaning the Plates

  1. Open the heat exchanger according to a.m.

  2. Steel wool or brushes of carbon steel must not be used

  3. Clean  the heat transfer surface of plate in the first step by rinsing with a powerful jet of water and scrubbing with a nylon or similar brush

  4. Take care not to damage the gaskets

  5. Wipe the gaskets dry with a cloth

  6. Inspect carefully the lower portion of each plate as hung in the unit  and clean appropriately, as this is the primary area where residual solid material tends to accumulate

  7. Do not use hydrochloric acids, or water containing in excess of (300 ppm) chlorides with stainless steal plates

  8. Limit cleaning solution concentration to 4% strength with temp not exceeding 60 C°

Cleaning the Gasket Groove

  1. Clean the plates from residues of old gasket

  2. Small patches of glue that are securely stuck to the gasket groove may remain there they provide an excellent foundation for the new gasket

  3. Wash the gasket groove so that it is completely free of oil and other greasy substances using a rag and acetone or other solvent not containing chlorine compounds

  4. Then let the plate dry off

Gluing the Gasket

  1. Glue is applied with a small flat brush those parts of the plate's gasket groove in which the gasket lies

  2. Inspect all gasket and surfaces that lie against the gasket

  3. The gasket is then placed into position on the plate

  4. After drying for about 30 seconds, the glue holds the rubber gasket firmly in place in the gasket groove

  5. The plate must then be held under light pressure with the aid of other plates or a stiff sheet of other material of suitable weight for about half an hour

  6. When the glue joint dried, the gasket should be coated with talc to prevent the plates subsequently sticking to each other

  7. The plates are then ready to assemble into the frame

Bostik        1782
3 M            EC 1099
Bond          Spray 77
Castrol       Plibond 20/30

Assembly

  1. Plates that have been provided with new gaskets must be checked to make sure that the gaskets are in the correct gasket groove

  2. Also check the half thickness gaskets on the first and last plates

  3. Use the plate sequencing specification sheet when started assembly plates to make certain that the plate pack is assembled correctly

Tightening the Heat Exchanger

  1. The plate pack must be compressed to a specific thickness - the A- dimension

  2. The A - dimension +/- 3 % gives the inside length in millimeters between the fixed and moveable cover
    A     → 0.5 x number of plates = 0.5 x 320
    A = 1600 mm     ± 3%

 

 

 

Tightening Sequence (Figure 2)

  1. The nuts be tightened alternately

  2. The movable cover plate must be moved parallel to the frame at all times and not drawn out of alignment

  3. Tighten bolts (2) alternately

  4. As the resistance increased also tighten bolts 3 and 4 always alternately

  5. Tighten bolts (1)

  6. Check the A - dimension along the heat exchanger

Troubleshoot

 

Possible reasons Trouble

  • Gasket failure

  • Possible corrosion in the areas between the ring and diagonal gasket

Leakage through the gasket leakage groove

  • High pressure or temperature

  • The compression (A - dimension) is incorrect

  • Incorrectly positioned or damaged gaskets

External leakage on the side of a plate pack at any position except the a.m position

  • A hole / crack in a plate caused by corrosion or mechanical damage

Internal leak

  • Cracks in the metal liner

  • The ring gasket on the first plate not sealing correctly to the liner

Leak occurs adjacent to a frame cover near a port

  • Foreign particles or other deposit's in the heat exchanger

  • Deviation in the volumetric flow

  • Inadequate pump performance

Impaired PHE performance (In the form of high press drop or insufficient heat transfer)
