Introduction

This procedure details the testing procedures and calibration I1, which recommended to be carried out every year as indicated by Y1 in the title block.

The TTG 7111 has been designed for the protection of Steam turbine Generators and the downstream users against the risk of excessive over voltages.

The excessive over voltages may occur when large loads are suddenly removed or when the A.V.R does not operate properly.

This relay shall be set at the maximum permissible operating voltage and time delayed to allow transient over voltages, originated by sudden load shedding to be adequately regulated by the excitation system.  

The TTG 7111 Relay is supplied from a Voltage Transformers (VT) providing secondary voltage to the relay.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category, (safety helmet, safety shoes, ... etc).

  • Issue work permit including the team work name.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • The unit (Generator & Transformer) must be out of service.  

Tools and Equipment

  • Secondary Voltage injection test set.

  • Complete tool set.

  • Air blower.

  • Contact cleaner (CRC).
     

Work to be carried out 

Preparation

  1. Look at Over Voltage Maintenance file (59) and compare the relay setting with those shown on the test sheet.

  2. Supply the relay with its nominal auxiliary supply: the green LED will light.

 Testing the Relay

  1. Remove the terminals (10,11) from the relay.

  2. Connect the voltage terminals from the injection set to the relay points (10,11).

  3. Increase the injected voltage slowly till the pickup value.

  4. Record the P.U value in your test sheet.

  5. Decrease the injection voltage till the relay reset ( Drop out Value )

  6. Record the D.O value in your test sheet

  7. Increase the voltage again to (P.U) value.

  8. Stop the test set.

  9. Restart the test set (with Voltage = P.U value).

  10. The relay will operate, the timer will account and stop when the trip contact close.

  11. Make sure that lock out relays (86GT & 86T) operate with Under Voltage relay operate after its trip time.

  12. Record the time value in your test sheet.

  13. Make reset to the under voltage relay and lock out relays (86GT & 86T).

  14. Put C.B of the unit at local position.

  15. Close C.B of the unit.

  16. Return C.B of the unit to remote position.

  17. Trip C.B of the unit by Over Voltage Relay by increasing the injection voltage to the pickup value.

  18. Stop the tester.

Return Relay and Associated Equipment to Service

  1. Make reset to Over Voltage Relay, alarm indication and lockout relays (86GT&86T).

  2. Disconnect the test leads from the Relay.

  3. Reconnect the relay terminals points (10,11) to its normal position
