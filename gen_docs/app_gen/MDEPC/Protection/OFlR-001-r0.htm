Introduction

This procedure details the testing procedures and calibration I1, which recommended to be carried out every year as indicated by Y1 in the title block.

The FTT 7111 has been designed for the protection of power transformers against the risk of over fluxing.

 This can cause saturation of the magnetic circuit leading to an increase of the losses. The associated heating should not be maintained for prolonged periods in order to avoid premature ageing of the insulation, which could lead to internal faults to break down.

The flux in the magnetic circuit is proportional to the voltage applied to the transformer, and inversely proportional to its frequency. The FTT 7111 relay is supplied from a voltage transformer providing these data.

 An image of the flux value is created by means of an integrating circuit whose output voltage, proportional to the ratio v/f, is applied to an adjustable level detecting unit. .

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category, (safety helmet, safety shoes, ... etc).

  • Issue work permit including the team work name.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • The unit (generator & transformer) must be out of service.

Tools and Equipment

  • Secondary injection test set.

  • Test leads

  • AVO meter.

  • Complete tool set.

  • Air blower.

  • Contact cleaner (CRC).

Work to be carried out 

Preparation

  1. Look at Over Flux maintenance file and compare the setting on relay with those shown on the test sheet.

  2. Supply the relay with its nominal auxiliary supply: the green LED will light.

 Testing the Relay

  1. Remove voltage terminals (9,10) from the relay.

  2. Connect the voltage terminals from the injection set to the relay points (9,10).

  3. Increase the injected voltage slowly until pick up.

  4. Record the P.U value in your test sheet.

  5. Decrease the injected voltage until the relay reset (Drop out Value).

  6. Record the D.O value in your test sheet.

  7. Increase the voltage again to (P.U) value.

  8. Stop the test set

  9. Restart the test set (with voltage = P.U value).

  10. The relay will operate, the timer will account and stop when the trip contact close.

  11. Make sure that lock out relays (86GT&86T) operate with over fluxing relay operate.

  12. Record the time value in your test sheet.

  13. Make reset to the over fluxing relay and lock out relays (86GT & 86T).

  14. Put C.B of the unit at local position.

  15. Close C.B of the unit.

  16. Return C.B of the unit to remote position.

  17. Trip C.B of the unit by Over Fluxing Relay by increasing the injection current to the pickup value

  18. Stop the tester.

Return Relay and Associated Equipment to Service

  1. Make reset to over fluxing relay, alarm indication and lock out relays (86GT&86T).

  2. Disconnect the test leads from the relay.

  3. Reconnect voltage terminals (9-10) to the relay.

  4. Make sure PT secondary circuits are in normal condition.
