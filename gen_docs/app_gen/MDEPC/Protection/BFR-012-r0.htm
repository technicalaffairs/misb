Introduction

This procedure details the steps required to completely check the proper operation and calibration of the relay. It is recommended this procedure be carried out on an annually bases.

The document is formatted the way to minimize the possibility of error causing personal injury or damage to equipment.

P143 is designed around a common hardware and software platform in order to achieve a high degree of conformity between products, using a wide variety of protection and automatic control function, the MICOM over current relays can provide protection for many applications including over head line. P143 is provided by Synchro check and circuit breaker fail protection with tow stage breaker fail protection.

 Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued.

  • The maintenance staff should be trained, qualified, and carry the suitable ID safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear your safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes ... etc.

  • Make sure that CT secondary circuits are shorted before isolating the relay current circuits for test distance protection.

  • Line isolation is required.

  • Transmission Line must be grounded.

Tools and Equipment

  • ISA DRTS tester

  • Test leads.

  • PC laptop with necessary software {ISAWIZARD and ENER VISTA}

  • Tools bag.

 

Work to be Carried Out 

Preparation

  1. Make sure CT secondary circuits are shorted at the incoming terminal blocks before isolate the relay current circuits for test.

  2. Be sure that the DC power of the relay is correct by applying a DC voltmeter across points (J1-),(J2+) on the rear of the relay.

 

Reading the Setting from the Relay using the PC

  1. Connect the personal computer to the relay.

  2. Run MICOM S1 program.

  3. Switch ON ISA tester

  4. After ISA tester completely in service connect currents, voltages and binary input leads between ISA tester and test bluge (X50-1) for MCOM P143.

  5. Make short between points (X50-1 13&14) and points (X50-1 15&16)

  6. Read the setting and safe it as urgent file for P143 [name the feeder].

 

Breaker Failure Protection 

Circuit Breaker Failure

  1. Disable the other protection functions.

  2. Connect DC voltage to initiate breaker fail input from source voltage of the relay.

  3. GENERATE PRE-FAULT CONDITION [I=0.001A].

  4. Choose Delta ramp with step ∆I 0.05 Amp to get pickup current of the relay

  5. Select fault and short current value equal to (I Setting*1.05) to determine the operating time.          

Check Synchronizing

  1. Disable the other protection functions.

  2. Disconnect voltage terminals from terminal block XV01[1,4,7,10,14 AND 15]

  3. Use phase R-N [phase R tester] as Bus Bar voltage  on (XV01 point 14, 15) and phase S-N [phase S tester] as feeder voltage on [XV01N POINT 4,10]

  4. GENERATE PREFAULT CONDITION [VR=57 v, VS=45 v, VT=57 v].

  5. Choose Delta ramp with step ∆V= 5 V on Phase S with phase angle = 0ْ  ( i.e. VR and VS in phase ) to  get pickup voltage (∆V) of the relay.

  6. Choose Delta ramp with step ∆ Ø = 2ْ on phase S (VR and VS= 57 V) to get pickup phase angle (∆Ø) of the relay.

  7. Choose Delta ramp with step ∆ F = 1 Hz on phase S (VR and VS= 57 V, VR and VS in phase) to get pickup phase angle (∆F) of the relay.

 

 

 

 

Return Relay and associated Equipment to Service

  1. Enable any function which was disabled.

  2. Return relay to service by removing the test leads from the relay terminals and the test set.

  3. Make sure CT and VT secondary circuits are in normal condition.

  4. Remove the disturbance alarm LED`s on the relay which is caused by the test.

  5. Request feeder to be returned in service.

  6. Take pharos readings of all AC currents and voltages and compare them to those for normal load condition.

  7. Check that these reading agree with station meters (MW, Mvar, Amperes and power factor).

  8. Place a copy of the test result in the field maintenance file.
