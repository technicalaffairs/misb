Introduction

This procedure details the testing procedures and calibration I1, which recommended to be carried out every year as indicated by Y1 in the title block

This procedure details the steps required to completely check the proper operation and calibration of the relay. The relay must be tested every year. This relay is  a negative – phase – sequence time over current relay used primarily to protect generators against the effect of phase fault or unbalance load.

Since the relay is protecting the generator from damage caused by fault on the system rather than from  internal fault ,it is providing essential back –up protection .the heating effected on the rotor of synchrouns machine caused by unbalance currents can best be expressed as function of the negative phase sequence current and of time.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Issue work permit including the team work name.

  • The generator must be out of service.

  • Put C.B of  unit at test position.

  • Disconnect excitation system for A.C generator (switch off "41 AC" breaker).

Tools and Equipment

  • Secondary injection test set.

  • Clamp meter, Test leads

  • AVO meter

  • Complete tool set.

  • Air blower.

  •  Contact cleaner (CRC).

Work to be carried out

Preparation            

  1. Look at field maintenance file and compare the setting on relay with those shown on the test sheet.

  2. Check DC. Circuit for Over Current Relay.

Testing the relay

  1. Connect test leads between  the current terminals ( 3 – 4 ) on the relay.

  2. Connect test leads between  the trip terminals ( 1 – 2 ) on the relay to timer terminals on the test set.

  3. Increase the current slowly until the relay energize and regist (P.U) value .

  4. Decrease the current slowly until the relay denergize and regist (REST) value.

  5. Increase the current again to (P.U) value

  6. Stop the test set .

  7. Restart the test set (current = P.U value ).

  8. The relay will operate , the timer will account and stop when the trip contact close.

  9. Regist the time value.

  10.  Record these values in the test sheet.

  11. Connect test leads between  the current terminals ( 5 – 6 ) on the relay.

  12. Repeat steps from  ( 2 to 10 ) for phase ( S ) .

  13. Connect test leads between  the current terminals ( 7 – 8 ) on the relay.

  14. Repeat steps from  ( 2 to 10 ) for phase ( T ) .

Test C.B tripping circuit of the unit.

  1. Remove relay 52GTX .

  2. Close C.B. for the unit at test position.

  3. Trip C.B. for the unit by Negative – phase – sequence Relay.

Return Relay and Associated Equipment to Service

  1. Disconnect the test leads from the Relay.

  2. Make sure CT and PT secondary circuits are in normal condition.

  3. Make reset to Negative – phase – sequence relay.

  4. Reconnect excitation system for A.C generator (switch on "41 AC" breaker)..

  5. Reconnect relay 52GTX .

  6. make sure the spring of the C.B on discharge position .

  7. Put C.B in service position .
