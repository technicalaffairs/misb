Introduction

This document lists the testing procedures P1, which must be performed on an annually bases as indicated by Y1 in the title block.

The T60 differential protection devices are designed for the fast and selective short circuit protection of transformers, motors and generators and of other two, three or four phase winding arrangement.

This procedure details the steps required to completely check the proper operation and calibration of the relay. It is recommended this procedure be carried out on an annually bases.

The document is formatted the way to minimize the possibility of error causing personal injury or damage to equipment.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued.

  • The maintenance staff should be trained, qualified, and carry the suitable ID safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear your safety and healthy equipment as safety Helmet, safety shoes ... etc.

  • Equipment isolation and grounding is required.

  • Make sure that CT secondary circuits are shorted before isolating the current circuits for testing the protection relay.

Tools and Equipment

  • Safety Helmet, safety shoes ... etc

  • ISA DRTS tester, Test leads.

  • PC laptop with necessary software {ISAWIZARD and ENER VISTA}

  • Tools bag.

 

 

Work to be Carried Out

Steps to prepare for relay testing

Preparation

  1. Make sure CT secondary circuits are shorted at the incoming terminal blocks before isolate the relay current circuits for test.

  2. Ensure that the DC power of the relay is correct by applying a DC voltmeter across points (B5a, B6a) on the rear of the relay.

Reading the setting from the relay using the PC

  1. Connecting the personal computer to the relay.

  2. Run the Enervista program.

  3. Read the setting and safe it as URGENT FILE FOR T60 [Name the feeder].

For Main Transformer of CTG

Differential Protection Test

  1. Connecting the Personal Computer to the ISA tester.

  2. Be sure that ISA tester is connected to EARTH.

  3. Switch ON  ISA tester.

  4. After ISA tester completely in service connect currents, voltages and binary input leads between ISA tester and test plug for T60. 

  5. RUN  ISAWIZARD software >>>>> GENERATION & DISTRIBUTION RELAYS >>>> Xpro advanced >>>> MANUAL MODE

 NOW you can test the relay.

  1.  Insert the test plug in the terminal block BP14 or BP16 (see figure 1).

          NOTE:  

  • Do not forget to GENERATE PREFAULT CONDITION  [I=0.001A, V=0V].

  • A-1 choose Delta ramp with step ∆I 0.05 Amp to get pickup current of the relay.

  • A-2 choose Shoot with current value equal to 1.05 of pick up current to get pickup time of the relay.

  • A-3 to test 2nd stage of differential, disable 1st stage of differential then shoot by setting current.

2nd and 5th harmonic restraining test

  1. B-1-Repeat steps from A-1 with frequency 100Hz for 2nd harmonic test

  2. B-2-Repeat steps from A-1 with frequency 250Hz for 5th harmonic test.

  3. Choose STORE ALL TESTS >>>>

  4. Go to RESULTS from main menu chose HEADER and apply all information about these FEEDER and OK

  5. Now print the results and you have complete test results for these relay.

Earth Fault

  1. RUN  ISAWIZARD software and choose GENERATION AND DISTRIBUTION RELAY >>>> X-PRO >>>>MANUAL MODE>>>>

  2. Insert the test plug in the terminal block BP15 (see figure 1).

  3. Generate Pre-Fault Condition [I=0.001A].

  4. Disable distance function .

  5. Choose Delta ramp with step ∆I 0.05 Amp to get pickup current of the relay

  6. Select fault and shoot current value equal to (I Setting*1.05) to determine the operating time.
     
    Note: that: the injection should be on phase R only.

  7. Store all tests

Restricted Earth Fault

  1. Disable earth fault and differential protection functions

  2. Insert the test plug in the terminal block BP15 (see figure 1).

  3. GENERATE  Pre-fault Condition [I=0.001A].

  4. Choose Delta ramp with step ∆I 0.05 Amp to get pickup current of the relay

  5. Select fault and shoot current value equal to (I Setting*1.05) to determine the operating time.

  6. STORE ALL TESTS

  7. Now you have complete test results for these relay

Return relay and associated equipment to service

  1. Return relay to service by removing the test leads from the relay terminals and the test set.

  2. Make sure CT and V T secondary circuits are in normal condition.

  3. Remove the disturbance alarm LED`s on the relay which is caused by the test.

  4. Request feeder to be returned in service.

  5. Take pharos readings of all AC currents and voltages and compare them to those for normal load condition.

  6. Check that these reading agree with station meters (MW, Mvar, Amperes and Power Factor).

  7. Place a copy of the test result in the field maintenance file.
