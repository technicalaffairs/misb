Introduction

This procedure details the testing procedures and calibration I1, which recommended to be carried out every year as indicated by Y1 in the title block.

The DTM 7033, three-phase percentage biased differential relay. Provides a particularly satisfactory solution for the protection of rotating machines against internal faults and used for differential protection of alternating current machines against phase to phase and phase to ground faults. For an internal fault the relay will close its contacts.

If on external fault therefore the relay will not operate.

The first draft is initiated by Eng. Sherif Ahmed El Beyaliy, tested by Eng. Khalied Diab & Eng. Ehab Abuo El-wafa at 12 June 2008, and accepted by General Manager Eng. Saad Kheder and approved by Eng. Abd El-moatey El-rakaiby Head Sector of Mahmoudia Power Station.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Safety and healthy equipment must be used (safety helmet, safety shoes, ... etc).

  • Issue work permit including the team work name.

  • The generator must be out of service.  

  • Disconnect the 220k.v. C.B and  the transformer isolators (main and reserve).

Tools and Equipment

  • Secondary injection test set.

  • Clamp meter, Test leads.

  • AVO meter.

  • Complete tool set.

  • Air blower.

  • Contact cleaner (CRC).

Work to be carried out 

Preparation

  1. Look at field maintenance file and compare the setting on relay with those shown on the test sheet.

  2. Check DC. Circuit for for high speed differential Relay.

Testing the Relay

  1. Connect test leads between the current terminals (3,6). Figure 1

  2. Increase the current slowly until the relay energize and reach (P.U) value.

  3. Decrease the current slowly until the relay not energize and reach (REST) value.

  4. Make sure that lock out relay (86 G) operates with Differential Relay energize.

  5. Record these values in the test sheet.

  6. Connect the test leads between the current terminals (10,7).

  7. Repeat steps (2,3,4,5) for terminals (10,7).

  8. Repeat all above steps for phases (S& T).

  9. Put 220 K.V C.B for the transformer at local position.

  10. Close C.B. for the unit at test position.

  11. Turn the switch to remote again.

  12. Trip C.B. for the unit by Differential Relay.

Return Relay and Associated Equipment to Service

  1. Disconnect the test leads from the Relay.

  2. Make sure CT and PT secondary circuits are in normal condition.

  3. Make reset to lock out relay and reset the flag of the differential relay.
