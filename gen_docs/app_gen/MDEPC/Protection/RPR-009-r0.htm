Introduction

This procedure details the testing procedures and calibration I1, which recommended to be carried out every year as indicated by Y1 in the title block.

This relay ( WTGA 7131) in practice monitors the effect of the prime mover ( Steam Turbine Generator) failure that causes the generator to “motor“ by drawing active power from the system. 

The anti motoring reverse power is connected to sense active power flowing from the system into the generator that operates like a synchronous motor.

The setting must be selected to avoid spurious trip under transient conditions like during parallel operation.

The WTGA 7131 is also provided one instantaneous unit used for alarm operation.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category, (safety helmet, safety shoes, ... etc).

  • Issue work permit including the team work name.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • The unit (Generator & Transformer) must be out of service.  

Tools and Equipment

  • Three Phase Secondary injection Voltage test set.

  • Single Phase Secondary injection Current test set.

  • Test leads.

  • AVO meter.

  • Complete tool set.

  • Air blower.

  • Contact cleaner (CRC).

 

Work to be carried out 

Preparation

  1. Look at Over Voltage Maintenance file (32) and compare the relay setting with those shown on the test sheet.

  2. Supply the relay with its nominal auxiliary supply: the green LED will light.

 

Testing the Relay

  1. Remove the terminals (15,14,23) from the relay.

  2. Connect the voltage terminals from the injection set to the relay points (15,14,23)

  3. Connect the current terminals from the injection set to the relay points (2,3) for testing phase A.

  4. Increase the injected voltage slowly to the nominal value 120 V.

  5. Increase the injected current slowly till the pickup.

  6. If there is no pick up reverse the current terminals on the relay.

  7. Record the P.U value in your test sheet.

  8. Decrease the injection current till the relay reset ( Drop out Value ).

  9. Record the D.O value in your test sheet.

  10. Make reset to the reverse power relay.

  11. Connect the current terminals from the injection set to points (10,11) on the relay.

  12. Repeat the previous steps {from step 4 to step 10} for testing phase C.

  13. Increase the current again to (P.U) value.

  14. Stop the test set.

  15. Restart the test set (with Nominal Voltage V=120 V & Current = P.U value).

  16. The relay will operate, the timer will account and stop when the trip contact close.

  17. Make sure that lock out relays (86GT & 86T) operate with reverse power relay operates after its trip time.

  18. Record the time value in your test sheet.

  19. Make reset to the reverse power relay and lock out relays (86GT & 86T).

  20. Put C.B of the unit at local position.

  21. Close C.B of the unit.

  22. Return C.B of the unit to remote position.

  23. Trip C.B of the unit by Reverse Power Relay by increasing the injection current to the pickup value.

  24. Stop the tester.

 

Return Relay and Associated Equipment to Service

  1. Make reset to Over Voltage Relay, alarm indication and lockout relays (86GT&86T).

  2. Disconnect the test leads from the Relay.

  3. Reconnect voltage terminals (15,14,23) to the relay.

  4. Make sure PT secondary circuits are in normal condition.
