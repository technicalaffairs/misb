Introduction

This procedure details the testing procedures and calibration I1, which recommended to be carried out every year as indicated by Y1 in the title block.

The Voltage Balance Protection is used to block relays or other devices that will operate incorrectly when voltage transformer fuse blows. The TTGB 7031 relay is fed with three phase voltage from two sets of voltage transformers.

If the three phase voltages from the two sets are balanced, the output contacts of the relay remain stable regardless of the magnitude of the voltages.

If a fuse blows in any phase of one set of potential transformers, this will cause the relay to operate and to close the contacts of one of the two output units.

This operation is used in the logic circuits to switch the voltage regulator from auto to manual or to inhibit the tripping of voltage sensitive relays as 51V and 40G.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category, (safety helmet, safety shoes, ... etc)

  • Issue work permit including the team work name.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • The unit (Generator & Transformer) must be out of service.  

Tools and Equipment

  • Two Secondary Voltage injection test sets.

  • Complete tool set.

  • Air blower.

  • Contact cleaner (CRC).

Work to be carried out 

Preparation

  1. Look at Voltage Balance Maintenance file (60) and compare the relay setting with those shown on the test sheet.

  2. Supply the relay with its nominal auxiliary supply: the green LED will light.

Testing the Relay

  1. Remove Voltage transformer terminals (6,7,8) from the relay (Side 1 – Before Power Transformer}. Figure 1.

  2. Remove Voltage transformer terminals (22,23,24) from the relay {Side 2 – After Power Transformer}. Figure 1.

  3. Connect the voltage terminals from the injection set No. 1 to the relay points (6,7).

  4. Connect the voltage terminals from the injection set No. 2 to the relay points (22,23).

  5. Increase the injected voltage to 110 V for both sides.

  6. Decrease the injected voltage at side 1 slowly till the pickup value.

  7. Record the P.U value in your test sheet.

  8. Increase the injected voltage till the relay reset ( Drop out Value )

  9. Record the D.O value in your test sheet.

  10. Make reset to the Voltage Balance relay.

  11. Repeat step 5.

  12. Decrease the injected voltage at side 2 slowly till the pickup value.

  13. Repeat steps  8,9  and 10.

  14. Make reset to the Voltage Balance relay.

  15. Stop the tester.

Return Relay and Associated Equipment to Service

  1. Make reset to Voltage Balance Relay, alarm indication.

  2. Disconnect the test leads from the Relay.

  3. Reconnect the terminals points (6,7,8) and (22,23,24) to its normal position at the relay.
