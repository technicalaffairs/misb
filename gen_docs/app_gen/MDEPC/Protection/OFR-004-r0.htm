Introduction

This procedure details the testing procedures and calibration I1, which recommended to be carried out every year as indicated by Y1 in the title block.

This relay ensures function 81 of ANSI-C37-2-1970 standard.

The HDG 7020 is a two level frequency relay whose characteristics of high  precision , rapid  operating time , wide setting ranges and the possibility of using each operating level either for over frequency or under frequency , make it suitable for use in a wide range of application.

The first draft is initiated by Eng. Mohammed Fahmey Mashaal, tested by Eng. Khalied Diab & Eng. Ehab Abu Elwafa at 19 August 2008, and accepted by General Manager Eng. Saad Kheder and approved by Eng. Abd Elmoatey Elrakaiby Head Sector of Mahmoudia Power Station.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category, (safety helmet, safety shoes, ... etc).

  • Issue work permit including the team work name.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • The generator must be out of service.  

Tools and Equipment

  • Variable frequency source.

  • Test leads.

  • AVO meter.

  • Complete tool set.

  • Air blower.

  • Contact cleaner (CRC).

 

 

Work to be carried out 

Preparation

  1. Look at field maintenance file and compare the setting on relay with those shown on the test sheet.

  2. Check DC. Circuit for frequency Relay.

 Testing the Relay

  1. Remove voltage terminals (10,11) from the relay as shown in fig(1)

  2. Connect test leads between the voltage terminals (10 – 11) on the relay.

  3. Increase the injection voltage to 110 V and frequency to 50 HZ

  4. Increase the frequency slowly until the relay operates and record over frequency (P.U) value and then stop the test set.     

  5. Restart the test set (with voltage=110v& frequency =over frequency P.U value)

  6. The relay will operate, the over frequency timer will account and stop when the trip contact close.

  7. Make sure that lockout relays (86GT&86T) operate.

  8. Register the time value.

  9. Decrease the frequency slowly until the relay return to normal condition and record (REST) value.

  10. Make reset to frequency relay & lockout relays (86GT&86T).

  11. Decrease the frequency slowly until the relay operates and record under frequency (P.U) value and then stop the test set.

  12. Restart the test set (with voltage=110v& frequency=under frequency P.U value).

  13. The relay will operate, the under frequency timer will account and stop when the trip contact close.

  14. Make sure that lockout relays (86GT&86T) operate.

  15. Make reset to frequency relay &lockout relays (86GT&86T).

  16. Increase the frequency slowly until the relay return to normal condition and record (REST) value.

  17. Make reset to frequency relay & lockout relays (86GT&86T).

  18.  Record all above (P.U. – Reset – Time) values in the test she

Test C.B Tripping Circuit of the unit

  1. Remove under voltage from its rack.

  2. Put 220 K.V C.B of the unit at local position.

  3. Close C.B of the unit.

  4. Return C.B of the unit to remote position.

  5. Trip C.B of the unit by frequency relay (under/over)

  6. Reinsert under voltage relay to its normal position.

  7. Make reset to frequency relay & lockout relays (86GT&86T)

Return Relay and Associated Equipment to Service

  1. Disconnect the test leads from the Relay.

  2. Reconnect voltage terminals (10-11) to the relay.

  3. Make sure PT secondary circuits are in normal condition.
