Introduction

This procedure details the testing procedures and calibration I1, which recommended to be carried out every year as indicated by Y1 in the title block.

The DTT 7031 is a biased three phase differential relay designed to protect high voltage transformer against internal faults.

It is very rabid operation limits the damage due to short-circuit between phases, between turns on the same phase, or between windings and earth/ground if the earth/ground fault currents can attain relatively high values.   

The first draft is initiated by Eng. Ahmed EL Sihy, tested by Eng. Khalied Diab & Eng. Ehab Abuo El-wafa at 18 May 2008, and accepted by General Manager Eng. Saad Kheder and approved by Eng. Abd El-moatey El-rakaiby Head Sector of Mahmoudia Power Station.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category, (safety helmet, safety shoes, ... etc).

  • Issue work permit including the team work name.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • The generator must be out of service.  

  • Disconnect the 220k.v. C.B and  the transformer isolators (main and reserve).

 

Tools and Equipment

  • Secondary injection test set.

  • Clamp meter, Test leads.

  • AVO meter.

  • Complete tool set.

  • Air blower.

  • Contact cleaner (CRC).

Work to be carried out 

Preparation

  1. Look at field maintenance file and compare the setting on relay with those shown on the test sheet.

  2. Check DC. Circuit for for high speed differential Relay.

 Testing the Relay

  1. Connect test leads between the current terminals (37,40). Figure 1

  2. Increase the current slowly until the relay trip and record the (P.U) value in the test sheet.

  3. Decrease the current slowly until the relay energize and record the (REST) value in the test sheet.

  4. Make sure that lock out relay operates with diff. relay trip.

  5. Record this value in the test sheet.

  6. Connect the test leads between the current terminals (33,36).

  7. Repeat steps (2,3,4,5) for terminals (33,36).

  8. Repeat all above steps for phases (S, T).

  9. Put 220 K. V C. B. for the transformer at local position.  .

  10. Close C. B. for the unit at test position.

  11. Turn the switch to remote again.

  12. Trip C. B. for the unit by Differential Relay.

Return Relay and Associated Equipment to Service

  1. Disconnect the test leads from the Relay.

  2. Make sure CT and PT secondary circuits are in normal condition.

  3. Make reset to lock out relay and reset the flag of the differential relay.
