Introduction

The DTP is a digital relay that provides differential (87) and backup instantaneous (87B) three phase protection functions for power transformers. Different models of the DTP are available for protecting and monitoring transformers with two, three, or four windings

This procedure details the steps required to completely check the proper operation and calibration of the relay. It is recommended this procedure be carried out on an annually bases. The document is formatted the way to minimize the possibility of error causing personal injury or damage to equipment.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued.

  • The maintenance staff should be trained, qualified, and carry the suitable ID safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear your safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes ... etc.

  • Line isolation is required.

  • Make sure that CT secondary circuits are shorted before isolating the current circuits for test distance protection.

  • The unit must be out of service.

Tools and Equipment

  • ISA DRTS tester.

  • Test leads.

  • PC laptop with necessary software {ISAWIZARD and ENER VISTA}

  • Tools bag

 

 

 

Work to be Carried Out

Preparation

  1. Make sure CT secondary circuits are shorted at the incoming terminal blocks before isolate the relay current circuits for test.

  2. Be sure that the DC power of the relay is correct by applying a DC voltmeter across points (P11(-), Q11(+)) see figure (1) on the rear of the relay.

Reading the Setting from the Relay using the PC

  1. Connecting the personal computer to the relay.

  2. Run the enervista program.

  3. Read the setting and safe it as URGENT FILE FOR DTP [name the feeder].

For STG Unit Overall Protection

Differential protection test

  1. Connect the personal computer to the ISA tester.

  2. Be sure that ISA tester is connected to EARTH.

  3. Switch ON ISA tester.

  4. After ISA tester completely in service connect leads between ISA tester and  and current leads (BP1 1,2,3,4 see figure 1) of test plug for DTP. 

  5. RUN ISAWIZARD software>>>>>GENERATION & DISTRIBUTION RELAYS>>>> Xpro advanced>>>>MANUAL MODE

  6. NOW you can test the relay.

  7. NOTE:  Don't forget to GENERATE PREFAULT CONDITION [I=0.001A, V=0V].   

  8. Choose Delta ramp with step ∆I 0.05 Amp to get pickup current of the relay

  9. Choose Shoot with current value equal to 1.05 of pick up current to get pickup time of the relay.

  10. To test 2nd stage of differential, disable 1st stage of differential then shoot by setting current

2nd and 5th Harmonic Restraining Test

  1. Repeat steps from 8 with frequency 100Hz for 2nd harmonic test

  2. Repeat steps from 8 with frequency 250Hz for 5th harmonic test.                   

  3. Choose STORE ALL TESTS >>>>

  4. Go to RESULTS from main menu chose HEADER and apply all information about these FEEDER and OK

  5. Repeat all steps for current leads (BP2 & BP3 1,2,3,4 see figure 1) of test plug for DTP.

  6. Now print the results and you have complete test results for these relay.

 

 

Return Relay and Associated Equipment to Service

  1. Enable any function which was disabled.

  2. Return relay to service by removing the test leads from the relay terminals and the test set.

  3. Make sure CT and VT secondary circuits are in normal condition.

  4. Remove the disturbance alarm LED's on the relay which is caused by the test.

  5. Request feeder to be returned in service.

  6. Take pharos readings of all AC currents and voltages and compare them to those for normal load condition.

  7. Check that these reading agree with station meters (MW, Mvar, Amperes and power factor).

  8. Place a copy of the test result in the field maintenance file.
