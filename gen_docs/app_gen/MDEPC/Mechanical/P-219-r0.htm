Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection I1, which must be carried out every six months which indicated by M6 in the little block.

This pump function to transfer water from high pressure drum to high pressure evaporator then to high pressure drum again.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued.

  • The maintenance staff should carry the suitable ID safety category.

  • Maintenance staff should be well trained, qualified or directly supervised by some one who is.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Personal protective equipment must be used as safety shoes, and safety helmet .... etc

  • Shutdown the pump.

  • Disconnect the power supply & rack out the circuit breaker.

  • Put red tag on the pump.

  • Close the shut–off valves in the (suction & discharge& balancing & min flow ).

  • Let the pumps cool down to about < 80oC measured at the branches, then seal off the cooling water pipes and close all other supply lines.

  • Loosen all auxiliary lines, such as circulation, cooling liquid, lube oil.

  • The safety department must check the isolation and surrounding the zone of work.

 

 

  

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete set of tools.

  • Crane.

  • Alignment indicators, filler.

  • Grease gun, Cleaning fluid.

Work to be carried out

  1. Check oil level and viscosity.

  2. Check heat.

  3. Check noise.

  4. Check vibration.

  5. Check foundation.

  6. Check mechanical seal.

  7. Check leakage of water.

  8. Check looses of bolts.

  9. Check cooling chamber of mechanical seal.

Dismantling Steps

  1. Disconnect the motor electrically.

  2. Isolate the pump from suction and discharge by closing the valves.

  3. Drain pump casing, bearing frame and cooling circuit.

  4. Disconnect all auxiliary circuits.

  5. Remove coupling guard.

  6. Remove coupling spacer to allow dismantling of the pump with disturbing either motor or pump casing.

  7. Loosen fixing screws on back frame foot of the pump.

  8. Unscrew fixing nuts on stuffing box.

  9. Remove the assembly consisting of bearing frame and stuffing box from pump casing making use of the extracting screws specially provided for this purpose.

  10. Dismantle the impeller.

  11. Dismantle the stuffing box.

  12. Dismantle the bearing assembly.

Procedures

  1. Change (O-rings) and gaskets if necessary.

  2. Change the oil if necessary.

  3. Check all wearing parts before assembly and repaired or replaced if necessary.

  4. Clean cooling chamber of stuffing box after removing the cover.

Assembling

The steps of assembling are the reverse steps of dismantling.
