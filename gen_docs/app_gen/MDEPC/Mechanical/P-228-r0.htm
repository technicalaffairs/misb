Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection I1, which must be carried out on an annually bases as indicated by Y1 in the little block.

This pump is used to transfer water from feed water tank to low pressure evaporator then to feed water tank again.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued.

  • The maintenance staff should be trained, qualified, and carry the suitable ID safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear the appropriate safety and healthy equipment as safety shoes, helmet, breathing mask, safety glasses.

  • Shutdown the pump.

  • Disconnect the power supply & rack out the circuit breaker.

  • Put red tag on the pump.

  • Close the shut–off valves in the (suction & discharge).

  • Let the pumps cool down to about < 80 ºC measured at the branches, then seal off the cooling water pipes and close all other supply lines.

  • Loosen all auxiliary lines, such as circulation, cooling liquid, lube oil.

  • The safety department must check the isolation and surrounding zone of work.

 

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete set of tools.

  • Crane.

  • Alignment indicators, filler.

  • Grease gun, cleaning fluid.

Work to be Carried Out

Before Disassembly

  1. Measure the alignment and record it.

  2. Measure the vibration and record it.

  3. Record the discharge pressure.

Dismantling

  1. Disconnect the motor electrically.

  2. Isolate the pump (Close the isolating valves) and drain it as completely as possible.

  3. Disconnect the auxiliary piping (water cooling).

  4. Uncoupling pump from motor and disconnect any safety devices as well as pressure and temperature measuring instrumentation.

  5. Disconnect pump from suction and discharge piping.

  6. Remove coupling spacer.

  7. Drain the oil.

  8. Remove the casing – adapter frame nuts.

  9. Pull out the complete bearing housing with rotor.

  10. Carry the complete bearing housing with rotor eventually to the workshop to continue dismantling.

Procedures

  1. Clean all parts of the pump.

  2. Change all the gaskets.

  3. Change all the packing.

  4. Change the oil.

  5. All parts ( as bearing ) must be checked prior to assembly and repaired  or replaced if necessary .

  6. Measure the radial clearance for the pump and adjust them.

  7. Inspect the run out of the shaft.

 

Assembling

The pump should be assembled in the reverse order of disassembly.

 

 

After Assembly

  1. Open the suction and delivery of the pump.

  2. Record the alignment.

  3. Record the discharge pressure.

  4. Record the vibration.
