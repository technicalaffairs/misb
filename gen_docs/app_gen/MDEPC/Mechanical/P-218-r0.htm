Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection I1, which must be carried out every six months as indicated by M6 in the little block.

This pump function is to transfer water from feed water tank to low pressure evaporator then to feed water tank again.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued.

  • The maintenance staff should be trained, qualified, and carry the suitable ID safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Personal protective equipment must be used as safety shoes, and safety helmet.

  • Shutdown the pump.

  • Disconnect the power supply & rack out the circuit breaker.

  • Put red tag on the pump.

  • Close the shut–off valves in the (suction & discharge& balancing &min .flow ).

  • Let the pumps cool down to about < 80oC measured at the branches, then seal off the cooling water pipes and close all other supply lines.

  • Loosen all auxiliary lines, such as circulation, cooling liquid, lube oil.

  • The safety department must check the isolation and surrounding the zone of work.

 

 

 

 

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete set of tools.

  • Crane.

  • Alignment indicators, filler.

  • Grease gun, Cleaning fluid.

Work to be carried out

  1. Check oil level and viscosity.

  2. Check heat.

  3. Check noise.

  4. Check vibration.

  5. Check foundation.

  6. Check packing.

  7. Check leakage of water.

  8. Check looses of bolts.

Dismantling

  1. Disconnect the motor electrically.

  2. Isolate the pump ( Close the isolating valves ) and drain it as completely as possible.

  3. Disconnect the auxiliary piping (water cooling).

  4. Uncoupling pump from motor and disconnect any safety devices as well as pressure and temperature measuring instrumentation.  

  5. Disconnect pump from suction and discharge piping.

  6. Remove coupling spacer

  7. Drain the oil.

  8. Remove the casing – adapter frame nuts.

  9. Pull out the complete bearing housing with rotor. 

  10. Carry the complete bearing housing with rotor eventually to the workshop to continue dismantling.

Procedures

  1. Change the gaskets if necessary.

  2. Change the packing if necessary

  3. Change the oil if necessary.

  4. All parts as bearing must be checked prior to assembly and repaired or replaced if necessary .

Assembling

The steps of assembling are the reverse steps of dismantling
