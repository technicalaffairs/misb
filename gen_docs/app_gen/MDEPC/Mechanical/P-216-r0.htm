Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection list I1, which must be carried out every six months as indicated by M6 in the little block.

This pump is used to transfer water from feed water tank to economizer then to high pressure drum.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued

    • .

  • The maintenance staff should be trained, qualified, and carry the suitable ID safety category.

    •

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear the appropriate safety and healthy equipment as safety shoes, helmet, breathing mask, safety glasses,

    •

  • Shut down the pump.

  • Disconnect the power supply & rack out the circuit breaker.

  • Put red tag on the pump.

  • Close the shutoff valves in the (suction & discharge & balancing & min. flow).

  • Let the pumps cool down to about < 80oC measured at the branches, then seal off the cooling water pipes and close all other supply lines.

  • Loosen all auxiliary lines, such as circulation, cooling liquid, lube oil.

  • The safety department must check the isolation and surrounding the zone of work.

    •

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete set of tools.

  • Crane.

  • Alignment indicators, filler

  • Grease gun, Cleaning fluid

Work to be Carried Out

Before Disassembly  

  1. Check oil level and viscosity.

  2. Check heat.

  3. Check noise.

  4. Check vibration level and record it

  5. Check foundation (Looses of bolts)

  6. Check packing.

  7. Check leakage of water.

  8. Check the alignment and record it.

Dismantling Steps

  1. Disconnect the motor electrically.

  2. Isolate the pump (Close the isolating valves) and drain it as completely as possible.

  3. Disconnect the auxiliary piping (water cooling).

  4. Uncoupling pump from motor and disconnect any safety devices as well as pressure and temperature measuring instrumentation.

  5. Disconnect pump from suction and discharge piping.

  6. Remove the stud bolts holding pump to base frame using nut and counter-nut and withdraw positioning dowels.

  7. Slide pump on base frame to transfer it to the work shop where it should be rested on two supports.

  8. Pump is to be dismantled from outboard side.

  9. Dismantle the bearing frame outboard side.

  10. Dismantle the bearing frame inboard side.

  11. Dismantle the hydraulic unit.

Steps of Maintenance

  1. Clean all parts of the pump.

  2. Change ( O- rings) , gaskets and packing if necessary.

  3. Change the oil if necessary.

  4. Check all wear parts (Shaft sleeves, impellers, wear rings, balancing disk, balancing disk chamber, bearings) prior to assembly (Control of clearances) and repaired or replaced if necessary.

  5. Measure the radial clearance for the pump and adjust them.

  6. Inspect the run out of the shaft.

Assembling

The pump should be assembled, the reverse order of disassembly

After Assembly 

  1. Check the water level.

  2. Open the suction and delivery of the pump.

  3. Check vibration level and record it.

  4. Check discharge pressure.
