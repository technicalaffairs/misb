Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection I1, which must be carried out on an annually bases as indicated by Y1 in the little block.

This pump is used to transfer water from high pressure drum to high pressure evaporator then to high pressure drum again.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued.

  • The maintenance staff should be trained, qualified, and carry the suitable ID safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • The safety department must check the isolation and surrounding the zone of work.

  • Wear the safety and healthy equipment as safety shoes, helmet, breathing mask, safety glasses .. etc

  • Shutdown the pump.

  • Disconnect the power supply & rack out the circuit breaker.

  • Put red tag on the pump.

  • Close the shut–off valves in the  (suction & discharge).

  • Let the pumps cool down to about < 80 ºC measured at the branches, then seal off the cooling water pipes and close all other supply lines.

  • Loosen all auxiliary lines, such as circulation, cooling liquid, lube oil.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set.

  • Crane.

  • Alignment indicators, filler.

  • Grease gun.

  • Cleaning fluid

Work to be Carried Out

Before Disassembly

  1. Measure the alignment and record it.

  2. Measure the vibration and record it.

  3. Record the discharge pressure.

Dismantling

  1. Disconnect the motor electrically.

  2. Isolate the pump from suction and discharge by closing the valves.

  3. Drain pump casing, bearing frame and cooling circuit.

  4. Disconnect all auxiliary circuits.

  5. Remove coupling guard.

  6. Remove coupling spacer to allow dismantling of the pump with disturbing either motor or pump casing.

  7. Loosen fixing screws on back frame foot of the pump.

  8. Unscrew fixing nuts on stuffing box.

  9. Remove the assembly consisting of bearing frame and stuffing box from pump casing making use of the extracting screws specially provided for this purpose.

Dismantling of the Impeller

  1. Unscrew impeller lock nut (right – hand thread) (item 58).

  2. Remove the impeller.

Dismantling of the stuffing box

  1. Disconnect cooling piping connected to seal cover.

  2. There are two ways to dismantle the mechanical seal :

  • Dismantling with cover item 3 still mounted.

  • Dismantling with cover item 3 released.

Dismantling the bearing assembly

  1. Dismantle half - coupling pump side.

  2. Loosen fixation screws and remove both deflector discs ( items 9 and 23 )

  3. Remove bearing covers ( items 4 and 5 )

  4. To dismantle the bearings, strike lightly with a block of wood on shaft end impeller side in order to get the shaft to slide towards coupling side ; bearings R2 and R3 remain on shaft ( the outer ring of bearing R2 remains in the bearing frame and should be removed separately ) : the inner ring of bearing  R1 remains fitted on shaft and should also be removed separately.

  5. Withdraw outer rings of bearings R2 and R1 from bearing frame.

  6. Remove bearing lock nut item 92 after releasing safety washer item S3.

  7. Withdraw bearings R2 and R3. These bearings are tight-fitted on the shaft. To remove them, tap with a copper drift on the inner ring or use an extractor.

 

 

 

Procedures

  1. Clean all parts of the pump.

  2. Change all O- rings and gaskets.

  3. Change the oil.

  4. All wearing parts must be checked before assembly and repaired or replaced if necessary .

  5. Clean cooling chamber of stuffing box after removing the cover.

  6. Measure the radial clearance for the pump and adjust them.

  7. Inspect the run out of the shaft.

Assembling

Reassembling the bearings

  1. Mount bearings R2 and R3 on shaft.

  2. Lock R2 and R3 into place by means of bearing lock nut item 92 and fit safety washer item S3 previously.

  3. Introduce shaft into bearing frame from driver side.

  4. From impeller side, slide bearing R1 on shaft and fit it into place in bearing frame.

  5. Fit bearing cover driver side item 5 with its gasket item J3

  6. Fit bearing cover pump side item 4 with its gasket item J2.

  7. Mount deflector disks items 9 and 23 and tighten their fixing screws.

Assembly of stuffing box

  1. Fit a new gasket item 4 on seal cover item 3.

  2. Fit complete stationary seal ring in cover with gasket item 1.1.2 and springs item 1.1.4 and place snap ring item 5.

  3. Check smooth slide and correct positioning of shaft sleeve item 2 on shaft.

  4. Fit a new graphite o-ring on shaft sleeve end.

  5. Fit rotary seal ring item 1.2 and gasket item 1.3 on shaft sleeve.

  6. Reassemble cover item 305 on stuffing box together with its o-ring items T3 and T4.

  7. Introduce the shaft protruding from the bearing frame into seal cover and stuffing box and secure bearing frame item 1 to stuffing box item 83.

  8. Carefully slide shaft sleeve onto shaft, the shaft being placed horizontally.

Assembling the impeller

  1. Mount impeller ( item 55 ) and impeller lock nut ( item 58 ), not forgetting o-ring ( item T1 )

  2. Care must be taken to tighten the impeller lock nut properly.

  3. Assemble the complete mechanism to pump casing.

  4. Fit frame foot ( item 109 ).

  5. Mount pump back on its base frame.

  6. Fill the bearing frame with oil.

After assembly

  1. Open the suction and delivery of the pump.

  2. Record the alignment.

  3. Record the discharge pressure.

  4. Record the vibration.
