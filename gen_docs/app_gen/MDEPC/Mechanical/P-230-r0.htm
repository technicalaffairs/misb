Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection I1, which must be carried out on an annually bases as indicated by Y1 in the little block.

This pump is used to transfer water from feed water tank to economizer then to high pressure drum.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued.

  • The maintenance staff should be trained, qualified, and carry the suitable ID safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • The safety department must check the isolation and surrounding the zone of work.

  • Wear the safety and healthy equipment as safety shoes, safety helmet, breathing mask ... etc

  • Shutdown the pump.

  • Disconnect the power supply & rack out the circuit breaker.

  • Put red tag on the pump.

  • Close the shut–off valves in the  (suction & discharge& balancing &min .flow ).

  • Let the pumps cool down to about < 80 ºC measured at the branches, then seal off the cooling water pipes and close all other supply lines.

  • Loosen all auxiliary lines, such as circulation, cooling liquid, lube oil.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set.

  • Crane.

  • Alignment indicators, filler.

  • Grease gun, Cleaning fluid.

Work to be Carried Out

Before disassembly

  1. Measure the alignment and record it.

  2. Measure the vibration and record it.

  3. Record the discharge pressure.

Dismantling

  1. Disconnect the motor electrically.

  2. Isolate the pump (Close the isolating valves) and drain it as completely as possible.

  3. Disconnect the auxiliary piping (water cooling).

  4. Uncoupling pump from motor and disconnect any safety devices as well as pressure and temperature measuring instrumentation.

  5. Disconnect pump from suction and discharge piping.

  6. Remove the stud bolts holding pump to base frame using nut and counter-nut and withdraw positioning dowels.

  7. Slide pump on base frame to transfer it to the work shop where it should be rested on two supports.

  8. Pump is to be dismantled from outboard side .

Dismantling the bearing frame outboard side

  1. Remove protecting guard.

  2. Withdraw deflector outboard side part 98 after loosening its radial set-screws.

  3. Withdraw cover outboard side part 16.

  4. Remove bearing lock nut part 92 and safety washer part S1

  5. Withdraw bearing frame outboard side part 14A with bearings R1 and R2 and spring washers Re.

  6. Remove spacer ring part 116A and inner ring of bearing R2.

  7. Withdraw deflector part 9 after loosing its radial set-screws.

Dismantling the balancing assembly

  1. Remove gland part 62.

  2. Remove packing rings.

  3. Remove stuffing box part 83 with closing plate part 305.

  4. Remove shaft sleeve part 60 using tooling.

  5. Withdraw two-piece thrust ring part 132.

  6. Fit balancing disk extracting tool and withdraw  balancing disk (part 101).

Dismantling the bearing Frame inboard side

  1. Dismantle half-coupling pump side.

  2. Withdraw deflector part 9A after loosening radial set-screws.

  3. Remove cover inboard side part 5.

  4. Withdraw bearing lock nut part 92 and safety washer part S1.

  5. Remove bearing frame part 14 with its bearing part R2.

  6. Remove spacer ring part 116.

  7. Withdraw inner ring of bearing part R2.

  8. Remove deflector part 9.

  9. Remove gland part 62 and packing rings.

  10. Remove shaft sleeve using special tooling ( as indicated for outboard side ).

Dismantling the hydraulic unit

  1. With the pump still in horizontal position, slightly loosen nuts ( part 145 ) of tie-rods part 144 in such a way that the casing elements remain assembled.

  2. Put pump casing in vertical position ( with driving shaft and facing downwards ) and rest it on dismantling support frame.

  3. Provide adequate support under shaft.

  4. Loosen and withdraw the nuts on suction side part 145.

  5. Lift discharge collector part 51 with its balancing disk chamber part 100 and the tie-rods part 144 hanging from discharge collector.

  6. Remove end diffuser part 65.

  7. Remove impeller nr 8 part 55 with its key.

  8. Remove inter-stage ring section part 54, them remove nr 7 and inter-stage ring section; repeat this dismantling procedure down to the first diffuser part 64  (mounted on suction collector part 52 ).

  9. Fit on shaft part 59 the nut with handling ring and lift shaft with the first impeller part 55.

  10. Dismantle impeller. 

procedures

  1. Clean all parts of the pump.

  2. Change all (O- rings), gaskets and packing.

  3. Change the oil.

  4. All wear parts (Shaft sleeves, impellers, wear rings, balancing disk, balancing disk chamber, bearings) must be checked prior to assembly (Control of clearances) and repaired or replaced if necessary .

  5. Measure the radial clearance for the pump and adjust them.

  6. Inspect the run out of the shaft.

Assembling

Fit beforehand

  1. Balancing disk chamber part 100 on discharge collector part 51, not forgetting o-ring T6.

  2. Bearings in bearing frames part 14 and 14A

  3. Wear rings part 68 in suction collector part 52 and in inter-stage ring sections part 54

  4. O-rings T1 (previously smeared with tallow) on inter-stage ring sections part 54

  5. Assemble first impeller part 55 on shaft part 59.

  6. Introduce shaft part 59 vertically into suction collector part 52 ( placed horizontally on an adequate support ). Support the shaft in such a way that the impeller does not come loose ( length of protruding shaft segment : 339 mm )

  7. Fit first diffuser part 64 making sure that the insert part 270 is correctly positioned.

  8. Fit first inter-stage ring section pressing it home against collector; the slightest play would falsify the reading of the axial clearance, to be taken when completing assembly.

  9. Fit next impeller and repeat steps described  in 7 to 9 up to the last stage.

  10. Mount discharge collector part 51.

  11. Fit the 4 tie-rods locking the 2 collectors together and tighten them evenly.

  12. Check total axial clearance.

Assembling the balancing system

  1. Fit balancing disk part 100.

  2. Fit two-piece ring part 132.

  3. Slide shaft sleeve part 60 on shaft.

Fit Successively the following Parts ( on coupling side )

  1. Shaft sleeve part 61.

  2. Deflector part 9.

  3. Bearing frame part 14 with bearing R2.

  4. Spacer ring part 116.

  5. Bearing lock nut part  92 with safety washers part S1.

  6. Cover part 5.

  7. Deflector part 9A

  8. On discharge side, mount stuffing box part 83

Fit Successively

  1. Shaft sleeve part 60.

  2. Deflector part 9.

  3. Bearing frame part 14A with bearings and springs washers part Re.

  4. Bearing lock nut part 92 with safety washer part S1.

  5. Cover part 16

  6. Fit packing rings and glands.

  7. Put pump back on its base frame.

  8. Connect the various pipe rings.

  9. In the case of oil-lubrication, fit oilers back on bearing frames.

  10. Check pump set alignment.

  11. Tight feet nuts.

After Assembly

  1. Open the suction and delivery of the pump.

  2. Record the alignment.

  3. Record the discharge pressure.

  4. Record the vibration.
