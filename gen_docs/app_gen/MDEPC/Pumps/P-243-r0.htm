Introduction

This pump is used to transfer condensate water from condenser hot well to dearators to feed pump of HRSG

This document outlines the major maintenance procedure (P1) which must be performed every 5 Years as indicated by Y5 in above title block.

Type     : multi stage - vertical type

Safety Precautions

  • Work permit must be issued & qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable saftey category.

  • Wear safety and helmets equipments as safety healthy safety shoes – gloves , etc .

  • Use safety fence ( Rope ) with caution marks surrounding the work area  .

  • Isolate the circuit breaker of the pump motor .

  • Disconnect electric terminal of the pump motor .

  • The pump must be isolated mechanically by closing delivery and suction valves .

  • Be sure pump must be completely drained

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set

  • Cleaning fluid

  • Feeler gauge & dial indicator & vibrometer straight edge

  • Rages

Work to be carried out

Pump in service

  1. Check discharge and pump pressure then compare it with the setting value and replace the worn - out components (if it is needed)

  2. Measure vibrations level for bearing and compare it with the setting value, then analyze it and determine what to do (if it is needed)

Pump out of service

  1. Check alignment and compare it with the setting value and record it

  2. Make test for bearings oil and add or change it (if it is needed)

  3. Check flanges for water leakage

  4. Check bolts fixation then re-tightens (if it is needed)

  5. Check glands for water leakage and re-pack (if it is needed)

  6. Check the pump casing against rust and re-paint it

Dissassembly refer to Figure (1)

  1. Disconnect any external auxiliary piping connections

  2. Disconnect the driver shaft from the pump shaft by removing the socket head cap screws (364) from the flanged coupling

  3. This will allow the pump shaft and impellers to drop down also if the coupling has a spacer remove the lower set of cap screws and nut (363) only

  4. Remove bolts that attach driver to discharge

  5. Lift driver off pump and set on wooden supports

  6. Be sure supports are high enough to clear shaft and shaft couplings

  7. Disconnect discharge piping from pump

  8. Remove anchor bolts (or nut) or if a pump has a suction can remove the nuts attaching discharge head to suction can

  9. Lift pump vertically unit pump suction clears the base

  10. Cover opening in foundation

  11. Lower pump into a horizontal position on suitable support and in a suitable area for disassembly

  12. Remove the adjusting nut (66) from the pump shaft (left hand threads)

  13. Remove the pump half of the flanged coupling (44) with key (46) from shaft pump

  14. Remove slinger (346) from shaft unbolt stuffing box (83) and remove from discharge head

  15. The stuffing box has (6) rings of packing a lantern ring (29) is in the bottom of the stuffing box next or 3 pieces rings of packing (13) with the ends of the packing (13) with the ends of the packing staggered around the shaft at 90* from each other

  16. The second lantern ring (29) i next followed by the top three rings of packing (13) then the packing gland (17)

  17. Remove suction bell plug (125)

  18. Measure end plug of shaft by pulling all the way up until the impellers hit measure and record the distance traveled.

  19. Remove shaft coupling (70)threads are left hand.

  20. Remove discharge case cap screws (356).

  21. Slide  discharge case (309)off shaft.

  22. Remove bowl cap screws(358)and slide top intermediate bowl (310)off shaft.

  23. Pull shaft upso impeller is not against bowl seat using an allen wrench remove the socket head cap screws(26).

  24. Slide impeller down the shaft 3/16” minimum

  25. Remove the split thrust rings (312).

  26. Slide the impeller off the shaft .

  27. Remove the straight key (23 )from the shaft .

  28. Repeat steps( 20 through 25 ) until all inter mediate bowls (310) and impellers (2)have been removed

  29. Slide suction bell (55)off over bottom end of shaft.

  30. All bowl bearings except rubber bearings are pressed into their respective bores and can be either pressed out or machined on the inside diameter until the wall is thin enough to collapse

Inspection

  1. Inspect & examine impellers for signs of rubbing or chipping .

  2. Inspect the ball bearing for damage or change it (if it is needed) .

  3. Inspect the wearing rings for wear or damage .

  4. Inspect the shaft for trueness with a dial indicator it must be true within (0.004 inches) .

  5. Check shaft run –out (not exceed 0.002 inches) .

Assembly refer to figure(1)

  1. Check shaft closely for nicks or burrs. Smooth with emery cloth as required .

  2. Check shaft straightness –shaft must be straight within (0.005”) .

  3. Slide the impeller on shaft until the impeller is just above the bottom set of key seats in the shaft .

  4. Place the straight key in its key  seat slide the impeller over the straight key until the circular key seat is completely above the impeller .

  5. Insert the split thrust rings into the circular key seat with the counter board holes away from the impeller .

  6. Alighn the holes in the key s and the impeller insert the socket head cap screws and tighten evenly this will pull the impeller into place.

  7. Insert the shaft with the first impeller attached into the suction bearing (323)

  8. Slide intermediate bowl(310)into shaft (6)and mate with suction bell (55) on bolted bowl assemblies rotate bowl to align ribs with ribs on suction bell case.

  9. Insert and uniformly tighten cap screws (358) on flanged bowls use heavy chain  tongs to tighten threaded bowls .

  10. Repeat steps 3,4,6,&9 until all bowls and impeller s are in place .

  11. Slide discharge case (309)over bump shaft.

  12. Install and tighten cap screws (356) or screw discharge case into top bowl if threaded

  13. Grasp shaft and rotate by hand to check for binding  also check end play by bulling all the way up and measuring distance traveled check this against end play recorded during bowl disassembly.

  14. If the suction bearing cavity is to be packed as previously explained was not packed with grease previously this should be done now.

  15. After grease ,remove enough grease when the plug is placed in the bottom of the bell the shaft is not forced up .

  16. Install suction bell plug (125)and tighten.

  17. Screw shaft coupling (70)into shaft.

  18. Check bolts and nuts for tightness , also check threaded discharge case bearing (320) tightness.

  19. On threaded bowls 6”,and 8”models ,after all connections are tight be sure to  drill a 3/16”hole into each joint then use drive pins (359 )to secure joint.

Starting the pump

  • Before starting the pump the following steps should be checked:

  1. Check the direction of rotation of the  pump for starting the pump for the first time

  2. Check the inlet for cooling oil bath of the thrust ball bearing

  3. Open the suction and delivery valves of the pump.

  4. Connect electric terminal of the pump motor.

  5. Put the circuit breaker of the pump motor in service.

Attendance and checking during operation

  • During operation of the pump the following shoud be checked

  1. The bearing temperature

  2. Pump suction & discharge pressure

  3. Pump vibration
