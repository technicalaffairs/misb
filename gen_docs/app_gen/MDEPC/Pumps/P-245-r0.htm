This document outlines the equipment major maintenance ( I2) , which must be carried out every year which indicated by (Y1) in the title block.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued.

  • The maintenance staff should be trained, qualified and carry the suitable ID safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear the appropriate  safety and healthy equipment as safety shoes, safety, helmet breathing  mask, safety glasses.

  • Shut down the pump.

  • Disconnect the power supply & rack out the circuit breaker .

  • Put red tag on the pump

  • Close the shutoff valve in the (suction & discharge)

  • Let the pumps cool down to about <80  oC  measured at the branches , then seal off the cooling water pipes and close all other supply lines .

  • Loosen all auxiliary lines, such as circulation , cooling liquid , lube oil .

  • The safety department must check the isolation and surrounding the zone of work.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete set of  tool

  • Crane.

  • Alignment indicators , filler .

  • Grease gun .

  • Cleaning fluid.

Work to be carried out

Pump in service

  1. Check oil level.

  2. Check Bearing Temperature.

  3. Check noise .

  4. Check vibration .

Pump out of service

Dismantling steps

  1. Disconnect the motor electrically .

  2. Isolate the pump ( close the isolating valves) and drain it as completely as possible .

  3. Disconnect the auxiliary piping ( water cooling )

  4. Uncoupling pump from motor and disconnect any safety devices as pressur and temperature measuring instrumentation

  5. Disconnect pump from suction and discharge piping

  6. Remove coupling spacer

  7. Drain the oil

  8. Remove the casing – adapter frame nuts

  9. Pull out the complete bearing housing with rotor

  10. Carry the complete bearing housing with rotor eventually to the work shop to continue dismantling .

 Inspection and Repair   

  1.  Check the gaskets and change If necessary

  2. Check packing and change If necessary.

  3. Check the oil and change If necessary

  4. All parts must be checked prior to assembly and repaired or replaced if necessary

Assembling

The steps of assembling are the reverse steps of dismantling
