Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, qualified maintenance staff carry the suitable safety ID category

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required

  • Use standard personal safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, goggles protective clothing rubber gloves .... etc

  • The door of the battery room must be provided with no smoking signs

  • The battery instructions must be displayed visibly on the wall

  • Water source must be available in the battery room

  • When working in the battery room bewares of parts under voltage

  • Disconnect DC switch of the battery from the main bus bar

Tools and Equipment

  • Hydrometer

  • Digital voltmeter

  • Thermometer

  • Battery impedance test equipment (tester)

  • Vaseline

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Work to be carried out

  1. Check the room ventilation

  2. Keep the batteries clean and dry protect them against solid liquid and gaseous contamination

  3. Inspect terminals of battery and clean any salt deposits with distilled water

  4. Clean battery room floor with water

  5. Inspect the outside of cells for cracks and leakage of electrolyte

  6. Check acid level in central between max / min levels

  7. Write down the voltage and acid density of every cell

  8. If you found a detective cell, replace with a new one

  9. Read overall voltage of bank and record on battery maintenance card

  10. Check terminal connectors for tightness

  11. Lubricate terminals of cells with a thin film of vaseline

  12. Read and record overall battery voltage immediately after AC power is switched off

  13. Disconnect the bars tight and check every one, clean and change if necessary

  14. Connect the AC power again to battery charger and connect a load (floating condition)

  15. Use tester to measure the internal impedance of each cell in the battery group

  16. Compare the reading you have taken to the readings recorded in previous read
