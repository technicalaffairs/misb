Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued.

  • The maintenance staff should be trained, qualified, and carry the suitable ID safety category.

  • Wear your safety helmet, shoes and safety belt when cleaning insulators.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Transformer isolation and proper grounding is required.

  • There is a danger of falling into the oil containment pit when working near large power transformers.  

  • There is a danger of falling off the top of the transformer when cleaning insulator, wear safety belt.

  • Disconnect supply of DC.

  • Before starting the cleaning of all porcelain surfaces of all transformer wear your breathing mask to protect lung tissues from the dust particulate.

  • Don't burn the cloth used in cleaning collect it and send it to the dump.

  • Fix any oil leaks to prevent pollution and save money of wasted oil.

  • Oil leaks to the ground have very bad impact on the environment, mainly on soil and ground water and eventually on the ecosystem. "It takes only I liter of hydrocarbon spilled in an aquifer to render 1.000.000 liters of potable water useless for human consumption"

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete tool kit/set, including a torque wrench.

  • Safety Helmet, safety belt,

  • Voltage indicator, portable earth for discharging.

  • Blower or vacuum cleaner.  

  • Oil Vessels

  • PVC Ladder - Brushes.

  • Fine cloth for cleaning purposes.

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Carryout the Oil Analysis.

  2. Check of silica gel & oil level of air breathers.

  3. Check if there is foreign noise.

  4. Inspect the control cabinet equipment.
