Introduction

This document outlines the maintenance procedures P1, which must be performed every three years as indicated by Y3 in the title block.

The winding resistance test set model WRT – 100 is specifically designed for the measurement of DC resistance in highly inductive transformers, reactors and motor windings.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear safety and healthy equipment as safety Helmet, safety shoes, breathing mask, and harness ....etc.

  • Transformer isolation and earthing on all its sides is required.

  • There is danger of falling into the oil containment pit when working near large power transformer.

  • A proper well fixed ladder should be used for climbing on tap of the transformer tank.

Tools and Equipment

  • Winding resistance test set model: WRT – 100

  • Two leads for current injection.

  • Two leads for voltage measurement.

  • Cable for earth the equipment.

 

 

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Establish a convenient location for the WRT-100 within 15 to 20 feet from all transformer connections where either horizontal or vertical mounting will allow easy access to the front panel for reading and adjusting. Be sure not to block the air intake on the left side or the exhaust fan on the right side of the WRT-100 case.

  2. Connect the current lead hook lugs marked C to the middle pair of the WRT-100 binding posts marked " CURRENT OUTPUT ".

  3. Connect one or both sets of potential lead hook lugs marked P to the corresponding potential inputs of the WRT-100 marked "A" and "B".

  4. Ensure that the TEST/DUMP switch is in the DUMP position and showing GREEN.

  5. Connect a suitable AC supply120 or 230Vac through the power cord and receptacle and energies the instrument by pressing  POWER breaker ON.

  6. The two digital  RESISTANCE displays should now display 1- - - indicating over-range. This condition will exist if there is less than 50% of the selected current flowing or if the resistance being measured is beyond the full scale value indicated by the RANGE matrix.

  7. Connect the potential leads to the transformer terminals of interest either by directly clipping if the size allows or by " braid-wrapping" and clipping braid.

  8. Connect the current leads to the appropriate terminals by directly clipping on to them.

NOTE:

a.       THIS IS A " FOUR-TERMINAL " MEASUREMENT.

b.       THE POTENTIAL CLIPS MUST NOT TOUCH THE CURRENT CLIPS.

  1. If the expected resistance is known, select the current and range combination that will best display the measurement using the RANGE LED matrix. If the value is unknown, start with the 10 amperes current range and RANGE position 4. This will measure up to ohms.

  2. Ensure the READY lamp is not flashing, then depress the TEST switch and wait until the current indicator reaches maximum or stops rising. The yellow READY lamp should now be flashing.

WARNING

  • The flashing CAUTION LAMP indicates that  current is flowing in the load.

  • Current Leads MUST NOT BE  DISCONNECTED while lamp flashes.

  • SHOCK DANGER EXISTS!

If the RESISTANCE display shows zero or close to it then reduce the full scale range by selecting 3,2, or 1 on the RANGE selector.

Don't Change the Current Setting While Current as Following:

  1. If the displayed value is still low then depress the DUMP switch , wait fir the READY lamp to stop flashing then select the next higher current range and repeat from step 10 and record the value in test sheet.

  2. To terminate each test depress DUMP and always wait until the caution lamp stops flashing before disconnecting test leads.

 

 

 

Tap Changer

  1. When  winding resistance is measured in a transformer equipped with a tap changer ( TC ) switch the possibility of a current discontinuity exists if the tap changer is rotated during a test .

  2. If a TC is not " making " before "breaking " an arc will be drawn internally. This condition is detected by the WRT – 100  and is displayed by lighting the " INTERRUPT " warning lamp. This will also discontinue the test and necessitate restarting of the test.

  3. A very slight arc situation may not trip the " INTERRUPT " device but may cause the " CROWBAR TRIPPED " warning on the potential channel. If this occurs faulty readings will result " DUMP " the test and start over.

 Description                                                                                                                                                            

 

The winding resistance test set WRT-100 has been designed for fast and extremely accurate resistance measurements of highly inductive transformers, generators, and motors

 

Measurements are made with well filtered regulated currents up to 10A on any configuration of windings over a wide range from 1 micro ohm to 2000 Ω

Two independent displays enable testing of 2 series windings or primary and secondary windings with simultaneous readout.

 

The resistance readings are displayed after the test current has stabilized. Digitally controlled test-voltage current stabilization overcomes high inductance quickly for fast measurements

   

The ultra stable digital micro-milliohm meters are immune to the AC interference fields prevalent in many work areas.

The WRT-100 features a "stored energy" indicator which gives either a flashing warning of a charged specimen or a continuous ready status. A solid state discharge circuit discharges the item under test after completion of the test or if test leads are accidentally disconnected.
