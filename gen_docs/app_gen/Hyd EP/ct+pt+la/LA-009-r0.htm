Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable ID safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, shoes, breathing-mask, safety glasses ... etc.

  • This test must be performed at the equipment under voltage

Tools and Equipment

  • Input supply (220 v a.c.)

  • LCM II instrument

Work to be Carried Out

Main unit (LCM Il instrument)

  1. Connect ac supply or dc internal battery

  2. Earthling of the LCM ll

  3. Installation of current probe

  4. Installation of clip-on current transformer

  5. Field probe installation

 

 

System installation to live arrestors

  1. Check that the LCM ll is wired for the correct main voltage and connect it to the main earth the instrument according to the instruction

  2. Connect the current probe with measuring cable to the instrument

  3. Connect the current transformer to the current probe and clip it around the arrester's earthling lead if a surge counter is present, the current transformer should be placed above the surge counter (between the arrester and the surge counter) to avoid influence of the surge counter earthling

  4. Connect the coaxial cable from the field probe to the current probe

  5. Put the field probe on to the tip of the telescopic rod. Lift the field probe up to the arrester base and leave it there during measurements. The antenna plat should not touch any metallic parts of the arrester base 

Leakage current information

In order to perform optimal condition monitoring of a metal oxide surge arresters, it is important that some arrester data are available. According to clause 6.4 of IEC 60099-5, which is a part of the application guide for metal oxide surge arresters, these data are:

Resistive current & third harmonic resistive current (peak values)

  1. Voltage dependence as a function of service voltage, preferably in the range of 0.4-0.9 per unit (p.u.) of the rated voltage. It is also preferable that the curves are shown as multiplier factors, i.e. the curves should be plotted relative to the values at a standard service voltage of 0.7 p.u. See the example in figure1 below

  2. Temperature dependence as a function of ambient temperature (assuming a certain over-temperature of the ZnO-blocks), preferably from -10°C to +40°C.
    It is preferable that these curves are plotted relative to the values at a standard ambient temperature of 20°C (multiplier factors). See the example in figure 2 below.

Warning levels/maximum recommended values of the above mentioned quantities after conversion to standard operating conditions (0.7pu and 20°C). If the warning levels are exceeded, the arrester should be assessed for replacement or the manufacturer should be contacted for further device.

It is recommended to ask for these arrester data for instance as a part of new contracts for metal oxide surge arresters
