Introduction

This document lists the test procedure to measure the earth electrode resistance by using TERCA 2 digital earth tester, The periodic testing of the earth resistance is required to maintain sufficiently low resistance values of grounding system. The test method is "fall of potential" or "62% method, and it is bsed on the princible of the potential drop across the resistance

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding work area is required.

  • Wear your safety equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, breathing mask, safety glass .. etc

  • The instrument is equipped with an audible alarm indicates the presence of stray voltage greater than 20V peak (AC/DC), this stray voltage > 20 V effects the operation of the instrument and place the operator at risk.

  • If this alarm sounds the operator must check the installation

  • Make sure that the suitable volt. supply is connected to the insterument

  • In case of the audible alarm sounds to indicate the presence of a stray voltage > 20 V do not press the "test" button and check the installation 

Tools and Equipment

  • Digital Earth Tester type TERCA 2  (Figure1)

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Connect the instrument and the augers X,Y and Z to the electrode to be tested as in "Figure 2"

  2. Follow the test method as it mentioned according to "Figure 2"

The 62 % Method

  1. For the connection details see figure 2

  2. Short circuit terminals X and Xv with the shorting strap provided 15 fig 2 and unshorted  terminals Y and Z (shorting strap 17)

  3. Drive earth auger Z into the ground (to its length if possible) at, for example, 30 m from the earth electrode X fig 2

  4. Drive earth auger Y into the ground on a straight line between X and Z and at18 m from X (62 % distance XZ)

  5. If the audible alarm sounds, to indicate the presence of a voltage > 20 V peak, do not press the "TEST" pushbutton, check the installation, If the alarm does not sound, continue with the test.

  6. Depress the "TEST' pushbutton.

  7. Note down the displayed resistance value.

  8. Move earth auger Y along the straight line between X and Z; firstly to 21 m from X ( 72 % distance XZ ) then back to 15 m from X (52 % distance XZ).

  9. Note the resistance value for each position. (1 ohm to 0.9 for agriculture / 2:3 ohm for desert)

  10. If the readings are the same within a few %, the earth electrode resistance measurement is correct.

  11. If this  is not the case, increase the distance X to Z (to 40 m for example) and repeat the test, using the same proportions 62 % , 72% and  52 %.
    (25, 29 and 21 m approx) from X. And so on  until a viable result is obtained.
