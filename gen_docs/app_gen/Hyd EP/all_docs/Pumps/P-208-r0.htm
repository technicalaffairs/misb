Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection list I1, to be performed every six months as indicated by M6 in the title block

There are two drainage pumps one in the North main sump tank and the other in the South main sump tank and their types are CP3300 ( used for draining the main sump tanks )

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the maintenance staff should carry the suitable ID safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, gloves, ... etc.

  • Pump should be out of service.

    •

  • Isolate the pump mechanically and electrically,

    • and hang caution marks.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set.

  • Torque wrench, wrench with different sizes and feeler gauge.

  • Special tools as mechanical seal, ball bearing ... etc.

  • Slinging wench "3 ton" upward the sump tank for lifting and lowering the submersible pumps in the sump tank

  • Cleaning articles ( fluid, rags, ….. etc )

  • Oil drainage pump.

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Make sure that the lifting equipment is in good condition.

  2. Get out the pump from the sump.

  3. Check the condition of carrying handle, lifting eyes, chains and wire cables.

  4. Check that the guide bars are vertical.

  5. Rinse the pump thoroughly with clean water before starting work, good clean of the outer pump surface

  6. Clean the pass of cooling pump system.

  7. Check the pump casing and impeller.

  8. Check the clearance between the impeller skirt and the pump casing ( not exceeds 2mm) if it exceeds 2mm replacing the wear ring ( 5 ).

  9. Check the condition of oil through the oil hole screw ( 8 ).

  • Take  some of oil from the bottom of oil casing and sure that no water mixed with oil, then check that the oil reaches up to oil hole.

  • If there is water mixed with oil. the oil then became cream-like and oil must be change ( about 8 liters )

  • Replace a new O ring ( 8 ), good tightness for the oil plug (8). 

Mark: if the oil full change because there is water in the casing oil, the pump must be inspect again after one operation week.

  1. Check the liquid ( water / oil ) in the stator casing.

Warning:

Resulting from leakage, there may be over pressure in the stator casing. Hold a rag over the inspection screw to prevent splatter.

  1. Tilt the pump so that any liquid in the stator casing can run out through the hole.

  2. If there is water in the stator casing the cause may be:
     
    - That the inspection cover (13) is no tight enough that O ring (13) is damaged.
    - That cable entries ( 1 , 2 ) are leaking.

  3. If there oil in the stator casing the cause may be:
     
    - That the inner mechanical seal (12) is damaged and must be replaced.
    - That an O ring (3) is damaged and must be replaced.

  4. Check the cable entry: make sure that the cable clamps are tight.

  5. Check the pipes, valves.

  6. Lay down the pump in it is seat and sure that it is in proper position.

  7. Check the function of operation of the pump.
