Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection I1, which must be performed on yearly bases as indicated by Y1 in the title block

This test shall be performed in accordance with IEC standard 34-1 publication and shall be made on equipment completely mounted in usual service condition, in dry and clean conditions, line side, neutral side and exciter terminal connections must be disconnected during the test

This document is initiated & tested by eng. Abdo Hussein Ahmed, approved by eng. Mohamed Nassr El-din Ahmed general director, accepted by eng. Youssef Mahmoud Aly: general director of Esna Hydraulic PS, and certified by eng. Kamel Ragheb: HPGC quality control general director & MPIS Coordinator

Safety Precautions

  • A work order must be issued

  • The generator fire fighting out of service

  • Generator brake is applied

  • Circuit breaker of generator is opened and rack out

  • Connect earth switch of generator C.B

  • Disconnect the stand still heating of generator

  • Position of rotor locking bolt on position

Tools and Equipment

  • Megger (500-5000)Vdc

  • Test wires

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Insulation resistance measurement of the stator winding:
    Apply the voltage of 1000 Vdc between each phase and frame, with the other phases connected together and to earth, during the test RTDS shall be short circuited and grounded.

  1. Polarization index (stator):
    Polarization index is the ratio of ten-minute to one-minute insulation resistance reading and is given by the following formula:
                                                 10 Insulation resistance
                   Polarization index = -----------------------
                                                  1 Insulation resistance

  2. The PI is a useful means of calculation if a generator is suitable for operation

  3. The recommended minimum value of PI for alternating current generator shall not be lower than (2)

  4. When the value of PI is less than (2), then warm up the machine until this minimum value is obtained.

  5. When only the one-minute insulation resistance value is available, it can be compared to the calculated recommended minimum insulation resistance for AC generator armature and field winding calculated by the following formula:

    Rm = kv + 1

    Where : Rm is a Minimum Insulation Resistance
                 kv is a rated machine potential in kv
