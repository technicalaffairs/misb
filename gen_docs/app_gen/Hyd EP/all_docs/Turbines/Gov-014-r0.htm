Introduction

This document outlines the preliminary adjustments for watt transducer amplifier modules that have not been in service or the major maintenance procedure P1 which must be performed every four years as indicated by Y4 in the title block

The main functions of governor are sense turbine speed control speed in a stable manner and provide for load sharing between units

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety ID category

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required

  • Wear the safety and healthy protective equipment as safety shoes, helmet .... etc

  • Turn the transfer switch to "off" position as request

  • Run the unit on line and loading at several setting as request

Tools and Equipment

  • Set of common and screw drivers

  • Digital multimeter and oscilloscope "two channels or more"

  • Extender card

Work to be carried out 

For watt Transducer Module

  1. Set P-1 for 29.36 k from PIN # 13 to junction of P-1 and R-1 (figure 1)

  2. Set P-2 for 30.66 k from PIN # 14 to junction of P-2 and R-3 (figure 1)

  3. Set P-3 for 22.61 k from PIN # 4   to junction of P-3 and R-9 (figure 1)

 

For Integrator Ramper or (Loading Gain Amplifier)

  1. Adjust P-1 for 8.4 k from PIN # 7 to P-1 slider

Procedures With unit shutdown

Not : Switch F 403 (220 Vdc) & F506 (220 Vac) "on" and transfer switch position "Gov")

  1. Pull the voltage comparator module # out of rack

  2. Raise 65UGP (unit generation potentiometer) to 200 on the dial and adjust 65 UGP/CAL (Calibration potentiometer) for 8.49 volts between EA-2 and EA-3 (CT) lower 65 UGP to "zero" on the dial

  3. Lift KK-63 to keep 52X-1, 52X-2, 52X-3 relays from operating

  4. Lift wire KK-6/HN-15 at 52X-2 relay to keep the gate limit from running to full gate (85%) when UCB is closed

  5. Lift KK-20 to take 65PSD (partial shutdown) out of service

  6. Lift KK-45 so the gate limit can be raised above 30% after UCB is closed

  7. Turn the transfer switch to "off" position and put the watt transducer amplifier module on an extender card, turn the transfer switch to "Gov" position and reset lock-up

Procedures With unit on line

  1. Synchronize the unit to the system, raise the speed adjustment to raise the gate setting to the gate limit
  2. Raise the gate limit so the gate position pointer is on 50%
  3. With the voltage comparator # 1 pulled and 65 UGP set at "zero" jumper PIN #13 on the watt transducer amplifier to CT (PIN #8) , (figure 1)
  4. Adjust P-2 on the watt transducer amplifier for 5.240 volts from (output #1) blue test jack (L1) to (CT) yellow test jack (figure 2)
  5. Adjust P-3 on the watt transducer amplifier for 4.245 volts from (output # 2) blue test jack (L2) to (CT) yellow test jack (figure 2)
  6. Remove the jumper (from PIN # 13 to CT) placed on the watt transducer amplifier in step # 10 and plug the voltage comparator #1 in, the 65UGP dial should go 100 ± 2
  7. Lower the load with the speed adjustment and disconnect the unit from system
  8. Reconnect KK-63, KK-6/HN-15, KK-20 and KK-45
  9. Synchronize the unite to the system and load the unit with 65 UGP to 50% gate
  10. Lift MA-19 (CT) to disable the speed sensor
  11. Move 65 UGP between 80 and 100 on the dial observe the wicket gate to see that they do not over shoot and then come back, or stop short and drift open for a 100 setting on the 65 UGP dial or close for a 80 setting:
  • If they do overshoot : turn P-1 on the unit loading gain amplifier device "EG" : CCW
  • If they stop short and then drift : turn P-1 on the unit loading gain amplifier device "EG" CW
  1. Check that the wicket gate opening is twice the setting on the 65 UGP dail ± 1% at several setting if not : adjust P-1 on the watt transducer amplifier until they are
  2. Reconnect MA-19, the wicket gate opening will agree with 65 UGP set point only if the frequency is 50 HZ
  3. Disconnect the unit from the system and shut it down, turn the transfer switch to "off" position, remove the watt transducer amplifier from the extender card and plug into the rack turn the transfer switch to "Gov" position and reset the lock-up
