Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection I1 to be performed on an annually bases as indicated by Y1 in the title block

The governor power supply furnishes various regulated DC voltages to the electronic portion of the governor by means of a DC/DC converter which is supplied by two separate power sources : AC station service (220 Vac) through an isolation transformer, it is then rectified and filtered, the resultant DC is then paralleled with DC output of pre-regulator (the six zener diode), which is supplied by DC station battery (220 Vdc)

The DC outputs of converter + 15 V, +12V, +5V, -15V

Each of the four regulated outputs has over voltage protector which is located in the crowbar circuit

Each output also has under voltage protector which is located in monitor board

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety ID category

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required

  • Wear the safety and healthy protective equipment as safety shoes, helmet .... etc

  • Turn the transfer switch to "off" / "test" position, as request

Tools and Equipment

Work to be carried out 

Preparation

  1. Turn the transfer switch to "off" position and then pull all modules out of racks

  2. Turn the transfer switch to "test" position

Procedures

Note : switches F403 (220 Vdc) & F506 (220 Vac) "on"

  1. Check the old indication marker "AC supply" and "DC supply" to be sure they are light indication both power sources are active, (figure 1)

  2. Measure the voltage between the brown test points marked "125 Vdc" and "0 Vdc" this voltage should be between 90 Vdc to 125 Vdc (the acceptable input range for DC/DC converter) but the best value is 110 Vdc

For the Monitor Board : (Under Voltage Test)

  1. Turn the potentiometers (P1,P2,P3,P4) which are located in the power supply monitor board, fully counter - clockwise (figure 1)
    Note : the power supply voltage adjustments and the crowbar adjustments are located inside the DC/DC converter

  2. Adjust the + 15 Vdc supply to + 14.5 Vdc adjust P1 clockwise until the "+15 V supply" indicating light goes out

  3. Adjust the + 12 Vdc supply to + 11.5 Vdc adjust P2 clockwise until the "+12 V supply" indicating light goes out

  4. Adjust the + 5 Vdc supply to +  4.75 Vdc adjust P3 clockwise until the "+5 V supply" indicating light goes out

  5. Adjust the  - 15 Vdc supply to - 14.5 Vdc adjust P4 clockwise until the "-15 V supply" indicating light goes out

For the DC/DC Converter : (Over Voltage Test)

  1. In order to calibrate the crowbar circuit adjust each of the voltage regulators to the desired crowbar set voltage (+15V, +12V, +5V, -15V)

  2. Slowly turn the crowbar adjustment counter clockwise for + 15 Vdc supply until the voltage drop suddenly record this value in test sheet

  3. Repeat step #9 for + 12 Vdc supply, + 5 Vdc supply and -15 Vdc supply

  4. Shut off the power (turn the transfer switch to "off" position) and turn back the voltage adjustments somewhat so the crowbar will not clamp the voltage when power supply is reapplied (turn the transfer switch to "test" position) the voltage regulators then may be calibrated for the specified output voltages

  5. Check and adjust each of the voltage regulators to the specified output voltages (+15 V, +12V, +5V, -15V)

  6. Plug in all modules, one at a time and make sure that the power supply voltages remain constant

  7. After all the modules are plugged in turn the transfer switch to "Gov" position
