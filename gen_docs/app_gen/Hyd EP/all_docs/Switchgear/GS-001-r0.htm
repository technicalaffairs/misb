General

The primary purposes of grounding for circuits, cables, equipment, and systems are to prevent shock hazard, protection of circuits and equipment, and reduction of electromagnetic interference (EMI) due to electromagnetic (EM) field, common ground impedance, or other from of interference coupling the various grounding systems used (lightning power, and/or signal) are integral parts of a facility or system whether they involve vehicles such as automobiles, ships, missiles, or aircraft; buildings; or building complexes. Like other facility components, grounding networks are exposed to the ambient EM environment this environment includes many sources ranging from incident radio frequency (RF) signals to lightning and from atmospheric noise to internally generated stray currents

 

Test applied to grounding system

  2. Soil resistivity

Equipments grounding connection test

Apparatus used : GTS - 300

The procedure

  1. Connect one one of the GTS-300 ten feet output leads to one ground clamp or ferule to used

  2. Connect the other ten foot lead of the GTS-300 to another ground clamp

  3. Start the test and adjust for continuous current rating for clamp

  4. Monitor the output voltage and current to maintain a constant current for three minutes, the voltage drop will be about 1.5 volt per 50 foot of cable, actual values will depend on the size and materials used, on equipment with a single ground about 300 amps will flow to the gridon equipment with multiple grounds, at least half of the test current will flow through the grid

  5. Check for hot spots or irregular heating / blistering of the cables

  6. As soon as all ground points are tested, disconnect the test leads from any test ground and short the two test leads together stsrt the test set and pass 300 amperes through the test leads for three minutes record the voltage drop in the leads
