Introduction 

This document outlines the equipment inspection I1, which must be performed every three years as indicated by Y3 in the title block.

This circuit breaker is outdoor in the switchyard. It’s air disconnectors The rated voltage 145 KV. The rated current 3150 A. and the short circuit current 31.5 KA, FKF mechanism.

The operating mech. FKF 2-6.

The first draft initiated, tested by: eng. Mamdouh Aly Hassan, accepted by general director eng. Nagy Nageeb Saleh, and approved by eng. Mohamed Abdel Bast head Sector of Aswan Dam 2 Hydraulic Power Station and certified by eng. Kamel Ragheb: HPGC MPIS coordinator.

Safety Precautions

  • A work order must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required

  • Wear the Personal safety and healthy protective equipment as safety shoes, helmet … etc.

  • The circuit breaker must be isolated from tow sides is required.

  • Switch on the CB earth

Tools and Equipment

  • Safety helmet, safety belt ………etc.

  • Hand tools

  • Portable earth for discharging

  • Voltage indicator and ladder

  • Fine cloth for cleaning purposes

  • SF6 gas instrument, temp. gauge, micro ohm measuring ohm contacts and close – open measuring times plant .

Work to be carried out

  1. Remove dust from CB parts and mechanism cabinet.

  2. Clean all porcelain surfaces and check it’s for damage.

  3. For dash – pot liquid level, with CB in closed (on) position , the liquid level must lit at –+3mm of the center of gauge .

  4. Inspect operating mech. component, inspect state of lubrication for motor gearing and record number of operations for operating counter.

  5. Measure the opening – closing time (ms) and record in sheet test with compare to supplier valves.

  6. Measure the main contacts resistance by micro ohm meter and compare reading to historical data.

  7. For SF6 gas check pressure regard to temp. , check alarm, trip values indication

  8. Measure gas for percentage ( %)  must be over ( 99%) .
