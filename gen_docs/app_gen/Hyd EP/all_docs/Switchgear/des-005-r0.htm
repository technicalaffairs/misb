Introduction

This document lists the equipment inspection I1, which must be performed every six months as indicated by M6 in the title block

Theses distribution boards are used as main feeding and sub-feeding for generating units auxiliaries and power station auxiliaries and they are fed from11/0.4kv step-down auxiliary transformers. The boards are equipped with LV circuit breakers type: withdrawable module

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Maintenance staff should be will trained and qualified or directly supervised by some one who is.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear your safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, goggles, and rubber glove. ...etc

  • The board should be isolated from both sides.

  • The 11kv circuit breaker of the auxiliary transformer is switched off and racked out.

  • The main 0.4kv circuit breaker of the distribution board {incoming} should be switched-off and racked out.

  • The sub circuit breakers of the distribution board {out going} should be switched-off and racked out.

  • The control voltage 230v dc should be switched-off.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete kit of tools

  • Voltammeter instrument

  • Clean dry rags.

  • Electrical grease

  • Test equipment

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Clean the main contacts of all circuit breakers and lubricate by thin film of electric grease.

  2. Clean all of auxiliary contacts with special type of spray.

  3. Check all of power circuit and control circuit terminals for tightness.

  4. Readjust the measuring instruments {ammeters, voltmeters.

  5. Clean the withdrawable modules with clean dry rags.

  6. Lubricate the guide rails of withdrawable modules and check for free move.

  7. Lubricate the operating mechanisms of the modules and adjusted.

  8. After finishing the following tests should be done by using special test equipment:

  1. Switching operations {on-off}.

  2. Indicating lamps and their circuits are healthy.

  3. Trip the operations and their remote signals by certain faults.

  4. Racking-in and raking out of the withdrawable modules
