Introduction

This document lists the equipment inspection I1, which must be performed every six months as indicated by M6 in the title block

This charger has three possible operating modes as follow:

- Floating mode         117.7  ± 1% v  for the 53 cells battery.

- Charging mode       119.25 ± 1% v  for the 53 cells battery.

- Boost charge mode:110 - 143 v for the 53 cells battery.

The change over from floating mode to charging mode occurs automatically each time the charger stops due to main failure or breakdown .

The change over to boost charge is made by placing the inverter I2 on the boost charge position {I2 is inside the charger)

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Maintenance staff should be will trained and qualified or directly supervised by some one who is.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear your safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, goggles, and rubber glove. ...etc

  • The charger must be out of service and isolated from both sides AC&DC.

Tools and Equipment

  • Digital DC voltmeter .

  • Blower or vacuum cleaner

  • Complete suitable kit of tools

  • Small screwdriver

  • Cleaning dry rag, brush, blower.

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Check the state of the charger before switching off.

  2. Switch off from both sides AC&DC of the charger.

  3. Inspect all components: transformer, rectifying circuit, smoothing circuit, control circuit-fuses-micro contacts  o/p circuit breaker, indicating lamps, ventilating ducts & filters etc.

  4. Clean the charger using blower and dry rags.

  5. Look for any loose of the power connections and wiring must be done.

  6. After that the charger must be turned back to operation, now the charger is in charging mode operation as mentioned above and will last for 24 hours and turn back automatically to floating mode.

  7. Check the following alarm:

    1. If the rectifier is faulty or has been stopped.

    2. If there is main failure

    3. If the charger is in boost charging mode.

  8. The previous three alarms have a local luminous alarm signals and an alarm loop for remote signaling
