Introduction

The measured voltage is coupled via an intermediate PT, which provides DC isolation from the primary circuit and rectifiers to one or two electronic triggers which upon picking up exit electronic timing elements.

The output of each relay is contactors, and there are signal lambs for both pick-up and tripping, which remain illuminated until reset manually. This relay is consisting of tow stage the first stage it over voltage with adjustable time delay and the second stage it instantaneous trip.

The input circuitry of the relay is arranged to prevent interference on the input leads from entering the measuring system. The output contact of the under voltage is energized when the input voltage is below setting (tripping contact made)

Work to be Carried Out

  1. The main transformer off

  2. Remove the terminals F1 (28,29)

  3. Connect the relay test unit to a power source

  4. Connect the injection device test leads between the voltage terminals at F1 (28,29) as shown in figure (1)

  5. Increase the injected voltage gradually until the relay first stage it pick-up

  6. Record this voltage on the test sheet

  7. Compare the result with final setting and measurement

  8. Turn off the injection device

  9. Connect the time terminal for the injection device to terminal F3 (15;16) and inject the voltage which appeared in step 6 suddenly

  10. Record the time on the test sheet

  11. Compare the result with final setting ad measurement

  12. Increase the injected voltage gradually until the relay second stage (instantaneous) it pick-up

  13. Record this voltage on the test sheet

  14. Compare the result with final setting and measurement
