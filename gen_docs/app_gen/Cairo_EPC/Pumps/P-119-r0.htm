Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection checklist I2 which must be performed  every six months , as indicated by M6 in the title block.

The burner fuel oil pumps (qty =3 /unit) have capacity of 8701 L/mint at 3.9Kg/Cm2 and 60°C each during normal operation two P/Ps are in service and the other in stand by the discharge press. of the pumps is controlled by a control valve, which recirculates the oil back to the day tank the suction of the burner oil pumps are connected to a common header the suction header is connected to the day tank the burner fuel oil pump have a common discharge.

The discharge header of the pumps supply the pressure control line and burner oil heaters through the flow indicator and transmitting station.

Safety Precautions

  • Work order must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear the safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, shoes, gloves … etc.

  • Isolate the circuit breaker of the rotary fuel oil pumps.

  • Disconnect electric terminals of rotary fuel oil motor.

  • The pump P/P must be isolated mechanically by closing valve discharge and delivery.

Tools and Equipment

  • Standard tool set, dial indicator

  • Clamp, feeler

  • Wires and lifting equipments

 

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Check free movement of pump shaft.

  2. Check pump bearing temperature and change the oil if needed

  3. Check for abnormal noise and repair the cause of the noise by changing the defected parts of pump or motor.

  4. Check pump discharge press. and capacity so inspect the stages of the pump and repair or change if needed

  5. Check pump motor alignment or make new alignment.
