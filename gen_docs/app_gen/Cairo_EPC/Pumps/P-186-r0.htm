Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection checklist I1, which must be performed every six months as indicated by M6 in the title block.

The transfer fuel oil pump function to feed liquid fuel (Solar) from the header tanks through the open stop valve and filter to the main fuel pump.

Safety Precautions

  • Work order must be issued

  • The maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category

  • Safety fence with marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear the safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, shoes, earplugs … etc.

  • Isolate the motor of the transfer pump.

  • Close the inlet valve of the pump.

Work to be Carried Out

Stuffing Box with Packing Rings

Excess leakage at the stuffing box during the early period of the operation stops itself after the run-in time.

If necessary tighten the gland nuts avoid excess tightening. There should be a slight leak at the stuffing box to carry away the frictional heat generated at the packing.

If the leakage is too great and tightening does not help, the packing should be renewed because they might have lost their elasticity as the following:

  1. Slide back the deflector (if fitted) away from the stuffing box towards the bearing cover.

  2. Remove the nuts and push back the gland towards the bearing cover.

  3. Remove the packing and the bearing ring from the stuffing box and clean the latter thoroughly.

  4. If the surface of the shaft sleeve indicates severe scoring it is to be replaced.

  5. If ready-formed packing rings are not available, cut sufficient lengths from new packing roll. From them into rings over a pin of the same diameter as the shaft. The ends of the rings should be cut at an angle to the axis of the shaft.

  6. If soft packing rings are available cut them at an angle, deform as shown in figure, slide them over the shaft and from them into rings carefully. It is preferred to have a gap of 1mm between the ends of the rings. Stagger the joints of the rings.
                                          

  7. Tighten the gland nuts uniformly until resistance to turning of the shaft is felt. Once again loosen the gland and tighten.

Mechanical Seal

  1. The life of the mechanical seal depends upon many factors including the purity and lubricating quality of the pumped liquid. In view of the various conditions of the operations and makes it is not possible to specify the life of mechanical seals.

  2. Dry running of the mechanical seal even for a few seconds should be avoided. If it is necessary to replace the mechanical seal check the true running of the shaft sleeve and the surface quality.
