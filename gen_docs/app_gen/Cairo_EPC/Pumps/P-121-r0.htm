Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection checklist I1 which must be performed every six months as indicated by M6 in the title block.

The igniter fuel oil pumps (2 per unit) supply oil to the ignitors warm oil burners, flushing pot and recirculating line controls the P/P discharge header press. Under normal operation only one pump is in service and the other is in stand by usually the stand by pump will stand if the P/P that is in service trips or if the discharge header press. Drops to the minimum operational press. Allowed by the ignitors or warm up burners.

Safety Precautions

  • Work order must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should the suitable safety category

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear the safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, shoes, gloves, earplugs … etc.

  • Isolate the circuit breaker of the motor

  • Disconnect electric element terminals of the motor

  • The pump must be isolated mechanically by closing delivery valve.

Tools and Equipment

  • Standard tool set, dial indicator

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Check free movement of pump shaft

  2. Check pump bearing temperature, lubricate the bearing or change if needed

  3. Check abnormal noise, check the parts of the pump and the motor and change the defected parts.

  4. Check pump discharge press and capacity

  5. Check pump motor alignment or make alignment
