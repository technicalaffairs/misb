Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection checklist I1, which must be performed every six months as indicated by M6 in the title block

The IMO Pump operate equally efficiently with both Light and Heavy oils and with Liquids having similar Lubricating properties. Its purpose is to Pump oil to fuel nozzle.

Must be greased through one nipple every 4000 operating hours on oil firing

Bearing must be replaced every 3 years of operation on oil firing.

Work to be carried out

Inspection of mechanical seal

Axial type mechanical seals are intended to prevent the leakage of fluid along the driven shaft of a pump. In order to insure that the seal does not wear during operation, it must be lubricated by pumped liquid. An insignificant amount of leakage may therefore occur and suitable provision should be made for collecting this leakage. If there is excessive fuel leakage the mechanical seals must be replaced as the following: 

Dismantling of Mechanical Seal

  1. Remove the screws (130 A) of the ball bearing, undo the front cover (501) and the screws (451). Be careful with the O- ring gasket (506). Remove the washer (130) and back off the power rotor carefully out of the pump stand the power rotor with the sealing washer (139) resting on two wooden crossbars. See fig. 1a.

  2. Undo the locking washer (123) and back off the bearing nut (124). Tap the shaft end lightly with the tin mallet until the ball bearing (122) releases the power rotor the inner ball ring is then supported by the distance sleeve (120). See fig. 1.

  3. Lift the ball bearing (122). Releases retaining cover (527), guiding bush (5110) and mechanical seal (509) from the power rotor. Remove the distance sleeve and the sealing washer with the O- ring gasket (139/140) is released.

  4. Press the sealing bush( 509A- D) out of the guiding bush (5110).when necessary  the O- Ring gasket (511 A) can be  released.

** CAUTION

The ball bearing is always to be washed in the new white spirit. Take Great Care to protect the ball bearing when pump parts are being washed as used Solvent is not clean and can therefore soil the race.

Reassembly of Mechanical Seal

  1. This takes place in reverse order

  2. Place the O- Ring (140) in its groove in the power rotor. Ease the sealing washer (139) over the O- Ring. Pass the sleeve (120) onto the shaft end and smear the upper side of the sealing washer (139) with grease.

  3. Reassembly – see fig- the spring unit (506B) in its groove and bush the seal (509) into the guiding bush (5110) being mindful of the O- Ring (509B) and make sure also that the sealing bush (509A) engages with the locating pin (502).

  4. Pass the guiding bush with seal (5110)/509) on the power rotor. Fit the grease retaining cover (527) and bush the ball bearing into the shaft.

  5. Apply the bearing locater to the inner ring of the ball bearing onto the shaft and fit the mounting screw into the centre hole on the shaft end. See fig. 1b.

  6. Advance the nut and washer of the mounting screw in order to push the inner ring of the ball bearing into its proper position on the shaft end and check that the sealing bush (509A) engages with the locating pin (502). Secure the ball bearing on power rotor with the bearing nut (124) and locating washer (123)

     CAUTION  Never strike the ball holder or outer ring of the bearing.

  7. Mount the power rotor in the pump. Place the washer (130) on the outer ring of the bearing .mount the front cover (501) to the pump casing. Fasten the outer ring of the bearing with the screws (130A) prepare pump of the testing. Proceed in accordance with the instructions given under the heading (Starting up).
