Introduction

The Gas turbine is equipped with a manually operated fire fighting system. It allows for protection of the unit against fire its most hazardous points, i.e. both the combustion chamber covers, where the fuel burners are located and the turbine outlet bearing which is located within the diffuser.A part from permanent installation the system is connected to fire host too, to fight the fire at other possible locations. The fire–fighting is based on a medium – foam, mixed with water and a foam extract, which extinguishes the fire by means of covering and therefore prevents the oxygen supply.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area.

  • Wear safety helmet, shoes, breathing-mask, safety glasses is required.

Tools and Equipment

  • A water tank with the separately stored foam extract inside.

  • CO2 cylinder for pressure and mixing the water with the extract by the bursting the separating membrane.

  • Various air-water foam – mixing nozzles at the above mentioned locations.

  • Manually operated valves to energize the system by CO2 and to select  the flow to the various areas.

  • The necessary pipes and one hose.   

  • The fire control- valves are located near a door in order to allow leaving the machine hall in case of emergency.

  • The foam is absolutely harmless to all machine parts &can be cleaned-off easily.        

 

 

Work to be carried out  

Description     (Refer to fig. 2)

The principal parts of the type LS 160 stationary foam extinguisher are a water tank (1) containing an extract bottle (6) and CO2 pressure bottle (2) a hose reel (8) with 25 m unusable hose, fitted with a foam pipe (9) and a connection for permanently – installed foam pipping (11).

In order to operate the extinguisher, simply open the valve on the CO2bottle (2) and the appropriate spherical cock (4), this allows the CO2   to flow  through the pressure line (5) into the extract bottle (6) the pressure ruptures the bursting disk (7) and the extract is forced into the water tank (1) .

A baffle plate on the extract bottle (6) ensures uniform mixing (Approx. 5%).  When foam is delivered through foam pipe (9), the mixture of water and extract flows through the spindle of the reel into the hose and foam pipe and expands approximately 15 times as it emerges.

When foam is delivered through the permanently – installed pipes (Connection 11) and nozzles, the foam expansion is approximately 70 :1 I.e. it is theoretically possible for 150 L of water extract to generate 11 m3 of foam, however, about 50% of this amount is lost through burning and mechanical destruction.

Operating Instructions to Fight a Fire:

  1. Open the valve on the pressure bottle (2) (turn anti-clockwise as far as   it will go)

  2. Open the appropriate spherical cock (4)

  3. In order to spread foam by means of the hose, unreel as much as required and open the cock on the pipe.

Refilling the installation after use:

 

Refilling the Tank

  1. Disconnect the CO2 pressure linen.

  2. Unscrew nuts (13) and remove the tank cover (12).

  3. Remove the extract bottle from the tank.

  4. Remove the cover (14).

  5. Renew the bursting disk (7) and refit the cover (14).

  6. Thoroughly wash out the water tank, extract bottle and pipes with water.

  7. Fill the water tank with 150 L of water.

  8. Replace the extract bottle.

  9. Fill the bottle with 8L of extract.

  10. Replace the tank cover (12).

 Refilling the CO2 Pressure Bottle

  1. Remove the pressure bottle (2) from its holder.

  2. Check the empty weight of the bottle (approx. 9.7 Kg).

  3. Open the bottle valve.

  4. Connect the bottle to the filling station and fill it with 5 Kg of CO2

  5. Close the bottle valve.

  6. Weight the bottle (total weight = empty weight plus 5 Kg).

  7. If the bottle is too heavy discharge some CO2

  8. Replace the bottle in its holder and reconnect the pressure line (5).

  9. Close the spherical cocks (4).

Maintenance

  1. Every three years, check the weight of the CO2 pressure bottle, if there has been any loss of weight, top up the bottle as described.

  2. Every five years, renew the foam extract.
