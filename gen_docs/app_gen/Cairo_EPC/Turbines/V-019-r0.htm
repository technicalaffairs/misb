Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection checklist I1, which must be performed every six months as indicated by M6 in the title block

High flow, balance valve is used where pneumatic equipment must be kept at specified working pressures and where pneumatic operations could be affected by line pressures fluctuation.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable ID safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, shoes, breathing-mask, safety glasses ... etc.

  • The gas turbine is out of service

Tools and Equipment

  • Safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, shoes, breathing-mask, safety glasses ... etc.

  • Wrench 17

  • 7/32 Allen Wrench.

  • Pointed probe

  • 1/4-inch female gauge.

Work to be carried out

Instillation

  1. Maximum inlet pressure and operating temperature ratings are 300 psig and 200°F.

  2. Install as close as possible to where regulated air is needed.

  3. Install the unit with the air flowing through the body in the direction indicated by the arrow.   

  4. Install the same pipe size unit as the pipe line use avoid using fittings, couplings, etc., that restrict the airflow, unless maximum flow is not needed.

  5. Regulator may be installed so that adjusting handle is in any potation. Attach gauge to one 1/4-inch female gauge port and plug the other l/4-inch female port, or use it as a regulated outlet port.

  6. Turning the adjusting screw in raises the regulated pressure and turning it out lowers the regulated pressure

  7. Panel mount regulators require a 3/4-inch diameter hole and are mountable on panels from 1/8-inch to 9/16-inch thick

Maintenance

Occasionally remove bottom plug and clean (plug, body, valve seat and vent air line on both sides of regulator). It is not necessary to remove regulator from air line to do so.

Disassemble

  1. Shut off air to regulator and vent air line on both sides of regulator. Turn adjusting screw out to relieve compression on spring. (Use 7/32-inch Allen Wrench on tamper-resistant models)  Remove screws, cover, spring, and spring disk. Diaphragm assembly can now be removed. By   removing bottom plug and spring, the valve can be removed from the bottom of   the regula­tor.

  2. If unit will not be regulated to pressure needed or if pressure becomes excessive - remove bottom plug, spring and valve assembly. Clean and check o-ring, valve stem and valve seat for water damage.    

  3. Replace worn or damaged parts. Install repair kit no. RRP-95-149 for se1f-relieving models, and repairs Kit RRP-95-150 for non-relieving models for complete overhaul

  4. If unit leaks at relief port (A) - install proper re­pair kit as listed.

  5. When replacing diaphragm Assembly- Diaphragm assembly RRP96-238 includes   relieving disk seal (not shown) installed Seal not required on non-relieving models.

  6. To replace o-ring GRP-78-023 – This o-ring is above a metal washer which cannot be removed. Using a pointed probe of some kind, pull o-ring out. Force new ring through washer hole into o-ring cavity.
