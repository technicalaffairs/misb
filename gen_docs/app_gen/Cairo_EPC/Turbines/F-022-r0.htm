Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection I1, which must be performed every six months as indicated by M6 in the title block.

The Dynapure Filter is designed to remove particles of mist from contaminated air, and discharge clean air to the atmosphere. The filter draws in contaminated air and removes the mist by spinning action of the drum and filter element. The collected particles are discharged to the drain, and the clean air is exhausted through the outlet. The filter can be used in many industrial applications. It is recommended that the Dynapure should continue to be operated at least twenty minutes after the machine being exhausted is shut off. This will allow time for the unit to purge itself of oil.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area.

  • Wear safety helmet, shoes, breathing-mask, safety glasses is required.

  • Turn off power.

Tools and Equipment

  • Wrench 13,17

  • Air Compressor.

  • Screwdriver.

  • Cleaning Fluid.

  • Clamp.

 

 

Work to be carried out  

Throw away liner Fil-38 replacement

  1. Allow drum to stop rotating.

  2. Unlatch and remove inlet cover.

  3. Remove throw away liner from drum.

  4. Replace new throwaway liner inside drum on top of primary filter. Position liner so the seam is opposite the primary filter seam, allow ends to butt together, do not overlap, Smooth out wrinkles.

  5. Replace inlet cover and secure latch.

If filter is dirty. Replace permanent filter and throwaway Liner

  1. Unlatch and remove inlet cover.

  2. Remove throwaway filter liner from drum.

  3. Inspect primary filter in drum if filter is dirty. Proceed with step. If filter is clean, Skip to step.

  4. Remove shaft nut and impeller (if included), the drum has two threaded holes in the face of the hub. Using a drum puller fastened to these holes, remove drum.

  5. Immerse the drum assembly with the permanent filter intact in kerosene or detergent. Agitate assembly till clean, being careful not to damage drum.

Caution

  • Do not use aromatic Hydrocarbon solvents or ketone solvents.

  • Do not use any Toxic solvents since Toxic vapors may be discharged from residual solvent left in filter when unit is restarted.

  1. If possible, Spin dry.

  2. Remove two of the hex screws that mount the motor. This will release the secondary filter retainer and still hold the motor in place. Remove retainer and pull out the retainer screen, Secondary filter, and E-Z kleen. Rinse secondary filter and E-Z kleen in solvent and wipe retainer and retainer screen with a rag. Allow filers to dry.

  3. Using a rag soaked with solvent. Clean the inside and outside of the casing and the outside of the motor. Check drain to make sure opening is not restricted.

  4. Replace E-Z kleen, secondary filter retainer screen and retainer in this order. Fasten retainer to motor.

  5. Replace drum and impeller and fasten securely with shaft nut. Drum must seat against shaft shoulder to provide proper running clearance in casing.

  6. Place new throwaway liner inside the primary filter. Position liner so the seam is opposite the primary filter seam. Allow ends to butt together. Do not overlap. Smooth out wrinkles.

  7. Replace inlet cover and secure latch.

  8. The installation should be checked periodically for air leaks in the hose. Hose connections. Drainage connections, etc. it is suggested a planned maintenance program be established to insure optimum performance.
