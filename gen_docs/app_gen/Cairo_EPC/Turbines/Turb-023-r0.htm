Introduction

Before starting a gas turbine a visual inspection of the equipment by the shift engineer is strongly recommended to ensure the operator that the unit is ready for operation. Following guide lines for this inspection do not intend to be complete but may help to prevent and reduce failures and increase the reliability of the equipment

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area.

  • Wear safety helmet, shoes, breathing-mask, safety glasses is required.

Tools and Equipment

  • Personal safety equipment, safety goggles, and rubber gloves and rubber apron.

  • Standard screwdrivers set

Work to be carried out  

Check in Control Room

Signalization

  1. Press button 58b3 "Lamp test" all signal lamp must be light.

  2. Press button 57b4 "function test" all signal light must be flash and all horn and sirens must sound.

  3. Press on secontic display button 109b1 "lamp test" (blue) all secontic lamps must light.

 

 

 

Cooling Oil Pump

  1. Start the cooling pump (R5717) with switch 8b1 "cooling oil pump" the cooling oil pump must run.

  2. Check the current increase of approx. 12A on the 125 volt   D.C consumption instrument (2g3).

  3. Alarm "cooling oil pump" has to come on.

  4. Switch off the pump.

Check in Control Room

  1. Check if selector switch on static starting equipment is in the correct position

  2. Check if 380 Volt starting c. b. (4a1) in panel k is in service position

  3. Check if 380 Volt c. b. (282a1) is in service position and on

Outside the Control Room

Following item should be checked

  1. Correct the status of hand valves of the fuel transfer system from fuel tank up to the motor operated fuel valve pos. GT5238 of the unit.

  2. Correct the status of hand valves of the gas supply system up to the motor operated main gas valve pos. GT 8001 of the unit.

  3. Check if both valves on the propane gas bottles open.

  4. Ignition gas pressure must be > 1.5 bar, pointer in automatic changeover valve in the green range.

  5. Check that there is  enough Turbotect in the additive tank

  6. Check that all drain valves on the fuel leakage tank are closed.

  7. Check that both fuel filters before fuel pump are ready for operation.

  8. Check that all mechanical and electrical maintenance works are finished.

  9. Check that all manholes and inspection-openings of the gas turbine are closed.

Inspect top of Combustion Chamber :

  1. Check if cable to sparkplug is connected properly.

  2. Check if all pipes, hoses to the burner are correctly connected and all bolts tightened.

  3. Remove all tools, rags, papers etc. which may have been left by the maintenance crow.

  4. Dry up any fuel – or oil leakages on the burner cover.

  5. Check that lub. Oil filters of the unit are ready for operation and selecting valve either in left or right position.

  6. Check that earthing brushes on generator shaft are properly on and generator shaft clean.

  7. Check the pressure of generator cooling water system. Pressure must be at least 0.8 bar.

  8. Check that CO2 fire fighting system is operating.

Check 11.5 kV Switchgear of the unit:

  1. Check that generator breaker 209 a1 (Z1) is in the service position.

  2. Check that  starting breaker 4a2(Z5)  is in the service position.

  3. Check that  feeder breaker 209 a2 (Z3) is in the service position.

Check on Control Panel

  1. Connect temp. and load recorder (Right switch to 1, left and middle switch to I).

  2. Connect vibration recorder (left switch to I, middle switch to R and right switch to 1).

  3. Take all counter reading for record purposes:

  • Starts.

  • Quick loadings.

  • Operating hours.

  • Correct hours.

  • Generator active power.

  • Generator reactive power.

  • Active power auxiliary.

  1. Make lamp and function test on alarm Secontic indication panels according to Signalization Step.

  2. Check if cooling oil pump is operation according to Cooling Oil Pump Step

  3. Clear all alarms.
