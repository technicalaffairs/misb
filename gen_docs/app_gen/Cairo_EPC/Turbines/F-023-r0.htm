Introduction

The maintenance procedures should be done on the twin fine filter when the pressure drop across the filter is about 0.4 bars.

The design of the twin oil filter is shown in figure 1 .the filter size is selected, so that the pressure loss with clean strainer coat 56 amounts to 0.15-0.25 bar depending on the oil temperature. The by-pass valve 12, 22 is set for maximum pressure drop of about 0.4bar. If this is exceeded. The filter is by-pass this bypass operation is shown by indicator see the figure. Filtration must now be immediately switched to the stands by filter. An indicator 44, 45, 46 on the lower part on the warm gear casing 3 announces which filter is operative.

The pipe from the filter to the bearing is kept as short as possible. To permit its mechanical cleaning during erection. This eliminates the danger of recontamination the already purified oil.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area.

  • Wear safety helmet, shoes, breathing-mask, safety glasses is required.

Tools and Equipment

  • Wrench 14-15,

  • Screwdriver, Hammer,

  • Allan Key 10.

  • Air compressor.

  • Cleaning Fluid.

  • Rope

 

Work to be carried out  

  1. Loosen the star knob screws 68,.69 on the filter cover 52 of the dirty set.

  2. Remove the screws, the filter cover and the gasket 71.

  3. Open the drain cock and drain the filter casing 51 into the drain pipe connected at C.

  4. Loosen the screws 67 of the filter support 53 by means of the socket wrench 70 attached to the star knob screw.

  5. Lift off the filter support 53 by its welded ring bolt and let it drip.

  6. Unscrew the clamps 60, above and below & carefully remove the strainer coat 56 and strainer coat protection 72 form the filter cylinder 57.

  7. Check it for defects. if there are imperfections, replace them by new ones.

  8. The cleaned or new coat is pulled back over the filter cylinder 57 clamped, and the whole unit is dropped into cleaned filter casing 51.

  9. The arrow on the filter points towards the oil outlet D and the centering in of the filter support 53 engages the support ring the filter support and spacer tube 66 are tightened with bolts 67.

  10. Mount the filter support cover 52 with its cleaned and lightly oiled gasket   71 upon the filter casing 51 and tighten the star knob screws 68, 69 firmly   and evenly close the drainers at C & D.

  11. Prior to the expiration of their life expectancy of 1 year, the strainer coat 56 may be washed in any one of the following solvents:

  • White spirits with flash point 39 ْC.

  • Kerosene (solvent naphta).

  • Burning oil.

  • Normal gasoline (not recommended, since it is highly flammable).

  • Perchlorethylene (not recommended, since it is highly noxious).

  1. After rinsing, gently squeeze out the strainer coats 56, but do not wring them out.

  2. Re-insert them after drying as described above.
