Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection check list I1, which must be performed on an annually bases as indicated by Y1 in the title block.

The circuit breaker can perform on an average the operation here under without needing contact and oil replacement at:

06: Opening operation at the maximum breaking capacity value indicated on nameplate.

12: Opening operation at 50% of maximum breaking capacity value indicated on nameplate

500:   Opening operation at the rated current.

1000: Opening operation at the 50% of rated current.

2000: Opening operation at the 25% of rated current.

After certain period of service, it is necessary to inspect from time to time the fixed contact elements: (10) file pitting; if any taken taken care not to modify the shape of element, then clean out filings.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued,

  • The qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable ID safety category.

  • Safety fence with cautions marks surrounding the work area is required.
  • Wear safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet and safety shoes ... etc

  • The gas turbine should be out of service.

  • Switch off the generator's circuit breaker and racked out.

  • Switch off the DC

Work to be Carried Out

Oil Refilling and Pole Washing

  1. Screw out cocks 21 and drain oil of poles into container.

  2. Replace the cocks (21) by any new screws or other means.

  3. Before filling the poles with fresh oil, wash them by pouring some fresh oil and by draining it out as indicated above ; this operation should be repeated three or four times.

  4. Whether washing has been sufficient or not it can be judged by the difference between inlet and outlet oil color.
    Note: The oil which had been used for washing the poles can be reused for filling only after filtering and drying.

  5. After washing, tighten cocks (21), fill the poles with filtered and dried oil up to the mark on the oil level indicator.

  6. Screw out the filling plug or cap and pour slowly the oil into each pole.

  7. After that oil has reached the level mark, wait a few minutes; then if necessary, fill up to the mark.

  8. Record the date of refilling and the number of opening operation in the maintenance file.

Inspection and Replacement of Fixed Contact Elements for Pole RM

  1. Put the circuit breaker out of service without draining oil out and perform the following operations on each pole:

  2. Screw nuts out of upper terminal (17).

  3. Slip out washer (24), gasket (25), insulating cap (15) and gasket (14).

  4. Screw out locking nut (13) and draw out the upper terminal (17), conductor (11), supporting the contact elements (10) by means of the proper kit spanner supplied with the breaker.

  5. Examine the contact elements and if replacement is necessary put the conductor (11) between the jaws of a bench vice covered with aluminum or bakelite plates or with a canvas.

  6. Hold the insulating insulating container (29) and force it out by turning it rightwards and leftwards taking care to gather the elements (10) and spring (28).

  7. Replace the worn out contact elements (10).

  8. Reassemble the pieces and fit the springs (28) with their parts upwards i.e. opposite to arcing plates.

  9. Record the date of refilling and the number of opening operation in the maintenance file.
