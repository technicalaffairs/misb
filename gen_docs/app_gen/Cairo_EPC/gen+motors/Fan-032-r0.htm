Introduction

This document outlines the major maintenance procedures P1, which must be performed every 16000 operating hours as indicated by H16000 in the title block.

The axial fans are used for forwarding the cooling air required for the generator they force the cooling air to form a steady circulation within the machine.

There are two axial fans for every generator. They are mounted on the rotor shaft next to the rotor end –bells the blades are mounted in diametrically opposed positions.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area.

  • Wear safety helmet, shoes, breathing-mask, safety glasses is required.

  • The gas turbine out of service.

  • The generator on a complete isolation.

Tools and Equipment

  • A torque wrench 901 is used to tighten the axial fan blades. Set the prescribed tightening torque on tool (see axial fan data).

 

 

 

Work to be carried out

Dismantling (removal and disassembly)

  1. All axial fan blades are to be dismantled before the turbo rotor is taken out. 

  2. They must be handled carefully and protected from damage.

  3. Replace axial fan blades that have been damaged due to external while they were installed in the machine.

 

Assembly and installation

 

The axial fan blades must not be mounted in place until after turbo rotor has been put in. observes the following points in mounting:

  1. Keep the same numbering. Due to imbalance, it is not permissible to exchange the axial fan blades one for other.

  2. Adjust the axial fan blade so that base runs parallel to marking see the check sheet.

  3. Grease the thread and the nut support surface of the axial fan blade 1ightky with molykote, and tighten the nut to approx. one – third of the required torque. See axial fan data.

  4. Make definitive tightening of the nut all at once using a torque wrench 901.

  5. Secure the threaded pin in place by punching.
