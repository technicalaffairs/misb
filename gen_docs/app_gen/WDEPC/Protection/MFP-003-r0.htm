Introduction

The GE Multilin Model 269 Plus motor management relay uses motor phase current reading combined with stator RTD temperature readings to thermally model the motor being protected.

In addition it takes into account the heating effects of –ve sequence currents in the rotor and calculates the cooling times of the motor.

The relay also monitors the motor and mechanical load for faults and problems.

The GE Multilin Model 269 plus Motor Management  is a modern microcomputer-based product designed to provide complete, accurate protection for industrial motors and their associated mechanical systems. The 269 Plus offers a wide range of protection, monitoring, and diagnostic features in a single, integrated package.

All of the relay set points may be programmed in the field using a simple 12 position keypad and 48 character alphanumeric displays. A built-in" HELP" function can instruct the user on the proper function of each of the programming keys.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued.

  • The qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable ID safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear safety and healthy equipments as safety helmet, safety shoes ... ets

  • Make sure CT secondary circuits are shorted

  • Motor out of service and work permit required

  • Ensure all CT path elements are secure after any test disturbance

Tools and Equipment

  • Single phase secondary injection test set MTM2.

  • Test plug, Variable resistance.

  • Ammeter, Voltmeter.

Work to be Carried Out

Relays Preparation

  1. Review the manual and drawings.

  2. Prepare the previous or commissioning results of the relay testing.

  3. Check the wires of the relay by hand.

  4. Check the AC source is available to the tester.

  5. Check the existence of the DC sources in panels.

  6. Disconnect the trip leads of the relay and connect one of them to the timer of the tester.

  7. Check all the leads used in tests

Test Steps 

  1. Connect the current leads of a tester unit to the relay points according to the drawings and scheme

  2. Inject current to each couple of points till the led of the device displays.

  3. Repeat the steps for each phase according to the test sheet.

  4.  Record the results

Testing the Relay and Protection Outputs

  1. Carefully follow these test procedure steps 

  2. Verify the test result match the relay setting.

  3. Test trip the circuit breaker associated with the relay.

Trip Test

  1. Request the operator to switch on the main circuit breaker.

  2. Operate the output auxiliary contact by injection to trip the circuit breaker.

  3. Check that the circuit breaker tripped.
