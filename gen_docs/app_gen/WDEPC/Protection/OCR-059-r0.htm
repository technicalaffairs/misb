Introduction

This document outlines the maintenance procedure P1, to be performed on an annually as indicated by Y1 in the title block

This procedure details the steps required to completely check the proper operation and calibration of the directional earth fault over current relay.

The document is formatted the way to minimize the possibility of error causing personal injury or damage to equipment

The AT31X1 relay is an over current, secondary, time independent device for electrical equipment at over loads and short circuits. They are mostly used in such cases where low consumption in the current circuits and high resetting are required.

Work to be Carried Out

Over Current Test

  1. Disconnected the circuit breaker.

  2. Remove the input current terminals (7,8,9,10) from terminal board (A).

  3. Connect test leads between the current terminal (7&10) for phase (R).

  4. Increase by injected current slowly until the red indication on the over current element phase R on the relay appears

  5. Record this value on the test sheet (adjust it if needed).

  6. Switch off the set.

  7. Repeat the above test from step (3) to step (6) but inject current between terminal (8,10) for phase (S) and terminals (9,10) for phase (T) on the terminals board (A).

Time Test

  1. Connect the delayed contact numbers (1,2) on the relay to timer terminals on the test set.

  2. Start the test by injecting the pick up current into the relay for phase (R)

  3. Record the value of time on the test sheet (adjust it if needed)

  4. The timer will stop once the relay has pick up.

  5. Repeat the above for phase (S) & (T)

Trip Test

  1. Put the circuit breaker on the test position.

  2. Request the operator to switch on the circuit breaker.

  3. Injecting the pick up current into the relay.

  4. Check that the circuit breaker tripped.

Return Relay to Service

  1. Disconnect the test leads from the terminal board and connect the relay terminals no 7,8,9 and 10 of the relay to normal work condition.

  2. Disconnect the test leads from timer terminal on the relay and connect the time delay terminals of the relay to the normal condition.

  3. If any equipment associated with the relay has been isolated, it should be returned to service by the following restoration steps:
    Connect the breaker trip coil to work condition.
