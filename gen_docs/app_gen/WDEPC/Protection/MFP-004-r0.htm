Introduction

The SR469 Motor Management Relay is a microprocessor based relay designed for the protection and management of medium and large horsepower motors and driven equipment. The SR469 is equipped with 6 output relays for trips, alarms, and start blocks. Motor protection, fault diagnostics, power metering, and RTU functions are integrated into one economical draw out package.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued.

  • The qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable ID safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear safety and healthy equipments as safety helmet, safety shoes ... ets

  • Make sure CT secondary circuits are shorted

  • Motor out of service and work permit required

  • Ensure all CT path elements are secure after any test disturbance

Work to be Carried Out

Relays Preparation

  1. Review the manual and drawings.

  2. Prepare the previous or commissioning results of the relay testing.

  3. Check the AC source is available to the tester.

  4. Check the existence of the DC sources in panels.

  5. Disconnect the trip leads of the relay and connect one of them to the timer of the tester.

  6. Check all the leads used in tests

Test Steps 

  1. Connect the current leads of a tester unit to the relay points according to the drawings and scheme.

  2. Inject current to each couple of points till the red led of the device displays.

  3. Repeat the steps for each pair of points according to the test sheet.

  4. Record the results.

Testing the Relay and Protection Outputs

  1. Carefully follow these test procedure steps 

  2. Verify the test result match the relay setting.

  3. Test trip the circuit breaker associated with the relay

Trip Test

  1. Request the operator to switch on the main circuit breaker.

  2. Operate the output auxiliary contact by injection to trip the circuit breaker.

  3. Check that the circuit breaker tripped.
