Introduction

Type MFAC relays provide high speed differential protection for various items of power system plant including generators, reactors, busbars, motors and the individual windings of power transformers the single element version type MFAC14 is applied when protection is required for earth faults only. The relay measuring element is basically an attracted armature unit of simple and robust construction, supplied from a bridge rectifier. setting are determined by a series of resistors, selected on a seven way plug bridge. type MFAC relays are suitable for use with 0.5A,1A and 5A current transformers at 50Hz or 60Hz .

This procedure details the steps required to completely check the proper operation and calibration of the MFAC14 relay. It is recommended this procedure be carried out on a yearly bases.

The document is formatted the way to minimize the possibility of error causing personal injury or damage to equipment.

Safety Precautions

  • Line isolation is required.

  • A work permit must be issued

  • The maintenance staff should carry the suitable ID safety category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Personal safety and healthy equipment must be used as safety shoes, safety helmet ... etc

  • Make sure CT secondary circuits are shorted before isolating the relay current circuits for test current injection.

  • Switch off the DC power supply before inserting or withdrawing the relay

  • Outage arrangement must be done.

Work to be Carried Out

Relays Preparation

  1. Review the manual and drawings.

  2. Carefully follow these test procedure steps

  3. Check the wires of the relay by hand.

  4. Check all the leads used in tests.

  5. Verify the test results match the relay setting - If not consult technical services.

  6. Make sure CT secondary circuits are shorted at the incoming terminal blocks before isolating the relay current circuits for test.

  7. Look at field maintenance file and compare the position of the setting switches on relay with those shown in the setting sheet.

Testing Procedure (s)

Voltage PICK-UP and DROUP OUT CHECK Test

  1. Connect the external power supply (220 V, 50 HZ) to the voltage injection tester at terminals # 1,3 or 1,4 and the tester output terminals #1,2 will be connected to the relay.

  2. Slowly increase the voltage until the internal contacts of the relay are closed. The relay must operate with a voltage between +/- 10% of the setting value.

  3. Record this value on the test sheet.

  4. Slowly decrease the voltage until the internal contacts of the relay are opened

  5. Record this value on the test sheet.

Trip Test

  1. Request the operator to switch on the main circuit breaker.

  2. Operate the output auxiliary contact by injection to trip the circuit breaker.

  3. Check that the circuit breaker tripped.

Return the Relay and Power Equipment to Service

  1. Verify the test result match the relay setting. If not consult technical services.

  2. Test trip the circuit breaker associated with the relay.

  3. Make a final check that all switches on the relay are set according to the setting sheet.

  4. Make sure the CT secondary circuit is in normal condition

  5. Remove the CT shorts which were installed in the Power Supply

  6. Return the relay and power equipment to service.
