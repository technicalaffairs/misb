Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection I1 on discharger resistor cabinet for battery, which must be performed on an annually bases as indicated by Y1 in the title block.

This document initiated by eng. El-Saeid Mohamed Matarid, tested by eng. Ashraf Kamal El-Din Otba, reviewed by eng. Reda Ragab karam, accepted by eng. Hamdy Ahmed Dorgham general director, and approved by eng. Maamoun Abou El-Magd Atta Allah: head sector of  Damanhour Power Station.

This document certified by eng. Mona El-Sharkawy: WDEPC inspection & quality control head sector, MPIS coordinator.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued.

  • The qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable ID safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area.

  • Wear safety helmet, shoes, breathing-mask, safety glasses is required.

  • Isolate the rectifier/charger out of service and earth.

  • The presence of current in load is signaled by flashing caution lamp and a display segment

Tools and Equipment

  • Safety helmet, shoes

  • Screw driver set, air blower

  • Fine cloth for cleaning purposes.

  • Portable earth discharging test loads.

Work to be carried out

Operation (Refer to figure 1)

  1. Connect the 220 V or 48 V battery - respecting polarities to the terminals marked "-" (common) and "+220 V DC" or "+ 48 V DC" respectively:

  2. Connect the control auxiliary circuit to 220v-50Hz socket:

  3. Rotate the selector switches CM2-CM3-CM4 in "0" position:

  4. Put at zero the hour meter "HM"

  5. Close the switch" 1 "

After Operation

Manual Control Battery Discharger:

  1. Rotate the selector switch CMI in "MAN" position

  2. Push the push button PM:

  3. Select by means of selector switch CM2 the right discharge circuit with regard to the voltage and current values of the battery in test (see table 1)

Pos.1   220V-56.5A-UPS SYSTEM with selector switch CM4 in pos. 0 for discharge regulation operate with the selector switch CM3 (pos.I-2-3) see tab.2

Pos.2  220v-67.4A -MAIN DISTRIBUTION with the selector switch CM4 in pos. 0 for discharge regulation operate with the selector switch CM3 (pos.3-4-5) (see tab.2):

Pos.3   48v-56.5A-MAIN DISTRIBUTION with the selector switch CM3 in pos. 0 for discharge regulation operate with the selector switch CM4 (pos.I-2-3) (see tab.2) :

To stop the discharge (interruption or emergency) push the pushbutton P A and open the switch I :

 Automatic Control  Battery Discharger:

  1. Rotate the selector switch CMI in pos. AUTO

  2. Select by means of the selector switch CM2 the right discharge circuit with regard to the voltage and current values of the battery in test (see tab. I) in this operation type the discharge regulation is automatic :

  3. Push the push button PM

  4. To stop the discharge (interruption or emergency) push the pushbutton PA and open the switch I

  5. Discharge Length is controlled by the timer T that is adjusted for 5 hours .

  6. The manual zero setting hour meter HM (see4) reports the elapsed time from the beginning of the discharge .

  7. After the 5 hours the supply circuit opens automatically.

  8. If during the discharge the voltage value of the battery in test drops under the minimum allowed value voltage relay disconnect  the supply circuit with the consequent
