General Description of the Circuit Breaker

The MHMe-ID interrupting circuit breakers are designed and built as single phase Modular components or sub-assemblies

   - One 280 liter vessel in welded aluminum sheet, proved with.

   - Self sealing gas filling coupling

   - A basket capable of containing 3.5kg of molecular sieves.

   - A safety plate to prevent over pressure effects

   - Two coupling flanges for attaching the pole vessel.

   - Two supporting insulators in epoxy resin.

   - One base box with pneumatic mechanism.

   - One puffer type interrupting assembly, including fixed and movable contacts.

The compressor proper of Westinghouse design and construction has the following nameplate rating:

Tools and Equipment

  • Electrical tool kit, and torque wrench

  • An industrial vacuum cleaner, plus clean, dry cloth rags

  • SF6 gas handling and reconditioning trailer.

  • SF6 gas detector, portable pressure gauge.

  • MP MOLYSLIP grease, soapsuds, and vacuum cleaner

Work to be Carried Out

Compressor "Operating Mechanism"

  1. Drain the lubricating oil through plug 45 figure 2, remove the opposite plug 54, remove the opposite plug 54 as well, clean out the crankcase bottom if possible use a blast of compressed air. Re-apply the plugs and refill with new heavy - duty, air compressor oil.

  2. Clean out filter 17 figure 2, and the inside of condensate trap 7 figure 1

  3. Remove the collar that fixes the air cleaner to the suction duct and clean up filter 5 figure 1, with petrol and dry it out with compressed air

  4. With piping system at 18 bar, check safety valve 3 for correct operation by pulling up the valve plunger

  5. Check the air pressure drop stages.  Alarm, and trip

  6. Check the pressure drop caused by the safety valve operation and by the subsequent air pressure recovery as follows:

  • The operating points of all pressure-operated contacts 63, 63L, 63AL, and 63R

  • The pressure at which the safety valve recloses

  • Check the compressor pump-up time ref. to the commissioning value

Circuit Breaker

  1. Make sure that all contact circuit terminals and auxiliary switch contacts are clean and tightened,

  2. Make sure that the control circuits are de-energized, and check that all pivot pins of the bistable spring devise and the auxiliary contact links of each pole are cleaned and lubricated if necessary, clean out, inspect and re-lubricate the pins, using MP MOLYSLIP grease.

  3. Switch ON the dc control circuit.

  4. Make sure that the control circuits and indicator circuits are energized, check the operating pressure of the gas alarm and interlock contacts 63GAL and 63G of the gas pressure gauge, by loosening and retightening the pressure gauge coupling so as to produce a transient, local pressure drop sufficient to close and re-open the gauge contacts and whether the operating pressures comply with the assigned values.
        - Normal pressure from 6 to 6.5bar, Alarm from 5.8 to 5.9bar, and trip "lockout at 5.5bar.

  5. If necessary, refill each breaker pole with SF6 gas

  6. Using soapsuds, make sure that the gauge couplings are gas-tight again

  7. Check SF6 gas leakage for CB compartment.

  8. Check the SF6 humidity ref to MPIOS doc no. TD-SS-006-r1
