Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear safety and healthy equipment as helmet, safety shoes, breathing mask, and safety glasses

    •

  • Breaker in service.

Tools and Equipment

  • SF6 gas handling and reconditioning trailer.

  • SF6 gas detector, Electrical tool kit, soap studs, and torque wrench

  • An industrial vacuum cleaner, plus clean and dry cloth, rags.

Work to be Carried Out

With the breaker in service, perform the inspections and maintenance operations listed hereunder and record in a logbook anything noteworthy.

 

 

  1. Make sure that after every compressor pump-up cycle, the water that might have collected in the compressor condensate tap is automatically and completely drained by the elctro-valve that is connected to the trap bottom, and drain the condensate water from the compressor manually and record the status to be maintained. .

  2. Check the compressor oil level and if necessary, fill up with air compressor oil.

  3. Read and record the gas pressure values of each pole (5.8bar). If not, check for SF6 gas leakage of CB compartment using gas detector and if there is leakage use soap studs to confirm the leakage, then correct the leakage.

  4. Clean the outside surface of circuit breaker from dust using vacuum cleaner.

  5. Read and record the number of breaker operations and the values indicated by the compressor hour meter, if provided

  6. Check the tightness of bolts using torque wrench referred to the attached table
