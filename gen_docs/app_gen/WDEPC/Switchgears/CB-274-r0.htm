Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection I2 , which must be performed every six months as indicated by M6 in the title block.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area.

  • Wear safety helmet, shoes, breathing-mask, safety glasses is required.

  • The circuit breaker must be isolated completely and properly earthed from both sides,

  • DC control power should be disconnected.

  • Wear breathing mask to protect your lung tissues from the dust particles before using a blower to clean the control cabinet or when cleaning the insulators.

Tools and Equipment

  • Safety helmet, shoes, breathing mask, safety glasses.

  • Hydraulic test gauges, hydraulic oil, and SF6 gas if needed,

  • SF6 test gauge, micro ohmmeter, and air Blower.

  • Megger device

Work to be carried out

General Procedures

Before putting the circuit-breaker into service carry out the following

  1. Check the tightness of the electrical connections on the circuit -breaker terminals.

  2. Establish the setting of the direct solid-state over current  relay  (if provided).

  3. Check that the value of the supply voltage for the auxiliary circuit is within 85% and 110% of the rated voltage of the electrical devices.

  4. Check that no foreign body, such as packing, has got into the moving parts.

NOTE

To gain access to the operating mechanism, safely discharge the closing springs (by closing and opening the circuit-breaker), then remove the cover of the operating mechanism.

  1. Check that air circulation in the circuit-breaker installation site is adequate so that there is no danger of overheating.

  2. Check that the gas pressure is correct. (green lamp on), if not, reset the correct pressure

  3. Carry out the checks indicated in the following table.

Manual Operating Mechanism

 

Carry out a few closing and opening operations to see if the operations and relative signals occur correctly             

N.B supply  the under voltage relay with the relative rated voltage (if provided).

Motor Operating Mechanism

  1.  Supply geared motor for spring charging at the relative rated voltage to see if the springs are charged correctly if the signal are correct the geared motor cuts off when the springs are charged.

  2. Carry out a few closing and opening operations, the geared motor recharges the springs after each closing operation                    

  N.B. Supply the under voltage relay  with the relative rated voltage (if provided)

 Under Voltage Relay  (If Provided)

  1. Supply the under voltage release at the relative rated voltage and carry out the circuit-breaker closing operation if the circuit-breaker closes correctly the signal changes over

  2. Disconnect the power supply from the release the circuit-breaker opens the signal changes over.

Key Lock With Possible Accept contact (Bch) to the closing circuit (if provided)

  1. Open the circuit-breaker.

  2. Turn the key and remove it

  3. Attempt the circuit-breaker closing operation, neither manual nor electric, closing take place

Auxiliary Contacts In The Operating Mechanism

  1. Insert the auxiliary contacts into suitable signaling circuits.

  2. Carry out a few closing and opening operations of the circuit-breaker to see if signals occur correctly.

Auxiliary Transmitted Contacts for Indicating that the Circuit-Breaker is Open/Closed Connected Isolated

  1. Insert the auxiliary contacts into suitable signaling circuits with the circuit-breaker inside the enclosure carry out a few closing and opening operation.

  2. Put the circuit-breaker in the withdrawn position to see if the signals of the relative operations occur correctly

Insulation Resistance

  1. With a 2500 V megger, measure the insulation resistance between the phase and between phases and exposed conductive part of the medium voltage circuit. The insulation resistance should be at least 50 MO, and in any case. Constant in time if values are different, find out the cause.

  2. With a 500 V megger (installed equipment permitting) measure the insulation resistance between the auxiliary circuits and the exposed conductive part. The insulation resistance should be a few MW and, in any case. Constant in time

Electromechanical Interlocking Device (if provided)

  1. With no supply to the interlocking device try to rack-in the circuit-breaker into the enclosure .Racking-in is not possible

  2. Supply the interlocking device at the relative rated voltage

  3. Carry out the racking-in operation .Racking-in is possible
