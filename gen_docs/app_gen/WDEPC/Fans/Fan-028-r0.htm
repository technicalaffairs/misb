Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category

  • Disconnect the power supply of the air fan motor 6 kv open the C.B and tage it out

  • Be sure the flue gas fan is stopped and the unit shutdown

  • Wear safety helmet, shoes

  • Safety fence with caution works surrounding the work area is required

Tools and Equipment

  • New grease, grease gun

  • Suitable keys from 12-46 mm

  • Grinding wheel

  • Suitable lifting device

    •

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Inspection of the bearing at evident wear increase radial clearance

  2. Exchange the lubricating material in the bearing

  3. Control the tightness of all screw (impeller, bearing and motor base)

  4. Check the coupling flexible intermediate ring change it if necessary

  5. Inject new grease by grease gun

  6. Isolate the dampers of the suction and delivery (mechanically)

Suction chamber is provided with a door enabling entry into the suction chamber at checking or at the general dismounting of the fan. The suction chambers of large fans are sectioned because of transportation reasons.

Inner bearing is located in the core of the regulating part and takes up radial force. It is cooled by air. It is possible to check the temperature of the inner bearing by means of a built - in thermometer the inner bearing is shown on fig 1

Outer bearing is fastened on the stand of the suction chamber, it takes up mainly axial force the bearing covers of large fans are provided with holes enabling the connection of the resistance thermometer fig 2 is the bearing of fans sizes 1000 - 1800

 

Figure 2

  1. When taking off the bearing case (3) with rear lid (5) remove the thermometer (10) and lubricating pipe (12). To disconnect the thermometer, it is necessary to pull away the cooling pipe of the shaft, eventually even the shaft

  2. The assembly of the new bearing is performed according to assembly regulations for fan bearings. Before fitting the bush and bearing onto the conical pin. The new bearing must be secured against sliding with a securing ring. The bearing body and lid must be cleaned and the lid filled with new grease.
    The pulling of the bush onto the pin is performed by tightening the nut (7)
    It is recommended to lubricate the thread on the shaft pin with molyka or graphite paste (the listed lubricants must not get on the contact area of the conical pin of the shaft). After thoroughly tightening the nut, secure it against becoming unscrewed

  3. After assembling the bearing test the tightness of the run by turning. The disassembled-parts of the inner bearing are shown on fig 3


Figure 3

  1. After disassembling the outer bearing, slide the electric motor to the proper distance and pull the coupling or belt pulley from the pin of the fan shaft

  2. At the inspection and exchange of the outer bearing lubricated with oil - fig. 2 (bearings of larger fans) you must first let out the oil (screw 12) through the hole in the lower part of the lid. After taking off the front lid (3) and ring (5) loosen the nut which fastens, bush and strike the auxiliary pipe to loosen the bush. After loosening the nut which fastens the bearing case to the stand flange pull, the bearing case (2) with the bearings and screwed on rear lid (4) from the shaft pin. After rinsing or exchanging the bearing slide the rear ring (6) case bearing with fastening bush and case onto the shaft slightly lighten nuts which fasten the case to the flange of the stand, then fasten the bearing with the nut of the fastening bush secure the nuts and tighten (the nuts which fasten the bearing case thoroughly) put on the front ring and fasten the lid with screws.
    Clean the joint between the case and lids and renew the packing with hermetic before assembly. After assembling the bearing fill it with oil so that the level would reach to the center of the oil gauge and test the light run of the bearing by turning the shaft. The disassembled outer bearing of larger fans is shown on fig 4

  3. Before dismounting the bearing mark the individual joints with a symbol (groove) and perform the mounting so that the symbol would be on the same place on both parts

  4. After mounting secure the position of the case of the outer bearing by pinning in

  5. When exchanging the bearing it is necessary to discover the cause of the damage and eliminate it in order to prevent the premature damaging of the new bearing  .
