Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued.

  • The qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable ID safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear safety and healthy equipments as safety helmet, safety shoes, ... etc

  • The transformer must be was isolated and proper grounding is required.

  • There is a danger of falling into the oil containments pit when working near large power transformers.

  • There is danger of falling off the top of the transformer when cleaning insulator, wear safety belt.

 

 

 

 

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete tool set.

  • Digital Avometer, test plug, and test leads.

  • Meager device.

  • Blower or vacuum cleaner.

  • PVC Ladder - Brushes.

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Determine that the oil level in the transformer tank and all liquid filled compartments, such as junction boxes or switches, is satisfactory.

  2. Test the dielectric strength of the Oil from the tank bottom that tests, check often based upon need.

  3. Clean all bushings if dirty, and inspect the porcelain for cracks.

  4. Check the pressure relief device.

  5. Check temperature gauge, liquid level gauges, pressure gauges, and other
    indicators. Record results.

  6. Carryout the Megger check or power factor check of insulation for comparison with
    previous observations.

  7. Clean fan blades and check fan operation by turning control switch to "Manual".

  8. Check paint on thank and accessories and repaint when required.

  9. Make certain that no tools or other objects have been left in or on ,the
    transformer.

  10. Check cables tighten.
