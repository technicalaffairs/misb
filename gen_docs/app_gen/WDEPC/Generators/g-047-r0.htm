Introduction

This document outlines the major maintenance procedures P2 on Generator, which must be performed every six years as indicated by Y6 in the title block.

The first draft initiated by eng. El-Said Mohamed Matarid, tested by eng. Reda Ragab Karam, accepted by general manager eng. Hamdy Dorgam, approved by eng. Gaber Mohamed Farahat head sector of Damanhour power station, and certified by eng. Sania Diab Attia Diab WDEPC inspection head sector and MPIS coordinator.

 

Safety Precautions

  • Work order must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should the suitable safety category

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area

  • Wear safety helmet, shoes, gloves .... etc

  • Steam turbine out of service at cold state

Tools and Equipment

  • Device for insertion and removal of rotor

  • Air blower, special tools as mention at list No

  • Testers (ohmmeter, voltmeter, ammeter)

  • 500 v megger

  • Portable direct current voltage test instrument up to a value twice the rated voltage of the generator

  • Revolving mirror or optical fiber endoscope

  • Halogen leak detector

  • Equipment for rotor removal (see that it is complete and in good working conditions)

  • Hosting cables for rotor

  • Complete tool kit for coil winder

  • 2 Hydraulic jacks with associated hand - operated pumps for lifting the generator frame (the minimum lifting capacity if each jack shall be at least equal to 1/4 the total generator weight)

Work to be carried out

  1. Disassemble the upper shields and internal protections to permit visual inspection of the stator and rotor end windings with the aid of an optical fiber endoscope or revolving mirror

  2. Inspect the bushing terminals and the terminal box through the manholes

  3. Check the insulation by direct current voltage (HV test) to be applied to each stator winding phase

  4. Inspect the lubrication plant

  5. Inspect the coolers (which shall be cleaned if necessary)

  6. Check the bearing insulation and seal housing collector end

  7. Check the rotor insulation

  8. Visual inspection of the teflon tubes of the stator end - winding

  9. Inspect the slip ring box

  10. Check the collector rings and reversing of the polarity if required inspection of the collector surfaces (and grinding if required)

  11. Check the turbine - generator alignment

  12. Check the instrumentation

  13. Leak test of the casing at the rated pressure to look for and eliminate any gas leads inspection of the seal oil unit and of the hydrogen system

  14. Check the stator water coolers

  15. Check the main and make up stator water filters

  16. Check the stator water pumps pressure and temperature regulation valves

  17. All checks and inspections already listed for the three - yearly partial overhaul

  18. Remove the rotor (after disassembling of slip ring box shields and internal protections)

  19. Inspect and check the stator winding and stator core (fastening of the end windings wedging etc)

  20. Inspect and check the rotor (retaining rings keys end windings fan blades etc)

Removal of Exciter

  1. Remove the exciter housing including the collector brush rigging

  2. Disconnect all oil lines protecting the openings against entrance of dirt

  3. Disconnect all electrical connections pushing the cable ends below the floor line if possible

  4. Remove all bolts and dowels holding the exciter base to the foundation

  5. Check to see that all parts are free and that no interference exists

Disassembly of Rotor

  1. Remove brush - holder rigging and complete direct connected exciter

  2. Remove the coward (side the turbine)

  3. Remove the outside air shield rings and outside shields

  4. Remove the inside air shield rings and inside air shields

  5. Remove the pedestal cop. Oil deflectors and bearing lining

  6. Place a jacking device or support under the coupling end of the rotor

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Program for removal or insertion of the Rotor

Caution:
     * Don't suspend or set down the rotor in the vicinity of the bearing surface (damage to the rotor)
     * Place protective material (thick cardboard press pan or sealing material)
     * Put the strips of wood between the two wire ropes (no slipping the wire and adjusting the
        middle when loaded angle between loops equal 45°)

  1. Insert sliding plate. You see the fig 1- item 1, fix the sliding plate on the rotor fan hubs in 12 O'clock position pull through the stator bar the sling (item 2, fig. 1) by using the copper wire

  2. Insert the sliding plate on the tensed sling between the supports (item 4 fig 1) in the upper position when fitted remove the sling (item 2 fig 1) and the adjusting screws (item 3 fig 1) press at both ends the sliding plate with the supports (item 4 fig 1 ) to the erection device turn the rotor until the sliding plate is reaching the 6 O'clock position loosen, the screws (item 5 fig1) until the sliding plate lays in the stator bar. Remove supports (item 4 fig 1 ) at driving side

  3. Fix the inter - mediate piece (item 7 fig 2) for the sliding pad on the fan hubs at driving end in the 12  O'clock position

  4. Turn the rotor until the inter - mediate piece is reaching the position of the sliding plate : by the same, the same, the sliding plate erection device has turned again in the 12 O'clock position and can now be removed

  5. Dismantle bearing brackets and upper bearing parts

  6. Mount the lifting jack (item 8 fig 2) at the B end

  7. Lift the rotor as high as possible using the crane at A end and the lifting jack at B end, make sure that the rotor does not touch the stator windings and iron core

  8. Pull two lengths of string through the stator bar and use them to pull the sliding pad (item 9 fig 2) from the B end to the A end (to keep connect the air pipe line to the B end) the distance between the pad and the end of the stator iron core should be at 150 mm

  9. Put the second sliding pad (item 10 fig 2) unclear the fan hub with the inter mediate piece (to keep connect the air pipe line to the A end)

  10. Lower the rotor at both ends until the A end lies on the pad (item 9 fig 2) in this position the rotor is 1.5 mm above the center line)

  11. With the aid of the crane, dismantle the lower bearing shell and the bearing rings at both ends

  12. Assemble bearing pedestal, bracket and two - paet ring (item 11) with threaded bolts (item 12) and nuts (item 13) to the rotor shaft at the B end (all items Nos. refer to (fig 2 section c-c)

  13. Change on B end of the rotor from the lifting jack to the crane sling then check with a spirit level that the rotor remains at horizontal position

  14. Open the compressed air valve and take out the rotor with slight lateral movements advancing the crane accordingly

Attention

  1. Watch the second sliding pad (item 10 fig 2 ) as soon as this pad is full contact with the stator core, the connection to the air supply must be changed over

  2. Continue the withdraw of the rotor until its center of gravity has approx reached the midpoint of the bearing pedestal support the gravity center line is approx 250 mm besides of the rotor midpoint i.e. the slot wedges centering screws

  3. Lower the rotor on the wooden support (item 14 fig 3) and set down the sling on the bearing pedestal support

  4. Dismantle lifting jack at the B end with the crane

  5. Lift the rotor with the sling and carefully pull it right out of the stator bore

Note : for insertion of the rotor. Proceed in the reversed sequence

 

General Maintenance Program

Stator Core   

  1. Check the compression of stack for conductor

  2. Check the compression of stack, end laminations (dirty and tighten)

  3. Stack seating of pressure fingers (eddy current, vibration gap)

  4. Check the correct seating of spacing bars

  5. Check the quality of the varnish

  6. Check the air vents and passages

  7. Clean all the dirty places

Stator  Winding

  1. Check the correct location of insulation sheets and slot wedge

  2. Check the seating of the winding

  3. Check the whipping case

  4. Check the supports for the winding

  5. Check the Embitterment of coil - end insulation

  6. Check the joints between terminals and connectors

  7. Varnish and dirt

  8. Measure the insulation resistance:

    9. Phase U :          M. ohm

    Neutral   :          M. ohm
    Megger  : ..........V, .......... C° .......... Mi

    Use the megger (1000 V - 5000V)

  9. Check the armature winding DC resistance per phase (Ref. Value = 0.006 Ω)

Rotor Winding

  1. Check the seating of balancing weights

  2. Check the seating of slot wedges position

  3. Check the seating of fans angle

  4. Check the crack of retaining rings

  5. Check the bearing surfaces

  6. Check the surfaces of bearing shoulders

  7. Check the opening of ventilation holes in slot wedges

  8. Check the winding connection

  9. Check the damper rings and clamps

  10. Check the winding insulation (intermediate layers)

  11. Check the coil insulation

  12. Check the coil deformation

  13. Check the spacers in the coil ends

  14. Clean all windings

  15. Insulation resistance          M ohm
    Megger .......... V, ..........C°, ............Min   (at  500 DC)

  16. Check the rotor winding resistance DC

Bearings

  1. Check the condition of running surfaces of bearing shells

  2. Check the condition of end surfaces of bearing shells

  3. Check the condition of bearing insulation

  4. Check the condition of oil seal

  5. Check the escaping of oil mist

Coolers and Air Filters

  1. Check the condition of cooling fans

  2. Check the condition inside of cooling tubes

  3. Check the pressure test (max. 4 bar) for new coolers and oil coolers (max. 2.5 bar)

  4. Check the condition of the air filter

 

Brushes Case

  1. Check the side clearance between brush and brushes holder

  2. Check the collector brushes ring should cleaned by using air blower

  3. Check the inspect all nuts bolts and cop screws for tightness

  4. Check the inspect the collector ring wear

  5. Check the inspect sparking brushes

  6. Check the clearance between brush holder and collector ring

  7. Check the brush wear brush top at least 6 mm above top of brush box

Shaft Grounding Brushes

  1. Remove the brushes from their holders 

  2. Clean brushes and holders until brush may move freely 

  3. Clean brushes clips contacts

  4. Use a soft brush seating stoner a convos a soft brush padded to remove excess film from shaft

  5. The brushes should be changed when down to its minimum length of approximately 20 mm

  6. Check spring force - it should beat least 200 g/c
