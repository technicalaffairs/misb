Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection I1 for collector rings brushes and brush - holders for generator which must be performed on daily - weekly - monthly and after shut down as indicated in the title block.

The first draft initiated by eng. Saad El-Nashar, tested by eng. Reda Ragab Karam, accepted by general manager eng. Hamdy Dorgam , approved by eng. Gaber Mohamed Farahat and certified by eng. Sania Diab Attia: WDEPC inspection head sector and MPIS coordinator.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category

  • Maintenance staff should be well trained qualified or directly supervised by some one who is 

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required

  • Personal protective equipment must be used (safety shoes, helmet, breathing mask, safety glasses)

Tools and Equipment

  • Personal protective equipment as safety shoes, helmet breathing mask and safety glasses

  • Brushes blower megger

  • Cleaning fluids such as - chlorothene

Work to be carried out

Visual daily checks

The following items should checked

  1. The absence of sparks in the brushes

  2. The normal noise level of the brushes

  3. The state of the flexile leads of the brushes

  4. The state of cleanliness

  5. The temperature of the cooling air (even by simply using a hand or thermometer)

Visual inspections and weekly checks
Besides the previous daily checks the following weekly check should be made

  1. Shaft % ground voltage measurement

  2. The state of brush wear

  3. The state of the cooling air filter cleaning

Visual inspections and monthly check
Besides the previous daily and weekly checks the following monthly check should be made

  1. The state of the contact surface of the rings with running machine a stroboscope lamp synchronized to the rotor speed can be used

  2. Measure and record the vibrations of the brushes (at least per ring)

Checks after machine shut down

  1. After the machine has been in continuous operation for more than a month during shut - down it is necessary

Removal of exciter

  1. Remove the exciter housing including the collector brush rigging

  2. Remove all bolts and dowels holding the exciter base to the foundation

  3. Check to see that all parts all parts are free and that no interference excite

  4. Check the state of the rings and measure their degree wear the depth of any grooves and any run
    out : if necessary grind the rings

  5. Check the wear and the state of every brush

  6. Check the freedom of movement of each brush

  7. Check the state of every brush holder and in particular the pressure springs substitute any worn out spring

  8. Check the tightness of all the screws in electrical and mechanical connections

  9. Check the cleanliness of the rings and insulating parts

  10. If necessary invert the polarity of the ringsw

Stator cooling system

Specification

  1. Motor power 30 kw / 2950 r.p.m

  2. Main pump flow 926 L/min

  3. Main pump pressure 8.2 bar

  4. Deionize water flow 120 L/min

  5. Normal water conductivity 0.2-0.5 micron S/cm

  6. Two cartridge filter 1.5 micron

Periodic inspections

  1. Daily
    Observe all the indicating instructions for recommended operating conditions of the liquid cooling system check liquid level in tank

Weekly

  1. Alternate use of the cooling liquid circulation pumps

  2. Check the pressure drop across the cooling liquid filter

  3. Check the temperatures (RTDS) at cooling liquid outlet connections

Six months

  1. Check the quality of the stator cooling liquid

  2. Calibrate conductivity instrument

 

 

Twelve months

  1. Test the operation of the automatic turbine trip devices which operate due to the reduced flow or high temperature of the armature cooling liquid

  2. Check the calibration of the automatic trip devices

  3. Check the turbine trip and alarm devices

Eighteen months

  1. Replace the filter cartridges

Gas drier & Hydrogen System

Routine checks

  1. During normal operation the following checks should be made
    Hydrogen purity 98 %
    Hydrogen pressure 4.15 (according to load)

  2. Every two days check the condition and color of the drier salts - the moisture saturated salts turn pink reactive the salts as previously described salts can be replaced each year

  3. Check the hydrogen consumption once a week

  4. Check the fullness of hydrogen and carbon dioxide bottles
