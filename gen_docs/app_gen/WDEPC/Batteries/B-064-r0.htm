Introduction

This document outlines the equipment  inspection I2 on UPS 220 V battery, which must be performed on yearly bases as indicated by Y1 in the title block.

Rated capacity (280Ah)

High rate charge volt     : 1.6v / cell                      

Final discharge volt       : 1.1v/cell

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued.

  • The qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable ID safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear safety and healthy equipments as safety helmet, safety shoes, ... etc.

  • No smoking is permitted in battery room.

  • Use Standard personnel safety equipment, goggles, rubber, gloves, rubber apron, eye rinsing device.

  • Do not place any Tools on Top of battery cells (Danger of short circuit).

  • Never use the same tools on these cells, you may be used before on lead acid cells.

 

 

Tools and Equipment

  • Personal safety equipment, safety goggles, and rubber gloves and rubber apron … etc.

  • Standard screwdrivers set, density meter, voltmeter, brushes, distilled water, and rags.

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Read overall voltage of bank and record on battery maintenance card.

  2. Disconnect AC supply to Battery charger.

  3. Clean the battery.

  4. Check the electrolyte level and add distilled water (if necessary).

  5. Check the pole and connectors and clean any salt deposits with distilled water.

  6. Check that connections are tight

  7. Lubricate the pole and connectors with Vaseline including the disco and nuts.

  8. Read voltage per cell and record on battery maintenance.

  9. Take the electrolyte density and record on battery maintenance card .

  10. Again read overall voltage of bank and record on battery maintenance card and compare the reading from step no. 1.

  11. If overall voltage is below 220 volts place bank on float (high charge or manual charge for 5 hours).

  12. Compare the present reading with the last reading.

  13. Check the temperature inside the battery room not exceeding 45° C maximum and 20 degree min.

  14. Check the ventilation of the battery room to ensure their correct operation.
