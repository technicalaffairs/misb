Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area.

  • Wear safety equipment, goggles, rubber gloves, rubber apron, eye rinsing device.

  • No smoking is permitted in battery room.

  • Do not place any tools on top of battery cells (Danger of short circuit)

  • Never use the same tools on these cells, you may use before on lead acid cells.

 

 

Tools and Equipment

  • Personal safety equipment,  safety goggles, rubber gloves and rubber apron.

  • Standard screwdrivers set, density meter, voltmeter, brushes, distilled water, and rags

Work to be carried out

  1. Read overall voltage of bank and record on battery maintenance card.

  2. Disconnect AC supply to battery charger.

  3. Clean the dust of battery.

  4. Check the electrolyte level and add distilled water (if necessary) to the maximum level if the cells are completely charged and to 10 mm below the maximum level if they are discharged.

  5. Check the pole and connectors and clean any salt deposits with distilled water.

  6. Check that connections are tightened.

  7. Lubricate the pole and connectors with Vaseline including the disco and nuts.

  8. Read voltage per cell and record on battery maintenance.

  9. Inspect the electrolyte density and record on battery maintenance card (1.19 kg I)

  10. Again read overall voltage of bank and record on battery maintenance card and compare the reading from step no. 1

  11. If overall voltage is below 220 volts place bank on float (high charge for 5 hours) after which you switch the charger back on normal charge.

  12. Compare the present reading with the last reading.

  13. Check the temperature inside the battery room not exceeding 45° C maximum and 20° C minimum.

  14. Check the ventilation of the battery room to ensure their correct operation.
