Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance stuff should be carry the suitable safety category ..

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear safety and healthy equipment as safety helmets, shoes, gloves,  etc.

  • Isolated the circuit breaker of the pump motor.

  • Isolate the Pump mechanically by closing delivery valves.

  • Isolate cooling water for the pump.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set.

  • Cleaning fluid, Filler, Feeler gauge.

  • Rags, Grease.

  • Lifting equipment.

Special Tools

  • Support for transport and dismantling ( see figure 5).

  •  Wrench for blades disassembly ( see figure 10).

  • Impeller puller ( see figure 8 ).

 Work to be Carried Out

Disassembly

To disassemble the pump the following procedures are recommended:

WARNING

  1. Make sure that the main circuit breaker of the pump electric motor is locked open.

  2. Make sure that the discharge butterfly valve is closed.

  3. Empty the suction basin of the pump.

  4. Make the scaffold ready in the basin; unscrew and take away the bolts (41) with their nuts and washers which connect the discharge elbow of the pump to the discharge piping.

  5. Disconnect the thrust bearing oil cooling water pipe at the relevant flanges (42)

  6. Disconnect the cooling and lube water to gland and guide bearing at the relevant flanges (43)

  7. Disconnect the wires at the junction box (38)

  8. Fix the special tool (36) on to its suitable site on the pump shaft (see Fig.5)

  9. Fix its stay bolts to the pump bracket to prevent shaft movements during next steps.

  10. Take off the flexible coupling with the following procedure (see Fig. 6)

  • Unscrew the bolts (1)

  • Remove the cover (2)

  • Remove the inner sleeve (3)

  • Unscrew the connecting bolts (4)

  • Remove the upper external sleeve (5)

  • Heat up the lower sleeve (6) up to 130°C about and remove it.

  1. Remove the Electric Motor
    (NOTE At this step the whole pump internal part could be pulled out to inspect them as per next paragraph.)

  2. Screw the bolts (44) and pull out the entire circulating pump internal hooking onto the proper eyelet (45) and place it horizontally on to suitable supports.

  3. To remove the thrust bearing the following procedure must be carried out

  • Unscrew the bolts (1)

  • Lift the ring (4)

  • Remove the 2 half rings (2)

  • Lift the sleeve (3)

  • Take off the thrust bearing (5)

  1. Take off the support for transport and dismounting

  2. Dismount the two half gland (14)

  3. Remove the discharge elbow (12)

  4. Remove the shaft protecting tube (9)

  5. Remove the diffuser (7) and the liner ring (3) that are coupled by means of the bolts (46)

  6. To dismount the impeller the following procedure must be carried out (see Fig. 4)

  • Unscrew the bolts (1) and remove the hub cap (6)

  • Unscrew the bolts (2)

  • Take off the cover (3)

  • Remove the clamping ring (4) carrying out the steps 1 and 2 as per next Fig. 5.

  • By means of the special tool (5) pull out the impeller

  • To uncouple the blades, the special tool wrench for blades disassembly shall be used as per Fig.5.

  1. To dismount the guide bearings (8) from their seat unscrew the bolts (1) (see Fig. 1) and pull out the bearing with the aid of the pulling bolts (2)

  2. Replacing the shaft sleeves is a delicate operation, necessarily performed by a skilled worker to avoid damages to the shaft and/or to the sleeve. Anyway hereunder is the list of the operations:

  • Cut the old sleeve without damaging the pump shaft.

  • Clean out of any metallic dust particles and fraze from pump shaft.

  • Heat uniformly the shaft sleeve up to 350°C.

  • Thread the shaft sleeve onto the pump shaft up to its seat and let it cool.

Note that this step must be carried out very fastly to avoid the sleeve cooling before it has completely threaded.

Inspection of Pump Components

A thoroughly inspection of the pump components shall be carried out every time the pump will be wholly or partly dismounted.

 Flexible Coupling

  1. Clean up the coupling with solvent and inspect internally for signs of galling in correspondence of the teeth.

  2. Observe the complete symmetry of the teeth .

 Thrust Bearing

  1. After the oil tank has been emptied check the cleanliness, in particularly of the oil passages A and B.

  2. Check the race of the antifriction bearing for sign of galling.

Gland Seal

  1. Take off the gland seal packing and check whether it is packed or not compressing it by hand.

  2.  Check the regularity of the shaft sleeve surface before renew it

 Guide Bearings

  1. Inspect upper and lower bearings (rubber lined) and relevant bearing sleeves

  2. Check both surfaces for signs of galling.

  3. Check the radial bearing clearances, if these values exceed the max. permissible ones (see Table 2) , new bearings should be mounted to reset an acceptable clearance between nominal and max. value; replacement of the shaft protecting sleeve shall be carried out only in presence of extensive damages.

Impeller- Liner Ring

External diameter of the impeller and internal surface of the liner ring shall be observed to notice signs of galling; possible light signs do not influence the pump performances.

Reassembly

In order to reassemble the pump, replace the various parts in reverse order to that given above.

WARNING

  1. Before replacing all parts take care that they are thoroughly clean and free from dust or other foreign matter.

  2. Bolts and nuts provided with safety system (safety washer, calking, etc.) must be replaced as per original conditions.

  3. Gaskets and 0 rings shall be put back as per original conditions taking great care of the integrity and of the functionality of the same.

Mounting of the Clamping Ring.

  1. Lightly oil clamping element. Do not use oil with molybdenum disulphide or high pressure additives and not grease

  2. .Slide on hub and insert clamping element

  3. Lightly tighten clamping screws, align hub

  4. Tighten diagonally opposite screws in stage with a torque wrench to tightening torque M = 12 Nm

  5. Tighten all screws in turn with torque wrench to torque M=12 Nm.

  6. Repeat tightening with torque M = 23 Nm

 

 Setting-up, Testing and Adjustment After Major Maintenance

The following controls, are necessary for the setting up of the pumping unit after major maintenance:

  1. Clearances

  2. Alignment

  3. Direction of rotation

  4. Gland packing

Clearances

Be sure that all the clearances indicated on table 1 be respected. In case of doubt apply for the manufacturer.. -

Alignment

Reassembling the pump taking care of the instructions and back positioning all the references, the pump unit does not need Any alignment.

The direction of rotation shall be checked (CW seen from EM)

Gland packing

  1. Check gland packing nuts not too tighten.

  2. Allow the pump to run for about 10 minutes, then tighten them gradually and observe leakage.

  3. Continue tightening at 5 to 10 minutes intervals until leakage is reduced to an acceptable level that ensure and adequate cooling of the wear parts.
