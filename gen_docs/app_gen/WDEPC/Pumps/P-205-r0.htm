Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection I1 for unit 5, which must be performed every six months as indicated by M6 in the title block.

The first draft is initiated by eng. Emad Mohamed El-Tohamy, tested by eng. Sawsan Elsaid Ali, reviewed by eng. Mohamed Mohamed Baranya, accepted by eng. Mohamed Mahmoud Kowela general manager, approved by eng Aly El-Fayoumy, and certified by eng. Mona Ahmed Al Sherqawy: WDEPC consultant engineer "A" and MPIS Coordinator.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued.

  • The qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable ID safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear safety helmet, safety shoes ... etc

  • The following mechanical consignment to be done:
         * Close the suction valve for pump "UV 101"
         * Close the discharge valves no. "UV 102, UV 103"
         * Close the heating valves no. "HV 111, HV 112"
         * Close the valves no. "HV 117, HV118, HV119"
         * Open the valve no. "HV 116, HV122, HV1123"
         * Close the valve no. "HV102"
         * Open the valve no. "HV107"
         * Open the ventilation  valve no "HV 109" until the pressure escaping.
         * Open the ventilation valve no "HV 109" to protect the motor and monitoring the body temperature
         * Close the valve no "HV 120"
         * Open the valve no "HV 121, HU 106"
         * Open the valves no "HU 114, HV 113"

  • The following electrical consignment to be done:
       * Disconnect the 6kv circuit breaker of the pump motor, rack it out and lock it.
       * Connect temporary earthing on the motor circuit breaker.
       * Disconnect the circuit breaker of the suction valve ref. UV 101 and rack it out and lock it 

Tools and Equipment

  • Sealing compressor

  • Sealing cutting box

    •

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Make a complete maintenance for the suction valve "FLAUV 01"

  2. Make a complete maintenance for the discharge valve "FLAYV 01, 02"

  3. Maintain all the motor cooling circuit valves

  4. Scavenge the cooling circuit to remove any precipitates or partial blocking in the lines

  5. General cleaning to the cooling water pipes and paint it if required

  6. Check the pump body thermal insulation
