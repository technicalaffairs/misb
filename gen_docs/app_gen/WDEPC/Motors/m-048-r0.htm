Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection I1 on compressor motor for C.B 220 kv, which must be performed on monthly, yearly bases, every six years and every 600 operating hours as indicated in the title block.

Technical Specification of Air Compressor:

The compressor proper of Westinghouse design and construction has the following nameplate rating:

Operating pressure 19 bar
Capacity at rated pressure  80 L/min
Capacity at mean value 120 L/min
Motor speed 1450 rpm
Motor power 1.5 KW
Supply voltage 380 V
Rated current 4 A
Oil sump capacity 250 cm3

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable ID safety category

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required

  • Wear safety and healthy equipment as helmet, safety shoes, breathing mask, and safety glasses

  • Breaker should be isolated, in full open position and grounding on both sides of CB is required

  • Switch off control current (dc) for CB

  • Switch on the ventilation

Tools and Equipment

  • Electrical tool kit, and torque wrench

  • An industrial vacuum cleaner, plus clean, dry cloth rags

Work to be carried out

Every month

  1. Check the oil consumed during the preceding month and if necessary add oil of the same type as used for the last filling

  2. If necessary shorten the time interval between subsequent checks and refilling so as to prevent the oil level from falling below the add mark of dipstick (55 fig. 2)

  3. Make sure that the condensate drain valve (14 fig 1) correctly operates

  4. Using a 10 mm allen key loosen and re-tighten the condensate drain plugs (13 fig 1) so as to exhaust any condensate that might have collected in the receivers (1 fig 1)

Every 200 operating hours or every year

  1. Drain the lubricating oil through plug (54 fin 2) remove the opposite plug (54 fig2) remove the opposite plug as well, clean out the crankcase bottom using, if possible a blast of compressed air re-apply plugs and refill with new heavy-duty air - compressor oil

  2. Clean out filter (17 fig 1) and the inside of condensate trap (7 fig 1)

  3. Remove the collar that fizes the air cleaner toi the suction duct and clean up filter (5 fig 1) with petrol and dry it out with compressed air

  4. With piping system at bar check safety valve (3, fig 1) for correct operation by pulling up the valve plunger

Every 600 operating hours

  1. Strip, clean up, inspect and re-assemble the intade and delivery valve assemblies (42-58, fig 2) the non-return valve (15 fig 1 ) as well as the breather and oil baffle placed on the crankcase rear over (46 fig 2)

Every 6 years

  1. Measure the time the compressor needs to bring the air system from atmospheric to the rated operating pressure and compare it with the time recorded during the commissioning tests (if possible, operate at the same ambient temperature as during those tests)

  2. If with a leak - free system, the pump - up time exceeds by more than 15 % the time that was measured with a brand new equipment it is recommended to submit the compressor to the following shop maintenance

  3. Disassemble clean up inspect and if necessary, replace the intake and delivery valve blades (42 - 58 fig 2) as well as the related gaskets

  4. Check the cylinder bore for out - of - round by measuring the diameter of the right angles and parallel to the crankshaft if the out - of - round is excessive refinish the cylinder walls bringing the cylinder bore to a diameter equal to the diameter of the new piston plus a clearance of 0.08 mm
