Introduction

This document out lines the testing procedure P1, to be performed on an annually bases as indicated Y1 in the title block.

The type STV static over excitation relay is designed for the specific application of protecting power transformer  from over excitation, which can occur either during the start-up or the shut down of a unit generator transformer.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Use safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet and safety shoes ... etc

  • Line isolation is required .

  • Make sure CT secondary circuits are shorted before isolating the relay current circuit for current injection.

Tools and Equipment

  • Test plug handle.

  • Secondary injection test set (multi amp Pulsar) figure 1

 

Works to be Carried Out

Preparation

Obtain copy of relay setting from field maintenance file and compare it with the setting on the relay.

Testing the Relay

  1. Connect test leads between the relay voltage terminal (6-7) as shown in figure 2.

  2. Make short connections between (1-10) and (3-9)

  3. Inject voltage at 120V with frequency 50 HZ and decrease the frequency slowly until the contact closes.

  4. Record the value of the frequency and voltage on the sheet.

  5.  Inject the voltage at 90V with frequency 40 HZ and increase the voltage slowly until the contact closes .

  6. Repeat the step no. 4.

  7. Inject the voltage at 90V with frequency 30 HZ and decrease the voltage slowly until the contact closes .

  8. Repeat step no. 4.

  9. Divide the voltage by the frequency every injection to get the relay setting.

Time Test

  1. Connect 125V DC at terminals (9-ve) and (10+ve).

  2. Connect terminals (4 and 5) to the start contact on the injection test set.

  3. Connect terminals (1 and 3) to the stop contact on the test set.

  4. Inject the voltage at 130V with frequency 50 HZ until the relay stops.

  5. Record the time value in the test sheet

Putting the Relay into operation

  1. Disconnect the test leads from the terminals (6-7) .         

  2. Disconnect the test leads from the trip terminals (1-3) .

  3. Disconnect the 125V DC terminals from (9-10) .

  4. Disconnect the short terminals (1-10) and (3-9).
