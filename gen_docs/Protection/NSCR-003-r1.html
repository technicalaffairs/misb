Introduction

The ITI 7521 has been designed for the protection of Steam turbine Generators against the risk of unbalanced loads.

The negative sequence component will induce twice rated frequency rotor currents to flow in the damper windings and the retaining rings causing heating in the rotor.

The unbalanced loading could be caused by external causes such as:

Open Circuit on transmission lines or Failure of the circuit breaker to clear unbalanced operation due to (L-L) and (L-G) faults.

For the previous reasons the protection function (46) shall be delayed so that the machine can withstand the unbalance or the operator could take care of this nuisance.

The ITI 7521 Relay is supplied from a 3 Current Transformers (CTs) providing secondary current to the relay.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category

  • Maintenance staff should be well trained qualified or directly supervised by some one who is

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required

  • Personal protective equipment must be used (safety shoes, helmet)

  • The generator must be isolated

  • Switch off the DC power supply before inserting or withdrawing the relay   

    •

 Tools and Equipment

  • Secondary injection test set T/D and T/I

  • Test leads

  • Multi-meter

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Check the minimum operating differential current

  2. Check the time delay

  3. Check the trip signal to generator circuit breaker

  4. Check the signal alarm

  5. Check the indication flag

Preparation

  1. Connect the secondary injection test set to AC supply

  2. Withdraw the relay from its case

  3. The relay connections must be in accordance with the connection diagram supplied with the relay as in attached figure

  4. Review the test certificate for required setting

Checking the Tripping Element

  1. Connect the current source to terminals 2 & 3 or 6 & 7 or 10 & 11

  2. Inject a current equal to 3*10*k%*In. The indicator Iamp should go out and the output unit should operate

  3. Set on the potentiometer after time interval

  4. The coefficient 3 is due to the fact that the test is single phase

  5. The coefficient 10 is the number of times settings

Checking the Operating Time

  1. Inject the current source on one of the phases simultaneously

  2. Starting the timer and the timer must be stopped by the change of state of the contacts on the output unit
