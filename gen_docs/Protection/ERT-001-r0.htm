Introduction

The GEOHM 40 D is a compact device to measure:

  • The earth resistance in electrical systems.

  • The soil resistivity which is important when dimensioning earthing systems.

  • The ohmic resistance of solid and liquid conductors.

This document outlines the equipment inspection II, which must be performed every two years as indicated by Y2 in the above title block.

There are two methods for measurements:  Three wire method, Four wire method

Three Wire Method

  1. Place the spikes for search electrode and auxiliary earth electrode at a distance of at least 20m and / or 40  from the earth electrode (see figure 2).

  2. Make sure there are no excessive transient resistance between search electrode and soil.

  3. The earth electrode is connected to terminal "E/C1" of the tester with one test lead, the search electrode to terminal "S/P2". and the auxiliary earth electrode to terminal "H/C2".

  4. Press the 3-pol pushbutton, and keep the pushbutton pressed at least until the diplayed value has stabilized (approx 10s)

  5. Read the digitally shown measured value as well as the assigned unit in Ω or kΩ.

  6. Check whether the above described display function cause signalling of faults.

  7. Eliminate the signaled fault and restart the measurement.

  8. When the pushbutton 3-pol is released, it is automatically disconnected.

  9. The resistance of the test lead to the earth electrode is directly entered into the measured result.

  10. To keep the error caused by the test lead as small as possible, for this method use a short connection lead with a large cross section between earth electrode and terminal "E/C1).

Four Wire Method

  1. Place the spikes for search electrode and auxiliary earth electrode at a distance of at least 20m and / or 40  from the earth electrode (see figure 3).

  2. Make sure there are no excessive transient resistance between search electrode and soil.

  3. The earth electrode is connected to terminal "E/C1" and / or "ES/P1" via two separate measuring leads,  the search electrode to terminal "S/P2". and the auxiliary earth electrode to terminal "H/C2".

  4. Press the 4-pol pushbutton, and keep the pushbutton pressed at least until the displayed value has stabilized (approx 10s)

  5. Read the digitally shown measured value as well as the assigned unit in Ω or kΩ.

  6. Check whether the above described display function cause signaling of faults.

  7. Eliminate the signaled fault and restart the measurement.

  8. When the pushbutton 3-pol is released, it is automatically disconnected.

  9. The resistance of the test lead to the earth electrode is directly entered into the measured result.

  10. To keep the error caused by the test lead as small as possible, for this method use a short connection lead with a large cross section between earth electrode and terminal "E/C1).

Measuring the Soil Resistivity

  1. The soil resistiviy is a factor for the magnitude of spreading resistance of an earth electrode. when planning earthing systems, the oil resistivity must be known for predetermination of the spreading resistance

  2. The earth tester GEOH 400D permits measurement of the soil resistivity according to wenner`s Method

  3. At a distance, four if possibl long, earth spikes are drived into the ground in a straight line and connected to the earth tester.

  4. Earth spikes are normally 30 to 50 cm long, longer spikes can be used for poorly conducting soil (Sandy soil) the driven - in depth of the spikes must at the most be 1/20 of distance

  5. Perform the measurement as shown in (figure 4) and then press 4-pol push - button

  6. The soil resistivity is calculated from the formula

    Pe = 2* ח * e * R

    Where : ח =  a3.1416
                 e = distance between spikes in meter
                R = determined resistance value in ohm

Measuring Ohmic Resistance

The earth tester GEOHM 400D can be used to measure the resistance of liquid and solid conductors provided they are non - conductive and non - in inductive

There are two methods for measurement :

Two Wire Method

  1. Connect the resistance Rx between the terminals ES/P1 and S/P2 as shown in (figure 5)

  2. Start the measurement by pressing 3-pol push - button

Four Wire Method

  1. Choose the four wire method to prevent the lead resistances from entering into the measured result

  2. Connect the resistance Rx as shown in (figure 6)

  3. Start the measurement by pressing 4 - pol push - button
