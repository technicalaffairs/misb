Introduction

This RADHL 4 pilot - wire relay is used for protection of 2 terminals of overhead lines where a dedicated metallic pilot - wire pair is available .The maximum length of circuit is related to the line CT ratio and should be limited so that the total charging current per phase under balanced three phase, single-end in feed conditions is less than 20 % of the CT rated primary current.

 

The relay is secure against power service, out -of- step and emergency loading conditions as it uses the balanced - bridge measuring principle, which addition offers the advantage of high - speed operation. Full three - terminal applications are feasible if a separate relay and one set of pilot - wire pairs are used for each phase and that the pilot-wire resistance doesn't exceeds 500 ohms. Current detectors are added when it's required that the RAHDL 4 defective or open pilot-wire.

 

The pilot-wire supervision option provides targets and alarm contact for open, short-circuited, or reversed pilot-wire. The CT`s at each terminal should have the same ratio. The demands for the main CT`s are small depending on that the voltage in the pilot-wire circuit is limited to a low value by built in zener diodes
 

The protection is fully stable at external faults providing that the main CT`s are approx identical and have an over current factor of more than 10.

Safety Precautions

  • Work order must be issued

  • The unit is out service.

  • Isolate the relay from CT

Tools and Equipment

  • Second. 1 - ph. injection test set (SVERKER 650 - Sweden)

  • Multimeter

  • Test - plug handle (RTXH 18)

  • Test wires

  • Variable resistance (0 - 450) ohm

 

 

Work to be Carried Out

 

 

The line is out of service (Single Feed End)

  1. Connect the circuit as shown in fig (1)

  2. Increase the current gradually until reach the operating value 

  3. Ensure that the relay operates at the setting current value (250,320 ,420)mA for phases (R, S, T) respectively

  4. The voltage across the test terminal should be about 11 v when the relay operates

  5. Ensure that the relay operates at the setting tine value (inst. time)

  6. Repeat for phases S & T

  7. Record this values on the test sheet

  8. Take off the equipments and the wires, then return the relay to its normal position
