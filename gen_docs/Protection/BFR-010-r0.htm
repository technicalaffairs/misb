Introduction

Breaker failure relay provides local backup protection in the case of a circuit breaker fails to clear the fault.

The RAICC relay is composed by:

  • Current measuring unit to determine the position of the primary circuit breaker

  • Time measuring unit to allow time for the primary circuit breaker to operate

  • Output to energize trip circuit breakers

The DC power to the (current & time) measuring unit is only sufficient during a fault for which the relay is initiated

Current detectors have a pickup time of (1) m sec

After its starting the relay performs two sequential attemps:

  • Trip order to all the breakers involved in the fault according a bus replica

  • Set the time measuring unit at the delay equal to breaker time plus a safety margin

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified stuff should be carry the suitable safety category .

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear your safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, ... etc.

  • The substation is out of service

  • Isolate the relay from CT

Tools and Equipment

  • Second 1ph injection test set (Sverker 650 - Sweden)

  • Multimeter

  • Test-plug handle (RTXH 18), Test wires

  • Variable resistance (0-450) ohm

 

Work to be Carried Out

The Line is out of Service

  1. Connect the circuit as shown in fig.

  2. Increase the current gradually until reach the operating value.

  3. Close the contact between d & c (start from equipment protection).

  4. Ensure that the relay operates at the setting current value (0.2) A.

  5. Ensure that the relay operates at the setting time value (0.150) sec.

  6. Repeat for phases S & T

  7. Record this values on the test sheet

  8. Take off the equipments and the wires, then return the relay to its normal position
