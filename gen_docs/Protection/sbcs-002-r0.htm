This document outlines the inspection checklist for Soot blower system. There is one Cabinet per unit .each cabinet divided in tow parts .for tow different jobs :

  • soot   blower  for  boiler.

  • soot blower for Air heater.

  1. We have one power supply for each cabinet .this power supply can supply the system by (110vac&120vdc&24vdc&12vdc&18.8vdc)

  2. The(24&12&18.8VDC)feed the controller cards (38cards)like ( input  , initial turn-on , alarm ,universal alarm, "AND" permissive ,"NOR"  gated lamp driver  , transfer, sequence ,gated output ,delay ,multiplier, isolated diode, 5output diode, delay, relay cards)modules

  3. The(120VDC) for driving dc header reducing station pilot solenoid valve(bv1 for boiler header, BV2for AH, BV3for AH start -up steam supply.

  4. At the boiler there are one control valve  watch adjust the pressure of the header equal35k bar and we have also 16 limit SW.

  5. AT the air hetar we have one control valve watch adjusts the pressure of the header equal35k bar , tow on/off valve at each air heater and tow limit SW.

Safety Precautions

  • A Work Order must be issued; the maintenance staff should be qualified and carry the suitable safety Category.

  • Safety Fence with caution marks surrounding the work Area is required.

  • Wear Safety helmet. Shoes

Tools and Equipment

  • Safety helmet, Shoes, and rags.

  • Screw Driver Kit.

  • Voltmeter.

Work to be Carried Out

Steps to prepare for check

  1.  Review Technical reference material and manufacture’s manual.

  2. Obtain copy of  (soot Blower Cabinets) field maintenance File..

  3. Check the power supply and measuring the out put voltage.
          * In put voltage 110VAC.
          * Output voltage (120&24&12&18.8VDC)

  4. Check the mechanical parts .

  5. Calibrated the control valve to adjust the pressure of the header.

  6. Check the limit SW .

Testing Procedure (soot blower)

  1. Open The Cabinet and Notice Each led on the system cabinet.

  2. If there is alarm we can know the problem  .

  3. If there are no alarm the system is good.

  4. Repeat all the previous steps for all cabinets and for all Units.
