Introduction

This procedure details the testing procedures P1, which recommended to be carried out on an annually bases as indicated by Y1 in the title block.

The first draft initiated by eng. Gaber Salah Taher, tested by eng. Osama Ahmed Hassanain at 30 January 2006, accepted by eng. Mohamed Osama Soliman Salim general director, approved by eng. Mohamed Abdel Baset Rekabi Head Sector of Aswan Dam 1 Power Station, and certified by eng. Kamel Ragheb Hakem: HPGC MPIS coordinator & inspection and quality control general director.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding work area is required.

  • Wear your safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes ... etc.

  • Unit must be shut down.

  • Circuit breaker open and local.

Tools and Equipment

  • Use suitable tools

  • Instrument secondary injection test (DHF)

  • Avometer, test leads

Work to be Carried Out

Preparation

  1. The CT secondary circuits should be shorted before isolating the relay current circuit for test.

  2. Look at field maintenance file and record the setting of the relay with results to be at MRDS

  3. Label the wires.

Testing the Relay

  1. Open the links on terminals X141 (11, 14) ( 31, 34 ) to isolate the CT  secondary circuit  

  2. Connect test leads between the Current terminals X141 (11, 14) terminal is the common figure 1.

  3. Open the links on terminals X02 ( 302 ,402 )

  4. Increase the Current gradually until the first stage value.

  5. Record the Current value in the test sheet.

  6. Increase the Current gradually until the second stage value

  7.  Record the Current value in the test sheet.

  8. Decrease the injection Current gradually to Zero

  9. Close the links terminals  X141 (11, 14)  

  10. Repeat the same last steps when you open the terminals X141 (31 , 34 )

  11. Close the links terminals X141 (31, 34) , X02 ( 302 ,402 )     

  12. Make sure CT secondary circuits are in normal condition.
