Introduction

This document earth fault relays are back - up relay for pilot - wire diff relay A pilot differential relay will operate for ground as will as short - circuits. The capability of detecting very resistive ground faults is limited by the measuring principle used, by adding a directional  ground - fault relay, more than 10 times as high fault resistance can be detected. The high sensitivity (down 10 % of rated current) is achieved by measuring the zero sequence current.

When faster fault clearing is required, the directional ground - fault relay can be used in a directional comparison scheme with a communication channel. In such a scheme the ground fault relay operates instantaneously but is allowed to trip only receipt of a carrier signal (permissive scheme) in this way tripping will be made only for faults on the protected line section.

The directional ground-fault relays have a characteristic angle of (+65º) RXPE 47. This means that maximum sensitivity is obtained when the operating current is lagging the polarizing voltage by 65º

Safety Precautions

  • Work order must be issued

  • The line is out of service

  • Isolate the relay from PT`s & CT`s

Tools and Equipment

  • Second. 1 - ph. injection test set (SVERKER 650 - Sweden)

  • Multimeter, Test wires, Test - plug handle (RTXH 18)

  • Variable resistance (0 - 450) ohm

Work to be Carried Out

 

 The line is out of service

  1. Connect the circuit as shown in fig (1)

  2. Increase the current gradually until reach the operating value 

  3. Ensure that the relay operates at the setting current value (300)mA

  4. Ensure that the relay operates at the setting tine value (2) sec

  5. Repeat for phases S & T

  6. Record this values on the test sheet

  7. Take off the equipments and the wires, then return the relay to its normal position
