Introduction

The relay type METI is designed specifically for directional control of the over current and earth fault relay type MCGG. Each relay is provided with adjustable characteristic angle settings, thus making the relay suitable for both phase and earth fault applications.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued

  • The maintenance staff should be qualified and carry the suitable safety ID category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear the safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet and safety shoes ... etc

  • Line isolation is required .

  • Make sure CT secondary circuits are shorted before isolating the relay current circuit for current injection

  • Test leads .

  • Small slot screw drivers .

  • Avometer

Works to be Carried Out

For the Directional Relay 3 phase voltage polarized type METI 31 + Over Current Relay MCGG 63

  1. Connect point ( 22) on MCGG 63 to point ( 23 ) on METI 31 to inject the same current  ( Ia ) on the two equipments . 

  2. Connect point ( 24) on MCGG 63 to point ( 25 ) on METI 31 to inject the same current  ( Ib ) on the two equipments .

  3. Connect point ( 26) on MCGG 63 to point ( 27 ) on METI 31 to inject the same current  ( Ic ) on the two equipments .

  4. Connect  points ( 24, 26, 28) on METI 31 with the neutral of the current transformer of GIS

  5. Connect  points ( 13, 14 ) on the ( DC/ DC ) power supply  MSTZ02 to points  ( 13, 14 ) on  METI 31 to supply 30 v

 Procedures of Vbc and Ia

  1. Connect the points (17-18)  (Vbc) on METI 31 to phase voltages Vb , Vc on the pulser tester.    

  2. Connect point ( 21 ) on MCGG 63 and point ( 24 ) on METI 31 to phase current Ia
    on the pulser tester.

  3.  Prepare the voltages (Va,Vb,Vc) and current ( Ia) on the pulser screen as  
         Va =  30v  and  angle 0º 
         Vb = 30v and angle 120º
         Vc = 30v and angle 240º 
          Ia = Iset on MCGG63 at angle 0º .        

  4. Inject the voltage Vbc and Ia and change the angle of Ia from 0º to 360º and record the limits of  the operating zone angle.

  5. Repeat steps ( 1 – 3 ) with other phases

          with Vca  & Ib  
          with Vab  & Ic

For the Directional Relay type METI11 single phase voltage polarized
 + over current relay MCGG22

  1. Connect point ( 28) on MCGG 22 to point ( 27 ) on METI 11 to inject the same current  ( In ) on the two equipments .

  2. Connect point ( 28) on METI 11 to the neutral of the current transformer of G.I.S .

  3. Supply METI 11 , MCGG 22 with 220 v on points ( 13+ , 14 - ) on the two modules . 

  4. Connect the points ( 22 , 21) on METI11 to voltage ( designed voltage )  on the pulser tester and point ( 27) on MCGG 22 and point ( 28 ) on METI11 ( which represent the neutral current ) to phase current Ia on the pulser.

  5. Prepare the phase voltages and current Ia on the pulser screen as

            Van   = 30v and  angle 0º

            Vbn  = 30v and  angle 120º

            Vcn  = 30v and  angle 240º

              Ia   = Iset on MCGG 22 at angle 0º

  1. Inject the voltage ( designed voltage ) and Ia and change the angle of  Ia from 0º to 360º and record the limits of the operating zone angle.

Output Stage

The regulated voltage E, is applied to the primary winding of a transformer by a transistor switch which is controlled by a 20 khz signal derived from the 40khz oscillator.

The transformer secondary voltage is then rectified and smoothed to produce the output voltage EL. The transformer provides electrical isolation between the input and output.

Figure 3 shows the arrangement of the input and output circuits of the MSTZ02, six pairs of terminals are provided for output connections. As two connections can be made to each terminal, up to twelve individual loads can be independently connected thus avoiding having to loop the d.c. supply from load to load. this facility affords increased security over the looping method.

The outputs are protected from induced impulse voltages by the voltage dependent resistor RVD

In very high security systems, it may be expedient to use two converters in parallel so that, if a failure of one occurs, the d.c. supply is maintained.

Parallel connection to maintain a secure supply is freely permitted because a diode D1 in each power supply will ensure that:

  • No abnormal load is imposed on the other power supply if one fails.

  • One power supply cannot feed back into the other.
