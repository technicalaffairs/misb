Introduction

The KBCH relay provides a package of functions primarily for the protection of transformers and generator transformers but may also be applied to protect reactors and generators. As a part of the K-range of relays, the KBCH can be integrated into an overall protection and control system by utilizing its integral serial communications facility. Winding KBCH140 offers 4 bias inputs per phase for all applications requiring up to 4 bias circuits.

All models are available with either 1A or 5A inputs on the primary and secondary side. Alternatively thumbs 120 and 140 can be provided with 1A CTs on the high voltage windings and 5A CTs on the low voltage windings. for  Auto transformers or, generator- transformer units the KBCH is    suitable  and also for other applications where biased differential protection is appropriate (e.g. generators, reactors and motors).All models are three phase units with internal vector group compensation and line current transformer ratio correction, thus eliminating the need for interposing transformers in most cases.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, qualified maintenance staff carry the suitable safety ID category,

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area is required,

  • Personal protective equipment must be used as safety shoes, and safety Helmet.

  • Line isolation and work permit required.

  • Make sure CT Secondary circuits are shorted.

  • Ensure all CT path elements are secure after any test disturbance.   

Tools and Equipment

  • ISA test set type ART/3 or Omicron

  • Voltmeter, Ammeter

  • Test plug test leads

 

Work to be Carried Out

Relays Preparation

  1. Review the manual and drawings.

  2. Prepare the previous or commissioning results of the relay testing.

  3. Check the wires of the relay by hand.

  4. Check the AC source is available to the tester.

  5. Check the existence of the DC sources in panels.

  6. Disconnect the trip leads of the relay and connect one of them to the timer of the tester.

  7. Check all the leads used in tests.

 

Test steps

  1. Connect the current leads of a tester unit to the relay points according to the drawings and scheme.

  2. Inject current to each couple of points till the red flag of the device displays.

  3. Repeat the steps for each pair of points according to the test sheet.

  4. Record the results

 

Testing the Relay and Protection Outputs

  1. Carefully follow these test procedure steps 

  2. Verify the test result match the relay setting. If not consult technical services.

  3. Test trip the circuit breaker associated with the relay.

 

Trip Test

  1. Request the operator to switch on the main circuit breaker.

  2. Operate the output auxiliary contact by injection to trip the circuit breaker.

  3. Check that the circuit breaker tripped.
