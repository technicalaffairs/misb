Introduction

The MBCH is a high speed biased differential relay suitable for protecting two or three winding transformers. It is extremely stable during through external faults and will not operate during inrush currents on energizing. It is manufactured by GEC Alstom.

This procedure details the steps required to completely check the proper operation and calibration of the MBCH relay. It is recommended this procedure be carried out on a yearly bases.

The first preliminary steps to prepare for relay testing describes the activities required prior to testing. Some of these steps could be performed days before the actual test. The second section Preparation for testing describes the steps to set up the equipment for performing the actual tests. The third section is the actual test.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, qualified maintenance staff carry the suitable safety ID category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required

  • Safety and healthy equipment must be used as safety shoes, and safety Helmet ... etc.

    •

  • Power transformer must be isolated

  • Make sure CT Secondary circuits are shorted.

  • Ensure all CT path elements are secure after any test disturbance.

  • Switch off DC power supply before inserting or withdrawing any card from the relay module.

Work to be Carried Out

Relays Preparation

  1. Obtain outage for transformer protected by this relay.

  2. Check that the two bus disconnect switches are opened and tagged.

  3. Inspect the CT secondary circuits and all cabling supplying the relaying including matching CT.

  4. Use the station drawings to isolate the following:

  • Breaker trip coils.

  • Breaker failure scheme associated with the relay.

  • Disturbance alarms and fault recorder associated with the relay.

Testing the Relay and Protection Outputs

  1. Carefully follow these test procedure steps.

  2. Verify the test results match the relay setting, if not consult technical services.

Preparation

  1. This procedure is used to perform off load relay testing.

  2. Look at the EEC panel nameplate and check that this EE system designation agrees with the power transformer that has been taken out of service for maintenance.

  3. Check there is no AC current from CT’s by using a clip on ammeter.

  4. Check there is no AC voltage from VT’s by using a voltmeter.

Testing Procedure (s)

Pick up Current Test

Phase A

  1. Connect the circuit as in Fig 1 and adjust the tester ( MANTA ) to I1 low , Φ-N and A-N         

  2. For the first winding, the current is injected at the terminals 25,27.       

  3. Press current  ( I ).

  4. Press dynamic then press fault.

  5. Raise  the current slowly from zero until the relay picks up.

  6. Record this current ,this is the pick up current and check that current is in the accepted range i.e ±10% setting.

  7. Repeat the steps 1 to 6 for the second winding and the third winding
    Note for the second winding the current is injected at the terminals 23,27-for the third winding the current is injected at the terminals 21,27 

  8. Repeat this test for phases B and C.

Operating Time Test

Phase A

  1. Connect the circuit as in Fig 1 and adjust the tester ( MANTA ) to I1 low , Φ-N and A-N

  2. Short circuit the output of  I1

  3. For the first winding, the current is injected at the terminals 25,27.     

  4. Press current  ( I ).

  5. Press dynamic then press fault and adjust the current to more than the pick up current.

  6. Press static then remove the short circuit of I1 output.

  7. Connect the output of I1 to terminal 25-27

  8. Press time ( toggle the time button to have time in seconds ).

  9. Press dynamic then press fault

  10. The relay picks up and the tester is stopped after the apparent time on the LCD.

  11. Record this time 

  12. Repeat the steps 1 to 11 for the second winding and the third winding

Note for the second winding the current is injected at the terminals 23,27 for the third winding the current is injected at the terminals 21,27

  1. Repeat this test for phases B and C.

Restraint Characteristic Test

First Winding with Second Winding

  1. Connect the circuit as in Fig 2 and adjust the tester to I1 & I2.

  2. Short circuit the output of  I1

  3. Short circuit the output of  I2

  4. Press I

  5. Press dynamic then press fault

  6. Adjust I1 to 0.6In

  7. Press I and adjust I2 to 0.6In .

  8. Press Φ and adjust the angle to 0.

  9. Press static.

  10. Remove the short circuit of I1 & I2 output and connect the leads as follow: - for I1  red at 25, black at 27  &   for I2  red at 27, black at 23.

  11. Press dynamic then press fault

  12. Decrease I2 slowly until the relay picks up and the tester is stopped.

  13. Record I1 & I2  then calculate

  14. Mean bias = (I1 + I2 ) \ 2

  15. Differential current = I1 - I2

  16. Using the curves ( curve  Is = setting ) and at the calculated mean bias determine the theoretical differential current and compare it with the calculated differential current

  17. Press reset of the tester.

  18. Repeat the steps from 1 to 11.

  19. Increase I2 slowly until the relay picks up and the tester is stopped.

  20. Calculate the mean bias and get theoretical differential current and compare it with the calculated differential current

  21. Repeat this test for first winding with third winding for I1  red at 25, black at 27  &   for I2  red at 27, black at 21.  

  22. Repeat the test steps from 1 to 21 for phase B and phase C.     

NOTE:  For relay 5A, the values of the calculated mean bias have to be divided by 5 before applying the bias curve and the theoretical differential current multiplied by 5 before comparing with the calculated differential current.             

For MBCH 16 only, repeat the above test with the fourth to sixth bias coils (terminal 19,17 and 15 respectively)

 

Second Harmonic Restraint Test

As the pick up test , but the injected current is 0.6 A with second harmonic with high level and then decrease the level of second harmonic until the relay picks up then record this ratio of  the second harmonic .

  1. Connect the circuit as in Fig 3 and adjust the tester to I4 .

  2. Short circuit the output of  I2.

  3. Press I.

  4. Press dynamic then press fault.

  5. Adjust I2 to 0.6A .

  6.  Press I and adjust the second harmonic ratio to 30% .

  7. Press static.

  8. Remove the short circuit of I2 output 

  9. For the first winding the current is injected at terminal 25 - 27.

  10. Press dynamic then press fault.

  11. Decrease the level of second harmonic until the relay picks up and record the ratio of second harmonic.

  12. Repeat the steps 1 to 11 for the second winding and the third winding
    Note:  -for the second winding the current is injected at the terminals 23,27-for the third winding the current is injected at the terminals 21,27

  13. Repeat this test for phases B and C.

Return Relay and Associated Equipment to Service

  1. Verify the test results match the relay setting, if not consult technical services.

  2. Trip the circuit breaker associated with the relay.

  3. Return relay to service by removing the test leads from the relay terminals block and the test set. Make sure CT and VT secondary circuits are in normal condition.

  4. Any equipment associated with the relay which has been isolated, must be returned to service by the following restoration step

  • Breaker trip coils.

  •  Breaker failure scheme associated this relay.

  •  Disturbance alarm and fault recorder associated with this relay.
