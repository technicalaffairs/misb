Introduction

This document lists the testing procedure P1, which must be performed on an annually bases as indicated by Y1 in the above title block

The  MBCZ16 Bus bar protection is a Modular, solid state, low impedance blast differential based on the circulating current principle, dual cratered of protection, provides continuous supervision of CT secondary circuits and constant monitoring of vital circuits extremely stable up to the max switch gear.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category

  • Maintenance staff should be well trained qualified or directly supervised by some one who is

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required

  • Personal protective equipment must be used as safety shoes, helmet ... etc

  • Outage arrangement is required.

  • Switches off  DC supply before inserting or with drawing any card from the relay .

  • Before with drawing MBCZ 16 modules from its case fit CT shorting to test bluge before inserted into test block.

  • The generator must be isolated

 Tools and Equipment

  • Sverker 650 test set.

  •  Test leads

  • Test plugs MMLB01

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Testing the differential relay ( Zone and Zone check ) of Bus #.

  2. Testing the associated Breaker fail modules .

Preparation

  1. Obtain outage for the Bus Bar # protected by this relay.

  2. Check that the bus bar circuit breakers and the disconnect switches are opened and tagged.

  3. Turn the Key switch on the alarm unit to the PROTECTION OUT position.

  4. Inspect the CT secondary circuits and all cabling supplying the relaying including matching CT.

  5. Use the station drawings to isolate the following

  • Trip output of each Bay.

  •  Back trip output and the remote end inter trip of each Bay.

  • Disturbance alarm and fault recorder of each Bay.

  1. Use the station drawings to get the following:

  • The breaker fail initiation of each Bay.

  • Trip contact to stop the injection while testing differential for each Bay.

  • Back trip contact to stop the injection while testing Breaker fail for each Bay.

  • The input points of current injection of each Bay on its test plug per phase for Example

            Phase A at 2 , 22 on test plug ( *D ) .

            Phase B at 4 , 24 on test plug ( *D ) .

                        Phase C at 6 , 26 on test plug ( *D ) .

          Note :   Relates to the tested Bay

  1. Turn the Key switch on the alarm unit to the PROTECTION IN position.

Testing Procedure(s)

Test the Breaker Fail Module

Phase A

  1. Insert the test plug *D

  2. Connect the circuit as shown in figure 1.

  3. Increase the current slowly from zero until the breaker fail module operates and the injection is stopped .

  4. This is the pick up current, compare it with the setting.

  5. Make sure that this module gives Trip and Back Trip and all other modules connected to this bus bar give Trip only.

  6. Make sure that, the trip and Back trip lock out relays of the tested Bay pick up and close their contacts.

  7. Make sure that, the trip lock out relays of other Bays connected to this bus bar pick up and close their contacts.

  8. Apply current ( just more than the pick up current ) suddenly and measure the trip time.

  9. Record the results in the test sheet.  

  10. Repeat this test for phases B and C

              Phase B at 4 , 24 on test plug ( *D )
              Phase C at 6 , 26 on test plug ( *D ) .
      Note:   Relates to the tested Bay

 

Test The Differential Relay

injection via test plug *D

Phase A

  1. Connect the circuit as shown in figure 2.

  2. Increase the current slowly from zero until the main alarm appears this is the alarm current. continue increasing the current until the zone and zone check trip and the injection is stopped.

  3. Make sure, that phase A led lights in each of zone and zone check modules.

  4. Make sure, that all breaker fail modules connected to this bus bar pick up and give trip only.

  5. Apply current ( just more than the pick up current ) suddenly and measure the trip time.

  6. Record the results in the test sheet.  

  7. Repeat this test for phases B and C.

           Note :   Phase B at 4 , 24 on test plug ( *D ) .

                        Phase C at 6 , 26 on test plug ( *D ) .
