Introduction

This procedure details the testing procedures P1, which must be carried out on an annually bases as indicated by Y1 in the title block.

The first draft initiated and tested by eng. Usama Ahmed Hassanin at 05 March 2005, accepted by eng. Mohamed Osama Soliman Salim general director, approved by eng. Mohamed Abdel Baset Rekabi Head Sector of Aswan Dam 1 Power Station, and certified by eng. Kamel Ragheb Hakem: HPGC MPIS coordinator & inspection and quality control general director.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding work area is required.

  • Wear your safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes ... etc.

  • Check from the station drawing that the test plug will isolate breaker trip coils, breaker failure scheme, and any alarm associated with the relay

  • If the test plug dose not isolate the previous steps you have to isolate it

Tools and Equipment

  • Test leads

  • Clamp ammeter

  • secondary injection test set dhf test set

    •

Work to be Carried Out

Preparation

  1. Make sure CT secondary circuit are shorted at the incoming terminal blocks before isolating the relay current circuit for test

  2. Look at field maintenance file and compare the setting on relay with those shown on the test sheet

Testing the Relay

  1. Check the pickup current 10% of In when In = 1A

  2. Open the links on terminals X151(11,21,31,13,23,33,44) to isolate the CT secondary circuit

  3. Connect test leads between the current terminals X151(11-41) terminal 41 is the common figure 1

  4. Increase the current until the relay trips instantaneously

  5. Record the current value in the test sheet

  6. Connect test leads between the current terminals X151(21-41) increase the current until the relay trips instantaneously

  7. Record the current value in the test sheet

  8. Connect test leads between the current terminals X151(31-41) increase the current until the relay trips instantaneously

  9. Record the current value in the test sheet

  10. Repeat the above steps from 1 to 9 to terminals X151(13-41), X151(23-41)
    and X151(13-41)

Return Relay and Associated Equipment to Service

  1. Return relay to service by removing the test leads from the relay terminal block and the test set i.e. Disconnect the test leads from the terminals X151(11,21,31,13,23,33,41)

  2. Connect the links 1 on terminals X151(11,21,31,13,23,33,41)

  3. Make sure CT and PT secondary circuits are in normal condition

  4. If any equipment associated with the relay has been isolated, it should be returned to service by the following restoration steps:

  •  Breaker trip coils.

  • Disturbance alarm and fault recorder associated with this relay.
