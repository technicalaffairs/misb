Introduction

The LFAA auto Re-closer relay provides facilities for single or two-shot enclosures of one or two circuit breakers following their tripping for a fault on an overhead line. The enclosures can be selected to be either high speed single-phase or high speed or delayed three-phase. When three-phase enclosure is desired the equipment will convert any single or two-phase tripping into three-phase tripping before initiating the enclosure For schemes with two circuit breakers the enclosure of the second circuit breaker can be either simultaneous with the first circuit breaker or it can be sequential. In the latter case the enclosure of the second circuit breaker must naturally be a delayed three phase enclosure. Different operating cycles can be selected using the keypad on the relay front-plate.

These allow the relay to perform various combinations of the following cycles:

One breaker schemes (LFAA 101) Single shot scheme:- single phase auto-recluse- single phase or three phase - auto recluse, depending on type of fault- three phase auto-recluse for all types of fault two shot scheme: any of the above cycles for the first shot, followed by delayed phase auto-recluse if protection re-operates during the reclaim time after the first recluse Two breaker schemes (LFAA 102) Combinations of single and three phase auto-recluse as above circuit breakers can be closed simultaneously or sequentially on the first shot a block diagram as shown in Figure

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued,

  • The maintenance staff must be qualified and carry the suitable safety ID category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required,

  • Personal protective equipment must be used as safety shoes, and safety Helmet.

  • Line isolation is required

  • Ensure CT Secondary circuits are shorted.

  • Ensure all CT path elements are secure after any test disturbance.

Work to be carried out

 

Relays Preparation

  1. Review the manual and drawings.

  2. Prepare the previous or commissioning results of the relay testing.

  3. Check the wires of the relay by hand.

  4. Check the AC source is available to the tester.

  5. Check the existence of the DC sources in panels.

  6. Disconnect the trip leads of the relay and connect one of them to the timer of the tester.

  7. Check all the leads used in tests.
