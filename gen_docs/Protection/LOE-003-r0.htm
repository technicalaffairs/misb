Introduction

This document out lines the testing procedure I 1, which must performed an annually bases as indicated Y1 in the above title block.

The CEH51A relay is a single phase off set mho relay and its used to detect a loss of excitation on synchronous machines. It is recommended that loss of excitation protection be considered for all synchronous generators.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, qualified maintenance staff carry the suitable safety ID category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required,

  • Personal protective equipment must be used as safety shoes, and safety Helmet.

  • The generator must be out of service

  • Make sure CT Secondary circuits are shorted.

  • Ensure all CT path elements are secure after any test disturbance.

Tools and Equipment

  •  Secondary injection test set with multi amp pulsar 

  •  Test leads

Work to be carried out

  1. Make sure CT secondary circuit are shorted at the incoming terminal blocks before isolating the relay current circuit for test.

  2. Obtain copy of relay setting from  maintenance file.

Testing the Relay

  1. Connect test leads between the current terminals ( 3 and 6) on the relay as shown in fig 1.

  2. Connect test leads between the voltage terminal ( 7 and 8 ) on the relay.

  3. Make short connections between terminals ( 4 - 5 ) & ( 9 -10 ).

  4. Increase the voltage to value 120v.

  5. Adjust the current angle at 90.

  6. Increase the current slowly until the contact closes.

  7. Record the current in the test sheet ,this current will be the minimum current that operate the relay.

  8. Adjust the voltage at 120v, the current at 2A,and increase the current angle until the contact closes and continue increasing the angle until the contact opens.

  9. Record the two angles’ values in the test sheet.

  10. Repeat step 9 for other current values and record the angles’ values.

Time Test

  1. Make short connection between terminals (2-10).

  2. Connect DC volt (125) between terminals 10 (-ve)  and 1 (+ve).

  3. Connect test leads between the relay contact (1 and 2 ) .

  4. Inject the voltage at 120V , the current at 2A the current angle at any value in the angle zone, until the relay trips.

  5. Record the time value at which the relay trips in the test sheet.

Return Relay and associated Equipment to Service

  1. Return relay to service by removing the test leads from the relay terminal block and the test set as following:-

  2. Disconnect the test leads from terminals (3-6) .

  3. Disconnect the test leads from terminals (7-8) .  

  4. Disconnect the test leads from trip terminals (1-2) .

  5. Disconnect the DC terminals (1-10) .  

  6. Disconnect short terminals (9-10) & (4-5) & (2-10) .

  7. Make sure CT and PT secondary circuits are in normal condition.

  8. If any equipment associated with the relay has been isolated, it should be returned to service by the following restoration steps.

  • Breaker, trip coils.

  • Breaker failure scheme associated this relay.

  • Disturbance alarm and fault recorder associated with this relay.
