Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Safety and Healthy equipment must be used (safety Helmet, safety shoes, ...etc).

  • Make sure the circuit breaker is closed, and the protected circuit is not energized.

Tools and Equipment

  • ME TEST KIT with its accessories:

  • Two plugs to test connection ( + / - )

  • Two plugs to SDE auxiliary connect.

Work to be Carried out

 

  1. Review technical reference material and manufacture's manual.

  1. Make sure that the breaker (400v) is in position (OFF).

  1. Change the switch (dc – source for the charger motor of breaker) to position OFF to charge the breaker spring manually when the breaker discharged.

  1. Remove the  breaker from it’s location to position OUT.

  1. Remove the cover of breaker and connect the two plugs for the test connection on breaker to the current injection outlets on the tester ( points  no.15- fig. 1 )

  1. Connect the two plugs for tripping contacts on the tester (points17-fig.1) to breaker auxiliary points ( 42 / 41).

  1. Set the duration of the injection on [ 6-thump wheels (point no.11- fig.1) ] on the tester. Where this duration is higher than the tripping time of the breaker (400v).

  1. Set the injected current (mA) on [ 4-thump wheel (point no.10 -fig1) ]. We begin with 100 mA.

  1. Press selector  push-button (point no.14-fig1) to select earth fault protection [ i.e LED ( point 8  is ON ].

  1. Press the start button on tester then LED ready be ON and record the value displayed on the screen (point 9 – fig1) which the time of the relay at the set injected current. Then record the injected current and its  time recorded on test sheet.

  1. Press reset push-button.

  1. Repeat from point 7 to point 11 with another value of  injected current and record the values.

  2. With many values of injected current and time recorded then draw the curve between current and time.

 Note:  Current injection stops on any of the following events:

  1. Press the stop / reset button.

  2. Change to state of the contact connected to the SDE terminals of the test kit .

  3. End of set duration.

  4. Tripping of the kits thermal protection system ( ? " LED on ).

  5. Injection circuit open, for example a faulty test plug connection ( ? " LED on ).

  6. Another test may be carried out when the READY LED comes on (end of cooling period).

  7. Reclose the circuit breaker after each test.

How to use Test Kit

 

Connection to AC Power Supply

  1. Choose the right voltage

  2. Plug on the input power cable 

  3. Switch on the ON/OFF switch

Power UP  

  1. Display and LEDs on for approximately 1 sec.

  2. Display and LEDs off for approximately 1 sec.

  3. LED remains on 0.00 on the display "ready"
    and "    " LEDs on 

 

          

Connection to Device under Test

  

Connect trip units to the kit, taking care to respect the polarities.

 

For Detailed Instructions on Connecting Circuit Breaker:

  1. Masterpact    => part B

  2. Compact       => part C

  3. Compact NS  => part D

     

 

 

To Measure the Tripping Time

Connect the following device to

  1. "SDE " terminal.

  2. "OF " auxiliary contact (N/O or N/C) or

  3. "SDE" auxiliary contact (N/O or N/C) or

  4.  The main contact (circuit breaker disconnected from the protected power circuit)
