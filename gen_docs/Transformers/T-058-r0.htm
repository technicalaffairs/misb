Introduction

This document describes inspection and maintenance methods which must be performed on a daily/monthly bases, D1/M1 as indicated in the above title block. Oil & winding and ambient temperature, load current and oil level in transformer should be daily checked and recorded.

The first draft is initiated by eng. Sherif Mahrous Omar tested by eng. Nafad Kamel Mahmoud, revised by UEEPC Quality control general director eng. Gamal Abd Elwahab, accepted by technical expert eng. Hassan Elbehiry and approved by eng. Mohamed Gamal Diab: Head sector of El-Kureimat PS.

Safety Precautions 

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area.

  • Wear the appropriate safety equipment as safety shoes, helmet,

Environmental Recommendation

  • Don't burn the cloth used in cleaning, collect it and send it to the dump.

  • Fix any oil leaks to prevent pollution and save money of wasted oil.

Oil leaks to the ground have very bad impact on the environment, mainly on soil and ground water and eventually on the ecosystem. "It takes only 1 liter of Hydrocarbon spilled in an aquifer to render 1,000,000 liters of potable water useless for human consumption

Tools and Equipment

  • Fine cloth for cleaning purposes.

  • Complete tool kit/set, including a torque wrench.

Work to be carried out

At least item 1 should be checked Daily and Recorded

  1. Check oil temperature and winding temperature by   reading  indications of thermometers and winding thermal relays. Compare them with the data previously obtained .    

  2. If oil or winding temperature are much higher or lower-considering load current and ambient temperature, thermometers should be inspected. The finned area of radiators and the inner surface of cooling water should be cleaned.

  3.  Load current and ambient temperature should be recorded at the same time.

  4. Check Oil level in transformer and in bushing by  reading the indication of the dial oil level gauge on the conservator.

  5.  Compare it with oil level-oil temperature curve for the transformer.

  6. When the indicated oil level is constant with the changing oil temperature, inspect the dial gauge and check the actual oil level.    

  7.  Check the oil level in bushing with the oil level gauge. 

  8.  Check oil leaks visually from radiators, flanges, pipes and transformer tank.

  9.  If oil leaks due to gasket, tighten bolts or exchange gasket.

  10.  If oil from welded parts apply adhesive material or weld again on the leaking parts.

  11.  Listen carefully to abnormal or unusual noise especially from oil pump motors and fan motors, if there is any noises exchange the bearing with new ones.

  12.  Check any abnormal vibration by your hand, if there is any vibration due to loose bolts, tighten them.

  13. Confirm tightness of bolts with a test hammer.

  14.  Check visually that all connections are normal without any discoloration due to local heating .

  15.   Inspect visually for dust especially on radiators because it may reduce cooling capacity of radiators

  16.   Inspect visually for rust, especially on radiators and repainting if necessary.

  17.  Inspection visually for dew in the terminal box, control cabinet and protective relays, if any dew found, dry it down and ensure space heaters in use.

 

Daily inspection items of tab changer

Tab changer main body

  1. Indication of oil gauge, check to see if the oil level far beyond the upper or lower limit and add oil in case of the indication of the lower limit.   

  2. Color of the silica gel in the breather, in case of dark blue, it is good,  check to see if it has turned into pink, replace or dry the desiccative when it has turned pink. 

  3. Color and level of the oil in the breather pot, check to see if the oil level is up to the red line, replace the oil when it is below the red line or it is contaminated.

  4. Head case and piping, check that no oil leakage.

Driving mechanism

Operating counter

  1. Record the number of operations.

  2. Check to see that the number of operations is excessively larger or smaller, in case of automatic operation, more than 50 operations per day is too large, in case of an excessive number of operations, readjusting the sensitivity of the voltage regulating relay is necessary.
