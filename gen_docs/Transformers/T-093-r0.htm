Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Maintenance staff should be well trained and qualified or directly supervised by some one who is.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, safety belt when cleaning suspension insulators, … etc.

  • Transformer must be out of service, isolated and grounded from both sides.

  • Taking out the ...kv breaker of bus bar ...kv.

  • Disconnect supply of DC.

Environmental Recommendation

  1. Before starting the cleaning of all porcelain surfaces of the transformers, wear your breathing mask to protect lung tissues from the dust particulate.

  2. Don't burn the cloth used in cleaning, collect it and send it to the dump.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete tool kit including a torque wrench tool.

  • Safety helmet, safety belt

  • Voltage indicator HV tester rod.

  • Thermometer, Oil Level Indicator

  • Fine cloth for cleaning purposes, new silica-gel.

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Read and record oil and winding temperature w.r.t. the load.

  2. Check and record oil level gauges.

  3. Check by ears for abnormal transformer noise.

  4. Check by ears and eyes for fans vibration or abnormal sound.

  5. Check visually for oil leak.

  6. Check the color of the silica-gel.

  7. Check the level of the sealing oil in the oil cup of the breather.

  8. Check for dust accumulation on radiator surface.

  9. Check for cleanless of the HV bushing.

  10. Check and confirm the operation of the cooling fans and pumps w.r.t. winding temperature.
