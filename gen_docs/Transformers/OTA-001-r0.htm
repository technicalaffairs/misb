 Drying the Transformers by using the Oil Treatment Apparatus

Introduction

This document details the steps required to eliminate moisture and other contaminants such as sludge and dissolved gases from the dielectric oil of the transformers using the oil treatment apparatus.

The first draft initiated by eng. Abd-Allah Elsayed M, revised by EDEPCo. quality control eng. Tawfeek Elabasery, accepted by eng El Sayed Attiah, approved by eng. Awad Soliman Head Sector of Talkha PS, and certified by eng. Aly Mostafa Al Lethy; EDEPCo. inspection & quality control head sector

Safety Precaution 

  • A work order must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear your safety helmet, safety shoes, and safety belt when cleaning suspension insulator.

  • Transformer isolation and proper grounding on all its sides is required.

  • It is advisable to use only fiber glass (PVC) ladders  ladder.

  • There is a danger falling into the containment pit when working near large transformer.

Tools and Equipment

  • Safety helmet, safety belt, safety shoes, and tool set

  • Dielectric oil treatment apparatus with its delivered hoses.

  • Screw driver set and fiber glass PVC ladder.

  • Fine cloth for cleaning purposes, silica gel.

  • Phase sequence meter.

Work to be carried out

  1. Check the greasing oil level of the vacuum pumps.

  2. Check the flexible connections on the transformer to be treated according to figure (3)

  3. Check the Main supply and direction of rotation of motors.

  4. Adjust safety thermostat  RG (see figure 1) at about 100 ºC and the temp. regulator R (see figure 2) at desired treatment temperature( 60ºC to 70ºC approximately). For more detail see trouble shooting item III

  5. Close valves 3,4,6,10,12,13,15 and 16 as well as the samples cock 11,18 and open valves 1, 17.

  6. Start PH1 by starting cycle button  (S1).

  7. Adjust valve 2 according to required output , then check it up when the oil level reaches about a half of the glass tube of (FR):
        - Open valve 3 and start (PH2) button (S3).   For more detail see trouble shooting item I
        - Be sure that the heating device ( RG ) is full of oil and be sure that valves 1 , 2 , 9 and 17 are opened , then switch on  the heating  by button ( S6 ) , ( S 8 )

  8. Open valve 8 and start vacuum pump (PV 1) by button S 5 (PV2 starts automatically )

  9. Check the setting under vacuum with the help of manometers (IV 1)  and (IV 2) and MP

  10. ( The vacuum in DG should be inferior to 12 m.bar for good functioning of the apparatus).

  11. Carryout several passages (3 to 5) of the transformer oil through the apparatus.

  12. After completing the treatment of the oil , stop the apparatus as the following :
    - Stop the heating and wait a while allowing the heating elements to cool down.
    - Stop the circulation pump (PH1) and (PH2).
    - Stop the vacuum pump (PV1). (PV2 stops automatically). For more detail see trouble shooting item II
    - Close valves 1 and 3.
    - Open valve 2.

  13. Remove all flexible connections between apparatus and the transformer.

  14. Take oil sample for dielectric strength test (the dielectric strength should be at least 40 kv)

  15. Clean the ( transformer, transformer site ) and remove all tools .

  16. Cancel and reset all alarms on the main  control board .

  17. Put the transformer back into normal operating condition.

Troubleshooting

(I) Level Defect : Pump PH2 stops automatically and a signal lamp indicates the defect.

To restart the apparatus switch on PH2 directly and switch on again the button S1. PH1 will only start when the level in the cell will be back to normal (level signal switched off). 

 

(II) Output Defect: The apparatus stops automatically

  1. Start the cycle again, adjust and check that the output  is correct.

  2. To adjust the output  act on value 2 watching the indicator ID proceed slowly and wait that a constant running is established.

  3. During the normal functioning of the apparatus oil level in DG should always be visible , that means between the inferior and the superior flanges of the float regulator FR.

  4. In case of disconnection of PH2 , the oil level increase in DG and to avoid this open valve 7 slightly for a while in order to get rid of air bubbles that may exist in PH2.

  5. The efficiency of DG is optimal at normal output of the apparatus.

  6. Take care that seals are perfectly tight and that air does not enter through the mechanical tightening of PH2.

(III) Overheating: 

In case of failure in the regulation, the safety thermostat RG1 isolates completely the apparatus when the indicated temperature is reached (indicated by signal lamp)
