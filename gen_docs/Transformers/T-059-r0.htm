Introduction

This document outlines the maintenance procedure P1, to be performed every six months as indicated by M6 in the title block.

Most of the periodical inspections should be made in detail when the transformer is de-energized.

The first draft is initiated by eng. Sherif Mahrous Omar, tested by eng. Nafad Kamel Mahmoud, revised by UEEPC Quality control general director eng. Gamal Abd Elwahab, accepted by technical expert eng. Hassan Elbehiry and approved by eng. Mohamed Gamal Diab: Head sector of El-Kuriemat power station.

Safety Precaution 

  • A work permit must be issued.

  • The qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear the appropriate safety equipment as safety shoes, helmet.

  • Be careful not to approach live parts.

Environmental Recommendation

  • Don't burn the cloth used in cleaning, collect it and send it to the dump.

  • Fix any oil leaks to prevent pollution and save money of wasted oil.

  • Oil leaks to the ground have very bad impact on the environment, mainly on soil and ground water and eventually on the ecosystem. "It takes only 1 liter of hydrocarbon spilled in an aquifer to render 1,000,000 liters of potable water useless for human consumption

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete tool kit, including a torque wrench.

  • Fine cloth for cleaning purposes.

 

Work to be carried out

The Recommended inspection frequency is described under each inspection item

Breather

  1. Check the discoloration of silicagel in breather due to moisture in breathed air.

  2. If the color has turned to pink by more than 2/3 of total quantity (dry out or exchange it).

 

Insulating Oil

  1. Measure dielectric strength with an oil tester and confirm it is more than 40 KV/2.5 mm gap.

  2. Measure moisture content in oil with Karl-Fischer method and confirm that it is less than 30 PPM.

  3. Measure acid value and confirm it is less than 0.2 mg KOH/g

  4. If the value from 0.2 to 0.4, filter or exchange with new oil.

  5. If the value more than 0.4, filter or exchange with new oil (immediately).

  6. If any measured values are not satisfactory filtering and degassing are recommended.

 

Analysis of gas dissolved in Oil and Resistivity measurement

  1. A periodic analysis of gas dissolved in oil is recommended every one or two years.

  2. The dissolved gas should be extracted from the oil and analyzed.

  3. When the quantities of combustible gases are obtained we can evaluate the trouble which may be present

  4. If there is indication of moisture, sludge, low dielectric strength and low resistivity, an inspection should be made on the inside of the tank for possible cause of trouble.

  5. Measure resistivity of the oil when a periodic test shows that the oil is in bad condition.

Protective Relays

1- Dial thermometer                   2- Winding thermal relay            3- Dial oil gauge                         

4- Sudden pressure relay            5- Buchloz relay                        6- Gas detector

7- Bursting plate breaking device

For the above protective relays: Operation tests should be made only when protective relays operated even if no troubles of transformer occurred.

Connections (occasionally)

  1. Inspect all connections outside the transformer to see whether they are in good conditions without any discoloration, which indicated hot connection.
