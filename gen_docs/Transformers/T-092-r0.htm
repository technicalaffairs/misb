Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the maintenance staff should carry the suitable ID safety category.

  • Maintenance staff should be well trained and qualified or directly supervised by some one who is.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, safety belt when cleaning suspension insulators … etc.

  • Transformer must be out of service, isolated and grounded from both sides.

  • Isolate the LV transformer and taking out the 6kv breaker out of bus bar 6kv.

  • Disconnect supply of DC.

  • Before starting the cleaning of all porcelain surfaces of the transformers, wear your breathing mask to protect lung tissues from the dust particulate.

  • Don't burn the cloth used in cleaning, collect it and send it to the dump.

Tools and Equipment

  • Safety helmet, safety belt, and tool set

  • Complete tool kit/set, including a torque wrench.

  • Fine cloth for cleaning purposes, Silica gel

  • Voltage indicator, ( HV tester rod ), Megger.

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Clean all porcelain surfaces.

  2. Inspect all painted surface, remove any signs of rust and repaint bare spots.

  3. Check for signs of oil leaks.

  4. Check oil level in tank and conservator .

  5. Check the condition of the silica gel, replace silica gel if it’s color changed from blue to pink.

  6. Check the condition of earthing.

  7. All bolted connections must be checked for tightness.

  8. Clean the body, cables and the room of service transformer.

  9. Check the break point for oil

  10. Check the coils of the transformers .

  11. Perform a megger test for coils .
