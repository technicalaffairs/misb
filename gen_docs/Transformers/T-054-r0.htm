Introduction

This document outlines the minor maintenance procedures P1, which must be performed from one to two years as indicated by Y1:2 in the title block.

Safety Precautions 

  • A work permit must be issued,

  • The maintenance staff must carry the suitable ID safety category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required,

  • Wear the appropriate safety equipment as safety shoes, helmet,

  • Transformer must be out of service and isolated and grounded from all sides.

  • The high voltage CB and low voltage CB of the transformer must be isolated and proper earthing at the two sides is required.

  • Use only fiberglass ladders.

  • Personnel carrying out the inspection should be trained and qualified or directly supervised by someone who is.

  • If it becomes necessary to enter the main tank, be sure that the oxygen level is sufficient to provide safe working conditions

 

Tools and Equipment

  • Dielectric strength oil tester.

  • Oil sampling bottles.

  • Reference thermometer and bath of water.

  • Spare parts for cooling equipment (oil pump, cooling fans…etc)

 

Work to be carried out

Insulating Oil

Sample and analyze gas taken from the insulating oil for main tank and tap-changer.

Temperature indicator

  1. Read and record oil and winding temperature.

  2. Record ambient temperature, load (MW, MVAR) and voltage.

Notes:

  • Transformer temperature directly affects the lifetime of the insulating material.

  • Maximum temperature rise limits are specified for the both oil and winding temperature.

  • In the daily inspection, check not only that temperatures are held within the maximum limit, but also that    the temperature lies in a satisfactory range in relation to their values in the test report, load condition and ambient temperature.

  • Over loading of transformer should be made only in accordance with the applicable standard.

Indicator level

  1. Check that the gauge indicator shows the normal oil level, prescribed in the oil temperature graph on the nameplate near the main nameplate.

  2. Clean the dial glass, or replace it if damaged.

  3. Check that the alarm contacts are not closed.

  4. Check that there is no oil leakage or rust.

Noise :      Check for abnormal sounds

Loose connections

Check for loose connections such as those found in main circuits, grounding circuits, auxiliary circuits, and in foundation bolts and others.

Oil leakage

Check for oil leaks in connections such as those of valves and meters-especially in gasket joints and welded parts.

Gas leakage

In case of nitrogen gas sealed transformer, measure the nitrogen gas pressure and check for gas leaks.

General cooling equipment

Oil Pumps, cooling fans, radiators   for example, if installed

  1. Check that the oil pumps are operating and have no oil leakage or abnormal noise. If any abnormal noise is heard, most likely the oil pump bearings need replacing.

  2. Check that the cooling fans operate smoothly and create no abnormal noise or vibration.

  3. If any abnormal noise is heard, it is very likely that the fan bearings need replacing.

  4. Check for excessive build up of grime on the radiator surface and clean it.

Silica gel breather

  1. Pay attention to the discoloration of the absorbent.

  2. Check the level of the sealing oil in cup.

Note:

  • The absorbent will turn from blue into pink if its moisture content becomes in excess of 20 or 25% in the gravimetric ratio when more than 70% if its volume is found discolored replace or dry the absorbent.

  • Too much sealing oil sometimes contaminates absorbent while less sealing oil causes  

  • Ineffective sealing function. Check to see if the oil level is held within a specified limit at all times.

Pressure relief device

Check for cracks damages and traces of oil leaking in the pressure relief plate if plate is provided.

Cooling equipment (if provided)

  1. Check for leaks.

  2. For forced oil cooled or fan cooled type transformers, check for abnormal rotating sounds and vibration.

  3. Check for dust on radiator surface.

Bushing (porcelain type)

  1. If the bushing is provided with an oil level gauge, check oil level and check for oil leaks.

  2. Evaluate the extent of the porcelain contamination by appropriate method.

Instrument            Check indicators and relays.

Cooler control cubicle

  1. Check for rust

  2. Check that bolts are tightened

  3. Check the wire color

  4. Check for loose connection

  5. Check of insulation resistance

Oil purifier (if provided)

  1. Check the filter element

  2. Check the mush clog gin of strainer.

  3. Check for leaks.

  4. Check the oil purifier is operated punctually by timer.

  5. Check the oil flow sight.
