Introduction

This document describes the inspection and maintenance methods to keep transformer in good condition.

In this inspection we perform some additional inspections and measurements of electrical characteristics of transformer as preventive maintenance actions and when any transformer trouble should be investigated thoroughly.

The first draft is initiated by eng. Sherif Mahrous Omar tested by eng. Nafad Kamel Mahmoud, revised by WDEPCO. Quality control general director eng. Gamal Abd Elwahab, accepted by technical expert eng. Hassan Elbehiry and approved by eng. Mohamed Gamal Diab Head sector of El-Kureimat power station.

 

Safety Precautions 

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Use safety helmet, safety belt, and tool set

  • Be careful not to approach live parts for exterior check.

  • De-energize the transformer by circuit breaker and line switches.

  • Ground the line terminals of the transformer.

  • Attach caution tags not to operate switches for circuit breakers and line switches

  • De- energizes the control cabinets for coolers and tap changer by AC and DC switches.

  • Attach caution tags on switch boxes.

 

Tools and Equipment

  • Fine cloth for cleaning purposes.

  • Complete tool kit/set, including a torque wrench.

 

Environmental Recommendation

  • Don't burn the cloth used in cleaning. Collect it and send it to the dump.

  • Fix any oil leaks to prevent pollution and save money of wasted oil.

  • Oil leaks to the ground have very bad impact on the environment, mainly on soil and ground water and eventually on the ecosystem. It takes only I liter of Hydrocarbon spilled in an aquifer to render 1,000,000 liters of potable water useless for human consumption

 

Work to be carried out

  1. A periodic analysis of gas dissolved in oil is recommended every one or tow years.

  2. The dissolved gas should be extracted from the oil and analyzed.

  3. When the quantities of combustible gases are obtained we can evaluate the trouble which may be present

  4. If there is indication of moisture, sludge, low dielectric strength and low resistivity, an inspection should be made on the inside of the tank for possible cause of trouble.

  5. Measure resistivity of the oil when a periodic test shows that the oil is in bad condition.

  6. Measure the insulation resistance between a pair of windings, and between each winding and ground using 2000 volt Megger.

  7. Measure the ratio of a transformer by two voltmeter method or with a ratio tester.

  8. If the transformer has tap, the turn ratio should be measured for all taps as well as for the full winding.

  9. The test voltage may be between 100 and 200 V at the rating frequency.

  10. Compare the test result with those in the test report.

  11. Measure the winding resistance by a bridge method or by drop of potential method, the oil temperature should be recorded at the same time. 

  12. If the winding resistance corrected to a specified temperature is different from the data obtained previously, more detailed investigation should be made.

  13. Measure the exciting current at low voltage (100-200 volts) applied on the lower voltage winding with other winding being short circuited, the voltage wave shape should be sinusoidal.

  14. If the measured exciting current is much larger than the original data at installation, more detailed investigation should be made.

  15. Measure the impedance voltage at low current (5-10 ampere) applied on the higher voltage winding with lower voltage winding being short circuited.

  16. If the measured impedance voltage is much different from the original data at installation, more detailed investigation should be made.

  17. Exciting current and winding resistance should be measured in the same way as mentioned before.
