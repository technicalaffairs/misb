Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection I2, which must be performed on an annually bases as indicated by Y1 in the title block.

The easy set to check the condition of insulation of transformer is to inspect the insulating oil.

When examination of oil samples will be performed periodically, changes of insulation resistance, ageing of insulation oil, ingress of humidity or beginning defects have been located by dissolved gas analysis could be recognized in time.

For supervision of the insulation conditions we recommend a revision interval. interior of the revision interval an improvement of the condition of the insulation system when purifying the oil is possible.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued.

  • The qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable ID safety category.

  • Maintenance staff should be well trained and qualified or directly supervised by some one who is.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, safety belt when cleaning suspension insulators … etc.

  • It is recommended to use only fiberglass PVC ladders when working in the switchyard.

  • Transformer must be out of service, isolated and grounded from all sides.

  • Taking out all circuit breakers which connected to all sides of the transformer.

  • Disconnect supply of DC.

Environmental Recommendation

  1. There is danger of falling into the oil containment pit when worked near large power transformer

    2.

  2. Before starting the cleaning of all porcelain surfaces of the transformers, wear your breathing mask to protect lung tissues from the dust particulate.

  3. Don't burn the cloth used in cleaning, collect it and send it to the dump.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete tool kit/set including a torque wrench tool.

  • Safety helmet, safety shoes, safety belt ... etc.

  • PVC ladder.

  • Voltage indicator, HV tester rod.

  • Fine cloth for cleaning purposes, new silica-gel.

  • One or two stage oil purifying plant with min. flow rate of 1000 l/h and heating capacity of 100kw.

  • A vacuum pump set of minimum 100 m3/h.

  • A vacuum resistance oil pump of minimum 3000 l/m.

  • Heat exchanger of approx. 100 kw.

  • Two compartment for fuller earth filling which can be filled alternately.

  • An oil tank of adequate size to contain the total oil volume of the transformer which shall be purified.

Work to be Carried Out

Purification, Regeneration or new Oil Filling

  1. With respect to the interpretation of the tests results the following measures could be necessary:

  2. Purification of the oil and the insulation.

  3. Regeneration of the oil.

  4. New oil filling.

Purification of the Oil

  1. Purification means cleaning, drying and degassing of the oil.

  2. The chemical condition of the oil will be changed.

Regeneration of the Oil

In case of very aged oils purification is no more sufficient. Ageing products have to be absorbed and removed. The regeneration of oil will be a purification of oil with following fuller earth process and inhabitation.

New Oil Filling

  1. In case of results which indicate that a regeneration is not economical the transformer  has to be filled with new oil.

  2. The process of "wash – treatment" described below has been successfully performed for many times.

  3. It can be used for purification, regeneration and new oil filling with regard to the sequence of the different processes.

The wash Treatment

The main Characteristics

  1. Heating the active core by hot oil when the transformer is under vacuum

  2. Washing of ageing formation out of the active core by quick sucking of the oil from the tank

  3. Drying out of the active core during the following vacuum treatment

  4. When regeneration the old oil: 

1- Drying                     2- Degassing                      3- Fuller

4- Inhibition of the oil parallel to above mentioned treatment have to be performed.

Remark:

For this processes the transformer tank has to be designed for 100% vacuum (pressure lower 1m bar)

If the work can be done in a hall where it is possible to untank the active core, the purifying resp. The processes of regeneration have to been done in the following sequence:

  1. Suck the old oil.

  2. Un tank of the transformer, cleaning with purified new oil and necessary revision works to be done (on load tap changer, gasket, etc.)

  3. Parallel to these works purification (fuller earth treatment ) and heating of the old oil up to approx. 60oC interior of the circuit oil purifying plant – fuller earth plant – oil tank.

  4. Assemble the active core into the tank.

  5. Heat – insulate the transformer.

  6. Evacuate the transformer.

  7. Suck the worm oil into the transformer tank and heating of the active core by oil within the circuit transformer–heater transformer.

  8. After heating of all parts of the transformer up to 80 ◦C the oil has to be pumped off as quick as possible.

  9. During the evacuation of the transformer the old oil with the ageing formations which have been washed out in an earlier stage, will be purified and treated with fuller earth. This process can be repeated, points (7 to 9), if it is necessary. The necessity has to be determined by check – measurements of the oil and the transformer.

  10. Finally the purified, regenerated oil will be inhibited of 0.3% (2.6 – ditertiary – butyl – para- kresol), to improve the stability against ageing.

  11. The so purified, regenerated oil will be sucked under vacuum into the transformer.

  12. The above mentioned program includes all works which are necessary for regeneration of old oil drying and cleaning of the active core.

  13. Additional measures, as for transformer with high grade humid insulation, make greater efforts with respect to the drying process necessary.

  14. Because of the low diffusion – coefficient of oil – impregnated materials to non – impregnated materials, long drying periods of the inner insulation layers will be necessary.

  15. In this particular case drying out by use of a vacuum – oven would be more economically.
