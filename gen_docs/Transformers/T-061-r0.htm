Introduction

This document describes the maintenance procedure P2 which should be performed on the auto transformer every five years Y5 as indicated by  in the title block.

The first draft is initiated by eng. Sherif Mahrous Omar tested by eng. Nafad Kamel Mahmoud, revised by UEEPCO. quality control general director eng. Gamal Abd Elwahab, accepted by technical expert eng. Hassan Elbehiry and approved by eng. Mohamed Gamal Diab Head sector of El-Kureimat power station.

Safety Precautions 

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area.

    •
  • Use safety helmet, safety belt, and tool set

  • De-energize the transformer by circuit breaker and line switches.

  • Ground the line terminals of the transformer.

  • Attach caution tags not to operate switches for circuit breakers and line switches

  • De- energizes the control cabinets for coolers and tap changer by AC and DC switches.

  • Attach caution tags on switch boxes.

 

Tools and Equipment

  • Fine cloth for cleaning purposes,
  • Complete tool kit/set, including a torque wrench.

 

 

Environmental Recommendation

  • Don't burn the cloth used in cleaning. Collect it and send it to the dump.

  • Fix any oil leaks to prevent pollution and save money of wasted oil.

  •  Oil leaks to the ground have very bad impact on the environment, mainly on soil and ground water and eventually on the ecosystem. "It takes only I liter of Hydrocarbon spilled in an aquifer to render 1,000,000 liters of potable water useless for human consumption.

 

 

Work to be carried out

 

Painting, repainting should be done once every five years.

 

 for tab changer

The internal inspection of tab changer and oil change must be done when 20000 operations have been made or 2 years have been passed since the beginning of service, afterwards every 50000 operations or every 5 years whichever comes earlier.

 

Inspected items:

    Diverter switch vessel:

 Head case.

  1. Inspect oil leakage and carbon accumulated inside the head case.

  2. Replace gasket or repair head case by welding as necessary and clean carbon.

 

 Bursting plate.

Check oil leakage or corrosion that can cause oil leakage and replace if necessary

 

Upper gear unit.
  1. Check the rain water inside the unit. Replace O-ring of cover and clean sealing surface.
  2. Check for rusting. Remove rust and apply grease.
  3. Check for oil leakage.
  4. Check for faulty engagement.
  5. Check for abnormal wear.

Replace the unit if oil leakage or abnormal wear or damage of the teeth is found.

 

Outer insulation cylinder.
  1. Check for spark trace inside cylinder. Investigate the cause and replace.
  2. Check for carbon sticking to the inner wall of cylinder. Wipe and clean.

 

 Connecting contacts.

    Check for crack or deformation of shunt. Replace as necessary.

 

 Bottom flange.

    Check for foreign matters. Examine and clean.

 

Diverter switch:

 Spur gear

  1. Check for abnormal engagement.

  2. Check for abnormal wear.

  3. Replace the spur gear if the teeth is damaged.

 

 Insulating driving shaft

    Check for spark trace. Investigate the cause and replace.

 

For insulating supporting staff:

    Check for stuck carbon. Clean off carbon.

 

 Shield ring.

    Check for spark trace. Investigate the cause and retape or replace.

 

 Quick motion mechanism.
  1. Check for broken or damaged springs (replace if necessary).
  2. Check for loose, damaged or fallen bolts or pins (Tighten up or replace if necessary).
  3. Check for abnormal wear, deformation or breakdown ( latch is regarded as broken down if it is not in a good condition to stop crank)(Replace if detective).

 

 Moving contact unit.
  1. Check slack moving contact spring or trigonal lever tension spring (disassemble and repair or replace as necessary).
  2. Check for crack or abnormal wear in upper or lower guide plate. (Replace if necessary).
  3. Check of wearing condition of moving arcing contact.
  4.  Replace when the contact tip has worn out from the original 9mm to 1mm.
  5. Fill off projection at the edge of contacts more than 2mm higher with attention paid to metal powder.

Diverter switch:

 Stationary contact.
  1. Check wear in stationary contact.
  2. Check crack or deformation of shunt (replace if necessary).
  3. Check damage connecting leads and terminals (replace if necessary).
  4. Check loose fastening bolts (re-tighten and secure).

 

 Current limiting resistor.
  1. Check measurement of resistance (record the value).
  2. Check crack of insulators as resistor box and spark trace on resistor. (Replace if necessary).
  3. Check for stuck carbon (clean and wash in fresh oil).
  4. Check for loosen terminal (tighten up and clamp).

Tap selector:

 Upper mechanism.
  1. Check Loose Geneva pinion roller etc. due to abnormal wear
  2. Check any deformation or abnormal wear in Geneva gear groove etc.
  3. If it is faulty and has to be replaced or adjusted, the work can not be done within the transformer and along repair program will be needed.

 

 Contact assembly.
  1. Check abnormal wear or arc trace, examine the operational sequence with diverter switch if there is arc trace, and check the operational condition in closing and opening if extreme wear is found.
  2. Check broken contact spring (replace if necessary).
  3. Check spark trace or crack in any insulation, investigate the cause and replace (for replacement of moving contact insulation cylinder, the tab selector has to be lifted out).
  4. Check loose terminal or any breakage on lead taping, give an additional tapping if necessary and check the coarseness on the surface and repair and tighten up.
  5. Check deformation or crack in change-over switch shunt (replace if necessary).
  6. Check any fallen matters on the bottom of the transformer tank under the tap selector, if there is any fallen matter; find out its original position to return it there.

Inspection of driving equipment of tap changer:

  1. The periodical inspections must be made once every 100,000 operations or 5 years.

  2. It is recommended that the inspection of driving equipment is made at the same time with the tab changer.

  3. The inspection need to opening the housing cover.

 

Inspected items:

Gear box.
  1. Check to see that the oil level is above the minimum lime (L line).
  2. Check for oil leakage.

 Control mechanism gear.

 

    Check gear teeth for want of grease and abnormal wear.

Bolts, nuts, pins, retainers and springs.
  1. Check bolts and nuts for looseness.
  2. Check pins, retainers and springs for breakdown or dislocation.

 

 Motors and breaking contactors and step relay.
  1. Check their making and breaking operations for chattering buzzing and sticking.
  2. Check their contacts for wrong contact.

 Thermostat and space heater.
  1. Check to see that the thermostat turns ON at the temperature below 10 C.
  2. Check that the space heater for disconnection.

 Wiring.
  1. Check the wiring for disconnection, the clamps for damage and the vinyl coverings for discoloration.
  2. Check the terminals for looseness.

 Pilot switches.
  1. Check to see that the motor drive stops after making one tap change only, by one operation of the manual operation switch in electrical operation.
  2. Check to see that the same result is obtained even when the manual operation switch is continuously kept operated
  3. Check the operation sequence for correctness.
  4. Check the cams and micro-switches for abnormality.

 Operator and tap position indicator (confirmation of the stopping position).

  1. Check to see that the motor drive stops within the green zone after electrical operation.

  2. Check the tap position indication for correctness.

 

 Tap position transmitters.

  1. Check the contacts for correct contact.

  2. Check to see that any dust or metal particles from contacts and not adhering.

  3. Check the stopping position for the agreement with the tap position in use.

 Interlock switch.

Check to see that operation of the manual switch does not start the driving motor if the hand crank is inserted into the manual operation shaft.

Electrical limit switches.
  1. Check to see that the driving motor does not start by the signal to make an electrical operation towards beyond the end positions.
  2. Check the operation sequence for correctness.
  3. Inspect the micros-witches for breakdown.

 Mechanical overrun prevention device

(confirmation of the function to mechanically prevent overrun beyond the end positions).
  1. Check to see that the output shaft does not rotate when the end position is tried to be passed over by hand crank operation.
  2. Check to see that the output shaft begins to rotate when the direction of the hand crank operation is reversed.

 Vapor tablet corrosion inhibitors.

Replace the used vapor tablet corrosion inhibitors with new ones (four pieces).

Transmission mechanism.
  1. Remove the protective covers to check for rusting and wear or dislocation of pins and washers.
  2. Opening the cover of the bevel gear mechanism and check the bevel gears for abnormality. Apply grease over the teeth.

Measurement of insulation resistance.
  1. Measurement is to be made only when the motor drive is electrically abnormal.
  2. Measure the insulation resistance between the live part and the ground after disconnecting the incoming cables from the external terminals. The resistance should be above 1 M ohm (by 500 V Megger at 20 C).

 Measurement for minimum operating voltage.
  1. Measure the minimum operating voltage when the motor drive is electrically abnormal.
  2. Check to see that the motor and breaking contactors and step relay operate at a voltage below 85 percent of the rated voltage.

Tap changing equipment in total (final overall test after inspection)

Examine the followings after finishing the total assembly of the tap changing equipment:
  1. Check the agreement of the tap position between the motor drive and tap changer.
  2. Check the coordination of the operating sequence between the motor drive and tap changer. In either direction the diverter switch should make its Owen operation before the motor contactor is not deenergized.
  3. Check the hand crank torque for the abnormality.
  4. Check the starting and stopping.
  5. Check the function of the electrical limit switches and overrun prevention device in electrical and manual operation.

 

 Remote control.

Make remote operation from the control panel and check the following:

  1.  Confirmation of the step by step operation.

  2.  Confirmation of the electrical limit switches.

  3.  Confirmation of the alarm circuits (stagnation between taps and tripping of the no-fuse breakers).

  4.  Confirmation of the agreement of the tap position indication between at the control panel and at the motor drive.
