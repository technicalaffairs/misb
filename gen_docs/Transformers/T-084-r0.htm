Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection I1 for step up and step down oil power transformer, which must be performed every month as indicated by M1 in the title block

The first draft initiated by eng. Ahmed Abd El halim El Nagar, tested by eng. Hassan Amer, revised by quality control eng. Adel Abd El Qader El Nagar at EDEPCO, accepted by general director eng. Ahmed El hosiny and approved by eng. Mohamed El Sarag damietta P.S head sector

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category

  • Maintenance staff should will trained & qualified or directly supervised by some one who is

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area are required

  • Personal protective equipment must be used (safety shoes, helmet, breathing mask, safety glasses)

  • There is a danger of falling into the oil containment pit when working near large power transformer

Tools and Equipment

  • Megger (500 - 5000) volt

  • Multimeter

  • Air blower

  • Complete maintenance tool set

  • Cleaning solvent

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Work to be Carried Out
 
No Maintenance Procedure Position Test result
1. General visual inspection on transformer and oil leaks and repair any exist damage Ok - Not ok   
2. Inspect all painted surface of the transformer and repair any exist damage Ok - Not ok   
3. Check oil level in the tank, conservator and oil filled bushings Ok - Not ok   
4. Make visual inspection of all cooling fans and repair any exist damage Ok - Not ok   
5. Switch on cooling fans manually and listen for abnormal noise Ok - Not ok   
6. Check oil temperature indicator Ok - Not ok   
7. Check winding temperature indicator      
8. Visual inspection of transformer control cabinet Ok - Not ok   
9. Visual inspection of transformer control cabinet of tap changer Ok - Not ok   
10. Checking the dehydrating breather and replace the abnormal by new Ok - Not ok   
11. Check the correct pressure of bushing sealing oil indicator Ok - Not ok   
12. Read and record the maximum winding temperature      
13. Read and record the maximum oil temperature      
14. Checking the earthing system Ok - Not ok   
15. Check fire fighting system on transformer Ok - Not ok   
16. Checking any alarms on control panel and replace any damage indicator lamps Ok - Not ok   
17. Take oil sampling and analysis it by chemical staff and be sure that oil sampling valves are closed every 6 month
