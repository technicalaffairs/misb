Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff must carry the suitable ID safety category,

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area is required,

  • Wear the appropriate safety equipment as safety shoes, helmet,

  • Transformer must be out of service

GENERAL PRECAUTIONS

  • Prepare necessary tools and spare parts after due planning

  • Before commencing work make sure that the machine is isolated from the power supply and make sure again by making use of electric detector that the circuit is dead.

  • Ground transformer bushing terminals while working on the transformer

    •

  • Should further information be needed or any particular problem arises which are not covered by these instructions such as internal inspection please ask  HITACHI LTD.

Work to be Carried out

Classification of Maintenance and Inspection

Routine maintenance& inspections

  • Maintenance and inspection under operating condition through daily protocol

Periodic Inspection

  • Inspection to be conducted periodically cycle of inspection is primarily in accordance with item 1.5

Routine Maintenance and Inspection

Transformer, Temperature and Load

  1. Read and record oil and if any winding temperature.

  2. Record ambient temperature, load (MW, MVAR) and voltage.

Notes:

  1. Transformer temperature directly affects the life of the insulating material.

  2. Maximum temperature rise limits are specified for the both oil and winding temperature.

In the daily inspection,

  1. Check not only that temperatures are held within the maximum limit, but also that the temperature lies in a satisfactory range in relation to their values in the test report, load condition and ambient temperature.

  2. Overloading of transformer should be made only in accordance with the applicable standard

Oil level

  1. Check oil level gauges

Noise

  1. Chick for abnormal sounds

Loose Connections

  1. Check for loose connections such as those found in main circuits, grounding circuits, auxiliary circuits, and in foundation bolts and others.

  2. Check valves for looseness.

Oil leakage

  1. Check for oil leaks in connections such as those of valves and meters-especially in gasket joints and welded parts.

Gas Leakage

  1. In case of nitrogen gas sealed transformer, measure the nitrogen gas pressure and check for gas leaks.

Silica Gel Breather

  1. Pay attention to the discoloration of the absorbent.

  2. Check the level of the sealing oil in cup.

Notes:

  1. The absorbent will turn from blue into pink if its moisture content becomes in excess of 20 or 25% in the gravimetric ratio When more than 70% if its volume is found discolored replace or dry the   absorbent.

  2. Too much sealing oil sometimes contaminates absorbent while less sealing oil causes ineffective sealing function. Check to see if the oil level is held within a specified limit at all times.

Pressure relief device

  1. Check for cracks damages and traces of oil leaking in the pressure relief plate.

  2. If plate is provided.

Cooling equipment (if provided)

  1. Check for leaks.

  2. For forced oil cooled or fan cooled type transformers, check for abnormal rotating sounds and vibration .

  3. Check for dust on radiator surface.

On  – load Tap changer

  1. Check for operating sounds

  2. Record the number of tap changing operations.

  3. Check oil level gauge of conservator.

Bushing (porcelain type)

  1. If the bushing is provided with a oil level gauge, check oil level and check for oil leaks.

  2. Evaluate the extent of the porcelain contamination by appropriate method.

Instrument

  1. Check indicators and relays.

Cooler control cubicle

  1. Check for rust

  2. Check that bolts are tight

  3. Check that wire color

  4. Check for losses connection

  5. Check of insulation resistance.

Oil purifier (if provided)

  1. Check the filter element

  2. Check the mush clog gin of strainer.

  3. Check for leaks.

  4. Check the oil purifier is operated punctually by timer.

  5. Check the oil flow sight.

Periodic Inspection

Insulation of Windings

  1. Measurement of winding insulation resistance.

  2. Measurement of dielectric loss factor (tan δ) of winding.

  3. Inspections by other insulation judging methods 

Insulating oil

  1. Measurement of .dielectric strength.

  2. Measurement of neutralization number.

  3. Measurement of water content.

  4. Gas analysis.

Oil preservation system

  1. Check oil level and for proper relation with oil temperature.

  2. Check for oil leaks.

  3. Check that the oil stop valve between tank and conservator is open.

  4. Refer to separate instructions, if any.

No-voltage Tap changer (if provided)

  1. Check for oil leaks.

  2. Checks tap position.

  3. Check that bolts are tight.

On –load Tap changer (If provided)

  1. Refer to separate instructions

Fan motor

  1. Check the bearings for unusual sound and Vibration.

  2. Check the oil seals for unseal sound and Vibration.

BREATHER

  1. Check the breather.

  2. Check the oil level

  3. Check the color of silica gel

Bushing (Porcelain type)

  1. Measurement of insulation resistance.

  2. Measurement  of tand. (If the bushing is of condenser type)

  3. Inspection of terminal and bushing top shield. Check for loose connection or change in color.

  4. Check  for contamination and damage of porcelain.

  5. Check for oil leaks.

  6. Check oil level, if provided.

Cooling equipment

1.     Clean the heat exchanger surface.

2.     Check for oil leaks and rust.

Indicator (winding temperature, Oil temperature) and protective Relays

  1. Check normal indication and operation.

  2. Check the auxiliary wiring and the signaling for the indicator and protective relays.

  3. Check for looseness of terminals and lead.

Tank

  1. Check for oil leaks.

  2. Inspection of finished surfaces. See if rainwater gets collected on the tank top and causes rusting on edges and corners.

Pressure Relief Device

  1. Inspection of pressure relief plate, if provided.

  2. Inspection of alarm contacts, if provided.

  3. Check for oil leaks.

  4. Check for insulation resistance.

  5. Refer to separate instructions

Buchholtz Relay

  1. Check whether or not any gas found in the relay.

  2. If any free gas is found in the relay. Check the gas for decomposable gases such as hydrogen, methane, ethane, accethylene, carbon mono oxide and so forth. As for gas analysis, refer to item 1.6.

  3. Refer to separate instructions.

Cooler Control Cubicle

Enclosure

  1. Check for loose bolts or bolts which have fallen off.

  2. Check for rust.

Terminal Blocks

  1. Check for loose connection or change in color.

Molded case circuit breaker (MCCB)

  1. Check for loose connection or change in color.

  2. Check for crack of body.

  3. Check for breakers which have tripped.

Contactors (Mgsw)

  1. Check for loose connection or change in color.

  2. Check for damage to contacts.

  3. Check for crack of body.

Auxiliary transformer

  1. Check for loose connection or change in color of coil. 

Thermal relay

  1. Check for loose connection.

  2. Check for damage to heat element device.

Enclosed Fuse (EF)

  1. Check for loose connection or change in color.

  2. Check for crack of tube.
