Introduction

This document outlines the minor maintenance procedures P1, which must be performed from two to three years as indicated by Y2:3 in the title block.

Safety Precautions 

  • A work permit must be issued,

  • The maintenance staff must carry the suitable ID safety category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required,

  • Wear the appropriate safety equipment as safety shoes, helmet,

  • Transformer must be out of service and isolated and grounded from all sides.

  • The high voltage CB and low voltage CB of the transformer must be isolated and proper earthling at the two sides is required.

  • Use only fiberglass ladders.

  • Personnel carrying out the inspection should be trained and qualified or directly supervised by someone who is.

  • If it becomes necessary to enter the main tank, be sure that the oxygen level is sufficient to provide safe working conditions

Tools and Equipment

  • Dielectric strength oil tester.

  • Megger 2000 volt.

  • Binoculars or infrared scope.

  • Oil sampling bottles.

  • Reference thermometer and bath of water.

  • Spare parts for cooling equipment (oil pump, cooling fans…etc)

Work to be carried out

Insulating oil (dielectric strength) 

Using an oil tester, make a dielectric strength measurement of the insulating oil in the main tank.

Insulation of Windings

  1. Measure the winding insulation resistance.

  2. Measure the dielectric loss factor (tan δ) of winding.

  3. Inspect by other insulation judging methods.

Insulating oil

  1. Measure the dielectric strength.

  2. Measure the neutralization number.

  3. Measure the water content.

  4. Gas analysis.

Oil preservation system

  1. Check oil level and for proper relation with oil temperature.

  2. Check for oil leaks.

  3. Check that the oil stop valve between tank and conservator is opened.

No-load Tap changer (if provided)

  1. Check for oil leaks.

  2. Checks tap position.

  3. Check that bolts are tightened

Fan motor

  1. Check the bearings for unusual sound and vibration.

  2. Check the oil seals for unseal sound and vibration.

Breather

  1. Check the breather.

  2. Check the oil level.

Bushing (Porcelain type)

  1. Measure the insulation resistance.

  2. Measure the tan δ. (If the bushing is of condenser type)

  3. Inspect the terminal and bushing top shield. Check for loose connection or change in color.

  4. Check for contamination and damage of porcelain.

  5. Check for oil leaks.

  6. Check oil level, if provided.

Cooling equipment

  1. Clean the heat exchanger surface.

  2. Check for oil leaks and rust.

Winding temperature oil, Oil temperature, protective relays

  1. Check normal indication and operation.

  2. Check the auxiliary wiring and the signaling for the indicator and protective relays.

  3. Check for looseness of terminals and lead

Tank

  1. Check for oil leaks.

  2. Inspect the finished surfaces. See if rainwater gets collected on the tank top and causes rusting on edges and corners.

Pressure Relief Device

  1. Inspect the pressure relief plate, if provided.

  2. Inspect the alarm contacts, if provided.

  3. Check for oil leaks.

  4. Check for insulation resistance.

Buchholez Relay

  1. Check whether or not any gas found in the relay.

  2. If any free gas is found in the relay. Check the gas for decomposable gases such as hydrogen, methane, ethane, acetylene, carbon monoxide and so forth.

Cooler control cubicle

Enclosure

  1. Check for loose bolts or bolts which have fallen off.

  2. Check for rust.

Terminal Blocks    

Check for loose connection or change in color.

Molded case circuit breaker (MCCB)

  1. Check for loose connection or change in color.

  2. Check for crack of body.

  3. Check for breakers which have tripped.

Contactors

  1. Check for loose connection or change in color.

  2. Check for damage to contacts.

  3. Check for crack of body.

  4. Check for loose connection or change in color of coil.

Thermal relay

  1. Check for loose connection.

  2. Check for damage to heat element device.

Enclosed fuse

  1. Check for loose connection or change in color.

  2. Check for crack of tube

high voltage Testing   (Each two years)

  1. DC Resistance for winding.

  2. Turns ratio for windings.

  3. Insulation for windings by megger (R60/R15)

  4. Tan delta for windings and bushings
