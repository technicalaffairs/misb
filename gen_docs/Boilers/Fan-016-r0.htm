Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Maintenance staff should be will trained and qualified or directly supervised by some one who is.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear your safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, breathing mask, safety glass ...etc

  • Isolate the circuit breaker for the Seal Air Fan.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set

  • Cleaning fluid feeler gauge, anti dust mask

  • Suitable lifting device.

Work to be Carried Out

Coupling

Coupling Disassembly and Grid Removal

1.

Remove the cover halves and grid.

2.

A round rod or screwdriver that will conveniently fit into the open loop ends of the grid is required.

3.

Begin at the open end of the grid section and insert the rod or the screwdriver into the loop ends.

4.

Use the teeth adjacent to each loop as a fulcrum and pry the grid out radially  in even, gradual stages, proceeding alternately from side to side.

Assembly of type T10 Steel FLEX Tapered Grid Couplings

Clearance Fit Hubs

  1. Clean all parts using a non-flammable solvent

  2. Check hubs, shafts and key ways for burrs.

  3. Don't heat clearance fit hubs with flange face flush with shaft ends or as otherwise specified and tighten setscrews

Interference Fit Hubs

  1. Furnished without setscrews

  2. Heat hubs to a maximum of 275 oF (135 oC) using an oven, torch, induction heater or an oil bath

Warning:

  • If and oil bath is used, the oil must be have a flash point of 350 oF (177 oC) or higher.

  • Don't rest hubs on the bottom of the container.

  • Don't use an open flame in a combustible atmosphere or near combustible materials.

  • Mount hubs as quickly as possible with flange face flush with shaft end.

  • Allow hubs to cool before proceeding

  • Insert setscrews (if required) and tighten

Mount Seals and Hubs

1.

Lock out starting switch of prime
  

2.

Clean all metal parts using a non-flammable solvent
 

3.

Lightly coats seals with grease and place on shafts BEFORE mounting hubs
 

4.

Heat interference fit hubs as previously instructed
 

5.

Seal keyways to prevent leakage
 

6.

Mount hubs on their respective shafts so the hub face is flush with the end of its shaft unless otherwise indicated.
 

7.

Tighten setscrews when furnished.
 

 

Gap and Angular Alignment

1.

Use a spacer bar equal in thickness to the gap specified in Table 1
 

2.

Insert bar as shown below left, to same depth at 90o intervals and measure clearance between bar and hub face with feelers.
  

3.

The difference in minimum and maximum measurements must not exceed the ANGULAR installation limits specified in Table 1.
  

Offset Alignment

1.

Align a straight edge rests squarely (or within the limits specified in Table 2) on both hubs as shown above and also at 90o intervals.

2.

Check with feelers.

3.

The clearance must not exceed the PARALLEL OFFSET installation limits specified in Table 1.

4.

Tighten all foundation bolts and repeat steps 2 and 3.

5.

Realign coupling if necessary.

 

  

Insert Grid

1.

Pack gap and grooves with specified lubricant before inserting grid.

2.

When grid are furnished in two or more segments, install them so that all cut ends extend in the same direction; this will assure correct grid contact with non-rotating pin in cover halves.

3.

Spread the grid slightly to pass over the coupling teeth and seat with a soft mallet.

 

Pack with Grease and Assemble Covers

1.

Pack the spaces between and around the grid with as much lubricant as possible and wipe off excess flush with top of grid.

2.

Position seals on hubs to line up with grooves in cover.

3.

Position gaskets on flange of lower cover half and assemble covers so that the match marks are on the same side.

4.

Push gaskets in until they stop against the seals and secure cover halves with fasteners, tighten to torque specified in Table2.

5.

Make sure gaskets stay in position during tightening of fasteners

 

CAUTION: Make certain lube plugs are installed before operating.

Maintenance Procedures

1.

Check alignment per. If the maximum operating misalignment limits are exceeded, realign the coupling to the recommended installation limits (see table2).

VERTICAL COUPLING

2.

Check tightening torques of all fasteners.

3.

Inspect seal ring and gasket to determine if replacement is required. If leaking grease, replace.

4.

When connected equipment is serviced:

a) Disassemble the coupling and inspect for wear.

b) Replace worn parts.

c) Clean grease from coupling and repack with new grease.

d) Install coupling using new gasket.

 

Periodic Lubrication

1.

 

The required frequency of lubrication is directly related to the type of lubricant chosen, and the operating conditions.

 

2.

Steelflex couplings lubricated with common industrial lubricants should be relubed annually.
3.

The use of Falk Long Term Grease (LTG) will allow relube intervals to be extended to beyond five years.
4.

When relubing, remove both lube plugs and insert lube fitting.

5.

Fill with recommended lubricant until an excess appears at the opposite hole.

Caution:    Make certain all plugs have been inserted after lubricating.

Bearing

Disassembly of the Renk Sleeve Bearing System Pedestal type "ER" - Finned Housing (with type 10 Seal On Both Sides)

  1. To disassemble the bearing liner and all associated parts from the bearing housing, carry out the following instructions. Carefully store all disassembled parts in a safe location.

  2. Thoroughly clean the exterior of the bearing housing. Loosen and remove the oil drain plug (1) at the bottom of the bearing housing (2). Drain the oil from the bearing housing.

  3. Loosen and remove the bolts (6) at the split line of the bearing housing (2) and bearing housing
    cap (5).

  4. Use the lifting eyebolts (10) to lift (by hand or hoist) the bearing housing cap (5) straight up, so that the cap is completely disengaged from the lower halves of the labyrinth seals (18), labyrinth seal carriers (13), bearing housing (2), and the bearing liner (16).

  5. Pull the bearing housing cap (5) forward out of and away from the bearing area. Loosen and remove the bolts (14) securing the upper halves of the seal carriers (13) to the bearing housing cap (5). Remove the seal carriers.

  6. Lift (by hand or hoist) the upper half of the bearing liner (15) and remove it.

  7. Loosen and remove the bolts at the split line of the oil ring (17). Carefully disengage the dowels holding the oil ring halves together and remove the oil ring.

  8. Remove the garter springs (19) that encircle the labyrinth seals (18). Lift off the upper half of each seal, then rotate the lower half of each seal out of the groove in the lower half of each seal carrier (13).

  9. Disconnect and remove RTD's, thermocouples, or any other temperature detecting instruments that enter the lower half of the bearing liner (16).

  10. Using a hoist or jack, raise the shaft slightly so that the lower half of the bearing liner (16) can be rolled out of the bearing housing (2).

  11. Roll out (be careful not to use excessive force) the lower half of the bearing liner (16) and
    remove it.

  12. Loosen and remove the bolts (14) securing the lower halves of the seal carriers (13) to the bearing housing (2). Remove the seal carriers.

  13. Thoroughly clean and inspect all individual parts which have been removed. Clean the interior of the bearing housing.

  14. To reassemble the Renk Bearing System, follow the preceding  instructions in the reverse order.

Reassembly

  1. Check mounting structure, making sure it is rigid, level and well supported.

  2. Inspect shaft to ensure it is smooth (Ra ≤ 0.4, equivalent to 32 micro-inch finish, or better), within the dimensions and tolerances given by RENK Corporation and free of burrs or rough spots.

  3. After removing the upper part of the housing (2) and the bearing liner (3 & 4), the interior of the housing and the remaining surfaces of the liner are to be cleaned thoroughly and checked for any damage caused in transit.

  4. Before the bottom liner half is replaced, apply oil to the spherical seats in the housing base and the liner. The shaft (5) may now be partially lowered into position. Special care must be taken so that the axial surfaces of the locating bearing are not damaged.

  5. As soon as the shaft has reached a distance of approx. 2 mm (0.08 Inch) from the liner base, the housing base can be precisely aligned. The control is made by checking the distances between the shaft and the lateral bores in the housing.

  6. After the split faces of the bottom liner half and the housing base are aligned, apply oil to the shaft and the liner. Then lower the shaft into place in the liner. With a slight hammer blow against the housing base settle the liner into its seating so that the liner axis and shaft axis are parallel.

  7. The loose oil ring (6) is installed next.

  8. The ring must be handled with special care as reliable operation of the bearing is also dependent on the effective and reliable functioning of the oil ring.

  9. The bolts must be tightly fastened.

  10. Split misalignment must be avoided and any burrs or edges carefully removed in order to ensure smooth running of the ring.

  11. In any dis- and reassembly, care must be taken that the oil ring is not distorted and its geometrical shape is maintained.

  12. The outside of the two liner halves is stamped with identification numbers or marks near the split line.

  13. Make sure that these marks align and the split faces are clean when placing-the top liner half (3) Into position.

  14. Incorrect fitting may lead to heavy damage due to clamping of the shaft.

  15. Check to ensure that the loose oil ring can still rotate freely on the shaft.

  16. After coating the housing split faces with a non hardening sealing compound, place the housing cap (3) into position.

  17. Care must be taken that the identification numbers or marks match.

  18. Ensure also that the anti rotation pin is seated without any contact to the corresponding hole in the liner.

  19. The cap bolts (7) must be tightened diagonally and securely to prevent any loss of oil through the split line of any loosening of the housing in cases where the installation is subject to vibration.

Oil Filling

  1. The bearing can be filled with the prescribed type of oil through the hole for the top sight glass (8) or through a thermometer hole (1).

  2. All holes and threads not used are to be closed by pipe plugs. Also check all connections for oil leaks.

  3. Filling the bearing with lubricant beyond the middle of the oil sight glass (11) does not impair the function of the bearing, but there is a possibility that excess oil may leak out through the shaft seals.

Shaft Seals

  1. Insert it into the groove of the carrier ring where the stop pin is always in the corresponding recess in the upper half of the carrier ring.

  2. Clean the drain holes in the lower part of the seal if it is dirty.

  3. Press the bottom half of the complete seal lightly against the underside of the shaft.

  4. Clean the contact faces of the carrier ring to the housing carefully from grease, rust, etc.

  5. Also coat it with the sealing compound before assembly.

Putting Into Service

Precautions before actual start-up

Bolting
     All bolts must be tightly fastened. Any loose part can cause vibrations.

Casing
    Make sure no foreign objects are present inside.

Rotor (Wheel and shaft)
    Make sure it turns manually without any resistance or static friction.

Pillow Blocks (Bearing)
    Sump up all precaution indicated in section "Lubrication".
    Secure bed-plates on their support and caps on their cast-iron frame.

Protection Guards and Inspection Doors
    Lock them with care to prevent any accident.

Flow Control Devices
    Must be placed in "closed" position.

Electric Motor
    Make sure all connections are correct and that the wheel rotates in the proper direction.

Setting After Start-up

Inlet Damper
    open progressively

Rotor
    Do not exceed the maximum specified temperatures or speed of rotation.

Pillow Blocks
    After 150 hours of operation, verify the tightening of each pillow block and motor nuts, and proceed with the installation of pins on the pillow blocks
