Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category                         

  • Safety fence  with caution marks surrounding the work area

  • Wear your safety helmet, shoes.... etc

  • Isolate the circuit breaker for the motor of this jockey pump.

  • Isolate the electrical cable feeding of the motor of this pump.

  • The pump must be isolated mechanically by closing suction and delivery valves.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set.

  • Cleaning fluid, manual puller

  • Suitable lifting device, feeler gauge

  • Hammer, Torque wrench

  • Dial indicator

Work to be Carried Out

Disassembly

  1. Disengage the coupling halves

  2. PUMP. Disassemble pump to the extent required as follows

  3. Remove the nuts from the gland bolts (17B) and remove packing glands (17) from the shaft (6). The packing gland halves are separable.

  4. Remove all nuts or cap screws from the upper casing (1B) and from the bearing caps (41&43) match mark bearing caps to lower casing (1A).

  5. Use the jack screws (not shown) on the bottom side of the lower casing split flange to separate the upper and lower casings. Turn the jack screws back below the split flange surface to avoid reassembly interference.

  6. Attach hoist to customer-furnished eye bolt in upper casing. Use eye bolt to lift upper casing only. Eye bolt must have 1^-13 UNC external thread.

  7. Place slings around the shaft near the bearing housings and lift rotating element from lower casing (1A). Tap lightly on the underside of the bearing housings to separate the housings from the brackets .

  8. Place rotating element in a convenient work place.

Disassembly of Rotating Element

  1. Loosen set screws and remove the coupling half, tap from the back of the hub or use a puller. Remove coupling key (46), and outboard deflector (40B).

  2. Take out cap screws to remove bearing covers (35,37) and the gaskets (73B). Remove inboard bearing cover seal (47) from cover (35) only if replacement of seal is required.

  3. Remove retaining ring (18A) from outboard end of shaft.

  4. Remove housings (31 & 33), bearings (16 & 18), and bearing housing seals (169) as units with a bearing puller. Remove deflectors (40A).

  5. Remove casing rings (7). On most pumps this may be done before removing the coupling half.

  6. Remove packing rings (13) lantern rings (29) if provided and stuffing box bushings ( 63 )

  7. Loosen shaft sleeve set screws then loosen shaft sleeves ( 14 &14 A) with spanner wrench.

Reassembly

Rotating Element

  1. Coat the shaft (6) lightly with oil. b. Place impeller key (32) in shaft keyway;

  2. Align impeller (2) on shaft and install with an arbor press or brass tubular sleeve and hammer. Guard against bending shaft. When assembled, the impeller vanes must rotate in the proper direction. (See Figure 3), and impeller hub must be centered on shaft journal.

 Figure 3: Vane Position for Proper Rotation

  1. Coat shaft sleeve 0-rings (14B) with oil and install in shaft sleeves (14 & 14A). Install shaft sleeve set screws in shaft sleeves. Assemble shaft sleeves to the shaft and hand tighten against the impeller. do not tighten shaft sleeve set screws.

  2. Install the stuffing box bushings (63).

  3. Locate casing rings (7) on impeller.

  4. Place inboard deflectors (40A) on shaft.

  5. If previously removed, install bearing housing seals (169) into housings (31 & 33). Insert bearings (16 & 18), into housings (31 & 33).

  6. Press housing-bearing-seal assemblies on shaft to seat bearings against shaft shoulders.

  7. Install bearing retaining ring (18A) in groove against the outboard bearing.

  8. Install gaskets (73B) on bearing covers. Use factory supplied parts or cut replacement gaskets from I/16 inch No. 444 Vellumoid. (SAE P3415A). EXCEPTION: For all models using 3306 size outboard bearing, gasket 73B is I/32 thick.

  9. Attach inboard and outboard bearing covers (35,37). In the assembled position, the grease drain tap must be located at bottom in a horizontal plane.

  10. Install outboard deflector (40B), and coupling key (46); assemble coupling half on the shaft and tighten the setscrew

Pump

Complete the assembly of pump as follows:

  1. Use factory supplied casing gasket (73A) or use the upper casing (1B) as a template to cut casing gasket (73A) from 1/64 inch Vellumoid (SAE P3313B). It is very important that specified material and thickness be used for casing gasket. Machined surfaces of both casings must be perfectly clean and free from burrs and nicks.

  2. Affix the new casing gasket to lower casing (1 A) with shellac.    

  3. Use slings around the shaftnear bearings to set rotating elemen into  lower casing. Position  the casing rings (7) and both bearing housings so thatall dowel pins engage in slots in the lower case split surface

  4. Assemble both bearing caps per match marks and tighten the cap screws.                     

  5. Adjust the shaft sleeves (14 & 14A) to center the impeller in the lower casing volute, andftighten both shaft sleeves with a spanner wrench, then. tighten shaft sleeve set screws to 130 inch-pounds torque.

  6. Cover the top side of the casing gasket with a mixture of graphite and oil. Install the gland bolts (17B). Carefully locate the upper casing on the, lower, making certain the dowel pins engage. Install cap screws and tighten working from the center of the casing to each end, to the torque values in Table II. If any cap screws require replacement, use only parts with equal or greater tensile strength; See Table II. Rotate shaft by hand to check that it turns freely.

  7. Push the stuffing box bushings (63) to the rear of the stuffing boxes. Insert two packing rings, lantern ring (29) if provided, and three packing rings.  Insert each ring separately and stagger the joints of successive rings 90°

  8. Insert the packing glands (17) and set the gland bolt nuts finger tight — DO NOT USE A WRENCH.

  9. Rotate shaft by hand to check that it turns freely.

  10. Replace all drain plugs, if removed during.disassembly.

  11. Relubricate the bearings.
