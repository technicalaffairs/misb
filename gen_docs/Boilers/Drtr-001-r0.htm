Introduction

This document outlines the major maintenance procedure P1, which must be performed every 50,000 operating hours to remove the (O2 ) from the water.

The first draft is initiated by eng. Mohamed Taha Elmansy, tested by eng. Abd Elkader Fath Alla at 05 Oct 2003, revised by EDEPC quality control eng. eng. Samir Abd El-Gelil, accepted by general director eng. Sobhi Elbakry, and approved by eng. Moneer Elwakil: head sector of  Damietta combined cycle  power station

Technical Data
Design temperature 340 oC
Design Pressure 9 BAR
Operation temperature 110/120 oC
Peration Pressure 0.4 /1  BAR
Capacity 182 m3

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance stuff should be carry the suitable safety category

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area is required

  • Wear your safety helmet, safety shoes …. etc

  • The HRSG (1&2 ) are out of service & depressurized the low pressure section

  • Drain the dearator  and stop & put red tag on the condensate  pumps

  • The inlet dampers are closed & locked mechanical when the gas turbine in service

  • Disconnect the power supply for the damper actuators

  • Seal air fan is in service when the gas turbine in service

Tools and Equipment

  • TIG welding machine    

  • Grinder & grinding wheel

  • Selected filler metal for TIG welding

  • Cleaning tools & keys kit

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Internal inspection for evidence of corrosion or scaling .

  2. Spray valve plugs must not bind, valve should seat tightly

  3. Inspect the tray banks . repair the defect

  4. Inspect the distributor pans, repair the defect

  5. The vessel should be cleaned of all debris or foreign bodies

  6. Check packing of controllers and valves, replace if necessary

  7. Check manhole gasket if there is evidence of leaks

  8. Check operation of all controllers, they should move freely and not have excessive play. 

  9. Make any necessary adjustments, paying particular attention to the overflow valve and controller as this is not used frequently and may have a tendency to corrode and freeze in position

  10. Open and close all gate valves that have not been used since last inspection. Lubricate when necessary .

  11. Inspect all piping connections for evidence of corrosion

  12. Inspect insulation

  13. After unit is returned to service, oxygen testing should be performed with more frequency to ascertain that the vent setting is correct.

Problem

Suggest

If outlet water free oxygen content is bigger than 0.005 ml/ liter or 7 ppb

Verify :

a. spray valves

b. deaerating trays

c. inlet steam press.

d. make up water flow ; it may not meet the  

    specification .

e. storaged water level , if deaerating heater 

     and storage tank are a compact design

f. vent valves ; whether they are opened and

     steam flaming is the right one

If hammering is produced inside

the deaerating heater

Verify :

a. deaerator temp. ; make up water flashing  

    could have been produced

b. storaged water level ; most probably it is too 

     high and produces instantaneous steam  

     condensation

c. inlet press. ,so that it flows continuously 

    and not in an intermittent way

d. inlet steam pressure

If deaerating heater press. And temp. are too high and therefore, too much steam is coming out for the vent valves

Verify :

a. inlet steam pressure

b. condensate inlet temp.

c. make up water flow as well as water

    pressure; most probably water pressure is

    too low and therefore ,make up water does 

    not flow

d. storaged water level, if deaerating heater  

    and storage tank are in one unit

If outlet water temperature is too low

Verify :

a. inlet steam pressure

b. heating steam and nozzles, if storage tank is

    a unit different to deaerator

c. deaerator inlet pressure steam control valve

d. make up water flow
