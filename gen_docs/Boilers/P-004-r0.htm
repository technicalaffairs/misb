Introduction

This document outlines the low pressure feed water pump general nspection steps I2, which must be carried out when there is abnormal sound in the pump and high vibration on bearings or discharge pressure < 9.5 bar at max. flow of the pump.

The pump technical data:
           Type:   single stage centrifugal pump               R.P.M   :     2900
        No of  stage        :    One stage                       Power   :     37 K W
        Suction press.      :    1.8  bar                      Max. flow  :    80 m3/ h
        Discharge press.  :    10 bar

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area,

  • Wear safety helmet, shoes.

  • Shut down the pump.

  • Disconnect the power supply & rack out the c.b

  • Put red tag on the pump

  • Isolate suction and outlet valves for the pump.

  • Drain the pump and lines

  • Isolate the minimum flow line.

  • The safety department must check the isolation and surrounding the zone of work.

    •

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set

  • Mechanical seal & oil seal 

  • Cleaning fluid

 

Work to be Carried Out

Dismantling

Note:

This operation is exceptional and should be carried out only in case of trouble occurring in pump itself, or in case of maintenance inspection. It requires complete motor-pump disassembling. Perform it in works.

Generates

  1. Refer to sectional drawing of pump.

  2. Disconnect motor electric supply.

  3. Close valves on suction and discharge to isolate pump.

  4. Drain pump casing (Plug 831) and turn on the drain valve (Item 6) of bearing.

  5. Disconnect available auxiliary piping (cooling - manometer).

  6. Remove the protector cover and mattress with care.

  7. Remove the coupling guard and spacer parts (1),

  8. Release the bearing support foot (2).

  9. Let the pump casing remains fixed to the base plate and to its piping. 

Impeller inspection

  1. Dismantle the bearing housing by unscrewing the bolts that hold the seal box and the pump casing.

  2. Remove assembly (rotor, sealing box, bearing) from pump casing (3) (Operation facilitated by using jack screws).

  3. Unscrew the impeller nut (Item 430), rotation opposite to the rotation of the pump. 

  4. Withdraw tab washer (Item 4308).

  5. Remove the impeller (Item 200) and key (440).

Mechanical seal inspection

  1. Dismantle as follows:

  2. Unscrew the nuts (Item 6516) of the mechanical seat cover.

  3. Withdraw the housing (Item 600).

  4. Keep on dismantling the parts in the ordrer they come and be careful not to damage the joints
    (Also refer to mechanical seal particular instructions).

Ball bearings inspection 
 Dismantle as follows:

Packing side :

  1. Remove the deflector (Item 450).

  2. Remove the adapter (Item 503).

Drive side :

  1. Remove the half coupling sleeve and the key(Item 449) with a hub puller.

  2. Remove the bearing cover (Item 5101).

  3. Withdraw the shaft (Item 400) with its ball bearing from the packing side by exerting pressure on the shaft at the impeller side (Fig. 3)
        (It is recommended to use a soft metal cylinder or a piece of wood to avoid hard shock on the shaft end)

 

Reassembling

Generalities

  • If the disassembly has been done with care, (without shock and parts well referenced) the reassemble does not present any difficulties.

  • All parts must be carefully cleaned, then check that assembly location  are defectless so that the face fitting can be made without force.

  • Gasket seal compound must be used between the parts (Item 503 . 510 and 5101) and under the head of screws  (Item 5106, 5109).

  • If ever you change the ball bearings, use only the type recommended by our technical department. For their assembling as well as changing, referto manufacturer's recommendations,
         Generally, they are to be tightly fixed to the shaft and may slide only in the bracket.

Bracket reassembling

Assembly of the bearings is the same as for their disassembly but in the reverse order (for assembly as well as for disassembly use the sectional drawing).

Sealing Reassembly

Mechanical seal:

  1. Slide the seat cover along the shaft with its stationary seal ring and gasket.

  2. Check that the stationary face locking dowel stops the stationary seal ring from rotating.

  3. Assemble the rotating part of the seal on the sleeve.

  4. Assemble sleeve gasket (Item 4100) on shaft.

  5. Assemble on shaft the sleeve fitted with mechanical seal rotary part and asdriving pin.

  6. Assemble stuffing box (Item 600) (fitted with cooled cover (Item 608) and its gaskets), and lock it to adapter (Item 503) by means of the screws (Item 6009).

  7. Fasten seal cover on stuffing box (Item 600).

Notes:

  • Seal is now in its place and no lateral adjustment is needed.

  • During the first running hours it is possible to find a certain heating up and a slight leakage; there is nothing to worry about unless this increases.

  • Usually, heating up and leakage progressively decrease and disappear quite quickly

  • However, never tolerate a lasting leakage superior to 5 cm3/h.this would mean a defect while assembling or during the manufacture and it is then necessary to find the origin of the defect, by checking the different operations done till them.

Rreassembling

  1. Check parallelism between casing and sealing box friction faces when assembling the sub-units: Pump casing, sealing box, and casing with compound gaskets: (Fig. 4).

Trouble Shooting for Low Pressure Feeding Water Pump

Cause of Trouble

Suggest Remedies
Bearings badly worn

Check quiet running of pump. Check coupling alignment

Check oil quality and change if bad
Case wear rings badly worn. Fit new case wear rings. Check out-of-round (true running) of rotor. Check presence of foreign bodies In pump  
Impeller damaged or disintegrated. Fit new Impeller. Check suction head (cavitation).
Check system for presence of foreign bodies . 

Rubbing faces of mechanical seal rings badly worn or scored, 0-ring damaged.

Replace damaged components by new ones.
Check rotating components for true running (out-of-round).

Check materials used In respect of their suitability.
Check correct seating of seal components and look out for signs of leakage. 
Rotor vibrates. Check suction pressure (cavitation). Check coupling alignment. Check pump Internals for presence of foreign bodies.
High temp. of bearings Check Bearing and oil quality
Defect in cooling fluid supply to mechanical seal Check unobstructed  flow through cooling water freed line
