Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear your safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, shoes.... etc

  • Isolate the circuit breaker for the motor of this jockey pump.

  • Isolate the electrical cable feeding of the motor of this pump.

  • The pump must be isolated mechanically by closing suction and delivery valves.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set.

  • Cleaning fluid, manual puller

  • Suitable lifting device, feeler gauge

  • Hammer, Torque wrench

  • Dial indicator

Work to be Carried Out

Disassembly     (see figure 1)

  1. Remove the motor with its coupling half, remove the bolts holding pedestal (s1) to the bearing housing (99) and remove the pedestal.

  2. Remove the pump half coupling and key (46)

  3. Remove the bearing cap (4)

  4. Remove bearing lock washer (69) and bearing lock nut (22)

  5. Remove nuts and flat washers from the upper end of the bolts.

  6. Remove the bearing housing (99) by lifting it up ball bearing (18) will be removed from the shaft during this operation.

  7. Remove deflect (40) and spacing sleeve (14 B)

  8. Remove discharge casing (1D) with gland (17) and packing ring (13) by lifting the discharge casing vertically.

  9. Remove the shaft sleeve (14) for the packed pump

  10. Remove  last stage impeller (2) stage casing (1F). O ring ( 1H ). diffuser (5) and impeller key (32)

  11. Repeat step 11 for as many stages as are supplied until all are removed .

  12. Lift shaft (6) with impeller hub sleeve (34) out of the suction casing (1 C)

  13. Check the condition of the bearing surface of both the impeller hub sleeve (34) and the bearing bushing (39) located in the suction casing (1C) the max. recommended diametric clearance between the OD of the two parts is (0.15 mm) if the clearance is exceeded first check clearance with a new impeller hub sleeve (34) it will be necessary to replace the bushing (39) one or both parts also must be replaced if the surfaces are badly scored or worn to replace bushing (39) it is necessary to destroy the bushing by a suitable means since it was installed with an adhesive into the suction casing (1C) after the bushing removal the bore in the suction casing is to be thoroughly cleaned and dried and the replacement bushing (39) is to be coated on the bottom end and on its OD with an adhesive.

  14. Clean all parts check for wear and damage check for damage to impeller hub surfaces if bearing (18) requires replacement drive out of the bearing housing (99)

Reassembly    ( see figure 1 )

All original parts to be re-used must have been thoroughly cleaned and checked. New parts must be thoroughly cleaned.

CAUTION: Surfaces where "O" rings seat must be cleaned and free of burrs. Assembly starts at the suction end of the pump. The suction casing (1C) with a checked and usable bushing (39) in place is placed on its base for assembly of the pump vertically from upper surface.

  1. Thread impeller hub sleeve (34) on the lower end of the shaft (6) a tier applying an adhesive like Loctite type 270 to the threads to secure the sleeve against loosening. Note that the thread is left hand. Thread the impeller hub sleeve (34) up fully against the shoulder of the shaft.

  2. Install the first stage impeller key (32) and impeller (2) on the shaft.

  3. Insert the shaft (4) with the impeller (2) and sleeve (34) just installed into the bearing bushing (39) in the suction casing (1C) using a small amount of bearing grease on both the outside diameter of the sleeve (34) and the wearring surface of the impeller (2).

  4. Install  the first intermediate stage casing.(1F) with its O ring (1H)

  5. Repeat the installation of each succeeding stage with its impeller key, impeller, intermediate stage casing diffuser and "0" ring until all stages have been installed. The discharge casing (ID) is next installed being sure that the shaft sleeve key (32A) has first been installed in the shaft (6). Position the discharge casing with its number no/./.le in the correct position with respect to the suction no/./le. Install the shaft sleeve (14}or (14F) with its "O" ring (14E).

  6. Install either the packing rings if not split or the rotary for the mechanical seal followed by the gland or the mechanical seal flange with stationary element.

  7. Install spacing sleeve (14B) with its deflector (40). Re­install bearing housing, (99) with ball bearing (18).

  8. Install tie bolts with their nuts and flat washers and tighten loosely.

  9. Install bearing lock washer (69) and bearing lock nut (22). L'sing the keyway in the shaft to prevent rotation. Tighten the bearing lock nut (22) securely so that all parts on the shaft are tightly abutted to each other. Position a tang of the lock washer (69) in one of the lock nut grooves to lock the nut in position.

  10. Install bearing cap (41) with its cap screws.

  11. Install pedestal (81) on bearing housing (99), and re-install the motor and shaft couplings as covered by coupling installation instructions.

  12. Now tighten the tie bolts diagonally, never in sequence. to the torque values shown in the fastener torque values table.
