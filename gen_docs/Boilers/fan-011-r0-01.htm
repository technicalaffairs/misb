Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area,

  • Wear your safety helmet, shoes ... etc

  • Isolate the circuit breaker for the motor of this GR FAN

  • Isolate the electrical cable feeding of the motor of this GR FAN

  • The GR FAN must be isolated mechanically by closing suction and delivery valve

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set

  • Cleaning fluid, Manual puller,

  • Suitable lifting device. Feeler gauge

  • Hammer, Dial indicator, Tourqe wrench 

Work to be Carried Out

Monthly

  1. Check the bearing oil level and adjuste as required

  2. Check the main bearing temperature. refer to start - stop sequence for alarm and trip temp

  3. Check the bearing cooling water flow

  4. Check the lube oil lins for oil line leakage

  5. Check the fan for smooth operation

  6. Check all anchor bolts and support steel fasteners bolts, and tighten if necessary

  7. Check the bearing oil for contamination

  8. Check the fan blades for waer

Every 6 Month

  1. Examine rotor and blade liners for any signs of erosion and remove all traces of deposit before carrying out this examination. Should there be evidence of severe abrasion, If there isn`t any severe abrasion, and the rotor is to be used again without repairs, check for fan balance.

  2. Examine fan housing and inlet box for signs of wear.

  3. Body bound bolts of rotor inner plate/shaft connection for wear

  4. Examine damper assemblies for waer of blades and blade spindles

  5. Examine bearings and couplings and make adjustments or replacements where necessary. Give particular attention to bearing seals

  6. Check position of rotor in fan housing relative to the inlet cone. Make adjustments where necessary. Check shaft seals at inlet box

  7. Check shaft seals at inlet box

  8. Tighten all hold down bolts in fan housing, sole plates, bearings, and base frames.

  9. Check coupling alignment
