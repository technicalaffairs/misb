Work to be carried out

 

Isolation

 

Notify "Operations" that the gauge is being taken out of service.

  1. It is not necessary to remove the gauge from its flanged mounting for normal port maintenance. However, for convenience it may be removed, if allowed by plant guidelines, and taken to the shop area by using the steam and water valves to isolate from drum pressure.

  2. Remove the gauge from drum service and depressurize by alternately closing the upper and lower  gauge valves tightly, opening the drain valve and drain the water from the gauge. For temperature equalizer gauge installations. *the downcomer valve must be closed.

Warning:

The drain valve must always remain open while it out of service for any reason. The ideal drain line should exhaust to an accumulator or collector tank which is vented to atmosphere.

Exhausting to a pressurized tank could result in a back pressure on the gauge and present a hazardous condition to personnel when performing maintenance on the equipment.

Disassembly

  1. Remove the cover plate(s), glass, packing strip, and cushion gasket (as an assembly)  from the centerplate.

  2. Remove the mica set, sealing gasket.

Inspection / Cleaning

  1. Careful examination of each cover plate is required in order to determine its physical condition.

  2. The top or flat portion of the cover plate ring is the mica bearing surface and must, therefore, be free from radial scratch marks or other imperfections that may cause leakage. This surface must be flat and smooth. To assure flatness of this surface, it should be lapped on a flat plate using 600 grit paper.

  3. The surface in the bottom of the glass pocket where the glass cushion gasket rests must be flat, smooth, and free of previous gasket residue. This surface may be cleaned with a soft wire brush.

  4. At the front side of the cover plate, the four counter bored cap screw seats are to be free of rust, dirt and thread lubricant residue. These may be cleaned with a wire brush.

 

 

 

  1. Removal of the used mica and gasket reveals port bottom (gasket seating surface), clearly indicates that it requires the removal of gasket material deposits and any other imperfection that would affect proper gasket sealing.

  2. A port refinishing tool shall be used to clean and polish the port bottom in order to obtain a totally flat surface with the correct finish. This tool is available from Diamond Power Specialty Company as part number 108586-2033.

  3. By using this tool and a variable speed drill for polishing, the correct sealing surface can be attained.
     

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Finishing Tool Assembly

 

The finished port seal surface must be flat, clean and free of all scratches and irregularities. The surface shall approximate a 32 finish. Maximum pocket depth must not exceed .536"

Assembly

  1. Preassemble the cover plate by first installing the cushion gasket in the bottom of the glass pocket. Then install new glass.

  2. Now that the port bottom is polished and cleaned and the tapped holes rechased, assembly of the port can begin. The sealing gasket is installed first in the center plate pocket, then the mica.set. All pieces of mica supplied in the kk must be used.

  3. Installation of the mica sets is very important. Be sure to put the mica piece, with the water soluble dot, towards the steam/water side (thickest of the multi piece mica set). The thickest piece can better accommodate gasket loads and, therefore, promote longer mica life. Be sure to handle the mica with clean hands to lengthen the life of these parts and promote good visibility. Keep the mica clean from lubricants, finger prints, and moisture.


     

  4. Next, place the previously assembled cover plate assembly over these components in the pocket. these components in the pocket

  5. Install bolts finger tight while holding the cover plate flat against the gasket. This will assure that the cover plate starts straight.

  6. Cover plate torquing is accomplished using the pattern shown. Torque to 25 ft-lbs in 5 ft-lb increments. The alter­nate criss-cross pattern will provide an equal distribution of load on the seal­ing gasket. Multiple passes with the torque wrench at final torque (25 ft-lbs) is required to ensure an equal gas­ket deflection. Observe the socket dur­ing the final torquing and continue the pattern passes until no movement of the socket occurs.

    NOTE: Under no circumstances should the cover plate bolts be torqued higher than 25 ft-lbs
