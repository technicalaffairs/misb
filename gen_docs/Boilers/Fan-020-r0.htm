Introduction

This document outlines the maintenance procedures P1, which must be performed on an annually bases as indicated by Y1 in the title block.

The first draft initiated by eng. Ekramy Mostafa Mohamad, accepted by technical expert eng Hassan El-Behiry, and approved by eng. Fathi El Sayed head sector of Kurimat PS.

This document certified by eng. Gamal El-Din Abdel Wahab UEEPC general director of inspection & quality control and MPIS Coordinator

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Maintenance staff should be will trained and qualified or directly supervised by some one who is.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear your safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, breathing mask, safety glass ...etc

  • Disconnect all power to motor prior to initiating any maintenance or repairs.

  • Avoid contact with rotating parts.

  • Act with care in accordance with prescribed procedures, in handling and lifting this equipment.

  • Be sure shaft key is fully captive before unit is energized.

  • Always be sure oil lubricated units are filled with correct oil to proper level before operating.

  • Provide proper safeguards for personnel against rotating parts and applications involving high inertia loads which can cause over speed

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set.

  • Cleaning fluid, feeler gauge

  • Suitable lifting device.

Work to be Carried Out

Permanent Checks

  1. The indications given by the temperature measuring devices, and by the other protection devices, must be in accordance with the values given by connection diagram.

  2. Check that there is no abnormal noise or vibration.

  3. Check the condition of the different parts of the machine in case of abnormal operation (for example: heating, abnormal noise, abnormal current ...), to dismantle it partially or completely and to overhaul it.

  4. Clean the inside of the machine, the rotor and stator ventilation ducts and the windings by dry, oil free compressed air, maximum pressure 3 bars.

  5. If the machine is too dirty, it can be cleaned with a cloth impregnated with turpentine.

  6. Check the insulation resistance of the windings after cleaning.

Air Filter Cleaning

  1. Unscrew bolts securing the air inlet panels to the ventilation hood and remove the panel including the hood.

  2. Clean the filter with compressed air or with water to remove the dusts.

  3. Use water with addition of sodium carbonate if the filter is too dirty.

  4. Use no other detergent.

  5. Dry the filter completely with warm air before machine start-up.

Preliminary Operations before Dismantling

  1. Disconnect all cables: supply, protections, accessories …

  2. Uncouple the machine.

  3. Remove the coupling flange.

  4. Remove the protection devices and the accessories of the bearings. (Temperature probes). DE and NDE

Supports for Dismantling

  1. Use two supports when dismantling a bearing, to remove the parts out of the shaft:

  2. A support "A" placed at the end of the shaft, or eventually at the end of the extension shaft.

  3. A support "B" placed as close as possible to the frame.

  4. If there is not enough space for a support, replace it with a sling.

  5. Make sure that the supports do not damage the machined parts of the shaft.

To Take a Part Out of the Shaft:

  1. Bring the bearing part next to the "A" support

  2. Put the "B" support in place, remove the "A" support and then remove the bearing part.

  3. Put the "A" support back in place and remove the "B" support

  4. If there is enough place, several parts can be removed simultaneously

Dismantling of Parts Assembled

With Sealing Paste

  1. Loosen these parts like (hoods, housing) after their securing bolts are removed.

  2. For that operation threaded holes are provided:
    - In covers, hoods and housings.
    - In end shields to loosen them.

Rotor Dismantling

  1. Carry out the preliminary operations before dismantling and dismantle the bearings in order to remove the rotor.

  2. Prepare a steel tube and put it in the shaft to handle the rotor.

  3. Measure the inside diameters of the tube "should be between 3 to 6 mm greater than the shaft diameters".

  4. Put a thin copper sheet (between 0.5 and 1 mm) lining its inside in order to protect the machined parts.

  5. Length of tube fitted to the shaft: about 350 mm.

  6. Tube length about 1500 mm.

  7. Place slings at both ends of rotor + tube and remove the assembly.

  8. Avoid all stator-rotor contact, which could damage the magnetic circuits and the windings.

Remove the Rotor

  1. Lay the rotor on a mobile trolley.

  2. Remove the slings.

  3. Take the trolley with the rotor away from the stator and remove the handling tube.

 

   

 

 

 

Reassembly

  1. Reassemble the various pieces in reverse an order of the steps used for dismantling.

  2. During reassembly take care that insulated parts do not scrape against iron parts and that the rotor does not scrape against the stator.

  3. Check the cleanliness of the various parts.

  4. Put oil on the surfaces in contact with the shaft and on the sleeve.

  5. Carefully clean the machined surfaces covered with sealing paste and put some more paste (Type Hylomar 32M or H for instance) before reassembly.

  6. Make sure to put paste on both sides that have to be jointed, place it evenly in thin coats free of lumps.

  7. Check that all bolts are well tightened and put the bolt stops.

Bearing Lubrication       (regard to lubricant oil equivalence chart at the last page)

Oil Lubricated Bearing Self Lubricated

  1. Each bearing is provided with an oil sight glass with the indication of "mini" and "maxi" oil level.

  2. Check The oil level must be between those two marks, motor in idle condition.

  3. Bearing oil and impurities must be periodically drained before to fill new oil.

  4. For that purpose the bearing is provided with an oil drain plug.

  5. Oil must be filtered, or new oil must be put:
    6 hours, then 150 hours after the first start-up, then the recommended draining interval is : 8000 hours.

  6. Recommended oil quantity according to ISO VG 46 specifications is 13 liters for Driving End bearing and 8 litres for Non-Driving end bearing.

  7. Check periodically the oil quality, specially after the first start-up.

  8. Oil must be changed as soon as its color or its viscosity is altered.

Putting the Equipment into Service

Checks Before Start-up

  1. The rotor locking device is removed.

  2. The rotor can easily turned.

  3. The frame is earthed, to protect the personnel in the event of a fault in the insulation.

  4. The electric connections are in accordance with the connection diagram.

  5.  The cables are strongly secured on the terminals.

  6. The thermal and electrical protection are working and are correctly adjusted.

  7.  The stator insulation resistance in normal.

  8. Oil has been put in the bearings.

Start-up

  1. During the first revolution check that:

  2. The direction of rotation is correct.

  3. There are no abnormal vibration on the bearings.

  4. There no noise caused by a contact between fixed and moving parts.

  5. Check the bearing temperatures after running 3 hours.
