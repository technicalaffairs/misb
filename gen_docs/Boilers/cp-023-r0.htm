Introduction

This document out lines the equipment inspection steps I1 required for the two-stage piston compressor, which must be performed every 2000 operating hours as indicated in the above title block.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence (rope) with caution marks surrounding the work area,

  • Wear safety helmet, shoes.

  • Isolate the circuit breaker for the motor of this compressor.

  • Isolate the electrical cable feeding of the motor of this compressor.

  •  The compressor must be isolated mechanically by closing delivery valve.

  • Close the cooling water supply.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set.

  • Cleaning fluid.

  • Manual puller, Feeler gauge.

Work to be Carried Out 

  1. Check of tightening of all bolted connections, namely connections of the crankshaft with a flywheel and a coupling bolts of frame bearings and of connecting rod bolts, tightening of piston nuts and of piston rods. ( See figure 1 )

Check of wear of piston rings - exchange when necessary

  1. Check of piston rings is carried out visually, when they are mechanically damaged, exchange them

  2. Worn piston rings result in a drop of the delivered medium. In this case it is necessary to exchange a complete set of piston rings.

Attention

Before Starting Mounting the Piston Ring on a Piston

  1. Check clearance in a lock after fitting into cylinder.

  2. A small clearance after heating to opr. temperature may cause a bearing of both ends of piston rings and may cause an excessive wear , resp. seizing in cylinder .

  3. A big clearance may cause non tightness and decrease of the capacity . ( see figure 2 )

  4. Check also side clearance between piston rings and a groove of piston ring should move freely.

  5. A big side clearance causes tilting of rings and its wear, a small clearance and thus non- movability of rings in a groove cause a seizing.

  6. Rings are to be exchanged also when maximum wear reached the values mentioned in records. ( see figure 2 )  

  7.  Clearances in locks of piston rings are also mentioned in records. ( see figure 2 )

Inspection of working valves in all stages,

and of non-return valve resp. exchange of plates and springs

  1.  Take out and dismount working valves and clean their individual parts in kerosene by fine brush – remove sediments and impurities.

  2. Be careful to protect the individual parts of valves against damage and deformations.

  3. Try to prevent their mutual change.

  4. Keep an uniform tightening of holding down screws and of screws in valve covers in order to prevent a crossing of valve lanterns and of valves.

 

 

Check the Function of Safety Valves

  1. Check there is no interval leakage.

  2. Check spring function.

  3. Recalibrate the safety valves according to pressure 1st stage safety valve P=0.33 Mpa & 2nd stage safety valve P= 1.59 Mpa .

Check Function of Pressure Gauges and Thermometers, Recalibrate if necessary

  1. Clean thermometer pockets.

Maintenance of glands of shut- off control elements

  1. Maintain the shut – off  and control fittings to be in a perfect condition.

  2. Check the tightness of control spindles and tighten the gland if necessary, respectively exchange the packing.

Cleaning of oil filter and of slot filter

  1. Clean the oil filter in frame by trichloro-ethylene or by technical benzene.

  2. Dry and remount the washed filter .

  3. Clean the slot filter after a certain operation period, stop the oil supply, remove filter and wash by benzene.

  4. Tighten the gland bolt and exchange the packing or exchange the packing ring to prevent any possible oil escape round a spindle.

  5. Exchange the ring on a dismounted filter after taking down a handle.

  6. Apply a thin layer of grease on a spindle and slide it, before putting on a new ring.

  7. Be careful during handling the lips and lamella to avoid any defect. ( see fig. 1 )

Cleaning of suction air filter

  1. Maintain the suction filter, mainly clean and wet the cylinder filter inserts and repaint.

  2. Dirty inserts are washed in worm water with detergent

  3. Clean dry insertion pieces are wetted in low – congealing oil.

  4. After oil dripping – approx. after 2-3 hours the inserts remounted. ( see FIG 1)

  Check of wear of packing glands

  1. Worn seal is indicated by whistling / blowing during the set run.

  2. Always exchange complete seal as an unit.

Check of control valve and of pressing – off device

  1. In case of any abnormal noise in regulating valves and depressing device of suction valves, disassemble the defects values and maintain or replace it by a new one.

  2. Check movability and tightness of pistons in the bush of control valve casing and in bushes of covers of suction valves.
