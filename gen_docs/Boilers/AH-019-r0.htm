Introduction

This document lists major maintenance procedures the P1, to be performed every five years as indicated by Y5 in the title block.

The ljungstrom air heater function is to absorb waste heat from flue gas, then transfer this heat to incoming cold air to increase the temperature of the incoming combustion or air process system

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued.

  • The maintenance staff must be qualified and carry the suitable safety ID category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required,

  • Wear safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes gloves etc.

  • The air heater must be isolated mechanically and electrically.

  • Be sure the cooling water of the upper and lower bearing is closed

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set.

  • Cleaning fluid, feeler gauge, hammer

  • Manual puller, torque wrench

 

    

Work to be Carried Out 

Before isolating the Air Heater Mechanically and Electrically

  1. Check any abnormal noise or vibration.

  2. Check any air or flue gases leakage from manholes.

  3. Check the oil level of upper guide bearing.

  4. Check the oil level of lower support bearing

  5. Check the oil level of drive unit.

  6. Check the oil level of drive unit for blowing equipment.

  7. Check auxiliary drive in service for a few minutes.

After isolating the Air Heater Mechanically and Electrically

  1. Wash air preheater at the cold end and hot end to clean it in out the unit until the PH of drain water equal washing water PH= 8

  2. Vent the water cooling system for guibe sporting bearing in top bottom end and clean it if needed .

  3. Check sealing clearance according to table No. 1 and figure No. 6

  4. Check the basket and chining it if needed

  5. Check the soot blower system in every air heater and spray all  nasal if needed

  6. Check the fir fitting system in every air heater and spray all nozal if needed .

  7. Check the washing system upper and lower every air heater and spray all nozal if needed .

  8. Check alignment limits for coupling

Replacement of Heat Transfer Surface

Cold End Surface Figure 3,4,5

  1. Open rotor housing cold end basket removal door.

  2. Rotate rotor to line up rotor basket removal door with opening in rotor housing.

  3. Remove cover from rotor basket

  4. Remove cold end element baskets the number of cold end basket to be removed and replaced in each sector varies with the size of the air preheater.

  5. A piece of rod or pipe with a hook on one fit slot in basket wrapper and a hook on the other end will be required to remove and replace baskets. Rod or pipe should be long enough to reach inner most basket

  6. Install new baskets starting with inner most baskets, which designated as “A” basket.

  7. Install sector cover using sealing compound under cover

  8. Rotate rotor to adjacent sector, remove cover and ensure element baskets have been replaced.

  9. Review the thickness of elements in cold end baskets

Hot and / or Intermediate Baskets Surface    Figure 3,4,5

  1. Hot and/or Intermediate element baskets may be removed from air outlet or gas inlet duct just above air pre heater as to which of these ducts is used depends on duct configuration and access.

  2. Once has been established as to which duct baskets are to be removed . Layout and burn opening in duct of sufficient size to accommodate largest element basket preferred location is center of long side of duct.

  3. Remove hot end radial and circumferential seals.

  4. Install monorail or similar arrangement in line with . and just above . Opening in duct for use of hoist in removing present basket and installing new baskets.

  5. Rotate rotor so that sector lines up with opening and monorail.

  6. Using a two line sling with right angle hooks of ¼ ” material which will slip under basket holding bars. Remove baskets from rotor compartments and transfer to out side of air pre heater .

  7. Install new element baskets. Repeat this procedure until all element baskets have been replaced.

  8. Reapply and set to proper clearances radial and circumferential seals.

  9. Remove hoist and close up opening in duct.

Disassembly of Rotor Support Bearing    Figure 7

  1. The process of disassembly of the support bearing maintenance or exchange if defected

  2. Disconnect the drive motor from met work and secure it.

  3. Drain oil from bearing housing .

  4. Disconnect oil piping.

  5. Remove oack strap and packing (10).

  6. Remove packing retainer and packing from bearing housing cover (6)

  7. Remove bearing housing cover.

  8. Install normal jacks and jacking beams to support the rotor fig No. (8) four jacks capacity 50 ton .

  9. Remove three (3) bearing housing bolts apart replace by all thread rods long and must tighten must to support bearing housing (6)

  10. Remove balance of support bolts.

  11. Back off must on all-thread rods to lower bearing housing keep it level as it is lowers. upper race of bearing (12) should remain on Truman (1).

  12. If bearing is to be replaced, remove upper race of bearing (12) from Truman (1) by puller or heat race to remove it

Assembly of Rotor Support Bearing    Figure 7

  1. Clean every parts well.

  2. Assembly every part in reverse sequence of disassembly.

Disassembly of Rotor Guide Bearing    figure 9

  1. The process of Disassembly of the guide bearing maintenance or exchange

  2. Disconnect the drive motor from met work and secure it.

  3. Drain oil from bearing housing and remove the packing (15)

  4. Remove bearing housing cover (3).

  5. Remove bolts which secure locking cap (9) plate lock washers and locking cap (9).

  6. Screw three eye bolts in to tapped holes in top of trunnion sleeve for use in removing the sleeve.

  7. Remove trunnion sleeve (7) with bearing (2) by ail pressure pump. Attaching a block hawk to a tapped hole in the top of trunnion (4) keep tension on the sleeve while apply.

  8. The bearing is secured to the sleeve, comprises a locknut, lock washer and sleeve. To replaced the bearing, bend out ears of lock washer and remove lock nut. Turn the sleeve bottom side up and tab the inner bearing race to remove bearing from sleeve.

Assembly of Rotor Guide Bearing   figure 9

  1. Clean every parts well.

  2. Assembly every part in reverse sequence of disassembly.

Adjustment of Rotor Sealing in Cold State

  1. The sealing system are comprised of radial seal, circumferential seal and rotor past seals .

  2. Radial seal figures (6) .

  3. Replace and adjust each radial seal individually according to the following procedure figure (6) .

  4. Shut down the heater and the drive and secure it.

  5. Attach the seal straight-edge to the support channels welded inside the connecting plate.

  6. Set the seal straight-edge to the trailing edge of on existing radial seal.

  7. Then remove the seal.

  8. Install the seal so it touches across the full length of seal straight-edge (Item #3) tighten the inner and outer bolts of each seal section first (Item #4), then tighten the remaining bolts .

  9. Check the seal clearance at both sector plates by referring to seal clearance table 1 adjust the seal return the seal to the straight-edge and reset the straight-edge.

  10. Set the remaining radial seals to the straight-edge.

Circumferential Seal              figure 6

  1. Replace and adjust each radial seal individually according to the following procedure:

  2. Shut down the air-heater and drive and secure it.

  3. Remove present seals and holding strips.

  4. Install new seal leaves so that the outer leaf overlaps the inner leaf such that the bend slots are not exposed .

  5. Locate seals on sealing angle in accordance with dimensions gives on cearance seal in table 1 the cold end circumferential seal should just contact the sealing angle.

  6. The hot end circumferential seal are to be set the specified clearance from the sealing angle.

  7. If the sealing angles hove grooved by seals the new seals are to be set at specified clearance in grooved.

Rotor Post Seals      figure (6)

  1. Remove present seals for rotor.

  2. Install new seals in same location.
        

Exchange of Toothed Wheel or the Drive   figure 10

The driving toothed wheel (1) of the rotor or the regenerative air heater with worn or damaged teeth must be exchanged. The process is following:

  1. Disconnect the drive motor from network and secure it against incompetent starting.

  2. Dismantle the rotor drive including the bracket from the stator housing with the use of a pulley block.

  3. Draw off the toothed wheel from the gearbox shaft with use of a draw off jig.

  4. Clean the shaft end, lubricate it with graphite and fit on a new toothed wheel on it . Apply slip-on flanges and screws screwed in the tapped hole in the shaft face.

  5. Secure the toothed wheel on the shaft with a washer and screws.

  6. Mount the drive with the bracket back on the stator housing so as to enable the toothed wheel meshing into the rotor pin gearing . the clearance between the toothed wheel foot and the pin of the rotor pin gearing should be 15.5 mm in cold state . Adjust the clearance bey rotor hand turning according to figure 7 along the whole circumference of the pin gearing it should be checked by means of a gauge in several spots of the circumference of rotor pin gearing, the rim of the pin gearing is not accurately circular.

  7. Check the oil charge of the gearbox, if necessary refill exchange to the oil.

  8. Having finished the drive assembly, checked the oil charge and adjusted the rum of the regenerative air heater.

Instructions for Lubricant exchange

  1. Before the first filling or the bearings or gearboxes with a lubricating charge/ grease or oil / all parts and surfaces being in contact with the lubricant must be cleaned orderly having cleaned the surfaces, wash them with kerosene or with special flushing oil .

  2. Don’t use cloth or rage releasing fibers for cleaning the bearings and sliding surfaces during the bearing assembly observe the possibly highest cleanness leave the bearings in the original packing till using them for the assembly.

  3. Apply always the lubricants of the same quality for the sieve lubricating spot if it’s necessary to apply some other lubricant quality to be settled with special technical service of the lubricant supplier remove first all residues of original lubricant, and wash the whole lubricating space orderly.

Return Regenerative Air Heater Into Service

  2. Ensure that the regenerative air heater is OK and record the readings of clearance, temperature, and vibration….etc and compare with reference values.
