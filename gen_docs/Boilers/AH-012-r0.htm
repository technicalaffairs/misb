Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection checklist I1, which must be performed every two years as indicated by Y2  in the title block.

Each Mazout fuel oil tank is equipped with a suction heater, which is a flooded type open to storage tank it's used to heat Mazout in the low temperature conditions and maintain the Mazout oil viscosity required for the proper operation, the medium of the Mazout is the auxiliary steam

Each Mazout daily tank is provided with a fuel oil heater to maintain the Mazout oil viscosity required for the proper operation of the burner fuel oil P/Ps the fuel oil heater uses the steam as a heating medium

Safety Precautions

  • Work order must be issued and the maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear safety helmet, shoes, gloves … etc.

  • Isolate the control circuit breaker for heat exch.

Tools and Equipment

  • Standard tool set.

  • Contact thermometer.

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Inspect for corrosion especially around connections and under inspection clean the corroded parts or repair  and plug any leaked tubes or repaint.

  2. Check inlet and outlet temperature, so clean the inner faces of the tubes from dirties or strange parts.

  3. Check abnormal noise, so repair the defected tubes or control the steam flow.

  4. Check steam flow rate from the auxiliary steam system at 1.17 kg/s

  5. Check steam trap conditions and change any defected parts from the internal parts of the trap.

  6. Retightened cover bolts if there is leakage.
