Introduction

This document outlines the major maintenance procedures P2, which must be performed every five years indicated by Y5 in the above title block.

 

The soot blower used for cleaning superheater, reheater, economizer tubes, and regenerative air heater baskets.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required,

  • Wear your safety helmet, shoes ... etc

  • Isolate the circuit breaker for the motor of this soot blower

  • Isolate the electrical cable feeding of the motor of this soot blower

  • The soot blower must be isolated mechanically by closing suction and delivery valve

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set

  • Cleaning fluid, Manual puller,

  • Suitable lifting device Feeler gauge

  • Hammer, Air wrench      

Work to be Carried Out

Adjustment with Solid Ring Packing

  1. Run the carriage forward (a few inches) to provide a working clearance between the bulkhead and the rear of the SG (solid gland)

  2. Thread the SG out of the hub until the first locking slot (total of 6) aligns with a locking notch (total of 4) in the rear of the hub

  3. Connect the motor power supply and return the carriage to the rear stop position Precautions must be taken to avoid binding with the poppet valve turn on linkage and/or hard stop the SG into the poppet valve mounting hardware Open the blowing medium isolation valve and pressurize the piping upstream of the poppet valve

  4.  Notify "Operations" that the soot blower will be test run

  5.  Run the soot blower, check for leakage or other problems   Make corrections/repairs as required

  6. If there is no leakage or other problems found, notify "Operations" that the soot blower may be placed into normal service

Solid Ring Packing Replacement 

It is recommended that complete packing set be replaced for maximum service life, since the total packing set height for the 5 ring set (2 end rings and 3 seal rings) is 18" (4572 mm) and the SG can adjust for about 75" (1905 mm)

  1. Notify "Operations" that the soot blower is being removed from remote service control

  2. Isolate the Mowing medium upstream of the poppet valve and relieve any trapped pressure Precautions should be taken to prevent any pressure buildup from an upstream isolation valve

  3. Disconnect the poppet valve turn on linkage and run the carriage forward approximately 10-12 inches (254 mm - 3048 mm)

  4.  Disable the motor power supply

  5. Remove the locking key from the SG

  6. Using the spanner wrench, unthread the SG from the hub

  7. disconnect the feed tube from the poppet valve and slide it towards the carriage Do this until there is adequate clearance between the end of the feed tube and bulkhead to remove the SG assembly and packing

  8. Remove the SG assembly by sliding it off of the feed tube

  9. If the packing set is only partially being replaced, remove the rear rings as required The rear end ring must be replaced when adding rings Do not damage/disturb the remaining rings or the front bushing

  10. If the complete packing set is being replaced, remove the packing rings and the front bushing The front bushing designed for use with the 5 ring packing set has two threaded holes (#10-24 @ 180°) for pulling the bushing from the carriage hub

  11. In some situations it may be necessary to support the hub end of the lance tube and unbolt it from the carriage By doing this, the feed tube can be pushed through the carriage hub to remove the front bushing and packing Also, the hub is clear for cleaning

  12. Remove the packing rings with a flexible packing hook Direct the hook to avoid damaging the feed tube or hub bore

     

  13. Clean the hub cavity packing bore and the internal threads to remove any debris/residue A solvent may be required to remove all residue

  14. Inspect the front bushing, the rear bushing and clean or replace as required The rear bushing is lightly pressed into the SG

  15. Slide the feed tube through the carriage hub and connect the lance flange to the hub flange A new sealing gasket must always be used The feed tube may be pulled by using a short piece of bar or tubing with an 0 D slightly  smaller than the feed tube I D The use of 0 D gripping devices can damage the 0 D sealing surface

  16. Install the front bushing over the feed tube (small pilot diameter towards hub) and into the hub cavity The flat end of the packing tool assembly may be used to seat the bushing at the hub shoulder It may help to slightly raise the feed tube to pilot the bushing into the small hub bore If the bushing is not seated in the hub properly, the packing and SG will be out of position

  17. Remove any raised metal on the feed tube 0 D with crocus cloth over a file or use a fine stone Check the end, mounting threads or snap ring groove, P/N and logo marking

  18. Before placing each section of the sealing ring packing over the feed tube, place the I D of the conical ring over the thumbnail and while rotating the packing slightly roll the sharp comer of the packing which first contacts the feed tube Remember that the packing seal rings are conically pointed towards the front busing This slightly increases the diameter and makes a nice lead-in, and prevents damage of the packing I D

  19. Slide each ring of packing (use proper sequence and bevel towards the front bushing) to the full in position and seat it with 3 to 4 solid impacts of the packing tool The bevel on the end of the packing tool must match the bevel of the packing rings The split end rings must be installed with the single skive cuts overlapping properly

  20. Slide the SG over the feed tube and thread into the hub until it makes contact with the packing Do not tighten

  21. Connect the feed tube and poppet valve as directed in the soot blower instruction manual A new sealing gasket, proper lubrication and proper torquing must always be used

  22.  Using the spanner wrench, thread the SG into the hub until there is adequate preload on the packing or use the spanner wrench with a torque wrench and torque the gland to 180 in-lb (2033 NM) For specific torque instructions,

  23.  Thread the SG out of the hub until the first locking slot (total of 6) aligns with a locking notch (total of 4) in the rear of the hub

  24. Install the locking key using the ??10-32 screw and lock washer Make certain the locking key is fully engaged in the notch of the hub before tightening the screw

  25. connect the motor supply and return the carriage to the rear stop position Precautions must be taken to avoid binding with the poppet valve turn on linkage and/or hard stop the SG into the poppet valve mounting hardware Open the blowing medium isolation valve and pressurize the piping upstream of the poppet valve

  26. Notify "Operations" that the soot blower will be test run

  27. Run the soot blower, check for leakage or other problems   Make corrections/repairs as required

  28. If there is no leakage or other problems found, notify "Operations" that the soot blower may be placed into normal service

 

Solid Gland Torque Instructions

Torquing of the solid gland requires the use of the spanner wrench and torque wrench with a 1/2" drive The net torque value applied to the solid gland is based on two factors: 

Working Wrench Length

The working length is a feature of the specific torque wrench design and is not the actual physical length of the torque wrench

Offset Angle

  1. The offset angle is that angle between the axis of the spanner wrench handle and the axis of the torque wrench If the handle on the spanner wrench and the torque wrench are in line, the offset angle is 0°

  2.  The gland torque value of 180 in-lb (2033 NM) is based on a torque wrench with a "working length" of 65 inches (1651 mm) with a 0° "offset angle" with the spanner wrench handle
