Introduction

This document outlines the major maintenance procedure P1, which must be carried out when the discharge pressure value < 88 bar at max. flow or flow decreasing at normal pressure 88 bar.

The pump technical data:
          Type:   Centrifugal pump multistage            RPM         :    2980 
        No of  stage            :    (1+8)                    Power       :    789 KW
        Suction pressure      :    1.8  bar                 Max .flow :    266 m3/ h
        Discharge pressure  :    88 bar

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required,

  • Wear safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes ... etc.

  • Shut down the pump.

  • Disconnect the power supply & rack out the circuit breaker

  • Close the shut–off valves in the (suction & discharge & balancing & min .flow )

  • Put red tag on the pump

  • Let the pumps cool down to about < 80oC measured at the branches, then seal off the cooling water pipes and close all other supply lines.

  • Loosen all auxiliary lines, such as circulation, cooling liquid, lub. oil.

  • The safety department must check the isolation and surrounding the zone of work.

  • Cleaning fluid,

  • Bakelite for ELEC insulation & alignment devices & filler

Inspections after Disassembly

Plain Bearings

  1. Check all bearing components and segmental pads for damages. Light pressure marks on the pads resp. in the bore of the bearing shells may be removed by means of scraper with regard to  the max. admissible axial and radial clearances  

  2. Check the fits the bearings of the seats of plain bearings (370) in the bearing housing (350.01). When mounted in position the bearing shell should not be able to move in its seat if the seat is too  slack ( i.e. if it is possible to move the bearing shell ), the two faces of the bearing housing must be  touched up evenly until the bearing shell can once more be clamped absolutely tight in its seat. If new bearing shells are fitted ,their seats should in principle be fitted in the manner described   above

Thrust Bearing

  1. Check the rubbing pattern on the thrust bearing segments (387) and, if necessary, remove any light pressure marks or scores.

  2. Check the rubbing pattern on the thrust  bearing plate (384) on both   sides. Remove deep scores by machining. Admissible wobble of the thrust bearing plate 0.005   mm. Surface finish Ra = 0.4 mic. If the thrust bearing segments are replaced by new ones, or if  any extensive touching-up work of the white metal rubbing faces in carried out, an BLUING Ink.

  3. Check is to be carried out in assembled condition with the thrust bearing plate (384). When fitting a new thrust bearing plate, the wobble of the plate should be checked with a dial micrometer. The rotor should not abut the pump body.

Attention:

  1. Provide one segment (387.01) of each set of bearing segments with a measuring borehole for temperature control.

  2. Measure the borehole depth on the former segment.

  2. Slacken hexagonal nut M12 (920.06)

Shaft Seal (Figure 3)

  1. Unscrew socket head cap screws (914,12)

  2. Dismantle seal cover (471) together with stationary seat ring (472.22),

  3. Stationary seal ring holder (476),tappet (485.21) and spring (477.21).

  4. Remove retaining ring (932.02).

  5. Remove shaft sleeve (523.01) together with the mechanical seal parts from shaft (210) by means of a special tool UA  2  1475901.

  6. Remember threads of the rotating seal ring holder (473.01/02) can be left-hand or right-hand.

  7. Force off and take off the casing part (130) and casing for seal (441) on the discharge side.

  8. Observe not to damage the 0-rings (412.07/08)

  9. Remove the spacer sleeve (525.07) on the discharge side.

  10. On the suction side, remove the casing for seal (441).

  11. Remove spacer sleeve (525.06) on the suction side.

 

Inspections after Disassembly

  1. Replace the damaged shaft sleeves (523) and sealing parts by new ones, in the case of  slight scoring of the sealing faces, the rotating and stationary seal rings can be sent back to the manufacturer for re-work.

  2. Generally change only the two seal rings (472.21 and475.21) and the appertaining holders together.

Figure (3) Mechanical seal

Attention:

0-rings of EPDM based (ethylene-propylene-rubber) not come into contact with organic oil or grease, clean the 0-rings before final installation and wet them with silicon oil or soapy water, don't  install 0-rings when dry.

When fitting a new shaft seal, make sure the reference dimension is re-established by machining, if necessary. Generally change seal rings (472.21 and 475.21) only-together. 

 

 

Balancing Device

  1. Remove the screws (914.10),loosen the collar (505.01) and remove the multiple ring (501.01).

  2. Pull off the spacer ring (504.02) and 0-ring (412.02).

  3. Pull the balancing piston (603.02) off the shaft with a suitable device in acc.to working   procedureUA2 14760  01

  4. Loosen, press off and remove the throttle bush (542.02).

  5. Pull the balancing counter disk (602.02) out of the casing insert (13-6) by means of an  appropriate device 

Attention:

If you do not intend to dismantle the body of the pump measure the approximate total radial play . attach the indicator to a fixed  support (e.g. flange or discharge nozzle) and place its tip against the seat of the balancing piston (Fig. 4-1), Carefully raise the shaft to its upper dead center, ensuring that here no additional sagging of the shaft, as this could give incorrect measurements. 

The clearance measured should not exceed 1.0 mm If it does, the pump must be dismantled and overhauled The rotor clearances can only be measured with full accuracy when the pump is dismantled.

 

Figure (4): Measuring the rotor clearance

  1. Check the balancing piston (603.02), balancing counter disk (602.02) and throttle bush (542.02) for damage.

  2. When rework is carried out by means of a lathe mandrel, sufficient rubbing accuracy of the rubbing faces and bores is ensured (see Fig. 5-1). The admissible wobble is 0.02 mm.

  3. Reduce the length of  the balancing piston (603.02] at the pump-side front faces in such a way that the axial clearance of  0.8 -1.2 mm between impeller and balancing disk is re-established. The rework necessitates re-adjustment of the rotor.

  4. If wear due to running in the radial clearance between balancing counter disk (602.02) or throttle bush (542.02) and balancing piston (603.02) is to be removed by rework, the admissible clearances must be observed (Fig. 6-1). Individual deep scores may remain as they are.

General Maintenance

  1. Check Min. flow valve and change the defect parts .

  2. Check the coupling and repair or change  the defect parts .

  3. Check the clearance between impeller and casing.

  4. Check the impeller {cracks & wear & cavities } and then repair or change if necessary.

  5. Change the wear rings if necessary

  6. Change all lube oil .

  7. Cleaning the lube oil tank and oil lines and change the oil filters

    9. Check the main lube oil pump and change the defect parts .   

Assembly Steps

  1. Assembling the pump insert.

  2. Push the casing insert with the counter disc over the shaft  and lock by means of the disc.

  3. Adjust the rotor.

  4. Fit the shaft seal.

  5. Mount the radial bearing and raise the rotor.

  6. Fit the thrust bearing and realize bearing clearance.

  7. Now the pump insert is ready to be installed in the barrel casing.

  8. Make alignment.

   TROUBLE SHOOTING FOR HP. F. W. P

Cause of Trouble

Suggest Remedies

Faults at the Suction Side

 

1-Pump not properly vented, air pocket In

suction line, vapor bubble at auction end, lines not properly vented.

 

Open vent valves or pressure gauge vent screws, open isolating valves In minimum flow device circuit (the balance water lines must also be open). Check layout of pipelines to ensure that fluid flows smoothly. 

2-Pump or suction line Incompletely primed

with fluid. 

Prime pumps and piping again, and vent them thoroughly. Check layout of pipelines.

3-Insufficient pressure differential between

suction pressure and ,vapor pressure, NPSH

required Is not attained (observe rate of pressure decrease), 

Check Isolating valves and strainers In suction line. The Instrument readings taken must be accurate. Consult manufacturer. 

4. Mouth of auction line la too close to

 surface of liquid level In the suction

vessel, or liquid level In vessel is too low

Check water level In reservoir and examine possibility of altering It (raise water level, alter mouth of suction line). 

General faults In the Installation

Refer to manufacturer If pump Is motor driven. Check operation of minimum flow device. 

5-Rotational speed too low, or rate of minimum flow by-pass excessive. 

Refer to manufacturer If pump la motor driven 

6. Rotational speed too high.  

Cross over two phase leads.

7. Reverse rotation.

Increase rotational speed. Fit larger diameter Impellers. Increase number of stages. Refer to manufacturer.

 

8.Total head required for the system Is higher than total head generated by the pump at duty point (back pressure too high).

Adjust pressure conditions by means of discharge valves.

Adjust rotational speed, alter Impeller diameter. Refer to manufacturer. 

9. Total head required for the system l3 lower than total head generated by the pump  (pump operates beyond the performance limit curve). 

Check temperature of Fluid pumped, take steps outlined in 9. above. 

10. Specific gravity of fluid pumped is different from figure specified originally. (Different operating temperature). 

Check operation of minimum flow device.
Refer to manufacturer. 

11. Operation at very low rate of flow (fault In min. flow device, rate of minimum flow Is too low). 

Check condition of Individual machines.

Refer to manufacturer. 

12. Pumps cannot possibly operate In parallel

under these condition. 

Clean out pump, check condition of suction system (check suction line and strainers). 

13. Foreign bodies lodged In Impeller. 

Realign pumping set when cold. 

14. Pump miss aligned or Incorrectly aligned, or shifting of foundation. 

Refer to manufacturer. 

15. Resonance, or Interference by other machines via the foundation.

Fit 3 new shaft. On no account straighten out a bent shaft. 

17.Rotating elements foul the stationary elements, pump runs very rough. 

Check condition of balancing device dismantle pump If necessary 

18. Bearings badly worn.

Check quiet running of pump. Check coupling alignment

(when cold). Check oil supply (pressure, temperature , quantity). Check oil quality and cleanliness.

19. Case wear rings badly worn.

 

Fit new case wear rings. Check out-of-round (true running) of rotor. Check presence of foreign bodies In pump

20. Impeller damaged or disintegrated.

Fit new Impeller. Check suction head (cavitation). Check system for presence of foreign bodies . 

21. Faulty casing seal (excessive Internal loss at throttling gap, rotor clearance exceeded due to wear), so that an excessive loss arises, or water leaks through the casing partition. Water leaks out of metallic sealing face to atmosphere. 

 Replace damaged components by new ones.

Cause of Trouble

Suggest Remedies

22. Rubbing faces of mechanical seal rings badly worn or scored, 0-ring damaged.

 

Replace damaged components by new ones. Check rotating components for true running (out-of-round). Check materials used In respect of their suitability. Check correct seating of seal components and look out for signs of leakage. 

23. Seal Incorrectly mounted. Unsuitable materials.

Carefully assemble seal. Check materials In respect of their suitability. 

24. Shaft chatters because bearings are worn or because shaft is miss aligned. 

Realign coupling (when cold). Pit new bearings. Check rotor for signs of damage. 

25. Rotor vibrates.

 

check suction pressure (cavitation). Check coupling alignment. Check pump Internals for presence of foreign bodies. 

26. Too high a surface pressure In the sealing gap, no fluid available for lubrication and cooling. 

Measure fit of mechanical seal anew. Refer to manufacturer. 

. 27.Defect in cooling fluid supply to mechanical seal

Check unobstructed  flow through cooling water freed line 

Cause of Trouble

Suggest Remedies

28. Excessive clearance gap between cooling housing and spacer sleeve.

Temperature of cooling circuit excessively

Fit a spacer sleeve or a new bush In the cooling housing.

 

29. Dirt In cooling circuit to mechanical seal causes scoring of rubbing faces of mechanical seal rings. 

Use treated cooling fluid. Fit filters In cooling fluid lines.

 

. 30.Excessively high axial thrust

Check balancing device and rotor clearances. Check axial adjustment of rotor. 

31.Excessive or Insufficient quantity of oil In bearing housing ,Inadequate cooling, unsuitable oil quality, dirty oil, water in the oil. 

Check oil quality and quantity,

32. Faulty bearing assembly (damage during assembly, wrong assembly, use of two different half bearings (not matched to each other)).

Check bearing components for signs of damage, and assemble them correctly. Check condition and correct register of oil feed hole (force lubrication). 
33. Dirt in the bearings

Thoroughly clean bearings, bearing housing, all feed line and oil reservoir, check condition of bearing oil seal and fit a filter In the oil line if necessary. 
34. Ingress of water into bearing housing

Remove all rust from bearings and bearing housings. Paint oil compartment with oil-resistant paint. Check bearings, Check clearance of oil scraper rings, change

the oil fill. 

35. Excessive cooling, the liquid cooled bearing promotes condensation In the bearing housing when the ambient air humidity

Monitor the cooling water temperature, vent the bearing compartment thoroughly via the vent plug. Adjust bearing temperature to 60°C. 

36. Excessive wear of balancing device component of leaching out of stationary component of balancing device.

Check functioning of device. Check balance water line.

Check suction pressure and discharge pressure. Check condition of balancing device and rotor clearances. The balance water pressure should not exceed the pump

suction pressure by more than 3 % of the total head at the operating point, unless otherwise specified. Refer to manufacturer.
