Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection I1 required for vertical rotary regenerative air pre heater, which must be performed on an annually bases as indicated by Y1 in the title block

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category

  • Use safety fence with caution mark surrounding the work area

  • Wear safety helmet - shoes - gloves ... etc

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tools set

  • Cleaning fluid, feeler gauge

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Clean up first the entire heater

  2. Examine the condition of heating element replace if necessary

  3. Repeat half yearly inspection

  4. Replace gland packing

  5. Examine trunnion bolts

  6. Examine rotor post to diaphragm weld

  7. Open pinion cover. Examine pinion and pin rack for wear and proper engagement

  8. Pack all electric motor bearing with first grease

  9. Examine the condition of gears and pinions and bearings

  10. Lubricate all coupling

  11. Examine oil line for cracks damage

  12. Examine cleaning device gear boxes

  13. Check steam blowing nozzle

  14. Check water washing nozzle

  15. Examine the cold end basket if corrosion has damaged the cold end baskets

  16. Check the seal clearance

Guide Bearing (fig 1)

  1. Open the cover and check whether locking plat bolts

  2. Drain the oil and inspection the roller from the top

  3. Ensure cooling water supply in order

  4. Support bearing during annual overhaul

  5. Check up the level of the support bearing using a motor level of 0.02 mm/meter

  6. Drain oil and inspect the roller using light

Drive unit maintenance during overhaul (fig 4)

  1. Disconnect all electrical supplies

  2. Remove holding dower bolts from auxiliary motor

  3. Disconnect auxiliary motor coupling and remove it

  4. Remove pinion cover

  5. Remove holding down bolts from main motor

  6. Disconnect main motor coupling remove the motor

  7. Remove holding down bolts from gear box

  8. Slide the gear box on to a temporary stand with the help

  9. Support the pinion temporary using wooden block between pinion bolton and gear reducer

  10. Remove the taper lock bush and remove the power

 

Element replacement (fig 3)

  1. Remove basket from removal door

  2. Remove basket end plate from one section of the rotor

  3. Remove the cold end baskets one by one by pulling

  4. Load new set of baskets in the same sector

  5. Continue this procedure until finish

Removal and replacement of hot end baskets (fig 2)

  1. Open the access door in the duct work

  2. Remove the redial seals

  3. Remove the hot end baskets from one sector though the access door

  4. Replace with new baskets depending upon the condition of the elements

  5. Continue this precedure until finished       

Seal replacement and setting (fig 5)

  1. Fix aluminum finger tabs at in board and out board of a diaphragm

  2. Move this diaphragm plate under the sealing surface and adjust the finger tab to the required seal clearance

  3. Set the channel type straight edge (redial) at a convenient location

  4. Move the finger tabs under the straight edge and set the straight edge to sit over the tabs firmly clamp the straight edge

  5. Set one radial seal assembly butting the seal leaves with the straight edge. move the seal under the same edge of the sealing surface and confirm the correctness of the straight edge setting

  6. Then proceed with the installation of the rest of the radial seals

  7. Install outboard radial seal tabs as per radial seal assembly drawing excess metal is to be trimmed off to obtain clearance shown and required
