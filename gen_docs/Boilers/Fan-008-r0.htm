Introduction

This document out lines the details equipment inspection I1, performed on a month bases and every six months as indicated by M1:M6 in the above title block.

The function of Seal Fan to supply the boiler with SEAL air, the forced air fan to supply the boiler with combustion air, and the GR fan to circulate the hot gas to increase the boiler efficiency

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area,

  • Wear your safety helmet, shoes ... etc

  • Isolate the circuit breaker for the motor of this SL FAN

  • Isolate the electrical cable feeding of the motor of this SL FAN

  • The SL FAN must be isolated mechanically by closing suction and delivery valve.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set,

  • Cleaning fluid, Manual puller,

  • Suitable lifting device. Feeler gauge

  • Hammer, Dial indicator, Torque wrench 

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Disconnect the fan side half coupling from the rotor and inspect both the fan side and the motor side half couplings for wear.

  2. Clean all parts and re-grease jacking fixtures are required to remove the coupling hubs.

  3. In addition to these Fixtures, the hydraulic jacks are required. Torque requirements for bolts.

  4. Check the alignment of the motor, intermediate shaft (if applicable), and the couplings. "Wear of damage may affect the Fan balance".

  5. Check all lube oil and hydraulic system hoses, piping, and fittings for leaks.

  6. Check rotor & blade liners.

  7. Check and repair as required housing & inlet box.

  8. Check and adjust as required coupling wear & alignment.

  9. Check and adjust as required damper blades & linkages.

  10. Check and adjust as required rotor body bound bolts.

  11. Check and adjust as required shaft bearings & seals.

  12. Check the Inlet cone clearances.

Monthly

  1. Check the bearing oil level and adjust as required

  2. Check the main bearing temperature. refer to start - stop sequence for alarm and trip temp

  3. Check the bearing cooling water flow

  4. Check the lube oil lins for oil line leakage

  5. Check the fan for smooth operation

  6. Check all anchor bolts and support steel fasteners bolts, and tighten if necessary

  7. Check the bearing oil for contamination

  8. Check the fan blades for wear

Every 6 month

  1. Examine rotor & blade liners for any signs of erosion, and remove all traces of deposit before carrying out this examination. Should there be evidence of severe abrasion, If there is not any severe abrasion, and the rotor is to be used again without repairs, check for fan balance.

  2. Examine fan housing and inlet box for signs of wear.

  3. Body bound bolts of rotor inner plate/shaft connection for wear

  4. Examine damper assemblies for wear of blades and blade spindles

  5. Examine bearings & couplings, and make adjustments or replacements if necessary. Give particular attention to bearing seals

  6. Check position of rotor in fan housing relative to the inlet cone, make adjustments if necessary, check shaft seals at inlet box

  7. Check shaft seals at inlet box

  8. Tighten all hold down bolts in fan housing, sole plates, bearings, and base frames.

  9. Check coupling alignment
