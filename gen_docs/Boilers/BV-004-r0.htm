Introduction

This document lists the maintenance procedure of weld-on electric stop valve, depending on external / internal leakage used for feeding the Boiler with the water after feeding pump.

The first draft initiated and tested by eng. Fawzy Ahmed Basuoni at 18 Dec 2003, accepted by General Director eng. Saad Mostafa, revised by EDEPC eng. Samir Abdel-Gelil, and approved by eng. Awad Mohammed Fathy Soliman Head  Sector of Talkha power station.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area.

  • Wear your safety helmet, shoes,  etc.

  • Isolate valve mechanically and electrically.

  • Drain the pipeline, which connected to this valve.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set.

  • Cleaning fluid.

  • Suitable lifting device.

Work to be Carried Out

Before Isolated the Valve Mechanically & Electrically

  1. Check any leakage from valve gland, if it found, retighten on gland bolts.

  2. Check any leakage from the pressure tight cover, if it found retighten the cover nut.

  3. Check any internal leakage.

  4. Check any leakage from the valve bodies (cracks or mechanical wear). Remark the point of leakage.

  5. Check the operation of the valve drive during closing/ opening of the valve.

After Isolated the Valve Mechanically & Electrically

  1. Be sure the valve was out of operation and the pressure inside the pipeline connected to the valve must be zero from the two end of the valve.

  2. Be sure the pipeline is drained.

  3. Be sure the circuit breaker isolated for electric drive type.

Disassembly the Valve

  1. Screw off nut (5) of gland bolt (6).

  2. Take up the gland follower (7).

  3. Screw off yoke (2) around valve body (1).

  4. Take out from hand wheel (11) all parts of the valve except body.

  5. Take out cone (3) from spindle (4).

  6. Screw off nut (10) and take out hand wheel (11)

  7. Turn the yoke (2) around spindle (4) until the yoke take out from spindle (4).

  8. Screws off the retaining screw (12).

  9. Take out yoke sleeve (9) from yoke (2).

  10. Take out the gland follower (12).

  11. Take out the gland packing (8), ring (13).

Inspection and Repair of All Parts

  1. Check the valves cone (3) and regrind the sealing surface of it if needed.

  2. Check the valves seat and regrind it if needed.

  3. Repair the valve cone (3) and valve seat in a specialist workshop if grinding can not repair the wear in there. ( in weld type the valve end must be cut from the pipe line)

  4. Check the Spindle (4) and repair it if needed or replace it.

  5. Check the yoke sleeve (9) and repair it if needed or replace it.

  6. Checks ring (13) and repair it if needed or replace it.

  7. Check the Gland follower (7) and repair it if needed or replace it.

  8. Clean every connection face.

  9. Check the body (1) from any cracks or wear by using penetration fluids and repair it if found by using suitable filler material or its equivalent acc. to table (1).

  10. If the valve damaged, change it w.r.t. the material as stated in table 1

 Assembly of Valve

  1. Clean every part well before any assembly.

  2. Assembly the valve parts in reverse sequence of disassembly.

Replace the Packing of Valve Gland

  1. Clean the stuffing box chamber and the cover well.

  2. Lubricate the gland bolts and nuts.

  3. Use suitable size of packing.

  4. Wire the suitable packing size on a part of shaft with the same diameter of valve spindle.

  5. Cut all packing rings along the shaft by using sharp knife.

  6. Fit the rings in the stuffing box chamber with the cut turned 180 °, starting at 9 clock.

  7. Tight the gland bolts.

  8. Open and close the valve and be sure that the valve operation is correct.

×    If you need any help please go to the operating instruction for repairs and maintenance D-4.

×     Put the valve into operation and check the list of checking before isolated.
