Safety Precautions

  • Disconnect all power to motor prior to initiating any maintenance or repairs.

  • Avoid contact with rotating parts.

  • Act with care in accordance with prescribed procedures, in handling and lifting this equipment.

  • Be sure shaft key is fully captive before unit is energized.

  • Always be sure oil lubricated units are filled with correct oil to proper level before operating.

  • Provide proper safeguards for personnel against rotating parts and applications involving high inertia loads which can cause overspeed

 

 

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set.

  • Cleaning fluid, feeler gauge

  • Suitable lifting device

 

PERMANENT CHECKS

  1. The indications given by the temperature measuring devices, and by the other protection devices, must be in accordance with the values given by connection diagram.

  2. Check that there is no abnormal noise or vibration.

 

CHECKS IN CASE OF ABNORMAL OPERATION

  1. In case of abnormal operation ( for example: heating, abnormal noise, abnormal current,... ), it is necessary to check the condition of the different parts of the machine, to dismantle it partially or completely and to overhaul it.

  2. Particularly, the cleaning of the inside of the machine, of the rotor and stator ventilation ducts and of the windings must be carried out with dry, oil free compressed air, maximum pressure 3 bars.

  3. If the machine is too dirty, it can be cleaned with a cloth impregnated with turpentine.

  4. After cleaning, check the insulation resistance of the windings.

  5. Refer to page "Insulation checks".

AIR FILTER CLEANING

 

  1. A dirty filter makes the machine to heat.

  2. When the stator temperature over-reaches the temperature data given in the connection diagram, it is necessary to clean the filters of the machine.

 

Procedure

  1. The filters are located in the air inlet.

  2. Unscrew bolts securing the air inlet panels to the ventilation hood and remove the panel including the hood.

  3. In order to remove dust out of the filter, clean it with compressed air or with water.

  4. If the filter is too dirty, it can be cleaned with water with addition of sodium carbonate.

  5. Use no other detergent.

  6. The filter must be completely dry before machine start-up.

  7. Dry the filter with warm air.

 

PRELIMINARY OPERATIONS BEFORE DISMANTLING

  1. Disconnect all cables: supply, protections, accessories 
          Ø      Uncouple the machine.
          Ø      Remove the coupling flange.

  2. If the machine must be completely dismantled (the rotor taken out of the stator), refer to the pages "Rotor dismantling" for the position to be given to the machine to carry out this operation.

SUPPORTS FOR DISMANTLING

  1. When dismantling a bearing, to remove the parts out of the shaft, use two supports:
          Ø     A support "A" placed at the end of the shaft, or eventually at the end of the extension shaft.
          Ø     A support "B" placed as close as possible to the frame.

  2. If there is not enough space for a support, replace it with a sling.

  3. Make sure that the supports do not damage the machined parts of the shaft.

  

 

 

  

To take a part out of the shaft:

  1. Bring the bearing part next to the "A" support.

  2. Put the "B" support in place, remove the "A" support and then remove the bearing part.

  3. Put the "A" support back in place and remove the "B" support.

  4. If there is enough place, several parts can be removed simultaneously.


DISMANTLING OF PARTS ASSEMBLED WITH SEALING PASTE

  1. Some parts like covers, hoods, housings, are put in place with the jointed sides covered with a coat of sealing paste.

  2. To dismantle these parts, it is necessary to loosen them after their securing bolts are removed.

  3. For that operation threaded holes are provided:
    - in covers, hoods and housings.
    - in end shields to loosen them.

 

Rotor Dismantling

  1. In order to remove the rotor, the preliminary operations before dismantling should be carried out and the bearings must be dismantled.

  2. In order to handle the rotor, a steel tube should be prepared.

  3. This tube will be put in the shaft.

  4. The inside diameters of the tube should be between 3 to 6 mm greater than the shaft diameters.

  5. Put a thin copper sheet (between 0.5 and 1 mm) lining its inside, in order to protect the machined parts.

  6. Length of tube fitted to the shaft: about 350 mm

  7. Tube length: about 1500 mm

  8. Place slings at both ends of rotor + tube and remove the assembly.

  9. Avoid all stator-rotor contact, which could damage the magnetic circuits and the windings.

 

Remove the Rotor

  1. Lay the rotor on a mobile trolley,

  2. Remove the slings, take the trolley with the rotor away from the stator, and

  3. Remove the handling tube.

 

 

 

 

 

 

Reassembly

  1. Reassemble the various pieces in reverse order of the steps used for dismantling.

  2. During reassembly take care that insulated parts do not scrape against iron parts and that the rotor does not scrape against the stator.

  3. Check the cleanliness of the various parts.

  4. Put oil on the surfaces in contact with the shaft and on the sleeve.

  5. Carefully clean the machined surfaces covered with sealing paste and put some more paste (Type Hylomar 32M or H for instance) before reassembly.

  6. Make sure to put paste on both sides that have to be jointed, place it evenly in thin coats free of lumps.

  7. Check that all bolts are well tightened and put the bolt stops.
