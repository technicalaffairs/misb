Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area

  • Wear your safety helmet, shoes ... etc

  • Isolate the circuit breaker of the fan motor

  • Disconnect electric terminals of fan motor

  • The fan must be isolated mechanically by closing delivery damper.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set

  • Cleaning fluid, Manual puller,

  • Feeler gauge, dial indicator,

  • Vibrometer straight edge.

Work to be Carried Out

Dismantling

  1. Disconnect the electric motors

  2. Disconnect the fan from the inlet ducts and the actuator from the dampers.

  3. Remove the driving shaft of the dampers and also the 2 dampers.

  4. Remove all the different guards

  5. Uncouple the electric motor and the barring device from the fan.

  6. Remove all the auxiliary equipments installed on the bearings pyrometric probes

  7. Discharge the bearings from their oil

  8. Break away the shaft tight from the inlet boxes.

  9. Remove the cooling impellers.

  10. Disconnect the shaped inlets from the casing.

  11. Remove the upper part of the fan casing which allows the dismantling of the rotor. 

  12. sling the rotor  

    Remark    :     normally all the bearing compounds are marked in order to avoid any mistake during the reassembly
     

  13. Unscrew the tow side parts of each bearing taking care not to damage the gaskets.

  14. Remove the upper part of each bearing taking care not to damage the gaskets.

  15. Sling up the rotor and put it down on the maintenance area.

    Remark: we advise to put the rotor on wooden made supports in order to avoid to damage the shaft or the impeller.
     

  16. Remove the half coupling on the motor side and the free wheel on the barring device side.

  17. Remove the central bolt of each bearing

  18. Slide the outer side of each bearing to the shaft tip

  19. Slide the outer spacers to the shaft tips

  20. To remove the bearing try to use a hub puller

 Remark: an already used rolling bearing can not be reused after dismantling

 

 

Reassembly of New Roller Bearings

  1. Check that the shaft has no damage

  2. Install the rolling bearings on the shaft as indicated here under, the rolling bearings are installed tightened on the shaft .

  3. Check that rolling bearing have no foreign substances. In this case, clean them by spirit or petrol.

  4. Heat the rolling bearing in an oil bath at a temperature of 100 to120 ºC without exceeding 120 ºC

  5. Check that the oil rings are well installed on the outer spacers stayed in place.

  6. Slid the rolling bearing on the shaft and lock them against the inner spacers.

  7. Assemble the outer spacers and the outer sides of the bearing housing taking care of the guide mark of the different parts and without forgetting the gaskets. 

  8. Assemble the brake washers.

  9. Screw the central bolts and brake them with the brake washers .

  10. Assemble the half coupling

  11. Sling the rotor

  12. Sling up the rotor and install it on its place on the casing

  13. Assemble the upper parts of bearing housings

  14. Fix the sides of the bearing housings.

  15. Fill the bearing with oil.

  16. Assemble the remaining parts of the fan in the reverse order of the disassembly

  17. Check that no water stays in the bottom of the casing.
