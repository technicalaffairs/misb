Work to be Carried Out

Disassembly of Heat Transfer Surface in Semi-Modular Air Preheaters

  1. Remove rotor housing basket installation/removal door.

  2. Rotate the rotor to line up the basket opening in the rotor with the basket installation/removal opening in the housing panel.

  3. Unbolt reversible pulley and install on the top side of the movable platform.

  4. Cut loose welds between the rotor structure (diaphragms and rotor shell) and the axial seal bars and the adjust­able circumferential basket seal angles.

  5. Hook the "Tugger" cable to the cold side basket cover frame and pull baskets out of rotor .  Repeat this step until all baskets have been removed from the layer.

  6. Adjust basket platform to level of next layer and repeat steps 4 and 5.

  7. Repeat steps 4 through 6 until all heat transfer surface is removed from the semi-modular rotor compartment.

  8. Rotate the rotor to the full sector closest to opposite of the last unloaded sector and repeat steps 4 through 7.

  9. Repeat steps 4 through 8 until all heat transfer surface has been removed from the semi-modular rotor.

  10. Unthread the 'Tugger" cable through the pulleys. Unbolt the reversible pulley and install on the bottom side of the movable platform. Thread the "Tugger" cable through pulley's.

Rotor Seal Assembly, Replacement and Setting    Fig 1, 2

Radial Seals

  1. Remove present radial seals and holding strips. It may be necessary to burn or twist bolts off to remove seals ... etc

  2. When installing the new seals start at the outboard end by inserting bolts along with washers in outboard and inboard bolt holes of outboard seal. The bolts are to be inserted in the trailing edge of the diaphragms

  3. Install radial seal section and secure with nuts and washers. Do not tighten but what seal may be moved in or out as required for final setting

  4. Install remaining bolts and washers in this section

  5. Repeat this procedure for the inboard radial seal leaf section to clear the rotor post seal.

  6. After new seals have been installed on all diaphragms, install seal straight edge assembly by bolting to support channels which have been welded, one inboard and one outboard in either the gas or air side of the hot and cold connecting plates

  7. Rotate the rotor such that the seals of one diaphragm are at the edge of a sector plate. By use of a feeler gauge set the inner and outer seals to the specified clearance and tighten the nuts

  8. Rotate rotor such that seals are at edge of opposite sector plate. Check clearance from sector plate inboard and outboard. If the variation in clearance is not greater than ± .020" (±0.5 mm) no further adjustment in seal setting is required

  9. If the variation is greater than ± 0.20" (±0.5 mm) reset the seals to the specified clearance from the sector plate having the largest clearance from the diaphragm using the clearances given on seal setting chart

  10. Rotate rotor such that diaphragm with the seals which have been set to the sector plate is located under seal straight edge. Adjust straight edge so that it just contacts seal and secure

  11. Rotate rotor such that seals of each diaphragm are located under straight edge. Adjust seal leaves so that they just contact the straight edge and secure

  12. After all redial seals have been set to straight edge and secured, remove straight edge and store for future use

Axial Seals

  1. For access to the axial seals, remove the covers from seal access openings which are located each side of the main pedestals

  2. Remove present axial seal leaves and holding strips by removing nuts which secure these components

  3. Install axial seal straight edge which is bolted to channels which have been welded to the inside of the rotor housing adjacent to the axial seal plate at both hot and cold end

  4. Set straight edge such that it is 3/16" (5mm) from point of maximum horizontal A run out of pin rack rail and rotor sealing angle. These points may be determined and marked by rotating the rotor by a stationary pointer

  5. Install holding strips and seal leaves and secure with nuts and washers. Do not tighten but what seal leaves may be moved in or out as required for final adjustment

  6. Rotate rotor such that axial seal is located under straight edge. Adjust seal leaves so that they just contact straight edge and secure

  7. Install holding strips and axial seal leaves on remaining diaphragms, setting the seals to the straight edge

  8. Proceed to check and reset axial seal plates, if necessary, by rotating rotor until the axial seals of one diaphragm are at edge of axial seal plate. Refer to the seal setting chart by use of feeler gauge check the clearance from the axial seal to the axial seal plate at both hot and cold end. If it does not vary more than ± .020" (± 0.5 mm) no adjustment of the axial seal plates is required

  9. If the variation is greater than the ± .020" (± 0.5 mm) move axial seal plate in or out by use of the adjusters which are located exterior of the air preheater in the main pedestal to obtain the specified clearance

  10. Rotate rotor such that axial seal is at exit edge of axial seal plate and check clearance. If the variation from specified setting is greater than ± .020" (± 0.5 mm) adjust axial seal plate to obtain required clearance at hot and cold ends

  11. Rotate rotor such that axial seal is at opposite axial seal plate. Follow above procedure in checking the clearances and adjustment of axial seal plate, if required

  12. Replace axial seal access covers

Bypass Seals

  1. The bypass seals are bolted to the by-pass seal angle which is attached to the connecting plate flange. These seals run from sector plate to sector plate on both the gas and air side at the hot and cold ends. Access to these seals is from the inside of the air preheater. To replace these seals remove the present seals and holding strips. Retain the holding strips for use with the new seals. It may be necessary to burn or twist bolts off to remove seals.

  2. When installing the new seals make sure you have the proper width seals. When installing the new seals make sure that the outer leaves stagger the inner leaves such that the bend slots are not exposed. It will be necessary to trim the seal leaves to fit against the axial seal plates and sector plates. It may also be necessary to trim one or more

  3. Of the outer seal leaves to maintain bolt hole alignment. When installing the new seals it is important that they be correctly positioned on the pin rack and rotor sealing angle to avoid run off during operation. At the cold end, the top of the seal leaves should be 1/2"-1"(13-19mm) from the bottom of the pin rack rail.

  4. Refer to the seal setting chart in this section of the O & M manual for the specified clearance between the bypass seals and sealing surfaces at both the hot and cold end.
    The seal leaves may have to be sprung in or out, to obtain this clearance.

Rotor Post Seals

  1. It is seldom necessary to replace the rotor post seals. However, if the occasion should arise, remove the present seals which are seal welded to the diaphragms and lugs and grind off the welds 

  2. When installing the new seals make sure 4543 you have the correct width seal. The seal for the cold end is wider than the hot end seal.

  3. The seals for each respective end are furnished in four 90 degree sections and are notched to fit over the inboard end of the diaphragms

  4. When installing the rotor post seals make sure you have 1/4" (6mm) clearance between the seal and the horizontal sealing surface. You should also have 1/16" (2mm) clearance between the seal and the vertical sealing surface. The seal sections are to be welded at the joints and seal welded to the lugs and diaphragms only. 

Replacement of Rotor Support Bearing

  1. Drain oil from bearing housing.

  2. Remove bearing housing cover. (Splits to be match marked.)

  3. Disconnect and remove thermocou­ple and thermostat

  4. Remove oil lines from bearing housing

  5. Remove bearing housing; hold down bolts

  6. Raise bearing housing by use of jacking bolts until load is removed from mounting pads and shims. Keep each set of pads and shims together and note location from which they were removed for reinstallation

  7. Lower bearing housing using jacking bolts until it contacts the support plate in center section.

  8. Install safety eye bolt (swivel type) to bearing housing

  9. Slide bearing housing from underneath rotor post until it is in an accessible location for removal and replacement

  10. Remove bolts and retainer plate from stub shaft

  11. Remove bearing from stub shaft and runner bore. A puller may be used if necessary. Use extreme care so that surface of runner will not be scratched or gouged

  12. Clean bearing surface at end of stub shaft and bore of runner

  13. Apply a coat of light oil to inner and outer race of new bearing and install on end of stub shaft and in runner bore. Jacking bolts may be used between O.D. runner and I.D. bearing housing to center runner so that radial bearing will slide easily into place. Install retainer plate and tighten bolts evenly and incrementally to 150 ft/lbs. (204 nm) torque

  14. Position bearing housing under rotor post

  15. Raise bearing housing evenly using jacking bolts until runner and flange of rotor post are seated. Check with feeler gauge

  16. Reinstall mounting pads and shims in same location as they were prior to removal

  17. Install bearing housing hold down bolts. Do not tighten

  18. Lower jacking bolts until bearing housing rests on mounting pads

  19. Using a precision level on top of bearing housing, check level of housing "Reshim" if necessary

  20. Tighten bearing housing hold down bolts to 660-690 ft./lbs. (895-935 nm) torque

  21. Remove blocks and lower rotor using jacks until runner and rotor post flange are fully seated

  22. Remove jacks and reinstall static spool segments removed for access to jack rotor

  23. Install oil suction line, oil inlet linen and dipstick

  24. Install thermocouple and thermostat

  25. Fill bearing housing to proper level with new oil

  26. Install bearing housing cover using a sealant between splits and also at area where cover bolts to trunnion flange

Replacement of Rotor Guide Bearing

  1. Block guide trunnion to prevent movement

  2. Drain oil from bearing housing

  3. disconnect water piping to heat exchanger

  4. Remove bolts which secure bearing housing cover to bearing housing

  5. Using a sling and eye bolts provided for this purpose, remove bearing housing cover along with the oil circulating system with internal discharge and suction lines attached

  6. Remove radial bearing ring

  7. Remove bars which lock bolts that secure locking cap to top of trunnion Loosen bolts and back out 3/8" (9.5 mm).

  8. Remove the pipe plug from the center of the trunnion and connect a hydraulic pressure.   ram having 5,000-10,000 psi (170 K/m2 - 340 K/m2) capacity. Using hydraulic pressure, break the adapter sleeve loose.   After breaking the adapter sleeve loose remove the locking cap bolts and locking cap

  9. Using a sling along with eye bolts in the adapter sleeve remove the adapter sleeve and bearing

  10. Remove the bearing from the adapter sleeve by first removing the SKF lock plate and back off locknut approximately 3/16" (4.8 mm). Hydraulic pressure may now be employed to break bearing loose from sleeve by connecting pressure hose to tapped hole in end of adapter sleeve

  11. Once bearing has broken loose remove locknut and bearing from adapter sleeve

  12. Force new bearing on adapter sleeve using the locknut and simultaneously

  13. Position guide bearing assembly unit over trunnion using sling and eye bolts in adapter sleeve

  14. Lower unit down on trunnion. Install locking cap at top of trunnion.

  15. Reinstall radial bearing ring

  16. Install housing cover and circulating system making sure that packing under cover is in proper position

  17. Fill housing to proper level with recommended lubricant
