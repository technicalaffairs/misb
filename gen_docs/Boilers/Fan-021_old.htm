Safety Precautions

  • Disconnect all power to motor prior to initiating any maintenance or repairs.

  • Avoid contact with rotating parts.

  • Act with care in accordance with prescribed procedures, in handling and lifting this equipment.

  • Be sure unit is electrically grounded and proper electri­cal installation wiring and controls are used consistent with local and national electrical codes. refer to "national electrical code handbook" – NFPA NO. 70. employ qualified electricians.

  • Be sure equipment is properly enclosed to prevent access by children or other unauthorized personnel in order to pre­ vent possible accidents.

  • Be sure shaft key is fully captive before unit is energized.

  • Always be sure oil lubricated units are filled with correct oil to proper level before operating.

  • Provide proper safeguards for personnel against rotating parts and applications involving high inertia loads which can cause overspeed.

  • Avoid extended exposure to equipment with high noise levels.

  • Observe good safety habits at all times and use care to avoid injury to yourself or damage to your equipment.

  • Be familiar with the equipment and read all instructions thoroughly before installing or working on equipment.

Start-up Preparations after Storage

  1. Motor should be thoroughly inspected and cleaned to restore to an "As Shipped" condition.

  2. Motors which have been subjected to vibration must be disassembled and each bearing inspected for damage.

  3. Oil and/or grease must be completely changed using lubricants recommended on the motor's lubrication plate, or in the Installation and Instruction Manual. After changing grease, the motor should be run under no load with the drain plug removed. This will allow all excess grease to be purged from the bearing cavity without danger to the winding. After running, replace drain plug.

  4. The winding must be tested to obtain insulation resistance and dielectric absorption ratio as described in Section III, Part 3.

  5. If storage has exceeded one year, the U.S.E.M. Quality Assurance Department, must be contacted prior to equipment start-up.

 

Initial Installation

 

Coupling, Pulley or Sprocket Installation

 

General

  1. Remove the Shaft locking Device shipped on motor.

  2. Wash protective coating from Motor Shaft Extension(s) with solvent.

  3. Install couplings, pulleys or sprockets on motor and driven equipment considering the following. Couplings, pulleys or sprockets should fit tight to shaft.

  4. Any burrs or irregularities on mating parts should be removed prior to attempting installation.

Note: Hammering or pounding with a mallet is not recommended to install couplings, pulleys, or sprockets, as the motor bearings can be damaged.

Ball or Roller Bearing Motors

  1. Pulleys or sprockets should be positioned as close to the shaft shoulder as possible to assure longest bearing life.

Note: Tension on the belt(s) or chain(s) should not be more than is recommended by the transmission drive manufacturer.

Rough Alignment

  1. Inspect sole plate mounting pads and bottom of motor feet for dirt or irregularities that would prevent the motor from seating properly.

  2. Position and shim the motor such that the coupling hubs are aligned within 1/32 of an inch. The motor shaft must be slightly lower than the driven shaft to allow for final adjustment shims. The motor shaft should be shimmed level within 1/32" during this procedure. 

Final Alignment

  1. Check to assure that coupling hubs are not eccentric. Mount a dial indicator on a stationary part and read the eccentricity of the outside diameters of the coupling hubs as they are rotated.

  2. Check to be sure that the motor feet lie in the same plane. Bolt the feet down securely. Mount a dial indicator on the driven half of the coupling. Take readings of the movement of the motor half of the coupling as the feet bolts are loosened. loosen a shaft end foot bolt, take a reading and re-tighten. On the same side of the motor, loosen the opposite shaft-end foot bolt and take a reading. Now add shims to the feet until motion of the coupling is less than 0.003" when either foot bolt is loosened. Repeat this procedure for the foot bolts on the opposite side of the motor.

  3. Check for angular misalignment of motor to driven unit shaft. (See Figure 3A.) Measure distance between coupling hub faces (with feeler gauges) at four places equally spaced around the outside diameters. Position motor as necessary to be within the max allowable misalignment of 0.001 in. per foot of coupling radius.

  4. Check for offset alignment. Fasten a dial indicator on one coupling hub with the indicator button contracting the alignment surface of the opposite coupling hub. (See figure 3B) rotate shaft onto which the indicator is attached and take readings at four points 90 Deg. apart. Relocate motor until total indicator movement, in a full rotation does not exceed .002 inches. Transfer indicator to opposite hub and repeat the offset alignment procedure. Recheck angular alignment as described in Step C.

  5. Miscellaneous
    After each corrective adjustment, tighten the foot bolts securely and recheck alignment. When making shim adjustments, change only one foot at a time. Recheck alignment after the motor has been in service approximately one week. Readjust as necessary.

 

Note: Alignment is at operating conditions and any thermal growth should be taken into account.

Electrical Connection

  1. Refer to the motor nameplate for power supply requirements and to the connection diagram on the motor.

  2. Be sure connections are tight. Check carefully and assure that they agree with the connection diagram, then carefully tape all connections with electrical tape, to be sure that they will not short against each other or to ground.

  3. Be sure the motor is grounded to guard against possible electrical shock.

  4. Refer to the National Electrical Code Handbook (NFPA No. 70) and to local electrical codes for proper wiring, protection, and wire sizing.

  5. Be sure proper starting equipment and protective devices are used for every motor.

 

Part Winding Starters

  1. Part winding starters used with part winding start motors should have the timer set at a minimum time consistent with the power company requirements.

  2. Two seconds is the recommended maximum time on part winding.

  3. Setting the timer for longer periods can cause permanent damage to the motor, and may void the warranty.

Reversing Rotation

  1. The direction of rotation may be reversed by interchanging any two, of the three power leads to the motor leads.

  2. Be sure that the power is off and steps are taken to prevent accidental restarting of the motor before attempting to change any electrical connections.

Vibration

  1. After alignment has been completed. run motor at minimum possible load (uncoupled from driven equipment if possible) and check for vibration. If vibration is excessive (See Table 1) loosen one Motor Mounting. Bolt.

  2. If. vibration decreases add shims and retighten bolt. Repeat as necessary for all bolts until vibration is reduced to acceptable limits. Recheck. coupling alignment after any change in shims.

  3. Recheck vibration after unit has run under full load and has reached operating temperature.

Disassembly of Ball or Roller Bearing Motors

 

See figure 4 for Ball Bearing Housing Cross Section.

  1. Disconnect power and assure against accidental starting of motor.

  2. Unbolt and remove grills or shrouding (if applicable).

  3. Remove bearing cap screws.

  4. Remove all thermostats. probes, thermocouples etc., from bearings and brackets.

  5. Remove bracket to stator bolts and remove brackets.

  6. Remove bearings from motor shaft with a bearing puller. Pull on inner bearing race to remove without damage.

  7. For reassembly see Section IX "Reassembly" Part 2.

 

Reassembly

  1. Clean all machined and mating surfaces on bearing caps, bracket fits. etc.

  2. Remove old grease from grease cavities and refill cavities approximately 50% full with recommended grease. See Table 2 for correct grease.

  3. Carefully inspect ball or roller bearings for nicks, dents or any unusual wear patterns. Replace bearing if damaged.

  4. Reassemble motor by reversing the disassembly procedure in Section VIII "Disassembly", Part 2, Items A through F. Bearings should be installed per bearing manufacturers recommended procedure.

  5. Touch up any scratched or chipped paint to protect motor surfaces.

LUBRICATION

Motor must be at rest and electrical controls should be locked open to prevent energizing while being serviced. If motor is being taken out of storage refer to Section III "Storage", Part 9 for preparation instructions.

Lubrication of Ball and Roller Bearing Motors

 

General

Units with grease lubricated bearings are pre-lubricated at the factory and normally do not require initial lubrication. Refer to Table2 for recommended greases and suggested. re-greasing intervals for longest bearing life.

Motors with Automatic Grease. Drain Construction (See Figure 4A)

  1. To re-lubricate bearings, add new grease until clean grease flows from the drain (drain is behind the grill and is automatically activated by the re-greasing operation). Grease accumulations at drain should be removed to prevent grease entering the motor. Excess

  2. grease in bracket reservoir will be automatically purged while motor is running.

  3. The lower grill should be removed periodically and any hardened or excess grease

  4. manually cleaned from bracket reservoir, drain and grill.

Motors with Removable Plug Drain Construction (see Figure 4B)

  1. To re-lubricate bearings, remove the drain plug and add new grease until clean grease! appears at the drain. Run the motor for approximately ten minutes, with the drain plug removed to allow purging of the excess grease.

  2. After the excess grease has been puged. replace the drain plug.
