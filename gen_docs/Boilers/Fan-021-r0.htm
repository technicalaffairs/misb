Introduction

This document outlines the maintenance procedures P1, which must be performed on an annually bases as indicated by Y1 in the title block.

The first draft initiated by eng. Ekramy Mostafa Mohamad, accepted by technical expert eng Hassan El-Behiry, and approved by eng. Fathi El Sayed head sector of Kurimat PS.

This document certified by eng. Gamal El-Din Abdel Wahab UEEPC general director of inspection & quality control and MPIS Coordinator

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Maintenance staff should be will trained and qualified or directly supervised by some one who is.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear your safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, breathing mask, safety glass ...etc

  • Disconnect all power to motor prior to initiating any maintenance or repairs.

  • Avoid contact with rotating parts.

  • Act with care in accordance with prescribed procedures, in handling and lifting this equipment.

  • Be sure unit is electrically grounded and proper electri­cal installation wiring and controls are used consistent with local and national electrical codes. refer to "national electrical code handbook" – NFPA NO. 70. employ qualified electricians.

  • Be sure equipment is properly enclosed to prevent access by children or other unauthorized personnel in order to pre­ vent possible accidents.

  • Be sure shaft key is fully captive before unit is energized.

  • Always be sure oil lubricated units are filled with correct oil to proper level before operating.

  • Provide proper safeguards for personnel against rotating parts and applications involving high inertia loads which can cause over speed.

  • Avoid extended exposure to equipment with high noise levels.

  • Observe good safety habits at all times and use care to avoid injury to yourself or damage to your equipment.

  • Be familiar with the equipment and read all instructions thoroughly before installing or working on equipment.

 

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set.

  • Cleaning fluid, feeler gauge

  • Suitable lifting device.

Work to be Carried Out

Disassembly

  1. Disconnect power to avoid starting of motor.

  2. Unbolt and remove grills or shrouding (if applicable).

  3. Remove bearing cap screws.

  4. Remove all thermostats. probes, thermocouples etc., from bearings and brackets.

  5. Remove bracket to stator bolts and remove brackets.

  6. Remove bearings from motor shaft with a bearing puller. Pull on inner bearing race to remove without damage.

Reassembly

  1. Clean all machined and mating surfaces on bearing caps, bracket fits.

  2. Remove old grease from grease cavities and refill cavities approximately 50% full with recommended grease. See Table 2 for correct grease.

  3. Carefully inspect ball or roller bearings for nicks, dents or any unusual wear patterns. Replace bearing if damaged.

  4. Reassemble motor by reversing the disassembly procedure.

  5. Touch up any scratched or chipped paint to protect motor surfaces.

Coupling, Pulley

  1. Remove the shaft locking device shipped on motor.

  2. Wash protective coating from motor shaft extension (s) with solvent.

  3. Install couplings, pulleys or sprockets on motor and driven equipment considering the following. Couplings, pulleys or sprockets should fit tight to shaft.

  4. Any burrs or irregularities on mating parts should be removed prior to attempting installation.

  5. Hammering or pounding with a mallet is not recommended to install couplings, pulleys, or sprockets, as the motor bearings can be damaged.

Rough Alignment

  1. Inspect sole plate mounting pads and bottom of motor feet for dirt or irregularities that would prevent the motor from seating properly.

  2. Position and shim the motor such that the coupling hubs are aligned within 1/32 inch. The motor shaft must be slightly lower than the driven shaft to allow for final adjustment shims.

Final Alignment

  1. Check to ensure that coupling hubs are not eccentric. Mount a dial indicator on a stationary part and read the eccentricity of the outside diameters of the coupling hubs as they are rotated.

  2. Check to assure that coupling hubs are not eccentric. Mount a dial indicator on a stationary part and read the eccentricity of the outside diameters of the coupling hubs as they are rotated.

  3. Check to be sure that the motor feet lie in the same plane.

  4. Bolt the feet down securely.

  5. Mount a dial indicator on the driven half of the coupling.

  6. Take readings of the movement of the motor half of the coupling as the feet bolts are loosened.

  7. Loosen a shaft end foot bolt, take a reading and re-tighten. On the same side of the motor, loosen the opposite shaft-end foot bolt and take a reading.

  8. Now add shims to the feet until motion of the coupling is less than 0.003" when either foot bolt is loosened.

  9. Repeat this procedure for the foot bolts on the opposite side of the motor.

  10. Check for angular misalignment of motor to driven unit shaft. (See Figure 2A.)

  11. Measure distance between coupling hub faces (with feeler gauges) at four places equally spaced around the outside diameters.

  12. Position motor as necessary to be within the max allowable misalignment of 0.001 in. per foot of coupling radius.

  13. Check for offset alignment; Fasten a dial indicator on one coupling hub with the indicator button contracting the alignment surface of the opposite coupling hub. (See figure 2B)

  14. Rotate shaft onto which the indicator is attached and take readings at four points 90 Deg. apart.

  15. Relocate motor until total indicator movement, in a full rotation does not exceed 0.002 inches.

  16. Transfer indicator to opposite hub and repeat the offset alignment procedure.

  17. Recheck angular alignment as described in Step C.

Miscellaneous

  1. Tighten the foot bolts securely and recheck alignment after each corrective adjustment .

  2. Change only one foot at a time when making shim adjustments.

  3. Recheck alignment after the motor has been in service approximately one week, readjust as necessary.

Note: Alignment is at operating conditions and any thermal growth should be taken into account.

Electrical Connection

  1. Refer to the motor nameplate for power supply requirements and to the connection diagram on the motor.

  2. Be sure connections are tight. Check carefully and assure that they agree with the connection diagram, then carefully tape all connections with electrical tape, to be sure that they will not short against each other or to ground.

  3. Be sure the motor is grounded to guard against possible electrical shock.

  4. Refer to the National Electrical Code Handbook (NFPA No. 70) and to local electrical codes for proper wiring, protection, and wire sizing.

  5. Be sure proper starting equipment and protective devices are used for every motor.

Reversing Rotation

  1. The direction of rotation may be reversed by interchanging any two, of the three power leads to the motor leads.

  2. Be sure that the power is off and steps are taken to prevent accidental restarting of the motor before attempting to change any electrical connections.

Vibration

  1. After alignment has been completed. run motor at minimum possible load (uncoupled from driven equipment if possible) and check for vibration.

  2. If vibration is excessive (See Table 1) loosen one Motor Mounting Bolt.

  3. If vibration decreases add shims and retighten bolt.

  4. Repeat as necessary for all bolts until vibration is reduced to acceptable limits.

  5. Recheck. coupling alignment after any change in shims.

  6. Recheck vibration after unit has run under full load and has reached operating temperature.

Lubrication

    Lubrication of Ball and Roller Bearing Motors

        Motors with Automatic Grease Drain Construction  (see figure 1A).

  1. To relubricate bearings, add new grease until clean grease flows from the drain.

  2. Remove grease accumulations at drain to prevent grease entering the motor.

  3. Excess grease in bracket reservoir will be automatically purged while motor is running

  4. Remove The lower grill periodically and clean any hardened or excess grease manually  from bracket reservoir, drain and grill.

Motors with Removable Plug Drain Construction (see figure 1B).

  1. To relubricate bearings, remove the drain plug and add new grease until clean grease appears at the drain.

  2. Run the motor for approximately ten minutes, with the drain plug removed to allow purging of the excess grease.

  3. Replace the drain plug after the excess grease has been purged.

Putting the Equipment into Service

Initial Starts as follow:

  1. Ensure that motor and device connections agree with wiring diagrams.

  2. Assure that voltage, phase, and frequency of line circuit (power supply) agree with motor nameplate.

  3. Check insulation resistance.

  4. Check all foundation and base bolts to assure they are tight.

  5. Check for proper or desired rotation.

  6. Assure that all protective devices are connected and operating properly.

  7. Run motor at no-load long enough before connecting it  to be certain that no unusual condition develops. Listen and feel for excessive noise, vibration, clicking, or pounding. If present, stop motor immediately. Investigate the cause and correct before putting into service.

  8. When checks are satisfactory to this point, operate at the minimum possible load and look for any unusual condition. Increase the load slowly and check unit for satisfactory operation.
