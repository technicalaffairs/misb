Introduction

This document lists the minor maintenance procedure P1, which must performed on an annually bases as indicated by Y1 in the title block.

The Ljungstrom air heater absorbs waste heat from flue gas, then transfers this heat to incoming cold air to increase the temperature of the incoming combustion or air process.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, gloves, ... etc.

    •

  • The regenerative air heater must be isolated mechanically and electrically

  • Be sure the cooling water of the upper bearing is closed.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set

  • Cleaning fluid, Feeler, gauge, Hammer

  • Suitable lifting device, Manual puller, Torque wrench

Work to be Carried Out

 

Before Isolating the Air Heater Mechanically and Electrically

  1. Check any abnormal noise or vibration.

  2. Check any air or flue gases  leakage from manholes.

  3. Check the oil level of upper guide bearing.

  4. Check the oil level of Lower support bearing.

  5. Check the oil level of drive unit.

  6. Check the temperature of the upper and lower bearing should be ≤ 50 oC

  7. Check the oil level of drive unit for blowing equipment.

  8. Check auxiliary drive in service for a few minutes.

  9. Check pressure after and before blowing orifice plate.

  10. Cheek leakage steam from the steam admission valves and   blowing equipment.

  11. Cheek any  leakage oil from drive unit of air heater.

  12. Cheek any leakage oil from upper and lower bearing

After Isolating the Air Heater Mechanically and Electrically

  1. Washing air preheater at the hot end to cleaning it in out age of the unit until the (PH) of drain water equal Washing water PH = 8 

  2. Vent the water cooling system for Guide Bearing in the top end and clean it if needed.

  3. Check sealing clearance according to Table No.( 1 ) and figure No. ( 3 , 4, 5)

  4. Change the oil of upper guide bearing, figure 1

  5. Change the oil of Lower support bearing, figure 1.

  6. Change the oil of drive unit for air heater figure 9.

  7. Change the oil of drive unit for blowing equipment, figure 6

  8. Change the Lubrication of coupling for main motor and coupling air motor, figure 9.

  9. Change the Lubrication of coupling for motor and reducer speed of blowing device, figure 6

  10. Change the Lubrication of coupling for reducer speed and mechanism of blowing device, figure 6.

  11. Check coupling alignment between air motor and reducer speed of drive unit table (2)

  12. Check coupling alignment between main motor and reducer speed of drive unit table (2).

  13. Check coupling alignment between the motor and reducer speed of blowing device figure (6) 

  14. Check coupling alignment between reducer speed and mechanism of blowing device figure (6) 

  15. Cleaning air filters of air supply once a gear figure (10) or if it required – check the level of oil fog lubricators and increase it if required for air line to air motor

  16. Cheek the rotor pin gear and meshing between the pinion gear and rotor pin and check pinion air seal, figure (8)

  17. Cheek the nozzle and cleaning it and tight jets of blowing arms, figure (6)

  18. Cheek and lubricate the mechanism of blowing device, figure (8)

  19. Cheek and lubricate reducer speed of drive unit, figure (8)

  20. Cheek the fire exiting washer pipe and prevent any leakage if found

  21. Repair any part if need

  22. Operate the Washing device and fire fighting system manually by air motor to tested it

  23. Cheek the air motor drive and meantance it if needed.

  24. Cheek the cold end and intermediate and replacement the bad condition basket figure (2).

Return Regenerative Air Heater into Service

  1. Operate the regenerative air heater by coordination with the shift engineer.

  2. Ensure that the regenerative air heater is OK and record the readings of clearance, temp., vibration, ... etc, and compare with ref. values.
