Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified stuff should be carry the suitable safety category .

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear your safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, breathing mask, safety glass ... etc.

  • Boiler out of service.

  • Provide the working area by good lighting.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set.

  • Cleaning fluid, Manual puller.

Work to be Carried out

  1. Check on the fixation points for tubes

  2. Check on the tubes surfaces for carbon and sulfur layers

  3. Examine the heating surface for erosion

  4. Examine the heating surface for cracks

How to Remove a tube Sample for Inspection

It sometimes becomes necessary to remove a tube sample from the furnace for a waterside inspection, or to be sent out for analysis. Certain procedures should be followed to insure that the sample is removed intact, and that it is documented as to its exact location

When Removing a Tube Sample

  1. The tube section should be saw-cut to prevent any slag from flowing inside the sample or the good section of tubing.

  2. The sample should be cut approximately 8-10 inches above and below the affected area.

The exact location should be documented as follows:

  1. Tubes should be numbered and counted from east to west and north to south.

  2. The wall and elevation should be noted and marked on the tube.

  3. An arrow should be marked to indicate which end of the sample is up.

  4. On a universal pressure unit, the sample should be identified as either first, second, or third pass.

  5. Select for the type of welding electrodes
