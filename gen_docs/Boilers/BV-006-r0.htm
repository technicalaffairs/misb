Introduction

This document out outlines the minor maintenance procedure as indicated by P1 in the above title block for the manual gate valve depending on external / internal leakage

This valve can be used in lines at any position.

The first draft initiated and tested by eng. Fawzy Ahmed Basuoni at 27 Dec 2003, accepted by General Director eng. Saad Mostafa, revised by EDEPCo. eng.. Samir Abdel-Gelil, and approved by eng. Awad Mohammed Fathy Soliman Head  Sector of Talkha power station.

  • Work order must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Wear safety Helmet, belt, shoes, .... etc

  • Isolate valve mechanically.

  • Drain the pipeline, which connected to this valve.

  • Cleaning fluid

  • Suitable lifting device.

After Isolating the Valve Mechanically

  1. Be sure the valve was out of operation and the pressure inside the pipeline connected to the valve must be zero from the two end of the valve.

  2. Be sure the pipeline is drained.

Disassembly the Valve 

  1. Turn the hand wheel (2) to open the valve.

  2. Screw off nut (5-2).

  3. Take out hand wheel (2) and take out key (5-5).

  4. Screw of nut (3-10) of bolts (3-7) which connected yoke (3-3) with bonnet (4-1).

  5. Turn the yoke (3-3) around stem (3-5) until the yoke take out from stem.

  6. Screws off the retaining screw (5-4).

  7. Take out nut (5-3) which fixed yoke sleeve (5-1) with yoke (3-3).

  8. Take out yoke sleeve (5-1) from yoke (3-3).

  9. Screw off the nuts (3-11) of gland bolts (3-8).

  10. Take out gland follower (3-2).

  11. Screw off nut (4-3) of bolts (4-2) which connected bonnet (4-1) with body (1).

  12. Takes up stem (3-5) with wedge (3-1) and bonnet (4-1).

  13. Take out gasket (4-4) between bonnet and body.

  14. Take out wedge (3-1) from stem (3-5).

  15. Withdraw the stem (3-5) from bonnet (4-1).

  16. Take out the gland packing (4-5) from stuffing box, cooling push.

  17. Screw of nuts of bolts for flange end for flange type valve.

Inspection and Repair of all Parts

  1. Check the valves wedge (3-1) and regrind the sealing surface of it if needed.

  2. Check the valve seat rings (3-4) and regrind it if needed. When grinding the seats of the movers, attention must be paid to maintaining the angles of the sealing surfaces. In order to secure the mover function it is not allowed to reduce the seat parts by more than 0.8 mm in total (not more than 0.4 mm for one surface).

  3. Repair the valve wedge (3-1) and valve seats (3-4) in a specialist workshop if grinding can not repair the wear in there.

  4. Check the stem (3-5) and repair it if needed or replace it.

  5. Check the yoke sleeve (5-1) and repair it if needed or replace it.

  6. Check cooling ring.

  7. Check the packing gland (3-2) and repair it if needed or replace it.

  8. Clean every connection face.

  9. Change gasket (4-4).

  10. Checks socket adapter (3) and replace it if needed.

  11. Check the body (1), bonnet (4-1) from any cracks or wear by using penetration fluids and repair it if found by using suitable filler material or its equivalent acc. to table (1).

  12. Change gasket for flange end.

  13. If the valve damaged, change it w.r.t. the material as stated in table 1

Assembly of Valve

  1. Clean every part well before any assembly.

  2. Assembly the valve parts in reverse sequence of disassembly.

Replace the Packing of Valve Gland

  1. Clean the stuffing box chamber and the cover well.

  2. Lubricate the gland bolts and nuts.

  3. Use suitable size of packing.

  4. Wire the suitable packing size on a part of shaft with the same diameter of valve spindle.

  5. Cut all packing rings along the shaft by using sharp knife.

  6. Fit the rings in the stuffing box chamber with the cut turned 180 °, starting at 9 clock.

  7. Tight the gland bolts.

  8. Open and close the valve and be sure that the valve operation is correct.

 o         If you need any help please go to the operating instruction for repairs and maintenance D-4.

o         After successfully assembly coordinate with the shift operation engineer to test the valve to check that every thing is OK.

Trouble Shouting

Trouble

Possible Reasons

Remedy

1.  Leakage

Sealing surfaces – seat damaged by foreign object or by flowing substance.

Seat grinding

Replacement of damage part.

2.  Broken packing cover

    (stuffing box follower)

Squeezing of soft seal ring due to fluctuating temperatures.

Replacements of the packing cover.

Loosened bolts of the cover due to piping vibration.

Retighten the bolts.

3.  Leakage packing

Worn packing

Adequate tightening of bolts.

Packing dried up.

Replacement of the packing.
