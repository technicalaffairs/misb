Introduction

This document lists the maintenance procedure of electric gate valve, depending on external / internal leakage.

This valve used as fully opening or closing completely (Open / closed only state)

The first draft initiated and tested by eng. Fawzy Ahmed Basuoni at 14 Jan 2004, accepted by General Director eng. Saad Mostafa, revised by EDEPC eng. Samir Abdel-Gelil, and approved by eng. Awad Mohammed Fathy Soliman Head  Sector of Talkha power station.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area.

  • Wear your safety helmet, shoes, … etc.

  • Isolate valve mechanically and electrically.

  • Drain the pipeline, which connected to this valve.

    •

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set.

  • Cleaning fluid.

  • Suitable lifting device.

Work to be Carried Out

Before Isolating the Valve Mechanically & Electrically

  1. Check any leakage from valve gland. If it found, retighten on gland bolts.

  2. Check any leakage from the pressure tight cover. If it found retighten the cover nut.

  3. Check any internal leakage.

  4. Check any leakage from the valve bodies (cracks or mechanical wear). Remark the point of leakage.

  5. Check the operation of the valve drive during closing/ opening of the valve.

After Isolating the Valve Mechanically & Electrically

  1. Be sure the valve was out of operation and the pressure inside the pipeline connected to the valve must be zero from the two end of the valve.

  2. Be sure the pipeline is drained.

  3. Be sure the circuit breaker isolated for electric drive type.

Disassembly the valve

  1. Screw off the nuts (24) of gland bolts (22).

  2. Take up gland follower (12).

  3. Dismantle the sleeve (11) with gland’s bolt.

  4. Screw off nut (18).

  5. Take up plate (17) by using two withdraws.

  6. Take out the dividing ring (16) and take up the ring (15).

  7. Turn the hand wheel (3) to open it then the end of spindle (8) pressed on the bottom of pressure tight cover (5) and take it up.

  8. Screw off the nuts (23) and bolts (21), which connected yoke (9) with nipple (2).

  9. Take out the yoke (9) with spindle (8), with wedge (7) with all parts above.

  10. Take out the wedge (7) from spindle (8).

  11. Turn the spindle ( 8 ) until goes out from spindle nut (10).

  12. Take out the gland follower (12) from spindle (8).

  13. Withdraws the spindle (8) from pressure tight cover (5).

  14. Take out the sealing (27) and gland sealing (28) from pressure tight cover (5).

  15. Screw off bolt (34), which connected electric drive (33) with box (32) .

  16. Lift the electric drive with suitable lifting device.

  17. Withdraw the coupling half from the drive gear shaft (38).

  18. Screw off bolts (35) and take out cover (36) of box (32) .

  19. Take out the gear drive (38) with its shaft (39) by hammering on the lower end.

  20. Takes out the bearing (31) of the gear drive (38).

  21. Screw off the nut (40) and then take out gear (41).

  22. With draw the gear wheel (30) from the spindle nut (10).

  23. Take out the box (32).

  24.  Screw off the bolts (42) and then take out flange (29).

  25. Take out the spindle nut (10) from yoke (9), bearing (26) and dust seal (20).

Inspection and Repair of all Parts

  1. Check the valve wedge (7) and regrind the sealing surface of it if needed.

  2. Check the valve seat (saddle) (4) and regrind it if needed. When grinding the seats of the movers, attention must be paid to maintaining the angles of the sealing surfaces. In order to secure the mover function it is not allowed to reduce the seat parts by more than 0.8 mm in total (not more than 0.4 mm for one surface).

  3. Repair the valve wedge (7) and valve seats (4) in a specialist workshop if grinding can not repair the wear in there.

  4. Check the spindle (8) and repair it if needed or replace it.

  5. Check the spindles nut (10) and repair it if needed or replace it.

  6. Check the bearing (26) and replace it if needed.

  7. Check the dust seal rings (20) and replace them if needed.

  8. Check the bush (14) and repair it if needed or replace it.

  9. Check the coupling insert and replaced if it needed.

  10. Check the gears (38) , (41) , (30)  and bearing (31).

  11. Check the body (1), nipple (25) from any cracks or wear by using penetration fluids and repair it if found by using suitable filler material or its equivalent acc. to table (1).

  12. If the valve damaged, change it w.r.t. the material as stated in table 1

Assembly of Valve

  1. Clean every part well before any assembly.

  2. Assembly the valve parts in reverse sequence of disassembly.

Replace the Packing of Valve Gland

  1. Clean the stuffing box chamber and the cover well.

  2. Lubricate the gland bolts and nuts.

  3. Use suitable size of packing.

  4. Wire the suitable packing size on a part of shaft with the same diameter of valve spindle.

  5. Cut all packing rings along the shaft by using sharp knife.

  6. Fit the rings in the stuffing box chamber with the cut turned 180 °, starting at 9 clock.

  7. Tighten the gland bolts.

  8.  Open and close the valve and be sure that the valve operation is correct.

×    If you need any help please go to the operating instruction for repairs and maintenance D-4.

×   After successfully assembly coordinate with the shift operation engineer to test the valve to check that every thing is OK

Trouble

Possible Reasons

Remedy

1.      Leakage

Sealing surfaces

Seat damaged by foreign object or by flowing substance.

Seat grinding

Replacement of damage part.

2.   Broken packing cover
      (stuffing box follower)

Squeezing of soft seal ring due to fluctuating temperatures.

Replacements of the packing cover.

Loosened bolts of the cover due to piping vibration.

Retighten the bolts.

3.     Leakage packing

Worn packing

Adequate tightening of bolts.

Packing dried up.

Replacement of the packing.

4.     Defect in electric servomotor

Defect in limit switches

Readjust limit switches.

Defect in moment switches.

Readjust moment switches.
