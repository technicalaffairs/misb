Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks must be surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear the safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes, ...etc).

    •

  • The boiler must be drained, and the boiler vents must be opened

  • Hearing and eye protection should be used when working on a valve which is under pres­sure.

  • Never strike a valve which is under pressure. Premature actuation can result.

  • Never stand in front of the discharge outlet of a pressure relief valve, which is under pres­sure.

  • Always approach and use any pressure relief valve with great care.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set.

  • Cleaning fluid, manual puller,

  • Suitable lifting device Feeler gauge.

  • Hammer, and air wrench.

    •

Work to be Carried Out

Disassembly

  1. Remove the forked lever pin 23, forked lever 22, cap 25, spindle nut cotter 28 and spindle nut12, Figure 1

  2. Remove the nozzle ring set screw  and verify location

  3. Remove the adjusting (guide) ring set screw  and verify location

Disassembly Retaining Spring Compression with Mechanical Jacking Device (Fig 7)

  1. Measure from bottom face of bottom spring washer tobonnet flange top face and record dimension. Cut two pieces of bar stock 1/8" longer than recorded

  2. Lubricate the spindle thread with "Never-Seez","Molykote-G", or equivalent, and install the spindle lifter  on the spindle

  3. Lubricate the thrust bearing with suitable bearing grease and install each part into the housing

  4. The bottom thrust washer is guided into position on its outside diameter.  Install the cage on the bottom thrust washer.  Place the top thrust washer  on top of the cage.

  5. Install the housing on top of the valve bonnet, taking care to insure that the key locates in the keyway slot in the spindle lifter.

  6.  If the jacking device has been properly installed, approx­imately 1/2" of the keyway should be visible.  The spindle lifter must be screwed (clockwise) onto the valve spindle until the proper length of keyway is visible.* 1/8" (3.18 mm): 1/2" H2.70 mm)

  7. Lubricate the spindle lifter external threads and screw the operating nut onto the spindle lifter  until it contacts the thrust bearing.

  8. Rotating the operating nut  clockwise raises the valve spindle thereby raising the lower spring washer and compressing the spring.

  9. After the spring load has been taken up and the valve has been jacked approximately 1/8", place spacer blocks under the lower spring washer.

  10. The jacking device can now be removed by reversing The spindle nut may now be installed to hold the spindle in position during disassembly. If desired, the jacking device may be kept in place.

  11. Using suitable lifting straps - cables - carefully lift the superstructure straight up and out of the body.

  12. Lay the superstructure down so that the spindle is in the horizontal position. Care must, be exercised to prevent the parts from being damaged.

  13. With the superstructure laying down, remove the spindle nut or jacking device from on the spindle. You can now carefully slide the internals, see Figure 1, disc holder, disc insert, guide, guide ring  and spindle as an assembly from the spring  and bonnet.

Reassembly

The safety valve should be reassembled in the reverse order of disassembly, this order of assembly will eliminate interference problems with each previously assembled group of parts

Lapping of Valve Seat

  1. Good seating on the nozzle (2) and disc insert (35) are of the greatest importance when reconditioning the safety valves

  2. This is made of a special grade of annealed cast iron perfectly flat on both sides which has small squares

  3. Use suitable lapping compound

  4. Never lap the disc insert against the nozzle. Lap each part separately against the cast iron lapping block of proper size

  5. Lap the block against the seat. Never rotate the block continuously, but use an oscillating movement

  6. When all the nicks and marks have disappeared, remove all the compound from the block and seat.

  7. Apply polish compound to another block and lap the seat with this.
