Safety Precautions

  • A work order must be issued.

  • The maintenance staff should be qualified and carry the suitable safety ID category.

  • Wear safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes …etc

    •

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required       

  • Isolate the circuit breaker for fan motor.

  • Unit out of service.

 

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set.

  • Cleaning fluid, feeler gauge.

  • Suitable lifting device.

Work to be Carried Out

Before Isolating the FDF Mechanically and Electrically

  1. Record the availability of operational data such as vibration level, bearing temperature, motor ampere, damper position … etc.

  2. Listen and check any abnormal noise from any components like bearing inlet damper …etc.

  3. Observe for any leakage in the fan like oil, water, air at fan connections

After Fan stopped and isolated

The following data may be recorded / ensured for reference during reassembly.

  1. Coupling alignment.

  2. Fan shaft level.

  3. Marking the assembled component by match marks.

Disassembly of the Coupling  figure (1)

  1. Dismantle the safety cover of coupling.

  2. Remove the two covers of coupling away from both.

  3. Then the two shaft can be separated (fan shaft and motor shaft).

Disassembly of the Front / Rear Bearing (Sleeve oil Pillow Blocks) figure 2.

  1. Remove temperature sensor.

  2. Disassembly all cooling water pipes (2).

  3. Drain the sump oil by screw off oil drain plug (15).

  4. Screw off the bolts (7), to separate two halves of bearing house.

  5. Screw off plunger screw nut (10) to take liner assemblies out (16).

Disassembly of Impeller / Fan Shaft

  1. Remove all thermometer connections and pipe lines.

  2. Dismount labyrinth seal assemblies.

  3. Remove fasteners in the horizontal and vertical flange joints of upper quadrant of the spiral casing at the discharge end.

  4. Pull and slide out the quadrant remove.

  5. Lift the rotor assembly and position on rotor transport stand with due care.

  6. The impeller can be separated from the shaft by removing the conical cover plate and the shaft flange to impeller connecting bolts.

Inspection after assembling

  1. Check the two halves of coupling for mechanical wear.

  2. Check the coupling spring.

  3. Check the two halves of hearing house for mechanical wear.

  4. Check every parts of bearing and liner assembly.

  5. Check the shaft surface finishing and its dimensions at ( bearing seating area, impeller and coupling area ).

  6. Check the impeller and its blades for mechanical wear and tear.

Reassembly

After Inspection

  1. Clean every part well and reassembly every part in reverses order ensuring the following:

    • Bearing clearance.

    • Shaft level.

    • Impeller clearance.

    • Fan motor alignment and coupling gap.

  2. Re- lubricating the coupling by recommended or equivalent grease and fill the front/rear bearing by recommended lube oil (through a fine mesh strainer) up the required level.

Inspection after Operation

  1. Measure and record the vibration of fan.

  2. Observe the noise of fan at bearing, inlet damper area.

  3. Check the lube oil level in the front/rear bearing.

  4. Check and record temperature of the front/rear bearing.

  5. Compare the values of vibration and temperature with limits value as shown in the table blow:
