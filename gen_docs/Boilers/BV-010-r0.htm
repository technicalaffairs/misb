Introduction

This document outlines the major maintenance procedures P1, which must be performed every three years as indicated by Y3 in the above title block.

This valve is used for open or closed the main steam to the high pressure turbine.

Work to be Carried out

Dismantling the Valve

  1. Use the handwheel to open the valve. Support the actuator where fitted.

  2. Remove capscrews which secure the actuator or the gearbox to the adaptor plate (238).

  3. Turn handwheel in a 'closing', clockwise direction, simultaneously drawing it away from the valve. Actuators:

  4. When the actuator disengages from the end of the stem thread do not turn the handwheel any further as this will simplify replacement (i.e. in the re-setting of limit switches).

  5. Place actuator in a safe position, taking care not to strain the actuator cables. If necessary disconnect the electric cables.

Caution: The valve spindle may be free to drop. If necessary, support the spindle.

  1. Handwheel: Place the handwheel and gearbox assembly in a safe place.

  2. Remove grubscrew (57) guide nut, (18) cotter (97) and key (59).

  3. Remove pillars (8) and adaptor plate.

  4. The stem guide (11) can now be removed from the spindle/belt (19). Note the position of the stem guide and orientation of the keyway for re-assembly.

  5. Remove gland stud nuts (522) in order to remove gland assembly: the gland plate (718), the gland cover (994), the gland cleaning ring (2058) and the gland follower (12).

  6. Remove jacking screw nuts (220) & distance pieces (201) also cover stud nuts (66) & washers (143)

  7. Remove the cover (200).

  8. Press bonnet (2) down so that it releases the load on the segmental locking ring. The cover (200) may be replaced upside down and the cover stud nuts tightened so as to dislodge the bonnet should it not move down easily.

  9. Remove Segmental locking ring (926) as follows:

  10. Remove master segment. This is the shortest segment.

  11. Remove remaining segments.

  12. The bonnet (2) complete with gasket (430), holding ring (424) and the belt assembly: the belt/stem (19), the discs and the disc spring (70) can be removed.

Caution - The discs and the spring will be free to drop once they are withdrawn from the seat space. Be prepared to take hold of the two discs and the spring to avoid them being damaged. It is preferable to make and fit the clamp.

  1. Drill out the small end of the disc holder pins 330, and then drive them out through the large end. Release the clamp.

N.B. If repacking is required remove stem/belt from lid to enable the old gland packing to be removed easier.

 

Assembly

  1. Assembly is generally a reverse of the dismantling procedure.

  2. Ensure that all parts are clean. Assemble the disc spring and discs together in the belt eye and clamp them together as shown in Figure 1.

  3. Fit the disc holder (29) and position new disc holder pins (330). Rivet these in place. The disc assembly now has to be introduced between the seats of the valve for which the spring has to be compressed to achieve this.

  4. If a clamp cannot be produced, a less satisfactory alternative is as follows:

  5. The discs and spring should be located in the belt assembly and entered into the valve body as far as the top of the seats with one disc already entered.

  6. Using a wood lever or some other such instrument carefully move back the free disc so that the assembly enters the space between the seats. The lever must only be used on the centre of the disc, and care taken not to damage the sealing faces.

  7. Position the bonnet over the stem and into the body.

  8. Fit the gasket, and push it into place using the holding ring.

  9. Fit segmental ring in the correct sequence with locking segment last.

  10. The bonnet can be lifted up to lock the segmental ring in place.

  11. Fit cover, jacking screw spacers and nuts. Evenly tighten the jacking screw nuts so as to keep the gasket square.

  12. Cover stud nuts (66) do not require a high degree of tightening as they do not contribute to gasket sealing.

  13. The next operation, which is to repack the gland, is very important.

  14. The gland must be re-packed so that the stem is absolutely concentric. This is important for both gland tightness and to prevent the scoring of the stem as it passes through the gland. New packing is therefore recommended.

  15. The first portion of packing is braided packing ring (61) which will be supplied long. Cut this to the equaled length just prior to fitting to avoid damage or unraveling of the braid.

  16. The ends should be cut square (there is no need to scarp or meter the ends) but is imperative that the braid is not cut short. Fit the braid followed by the exfoliated graphite packing rings (921). The rings (921) are die pressed circular rings which will help centralize the stem.

  17. The last portion of 'packing' is another braided packing ring (61) prepared as described above.

  18. Fit the gland (12), and (718) the cleaning ring (2058), the gland cover, (994) and gland stud nuts (522).

  19. Tighten the gland stud nuts (522) evenly, a small amount at a time so as to keep the stem centralized.

  20. Proceed to build up the valve by fitting pillars and replacing the stem guide to its previous position.

  21. When refitting the actuator, offer it up to the bare end of the stem thread. The stem may be lubricated with grease at this point (refer to Publication 80106C). 

  22. Rotate the actuator/handwheel anti-clockwise to engage it on the stem. Provided the actuator is engaged in exactly the same relation to the stem, then its limit switches will not require resetting, but must be checked to confirm this.

  23. Complete the refitting of the valve.

  24. Instigate safe conditions to hydraulically test the valve and adjust the gland packing and gasket to be leak-tight at pressures up to 1.1 times the designed operating pressure. It may be beneficial to check the operation of the actuator by reference to the position of the belt guide (11) before replacing the actuator covers.

  25. Replace the covers before operating the actuator electrically. When complete make a complete check of the operation of the valve and actuator (refer to Section 3 on Installation and Commissioning). Take care whilst actuator covers are open.

  26. Reinstate the job-site to safe working conditions e.g. remove lifting tackle, replace flooring, handrails etc. cancel permits.

  27. For actuator assembly checking and re-setting refer to the actuator information.

  28. For faults not specially covered, e.g. 'blown' fuses, follow the accepted safe methods of investigation and rectification.

  29. IF IN DOUBT ASK!

  30. Consult HOPKINSONS if anything is not clear. Safe working practices must prevail at all times.
