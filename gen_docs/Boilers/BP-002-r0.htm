Introduction

This document outlines the equipment inspection checklist I1  (to check leakage from pipes of boiler equipment)

The first draft initiated and tested by eng. Fawzy Ahmed Bassiouny, accepted by general manager eng. Saad Mostafa, revised by eng. Samir Abed El Gelil at EDEPC, and approved by eng. Awad Mohammed Fathy Soliman Head Sector of Talkha power station.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area,

  • Wear safety helmet, shoes.

  • Boiler out of service and pressure should be zero.

  • Vents opened

  • Drain all boiler walls and super heaters, Reheaters, and economizer

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set,

  • Suitable lifting device

  • Grinder & grinding wheel.

  • Selected filler metal for TIG welding (Argon) and suitable weld wire table 1

  • Welding machine.

  • Cleaning fluid.

Work to be Carried Out

Before Boiler Shutdown

  1. Check quantity of feeding water on chart at control room.

  2. Check any leakage from pipes of ceiling super heaters 1, 2, 3, 4, and re heaters 1, 2, evaporator 'walls" and economizer.

  3. Check drum water level on chart at control room.

After Boiler Shutdown

  1. Be sure the feed water pump out of operation,

  2. Check the pressure inside boiler equal zero and pressure inside all walls zero.

  3. Be sure walls drum, Super heaters, and Re-heaters drained.

  4. Vents opened.

Inspection of the Defected Pipes

  1. Clean every part well before any inspection.

  2. Operate the feed pump to increase pressure boiler from 0 to10MPa (hydraulic pressure test to detect any water leaks )

  3. Remove the steel sheet cover and thermal insulation of the chamber at the respective damage pipe.

  4. Check all pipes to find damage and remark on it.

Repair the Defected Pipes

  1. After finding out of the place of failure pipes.

  2. Remove the fixing parts as the suspensions, and bonds

  3. Cut out the damaged pipes.

  4. If you find any cracks you can weld re. table1

  5. Grind the edges to be ready for welding.

  6. New pipes should be prepared to be replaced

  7. It should be fitted and welded re. table1

  8. The length of replaced pipe must not less than 250 mm in the straight part of the part

  9. The weld must be chosen in the way ensuring the distance from the part welded to the pipe min. 20 mm, the distance from the suspension 50 mm and the distance from the end of bend 100 mm.

  10. After successfully welding carryout the hydraulic pressure test again.

Note:  If you need any help please go to the operating instruction for repairs and maintenance D-3.
