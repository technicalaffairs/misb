Introduction

This document lists the equipment inspection I1, of vertical rotary regenerative air heater, which must be, performed every month and six months as indicated by M1, M6 in the title block.

Regenerative air heater used for saving heat from flue gasses and use this heat for heating combustion air to increase boiler efficiency.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area,

  • Wear your safety helmet, shoes ... etc

  • Switch off the circuit breaker for the motor of regenerative air heater

  • Disconnect the electrical cable feeding of the motor regenerative air heater

  • The regenerative air heater must be isolated mechanically

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set

  • Cleaning fluid, Manual puller,

Work to be Carried Out

Monthly

  1. Check the bearing oil level and adjust as required.

  2. Check the main bearing temperature refer to " start - stop sequence" for alarm and trip temp.

  3. Check the bearing cooling water flow.

  4. Check the lube oil cct for oil leakage.

  5. Check the regenerative air heater for smooth operation.

  6. Check all bolts and support steel fasteners bolts, and tighten if necessary.

  7. Check the bearing oil for contamination.

  8. Check the oiller of the air heater.

  9. Check the air filter

  10. Check the differential pressure of the oil filter of the guide bearing

  11. Check the cooling water temperature of the guide bearing (not increase than 38c)

  12. Check the clearance regard to figure 1

Every 6 month

  1. Examine all heating surface for any signs of erosion

  2. Examine air heater housing for signs of wear

  3. Examine damper damper assemblies for wear of blades and blade spindles

  4. Examine bearings and couplings and make adjustments or replacements if necessary. Give particular attention to bearing seals and change if needed

  5. Check all seal clearance and adjust as required. (see Figure no 1)

  6. Check shaft seals.

  7. Check coupling alignment.

  8. Check the over running clutches.

  9. Check and inspect the air motor.
