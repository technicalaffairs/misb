Introduction  

This document out lines the major maintenance procedures P1, to be performed every two year as indicated by Y2 in the above title block.

Forced Draft Fan used to supply the boiler with combustion air, GR Fan used to increase reheater temperature, and SL Fan used to supply boiler with seal air.

The first draft initiated by eng. Abdalla Mohamed Abdalla, reviewed by director eng. Gamal Mahmoud Ahmed Arafa, accepted by general director eng. Mohamed Abd El-Salam Mahmoud, and approved by eng: Michael Goerge: head sector of Ayoun Moussa steam power station 2x320 MW

This document is accepted by eng. Samir Abd El Gilil EDEPC MPIS coordinator, and certified by accountant; Mahmoud Mohamed Soliman; EDEPC admin and financial inspection, quality control General Director and supervisor of inspection and quality control head sector.

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued; the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category,

  • Safety fence with caution marks must be surrounding the work area is required,

  • Wear your safety and healthy equipment as safety helmet, safety shoes.... etc

  • Isolate the circuit breaker for the motor of the required fan.

  • Isolate the electrical cable feeding of the motor of the required fan.

  • Isolate the required fan mechanically.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set.

  • Cleaning fluid, manual puller,

  • Suitable lifting device, feeler gauge.

  • Hammer, Dial indicator, and torque wrench

 

 

Work to be Carried Out

 

Fan Disassembly Sequence    (see Figures 1, 2, 3, 4 )

  1. Disconnect the coupling between the fan rotor and motor rotor.

  2. Remove the suction screen bolt.

  3. Remove the suction screen by using the hoist.

  4. Remove the silencer bolt.

  5. Remove the silencer by using the hoist.

  6. Remove the inlet cones tighting bolts.

  7. Remove the fan upper half housing bolt.

  8. Remove the fan upper half housing.

  9. Remove the bearing instruments.

  10. Remove the bearing coupling water hoses and fitting.

  11. Drain the bearings oil.

  12. Remove the bearing upper half.

  13. Remove the rotor and inlet cones by using the hoist.

Fan Assembly Sequence

  1. Clean every part well.

  2. The  Fan should be assembled in the reverse order of disassembly

Fan Trouble Shooting

Excessive Vibration

  • Accumulated material on rotor wheel

  • Worn or corroded wheel

  • Rotor wheel or sheaves loose on shaft

  • Rotor out of balance

  • Sheaves eccentric or out of balance

  • Bearings or drive misalignment

  • Belts too loose or too tight

  • Loose or worn bearings

  • Fan mounting bolts loose

  • Weak resonant foundation

  • Fan operating in an unstable condition

Insufficient Air Flow

  • Duct elbows near fan inlet or outlet

  • Restricted inlet or outlet

  • Rotor rotating in wrong direction

  • Fan speed lower than design

  • System resistance higher than design

  • Damper or VIV not properly adjusted

  • inlet screen clogged

  • Dirty filter bank

  • Faulty duct work.

Excessive air flow

  • System resistance less than design

  • Fan speed too high

  • Improper damper adjustment.

Excessive Horse Power Draw

  • Fan speed higher than design

  • Gas density higher than design

  • Rotor wrong rotation

  • Static pressure less than anticipated.
