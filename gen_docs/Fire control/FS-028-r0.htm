Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued and the maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category

  • Maintenance staff should well trained & qualified or directly supervised by some one who is.

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area are required.

  • Personal protective equipment must be used as safety shoes, safety helmet, ...etc.

  • Unit out of service

Tools and Equipment

  • Suitable tool set,  oil bath ... etc

  • Safety helmet and safety shoes, ... etc.

  • Temperature Indicator

  • Heater (oil bath)

    •

Work to be Carried Out

  1. Disconnect power supply of the system.

  2. Disconnect all connections for each fire detectors frequently.

  3. Connect power supply of the system.

  4. Check all fire detectors.

  5. Each fire detectors should be tested for proper operation by heating it in oil bath.

  6. Check the temperature, which close the contact of fire detector

  7. The temperature setting of the detectors is stamped on its identification plate.

  8. Repeat all steps ( 4- 5 – 6 ) all fire detectors.

  9. If any detector is out of calibration limit it should be replaced.

NOTE

Do not use a propane torch to heat the detectors because the light flam temperature of the torch may destroy calibration of the detectors.

It is not possible to accurately check the calibration of the detectors by heating it in an oil bath or with a heat lamp because the set point of the detectors is sensitive to the rate of heading which cannot be easily determined without specialized equipment.

Return System into Service

  1. Connect all detectors in the same place.

  2. Connect the power supply to the system.

  3. Check all alarms.

  4. Put the system at automatic mode.
