Introduction

This document outlines the major maintenance procedures P1, which must be performed on an annually bases as indicated by Y1 in the above title block.

Deluge valve is open to allow passing water to mixing with foam through ejector if two fire detectors operate at 90 °C

Safety Precautions

  • A work permit must be issued, the qualified maintenance staff should carry the suitable safety category, 

  • Safety fence with caution marks surrounding the work area is required.

  • Wear your safety helmet, safety shoes, eye glass, nose mouth "dust cover" .... etc

    •

  • Close the water supply line-isolating valve.

  • Disconnect the DC power from solenoid valve.

Tools and Equipment

  • Complete maintenance tool set.

  • Safety and healthy equipment

  • Rags.

Work to be Carried out

  1. Remove the restrictor orifice (part #1 Drw 1) and check its cleaning.

  2. Clean the 1/4 inch "Y" strainer (part # 3 Drw 1) located in the 1/4 priming by-pass line to remove any accumulated sediment.

  3. Inspect the valve seat if a continuous flow of water from the ½ inch ball drip valve (part # 6 Drw 1) is observed following this procedure.

Valve Disassembly

  1. Close the main shut off valve (part # 7 Figure 1)  

  2. Close the ¼ inch valve (part # 2 Figure 1)  

  3. Open main drain valve (part # 5 Figure 1) to drain system piping

  4. Open manual emergency station model (DL -- A) to drain    

  5. Drain pilot sprinkler

  6. Open ½ inch water flow test valve (part # 4 Figure 1) to relieve  

  7. Water pressure in lower chamber

  8. Remove the deluge valve cover

  9. Lift the diaphragm piston assembly out of the valve.

Valve Inspection and Repair

  1. Inspect the valve seat (Part # 4 Figure 2) for irregularities and remove any foreign substance, clean the valve seat if any

  2. Grind the valve seat with grinding paste if any scratches are observed on the seat

  3. Inspect rubber seal (Part # 7 Figure 2) and replace it if any sign of wear is noticed

  4. Inspect the neoprene diaphragm (Part #6 Figure 2) and replace it if it is cracked or  shows signs of wear

Valve Re-assembly

  1. Assemble the neoprene diaphragm (Part # 6 Figure 2).

  2. Assemble the rubber seal (Part # 7 Figure 2).

  3. Assemble the valve diaphragm piston set

Assemble the Valve Cover (Part #2 Figure 2)

  1. Start the system before the system the following steps must be checked:-

  2. Close ½ inch water flow test valve (part # 4 Figure 1).

  3. Close manual emergency station valve model (DL-A).

  4. Close main drain valve (Part # 5 Figure 1).

  5. Open the ¼ inch valve (Part # 2 Figure 1).

  6. Open the main shut-off valve (Part # 7 Figure 1).

 

Attendance and Checking During Operation

  1. Check for any leakage in the system.

  2. Check the water pressure in system.
